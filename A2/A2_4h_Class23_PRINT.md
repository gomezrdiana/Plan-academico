### CLUSTER 2 — COMPARATIVOS/SUPERLATIVOS + GERUNDIO vs. INFINITIVO (foco de hoy)

Dos bloques de error que más bloquean el salto a B1. Se trabajan AMBOS, en este orden:

**A) Comparativos y superlativos**
- Comparativo = comparar 2 cosas: adjetivo corto + -ER + THAN / MORE + adjetivo largo + THAN: *"taller than" / "more complicated than"*
- Superlativo = el extremo entre 3+: THE + adjetivo corto + -EST / THE MOST + adjetivo largo: *"the tallest" / "the most complicated"*
- Irregulares: good → better → the best · bad → worse → the worst
- Errores típicos canónicos (cosechados de las guías A2 M27/M28/M29):
  - ❌ *"She is more taller than me."* → ✅ *"She is TALLER than me."* (no doble comparativo)
  - ❌ *"My job is gooder than yours."* → ✅ *"My job is BETTER than yours."* (irregular)
  - ❌ *"It is more complicated that yours."* → ✅ *"It is more complicated THAN yours."* (than, no that)
  - ❌ *"She is the most tallest."* → ✅ *"She is THE TALLEST."* (no doble superlativo)
  - ❌ *"He is the goodest."* → ✅ *"He is THE BEST."* (irregular)
  - ❌ *"She is most intelligent."* → ✅ *"She is THE most intelligent."* (falta 'the')
  - ❌ *"the most big project"* → ✅ *"the biggest project"* (corto → -est, no 'most')
  - ❌ *"He is very taller."* → ✅ *"He is much taller." / "He is very tall."* (intensificador NO con comparativo)

**B) Gerundio vs. infinitivo**
- Gerundio (verbo + -ing) = como sujeto / después de preposición / después de ciertos verbos: *"Speaking is easy." / "before starting" / "I enjoy reading."*
- Infinitivo (to + verbo) = después de ciertos verbos cuando la acción NO ha empezado: *"I want to travel." / "I decided to change."*
- Regla nuclear: el PRIMER verbo decide. *want/decide/need/forget/hope* → infinitivo · *enjoy/finish/stop/like/love/avoid* → gerundio. Después de PREPOSICIÓN siempre -ing.
- Errores típicos canónicos (cosechados de las guías B1 Grammar M20/M21/M22):
  - ❌ *"Work is important."* (verbo como sujeto) → ✅ *"WORKING is important."* (sujeto = gerundio)
  - ❌ *"Speaking are easy."* → ✅ *"Speaking IS easy."* (gerundio-sujeto = singular)
  - ❌ *"Before to start, I check the plan."* → ✅ *"Before STARTING, I check the plan."* (prep + -ing)
  - ❌ *"I never speak without to think."* → ✅ *"I never speak without THINKING."* (without + -ing)
  - ❌ *"I enjoy to read."* → ✅ *"I enjoy READING."* (enjoy + gerundio)
  - ❌ *"I decided changing jobs."* → ✅ *"I decided TO CHANGE jobs."* (decide + infinitivo)
  - ❌ *"I want working here."* → ✅ *"I want TO WORK here."* (want + infinitivo)

**Origen del contenido (no inventado):** los patrones de error provienen de los listados "Ejemplos típicos" ya presentes en guías A2 de comparativos/superlativos (M27 unequal: *"more taller" → "TALLER"*; M28 superlative: *"the most tallest" → "THE TALLEST"*; M29 degrees: intensificador con comparativo) y de guías B1 Grammar de gerundios (M20 gerund-as-subject: *"Work is important" → "WORKING is"*; M21 gerund after preposition: *"before to start" → "before STARTING"*; M22 double verbs: *"I decided changing" → "I decided TO CHANGE"*), más la pedagogía estándar de errores A2→B1. ### Concepto (14 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
BLOCK A — COMPARATIVE vs SUPERLATIVE  [review/fix]

COMPARATIVE = 2 things (+ THAN)
  short adj + -ER:   "She is TALLER than me."
  long adj + MORE:   "It is MORE COMPLICATED than that."
  NEVER both:  WRONG "more taller"   RIGHT "taller"

SUPERLATIVE = the extreme, 3+ (THE + ...)
  short adj: THE + -EST:  "She is THE TALLEST."
  long adj:  THE MOST + adj:  "the most reliable"
  NEVER both:  WRONG "the most tallest"  RIGHT "the tallest"
  NEEDS 'the':  WRONG "she is most intelligent"
                RIGHT "she is THE most intelligent"

IRREGULARS:  good -> better -> the best
             bad  -> worse  -> the worst
THAN not THAT:  "...more complicated THAN yours."
intensifier + comparative? NO -> "He is MUCH taller" (not "very taller")
```

## 9. CORRECCIÓN SISTEMÁTICA B — GERUNDIO vs. INFINITIVO (01:07–01:31, 24 min) — SENTADO

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"The other big block before B1: when do I say 'reading' and when 'to read'? Subject, after a preposition, and 'the first verb decides'. We make the choice automatic."*

### Concepto B1 — gerundio: sujeto y después de preposición (10 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
BLOCK B — GERUND vs INFINITIVE  [review/fix]

GERUND (verb + -ing) as SUBJECT
  "WORKING is important."  (not "Work is important.")
  gerund subject = SINGULAR -> "Speaking IS easy." (not "are")

GERUND after a PREPOSITION (before/after/without/about + -ing)
  "Before STARTING, I check the plan."  (not "before to start")
  "I never speak without THINKING."     (not "without to think")
```

### Concepto B2 — "el primer verbo decide" (10 min)

**Tablero — tercera sección:**

```
BLOCK B — DOUBLE VERBS: the FIRST verb decides

INFINITIVE (to + verb) -> want / decide / need / forget / hope
  "I want TO WORK here."   "I decided TO CHANGE jobs."
  (the second action has NOT started yet)

GERUND (verb + -ing) -> enjoy / finish / stop / like / love / avoid
  "I enjoy READING."   "I finished WORKING."   "I stopped ARRIVING late."
  (the action has started / is the activity itself)

RULE: never "I enjoy to read" / never "I decided changing".
```

**Errores típicos modelados (canónicos — cosechados de las guías B1 Grammar M20/M21/M22, SIN nombres):**

- ❌ *"Work is important."* → ✅ *"WORKING is important."* (sujeto = gerundio)
- ❌ *"Speaking are easy."* → ✅ *"Speaking IS easy."* (gerundio-sujeto = singular)
- ❌ *"Before to start, I check the plan."* → ✅ *"Before STARTING, I check the plan."* (prep + -ing)
- ❌ *"I never speak without to think."* → ✅ *"I never speak without THINKING."* (without + -ing)
- ❌ *"I enjoy to read."* → ✅ *"I enjoy READING."* (enjoy + gerundio)
- ❌ *"I decided changing jobs."* → ✅ *"I decided TO CHANGE jobs."* (decide + infinitivo)
- ❌ *"I want working here."* → ✅ *"I want TO WORK here."* (want + infinitivo)

### Práctica guiada (4 min)

5-6 estudiantes AL AZAR: das un disparador (*"before ___" / "I enjoy ___" / "I decided ___" / "___ is important (verb as subject)"*); ellos eligen y producen gerundio o infinitivo correctamente.

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"First verb or preposition? Weigh it — choose gerund or infinitive on purpose."*

## 11. SIMULACIÓN PROFESIONAL — "THE REFERENCE CALL" (01:43–02:08, 25 min) — DE PIE

## 12. SIMULACIÓN INTEGRADA — "THE PROJECT PITCH" (02:08–02:30, 22 min) — DE PIE

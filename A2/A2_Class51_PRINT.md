## BLOCK 1 — OPENER — 10 min

### (1 min) Welcome + frame
> *"Welcome to Cl 51 of 55. Today opens JUSTICE v3 — day ONE of five. Today and the next class are PREPARATION for the presentations on Cl 53. Each of you picks ONE topic from your own work or life and builds a 5-minute talk + 2-minute Q&A. Today: topic + structure + draft + first oral run-through. Next class: rehearsal + peer Q&A practice. Cl 53: the presentations. Cl 54: cycle close. Cl 55: Final, administered by coordination."*

### (2 min) Phrase of the Day — on the board (already written)

```
PHRASE OF THE DAY — CL 51
"Justice day one — give your topic the time it
 deserves, give the audience the structure they
 expect; one hook, three points, one close —
 fair to your idea and fair to their ears."
```

Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Justice day 1 — fair to your idea (the time and the structure your topic deserves) and fair to the audience (the structure they expect). One HOOK 30 seconds, three POINTS 1 minute each, one CLOSE 30 seconds, then 2 minutes Q&A. Fair to both. Five minutes total + 2 Q&A — no more, no less."*

### (2 min) 60-sec mystery — fast diagnostic
Open the envelope. Read aloud:
> *"60 seconds. From the previous class's homework (Cl 50, mastery — 9-cluster paragraph), give me TWO sentences from your paragraph: ONE that used a CONDITIONAL (1 or 2) and ONE that used REPORTED SPEECH (said or told me). Go."*

Pick 3 students at random. Fluent = +5 EP. Hesitates = *"Sit. Thank you."* If anyone asks about Final grades: *"Coordination handles results — not me, not today."* Note who did not bring the 3 homework parts (mastery paragraph + audio + listening).

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing 30-second topic pitch
Everyone stands in a circle. You call a student's name. They give a 30-SECOND pitch on a topic they MIGHT present — work topic, hobby, place they know, problem they've solved. Just brainstorm — no structure yet. Move to the next student. 5–6 students in 3 min.

Goal: surface topic ideas, hear who already has a clear angle vs who is searching.

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1:** *"Fair to your idea — what does your topic actually need to land?"*

### (2 min) Bridge to today's content
> *"Today is prep day 1. By the end of today every one of you has: ONE chosen topic + ONE structured draft on paper + ONE first oral run-through completed with a partner. Tomorrow you rehearse twice and do peer Q&A. Cl 53 is the day. No grades from me — ever. Coordination handles results. Let's start."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 50 min — TOPIC + STRUCTURE + DRAFT

### (10 min) Topic selection — silent then pair

Sit. Silent 4 min: each student writes 3 candidate topics on paper. Then pair 4 min: read all 3 to partner, partner picks the strongest 1 + says WHY in one sentence. Then silent 2 min: each student writes their ONE chosen topic at the top of a clean page.

Rules for topic choice (you say out loud once):
> *"Pick a topic you ALREADY know. No research topics. Pick a topic that connects to your real work or life — what you do every day, a problem you solved, a place you know, a service you provide, a decision you made. Five minutes is short — pick narrow, not broad. Not 'My company' — pick 'How we handle complaint calls' or 'The morning shift at our warehouse'. Narrow lands."*

If a student freezes, do NOT pick the topic for them. Say: *"Three candidates. Now."* and walk away for 60 sec.

### (10 min) STRUCTURE template — on the board + handout

Write on the board (before class) AND hand out 1 printed template per student:

```
STRUCTURE TEMPLATE — 5 MIN TALK + 2 MIN Q&A
(JUSTICE day 1 — fair to idea, fair to audience)

HOOK (30 sec)
  ONE sentence that earns attention.
  Choices:
    - A question:  "How do you handle 50 calls in one hour?"
    - A surprise:  "Our shift starts at 4 AM — and that is the easy part."
    - A short story (2 sentences):  "Last week a client called me at
       11 PM. I want to tell you what I did."

3 POINTS (1 min each = 3 min total)
  POINT 1: ___________________________________
    Example / detail / number:
  POINT 2: ___________________________________
    Example / detail / number:
  POINT 3: ___________________________________
    Example / detail / number:

  Each point: ONE sentence stating the point + 1-2 sentences of
  example/detail/number from your own work. Use:
    - 1 conditional (Cond 0 or 1): "IF the client calls after 6..."
    - 1 modal (should / must / might / can): "You SHOULD..."
    - 1 connector (because / so / although / however): "...BECAUSE..."

CLOSE (30 sec)
  ONE sentence that lands the takeaway.
  Choices:
    - Recap:  "Three rules: listen first, log fast, escalate clean."
    - Call:   "Next time you take a call, try this."
    - Image:  "That is what 4 AM looks like — and why we own it."

Q&A (2 min) — 2 to 3 questions max.
  Be ready to say: "Good question. The short answer is ___."
  If you don't know:  "I don't know — I'd check with the team."

TOTAL: 5 min talk + 2 min Q&A.
```

You read the template aloud once with cohort tracking on their copy.

### (25 min) DRAFT writing in class — silent

Sit. Silent. Each student writes the FULL draft on their paper following the template. Word count target: ~600 words = ~5 min spoken.

You circulate. Coach 1-on-1 in whisper mode — ONE sentence per student max. Common nudges:
- *"Narrow. 'My company' is too wide. What's the ONE thing about your company?"*
- *"Number. Put a number in point 1 — '50 calls' lands better than 'many calls'."*
- *"Audience eyes — your hook is the only thing they hear in second 1. Make it earn the next 4:30."*

At minute 22, you call out: *"Three minutes left. Finish the CLOSE — one sentence. Don't leave the close blank."*

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2:** *"Fair to your idea — does this draft give your topic the time it deserves? Fair to their ears — does it give them the structure they expect?"*

### (5 min) Model demo — you read a model talk

Stand. You read ONE complete model talk out loud — 5 min compressed to ~3 min for time, cohort listening. Use a generic workplace topic (e.g., "How we handle a Monday morning shift change") — never name a student's life. Model the HOOK + 3 POINTS + CLOSE rhythm. End by saying: *"That is the shape. Yours is on your paper. Stand up — partner work."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — PEER SHARE + 2 ADJUST ROUNDS + ORAL RUN

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Pairs work through 3 cycles: peer feedback round 1, adjust, peer feedback round 2, adjust, first oral run.

### (2 min) Setup
> *"Stand. Pair up. You will exchange drafts — read your partner's draft silently, then give 2 minutes of structured feedback using the card I'm handing out. Then adjust your own draft for 4 minutes. Then switch partners. Same again. Then sit and read your draft OUT LOUD once to your new partner, no notes hidden — exactly as you'd say it on the day. The card structures the feedback. Justice day 1: fair feedback — one strength + one specific improvement."*

Hand out printed feedback card (one per student, two sides — round 1 + round 2):

```
PEER FEEDBACK CARD — CL 51 PREP — Round 1

Partner's topic: ____________________________________

HOOK (30 sec):  Did it earn my attention?  YES / NO
  Why or why not (1 sentence): ________________________

3 POINTS:  Were they clear and separate?  YES / NO
  Which point needs sharpening (1 number): ___________
  Suggestion (1 sentence): ___________________________

CLOSE (30 sec):  Did it land a takeaway?  YES / NO
  Suggestion (1 sentence): ___________________________

ONE STRENGTH (1 sentence): _______________________________
ONE SPECIFIC IMPROVEMENT (1 sentence): __________________

Round 2 — repeat with new partner on the back of this card.
```

### (~40 min) 3 cycles

**Cycle 1 — Peer feedback round 1 (10 min):** Pair A reads partner B's draft silently (3 min). Then B reads A's draft silently (3 min). Then 4 min: each gives the other their card feedback out loud. You circulate — fix any unfair feedback ("bad" without specific = not feedback; ask: *"What ONE thing would make it better?"*).

**Cycle 2 — Adjust round 1 (8 min):** Sit. Silent. Each student adjusts their draft based on partner feedback. You circulate — coach in whisper.

**Cycle 3 — Peer feedback round 2 — NEW PARTNER (10 min):** Switch partners. Repeat the read-silently → card feedback pattern (3 min read + 3 min read + 4 min feedback). **Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3:** *"Justice day 1 — one strength + one specific improvement. Fair feedback only."*

**Cycle 4 — Adjust round 2 (5 min):** Sit. Silent. Each student adjusts their draft based on the second partner's feedback. Short cycle — only top 1–2 fixes per student.

**Cycle 5 — First oral run-through — NEW PARTNER (7 min):** New partner. Each student reads their full draft OUT LOUD to the partner, eyes mostly up off the page (notes glanced at, not read word for word). Time it — 5 min is the target. Partner listens, gives ONE final note out loud at the end. Swap. You circulate — coach on PACE (most students rush the first time).

### Cohort close — none (time goes to oral run)

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE — 15 min

### (4 min) Error paper recap — 5 prep-specific errors

Write on the board (anonymous; NAMES only in your personal notebook):

```
ERROR PAPER — CL 51 PREP (5 ERRORS)

1. Topic too wide:  "My company" -> "How we handle complaint calls in our company"  (narrow)
2. Hook flat:       "Today I will talk about ___" -> "How do you handle 50 calls in one hour?"  (earn attention)
3. Points blurred:  "Many things matter, like..." -> "POINT 1: ___. POINT 2: ___. POINT 3: ___."  (separate)
4. No numbers:      "We have many customers" -> "We handle 200 customers a day"  (concrete)
5. Close missing:   blank -> "Three rules: listen first, log fast, escalate clean."  (always close with a sentence)
```

Cohort echoes the 5 corrections in chorus 1×. **Anonymous Error Paper delivered physically with the guide (NOT photographed).** NAMES in personal notebook.

### (3 min) Phrase of the Day close
1. *"Phrase from memory — one student."* Pick one at random.
2. *"In one sentence: what does your topic deserve that you didn't give it yet?"* Pick another at random.
3. *"Justice day 1 opened the block. Four days to go: tomorrow rehearsal, day after that the presentations, the day after the cycle close, and the day after that the Final."*

### (5 min) Homework — explained out loud

```
HOMEWORK CL 51 — DUE BEFORE NEXT CLASS Cl 52, 7:00 PM
TIME: 30 min (this homework is ABOVE A2 standard — it is presentation prep). NOT fragmented.

(1) POLISH DRAFT — 10 min — bring the FINAL written draft on
    paper. Apply both partners' feedback. Target ~600 words =
    5 min spoken. Include:
    - 1 clear HOOK sentence
    - 3 NUMBERED POINTS, 1 example/number per point
    - 1 conditional + 1 modal + 1 connector somewhere in the talk
    - 1 CLOSE sentence
    - 2 Q&A prep notes (likely questions + your short answer)

(2) AUDIO eyes closed — 5 min — Record the FULL 5-minute
    talk eyes closed, no notes, in one take. Time yourself.
    If it's under 4:30 or over 5:30, run it AGAIN until in
    range. Label: A2-51-prep.

(3) LISTENING — short — Watch any 5-minute talk in English
    (TED short, vlog, presentation). Note: HOW did the
    speaker open? HOW did they close? 1 sentence each.
    Bring on paper.

RULE: Not fragmented. Bring the polished draft + the audio
in your phone (you may need to play 30 sec for partner).
```

Homework due before next class, 7 PM."*

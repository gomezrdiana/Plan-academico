### 3. 00:13-00:23 | ROMPEHIELO: "How TIRED are you?" (10 min) — DE PIE

Escribe en tablero:

```
DEGREES OF ADJECTIVES — niveles de intensidad

Order weak → strong:
- A LITTLE  (un poco)        - A LITTLE tired
- PRETTY    (un poco/bastante) - PRETTY tired
- QUITE     (bastante)       - QUITE tired
- VERY      (muy)            - VERY tired
- REALLY    (realmente, muy)  - REALLY tired
- EXTREMELY (extremadamente)  - EXTREMELY tired
- TOTALLY   (totalmente)     - TOTALLY exhausted

QUESTION:
"How + adj + verb?"
- How tall are you?
- How tired do you feel?
- How busy is your week?

ANSWER with degree:
- Pretty tall. / Very tired. / Extremely busy.
```

Round (8 min): "STAND UP." Señala estudiantes. Pregunta + adjetivo:
- "How tired are you today?" → I'm pretty tired. / very tired.
- "How busy is your week?" → Quite busy.
- "How tall is your dad?" → Pretty tall.
- "How important is this?" → Very important.

Si dicen mal: "No. Listen." + correcto + 2x.

### 4. 00:23-00:38 | LIBRO M29 (15 min) — SENTADOS

"Open Module 29 — How Tall Are You? Read with partner. 5 min."

5 min leen. 10 min te explican (tú NO):
1. Lista 5 degrees del weak al strong. → a little, pretty, quite, very, extremely.
2. ¿Cómo se hace pregunta de degree? → "How + adj + verb...?"
3. ¿Cómo se responde? → con uno de los degrees.
4. ¿Diferencia entre VERY y EXTREMELY? → very = mucho; extremely = muchísimo (más fuerte).
5. ¿REALLY = VERY? → Sí, son sinónimos.

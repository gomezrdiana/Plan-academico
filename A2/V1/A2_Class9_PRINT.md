### ☐ 00:13–00:28 | LIBRO: MÓDULO 11 — SECOND CONDITIONAL (15 min)

**⚠️ MÁXIMO 15 MINUTOS. 5 de lectura + 10 de explicación. Si no alcanza, corta. NO te pases.**

Di: "Abran su libro A2 en el Módulo 11 — 'I'd Eat a Cookie.' Lean la Guía con su compañero. Tienen 5 minutos."

**Primeros 5 min:** Leen en parejas. Camina. A los 5 minutos EXACTOS: Di: "Tiempo. Dejen de leer. Pongan atención."

**Siguientes 10 min:** Siéntate con cada pareja. ELLOS te explican:

1. ¿Qué es el second conditional? →
   - Habla de situaciones IMAGINARIAS, IMPROBABLES o IMPOSIBLES.
   - NO es real. NO es probable. Es FANTASÍA o HIPOTÉTICO.
   - "If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world." (No tengo un millón — es imaginario.)
2. ¿Cuál es la estructura? →
   - IF + past simple, WOULD + verbo base.
   - "If I had money, I would buy a car."
   - "If she lived in Paris, she would speak French."
   - "If we had more time, we would study more."
3. ¿Qué significa "would"? →
   - "Would" = "-ía" en español. Condicional.
   - "I would buy" = "compraría"
   - "She would go" = "iría"
   - "We would eat" = "comeríamos"
   - Contracción: "I'd" = "I would." "She'd" = "She would." "We'd" = "We would."
4. ¿Cómo se hace negativo? →
   - "Would not" = "wouldn't."
   - "If I had money, I wouldn't waste it."
   - "If she lived here, she wouldn't need a car."
   - En la cláusula "if": "If I didn't have money, I would stay home."
5. ¿Cuál es la diferencia con el first conditional? →
   - FIRST: probable/posible. "If it rains (maybe it will), I will stay home."
   - SECOND: improbable/imaginario. "If I were president (I'm not), I would change the laws."
   - FIRST: if + present, will + verb.
   - SECOND: if + past, would + verb.

Ahora dales situaciones. Di cada una y ellos construyen la frase:

**Situación 1:** Di: "Si tuviera dinero, compraría un carro." (No tengo dinero — imaginario)
→ "If I had money, I would buy a car."

**Situación 2:** Di: "Si ella viviera en Londres, hablaría inglés todos los días." (No vive en Londres)
→ "If she lived in London, she would speak English every day."

**Situación 3:** Di: "Si yo fuera presidente, cambiaría las leyes." (No soy presidente)
→ "If I were president, I would change the laws."

**Situación 4:** Di: "Si no lloviera, iríamos al parque." (Está lloviendo)
→ "If it didn't rain, we would go to the park."

**Situación 5:** Di: "Si tuviéramos más tiempo, no estaríamos estresados."
→ "If we had more time, we wouldn't be stressed."

**Situación 6:** Di: "¿Qué harías si ganaras la lotería?"
→ "What would you do if you won the lottery?"

Si está mal: "No. Listen." + versión correcta + repiten 2 veces.

**Escribe en el tablero:**
```
SECOND CONDITIONAL = IMAGINARY / IMPOSSIBLE

IF + PAST SIMPLE, WOULD + VERB

If I had money, I would buy a car.
If she lived in London, she would speak English.
If I were president, I would change the laws.
If we had more time, we would study more.

"WOULD" = -ÍA:
would buy = compraría
would go = iría
would eat = comería
I'd = I would / She'd = She would

NEGATIVE:
wouldn't = would not
If I had money, I wouldn't waste it.
If I didn't have a car, I would walk.

QUESTIONS:
What would you do if you won the lottery?
Would you travel if you had money?

⚠️ IF + PAST (NOT "would"):
"If I had" ✓ NOT "If I would have" ✗

FIRST vs SECOND:
If it rains, I WILL stay home. (probable)
If it rained, I WOULD stay home. (imaginary)
```

Di cada frase. La clase repite 2 veces.

### ☐ 00:38–00:58 | SIMULACIÓN: ¿QUÉ HARÍAS SI GANARAS LA LOTERÍA? (20 min)

**Qué es:** Los estudiantes imaginan que ganan la lotería y planifican su vida imaginaria con second conditional. Luego expanden a otras situaciones hipotéticas.

**Paso 1 — Tablero (3 min).** Escribe en el tablero ANTES de empezar (o prepáralo durante la actividad anterior):

```
🎰 WHAT WOULD YOU DO IF...?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
IMAGINARY SITUATIONS:

1. ...you won the lottery?
2. ...you could live anywhere in the world?
```

- "Would you travel if you had a million dollars?"
- "If you were invisible, what would you do first?"

Respuestas que deben usar:
- "If I won the lottery, I would buy a house."
- "I would travel the world if I had money."
- "I wouldn't work if I didn't have to."
- "If I were president, I'd change the education system."

Camina con papel de errores. Errores comunes:
- "If I would have money" → debe ser "If I HAD money" (past simple después de "if," NO "would")

### ☐ 01:08–01:18 | HISTORIA INTERACTIVA: LA VIDA PARALELA DE SANTIAGO (10 min)

**Qué es:** Cuentas una historia sobre Santiago, que imagina cómo sería su vida si todo fuera diferente. Toda la historia es en second conditional. Los estudiantes repiten, responden preguntas y participan.

Di: "Les voy a contar la historia de Santiago. Santiago tiene una vida normal. Pero SIEMPRE se imagina cómo sería si todo fuera diferente."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO. Pausa para que repitan y participen.**

"Santiago is a student. He studies every day. But he always thinks: 'If I were rich, I wouldn't study. I would travel the world.'"

Para. Di: "Say it: 'If I were rich, I wouldn't study. I would travel the world.'" Repiten.

"Santiago takes the bus to school every morning. The bus is always crowded. He thinks: 'If I had a car, I would drive to school. I wouldn't take this horrible bus.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'If I had a car, I would drive to school.' Does Santiago have a car?" Clase: "No!" Di: "It's imaginary!"

"In English class, Santiago struggles with pronunciation. He thinks: 'If I lived in the United States, I would speak English perfectly. If I had an American friend, I would practice every day.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'If I lived in the United States, I would speak English perfectly.' Is this possible right now?" Clase: "No!" Di: "That's why it's second conditional — imaginary!"

"At lunch, Santiago eats a sandwich. He looks at his friend's pizza. He thinks: 'If I had more money, I would eat pizza every day. I wouldn't eat sandwiches anymore.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'If I had more money, I would eat pizza every day. I wouldn't eat sandwiches.' What does Santiago want?" Clase: "Pizza!"

"After school, Santiago walks home. It starts raining. He thinks: 'If I had an umbrella, I wouldn't get wet. If I lived closer to school, I would already be home.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'If I had an umbrella, I wouldn't get wet.' 'If I lived closer, I would already be home.'" Repiten ambas.

"Santiago gets home. His mom is cooking. She asks: 'How was school?' Santiago says: 'Mom, if I were president, I would give every student a car, free pizza, and no homework.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'If I were president, I would give every student a car, free pizza, and no homework.'" Repiten. La clase se ríe.

"His mom laughs: 'Santiago, if you studied more, you would get better grades. If you got better grades, you would get a scholarship. If you got a scholarship, you would go to a great university. And if you went to a great university, you would get a great job. And THEN you could buy your own car and pizza.'"

Para. Di: "Wow! That's a chain! Say: 'If I studied more, I would get better grades.' 'If I got better grades, I would get a scholarship.'" Repiten las frases una por una.

"Santiago thinks for a moment. Then he says: 'OK, Mom. But if I could choose, I would STILL want to be president.'"

Para. La clase se ríe. Di: "What would YOU do if you were Santiago? Would you study or would you be president?" Estudiantes responden.

Ahora señala una pareja: Di: "Santiago is thinking about his weekend. What imaginary things does he think about? Use 'If I had... I would...' or 'If I were... I would...'" La pareja inventa. Pasa a 3-4 parejas más.

Ejemplos que podrían dar:
- "If I had a girlfriend, I would take her to the movies."
- "If I were taller, I would play basketball."
- "If I didn't have homework, I would play video games all day."
- "If I lived on the beach, I would swim every morning."

Di: "¿Santiago va a dejar de imaginar algún día?" La clase vota.

> La historia usa second conditional en CADA situación. If + past, would + verb. Wouldn't para negativo. "If I were" para ser/estar. La cadena de la mamá refuerza la estructura en cascada.

### ☐ 01:18–01:28 | COMPETENCIA: FIRST O SECOND? (10 min)

**Qué es:** 2 equipos. El profesor da una situación. Los equipos deciden si es first o second conditional y construyen la frase.

Divide en 2 equipos de 8. De pie.

Di: "Voy a dar situaciones. Ustedes deciden: ¿es PROBABLE (first conditional) o IMAGINARIO (second conditional)? Luego forman la frase. El primer equipo correcto gana 2 puntos — 1 por elegir bien y 1 por la frase correcta."

**Las 12 situaciones (una a la vez):**

1. "Maybe it will rain tomorrow. If it rains..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If it rains, I will stay home."

2. "You are NOT a millionaire. If you were a millionaire..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I were a millionaire, I would buy a yacht."

3. "Maybe your friend will call tonight. If your friend calls..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If my friend calls, I will talk to her."

4. "You DON'T live in Japan. If you lived in Japan..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I lived in Japan, I would eat sushi every day."

5. "Maybe you will have time this weekend. If you have time..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If I have time, I will go to the gym."

6. "You are NOT invisible. If you were invisible..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I were invisible, I would go to the movies for free."

7. "Maybe the teacher will give homework. If the teacher gives homework..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If the teacher gives homework, I will do it tonight."

8. "You DON'T have a superpower. If you had a superpower..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I had a superpower, I would fly everywhere."

9. "Maybe you will get a raise. If you get a raise..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If I get a raise, I will save the money."

10. "You are NOT the president. If you were the president..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I were the president, I would improve education."

11. "Maybe it will be sunny tomorrow. If it's sunny..." (PROBABLE → first)
→ "If it's sunny, I will go to the park."

12. "You DON'T speak 10 languages. If you spoke 10 languages..." (IMAGINARIO → second)
→ "If I spoke 10 languages, I would travel everywhere."

2 puntos por respuesta completa correcta. Si confunden first y second: "¿Es probable o imaginario? Piénsenlo." Dales 5 segundos. Si no: "No. Listen." + versión correcta. Ambos equipos repiten.

Errores que buscar:
- Usar "will" en second conditional → "No. Listen. If I HAD, I WOULD." (imaginario = would)
- Usar "would" en first conditional → "No. Listen. If it RAINS, I WILL." (probable = will)
- "If I would have" → "No. Listen. If I HAD." (no "would" después de "if")

Equipo ganador celebra. Puntajes en el tablero.

> De pie. Rápido. Ruidoso. Distinguir first y second es la habilidad clave de esta clase.

### ☐ 00:13–00:28 | LIBRO: MÓDULO 15 — PRESENT PERFECT (REGULAR VERBS) (15 min)

**⚠️ MÁXIMO 15 MINUTOS. 5 de lectura + 10 de explicación. Si no alcanza, corta. NO te pases.**

Di: "Abran su libro A2 en el Módulo 15 — 'I Have Visited London.' Lean la Guía con su compañero. Tienen 5 minutos."

**Primeros 5 min:** Leen en parejas. Camina. A los 5 minutos EXACTOS: Di: "Tiempo. Dejen de leer. Pongan atención."

**Siguientes 10 min:** Siéntate con cada pareja. ELLOS te explican:

1. ¿Qué es el present perfect? →
   - Se usa para acciones realizadas en un período INDEFINIDO o acciones NO TERMINADAS.
   - A pesar de su nombre, es un tiempo PASADO.
   - "I have played soccer." = "He jugado fútbol." (No dice cuándo — indefinido.)
   - Es diferente del past simple: "I played soccer yesterday." (Dice cuándo — definido.)
2. ¿Cómo se forma? →
   - HAVE/HAS + participio pasado del verbo.
   - Participio regular = verbo + "-ed" (igual que el past simple regular).
   - "I have played." "She has studied." "We have visited."
3. ¿Cuándo se usa "have" y cuándo "has"? →
   - HAS = he, she, it. "She HAS studied." "He HAS arrived."
   - HAVE = I, you, we, they. "I HAVE played." "They HAVE visited."
4. ¿Cómo se hace negativo? →
   - have not = haven't. has not = hasn't.
   - "I haven't talked to Miguel." "She hasn't needed new shoes."
5. ¿Cómo se hacen preguntas? →
   - Have/Has + sujeto + participio.
   - "Have you studied?" "Has she arrived?"
   - Respuesta corta: "Yes, I have." / "No, I haven't." "Yes, she has." / "No, she hasn't."
6. ¿Cuál es la contracción? →
   - I've, you've, we've, they've, he's, she's, it's.
   - "I've played." "She's studied." "We've visited."

Ahora dales situaciones. Di cada una y ellos construyen la frase:

**Situación 1:** Di: "Yo he jugado fútbol." (afirmativa)
→ "I have played soccer." / "I've played soccer."

**Situación 2:** Di: "Ella ha estudiado." (afirmativa con has)
→ "She has studied." / "She's studied."

**Situación 3:** Di: "No he hablado con Miguel." (negativa)
→ "I haven't talked to Miguel."

**Situación 4:** Di: "¿Has visitado Londres?" (pregunta)
→ "Have you visited London?"

**Situación 5:** Di: "Ellos no han jugado fútbol." (negativa)
→ "They haven't played soccer."

**Situación 6:** Di: "¿Ha llegado Cristian?" (pregunta con has)
→ "Has Cristian arrived?"

Si está mal: "No. Listen." + versión correcta + repiten 2 veces.

**Escribe en el tablero:**
```
PRESENT PERFECT = HAVE/HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE (-ed)

AFFIRMATIVE:
I have played = I've played (he jugado)
She has studied = She's studied (ha estudiado)
We have visited = We've visited (hemos visitado)

NEGATIVE:
I have not = I haven't played
She has not = She hasn't studied

QUESTIONS:
Have you played? — Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
Has she studied? — Yes, she has. / No, she hasn't.

HAS = he, she, it
HAVE = I, you, we, they

PAST SIMPLE vs PRESENT PERFECT:
I played soccer YESTERDAY. (specific time = past simple)
I have played soccer. (no specific time = present perfect)

⚠️ PRESENT PERFECT = NO specific time
⚠️ Regular participle = same as past simple (-ed)
```

Di cada frase. La clase repite 2 veces.

### ☐ 00:38–00:58 | SIMULACIÓN: ¿QUÉ HAS HECHO EN TU VIDA? (20 min)

**Qué es:** Los estudiantes hablan sobre sus experiencias de vida usando present perfect. Se enfocan en cosas que han hecho (o no han hecho) sin mencionar cuándo.

**Paso 1 — Tablero (3 min).** Escribe en el tablero ANTES de empezar:

```
🌍 WHAT HAVE YOU DONE IN YOUR LIFE?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
EXPERIENCES:

1. Have you visited another country?
2. Have you studied another language (besides English)?
3. Have you cooked for your family?
4. Have you worked in a restaurant?
5. Have you played a musical instrument?
6. Have you traveled alone?
7. Have you helped a stranger?
8. Have you watched a movie more than 3 times?

ANSWER WITH:
"Yes, I have. I've ___."
"No, I haven't. I haven't ___ yet."
"She/He has ___."
```

**Paso 2 — Modelo (2 min).** Di: "Yo primero. Escuchen."

Di: "Have I visited another country? Yes, I have. I've visited two countries."
Di: "Have I played a musical instrument? No, I haven't. I haven't played any instrument."

**Paso 3 — Parejas (10 min).** Di: "Parejas. Pregunten y respondan TODAS las 8 preguntas del tablero. RESPUESTAS COMPLETAS — no solo 'yes' o 'no.' Agreguen un detalle."

Preguntas que deben practicar:
- "Have you visited another country?"
- "Have you worked in a restaurant?"
- "Have you traveled alone?"

Respuestas que deben usar:
- "Yes, I have. I've visited Ecuador."
- "No, I haven't. I haven't worked in a restaurant."
- "Yes, she has. She's traveled to three countries."

A los 5 minutos: Di: "Switch. Ahora pregunten a una NUEVA pareja. Compartan lo que su primer compañero les dijo: 'He has visited...' 'She hasn't played...'"

Camina con papel de errores. Errores comunes:
- "I have visit" → "I have VISITED" (participio = -ed)
- "She have studied" → "She HAS studied" (he/she/it = HAS)
- "Have you visited yesterday?" → No — present perfect NO lleva tiempo específico. "Have you visited London?" o "Did you visit London yesterday?"

Si escuchas un error: "No. Listen." + versión correcta. Repiten 2 veces.

**Paso 4 — Presentar (5 min).** Di: "4 parejas van a compartir. Cada persona dice 1 cosa que su compañero HA hecho y 1 cosa que NO ha hecho."

### ☐ 01:08–01:18 | HISTORIA INTERACTIVA: LA LISTA DE DANIELA (10 min)

**Qué es:** Cuentas una historia sobre Daniela, que tiene una lista de cosas que quiere hacer en la vida. Revisa cuáles ha hecho y cuáles no. Toda la historia usa present perfect.

Di: "Les voy a contar la historia de Daniela. Daniela tiene una lista de 10 cosas que quiere hacer en su vida. Vamos a ver cuáles ha hecho."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO. Pausa para que repitan y participen.**

"Daniela is 25 years old. She has a list of things she wants to do. Number 1: Visit Paris. Has Daniela visited Paris? YES! She has visited Paris. She visited it last year."

Para. Di: "Say it: 'She has visited Paris.' Has SHE visited? Yes, she HAS." Repiten.

"Number 2: Learn to cook Italian food. Has she learned? YES! She has learned to cook pasta and pizza. Her family has loved her cooking."

Para. Di: "Say: 'She has learned to cook Italian food.' 'Her family has loved her cooking.'" Repiten.

"Number 3: Study a third language. Has she studied one? NO. She hasn't studied a third language yet. She has only studied English and Spanish."

Para. Di: "Say: 'She hasn't studied a third language.' The word 'yet' means 'todavía no.' She hasn't done it YET." Repiten.

"Number 4: Help in a community project. Has she helped? YES! She has helped in two projects. She has volunteered at a school and a hospital."

Para. Di: "Say: 'She has helped in two projects.' 'She has volunteered.' What has she done?" Clase: "She has volunteered!"

"Number 5: Watch 100 movies in English. Has she watched 100? NO! She has watched 67 movies. She hasn't watched 100 yet, but she's close!"

Para. Di: "Say: 'She has watched 67 movies.' 'She hasn't watched 100 yet.' How many has she watched?" Clase: "67!"

"Number 6: Travel alone. Has she traveled alone? YES! She has traveled alone to Ecuador. She has loved every minute."

Para. Di: "Say: 'She has traveled alone.' Have YOU traveled alone?" 2-3 estudiantes responden: "Yes, I have" o "No, I haven't."

"Daniela looks at her list and smiles. She has completed 4 out of 6 things. She hasn't finished everything, but she has started. And that's what matters."

Para. Di: "How many things has she completed?" Clase: "Four!" Di: "How many hasn't she completed?" Clase: "Two!"

Señala 3-4 parejas: "What has Daniela done? What hasn't she done? Tell me using 'She has...' or 'She hasn't...'" La pareja produce frases.

Di: "Now YOU — what have YOU done from this kind of list? Tell your partner 2 things you HAVE done and 1 you HAVEN'T done." Parejas practican 1 minuto.

> La historia usa present perfect en afirmativo, negativo e interrogativo. Have/has + participio (-ed). "Yet" aparece naturalmente para reforzar el negativo.

### ☐ 01:18–01:28 | COMPETENCIA: ¿PAST SIMPLE O PRESENT PERFECT? (10 min)

**Qué es:** 2 equipos. El profesor da una frase en español. Los equipos deciden si es past simple (tiempo específico) o present perfect (sin tiempo específico) y construyen la frase.

Divide en 2 equipos de 8. De pie.

Di: "Voy a dar frases en español. Ustedes deciden: ¿hay un tiempo ESPECÍFICO (yesterday, last week, at 3:00) → PAST SIMPLE? ¿O NO hay tiempo específico → PRESENT PERFECT? Luego construyen la frase. 2 puntos por respuesta completa correcta."

**Las 12 frases (una a la vez):**

1. "Jugué fútbol ayer."
→ PAST SIMPLE (yesterday) → "I played soccer yesterday."

2. "He jugado fútbol." (sin tiempo)
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "I have played soccer." / "I've played soccer."

3. "Ella estudió anoche."
→ PAST SIMPLE (last night) → "She studied last night."

4. "Ella ha estudiado."
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "She has studied." / "She's studied."

5. "Visitamos a mis padres el mes pasado."
→ PAST SIMPLE (last month) → "We visited my parents last month."

6. "Hemos visitado a mis padres."
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "We have visited my parents." / "We've visited my parents."

7. "¿Estudiaste la semana pasada?"
→ PAST SIMPLE → "Did you study last week?"

8. "¿Has estudiado?"
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "Have you studied?"

9. "No hablé con Miguel ayer."
→ PAST SIMPLE → "I didn't talk to Miguel yesterday."

10. "No he hablado con Miguel."
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "I haven't talked to Miguel."

11. "¿Llegó Cristian a las 7:00?"
→ PAST SIMPLE (at 7:00) → "Did Cristian arrive at 7:00?"

12. "¿Ha llegado Cristian?"
→ PRESENT PERFECT → "Has Cristian arrived?"

2 puntos por respuesta correcta. Si confunden: "¿Hay un tiempo específico? Yesterday, last week, at 3:00 → PAST SIMPLE. ¿No hay tiempo? → PRESENT PERFECT."

Errores que buscar:
- "I have played yesterday" → "I PLAYED yesterday" (tiempo específico = past simple)
- "I played soccer" (cuando quiere decir "he jugado") → "I HAVE played soccer" (sin tiempo = present perfect)
- "She have studied" → "She HAS studied" (he/she/it = has)

Equipo ganador celebra. Puntajes en el tablero.

> De pie. Rápido. Ruidoso. Distinguir past simple y present perfect es la habilidad clave de esta clase.

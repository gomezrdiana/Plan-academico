## 6. SYSTEMATIC A — QUESTIONS (aux + S + V in 5 tenses) — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the question errors that block B1: the structure AUXILIARY + SUBJECT + VERB in 5 tenses (present simple / past simple / present continuous / present perfect / future). Never 'Where you go?' Never 'What you doing?'"*

### Concept (11 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
A) QUESTIONS   [review/fix]

STRUCTURE:   AUXILIARY + SUBJECT + VERB (+ rest)
             (yes/no = aux first; wh- = wh-word + aux + S + V)

PRESENT SIMPLE  -> DO / DOES + S + base
  "DO you work here?"
  "DOES she speak English?"
  "WHERE do you live?"

PAST SIMPLE  -> DID + S + base
  "DID they call?"
  "WHERE did you go?"
  "WHY didn't he come?"

PRESENT CONTINUOUS  -> AM / IS / ARE + S + -ing
  "ARE you working?"
  "WHAT is she doing?"
  "WHO is calling?"

PRESENT PERFECT  -> HAVE / HAS + S + past participle
  "HAVE you finished?"
  "HAS he arrived?"
  "HOW LONG have you worked here?"

FUTURE (will)  -> WILL + S + base
  "WILL you help me?"
  "WHEN will it start?"
  "WHO will lead the meeting?"

RULE: aux comes FIRST in yes/no questions.
RULE: with wh- words, order = wh + aux + S + V (NEVER wh + S + aux).
```

**Canonical typical errors (from previous A2 guide banks, NO names):**

- ❌ *"Where you go?"* → ✅ *"Where DO you go? / Where DID you go?"* (missing auxiliary)
- ❌ *"What you doing?"* → ✅ *"What ARE you doing?"* (missing is/are)
- ❌ *"Why she didn't come?"* → ✅ *"Why DIDN'T SHE come?"* (order: aux + S, not S + aux)
- ❌ *"You have finished?"* → ✅ *"HAVE you finished?"* (aux first in yes/no)
- ❌ *"When it will start?"* → ✅ *"When WILL IT start?"* (aux before subject in wh-)

**Insertion #1 of Phrase of the Day:** *"As today's phrase says — ask before you speak. Auxiliary first, subject next, verb last. That's how a real question sounds."*

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a wh-word + verb + tense (*"where / live / present simple"* → *"Where DO you live?"*; *"what / do / past simple"* → *"What DID you do?"*; *"why / not come / past simple"* → *"Why DIDN'T he come?"*). Cohort answers in chorus. 8 items mixing the 5 tenses.

## 7. SYSTEMATIC B — NEGATIVES + AUXILIARIES — 14 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — we CORRECT the negative errors that block B1: AUXILIARY + NOT + base verb. Never 'I no go.' Never 'She didn't went.' Never 'He hasn't came.'"*

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
B) NEGATIVES + AUXILIARIES   [review/fix]

STRUCTURE:  AUXILIARY + NOT + verb base
            (except -ing after be / past participle after have)

PRESENT SIMPLE  -> DON'T / DOESN'T + base
  "I DON'T work here."
  "She DOESN'T speak English."
  NEVER "I no go." / "She no speak."

PAST SIMPLE  -> DIDN'T + base
  "They DIDN'T call."
  "He DIDN'T come."
  NEVER "She didn't went." / "They didn't called."

PRESENT CONTINUOUS  -> ISN'T / AREN'T + -ing
  "I'M NOT working."
  "They AREN'T coming."
  NEVER "They not are working."

PRESENT PERFECT  -> HAVEN'T / HASN'T + past participle
  "I HAVEN'T finished."
  "He HASN'T arrived."
  NEVER "He hasn't came." / "I haven't went."

FUTURE  -> WON'T + base
  "I WON'T help."
  "It WON'T start."
  NEVER "I will no help." / "It will don't start."

RULE: negative = auxiliary + NOT + base (no loose "no").
RULE: after didn't / doesn't / don't / won't = BASE verb only.
RULE: after haven't / hasn't = PAST PARTICIPLE (come, gone, done).
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"I no go."* → ✅ *"I DON'T go."* (auxiliary + not, no loose "no")
- ❌ *"She didn't went."* → ✅ *"She DIDN'T GO."* (after didn't = base, NOT past)
- ❌ *"I no like."* → ✅ *"I DON'T LIKE."* (don't, not "no")
- ❌ *"He hasn't came."* → ✅ *"He HASN'T COME."* (past participle, not past simple)
- ❌ *"They not are working."* → ✅ *"They AREN'T working." / "They are NOT working."* (not after be)

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"As today's phrase says — put the words in the right place. NOT goes after the auxiliary, never alone. Don't. Didn't. Haven't. Won't."*

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a subject + verb + tense in affirmative (*"I / work / present simple"* → deny: *"I DON'T work."*; *"she / go / past simple"* → *"She DIDN'T GO."*; *"he / finish / present perfect"* → *"He HASN'T FINISHED."*; *"they / call / future"* → *"They WON'T CALL."*). Cohort answers in chorus. 8 items mixing the 5 tenses.

## 8. SYSTEMATIC C — WORD ORDER — 14 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — we CORRECT the word order errors that block B1: SVO + frequency adverbs in the right slot + objects in the right order. Never 'I drink usually coffee.' Never 'Always I study.' Never 'I am tired usually.'"*

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
C) WORD ORDER   [review/fix]

BASIC ORDER:  SUBJECT + VERB + OBJECT  (SVO)
              Never split V and O with an adverb.

FREQUENCY ADVERBS  (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never)

  -> BEFORE the main verb:
       "I USUALLY drink coffee."
       "She ALWAYS studies."
       "They NEVER call."

  -> AFTER "to be":
       "I AM usually tired."
       "She IS always late."
       "They ARE never on time."

  NEVER between V and O:
       "I drink usually coffee." X
  NEVER at the very start (in standard speech):
       "Always I study." X
  NEVER at the end (with be):
       "I am tired usually." X

OBJECTS  (direct + indirect)
  -> indirect first, no preposition:
       "I gave HIM the report."
  -> direct first, with TO + indirect:
       "I gave the report TO HIM."

ADVERB OF MANNER  (well, badly, quickly, slowly)
  -> end of the clause:
       "He SPEAKS English WELL."
       NEVER "He speaks well English." X

TIME EXPRESSIONS  (every day, yesterday, last week)
  -> end of the clause (or start, with comma):
       "She works EVERY DAY."
       "EVERY DAY, she works."
       NEVER "She every day works." X

RULE: SVO, no splitting.
RULE: frequency = BEFORE main verb, AFTER to be.
RULE: manner / time = end of clause.
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"I drink usually coffee."* → ✅ *"I USUALLY drink coffee."* (adverb BEFORE the verb)
- ❌ *"She every day works."* → ✅ *"She works EVERY DAY." / "EVERY DAY, she works."* (time expression at end or start)
- ❌ *"Always I study at night."* → ✅ *"I ALWAYS study at night."* (frequency adverb INSIDE)
- ❌ *"I am tired usually."* → ✅ *"I AM usually tired."* (after be, not at end)
- ❌ *"He speaks well English."* → ✅ *"He SPEAKS English WELL."* (manner at end, not between V and O)

**Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Put the words in the right place. Frequency before the verb but after to be. Manner at the end. SVO — never split the verb from its object."*

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a set of scrambled words + adverb type (*"coffee / drink / usually / I"* → *"I USUALLY drink coffee."*; *"tired / am / usually / I"* → *"I AM usually tired."*; *"English / speaks / well / he"* → *"He speaks English WELL."*; *"every day / works / she"* → *"She works EVERY DAY."*). Cohort answers in chorus. 8 items mixing frequency, manner, time.

## 10. SIMULATION — "THE JOB INTERVIEW" — 17 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. One of you is the INTERVIEWER — you ask correctly-formed questions in at least 4 of the 5 tenses ('DO you work in customer service now?' / 'WHERE DID you study?' / 'WHAT ARE you doing in your current role?' / 'HAVE you ever managed a team?' / 'WILL you be available to start in July?'). The other is the CANDIDATE — you answer with correct negatives or affirmatives ('No, I DON'T.' / 'Yes, I HAVE.' / 'I HAVEN'T FINISHED yet but I WILL by Friday.') AND with adverbs in the right place ('I USUALLY start my day at 7.' / 'I AM always punctual.' / 'I rarely miss deadlines.'). Then you rotate."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — the 3 forced forms + 2 cases:

```
THE JOB INTERVIEW FORCES (3 sub-clusters):

A) Questions    -> "DO you work in customer service?"            (present)
                   "WHERE DID you study?"                         (past)
                   "WHAT ARE you doing in your current role?"     (continuous)
                   "HAVE you ever managed a team?"                (pres. perf.)
                   "WHEN WILL you be available to start?"         (future)

B) Negatives    -> "No, I DON'T."
                   "I DIDN'T finish the certificate, but I'm
                    finishing it now."
                   "I HAVEN'T managed a team yet."
                   "I WON'T be available until July 15."

C) Word order   -> "I USUALLY arrive at 7."        (freq. before V)
                   "I AM always punctual."          (freq. after be)
                   "I speak English WELL."          (manner at end)
                   "I work in the office EVERY DAY."(time at end)

CASE 1 — Customer service role     CASE 2 — Office / administrative role
- ask about hours, experience,     - ask about software experience,
  team management, availability     teamwork, attention to detail,
                                    deadlines
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"Interviewer: 'Good afternoon. Thank you for coming. So — DO you currently work in customer service? Yes? Great. WHERE DID you study? OK. WHAT ARE you doing in your current role exactly? Mm. HAVE you ever managed a team of five or more? And WHEN WILL you be available to start with us?' / Candidate: 'Yes, I DO. I studied at the technical institute downtown. In my current role I USUALLY handle 30 calls a day and I AM always on time for my shift. I HAVEN'T managed a team of five yet, but I DID supervise two trainees last quarter. I WON'T be available until July 15 — I have to give notice — but I CAN start on the 16th.'"*

**Insertion #4 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good interview sounds like today's phrase — ask before you speak (auxiliary first), put the words in the right place (usually before the verb, always after to be). That's how a real conversation flows."*

### Round 1 (6 min) — Pairs

### Round 2 (3 min) — New scenario

Switch cases (customer service → office, or vice versa). New interview, same 3 sub-clusters forced.

### Close (2 min)

Cohort counts how many of the 3 forms appeared correctly (≥3 well-formed questions in at least 2 tenses + ≥2 negatives with correct auxiliary + ≥1 well-placed frequency adverb).

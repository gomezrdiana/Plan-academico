## CONTEXTO DE HOY

**Módulo 23: PHRASAL VERBS NO SEPARABLES** — verbo + preposición que NO se pueden separar:
```
ASK FOR (pedir)        LOOK FOR (buscar)         WAIT FOR (esperar)
LOOK AT (mirar)        LOOK AFTER (cuidar)       LISTEN TO (escuchar)
TALK TO (hablar con)   THINK ABOUT (pensar en)   AGREE WITH (estar de acuerdo)
DEPEND ON (depender)
```

**Contraste con M24 (Separables — viene mañana):**
- M23: ask for, look for → NO separables ("I'm looking FOR my keys" — no "I'm looking my keys for")
- M24: turn on, pick up → SÍ separables (mañana)

**VIRTUD HOY:** PRUDENCIA v2 — DÍA 1 (planear bien lo que se pide).

## 3. LIBRO: MÓDULO 23 — PHRASAL VERBS NO SEPARABLES (00:13–00:28, 15 min)

**CONCEPTO CLAVE:**
```
PHRASAL VERBS NON-SEPARABLE
verb + preposition stays TOGETHER, object goes AFTER

10 most common:
ASK FOR        LOOK FOR        WAIT FOR        LOOK AT
LOOK AFTER     LISTEN TO       TALK TO         THINK ABOUT
AGREE WITH     DEPEND ON

WITH PRONOUN: stays together, pronoun goes AFTER
- "I'm looking FOR IT."  ✓
- "I'm looking IT for."  ✗ (mal — no se separa)

CONTRAST WITH M24 (mañana = SEPARABLES):
- M23: I'm waiting FOR the bus / I'm waiting FOR it.    (NO separable)
- M24 (mañana): Pick UP the kids / Pick THEM up.        (SÍ separable)
```

**Setup:** "Open Module 23. Read with partner. 5 min." Leen.

**9 min:** Te explican (tú NO):
1. ¿Qué es un phrasal verb? → Verbo + prep que juntos significan algo nuevo.
2. ¿Qué significa "no separable"? → El objeto va DESPUÉS del phrasal verb.
3. Lista 5 phrasal verbs.
4. ¿Por qué importan? → Son los verbos más usados en inglés conversacional.

## 7. HISTORIA INTERACTIVA ABSURDA — "PEDRO Y LA SANDÍA" (01:08–01:20, 12 min)

**Setup (1 min):** "Sit. Story. Pause + repeat. ENGLISH ONLY."

> "Yesterday, my neighbor Pedro was LOOKING FOR his keys."

Para. Repiten.

> "He looked everywhere. Finally, he found them inside a watermelon."

Para. Repiten. Pregunta: "Why was the watermelon there?" Espera reacciones absurdas.

> "Pedro TALKED TO the watermelon for 10 minutes."

Para. "Say: 'Pedro TALKED TO the watermelon.'" Repiten.

> "He ASKED FOR an explanation. The watermelon didn't answer."

Para. Repiten.

> "So Pedro DEPENDED ON his cat to understand. The cat just stared."

Para. Repiten.

> "Then Pedro LOOKED AT the watermelon one more time. He AGREED WITH the cat: this watermelon is suspicious."

Para. Repiten.

> "Now Pedro is THINKING ABOUT moving to a new house with no fruit."

Para. Repiten.

**Estudiantes AGREGAN (5 min):** Cada estudiante 1 frase con phrasal verb sobre Pedro/sandía/gato/casa.

**Cierre (1 min):** Aplauso para Pedro.

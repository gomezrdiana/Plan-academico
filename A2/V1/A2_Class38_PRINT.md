## 6. BOOK M41 — -ING VS -ED — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Open Module 41, page 326. This Is Interesting! Today we stop asking who owns the noun and start using TWO adjectives per emotion verb: -ING for the thing or person that creates the effect, -ED for the person who feels it. Spanish: 'ser' vs 'estar'. English: -ING vs -ED. Plus one irregular: 'We are LOST.'"*

### Structures (5 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
M41 STRUCTURES (Book p.328)

A) -ING = produces the effect      (Spanish "ser ___")
   "The teacher is BORING."     →  "El profesor es aburrido."
   "He is AMAZING."             →  "Él es asombroso."
   "The class is INTERESTING."  →  "La clase es interesante."

B) -ED  = feels the effect         (Spanish "estar ___")
   "The student is BORED."      →  "El alumno está aburrido."
   "She is AMAZED."             →  "Ella está asombrada."
   "I am INTERESTED in the class."→ "Estoy interesado en la clase."

C) IRREGULAR past participles (p.328):
   To LOSE → Lost → Have lost
   "We are LOST."               →  "Estamos perdidos."

D) THREE FORMS — affirmative, negative, interrogative
   AFF:  "I am tired."          /  "The movie is boring."
   NEG:  "I am not tired."      /  "The movie is not boring."
   INT:  "Are you tired?"       /  "Is the movie boring?"
```

Read. Cohort repeats 2×. Emphasis: "interesting" /ˈɪntrəstɪŋ/, "interested" /ˈɪntrəstɪd/, "tiring" vs "tired", "amazing" vs "amazed", "embarrassed" /ɪmˈbærəst/, "lost" /lɒst/.

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
ONE VERB → TWO ADJECTIVES

  -ING  = creates the effect    (ser ___)
  -ED   = feels the effect      (estar ___)

  TO BORE      →  BORING        /  BORED
  TO AMAZE     →  AMAZING       /  AMAZED
  TO TIRE      →  TIRING        /  TIRED
  TO INTEREST  →  INTERESTING   /  INTERESTED
  TO EXCITE    →  EXCITING      /  EXCITED
  TO SURPRISE  →  SURPRISING    /  SURPRISED
  TO ANNOY     →  ANNOYING      /  ANNOYED
  TO CONFUSE   →  CONFUSING     /  CONFUSED
  TO DISTRACT  →  DISTRACTING   /  DISTRACTED
  TO EMBARRASS →  EMBARRASSING  /  EMBARRASSED
  TO FRUSTRATE →  FRUSTRATING   /  FRUSTRATED
  TO RELAX     →  RELAXING      /  RELAXED
  TO WORRY     →  WORRYING      /  WORRIED
  TO DEPRESS   →  DEPRESSING    /  DEPRESSED
  TO DISAPPOINT→  DISAPPOINTING /  DISAPPOINTED

IRREGULAR (p.328):  TO LOSE → Lost → Have lost  "We are LOST."

NEVER:
  ✗ "I am boring in the class."  → "I am BORED in the class."
  ✗ "The book is bored."         → "The book is BORING."
  ✗ "I am very tiring."          → "I am very TIRED." (if you FEEL it)
  ✗ "The movie is depressed."    → "The movie is DEPRESSING."
  ✗ "She is interesting in the class." (meaning she feels it)
                                  → "She is INTERESTED in the class."
```

**Book examples (p.329 verbatim):**
- *"This is a really boring book."*
- *"Mark was sleeping in the class. He was really bored."*
- *"Santiago is very interested in the English class."*
- *"The soccer game was very exciting!"*
- *"I am excited to visit New York next year."*
- *"Running the Boston Marathon is really tiring!"*
- *"I didn't sleep last night. I am super tired."*
- *"Henry told us an amazing story."*
- *"My little brother is very annoying with his noisy games."*
- *"The movie was very confusing. I didn't understand it."*
- *"I arrived late in class today. It was very embarrassing!"*
- *"We are lost."* (p.328 — irregular)

> Students explain to YOU: *"Which one CREATES the effect — -ING or -ED? Which one FEELS it? If I say 'The book is bored', what is wrong? If I say 'I am interesting in the class', what did I really mean? What is the past participle of 'lose'?"*

## 7. SPEED DRILL — -ING / -ED — 15 min

### Drill 1 — Fast chorus (5 min) — STAND UP

You give subject + verb + hint (P = produces = -ING / F = feels = -ED). Cohort answers with full sentence:

```
the teacher / bore / P           → "The teacher is boring."
the student / bore / F           → "The student is bored."
the movie / depress / P          → "The movie is depressing."
I / depress / F                  → "I am depressed."
the class / interest / P         → "The class is interesting."
Santiago / interest / F          → "Santiago is interested in the class."
the game / excite / P            → "The game is exciting."
I / excite / F (about NY)        → "I am excited to visit New York."
the marathon / tire / P          → "The marathon is tiring."
I / tire / F (last night)        → "I am tired. I didn't sleep last night."
the story / amaze / P            → "The story is amazing."
I / amaze / F                    → "I am amazed."
my brother / annoy / P           → "My brother is annoying."
the movie / confuse / P          → "The movie is confusing."
Juan / confuse / F               → "Juan is confused in class today."
I / embarrass / F (arrived late) → "I was embarrassed."
the meeting / embarrass / P      → "The meeting was embarrassing."
we / lose / F (irregular)        → "We are lost."
```

Each pair writes **8 pairs -ING / -ED** on the same verb:
- 3 from class/study (boring/bored, interesting/interested, confusing/confused, tiring/tired)

- 2 negative emotion (frustrating/frustrated, annoying/annoyed, worrying/worried, embarrassing/embarrassed, depressing/depressed, disappointing/disappointed)
- Minimum 1 pair must use the irregular **lost**.

At 8 min, swap pairs + check -ING vs -ED · ser vs estar · spelling (embarrassed double r and double s, surprised, lost without -ed).

## 8. SIMULATION — "THE END-OF-DAY TEAM DEBRIEF" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. At 6 PM the team closes the day. Each person describes the day in two layers: the EVENTS (-ING — what happened was tiring / interesting / frustrating / surprising / amazing) and the PEOPLE (-ED — how each one feels: tired / interested / frustrated / surprised / amazed). The manager asks; the team answers. Both layers, both adjectives, real work talk."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — debrief script + 2 cases:

```
THE END-OF-DAY TEAM DEBRIEF ASKS (-ING vs -ED):
- "How was the day?"            / "The day was ___ING. I am ___ED."
- "How was the meeting?"        / "The meeting was ___ING. We are ___ED."
- "Are you ___ED?"              / "Yes, I am ___ED." / "No, I am not ___ED."
- "Is the project ___ING?"      / "Yes, it's very ___ING."

CASE 1 — Office team (project)         CASE 2 — Retail/shop team
- the big presentation                 - a long shift on the sales floor
- the client meeting                   - a confused customer
- the missed deadline                  - a surprising sales record
- the new colleague                    - the new product launch
- the slow lunch break                 - a frustrating return
- the email backlog                    - the closing inventory
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"How was the day? — The day was TIRING. I am TIRED. — How was the big presentation? — It was AMAZING. The client was AMAZED. — Was the missed deadline frustrating? — Yes, it was very FRUSTRATING. The whole team is FRUSTRATED. — How was the new colleague? — He is INTERESTING. We are INTERESTED. — In the shop: how was the long shift? — It was TIRING. We are TIRED. — Was the return frustrating? — Yes, the customer was ANNOYED, and the situation was ANNOYING."*

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"This week is TIRING and we are TIRED — but the strong person who keeps showing up is INTERESTING, and the work we do today is AMAZING for the customer."*

### Round 1 (8 min) — Pairs

One = manager (asks 4+ questions, **alternates -ING (events/projects) and -ED (people)**); the other = team member (answers with full sentences using **at least 4 -ING and 4 -ED distinct pairs** from p.327, minimum 1 NEGATIVE "I am not ___ED" and 1 with the irregular "We are LOST" if it fits). Cover one full case.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Switch

Switch roles **and** case (manager now covers the other scenario; force different -ING / -ED verbs from Round 1, minimum 1 full INTERROGATIVE "Are you ___ED?" / "Is the meeting ___ING?", at least 1 pair with strong NEGATIVE emotion (frustrating/frustrated, worrying/worried, depressing/depressed, disappointing/disappointed) and at least 1 POSITIVE pair (amazing/amazed, exciting/excited, surprising/surprised, relaxing/relaxed)).

### Close (3 min)

1 pair in front of the group: 60 sec live debrief + 30 sec Q&A. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook (typical: -ING vs -ED reversed).

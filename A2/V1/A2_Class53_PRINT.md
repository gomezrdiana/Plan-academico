## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 15 min — SETUP + LAST-MINUTE COACHING

### (3 min) Room setup
- Move presenter chair / table to clear stage spot at front.
- Cohort chairs in a clean row facing front (4 audience seats; presenter walks to the spot).
- Phrase of the Day visible on the board behind the presenter (not next to them — behind them).
- Time card stack on YOUR table.
- Personal notebook open; envelope photo already taken.

### (12 min) Last-minute whisper coaching — 3 min per presenter to position 1–4

While room is being arranged, you call each of positions 1–4 to a corner one at a time. 3 min × 4 = 12 min. ONE final note per student, in whisper:
- *"Your hook landed Cl 52 — open the same way today."*
- *"Slow the second sentence — you sped up yesterday."*
- *"Eyes on position 3 of the audience first — friendly face."*
- *"Close strong — the close was your weakest moment in rehearsal."*

Position 5 gets the same 3-min coaching DURING position 1's talk (you whisper at the back).

No coaching during the talks themselves except your time card.

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1:** *"Full five minutes. Full turn. Their eyes."*

> **If a student requests to skip / pull out:** *"You'll do your full 5 minutes. The talking-points card is enough. Position [N] — your slot stands."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 80 min — THE PRESENTATIONS

ONE event, no format change for 80 minutes. 5 presenters × ~14 min each + 10 min total transitions / applause buffer.

### Per-presenter cycle (~14 min)
1. **Position called** (5 sec): *"Position [N]: [name]. Five minutes. Begin."*
2. **5 min talk** — stand at the spot, hook + 3 points + close. You watch time. At 4:30 = no card. At 5:00 = raise 5:00 card silently. At 5:30 = raise 5:30 card. At 6:00 = stand and say *"Close it."*
3. **2 min Q&A** — you call 2 students at random from cohort, one at a time. Each asks the question they wrote during the talk. Presenter answers. If Q&A runs to 2:30 = *"Last question."*
4. **Transition** (~5–7 min) — presenter sits, applause, you take 1 silent note in personal notebook (1 sentence: voice / structure / Q&A / eyes; NAMES — never read aloud), call next position.

### Time discipline (you only)
- 5:00 card — silent raise, presenter sees it
- 5:30 card — silent raise, last 30 sec, presenter knows to close
- 2:00 card during Q&A — silent raise to ask the question
- 2:30 — verbal *"Last question."*
- 6:00 talk — verbal *"Close it."*

### Between presenters (do NOT do)
- ✗ No public ranking ("That was the best so far")
- ✗ No content correction ("You should have said X")
- ✗ No grade hint ("Excellent" / "Needs work")
- ✗ No long comment — applause + position call is enough

### Between presenters (DO)
- ✓ *"Position [N+1]. Five minutes. Begin."*
- ✓ Lead the applause (3 claps, brief)
- ✓ Take 1 silent line in notebook

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2** between position 3 and position 4: *"Full turn — the next person's five minutes are theirs."*

### If something goes wrong
- **Frozen after hook:** wait 10 sec in silence; if still frozen: *"Look at position 1 of the audience. Start with your second sentence."* Wait another 10 sec.
- **Reads from script:** *"Use the cues. The audience is in front of you."* (Once. If they continue reading, let the talk run; note in notebook.)
- **Audience phone use:** stop the talk silently with a hand gesture to the audience member, then resume.

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE — 15 min

### (5 min) Cohort reflection — 1 thing heard well + 1 thing to take

Go around the cohort, 30 sec per student max (5 students × 1 min = 5 min, with buffer):

> *"30 seconds. One thing you heard another presenter do WELL — name the moment specifically. One thing you would TAKE from any presenter into your own work. No 'best' — no ranking. Specific moment + take."*

Examples to model (one quick model from you first if cohort hesitates):
- *"What I heard well: the close on position 2 — the recap of three rules landed clean. What I'd take: the way position 4 used a number in point 1 — '50 calls' landed better than 'many calls'."*

You take notes in personal notebook (NAMES, never read aloud).

### (3 min) Phrase of the Day close

1. *"Phrase from memory — one student."* Pick one at random.
2. *"In one sentence: which of the three fairnesses (full five / full turn / their eyes) was hardest for you today?"* Pick another at random.
3. *"Justice day 3. Two days of Justice left — tomorrow Cl 54 cycle close, day after Cl 55 Final."*

### (4 min) Cl 54 announce + NO scores message + NO homework

> *"Cl 54 — next class. Cycle close + pre-Final review. Walk-through of the Final structure (sections + minutes only — coordination owns the criteria), confidence-building mini-mock from the review arc, error paper showcase from the cycle, and a brief retrospective of the cohort's journey to here. Cl 55 — the Final. Coordination administers. An external evaluator runs the Final — I am not the proctor. Calendar fact only. NO scores from today's presentations from me at any point — coordination handles results. NO homework today — the rest of today is yours. Get rest. JUSTICE day 4 next class."*

If asked directly about scores / how they did: *"Coordination handles results — not me. Your job today was the talk. You gave the talk."* Do NOT soften, do NOT hint, do NOT pull aside.

### (3 min) Applause close + room reset
Lead a final applause for the cohort: *"Five talks, full turns, eyes up. Applause for the cohort."*

Pack the envelope with the order slips (sealed for coordination delivery). Take the cohort photo if scheduled.

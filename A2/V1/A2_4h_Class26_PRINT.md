## 7. SYSTEMATIC A — PREPOSITIONS — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the preposition errors that block B1: time (at/on/in), place (at/in/on) and the fixed prepositions of verbs and adjectives (good AT, depend ON, listen TO, worried ABOUT)."*

### Concept (14 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK A — PREPOSITIONS   [review/fix]

TIME:
  AT + HOUR      "at 8", "at noon", "at midnight"
  ON + DAY/DATE  "on Monday", "on May 5", "on my birthday"
  IN + MONTH / YEAR / PART OF THE DAY
                 "in May", "in 2026", "in the morning",
                 "in the afternoon", "in the evening"
  (BUT: "at night" — exception)

PLACE:
  AT + specific point   "at the office", "at the bus stop",
                        "at my house" (as a meeting point)
  IN + inside / city / country
                        "in the office" (inside),
                        "in Bucaramanga", "in Colombia"
  ON + surface          "on the table", "on the wall"

FIXED with verbs/adjectives:
  good AT (English)         depend ON (you)
  listen TO (me)            worried ABOUT (the test)
  arrive AT (the office)    arrive IN (Bucaramanga / a country)
  live IN (a city/country)  interested IN (music)
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M1/M6/M8 + dependencies, NO names):**

- ❌ *"I work in Monday."* → ✅ *"I work ON Monday."* (days → on)
- ❌ *"She arrives at the morning."* → ✅ *"She arrives IN the morning."* (parts of day → in)
- ❌ *"We meet at September."* → ✅ *"We meet IN September."* (months → in)
- ❌ *"He's good in English."* → ✅ *"He's good AT English."* (good AT)
- ❌ *"I depend of you."* → ✅ *"I depend ON you."* (depend ON)
- ❌ *"Listen me."* → ✅ *"Listen TO me."* (listen TO)
- ❌ *"I'm worried for the test."* → ✅ *"I'm worried ABOUT the test."* (worried ABOUT)
- ❌ *"We arrive to the office at 8."* → ✅ *"We arrive AT the office at 8."* (arrive AT places)
- ❌ *"I live at Bucaramanga."* → ✅ *"I live IN Bucaramanga."* (cities/countries → in)

## 9. SYSTEMATIC B — ARTICLES + QUANTIFIERS — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"The other big block before B1: choosing a/an/the/nothing and choosing the right amount word — much/many/some/any/a lot of. The right dose, not too much, not too little."*

### Concept B1 — articles a/an/the / no article (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK B1 — ARTICLES   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M11)

A / AN  = one (singular countable, new / first mention)
   A car · A new car · A meeting
   AN  -> when the next word starts with a VOWEL SOUND
   AN hour ( /a-ʊ-ər/, silent h)   A university ( /j/ sound)
   AN apple   A European   AN MBA   A one-time event

THE = specific / known by both / unique / mentioned before
   THE sun · THE boss · THE meeting we had yesterday
   With cardinal points: "IN THE east", "IN THE south"
   With superlatives: "THE best", "THE most important"

NO ARTICLE  = abstract / general / uncountable in general
   "I like music."      (NOT "the music" -- in general)
   "I need money."      (uncountable -- no a/an)
   "She studies English."
```

### Concept B2 — quantifiers much/many/some/any/a lot of (10 min)

Third board section:

```
BLOCK B2 — QUANTIFIERS   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M14, M27)

COUNTABLE plural -- MANY / A LOT OF (any sentence)
   "MANY people", "A LOT OF people"
   "How MANY questions?"

UNCOUNTABLE -- MUCH (only negative / question) / A LOT OF (affirmative)
   "I don't have MUCH time."   "How MUCH water?"
   "I drink A LOT OF water."   (NOT "I drink much water")

SOME -- affirmative + polite offers/requests
   "I have SOME questions."
   "Would you like SOME coffee?"
   "Can I have SOME water, please?"

ANY -- questions and negatives
   "Do you have ANY questions?"
   "I don't have ANY money."

A / AN -- NEVER with uncountable
   "I need money" or "I need SOME money."  (NOT "a money")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M11/M14/M27, NO names):**

- ❌ *"I bought new car."* → ✅ *"I bought A new car."* (singular countable new → a/an)
- ❌ *"I have an university."* → ✅ *"I have A university."* (sound /j/ → "a")
- ❌ *"There is many people."* → ✅ *"There are MANY people."* (plural countable → are/many)
- ❌ *"I drink much water."* (affirmative) → ✅ *"I drink A LOT OF water."* (much only neg/question)
- ❌ *"Do you have some questions?"* → ✅ *"Do you have ANY questions?"* (question → any)
- ❌ *"I don't have some money."* → ✅ *"I don't have ANY money."* (negative → any)
- ❌ *"I need a money."* → ✅ *"I need (SOME) money."* (uncountable → no a/an)
- ❌ *"The sun rises at east."* → ✅ *"The sun rises in THE east."* (cardinals with the + IN)
- ❌ *"I like the music."* (in general) → ✅ *"I like music."* (abstract general → no the)

### Guided practice (4 min)

5–6 students at random: give an incomplete or incorrect phrase (*"I bought ___ new car" / "There is ___ people" / "I drink ___ water (every day)" / "Do you have ___ questions?" / "I don't have ___ money" / "I like ___ music (in general)"*); they complete/correct with the right article/quantifier.

## 11. SIMULATION — "THE APPOINTMENT BOOKING" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"A real appointment booking call. You schedule meetings, deliveries, deadlines, doctor visits — using time prepositions (AT hour, ON day, IN month/part of day) and place prepositions (AT the clinic, IN Bucaramanga). The questions FORCE you to choose the right preposition every time."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Call to schedule a real appointment (any everyday role: medical appointment, client meeting, order delivery, technical visit, interview). Caller proposes dates/times/places; receiver confirms or reschedules.
- Time: *"AT what time? ON which day? IN what month?"*
- Place: *"AT which office? IN which city? Inside or outside?"*
- Dependencies: *"Are you good AT this? Do you depend ON anyone for confirmation?"*

**Roles:**
- A = client / caller (proposes day, hour, place; uses time/place prepositions)
- B = reception / scheduler (confirms or proposes alternative with right preposition)

### Model (3 min)

> *You (client): "Hi — I need to schedule a meeting. Can we meet ON Wednesday, AT 10 IN the morning, AT your office IN Bucaramanga?"*
> *Peer teacher (reception): "Let me check. Wednesday AT 10 is not available. Can we move it to Thursday AT 2 IN the afternoon? The meeting will be AT the office, IN the small room ON the second floor."*
> *You: "Thursday AT 2 works. Should I arrive AT the office AT 1:50, or IN the lobby first?"*
> *Reception: "Arrive AT the office AT 1:50. I'm worried ABOUT the parking — depend ON the guard, his name is Carlos. He's good AT finding spots."*

### Round 1 — Pairs (10 min)

Each pair: ~5 min each as reception. Caller makes min. 6 proposals/questions (3 with time prepositions, 3 with place prepositions or dependencies). Receiver uses at least: 1 AT + hour, 1 ON + day, 1 IN + month or part of day, 1 AT + specific place, 1 IN + city, 1 dependency (good AT / depend ON / worried ABOUT) — correctly.

### Round 2 — Roles swap + new scenario (6 min)

Swap roles and appointment (meeting → delivery, → medical appointment, → interview). New booking.

### Close (3 min)

1 pair performs in front of the group. Cohort counts correct prepositions by category. Note errors with NAMES.

> **Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good appointment sounds like the phrase — choose the right preposition, the right amount, not too much, not too little."*

## 12. INTEGRATED SIMULATION — "THE OFFICE TOUR & SUPPLY ORDER" — 22 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Integration. You give a new colleague a quick tour of the office (articles + place prepositions) and then place a supply order at the right amount (quantifiers). Right preposition, right article, right amount."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Brief office tour to a new colleague + supply order to the provider. Forces both blocks:
- Articles + place: *"This is THE meeting room. THE coffee machine is ON the counter, next to A new printer that arrived IN May."*
- Quantifiers: *"We need A LOT OF paper, MANY pens, but NOT MUCH ink. Do you have ANY blue markers? I don't have ANY left."*

### Forced vocabulary

- Min. 3 different articles (1 *a/an*, 1 *the*, 1 no article in general sense)
- Min. 3 different quantifiers (1 *many*, 1 *a lot of* affirmative with uncountable, 1 *any* in question or negative)

### Model (3 min)

> *"Office tour: This is THE meeting room — we meet here ON Mondays AT 8 IN the morning. ON the wall you'll see A new whiteboard, and IN the corner there's A coffee machine — THE coffee is free. We have A LOT OF coffee, but NOT MUCH sugar — we ran out. In THE supply room (down THE hall, ON the left), we have MANY notebooks and pens, but we don't have ANY blue markers. So, supply order: I need A box of blue markers, A LOT OF printing paper, MANY sticky notes, and SOME more sugar, please."*

### Round 1 — Trios (9 min)

Trios. One gives the tour + order 3 min (the other 2 are new colleague + provider and ask 1 thing forcing a form: *"Where exactly is THE meeting room?"* / *"How MANY pens do you need?"* / *"Do you have ANY paper left?"*). Rotate.

### Round 2 — Groups rotate (5 min)

New trios, new scenario (another office, another provider). Repeat the pattern.

### Close (3 min)

1 trio performs in front of the group. Cohort votes: did correct prepositions (time + place) + correct articles (a/an/the/0) + correct quantifiers (many/a lot of/any in correct position) appear? Note errors with NAMES.

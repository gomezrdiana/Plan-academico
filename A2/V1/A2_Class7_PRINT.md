### ☐ 00:13–00:28 | LIBRO: MÓDULO 8 — RELATIVE PRONOUN "THAT" (15 min)

**⚠️ MÁXIMO 15 MINUTOS. 5 de lectura + 10 de explicación. Si no alcanza, corta. NO te pases.**

Di: "Abran su libro A2 en el Módulo 8 — 'I Think that I'm Learning!' Lean la Guía con su compañero. Tienen 5 minutos."

**Primeros 5 min:** Leen en parejas. Camina. A los 5 minutos EXACTOS: Di: "Tiempo. Dejen de leer. Pongan atención."

**Siguientes 10 min:** Siéntate con cada pareja. ELLOS te explican:

1. ¿Qué hace "that" en estas oraciones? →
   - "That" CONECTA dos oraciones en una. Funciona como "que" en español.
   - "I know something." + "You are sick." = "I know that you are sick."
2. ¿Con qué verbos se usa "that"? →
   - know, think, see, hear, feel, say, believe, hope, understand
   - "I think that she is smart."
   - "I know that he works here."
   - "I feel that this is wrong."
   - "She said that she was tired."
3. ¿Se puede omitir "that"? →
   - SÍ. "That" es OPCIONAL. Las dos formas son correctas:
   - "I think that you're right." = "I think you're right."
   - "I know that he's here." = "I know he's here."
4. ¿Qué pasa cuando el primer verbo está en PASADO? →
   - Si el verbo 1 está en pasado, el verbo 2 TAMBIÉN va en pasado:
   - "I know that you ARE sick." → "I KNEW that you WERE sick."
   - "I think that she IS smart." → "I THOUGHT that she WAS smart."
   - "He says that he WANTS to go." → "He SAID that he WANTED to go."
5. ¿Cuáles son los cambios de pasado más comunes? →
   - know → knew, think → thought, see → saw, hear → heard
   - feel → felt, say → said, believe → believed, hope → hoped
   - am/is → was, are → were, want → wanted, like → liked

Ahora dales situaciones. Di cada una y ellos construyen la frase:

**Situación 1:** Di: "Yo sé que tú eres inteligente."
→ "I know that you are smart."

**Situación 2:** Di: "Yo creo que ella es amable."
→ "I think that she is kind."

**Situación 3:** Di: "Él dijo que estaba cansado." (pasado)
→ "He said that he was tired."

**Situación 4:** Di: "Yo sentí que eso estaba mal." (pasado)
→ "I felt that it was wrong."

**Situación 5:** Di: "Ellos saben que nosotros estamos aquí."
→ "They know that we are here."

**Situación 6:** Di: "Yo pensé que ella estaba enferma." (pasado)
→ "I thought that she was sick."

Si está mal: "No. Listen." + versión correcta + repiten 2 veces.

**Escribe en el tablero:**
```
"THAT" CONNECTS TWO SENTENCES:

PRESENT:
I know that you are smart.        Sé que eres inteligente.
I think that she is kind.         Creo que ella es amable.
I feel that this is important.    Siento que esto es importante.
He says that he wants to go.      Él dice que quiere ir.

PAST → PAST (both verbs change):
I KNEW that you WERE sick.        Supe que estabas enfermo/a.
I THOUGHT that she WAS smart.     Pensé que ella era inteligente.
He SAID that he WANTED to go.     Él dijo que quería ir.
I FELT that it WAS wrong.         Sentí que estaba mal.

"That" is OPTIONAL:
"I think that you're right." = "I think you're right." ✓

Verbs: know, think, see, hear, feel, say, believe, hope
```

Di cada frase. La clase repite 2 veces.

### ☐ 00:38–00:58 | SIMULACIÓN: CÍRCULO DE OPINIONES (20 min)

**Qué es:** Los estudiantes se sientan en círculo y comparten opiniones, pensamientos y sentimientos usando "that." Practican presente y pasado.

**Paso 1 — Tablero (3 min).** Escribe en el tablero ANTES de empezar (o prepáralo durante la actividad anterior):

```
💭 OPINION CIRCLE — WHAT DO YOU THINK?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
USE THESE STARTERS:

PRESENT:
"I think that..."         "I know that..."
"I feel that..."          "I believe that..."
"I hope that..."          "I see that..."

PAST:
"I thought that..."       "I knew that..."
"I felt that..."          "He/She said that..."
"I heard that..."         "I hoped that..."

TOPICS:
1. Learning English        5. Social media
2. Your city               6. Food
3. Weekends                7. Work / study
4. Family                  8. The future
```

**Paso 2 — Modelo (2 min).** Di: "Yo primero. Escuchen."

Di: "Topic: learning English. I think that English is important for my future. I know that practice is the best way to learn. I feel that this class is helpful."

Di: "Now past: I thought that English was very difficult. I believed that I was never going to learn. But I was wrong!"

Repite con 1 estudiante. Di: "Topic: your city. Tell me something with 'I think that...'"

**Paso 3 — Círculo (10 min).** Di: "Formen un círculo. Voy a decir un tema. La primera persona dice UNA frase con 'I think that...' o 'I know that...' o 'I feel that...' Luego la siguiente persona dice la suya. Vamos en orden."

**Ronda 1 — Presente (tema: learning English).** Cada estudiante dice una frase. Ejemplos:
- "I think that English is fun."
- "I know that vocabulary is important."
- "I feel that speaking is the hardest part."
- "I believe that I can learn."
- "I hope that I pass the test."

**Ronda 2 — Pasado (tema: your city).** Di: "Ahora en PASADO. Todos usan 'thought,' 'knew,' 'felt,' 'said.'" Cada estudiante dice una frase. Ejemplos:
- "I thought that my city was boring, but it's not."
- "I knew that the traffic was bad."
- "My mom said that this city was beautiful."
- "I felt that the weather was too hot."

A los 5 minutos: cambia de tema. Di: "New topic: food."

**Ronda 3 — Mixto (tema: food).** Pueden usar presente O pasado. Ejemplos:
- "I think that pizza is the best food."
- "I knew that chocolate was delicious."
- "My friend said that sushi was amazing. I believed that he was right."

Camina con papel de errores. Errores comunes:
- "I think that she is smart" en pasado → "I thought that she WAS smart" (verbo 2 también cambia)
- "He said that he wants" → debe ser "He said that he WANTED" (pasado + pasado)
- "I am know that" → debe ser "I know that" (sin "am")

Si escuchas un error: "No. Listen." + versión correcta. Repiten 2 veces.

**Paso 4 — Presentar (5 min).** Di: "4 estudiantes van a compartir su MEJOR frase con la clase. Una en presente y una en pasado."

4 estudiantes comparten. Después de cada frase: clase repite. Corrige si es necesario.

> Camina con papel de errores durante Pasos 3-4. Error más común: no cambiar el verbo 2 a pasado cuando el verbo 1 es pasado.

### ☐ 01:08–01:18 | HISTORIA INTERACTIVA: EL CHISMOSO DEL BARRIO (10 min)

**Qué es:** Cuentas una historia absurda sobre un vecino que siempre reporta lo que los demás dicen, piensan y sienten — todo con "that." Los estudiantes repiten, responden preguntas y participan.

Di: "Les voy a contar la historia de Don Ernesto. Don Ernesto es el chismoso del barrio. Él SIEMPRE sabe lo que todos piensan, dicen y sienten."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO. Pausa para que repitan y participen.**

"Don Ernesto wakes up every morning and looks out his window. He sees everything. He KNOWS everything. He THINKS he knows everything."

Para. Di: "Say it: 'He thinks he knows everything.'" Repiten.

"Monday morning. Don Ernesto sees his neighbor María leaving the house early. He says to his wife: 'I think that María has a new job. I know that she is hiding something.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'I think that María has a new job.'" Repiten. Di: "Does Don Ernesto really know?" Clase: "No!"

"His wife says: 'Ernesto, stop!' But Ernesto continues. He sees Carlos carrying a big box. He says: 'I believe that Carlos is moving. I feel that something is wrong.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'I believe that Carlos is moving. I feel that something is wrong.'" Repiten. Di: "Is Carlos really moving?" Clase no sabe. Di: "Let's find out!"

"Tuesday. Don Ernesto talks to his friend Pedro. He says: 'Pedro, I SAW that María LEFT early yesterday. I HEARD that she GOT a new job. I THOUGHT that she WAS hiding something.'"

Para. Di: "Notice: past + past! Say: 'I saw that María left early.' 'I heard that she got a new job.' 'I thought that she was hiding something.'" Repiten cada frase.

"Pedro says: 'Ernesto, María told me that she was going to the GYM. That's it. The gym.' Don Ernesto feels embarrassed."

Para. Di: "Say: 'María told me that she was going to the gym.'" Repiten. La clase se ríe.

"But Don Ernesto doesn't stop! Wednesday. He sees Carlos again. Carlos has ANOTHER big box. Ernesto tells his wife: 'I KNEW that Carlos WAS moving! I SAID that something WAS wrong!'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'I knew that Carlos was moving. I said that something was wrong.'" Repiten.

"His wife calls Carlos. 'Carlos, are you moving?' Carlos laughs. 'No! I work for a delivery company. I carry boxes every day!' Don Ernesto is embarrassed AGAIN."

Para. Di: "Poor Don Ernesto! He said that Carlos was moving, but Carlos wasn't! Say: 'He said that Carlos was moving, but he was wrong.'" Repiten.

"Thursday. Don Ernesto decides: 'I will not be a chismoso anymore. I KNOW that people don't like it. I FEEL that it is wrong. I THINK that I should stop.'"

Para. Di: "Say: 'I know that people don't like it. I feel that it is wrong. I think that I should stop.'" Repiten.

"Friday. Don Ernesto sees a NEW neighbor moving in. He runs to his wife: 'I THINK that the new neighbor is a doctor! I BELIEVE that he has a lot of money! I FEEL that—' His wife says: 'ERNESTO!'"

Para. La clase se ríe. Di: "Can Don Ernesto stop? Never!" Di: "Will he ever change?" La clase vota.

Ahora señala una pareja: Di: "Don Ernesto sees YOU this morning. What does he think? What does he say to his wife? Use 'I think that...' 'I know that...' 'I saw that...'" La pareja inventa. Pasa a 3-4 parejas más.

Ejemplos que podrían dar:
- "I think that she has a boyfriend." — "I saw that he left early."
- "I knew that they were late." — "I heard that they were fighting."
- "I believe that he is rich." — "I felt that something was different."

> La historia usa "that" con know, think, see, hear, feel, say, believe en presente Y pasado. La regla de pasado + pasado aparece naturalmente en el contexto.

### ☐ 01:18–01:28 | COMPETENCIA: TRADUCE RÁPIDO (10 min)

**Qué es:** 2 equipos. El profesor dice una frase en español. Los equipos la traducen al inglés con "that."

Divide en 2 equipos de 8. De pie.

Di: "Voy a decir una frase en español. Ustedes la dicen en inglés con 'that.' El primer equipo correcto gana el punto."

**Las 12 frases (una a la vez):**

1. "Yo sé que tú estás aquí."
→ "I know that you are here."

2. "Ella cree que él es amable."
→ "She believes that he is kind."

3. "Yo pensé que ella estaba enferma." (pasado)
→ "I thought that she was sick."

4. "Él dijo que quería ir." (pasado)
→ "He said that he wanted to go."

5. "Yo siento que esto es importante."
→ "I feel that this is important."

6. "Nosotros sabemos que el inglés es útil."
→ "We know that English is useful."

7. "Yo escuché que ellos se mudaron." (pasado)
→ "I heard that they moved."

8. "Ella pensó que yo estaba enojado." (pasado)
→ "She thought that I was angry."

9. "Yo espero que mañana sea un buen día."
→ "I hope that tomorrow is a good day."

10. "Mi mamá dijo que la comida estaba lista." (pasado)
→ "My mom said that the food was ready."

11. "Yo creo que tú puedes hacerlo."
→ "I think that you can do it."

12. "Ellos sabían que nosotros estábamos ahí." (pasado)
→ "They knew that we were there."

1 punto por respuesta correcta. Si no cambian el verbo 2 a pasado: "¿Seguro? Piénsenlo otra vez." Dales 5 segundos. Si no: "No. Listen." + versión correcta. Ambos equipos repiten.

Errores que buscar:
- Presente + pasado mezclado: "I thought that she IS sick" → "No. Listen. I thought that she WAS sick."
- "I am think that" → "No. Listen. I THINK that."
- Omitir "that" está BIEN — no lo marques como error.

Equipo ganador celebra. Puntajes en el tablero.

> De pie. Rápido. Ruidoso. La presión de traducir en vivo fuerza la producción.

### ☐ 01:28–01:35 | JUEGO: TELÉFONO ROTO CON "THAT" (7 min)

**Qué es:** Un estudiante susurra una frase con "that" al siguiente. Al final, el último estudiante dice la frase en voz alta. ¿Cambió?

Di: "Vamos a jugar teléfono roto pero SOLO con frases con 'that.' Yo empiezo con una frase. La susurran al siguiente. El último la dice en voz alta."

**Forma 2 filas de 8 estudiantes.**

**Las 4 frases (una por ronda):**

**Ronda 1:** Susurra al primer estudiante: "I think that she is the best student in the class."
→ El último dice lo que escuchó. Compara con la original. La clase se ríe si cambió.

**Ronda 2:** Susurra al primer estudiante: "He said that he wanted to eat pizza for dinner."
→ El último dice lo que escuchó. Compara.

**Ronda 3:** Susurra al primer estudiante: "I knew that they were going to be late again."

### ☐ 01:35–01:50 | GOLDLIST + AUTOCHEQUEO + TAREA + CIERRE (15 min)

**RECOGE TODOS LOS CUADERNOS DE GOLDLIST.** Guárdalos en el salón. Los estudiantes NO se los llevan.

> **NOTA PARA EL PROFESOR — MUY IMPORTANTE:** A partir de esta clase y en TODAS las clases siguientes, usa estas frases NATURALMENTE durante tus instrucciones y actividades. NO digas "esto es de tu GoldList." Simplemente úsalas como parte normal de la clase. Ejemplos:
> - Cuando alguien diga algo sorprendente, di: "Are you serious?"
> - Cuando alguien pregunte de dónde sacaste información, di: "I heard that it's really good."
> - Cuando confirmes algo, di: "That's what I thought."
> - Cuando alguien se equivoque, di: "Don't worry about it."
> - Cuando algo te recuerde algo, di: "That reminds me of..."
> - Cuando quieras ser directo, di: "To be honest..."
> - Cuando alguien comparta algo, di: "I'm glad that you told me."
> - Cuando descubras algo nuevo, di: "I had no idea."
> La exposición inconsciente es lo que hace que el GoldList funcione. Ellos van a escuchar estas frases una y otra vez sin darse cuenta de que las están aprendiendo.

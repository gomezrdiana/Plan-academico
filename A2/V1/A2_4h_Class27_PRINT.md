## 7. SYSTEMATIC A — 3rd PERSON -S — 20 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the 3rd person -s errors that block B1: when to add -s/-es, when to use DOES/DOESN'T, and when the auxiliary already has the -s so the verb goes back to base."*

### Concept (12 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK A — 3RD PERSON -S   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M2/M4)

SUBJECT             VERB IN PRESENT SIMPLE
I / You / We / They -> verb BASE        ("I work", "they go")
He / She / It / sg  -> verb + S / ES    ("He WORKS", "She GOES")

SPECIAL VERBS in 3rd person singular:
   have -> HAS         do -> DOES         go -> GOES
   watch -> WATCHES    cost -> COSTS      study -> STUDIES

AUXILIARIES carry the -S:
   negative:  he / she / it + DOESN'T + verb BASE
              ("She DOESN'T like coffee.")
   question:  DOES + he / she / it + verb BASE + ?
              ("DOES he PLAY soccer?")
              NEVER "Does he plays?" — the -s is in DOES already.

INDEFINITE singular pronouns are 3RD PERSON SINGULAR:
   everybody / nobody / somebody / someone / everyone
   -> "Everybody HAS an opinion." (not "have")

SINGULAR NOUNS treated as singular (one unit):
   my family / the team / the company -> usually + S
   ("My family LIVES in Cali.")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M2/M4, NO names):**

- ❌ *"He go to work every day."* → ✅ *"He GOES to work every day."* (3rd singular → -s)
- ❌ *"She don't like coffee."* → ✅ *"She DOESN'T like coffee."* (doesn't in 3rd singular)
- ❌ *"My brother work at a bank."* → ✅ *"My brother WORKS at a bank."* (singular noun → -s)
- ❌ *"Does he plays soccer?"* → ✅ *"Does he PLAY soccer?"* (DOES has the -s; verb BASE)
- ❌ *"He have a car."* → ✅ *"He HAS a car."* (have → has)
- ❌ *"She watch TV every night."* → ✅ *"She WATCHES TV every night."* (watch + es)
- ❌ *"It cost too much."* → ✅ *"It COSTS too much."* (it = 3rd singular)
- ❌ *"Everybody have a different opinion."* → ✅ *"Everybody HAS a different opinion."* (everybody = singular)

> **Insertion #1 of Phrase of the Day:** *"As today's phrase says — match the verb to the subject. One subject, one rule. The right form for the right number."*

### Guided practice (5 min)

Board: give subject + verb (*"he / go" / "she / have" / "it / cost" / "my brother / work" / "everybody / want" / "they / play" (plural contrast) / "she / watch" / "does he / play?"*); cohort produces the correct form in chorus. 8–10 items mixing 3rd singular vs plural vs questions.

### Close A (3 min)

## 9. SYSTEMATIC B — THERE IS / THERE ARE — 17 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"The second block: when we describe what EXISTS in a place, we use THERE IS for one and THERE ARE for many. Past: THERE WAS / THERE WERE. Never 'It have', never 'Have many people'."*

### Concept (8 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK B — THERE IS / THERE ARE   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M14)

EXISTENCE in a place / situation -> THERE + BE

NUMBER       PRESENT          PAST
singular     THERE IS         THERE WAS
plural       THERE ARE        THERE WERE

   "There IS a problem with the printer."   (one)
   "There ARE many people in the office."   (many)
   "There WAS a meeting yesterday."         (one, past)
   "There WERE 5 people in the call."       (many, past)

QUESTIONS:
   "IS there a meeting today?"   (one)
   "ARE there any questions?"    (many)

NEVER use "It have / It has" for existence:
   ❌ "It have three rooms."   ✅ "There ARE three rooms."
   ❌ "Have many cars here."   ✅ "There ARE many cars here."
                                 (don't forget THERE)
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M14, NO names):**

- ❌ *"There is many people in the office."* → ✅ *"There ARE many people in the office."* (plural → are)
- ❌ *"There are a problem with the printer."* → ✅ *"There IS a problem with the printer."* (singular → is)
- ❌ *"It have three rooms in my house."* → ✅ *"There ARE three rooms in my house."* (no "it have")
- ❌ *"Have many cars in the parking."* → ✅ *"There ARE many cars in the parking."* (missing there)
- ❌ *"There was 5 people in the meeting."* → ✅ *"There WERE 5 people in the meeting."* (past plural → were)
- ❌ *"There is some chairs here."* → ✅ *"There ARE some chairs here."* (plural → are)
- ❌ *"Is there any questions?"* → ✅ *"Are there any questions?"* (plural → are)

### Guided practice (5 min)

5–6 students at random: give a noun + number (*"problem / singular present" / "people / plural present" / "rooms / plural present" / "meeting / singular past" / "5 cars / plural past" / "questions / plural present, question"*); they build the sentence with there + correct be.

### Close B (4 min)

Pairs STANDING: each pair describes the room using 4 sentences (2 with THERE IS, 2 with THERE ARE, including at least 1 in past or 1 as question). 1 pair shares. Note errors with NAMES.

> **Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"One thing or many? Match the verb to the subject — the right form for the right number."*

## 10. SYSTEMATIC C — CAUSATIVE — 20 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"The third block: when you don't do something yourself, but someone else does it for you, or you ask/tell someone to do it. Three families: WANT/TELL/ASK + TO, MAKE/LET WITHOUT 'to', HAVE/GET + V3 for the passive."*

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK C — CAUSATIVE   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M44)

FAMILY 1: want / tell / ask / would like + OBJECT + TO + verb
   "I WANT YOU TO GO."
   "She TOLD HIM TO BRING the report."
   "She ASKS ME TO COME early."
   ❌ "I want THAT you go."          (no "that" -- Spanish calque)
   ❌ "She told him THAT bring..."   (no "that")

FAMILY 2: make / let + OBJECT + verb BASE  (NO "to"!)
   "I MADE HIM WORK hard."
   "I LET HIM GO."
   ❌ "I made him TO work."          (no "to" after make)
   ❌ "I let him TO go."             (no "to" after let)

FAMILY 3: have / get + OBJECT + V3   (causative PASSIVE)
   Someone does something FOR me / TO my object:
   "I HAD my hair CUT."              (the barber cut it)
   "I GET my car WASHED on Saturdays."
   "She HAS her nails DONE every month."
   Active version (someone specific):
   "I HAD HIM CUT my hair."          (have + person + base verb)
   ❌ "I had he cut my hair."        (need object pronoun: him)
   ❌ "I get my car wash."            (need V3: WASHED)

TRANSLATION TRAP:
   Spanish "quiero que tu vayas" -> "I WANT YOU TO GO."
   NOT "I want that you go."
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M44 + Spanish interference, NO names):**

- ❌ *"I want that you go."* → ✅ *"I WANT YOU TO GO."* (want + object + to + verb)
- ❌ *"She told him that bring the report."* → ✅ *"She TOLD HIM TO BRING the report."* (tell + object + to + verb)
- ❌ *"I made him to work hard."* → ✅ *"I MADE HIM WORK hard."* (make + object + verb BASE)
- ❌ *"I let him to go."* → ✅ *"I LET HIM GO."* (let + object + verb BASE)
- ❌ *"I had he cut my hair."* → ✅ *"I HAD HIM CUT my hair."* (need object pronoun + base verb)
- ❌ *"I get my car wash every Saturday."* → ✅ *"I GET my car WASHED every Saturday."* (get + object + V3)
- ❌ *"She asks me come early."* → ✅ *"She ASKS ME TO COME early."* (ask + object + to + verb)

### Guided practice (5 min)

6 students at random: give a situation + family (*"want + your team + finish on time" / "tell + him + bring the report" / "make + your kids + study" / "let + me + help you" / "have + your hair + cut" / "get + your car + washed"*); they build the sentence with the correct family structure.

### Close C (5 min)

Pairs STANDING: each pair builds 4 sentences — 1 per family + 1 contrast (have someone do vs have something done: *"I had HIM CUT my hair"* vs *"I had my hair CUT"*). 1 pair shares. Note errors with NAMES.

> **Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"The right form for the right verb family — match the structure, not too much, not too little."*

## 12. SIMULATION — "THE TEAM BRIEFING" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"A real team briefing. A team leader assigns tasks for the week. The instructions FORCE 3rd person -s (describing who does what) AND causative structures (assigning: 'I need you to...', 'I want her to...', 'I'll have IT done by Friday')."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Brief team meeting (any everyday role: store, office, support, restaurant, reception). Leader assigns tasks for the week; members confirm what each will do.
- 3rd -s: *"Maria HANDLES the morning shift. The new employee STARTS at 9. Carlos GOES to the warehouse on Wednesdays."*
- Causative: *"I need YOU TO finish the report by Thursday. I'll have the contracts SIGNED today. I want HER TO call the client."*
- There is/are: *"There ARE three pending orders. There IS one urgent ticket. There WERE 5 complaints last week."*

**Roles:**
- A = team leader (assigns tasks, describes who does what — forces 3rd -s + causative)
- B = team member (confirms assignments, asks, describes what's done — forces there is/are + 3rd -s)

### Model (3 min)

> *You (leader): "Good morning. There ARE three urgent items this week. First, I need YOU TO finish the inventory by Thursday. Maria HANDLES the morning shift and SHE HAS the keys, so check with her. Second, I'll have the new printer INSTALLED on Wednesday — the technician COMES at 10. Third, I want Carlos TO train the new employee. He STARTS Monday. Questions?"*
> *Peer teacher (member): "Yes — there ARE three pending orders from last week. Should I have them DELIVERED this week, or do you want me TO wait?"*
> *You: "Have them DELIVERED. And let HIM HELP you — he LEARNS fast."*

### Round 1 — Pairs (10 min)

Each pair: ~5 min each as leader. Leader assigns min. 6 tasks covering the 3 blocks (2 with 3rd -s describing a colleague, 2 with causative assigning, 2 with there is/are describing the state). Member confirms using: 1 sentence in 3rd -s, 1 with causative, 1 with there is/are — correctly.

### Round 2 — Roles swap + new scenario (6 min)

Swap roles and scenario (office → store, → restaurant, → support). New briefing.

### Close (3 min)

1 pair performs in front of the group. Cohort counts correct forms by category. Note errors with NAMES.

## 6. SYSTEMATIC A — PHRASAL VERBS — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the phrasal verb errors that block B1: the 10 most common phrasal verbs, the difference between SEPARABLE (pronoun in the middle) and INSEPARABLE (never break)."*

### Concept (11 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
A) PHRASAL VERBS   [review/fix]

10 MOST COMMON (A2):
  PICK UP      PUT ON       TAKE OFF     LOOK FOR     GIVE UP
  TURN ON      TURN OFF     GET UP       SIT DOWN     FIND OUT
+ useful: RUN OUT (of)  COME BACK  GO AWAY  LOOK AFTER  WORK OUT  SET UP

SEPARABLE  (pronoun = IN THE MIDDLE, ALWAYS)
  PICK UP   PUT ON   TAKE OFF   TURN ON   TURN OFF
  FIND OUT  SET UP   GIVE UP

  With NOUN: 2 positions OK
    "Pick up the report."   OR   "Pick the report up."
  With PRONOUN: ONE position only — IN THE MIDDLE
    "Pick IT up."           NEVER "Pick up IT."
    "Turn IT on."           NEVER "Turn on IT."
    "Take IT off."          NEVER "Take off IT."

INSEPARABLE  (verb + particle NEVER break)
  LOOK FOR     LOOK AFTER     GET UP     SIT DOWN
  COME BACK    GO AWAY        RUN OUT OF  WORK OUT

  With NOUN or PRONOUN: same position
    "I look FOR the keys / I look FOR them."
    NEVER "I look the keys for / I look them for."

RULE: separable + pronoun = in the middle, no exceptions.
RULE: inseparable = the verb and particle are glued.
```

**Canonical typical errors (from previous A2 guide banks, NO names):**

- ❌ *"I picked up it at 8."* → ✅ *"I picked IT UP at 8."* (separable + pronoun = middle)
- ❌ *"Please turn on it."* → ✅ *"Please turn IT ON."* (separable + pronoun = middle)
- ❌ *"She took off it."* → ✅ *"She took IT OFF."* (separable + pronoun = middle)
- ❌ *"I look the report for."* → ✅ *"I look FOR the report."* (inseparable, no break)
- ❌ *"I look them after."* → ✅ *"I look AFTER them."* (inseparable, no break)

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a phrasal verb + noun and pronoun (*"pick up / the report / it"*); cohort produces TWO sentences: the noun one (2 positions for separables) + the pronoun one (1 position, in middle). For inseparables, one form with pronoun, reinforce it never breaks. 8 items mixing 5 separables + 3 inseparables.

## 7. SYSTEMATIC B — PASSIVE VOICE IN 4 TENSES — 17 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — we CORRECT the passive errors that block B1: the structure BE + PAST PARTICIPLE in 4 tenses (present / past / present perfect / future). Never 'It was do.' Never 'has updated' without 'been.'"*

### Concept (13 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
B) PASSIVE VOICE   [review/fix]

STRUCTURE:  subject (RECEIVES action) + BE + PAST PARTICIPLE
            (+ BY agent, optional)

PRESENT SIMPLE  -> is / are + past participle
  "The report IS WRITTEN every week."
  "The doors ARE LOCKED at 9."
  "The forms ARE SIGNED every morning."

PAST SIMPLE  -> was / were + past participle
  "The meeting WAS RESCHEDULED by the client."
  "The keys WERE FOUND yesterday."
  "The system WAS UPDATED last night."

PRESENT PERFECT  -> has / have BEEN + past participle
  "The system HAS BEEN UPDATED."
  "The reports HAVE BEEN SENT."
  "The printer HAS BEEN FIXED."

FUTURE (will)  -> will BE + past participle
  "The software WILL BE INSTALLED by IT tomorrow."
  "The forms WILL BE SIGNED next week."
  "The new computers WILL BE DELIVERED on Friday."

WHEN TO USE PASSIVE (A2 -> B1):
  - the DOER doesn't matter ("The report is sent every day.")
  - the DOER isn't known ("My keys were stolen.")
  - the DOER is obvious or not worth saying
    ("The system has been updated." — by IT, obviously)

RULE: passive = BE in the right tense + PAST PARTICIPLE. Always.
RULE: present perfect passive = HAS/HAVE + BEEN + past participle.
      Never forget BEEN.
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"It was do yesterday."* → ✅ *"It WAS DONE yesterday."* (past participle, not base)
- ❌ *"The report is write every day."* → ✅ *"The report IS WRITTEN every day."* (participle)
- ❌ *"The system has updated."* → ✅ *"The system HAS BEEN UPDATED."* (missing "been")
- ❌ *"The meeting was reschedule."* → ✅ *"The meeting WAS RESCHEDULED."* (participle with -ed)
- ❌ *"The software will install tomorrow."* → ✅ *"The software WILL BE INSTALLED tomorrow."* (missing "be")
- ❌ *"It is making by the team."* → ✅ *"It IS MADE by the team."* (present simple passive, not progressive)

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Know WHO does it — if it doesn't matter, put the doer DOWN: passive voice. BE + past participle. Always."*

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a work process + a tense (*"reports / send / present"* → *"Reports ARE SENT every day"*; *"meeting / reschedule / past"* → *"The meeting WAS RESCHEDULED"*; *"system / update / present perfect"* → *"The system HAS BEEN UPDATED"*; *"software / install / future"* → *"The software WILL BE INSTALLED tomorrow"*). Cohort answers in chorus. 8 items mixing the 4 tenses.

## 9. SIMULATION — "THE HANDOVER BRIEFING" — 20 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You are at work and a colleague hands over their shift to you. They USE phrasal verbs (picked up, turned off, set up, found out, looked after) AND passive in 4 tenses (the meeting WAS RESCHEDULED, the printer WAS TURNED OFF, the system HAS BEEN UPDATED, the new software WILL BE INSTALLED). You receive the briefing and ask 2 follow-up questions that force more phrasal verbs + more passive. Then you rotate."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — the 2 forced forms + 2 cases:

```
THE HANDOVER BRIEFING FORCES (2 sub-clusters):
- Phrasal verbs  -> "I PICKED UP the report at 8."
                    "I TURNED IT OFF before leaving."
                    "I FOUND OUT the meeting was rescheduled."
                    "I SET UP the projector for the morning."
                    "I LOOKED AFTER the customers."
- Passive voice  -> "The meeting WAS RESCHEDULED by the client."  (past)
                    "The printer WAS TURNED OFF when I arrived."   (past)
                    "The system HAS BEEN UPDATED overnight."       (pres. perf.)
                    "The new software WILL BE INSTALLED by IT."    (future)
                    "Reports ARE SENT every morning."              (present)

CASE 1 — Store / shop                CASE 2 — Office / customer service
- pick up cash, turn on POS,        - pick up tickets, turn on system,
  set up displays, find out         set up workstation, find out
  promotions                         meeting changes
- "displays were set up by night    - "tickets were assigned to me;
  shift; cash was counted; new       system has been updated; new
  prices will be applied tomorrow"   policy will be sent today"
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"Handover for the store team: Hi — I picked UP the cash at 8 and I turned the alarm OFF. The displays were SET UP by the night shift and the prices were UPDATED yesterday. I found OUT that the supplier meeting WAS RESCHEDULED by the client to 3pm — please pick THAT UP on the calendar. The POS system HAS BEEN UPDATED overnight, so the receipts WILL BE PRINTED in the new format. The new promotions WILL BE APPLIED tomorrow morning. Look AFTER the morning customers and turn the lights OFF at close. Any questions?"*

**Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good handover sounds like today's phrase — know who does it (passive when the doer doesn't matter) and know where it goes (the pronoun in the middle of the phrasal verb). Pick the verb up, put the doer down."*

### Round 1 (7 min) — Pairs

Pairs. One does the handover (~2.5 min) using: ≥3 phrasal verbs (2 separables with pronoun in middle + 1 inseparable) + ≥3 passives in 3 different tenses (past + present perfect + future). The other listens and asks **2 questions** that force more forms:
- 1 question forcing phrasal verb: *"And when did you turn the alarm off?" / "Did you pick THEM UP yourself?"*
- 1 question forcing passive: *"By whom was the meeting rescheduled?" / "Has the system already been updated?" / "When will the software be installed?"*

Then rotate: each one does a handover.

### Round 2 (4 min) — New scenario

Switch cases (store → office, or vice versa). New handover, same 2 sub-clusters forced. Listener repeats the 2 mandatory questions (1 phrasal + 1 passive).

### Close (3 min)

1 pair in front of the group: 90 sec handover + 60 sec Q&A (the 2 mandatory partner questions + 1 teacher question). Cohort counts how many of the 2 forms appeared correctly (≥3 phrasal verbs with pronoun in correct position + ≥3 passives in at least 3 tenses). Note errors with NAMES.

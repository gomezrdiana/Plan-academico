## 6. BOOK M43 — ADVERBS OF MANNER — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Open Module 43, page 340. How Do You Do? Today we stop describing a CHANGE of state and start describing HOW the action is done — with an adverb of manner. Most are 'adjective + -ly' (sad → sadly, happy → happily). Some are irregular: 'good' becomes 'well', 'fast' stays 'fast', 'hard' stays 'hard'. The adverb goes AFTER the main verb, or AFTER the direct object: 'He plays well. He plays soccer well.' Questions use 'how' + auxiliary: 'How do you drive?'"*

### Structures (5 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
M43 STRUCTURES (Book p.342)

A) FORMATION
   regular:    adjective + -ly     →  sad → sadly, nice → nicely
   -y → -ily:                       →  happy → happily, easy → easily
   IRREGULAR:  good → WELL,  fast → fast,  hard → hard,  early/late

B) PLACEMENT
   "I play well."             (after the verb, no object)
   "He plays soccer well."    (after the direct object)
   "Suddenly, I heard a noise." (at the start, emphasis — p.343)

C) QUESTION
   "How + do/does/did + subject + verb (+ object)?"
   "How do you drive?"      →  "I drive carefully."
   "How does Helen talk?"   →  "She talks beautifully."

D) FREQUENCY + MANNER together (p.346)
   "He USUALLY holds the pen HARD."   (freq. BEFORE verb + manner AFTER)
   "She SOMETIMES writes intelligently."
   "They OFTEN meet early."
   "I ALWAYS believe you easily."
```

Read. Cohort repeats 2×. Emphasis: "-ly" /li/, "well" /wɛl/, "fast" /fæst/, "hard" /hɑːrd/, "early" /ˈɜːrli/, "happily" /ˈhæpɪli/, "beautifully" /ˈbjuːtəfli/, "carefully" /ˈkɛərfəli/, "completely" /kəmˈpliːtli/.

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board:

```
HOW DO YOU DO IT? = ¿Cómo lo haces?

  FORMATION
    adjective + -ly                → sad → sadly, nice → nicely
    -y → -ily                      → happy → happily, easy → easily
    IRREGULARS                     → good → WELL, fast → fast,
                                     hard → hard, early/late (same)

  PLACEMENT
    after the verb                 → "I play well."
    after the direct object        → "He plays soccer well."
    at start (emphasis)            → "Suddenly, I heard a noise."

  QUESTION
    "How + do/does/did + subject + verb (+ object)?"
    "How do you drive?"  →  "I drive slowly."

  FREQUENCY + MANNER together
    "He USUALLY holds the pen HARD."
    "She SOMETIMES writes intelligently."

NEVER:
  ✗ "He plays good."         → "He plays WELL."
  ✗ "He plays fastly."       → "He plays FAST." (irregular, no -ly)
  ✗ "He sings beautiful."    → "He sings BEAUTIFULLY."
  ✗ "He plays well soccer."  → "He plays SOCCER WELL."
  ✗ "How drives Antonio?"    → "How DOES Antonio DRIVE?" (auxiliary)
  ✗ "He happyly works."      → "He works HAPPILY." (-y → -ily)
```

**Book examples (p.343 verbatim):**
- *"He plays well."*
- *"He plays soccer well."*
- *"The dog plays nicely."*
- *"William studies intelligently."*
- *"My class starts early."*
- *"John is speaking seriously."*
- *"Helena writes beautifully."*
- *"Philip drives fast."*
- *"Suddenly, I heard a noise."*
- *"Sebastian worked carefully."*
- *"Mike speaks English correctly."*
- *"He usually holds the pen hard."* (p.346 — freq + manner)

> Students explain to YOU: *"How do you form an adverb from an adjective? Three irregulars? Where does the adverb go — before or after the verb? And if there is a direct object? How do you make a 'how' question? And if you mix frequency (always, usually) with manner (well, fast), which goes where?"*

## 7. SPEED DRILL — HOW + VERB + ADVERB — 15 min

### Drill 1 — Fast chorus (5 min) — STAND UP

You give subject + verb (+ object) + hint (L = -ly regular / I = irregular / F+M = freq + manner). Cohort forms **question + answer**:

```
Antonio / do the homework / well / I       → "How does Antonio do the homework? — He does it well."
Helen / talk / beautiful → beautifully / L → "How does Helen talk? — She talks beautifully."
the boys / talk / quick → quickly / L      → "How do the boys talk? — They talk quickly."
the dog / walk / slow → slowly / L          → "How does the dog walk? — It walks slowly."
Bilbo / play / alone / I                    → "How can Bilbo play? — He plays alone."
we / study / together / I                   → "How should we study? — We should study together."
Sam / open the door / complete → completely / L → "How did Sam open the door? — He opened it completely."
Ricardo / start the class / easy → easily / L → "How did Ricardo start the class? — He started it easily."
we / play tennis / hard / I                 → "How should we play tennis? — We should play hard."
Roger / arrive / late / I                   → "How does Roger arrive? — He arrives late."
Sebastian / work / careful → carefully / L  → "How did Sebastian work? — He worked carefully."
Philip / drive / fast / I                   → "How does Philip drive? — He drives fast."
Andres / hold the pen / usually + hard / F+M → "How does Andres hold the pen? — He usually holds the pen hard."
Camila / write to her mother / sometimes + intelligently / F+M → "How does Camila write to her mother? — She sometimes writes intelligently to her mother."
the children / grow / usually + fast / F+M  → "How do the children grow? — They usually grow fast."
```

15 items, fast pace. If they fail, repeat until correct chorus.

### Drill 2 — Pairs (10 min) — SIT

Each pair writes **8 question+answer pairs**: 4 with **-ly regular** (include at least 1 with -y → -ily like "happily" / "easily"), 2 with **IRREGULAR** ("well" / "fast" / "hard" / "early" / "late" / "alone" / "together"), 2 combining **frequency + manner** ("usually + hard", "sometimes + intelligently"). Minimum 2 sentences must include a DIRECT OBJECT ("He plays SOCCER well"). Topics: work / school / sports. At 8 min, swap pairs + check -ly correct · irregulars · placement after verb or object.

## 8. SIMULATION — "THE PERFORMANCE REVIEW" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You are a supervisor running a performance review for a colleague. For each task, you ASK 'How does she do it? How does he handle it?' and the colleague ANSWERS with an adverb of manner — '-ly' regulars (carefully, intelligently, beautifully), irregulars (well, fast, hard, early), and at least one combining frequency + manner ('He usually finishes early.'). The adverb goes after the verb or the direct object."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — review checklist + 2 cases:

```
THE PERFORMANCE REVIEW ASKS (how + verb + adverb):
- How does ___ do the report?     / She does it carefully.
- How does ___ handle clients?    / He handles them well.
- How does ___ arrive?            / She arrives early.
- How does ___ work + frequency?  / He usually works hard.

CASE 1 — Office team review        CASE 2 — Store / shop team review
- write the weekly report          - serve a customer
- answer client emails             - count the cash at closing
- arrive at the office             - clean the floor
- run a meeting                    - stock the shelves
- handle a complaint               - speak to a manager
- finish the day                   - finish the shift
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"How does she write the weekly report? — She writes it carefully. How does he answer client emails? — He answers them quickly. How does she arrive at the office? — She arrives early — irregular, no -ly. How does he run a meeting? — He runs it well — irregular of good. How does she handle a complaint? — She USUALLY handles it patiently — frequency before the verb, manner after. How does he finish the day? — He finishes it completely. In the store: how does she serve a customer? — She serves them nicely. How does he count the cash? — He counts it slowly and correctly."*

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"How does a strong person finish a performance review? Quietly, carefully, and completely — not loudly, not fast, not alone."*

### Round 1 (8 min) — Pairs

One = supervisor (asks 4+ questions with **how + verb (+ object)**, mix with and without direct object); the other = colleague (answers with adverb of manner — minimum 2 with **-ly regular** (at least 1 with -y → -ily like "happily" / "easily"), minimum 1 with **IRREGULAR** ("well", "fast", "hard", "early", "late"), minimum 1 combining **frequency + manner** ("usually + hard", "always + carefully")). Cover one full case.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Switch

Switch roles **and** case (the supervisor now reviews the other team; force "how" with different verbs, mixed regular / irregular answers, at least 1 with DIRECT OBJECT ("He counts the cash slowly") and at least 1 with FREQUENCY + MANNER ("She always finishes early")).

### Close (3 min)

1 pair in front of the group: 60 sec live performance review + 30 sec Q&A. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

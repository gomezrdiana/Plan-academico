## BLOCK 1 — OPENER — 10 min

> *"Welcome to Cl 52 of 55. Today is JUSTICE v3 day 2 — and it is the rehearsal class. You all wrote your drafts and recorded the first audio. Today you each present TWICE in front of a small group, take 2 questions between, adjust once, and walk out with a talking-points card you'll use on Cl 53. Tomorrow Cl 53: the day. Cl 54: cycle close. Cl 55: Final, coordination administers."*

### (2 min) Phrase of the Day — on the board (already written)

```
PHRASE OF THE DAY — CL 52
"Justice day two — give the rehearsal the same
 five minutes you'll give the real one; give the
 questioner a real answer; the talk earns its
 weight in the second run."
```

Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Justice day 2 — the rehearsal is not the warm-up. The rehearsal is the rehearsal — five real minutes, no shortcuts. The questioner deserves a real answer — not 'I don't know' as a hide. And the talk only earns its weight on the SECOND run — the first one is muscle memory, the second one is fluency. Don't skip the second one."*

### (2 min) 60-sec mystery — fast diagnostic
Open the envelope. Read aloud:
> *"60 seconds. From the previous class's homework (Cl 51, polished draft + 5-min audio + 5-min listen): tell me ONE thing the speaker you listened to did well at the OPEN and ONE at the CLOSE. Go."*

Pick 3 students at random. Fluent = +5 EP. Hesitates = *"Sit. Thank you."* If anyone asks about Final grades: *"Coordination handles results — not me, not today."* Note who did NOT bring the polished draft + the audio file on phone.

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing 30-second "TIME YOUR HOOK"
Everyone stands in a circle. Each student says their HOOK out loud — just the first 30 seconds of their talk — eyes up, no notes. You time with second-hand: under 25s = too fast, over 35s = too long. Quick coaching: *"Slow down 1 beat / cut 1 sentence / lower the first word."*

Goal: surface the rhythm before the full rehearsal. Every student hears their hook in front of others once before the real rehearsal.

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1:** *"Five real minutes — the rehearsal is the rehearsal."*

### (2 min) Bridge to today's content
> *"Today's block 2: I check your audio on phone (10 min listen-back), then whisper-coach you individually while the rest rehearse silently in pairs. Then TWO of you do FULL rehearsals in front of the group with cohort Q&A — to model what tomorrow looks like. Block 3 is the real rehearsal day — small groups of 3, every student presents twice, with Q&A between. Block 4: error paper, homework, Cl 53 brief. No grades. Coordination handles results. Let's start."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 50 min — AUDIO + COACH + 2 MODEL REHEARSALS

### (10 min) Audio listen-back check — silent pair work

Sit. Pairs. Each student plays the 30 first seconds + 30 last seconds of their audio recording (NOT the full thing) on their phone to their partner. Partner listens, marks the pacing on the back of their feedback card from previous class: *under 4:30 / 4:30–5:30 / over 5:30*, and notes PACE (fast / steady / slow) + VOLUME (low / medium / strong). 30+30 sec × 2 students = 2 min per pair audio check. Remaining 8 min: each student adjusts pace / volume notes on their draft based on partner's feedback.

If a student did NOT record the audio (homework not done): they read the first 30 seconds and last 30 seconds OUT LOUD to partner instead, and partner gives the same feedback. NAMES into notebook for the homework list.

### (15 min) Individual coaching in whisper mode + silent pair rehearsal

Stand. Pairs go to two opposite sides of the room and rehearse SILENTLY (mouthing the words, no sound) — full talk run-through, looking at draft on paper occasionally. Meanwhile you call ONE student at a time to a corner for 90 seconds of whispered coaching — ONE specific note per student, never two. Common notes:
- *"Hook too long — cut the first sentence."*
- *"Point 2 has no number — add one."*
- *"Close is the weakest moment — say the close one more time before sitting."*
- *"Eyes — when you say the hook, look at one person, not the paper."*
- *"Voice — drop the volume on the second sentence; raise it on the last."*

Rotate through all students in 15 min. If cohort is 5 students = ~3 min per student including transition.

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2:** *"The talk earns its weight in the second run — that's what today's rehearsals are for."*

### (25 min) 2 students do FULL rehearsal in front of group — model the day

Sit (cohort) and stand (presenter at front of room marked spot). You pick the 2 students whose drafts you read in advance are MOST READY (NOT to praise them, but because the model has to be clean for the rest to see the rhythm). Each does:

1. **5 min FULL talk** — hook + 3 points + close, no script, talking-points card only allowed.
2. **2 min Q&A** — cohort prepares 1 question each in silence during the talk (write on Q&A prompt card below); you call 3 students at random to ask.
3. **3 min structured feedback** — cohort gives feedback using the format: *"ONE strength — [specific sentence/moment]. ONE improvement using a connector from the Phrase: BECAUSE / SO / ALTHOUGH / HOWEVER + suggestion."* You collect a 4th feedback at the end, generic to the cohort.

Q&A prompt card (hand out before this section):
```
Q&A PROMPT CARD — CL 52 REHEARSAL

Presenter 1 name: ___________
My question (write during the talk): _______________

Presenter 2 name: ___________
My question (write during the talk): _______________

(Back side — use during Block 3 rehearsals.)
```

2 students × 10 min each = 20 min. 5 min buffer for transitions and your model intro: *"This is what tomorrow looks like — 5 min talk, 2 min Q&A, then move on. Watch the rhythm. Watch the close. Watch how Q&A handles a tough question."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — SMALL-GROUP REHEARSALS

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Small groups of 3 (or 2 + you joins as the third in the smallest group). Each student presents TWICE with peer Q&A between.

### (2 min) Setup
> *"Stand. Form small groups of 3. Each group has a clear stage spot. In each group: 1 PRESENTER (does the 5-min talk) + 2 AUDIENCE (listen, prepare 1 question each, ask after the talk). After Q&A: 2 min of group feedback (1 strength + 1 specific from each audience member). Then rotate. Each student does 2 full rounds today — first round one mode, second round adjusted. Justice day 2: same five minutes you'll give the real one — don't shorten the rehearsal. I circulate and whisper-coach."*

### (~40 min) 6 rotations × 7 min each

Group of 3, each student presents twice. Cycle:

**Rotation 1 (7 min):** Presenter A — 5 min talk + 2 min Q&A (one question from each of B + C).
**Rotation 2 (7 min):** Presenter B — 5 min talk + 2 min Q&A (one question from each of A + C).
**Rotation 3 (7 min):** Presenter C — 5 min talk + 2 min Q&A (one question from each of A + B).
**Rotation 4 (7 min):** Presenter A again — same talk, ONE adjustment from the feedback applied.
**Rotation 5 (7 min):** Presenter B again — same, one adjustment.
**Rotation 6 (7 min):** Presenter C again — same, one adjustment.

(Adjust math if cohort = 4 or 5: 2 groups × 6 rotations of ~6 min, or 1 group of 5 with rounds adjusted.)

You circulate. Coach in whisper. Common second-round nudges:
- *"Hook landed in second 8 — slow down beat 1."*
- *"Point 3 was the weakest — fix the example."*
- *"Q&A: when you don't know, say 'I don't know — I'd check with the team'. Don't freeze."*
- *"Eyes — find ONE listener per point. 3 points, 3 listeners."*

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3** during rotation 4 transition: *"The talk earns its weight in the SECOND run — this is the run."*

### Cohort close — none (time goes to second-run rehearsals)

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE — 15 min

### (4 min) Error paper recap — 5 rehearsal-specific errors

Write on the board (anonymous; NAMES only in your personal notebook):

```
ERROR PAPER — CL 52 REHEARSAL (5 ERRORS)

1. Talk too short (under 4:30):  add 1 example to point 2 (where it ran light)
2. Talk too long (over 5:30):    cut the longest sentence in the longest point
3. Close drops to a mumble:      stand still, eyes up, voice strong on the close sentence
4. Q&A frozen:                   use "Good question. The short answer is ___."  or  "I don't know — I'd check."
5. Eyes on the paper, not the audience:  3 points = 3 listeners; one per point
```

Cohort echoes the 5 corrections in chorus 1×. **Anonymous Error Paper delivered physically with the guide (NOT photographed).** NAMES in personal notebook.

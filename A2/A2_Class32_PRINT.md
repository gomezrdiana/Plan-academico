## ⚠️ NOTA SOBRE EL CONTENIDO — M33 HOW MUCH MONEY DO YOU HAVE?

**M33 oficial (libro pág 283-292):** "How Much Money Do You Have?" — Specifying Uncountable Quantities. Objetivo del libro: sin dudar, formar preguntas usando **"how much"** y responder correctamente, con vocabulario de sustancias incontables y sus unidades. (El libro pasa de Module 32 directo a Module 33 — **sin salto entre 32 y 33**, ver NOTA INTERNA.)

Vocabulario base (libro pág 284, Vocabulario 1):

```
M33 VOCABULARY (libro pág 284)
  a kilo            → un kilo
  a liter           → un litro
  a gallon          → un galón
  a bit of...       → un poco de...
  wine              → vino
  whisky            → whisky
  a glass           → un vaso
  a bottle          → una botella
```

```
CONCEPTO — Preguntar y responder CUÁNTO hay (sustancias INCONTABLES)

GUÍA 1 "How much?" (libro pág 285, reglas literales):

  1. "how much...?" significa "¿Cuánto...?"
  2. Se usa para sustancias INCONTABLES (líquidos, polvos, gases, etc.).

  Pregunta con palabra interrogativa:
     How much + [sustancia] + do + [sujeto] + have?
     → "How much milk do you have?" → "¿Cuánta leche tienes tú?"

  3. Se miden por UNIDADES:
     a. "I have 1 liter of milk."
     b. "I have 1 kilo of butter."
     c. "I have 300 pesos."  (la plata es incontable; pesos/dólares = las unidades)

  4. También con "there is / is there":
     → "How much milk is there?" → "¿Cuánta leche hay?"

  5. "a lot of" / "lots of" = "mucho" (incontables)
     → "I have a lot of money. / I have lots of money."

  6. "a bit of..." = "un poco de..."
     → "I have a bit of money."

RESPUESTAS (libro pág 288-292):
  - unidad exacta:  "There are 4 litres of milk."  "There are 2 kilos of cheese."
  - dinero (unidad):"There are sixty-seven dollars."  "She has ninety-five dollars."
  - mucho:          "There is a lot of ice cream. / lots of pizza."
  - un poco:        "There is a bit of coffee."  "He has a bit of broccoli."
```

**Conexión M32 → M33:**
- Cl 30 M30: *"Is X tall enough? — He's too tall."* (si la cualidad LLEGA o EXCEDE el nivel)
- Cl 31 M32: *"How many people are there? — There are a few."* (CANTIDAD de cosas CONTABLES)
- HOY M33: *"How much money do you have? — I have a bit."* (CANTIDAD de sustancias INCONTABLES, medidas por unidades)

> M33 NO es contable: mide CANTIDAD de sustancias incontables por unidades. Si dicen *"How many milk...?"* → corta: con incontables es **how much** (los contables fueron M32, otra clase). Si dicen *"How much moneys / dollars do you have?"* contando el sustantivo → corta: la plata es incontable; las **unidades** (dollars, pesos) son las que se cuentan. Si dicen *"How much milk you have?"* → corta: falta el ayudante (*"do you have?"* / *"is there?"*).

## 6. LIBRO M33 — HOW MUCH + VOCABULARIO (00:23–00:38, 15 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO:** *"Open Module 33, page 283. How much money do you have? We stop counting countable things — now we measure UNcountable substances by units: 'How much ___ do you have? / is there?' and answer with a unit (liter/kilo/gallon/dollars), 'a lot of / lots of', or 'a bit of'."*

### Vocabulario (5 min)

```
M33 VOCABULARY (libro pág 284)
a kilo  = un kilo        a liter = un litro
a gallon = un galón      a bit of... = un poco de...
wine = vino              whisky = whisky
a glass = un vaso        a bottle = una botella
```

Lee. Repiten 2x. Énfasis: "liter" /ˈliːtər/, "gallon" /ˈɡælən/, "a bit of" /əˈbɪtəv/ (la "t" enlaza), "whisky" /ˈwɪski/.

### Concepto (10 min)

**Tablero:**

```
HOW MUCH...? = ¿Cuánto...? (only UNCOUNTABLE: milk, money, coffee)
Needs a helper. Measured by UNITS.

  How much + [substance] + do + [subject] + have?
     "How much milk do you have?"
  How much + [substance] + is there?
     "How much milk is there?"

ANSWERS:
  unit     → "There are 4 litres of milk."  "There are 2 kilos of cheese."
  money    → "There are sixty-seven dollars."  (money = uncountable;
             dollars/pesos = the UNITS)
  a lot of → "There is a lot of ice cream. / lots of pizza."
  a bit of → "There is a bit of coffee."  (a little)

NO "how many" here (that is countable — M32, last class).
M33 is NOT count of items — it is amount of a substance, by units.
```

**Ejemplos modelados** (libro pág 288-292, inserción #1 Frase del Día):
- *"How much milk is there? — There are 4 litres of milk."* (unidad, pág 288)
- *"How much money is there? — There are sixty-seven dollars."* (dinero = unidad, pág 288)
- *"How much coffee is there? — There is a bit of coffee."* (a bit of, pág 288)
- *"How much ice cream is there? — There is a lot of ice cream."* (a lot of, pág 288)
- *"How much money does Doris have? — She has ninety-five dollars."* (con "have", pág 290)
- *"How much help do you have? Not a lot — so a fair person gives a bit of time to whoever needs it."* ← Frase del Día

## ⚠️ NOTA SOBRE EL CONTENIDO DE LOS MÓDULOS

**M39 oficial del libro (línea 22137):** "Mine, All Mine!" — Possessive Pronouns.

Distinción clave:
- **Possessive ADJECTIVES** (ya visto M15 A1): my, your, his, her, our, their + NOUN → *"This is MY pen."*
- **Possessive PRONOUNS** (M39 hoy): mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs (sin noun, reemplazan al noun) → *"This pen is MINE."*

| Subject | Object | Possessive Adj | Possessive Pronoun |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | me | MY (book) | MINE |
| you | you | YOUR (book) | YOURS |
| he | him | HIS (book) | HIS (igual) |
| she | her | HER (book) | HERS |
| we | us | OUR (book) | OURS |
| they | them | THEIR (book) | THEIRS |

> Nota: HIS es igual como adjetivo Y como pronombre. Es la única excepción.

**M40 NO EXISTE** (saltado entre M39 y M41) — confirmado en memoria.

**M41 oficial del libro (línea 22871):** "This Is Interesting!" — adjective endings -ED vs -ING.

Distinción clave:
- **-ED** describe cómo te SIENTES → *"I am BORED."* (yo me siento aburrido)
- **-ING** describe la cosa/persona que CAUSA el sentimiento → *"The movie is BORING."* (la película causa aburrimiento)

| Sentimiento | -ED (cómo te sientes) | -ING (la cosa que lo causa) |
|---|---|---|
| boredom | bored | boring |
| excitement | excited | exciting |
| interest | interested | interesting |
| confusion | confused | confusing |
| surprise | surprised | surprising |
| frustration | frustrated | frustrating |
| tiredness | tired | tiring |
| relaxation | relaxed | relaxing |

Frase clave: *"I am INTERESTED in this topic because it is INTERESTING."*

## 8. LIBRO M39 — POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS + SPEED DRILL (00:30–00:50, 20 min) — SENTADO

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 39. Today: WHERE we left the noun out — and what word replaces it."*

### Concepto (8 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
M39 — POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS

POSSESSIVE ADJECTIVE → POSSESSIVE PRONOUN
(M15 review)            (M39 today)

MY book      →  MINE          "The book is MINE."
YOUR book    →  YOURS         "Is this YOURS?"
HIS book     →  HIS (igual)   "It's HIS."
HER book     →  HERS          "Hers is on the table."
OUR book     →  OURS          "OURS arrived first."
THEIR book   →  THEIRS        "THEIRS is broken."

NEVER say: "mine book" — that doesn't exist
Say: "my book" (adjective) OR "the book is mine" (pronoun)
```

**Ejemplos modelados:**

- *"This pen is mine."* (no 'pen' after mine — eso lo distingue del adjetivo)
- *"Is that yours?"* (no noun after yours)
- *"His is bigger than mine."* (his = pronoun aquí)
- *"Ours is in the car."*

> **Inserción #1 Frase del Día:** *"As today's phrase says — 'This pen is MINE, that book is HERS, and those keys are THEIRS.'"*

### Speed Drill (12 min)

**Drill 1 (5 min) — Coro:**

Tú das un adjetivo + noun. Cohorte responde con la pronombre version:

```
"It's MY pen" → "It's MINE"
"This is YOUR seat" → "This is YOURS"
"That is HER book" → "That is HERS"
"OUR car" → "OURS"
"THEIR house" → "THEIRS"
(siguen 10 más)
```

**Drill 2 (7 min) — Parejas:**

## 10. LIBRO M41 — -ED vs -ING ADJECTIVES (01:15–01:40, 25 min) — SENTADO

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 41 (skipping M40 which doesn't exist in the book). Today: a tiny but DEADLY distinction. -ED tells how YOU feel. -ING tells what THE THING is like."*

### Concepto (10 min)

**Tablero:**

```
M41 — ADJECTIVE ENDINGS -ED vs -ING

-ED describes how YOU FEEL (the experiencer)
-ING describes what causes the feeling (the source)

EXAMPLES — same root, different meaning:

I am BORED                    The movie is BORING
(I feel the boredom)          (the movie causes boredom)

I am EXCITED                  The trip is EXCITING
(I feel excited)              (the trip causes excitement)

I am INTERESTED              The book is INTERESTING
(I have interest)             (the book is interesting)

I am CONFUSED                 The instructions are CONFUSING
I am SURPRISED                The news is SURPRISING
I am FRUSTRATED              This problem is FRUSTRATING
I am TIRED                    The work is TIRING
I am RELAXED                  Yoga is RELAXING


COMMON ERROR:
❌ "I am very boring today."   (you're saying YOU bore others!)
✅ "I am very BORED today."

❌ "This movie is interested."  (movies don't have feelings)
✅ "This movie is INTERESTING."
```

### Ejercicios del libro (10 min)

Libro M41 ejercicios. Énfasis en distinción persona/cosa.

### Speed Drill (5 min)

Tú dice una situación, cohorte completa con -ED o -ING:

```
"The movie...?" → "is BORING"
"I... after the meeting" → "am TIRED"
"This puzzle is..." → "CONFUSING"
"My weekend was very..." → "RELAXING"
"She was... by the surprise" → "SURPRISED"
"This homework is..." → "FRUSTRATING"
```

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"By the way, are these phrases INTERESTING? Or am I making you BORED?"* (modela uso natural)

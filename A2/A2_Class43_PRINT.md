## 6. SYSTEMATIC A — COMPARATIVES — 13 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the comparative error that blocks B1: choosing between '-er than' (short), 'more ... than' (long), and 'as ... as' (equal). NEVER both -er and more together."*

### Concept (9 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
A) COMPARATIVES   [review/fix]

SHORT (1 syllable)  -> -ER + THAN
  tall  -> TALLER than          fast  -> FASTER than
  old   -> OLDER than           big   -> BIGGER than (double g!)

LONG (3+ syll. / many 2)  -> MORE + adj + THAN
  expensive -> MORE EXPENSIVE than
  difficult -> MORE DIFFICULT than
  reliable  -> MORE RELIABLE than

EQUALITY -> AS + adj + AS
  "as fast as"     "as good as"     "as expensive as"

IRREGULARS (memorize):
  good -> BETTER      bad -> WORSE      far -> FARTHER/FURTHER

RULE: never put MORE in front of an -ER word.
RULE: never forget THAN after a comparative.
```

**Canonical typical errors (from previous A2 guide banks, NO names):**

- ❌ *"This is more better."* → ✅ *"This is BETTER."* (irregular, no "more")
- ❌ *"He is more taller than me."* → ✅ *"He is TALLER than me."* (short = -er, no "more")
- ❌ *"This product is cheap that one."* → ✅ *"This product is CHEAPER THAN that one."* (missing "than")
- ❌ *"Mine is so good as yours."* → ✅ *"Mine is AS GOOD AS yours."* (equality = as...as, not "so...as")
- ❌ *"This is more easier."* → ✅ *"This is EASIER."* (short = -er, no "more")

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a pair of things (*"option A / option B"*) + an adjective (*"fast / expensive / good / big / reliable"*); cohort produces the correct sentence in chorus. 6–8 items mixing short, long, irregulars, plus 2 equality (as...as).

## 7. SYSTEMATIC B — SUPERLATIVES — 10 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"One contrast that blocks B1: 'the tallest' (short) vs 'the most expensive' (long). And ALWAYS with THE. Never 'the more big.'"*

### Concept (7 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
B) SUPERLATIVES   [review/fix]

SHORT  -> THE + -EST
  tall  -> THE TALLEST          fast  -> THE FASTEST
  old   -> THE OLDEST           big   -> THE BIGGEST

LONG  -> THE MOST + adj
  expensive -> THE MOST EXPENSIVE
  difficult -> THE MOST DIFFICULT
  reliable  -> THE MOST RELIABLE

IRREGULARS:  good -> THE BEST     bad -> THE WORST

RULE: always THE. "She is THE biggest" (not "She is biggest").
RULE: never "the more" + adjective.
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"She is the more big in the team."* → ✅ *"She is THE BIGGEST in the team."* (short = the + -est)
- ❌ *"This is most expensive product."* → ✅ *"This is THE MOST EXPENSIVE product."* (missing "the")
- ❌ *"He is the most tall."* → ✅ *"He is THE TALLEST."* (short = the + -est)
- ❌ *"It is the better."* → ✅ *"It is THE BEST."* (irregular: best)
- ❌ *"Of all the options, this is more good."* → ✅ *"Of all the options, this is THE BEST."* (superlative, not comparative)

### Guided practice (3 min)

Board: give a group (*"of all the products / in the team / of these tools"*) + an adjective; cohort produces the correct sentence with "the + -est" or "the most + adj". 6 items.

## 8. SYSTEMATIC C — GERUND vs INFINITIVE — 14 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — we CORRECT the decision error that blocks B1: after ENJOY/AVOID/FINISH = verb + -ING; after WANT/NEED/DECIDE = TO + base. Never 'I enjoy to read.' Never 'I want studying.'"*

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
C) GERUND vs INFINITIVE   [review/fix]

GROUP -ING  (after these verbs, use verb + -ING)
  ENJOY      AVOID       FINISH       MIND
  SUGGEST    KEEP        PRACTICE
  examples:
    "I ENJOY READING."        "She FINISHED WORKING."
    "They AVOID DRIVING at night."   "I PRACTICE SPEAKING English."

GROUP TO + base  (after these verbs, use TO + base verb)
  WANT       NEED        DECIDE       PLAN
  LEARN      HOPE        WOULD LIKE
  examples:
    "I WANT TO READ."         "She NEEDS TO WORK."
    "They DECIDED TO STAY."   "I LEARNED TO DRIVE."

RULE: enjoy / avoid / finish + -ing  (NEVER + to)
RULE: want / need / decide + to + base  (NEVER + -ing)
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"I enjoy to read."* → ✅ *"I enjoy READING."* (enjoy + -ing)
- ❌ *"I want studying English."* → ✅ *"I want TO STUDY English."* (want + to)
- ❌ *"I need going home."* → ✅ *"I need TO GO home."* (need + to)
- ❌ *"She finished to clean."* → ✅ *"She finished CLEANING."* (finish + -ing)
- ❌ *"They decided going."* → ✅ *"They decided TO GO."* (decide + to + base)

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Compare with care, choose with reason — enjoy + ING or want + TO. One form, not the other."*

### Guided practice (4 min)

Board: give a main verb + a base verb (*"enjoy / read"* → *"I enjoy reading"* / *"want / read"* → *"I want to read"*); cohort answers in chorus. 6–8 items mixing both groups.

## 9. SPEED DRILL — 3 SUB-CLUSTERS AT SPEED — 13 min

### Drill 1 — Fast chorus (7 min) — STAND UP

You give cue + adjective/verb + hint (A = comparative · B = superlative · C = gerund/infinitive). Cohort answers with full sentence:

```
"option A vs B / fast" / A         → "Option A is FASTER than option B."
"product X vs Y / expensive" / A   → "Product X is MORE EXPENSIVE than product Y."
"mine / yours / good" (equal) / A  → "Mine is AS GOOD AS yours."
"this / that / good" / A           → "This is BETTER than that." (irregular)
"this / that / easy" / A           → "This is EASIER than that." (NOT "more easier")
"in the team / tall" / B           → "She is THE TALLEST in the team."
"of all / expensive" / B           → "This is THE MOST EXPENSIVE."

"I enjoy / read" / C               → "I enjoy READING." (NOT "to read")
"I want / read" / C                → "I want TO READ." (NOT "reading")
"I need / go home" / C             → "I need TO GO home."
"she finished / clean" / C         → "She finished CLEANING."
"they decided / stay" / C          → "They decided TO STAY."
"they avoid / drive at night" / C  → "They avoid DRIVING at night."
```

## 10. SIMULATION — "THE PERFORMANCE REVIEW" — 17 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You are a manager doing a performance review of 2–3 team members. You COMPARE them (X is more productive than Y; Z is the most reliable; A is as good as B) and then RECOMMEND next steps with the right verb form (I suggest promoting X; we need to train Z; I decided to reassign Y; we should keep developing A). Comparatives + superlatives + gerund/infinitive — all in one professional scene, under light pressure."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — the 3 forced forms + 2 cases:

```
THE PERFORMANCE REVIEW FORCES (3 sub-clusters):
- Comparatives  → "X is FASTER than Y." / "Z is MORE RELIABLE than A."
                  "A is AS GOOD AS B."
- Superlatives  → "X is THE FASTEST in the team."
                  "Z is THE MOST RELIABLE of all."
- Decisions     → "I suggest PROMOTING X."   (suggest + -ing)
                  "We need TO TRAIN Z."      (need + to)
                  "I decided TO REASSIGN Y." (decide + to)
                  "Keep DEVELOPING A."       (keep + -ing)

CASE 1 — Store / shop                CASE 2 — Office / customer service
- compare 3 sellers' performance     - compare 3 agents' performance
- "who is the most reliable?"        - "who closes the most tickets?"
- "who is faster at closing?"        - "who is better with clients?"
- recommend: promote / train /       - recommend: promote / train /
  reassign / keep developing           reassign / keep developing
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"Performance review for the store team: Carlos is FASTER than Andres at closing sales. Andres is MORE RELIABLE than Mateo with the inventory. Of all three, Carlos is THE BEST seller, but Andres is THE MOST CONSISTENT. I suggest PROMOTING Carlos to senior seller. We need TO TRAIN Mateo more on inventory. I decided TO KEEP Andres on the morning shift — he is as good as anyone else there. We should keep DEVELOPING the whole team."*

**Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good performance review sounds like today's phrase — compare with care, choose with reason: who is faster, who is the most reliable, and what do we decide to do next."*

### Round 1 (6 min) — Trios

Trios. One does the review (~2 min) using the 3 sub-clusters at least once each (≥2 comparatives + ≥1 superlative + ≥2 decisions with gerund/infinitive); the other 2 are coordination colleagues and each asks 1 question that forces a specific form (*"And who is the most experienced?" / "What do you suggest doing about training?"*). Rotate: each one does a review.

### Round 2 (3 min) — New scenario

Switch cases (store → office, or vice versa). New review, same 3 sub-clusters forced.

### Close (2 min)

1 trio in front of the group: 60 sec review + 30 sec Q&A. Cohort counts how many of the 3 forms appeared correctly (≥2 comparatives, ≥1 superlative, ≥2 gerund/infinitive). Note errors with NAMES.

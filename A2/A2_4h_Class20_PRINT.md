### M43 oficial del libro (página 340): "How Do You Do?" — Adverbs of Manner

**Habilidades objetivas (libro pág 340):**
- Sin dudar, formar y usar los adverbios de manera en la ubicación correcta.
- Sin dudar, distinguir entre adverbios de frecuencia y adverbios de manera y colocar ambos correctamente en una oración.

Distinción clave (libro pág 342, Guía 1):
- El adverbio de manera responde a **"How…?"** — *"How do you drive?"*
- La mayoría se forma con **adjetivo + "-ly"** (= "-mente" en español): *sad → sadly*, *happy → happily* (la "-y" cambia a "-ily").
- **Irregulares:** *fast → fast* (NO existe "fastly"), *good → well*, *hard → hard*.
- **Ubicación:** después del verbo principal, o después del objeto directo si lo hay: *"I play **well**." / "He plays soccer **well**."*
- **Adverbio de manera vs. de frecuencia:** *"I **always** play **well**."* — frecuencia antes del verbo, manera después.

**Ejemplos del libro (pág 343) — usar estos, NO inventar:**

| Adjetivo | Adverbio de manera (libro) |
|---|---|
| He is a good player. | He plays **well**. |
| He is a bad player. | He plays **badly**. |
| James has a nice dog. | The dog plays **nicely**. |
| William is an intelligent student. | William studies **intelligently**. |
| I have an early class. | My class starts **early**. |
| John is a serious doctor. | John is speaking **seriously**. |
| Helena wrote a beautiful story. | Helena writes **beautifully**. |
| Philip has a fast car. | Philip drives **fast**. |
| I heard a sudden noise. | **Suddenly**, I heard a noise. |
| Sebastian was a careful doctor. | Sebastian worked **carefully**. |
| Mike speaks correct English. | Mike speaks English **correctly**. |
| Edgar has a slow dog. | Edgar's dog runs **slowly**. |
| You have a hard game. | You play **hard**. |
| The class is together. | The class worked **together**. |

### M44 oficial del libro (página 347): "I Want You to Go" — The Active Causative

**Habilidades objetivas (libro pág 347):**
- Sin dudar, formar y usar frases de volición en el afirmativo, negativo e interrogativo.
- Sin dudar, distinguir entre las frases en las cuales NO hay un cambio de sujeto. (*"I want to go."* vs. *"I want you to go."*)

Distinción clave (libro pág 348, Guía 1):
- Para decir "quiero que OTRA persona haga algo", **NO** se usa *"I want that you go"* (calco del español — INCORRECTO).
- La forma es: **Sujeto + verbo de volición + otra persona (pron. objeto) + verbo principal (infinitivo) + complemento.**
  - *I want **you to go** to the store.*
  - *You want **me to go** to the store.*
  - *I need **the teacher to teach** the class.*
  - *The teacher would like **the student to study** the guides.*
- Interrogativo y negativo funcionan normal.
- Estas frases **sólo expresan deseos**, no órdenes ni solicitudes.

**Ejemplos del libro (pág 349) — usar estos, NO inventar:**

| English (libro) | Español |
|---|---|
| I want a job. | Quiero un trabajo. |
| I want to get a job. | Quiero conseguir un trabajo. |
| I want **you to get** a job. | Quiero que consigas un trabajo. |
| You need to buy a car. | Necesitas comprar un auto. |
| You need **me to buy** a car. | Necesitas que compre un auto. |
| Anita would like to give a flower. | A Anita le gustaría dar una flor. |
| Anita would like **you to give** her a flower. | Anita quiere que le des una flor. |
| Brayan wants **us to play** soccer. | Brayan quiere que juguemos fútbol. |
| Connie needs **you to talk** to her. | Connie necesita que hables con ella. |
| Eddy wants **Norman to see** the new house. | Eddy quiere que Norman vea la nueva casa. |
| The teacher needs **you to hand in** your test. | El profesor necesita que entregues tu examen. |
| Your friends want **you to play** with them. | Tus amigos quieren que juegues con ellos. |

**Practice Guide M44 (libro pág 351) — interrogativos con "want/need/would like" (usar estos):**
- *"Do you want to climb the tree?"* → *"Do you want your brother to climb the tree?"*
- *"Where do you want to go?"* → *"Where do you want us to go?"*
- *"When do you want your friends to go?"*
- *"What would you like the teachers to drink?"*
- *"When do you need me to finish the exam?"*
- *"What does the teacher need us to study today?"*
- *"Who do you want me to share with?"*

**Conexión M43 ↔ M44:** el adverbio de manera de M43 se incrusta naturalmente en el causativo de M44 → *"I want you to do it **well**." / "I need you to work **carefully**." / "She would like us to arrive **early**."* Ese es el puente que une los dos módulos hoy.

## 7. LIBRO M43 — ADVERBIOS DE MANERA (00:28–00:50, 22 min) — SENTADO

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 43, page 340. Today: not WHAT you do — HOW you do it. 'How do you drive? — I drive carefully.'"*

### Concepto (12 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
M43 — HOW DO YOU DO? (ADVERBS OF MANNER)

Answers "HOW?"   How do you drive? -> I drive CAREFULLY.

RULE: adjective + "-ly"
  careful  -> carefully       slow  -> slowly
  serious  -> seriously       sudden -> suddenly
  happy    -> happily   (-y becomes -ily)

IRREGULARS (memorize):
  good -> WELL      fast -> FAST (no "fastly")
  hard -> HARD

PLACE: after the main verb, or after the direct object
  I play WELL.        He plays soccer WELL.

MANNER vs FREQUENCY:
  I ALWAYS play WELL.   (frequency before verb, manner after)
```

**Ejemplos modelados (del libro, pág 343 — léelos tal cual):**

- *"He is a good player. → He plays **well**."*
- *"He is a bad player. → He plays **badly**."*
- *"William is an intelligent student. → William studies **intelligently**."*
- *"John is a serious doctor. → John is speaking **seriously**."*
- *"Helena wrote a beautiful story. → Helena writes **beautifully**."*
- *"Philip has a fast car. → Philip drives **fast**."*
- *"I heard a sudden noise. → **Suddenly**, I heard a noise."*
- *"Sebastian was a careful doctor. → Sebastian worked **carefully**."*
- *"You have a hard game. → You play **hard**."*

> **Inserción #1 Frase del Día:** *"As today's phrase says — 'I do hard things CAREFULLY.' That's two adverbs of manner: 'hard' (irregular) and 'carefully'."*

## 9. LIBRO M44 — EL CAUSATIVO ACTIVO "I WANT YOU TO…" (01:05–01:25, 20 min) — SENTADO

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 44, page 347. The difference between 'I want to go' (ME) and 'I want YOU to go' (someone else). Spanish 'quiero que tú vayas' is NOT 'I want that you go.'"*

### Concepto (12 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
M44 — I WANT YOU TO GO (THE ACTIVE CAUSATIVE)

WRONG: "I want that you go."   <-- never (calco del espanol)

PATTERN:
 SUBJECT + want/need/would like + PERSON + to + VERB + rest

  I    want        to go        -> me goes (no change of subject)
  I    want   YOU   to go       -> YOU goes (change of subject!)
  You  need   ME    to buy a car
  The teacher would like the student to study

NEGATIVE + QUESTION work normally:
  "I don't want you to go."
  "Do you want me to go?"

These only express WISHES - not orders, not requests.
```

**Ejemplos modelados (del libro, pág 349 — léelos tal cual):**

- *"I want a job. / I want to get a job. / I want **you to get** a job."* (los 3 contrastes)
- *"You need to buy a car. / You need **me to buy** a car."*
- *"Anita would like **you to give** her a flower."*
- *"Brayan wants **us to play** soccer."*
- *"Connie needs **you to talk** to her."*
- *"Eddy wants **Norman to see** the new house."*
- *"The teacher needs **you to hand in** your test."*
- *"Your friends want **you to play** with them."*

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"As today's phrase says — 'I want YOU to do them well too.' That's M44: a change of subject — and an adverb of manner from M43 inside it."*

### Práctica guiada (8 min)

Libro M44 ejercicios pág 350 + Practice Guide pág 351 (traducir; interrogativos). Resuelve 5-6 en voz alta. Énfasis: contrastar SIEMPRE las dos versiones:

- *"Quiero que me cuentes algo." → "I want you to tell me something."* (con cambio)
- *"Where do you want to go?"* vs *"Where do you want us to go?"*
- *"When do you need me to finish the exam?"*

Pide a 4-5 estudiantes AL AZAR transformar *"I want to + verb"* → *"I want + person + to + verb"*.

**Modelo:**

> *Tú: "It was a normal Monday at the office. My boss arrived and he spoke very [pause]..."*
> *Cohorte: "...he spoke very SLOWLY!" / "...LOUDLY!" (repiten la frase completa)*
>
> *Tú: "Then he looked at me and said: 'I want you to [pause]...'"*
> *Cohorte: "I want you to FINISH the report!" / "...to WORK FAST!"*
>
> *Tú: "I tried to work, but the computer started [pause]..."*
> *Cohorte: "...working WRONG!" / "...running SLOWLY!"*
>
> *Tú: "Suddenly the boss said he needs all of us to [pause]..."*
> *Cohorte: "...he needs us to STAY late!" / "...to do it CAREFULLY!"*

8-9 min narrativa interactiva. Mantén el absurdo cotidiano (un día de oficina que se descontrola), NUNCA fantasía. Tú anotas errores con NOMBRE.

## 11. SIMULACIÓN PROFESIONAL — "PERFORMANCE REVIEW" (01:37–02:02, 25 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Real workplace scene. A short performance review. The manager describes HOW the work was done (adverbs) and says what they WANT the employee to do next (causative)."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Reunión breve de evaluación de desempeño en un trabajo real (oficina, tienda, restaurante, call center). El gerente comenta cómo se hizo el trabajo usando adverbios de manera ("You worked **hard** but you answered the phone **slowly**") y luego dice qué quiere para el próximo mes usando el causativo ("I want you to **arrive early**. I need you to **speak more clearly**."). El empleado responde y también pide algo ("I would like you to **explain the goals more carefully**.").

**Roles:**
- A = gerente/supervisor (evalúa con adverbios de manera + da deseos con "I want/need you to…")
- B = empleado (responde, se compromete, y también pide algo con el causativo)

### Modela (3 min)

> *Tú (gerente): "Last month you worked **hard** and you learned **fast**. But you answered customers a little **slowly** and you closed the register **incorrectly** twice."*
> *Peer teacher (empleado): "Thank you. What do you want me to improve?"*
> *Tú: "I want you to **arrive early** and I need you to **count carefully**."*
> *Empleado: "Understood. And I would like you to **train me more slowly** at first."*

### Round 1 (10 min) — Parejas

Cada pareja: ~5 min cada uno como gerente. El gerente da mínimo 4 adverbios de manera + mínimo 3 frases "I want/need you to…". El empleado responde y pide al menos 1 cosa con el causativo.

### Round 2 (6 min) — Roles cambian + nuevo trabajo

Cambian de rol y de escenario (de tienda a oficina, o a restaurante). Nueva evaluación.

### Cierre (3 min)

1 pareja representa frente al grupo. Aplauso. Cohorte cuenta cuántos adverbios de manera + cuántos causativos correctos escuchó. Anotas errores con NOMBRE.

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"A real review sounds like the phrase — 'I do hard things carefully, and I want you to do them well too.'"*

## 12. SIMULACIÓN INTEGRADA M43+M44 — "WHO DOES WHAT, AND HOW?" (02:02–02:25, 23 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Integration. You're organizing a real task with a team. You assign WHO does WHAT (causative) and you say HOW it must be done (adverb of manner)."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Organizar una tarea real en equipo — un evento pequeño, abrir una tienda, preparar una entrega, planear un viaje de trabajo. Cada persona reparte tareas usando el causativo Y especificando la manera: *"I need you to call the client **early**. I want María to check the numbers **carefully**. I'd like us to finish **fast** but **right**."*

### Vocabulario forzado

- **M44:** mínimo 5 frases causativas con cambio de sujeto ("I want/need/would like + persona + to + verbo"), al menos 2 verbos de volición distintos
- **M43:** cada frase causativa debe llevar un adverbio de manera (well/fast/hard/carefully/early/slowly/together…)

### Modela (3 min)

> *"OK team — for the event: I need you to set up the chairs **carefully**. I want Carlos to welcome people **warmly**. I'd like the music team to start **on time** and play **softly** at first. And I want all of us to finish **together**, not alone."*

### Round 1 (9 min) — Grupos de 3

Trios. Una persona es el organizador 3 min; reparte tareas a los otros 2 con causativo + adverbio. Rotan: cada uno organiza una vez. Los otros responden ("OK, I'll do it carefully" / "Do you want me to start now?").

### Round 2 (5 min) — Grupos rotan

Nuevos trios, nuevo escenario (otra tarea cotidiana/profesional). Repetir el patrón.

### Cierre (3 min)

1 trio representa frente al grupo. Cohorte vota: ¿cuántas frases causativas + cuántos adverbios de manera escucharon? Anotas errores con NOMBRE.

## 15. ERROR PAPER LIVE + FRASE DEL DÍA CIERRE (02:57–03:05, 8 min) — DE PIE

### Error Paper Live (5 min)

**Protocolo doble:** en clase los 4 errores van ANÓNIMOS en el papel físico; en tu libreta personal aparte van con NOMBRE + cita literal para el reporte de coordinación.

**4 errores típicos:**

```
1. ❌ "He drives fastly."          → ✅ "He drives FAST." (irregular: no "fastly")
2. ❌ "She speaks good English."   → ✅ "She speaks English WELL." (manera = well, no "good")
3. ❌ "I want that you go."        → ✅ "I want YOU to go." (causativo, NO "that")

```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT M14 WHILE / DURING / AGAIN (~52 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board el titulo y las REGLAS del libro
   (M14, p.121-122), tal cual:

   WHILE, DURING, AND AGAIN

   1) WHILE -> "mientras". Exactly like Spanish. It connects
      TWO COMPLETE clauses (subject + verb).
        "I studied while you worked."
        "I was studying while you were working."
   2) DURING -> "durante". Exactly like Spanish. It connects
      a phrase to an EVENT or PERIOD (a noun, not a clause).
        "I studied during the party."
        "I won't study during the night."
   3) AGAIN -> "otra vez / de nuevo". Exactly like Spanish.
      Usually at the END of the phrase (but can move with a
      time phrase).
        "I ate at 1:00. I ate again at 2:00. I will eat
         again at 4:00."

2. SEÑALA la diferencia clave. DI: "WHILE + a full clause
   (subject + verb). DURING + a thing/period (a noun, no
   verb). AGAIN goes at the end."

3. ESCRIBE EJEMPLOS del libro (p.123), tal cual:

      Richard visited his mother while she ate lunch.
      I lived in Canada during the war.
      I was in the park again on Monday.
      The girls ate lunch during the afternoon.
      Will you shower while I get dressed?
      I was helping my mom while you visited your friends.
      Was Fritz hanging out with Dale during the class?
      Beatriz was finishing the project while I was sleeping.
      My friends worked again at 7:30.
      When will you visit Canada again?

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "The phrase
   shows all three — one shares WHILE another rests, support
   DURING the hard days, and help AGAIN and again."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I studied while the party" (while + un
   sustantivo) -> ESCRIBE "I studied DURING the party." DI:
   "WHILE needs a verb. A thing/period takes DURING."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "We will eat while the children will
   take a taxi" (futuro doble con while) -> TACHA el segundo
   "will", ESCRIBE "We will eat while the children TAKE a
   taxi." DI: "After 'while' in the future, the second clause
   uses the PRESENT, not 'will'. Same as Spanish." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I again ate at 2:00" (again mal
   colocado) -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "I ate AGAIN at 2:00." DI:
   "Again usually goes at the end, near the time phrase."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. PRACTICA dirigida (oral): TU das la primera mitad, el
   grupo cierra con while / during / again:
        "I cooked ___ you served." (while)
        "We were busy ___ the rush." (during)
        "We cleaned the kitchen, and then we cleaned it ___."
          (again)
   8-10 vueltas, distintos estudiantes.

9. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien una dos personas con
   'while', DI suave: "That is justice — fair share. Two
   people, two jobs, at the same time. 'I cooked WHILE you
   served.'"

10. PIDELES en parejas: "Student A says what they did;
    Student B adds what they did at the same time with WHILE,
    or in a period with DURING, or that it happened AGAIN.
    Then switch. 6 exchanges. Theme: a team at work. 5 min."

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
    2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
    NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. ESCRIBE el uso continuo + pregunta (p.123):
        "Beatriz was finishing the project while I was
         sleeping."
        "Was Fritz hanging out with Dale during the class?"
    DI: "While loves two continuous actions at once. During
    takes a period. Again can ask: 'When will you visit
    again?'" Practica 4-5 vueltas en coro.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "During you slept, I cooked" (during +
    clausula) -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "WHILE you slept, I cooked."
    o "DURING the night, I cooked." DI: "A clause = WHILE.
    A period/noun = DURING." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas round 2: "Now a 4-line TEAM STORY of
    a busy day: 2 things that happened WHILE something else
    happened, 1 thing DURING a period, 1 thing you did
    AGAIN. Theme: a shift at work where the team shared the
    load. 5 minutes."

15. CIRCULA. CORRIGE cuando el mismo error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas.

16. CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo separe WHILE (clausula) de
    DURING (periodo) y coloque AGAIN al final. DI: "Stand up
    — we go to the simulation. Team coordination on a busy
    shift."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 32 min y aun confunden while/during, NO AVANCES al team story. Quedate en "WHILE + verbo / DURING + periodo" hasta que salga limpio; deja el team story para tarea. Mejor LAND uno bien que rush dos shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas usando WHILE (dos clausulas) + DURING (periodo) + AGAIN (al final) sin confundirlos.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: TEAM COORDINATION ON A BUSY SHIFT (~36 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "TEAM COORDINATION ON A BUSY SHIFT"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. Two TEAMMATES describe a busy
   shift to a supervisor (or to each other). In the whole
   conversation use, AT LEAST:
     - 3 'while' (two actions at once: 'I served WHILE you
       cooked')
     - 2 'during' (a period: 'It was full DURING the lunch
       hour')
     - 2 'again' (a repeated task: 'We cleaned table 5
       AGAIN')
   And give fair credit to the teammate (justice). Rotate."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This shift IS the phrase. One
   shares the work WHILE another rests, you support each
   other DURING the rush, and you help AGAIN and again."

4. ESCRIBE 2 mini-scenarios en board:

   SCENARIO 1 — Restaurant / cafe busy service
     "I took the orders while you cooked." "It was packed
     during the lunch rush." "Table 7 ordered again." "Maria
     refilled the drinks while I cleaned — thank you, Maria."

   SCENARIO 2 — Warehouse / delivery team
     "I loaded the truck while Carlos checked the list." "We
     were short-staffed during the morning." "The same client
     called again." "We unloaded everything together, again,
     after lunch."

5. PIDELES round 1 (5 min): pareja escoge un scenario.
   Roles claros. Empiezan describiendo el turno.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back: 'I did X WHILE you
   did Y', 'DURING the rush...', 'we did it AGAIN'. Don't
   freeze."

8. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 (5 min): cambien
   de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si alguien
   describe el turno como si trabajara solo, DI suave:
   "Justice. Who worked WHILE you worked? Name them. Fair
   share."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. OBSERVER COUNT (ligero): en round 2, asigna a 2
    estudiantes como observers — cuentan en silencio cuantos
    usos CORRECTOS de while/during/again oyeron en la pareja
    vecina + cuantos creditos justos al equipo. Reportan:
    "Pair X — 5 correct, 2 fair credits." Solo conteo.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (un turno completo + credito al equipo). COHORTE
    cuenta cuantos while/during/again correctos cazaron.
    Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What did you do WHILE your teammate worked?" / "What happened DURING the rush?" / "What did you have to do AGAIN?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 2 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente + observer count reportado.

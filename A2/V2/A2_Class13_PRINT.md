## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT M15 PRESENT PERFECT (REGULAR) (~52 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board el titulo y las REGLAS del libro
   (M15, p.133-134), tal cual:

   THE PRESENT PERFECT — REGULAR VERBS

   1) Unlike the past simple, we use the PRESENT PERFECT for
      actions done (or not done) in an INDEFINITE /
      unspecified period, or for UNFINISHED actions. (Note:
      despite its name, it is a PAST tense.)
   2) Form it with "to have" as the auxiliary (= "haber") +
      the PAST PARTICIPLE of the verb.
   3) Most participles (the "regular" ones) add "-ed" to the
      verb — same as the past simple, like Spanish "-ado /
      -ido".

        Subject + HAVE/HAS + participle (+ complement)
        "I have played hockey."  = Yo he jugado hockey.

   4) Use "have" normally; he / she / it use "HAS".

   FORMS:
        I have (not) played.   /  Have I played?
        He has (not) played.   /  Has he played?
        We have (not) played.  /  Have we played?

   ABBREVIATED:
        I've played. / I haven't played.
        He's played. / He hasn't played.

   SHORT ANSWER:
        "Have you visited London?"  "Yes, I have. / No, I
         haven't."
        "Has Valeria visited London?" "Yes, she has. / No,
         she hasn't."

2. SEÑALA la estructura. DI: "HAVE / HAS + verb + -ed. He,
   she, it = HAS. Everyone else = HAVE. The verb ALWAYS
   takes -ed (regular)."

3. ESCRIBE EJEMPLOS del libro (p.135), tal cual — pasado
   simple vs present perfect:

      I played soccer yesterday.    -> I've played soccer.
      You lived in Madrid last year. -> You've lived in Madrid.
      Jeff showered in the morning. -> Jeff has showered.
      We visited my parents last month. -> We've visited my
        parents.
      They helped the children.     -> They've helped the
        children.
      I didn't talk to Miguel.      -> I haven't talked to
        Miguel.
      Did you study last week?      -> Have you studied?
      Did Sandra visit the apartment? -> Has Sandra visited
        the apartment?

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "The phrase is
   the present perfect of justice — 'I have shared, I have
   helped, I have treated everyone as an equal.'"

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I have play hockey" (sin -ed) ->
   ESCRIBE "I have PLAYED hockey." DI: "The participle needs
   -ed: played, worked, visited, helped." COHORTE repite
   2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "He have played" (have con he) ->
   TACHA, ESCRIBE "He HAS played." DI: "He / she / it = HAS,
   never have." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "You have visited London?" (orden de
   pregunta) -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "HAVE you visited London?"
   DI: "Question: HAVE / HAS first, then the subject."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. PRACTICA dirigida (oral): TU das el sujeto + verbo, el
   grupo arma el present perfect:
        "I / work in an office -> 'I have worked in an
          office.'"
        "She / study English -> 'She has studied English.'"
        "They / help the team -> ?"
   8-10 vueltas, distintos estudiantes (afirm + neg).

9. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien diga "I have helped"
   o "I have shared", DI suave: "That is justice you can
   name. No date needed — it is part of your life."

10. PIDELES en parejas: "Student A asks 'Have you ...?' (a
    regular verb: worked, studied, visited, helped, lived);
    Student B answers with the short answer + one more
    sentence. Then switch. 6 exchanges. Theme: your
    experience. 5 minutes."

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
    2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
    NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. ESCRIBE la version negativa + short answer (p.134-145):
        "I haven't talked to the manager yet."
        "Have you finished the report?" "Yes, I have."
        "Has she helped the new person?" "No, she hasn't."
    DI: "Short answer = Yes/No + subject + have/has. Don't
    repeat the verb." Practica 4-5 vueltas en coro.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "Yes, I have visited" (short answer
    repitiendo el verbo) -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "Yes, I HAVE."
    DI: "Short answer stops at have/has. No verb." COHORTE
    repite 2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas round 2: "Now a 4-line SELF-INTRO of
    your experience for a job. 4 present perfect sentences
    with regular verbs (I have worked / studied / visited /
    helped). Theme: what you have done that is useful at
    work. 5 minutes."

15. CIRCULA. CORRIGE cuando el mismo error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas.

16. CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo arme have/has + verbo+ed y
    use el short answer sin repetir el verbo. DI: "Stand up
    — we go to the simulation. A job interview about your
    experience."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 32 min y aun olvidan -ed o usan have con he/she, NO AVANCES al self-intro. Quedate en "have/has + verb+ed" hasta que salga limpio; deja el self-intro para tarea. Mejor LAND uno bien que rush dos shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas armando have/has + participio -ed (afirm/neg/pregunta) + short answer sin repetir verbo.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: JOB INTERVIEW — YOUR EXPERIENCE (~36 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "JOB INTERVIEW — YOUR EXPERIENCE"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the INTERVIEWER asking
   'Have you ...?'; the other is the CANDIDATE answering with
   the present perfect (regular verbs). In the whole
   conversation use, AT LEAST:
     - 4 'Have you ...?' questions
     - 4 present perfect answers (I have worked / studied /
       visited / helped / lived)
     - 2 short answers (Yes, I have / No, I haven't)
   Mention one fair thing you have done for a team (justice).
   Then rotate roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "An interview IS this phrase.
   You name what you have done — 'I have helped, I have
   shared, I have treated people fairly.'"

4. ESCRIBE 2 mini-scenarios en board:

   SCENARIO 1 — Interview for a customer-service job
     Interviewer: "Have you worked with clients?" Candidate:
     "Yes, I have. I have worked in a store. I have helped
     many customers. I have studied basic English."

   SCENARIO 2 — Interview for a team / assistant role
     Interviewer: "Have you helped a team before?" Candidate:
     "Yes, I have. I have shared tasks fairly. I have
     supported new people. I haven't managed a budget yet."

5. PIDELES round 1 (5 min): pareja escoge un scenario.
   Roles claros. El interviewer arranca con "Have you ...?"

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back: 'I have worked...',
   'Yes, I have', 'I haven't ... yet'. Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 (5 min): cambien
   de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: cuando un
   candidato mencione ayudar a otros, DI suave: "Justice —
   name it clearly: 'I have helped the team. I have shared
   the work.' Fair experience counts."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. OBSERVER COUNT (ligero): en round 2, asigna a 2
    estudiantes como observers — cuentan en silencio cuantos
    present perfect CORRECTOS (have/has + -ed) + short answers
    bien hechos oyeron en la pareja vecina. Reportan: "Pair X
    — 5 correct, 2 short answers." Solo conteo.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (una mini-entrevista). COHORTE cuenta cuantos
    present perfect correctos cazaron. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Have you worked before?" / "Have you helped a team?" / "Have you studied English?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 2 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente + observer count reportado.

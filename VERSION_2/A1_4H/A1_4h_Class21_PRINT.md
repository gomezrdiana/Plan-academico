## BLOQUE 2 (90') — M33 presente continuo + M34 simple vs. continuo

- **Puente (3'):** el presente simple (Cl 5-6) = lo que haces SIEMPRE. Hoy: lo que estas haciendo AHORA MISMO (*I am playing right now*).
- **M33 — vocab (p.376):** take a bus · take a taxi · drive (conducir) · walk (caminar) · watch (mirar) · clean (limpiar). **Tablero verbatim (Guia 1, p.377):** el continuo = accion en este momento; se forma con *be* + verbo + *-ing* ("-ando/-iendo"):

```
I am playing.  You are playing.  He/She/It is playing.  We/They are playing.
Negativo: I am not playing.   Pregunta: Are you playing today?
Resp. corta: Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
Verbo termina en "e" -> se quita: Like -> Liking
A veces se dobla la consonante: Run -> Running
```

- **M34 — contraste (Guia 1, p.396):** el simple = habitual, el continuo = en este momento; hay que diferenciarlos: *"I play soccer, but I am playing golf today."*

```
USUALLY (simple)          TODAY / NOW (continuo)
I play at seven.          I'm playing at eight.
We live in the house.     We're living in the apartment.
He helps his father.      He's helping his mother.
Does he help his father?  Is he helping his mother?
```

- **Historia interactiva (25'):** "hoy no, hoy es diferente." Narras 2-3 frases con contraste (*"Every day I take a bus, but today I'm taking a taxi! She usually cleans the house, but she's watching a movie right now. What are you doing?"*) → PAUSA → coro repite → un estudiante AGREGA que hace SIEMPRE alguien vs que esta haciendo HOY y TU lo tejes. NUNCA huecos. Cierre: 2-3 recuentan.
- **Meta en 1 linea:** *"By the end of this block, each of you will say 2 things you USUALLY do (simple) and 2 things you are doing THESE DAYS / right now (continuous)."* Preparacion en parejas (6', oral) → ejecucion → feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

## BLOQUE 3 (85') — M35 going to + Simulacion (cierre de contenido)

- **M35 — vocab + regla (10'):** vocab (p.404): buy/bought (comprar) · feel/felt (sentirse) · travel (viajar) · come/came (venir) · meet/met (reunirse) · finish (terminar). **Tablero verbatim (Guia 1, p.405):** para eventos fijos del futuro, ingles usa *to be going to* (= "ir a"); es *go* en presente continuo + otro verbo:

```
I am going to play.   Are you going to play?   She is going to travel.
They are not going to come.   What are you going to do tomorrow?
Hablado (informal): "going to" -> "gonna" (NO se escribe): I'm gonna play.
```

- **Drill de movimiento (25'), TODOS DE PIE:**
  - Ronda 1 — continuo AHORA: tu das un verbo (*drive/walk/watch/clean*) y la cadena dice *"I'm driving right now."*; si esta bien, cambia de lugar.
  - Ronda 2 — *going to*: tu preguntas *"What are you going to do tomorrow?"* y la cadena responde (*"I'm going to study. I'm going to meet my friends."*).
  - Ronda 3 — mezcla: *"What do you usually do? What are you doing now? What are you going to do tomorrow?"* Cierre: 2 min de produccion libre.
- **Simulacion (40'): THE WEEKEND PLAN — CATCHING UP.** Escenario cotidiano solo con tablero y sala: dos personas se ponen al dia — que estan haciendo estos dias y que van a hacer el fin de semana. Tablero: verbos (*travel, buy, meet, finish, watch, clean*) + tiempos (*right now, today, tomorrow, next week*).
  - Al frente: **1 estudiante cuenta sus planes (guest)** + 1 el que pregunta. El que pregunta usa (*"What are you doing these days? What are you going to do this weekend?"*); el guest responde con continuo + *going to* (*"I'm studying English. I'm going to travel to my family's city next week."*).
  - El RESTO son OBSERVERS ACTIVOS: 2 errores (sobre todo *-ing* o *going to* + verbo), 1 acierto, 1 pregunta.
  - TU eres COACH desde afuera: NUNCA juegas guest ni el que pregunta. Congelados 15+ seg → UN prompt y te retiras.
  - Minimos por ronda: 3 con presente continuo + 2 con *going to* + 1 con presente simple (habitual).
  - Rotacion cada 4-5 min. Debrief (5'): observers reportan (anonimo); correccion clave — *"I going to study"* → *"I'm going to study,"* coro 2x.
- **MY WORLD — avance de proyecto hoy: pieza 19 (FINAL) "MY NOW AND MY PLANS".** Cada estudiante dice al grupo: 2 cosas que esta haciendo estos dias (*"I'm studying English. I'm reading a book."*) + 2 planes con *going to* (*"I'm going to finish this level. I'm going to travel next year."*). Con esta pieza, MY WORLD queda COMPLETO (19 piezas) para presentarlo entero en la proxima clase.

## ANEXO — M33 + M34 + M35 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Module 33 — "What Are You Doing? / The Continuous" (p.375-394). Objetivos:** vocab; formar frases/preguntas con presente continuo; distinguir simple vs continuo. **Vocab (p.376):** take a bus · take a taxi · drive · walk · watch · clean. **Guia 1 (p.377):** (1) el simple = habitual, el continuo = acciones en el presente momento (*"I play soccer, but I am playing golf today."*); (2) se forma con *be* + verbo + *-ing*; (3) verbo en "e" → se quita para agregar *-ing* (Like → Liking); (4) a veces se dobla la consonante (Run → Running); (5) abreviaciones y respuesta corta iguales a *be*. **Ejemplos (p.378):** *"Are you taking a bus today? — No, I'm taking a taxi." · "What are the girls watching? — They're watching Harry Potter." · "What are you cleaning? — I am cleaning my house." · "What are you making? — I'm making breakfast."*

**Module 34 — "Usually, but not Today / Present Simple vs. Present Continuous" (p.395-402). Objetivo:** diferenciar y usar ambos. **Sin vocabulario nuevo.** **Guia 1 (p.396):** el simple = habitual, el continuo = en el presente momento; es muy importante diferenciarlos (*"I play soccer, but I am playing golf today."*). **Ejemplos (p.397-402):** *"I play at seven o'clock. / I'm playing at eight o'clock." · "We live in the house. / We're living in the apartment." · "Does he help his father? / Is he helping his mother?"*

**Module 35 — "'Going to' and 'Gonna' / Another Form of the Future" (p.403-416) — ULTIMO modulo del libro. Objetivos:** vocab; formar frases/preguntas con *going to*. **Vocab (p.404):** buy (bought) · feel (felt) · travel · come (came) · meet (met) · finish. **Guia 1 (p.405), reglas tal cual:**
1. Para eventos fijos en el futuro, el espanol usa "ir a" y el ingles "to be going to".
2. La construccion es *go* en presente continuo + un verbo adicional: *"I am (not) going to play. / Am I going to play?"* (las 7 personas).
3. "going to" a menudo sale rapido como "gonna": comun en conversacion, NO en trabajo escrito (*"I'm going to play." → "I'm gonna play."*).
**Ejemplos (p.406):** *"Are you going to be a teacher? — Yes, I'm going to be a teacher." · "What are you going to do tomorrow? — I'm going to study in the morning and find a class in the afternoon." · "Who are they going to call? — They are going to call their friends." · "When are we gonna go to the restaurant? — We are gonna go at 12:00."*

**Plan B:**

- Si sale *"I going to study"* / *"I studying"*: falta el *be* — regla fisica: SIEMPRE *am/is/are* antes del *-ing* y antes de *going to*; coro *"I'm studying / I'm going to study"* 2x.
- Si confunden simple vs continuo: *usually* = simple (dibuja un calendario), *right now / today* = continuo (senala el piso "aqui-ahora"); coro contrastes 2x.

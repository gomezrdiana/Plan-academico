## BLOQUE 2 (85') — M17 "Have" + a/an/some/any

- **Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.210):** Money (dinero) · A question (una pregunta) · Pasta · A sandwich · Vegetables (verduras) · Fruit (fruta) · Ice cream (helado) · Water (agua) · Soda (gaseosa) · Coffee (cafe). Coro + individual.
- **Tablero verbatim (M17, Guia 1, p.211):**
1. En presente afirmativo, para he/she/it NO decimos "haves" sino **has** (pasado: *had*). *He has a car. — He doesn't have a car. — Does he have a car?*
2. Para UN elemento singular: *a/an*. *I have an apple. — I don't have an apple. — Do I have an apple?*
3. Para PLURALES o INCONTABLES: *some* en afirmativo, *any* en negativo e interrogativo. *I have some apples. — I don't have any apples. — Do I have any apples?*
4. *for* = para. 5. *or* = o. 6. *only* = solo (antes del verbo principal o despues de *to be*). 7. *too* = tambien (al final).

```
                Affirmative        Negative              Question
Singular (a/an) I have a sandwich  I don't have a ...    Do I have a ...?
Plural/uncount. I have some water  I don't have any ...  Do I have any ...?
```

- **Historia interactiva (25'):** "la nevera del edificio." Narras 2-3 frases con *have + some/any* + comida (*"I have some pizza. She has a sandwich, too. The dog has some coffee — but it doesn't have any water!"*) → PAUSA → coro repite → un estudiante AGREGA que tiene o no tiene alguien y TU lo tejes; luego lo vuelves PREGUNTA (*"Do you have any soda?"*). NUNCA huecos. Cierre: 2-3 recuentan.
- **Instalacion some/any (12') DE PIE:** tu das una afirmativa (*"I have some fruit."*) → la cadena la vuelve negativa (*"I don't have any fruit."*) y pregunta (*"Do you have any fruit?"*). Contraste *a/an* (singular) vs *some/any* (plural/incontable). 5 individuales.
- **Meta en 1 linea:** *"By the end of this block, each of you will ask and answer 2 questions with 'Do you have any...?' about real food or things you have."* Preparacion en parejas (6', oral) → ejecucion → feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

## ANEXO — M17 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A1, Module 17 — "Do You Have Any Pizza? — 'Have' and Indefinite Adjectives" (p.209-222). Habilidades objetivas:** "Sin dudar, conjugar y usar el verbo 'have' correctamente. Sin dudar, diferenciar entre los adjetivos indefinidos 'any' y 'some' y formar frases afirmativas, negativas e interrogativas."

**Vocabulario (p.210):** Money (dinero) · A question · Pasta · A sandwich · Vegetables (verduras) · Fruit · Ice cream (helado) · Water · Soda (gaseosa) · Coffee.

**Guia 1 (p.211), reglas tal cual:**
1. En presente afirmativo, para "he/she/it" no decimos "haves" sino "has" (pasado: "had"). *Do he have a car? → Does he have a car? · He has a car.*
2. Para cantidades singulares, usamos "a/an" antes del objeto. *I have an apple. — I don't have an apple. — Do I have an apple?*
3. Para elementos PLURALES O INCONTABLES: "some" en afirmativo, "any" en negativo e interrogativo (parecido a "alguna/ninguna"). *I have some apples / water. — I don't have any apples / water. — Do I have any apples / water?*
4. "For" → "para" (*I have something for you.*). 5. "Or" → "o" (*Do you have a hat or an apple?*). 6. "Only" → "solo", antes del verbo principal o despues de "to be" (*I only have a hat.*). 7. "Too" → "tambien", al final (*I have a car, too.*).

**Ejemplos del libro (p.212):** *Do you have any pizza? — Yes, I have some pizza. / No, I don't have any pizza. · Does Harold have any pasta? — Yes, he has some pasta. · Do we have any fruit? — Yes, we have some fruit. · Do you have any soda or coffee? — I only have some soda. · Do they have a sandwich or a hotdog? — They only have a sandwich. · Does the restaurant have any ice cream? — No, it doesn't have any ice cream. · I have a car. My sister has a car, too.*

**Practica Verbal (encabezados, p.215-222):** *PV1 Have con a/an — Afirmativo · PV2 Negativo y Preguntas · PV3 Have con any/some — Afirmativo · PV4 Negativo y Preguntas.* Ejemplos: *"Mafe has a hamburger. · The secretaries have an office. · Mafe doesn't have any pizza. · Does the mother have any money?"*

## BLOQUE 2 — HOW DO YOU WORK? (40')

**Objective: everyone describes HOW they do real tasks using adverbs of manner in the right place.**

1. Meta en 1 linea: *"By the end of this block, each person has described how they do three real tasks — with adverbs after the verb, prudently and honestly."*
2. **Avance de proyecto hoy: una linea para MY STORY — the manner: "I started slowly, I studied carefully, and now I speak more confidently."**
3. Board — el molde oral (Grammar formaliza hoy con M43; aqui NO se enseña la regla):
```
HOW WE DO THINGS (adverb after the verb / object)
  "I work carefully."   "She speaks clearly."
  "He answers quickly."  "We finished the report well."
  Irregulars: good -> WELL · fast -> fast (no 'fastly')
  Frequency + manner: "I always plan carefully."
(most adverbs = adjective + -ly ; happy -> happily)
```
4. Modela 45 seg: describe como haces 2 tareas reales ("I answer clients politely and I check numbers carefully.").
5. **Preparacion en parejas (7'):** cada uno elige 3 tareas reales de su trabajo + el adverbio que describe COMO las hace (bien/rapido/con cuidado/despacio).
6. **Ejecucion (15'):** en parejas, A pregunta "How do you ___ at work?", B responde con adverbio ("I do it carefully") + por que es lo prudente. Cambio de pareja a mitad.
7. Producto verificable (8'): vuelta completa — cada uno da al grupo UNA linea "How I do ___: ___ly" (20 seg), 1 debe usar un irregular (well/fast/hard). Cuentas moldes limpios en el board.
8. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues. Errores ("goodly", "I speak good", adverbio mal ubicado) -> board anonimo; nombres a tu cuaderno.

## BLOQUE 3 — "I WANT YOU TO..." — THE DELEGATION (35')

**Objective: hold a realistic delegation/handover using "want/need/would like + someone + to + verb" — and close the book.**

1. Board: `DELEGATION MEETING — "I NEED YOU TO..." — 3 ROTATIONS`
2. Molde (Grammar formaliza hoy con M44 — el ULTIMO modulo del libro; aqui NO se enseña la regla):
```
EXPRESSING WISHES FOR OTHERS (A2 Book M44)
  "I want to finish."        (yo termino)
  "I want YOU to finish."    (yo quiero que TU termines)
  "I need the team to send the report."
  "I would like you to call the client."
(subject + want/need/would like + person + TO + verb)
```
3. Escenario profesional realista (solo tablero y sala): jefe delega tareas al equipo antes de una entrega. **1 guest = quien delega** ("I need you to call the supplier, I want Ana's area to check the numbers, and I would like you to send it carefully before noon."); otro estudiante rota como quien recibe y confirma ("You want me to call today? You need us to finish first?"); el resto = **OBSERVERS ACTIVOS** (2 errores, 1 acierto, 1 pregunta). Tu = COACH afuera — NUNCA juegas guest.
4. 3 rotaciones (~9'). Debrief por TIPOS (anonimo): moldes limpios vs "I want that you go" (calco del español) / "I want you go" (falta 'to') / "I need you finishing".
5. **Cierre SINTESIS dia 1 (4'):** *"This was the last structure in the book — you have now met every one. Prudence + fortitude: name one thing you will finish this level carefully AND bravely."* — 2-3 respuestas. Sin fiesta ni celebracion: es un hito de trabajo. *"The book is closed. What remains is you — and the last road."*

## ANEXO — verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**A2 Book M43 "How Do You Do?" — Adverbs of Manner (p.340-345):**
```
Guia 1 (p.342): describen COMO se hace una accion (responde "how?").
  Mayoria = adjetivo + "-ly" (sad->sadly; happy->happily).
  Irregulares: fast->fast (no 'fastly'); good->well.
  Posicion: despues del verbo principal o del objeto directo.
  "I play well." "He plays soccer well."
Ejemplos (p.343): "He plays badly." "William studies
  intelligently." "Philip drives fast." "Sebastian worked
  carefully." "Mike speaks English correctly." "The class worked
  together." Frecuencia+manera: "I always believe you easily."
```
**A2 Book M44 "I Want You to Go" — The Active Causative (p.347-351):**
```
Guia 1 (p.348): para expresar lo que queremos que OTRO haga, NO
  "I want that you go". Forma: sujeto + verbo de volicion
  (want/need/would like) + persona/pron. objeto + TO + verbo.
  "I want you to go to the store." "I need the teacher to teach."
Ejemplos (p.349): "I want you to get a job." "You need me to buy a
  car." "Anita would like you to give her a flower." "Brayan wants
  us to play soccer." "The teacher needs you to hand in your test."
  Interrog.: "Do you want your brother to climb the tree?" "Where
  do you want us to go?"
```

Dos modulos en un dia: si el tiempo aprieta, B2 prioriza los 6 adverbios mas utiles (well/fast/hard/carefully/quickly/slowly) y B3 se centra en want/need + to.

## BLOQUE 2 — CLOSED vs OPEN EN PAPEL (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone decides simple past vs present perfect by reading the TIME period, fast and clean.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M17, Guide 1, p.163-164):
```
PRESENT PERFECT vs SIMPLE PAST (A2 Book M17, p.163-164)
SIMPLE PAST  = accion terminada en un punto ESPECIFICO del pasado.
   "I studied at 2:00."  (yesterday / last year / in 2020 = period CLOSED)
PRESENT PERFECT = accion en un periodo INDEFINIDO o ABIERTO, o no terminada.
   "I have studied."            (unspecified period)
   "I have studied for 2 hours." (started in past, not finished)
   "I have studied today."       (today = period still OPEN)
KEY: "last year" is CLOSED -> simple past.  "this year" is OPEN -> present perfect.
NEG/QUESTION: "I didn't say hello" = won't happen. "I haven't said hello" = not YET.
```
2. Señala la logica prudente: *"One question decides everything: is the time period closed or still open?"*
3. **Drill en circulo (10', DE PIE):** cadena. Tu lanzas una frase con marca de tiempo ("call the client / at 2:00") o sin marca ("visit the new office / —"); estudiante 1 dice si es CLOSED o OPEN, estudiante 2 produce la forma correcta, estudiante 3 lanza la siguiente. Velocidad creciente.

## ANEXO — M17 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**A2 Book, Module 17 — "You Didn't, or You Haven't? / The Present Perfect vs The Simple Past" (p.163-164). Guide 1 — Past Simple or Present Perfect (verbatim):**
1. **Pasado simple:** accion terminada en un punto especifico en el pasado. E.g. "I studied at 2:00." -> "Yo estudie a las 2:00."
2. **Presente perfecto 1:** accion hecha (o no hecha) en un periodo desconocido o no especificado. E.g. "I have studied." -> "Yo he estudiado."
3. **Presente perfecto 2:** la accion empezo en el pasado y no ha terminado. "I have studied [for 2 hours]."
4. **Presente perfecto 3:** la accion termino, pero en un periodo que todavia esta abierto. "I have studied today."
5. a. "I studied last year." — el periodo "last year" esta CERRADO -> pasado simple. b. "I have studied this year." — "this year" sigue ABIERTO -> presente perfecto.
6. En negativo/interrogativo hay dos implicaciones: a. Pasado simple = algo que no paso y no va a pasar ("I didn't say hello"). b. Presente perfecto = algo que no ha pasado TODAVIA, pero se puede ("I haven't said hello").

**Apoyo (Present Perfect regular, A2 M15 p.133-135):** have/has + participio (-ed). "I played soccer yesterday." -> "I've played soccer." / "Have you visited London?" "Yes, I have." / "No, I haven't."

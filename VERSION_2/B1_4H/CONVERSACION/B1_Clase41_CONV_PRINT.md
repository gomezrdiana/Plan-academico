## BLOQUE 2 — EVERYONE, SOMEONE, MYSELF (40')

**Objective: everyone uses indefinite, reflexive and reciprocal pronouns to describe real work — who does what, alone or together.**

1. Meta en 1 linea (arriba). **Avance de proyecto hoy: la primera linea de tu presentacion final — "what I built in this level, and who I became."**
2. Board — el molde oral (Grammar formaliza hoy M12; aqui NO se enseña la tabla):
```
WHO DOES WHAT
INDEFINITE: everyone / someone / anyone / no one (+ SINGULAR verb)
  "Everyone IS busy." "No one wants to fail." "Can anyone help?"
REFLEXIVE: myself / yourself / himself / ourselves / themselves
  "I taught MYSELF." "I did it BY MYSELF." (alone)
RECIPROCAL: each other (2) / one another (3+)
  "We helped EACH OTHER."
```
3. Modela 60 seg con lo tuyo real ("Everyone in this room started nervous; I pushed myself; we corrected each other"). Señala: *"'Everyone IS' — always singular. 'By myself' = alone. 'Each other' = both ways."*
4. Preparacion en parejas (7'): cada uno prepara 6 frases sobre su trabajo/este curso: 2 indefinite, 2 reflexive (1 con "by myself" = alone), 2 reciprocal. Real.
5. Ejecucion (16'): cada uno lo dice a su pareja; la pareja escucha con tarea: ¿verbo singular con everyone/someone? ¿reflexivo correcto? ¿each other vs one another? Luego la pareja RE-CUENTA dos ("He taught himself; they supported each other").
6. Producto verificable (8'): 3-4 voluntarios AL FRENTE (45-60 seg): 2 indefinite + 1 reflexive + 1 reciprocal, sobre este nivel. El grupo confirma: "singular verb; right reflexive".
7. Feedback: primero el logro, luego la forma. Errores de 2+ parejas -> board anonimo. Nombres a tu cuaderno.

## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B (no leer literal en clase)

**Modulo ancla: B1 Book M12 "Advanced Pronouns — I Can Do It Myself!" (p.145-155) — ULTIMO modulo del libro.** Conv lo APLICA oralmente; Grammar lo formaliza hoy. Con Cl 41 el libro B1 (M1-M12) queda COMPLETO.

Reglas y ejemplos del libro (referencia):
- **Guide 1 (p.147) — indefinite:** every/some/any/no + one/body/where/thing. *"Indefinite pronouns are always used as a 3rd person singular (even if they refer to multiples). CORRECT: Everyone is here. INCORRECT: Everyone are here."* Some = afirmativo; any = negativo/pregunta; no = ausencia. Libro p.148: *"Everyone is angry that the flight arrived late / No one can help me. And I can't help anyone."*
- **Guide 2 (p.151) — reflexive:** myself/yourself/yourselves/himself/herself/itself/ourselves/themselves. *"When the subject and object of a verb are the same, we use the reflexive pronoun."* *"We also often use 'by' + reflexive pronoun to indicate 'alone': 'I am eating by myself.'"* Libro p.152: *"I am going to teach myself Spanish / Ray and Gil built the house by themselves."*
- **Guide 3 (p.155) — reciprocal:** *"'Each other' - 2 actors. 'One another' - 3 or more actors."* *"Becky and Sandy talked with each other."* Nota del libro: *"'One another' is becoming less common... Many speakers use 'each other' for any number."*

## BLOQUE 2 — WHOSE IS IT? / TIRING vs TIRED (40')

**Objective: everyone claims ownership and names a real work feeling, fast, across both structures.**

1. Meta en 1 linea (arriba). **Avance de proyecto hoy: una linea para MY STORY — "this progress is mine: I earned it, and the journey was exciting, not exhausting."**
2. Board — el molde oral (Grammar lo formaliza hoy mismo con M39 + M41; aqui NO se enseña la regla):
```
OWNERSHIP & FEELINGS AT WORK
OWNERSHIP: "Whose laptop is this?" - "It's mine." / "It's hers." / "It's theirs."
           "Whose idea was better?" - "Mine was."
FEELINGS:  "-ING" = the thing causes it: "The report is tiring."
           "-ED"  = I feel it:           "I am tired."
           "The meeting was boring; I was bored."  "The news is exciting; I'm excited."
(mine/yours/his/hers/ours/theirs · whose...? · -ing thing vs -ed person)
```
3. Modela 45 seg: reclama algo tuyo del trabajo ("This project is mine") + nombra un sentimiento real ("The deadline was stressful, but the result is exciting").
4. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno elige 2 objetos/tareas reales (¿de quien es?) y 2 situaciones reales (¿tiring o tired? ¿interesting o interested?).
5. Ejecucion rapida (14'): en parejas, A pregunta "Whose ___ is this?" y B responde con pronombre; luego B nombra una situacion y A reacciona con la forma correcta (-ing thing / -ed person). Cambio de pareja a mitad.
6. Producto verificable (10'): vuelta completa — cada estudiante dice al grupo UNA frase de posesion Y una de sentimiento (20 seg). Tu cuentas moldes limpios (mine vs my / tiring vs tired) en el board.
7. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues. Errores 2+ parejas -> board anonimo. Nombres a tu cuaderno.

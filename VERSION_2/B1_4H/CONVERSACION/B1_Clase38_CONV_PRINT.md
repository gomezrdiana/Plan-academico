## BLOQUE 2 — WHAT DID THEY SAY? (40')

**Objective: everyone has reported six real work messages using say/tell/ask + the verb stepped back.**

1. Meta en 1 linea (arriba). **Avance de proyecto hoy: una linea de MY STORY — "the most important thing a boss or teacher ever told me."**
2. Board — el molde oral (Grammar lo formaliza hoy mismo con M8; aqui NO se enseña la tabla):
```
REPORT IT — THE VERB STEPS ONE FOOT BACK
Direct:   "I am busy."        -> She SAID she WAS busy.
Direct:   "I will call you."  -> He TOLD me he WOULD call.
Direct:   "Are you free?"     -> They ASKED IF I was free.
SAY = no person needed | TELL = needs a person | ASK = needs a person / IF
now -> then | today -> that day | here -> there
```
3. Modela 60 seg reportando 3 mensajes reales tuyos ("what a student told me yesterday..."). Señala: *"Hear it? IS becomes WAS, WILL becomes WOULD — one foot back, every time."*
4. Preparacion en parejas (7'): cada uno recuerda 6 cosas REALES que le dijeron esta semana (jefe, cliente, profesor, familia); notas minimas. Reales y cotidianas, nada inventado.
5. Ejecucion (16'): cada uno REPORTA sus 6 mensajes a su pareja (say/tell/ask + verbo atras); la pareja escucha con tarea: ¿el verbo dio el paso atras? ¿say/tell/ask correcto? Luego la pareja RE-REPORTA dos de ellos ("His boss told him that...").
6. Producto verificable (8'): 3-4 voluntarios reportan 2 mensajes AL FRENTE; el grupo confirma: "verb stepped back, right reporting verb".
7. Feedback: primero el logro (¿se entiende el mensaje?), luego la forma. Errores de 2+ parejas -> board anonimo. Nombres a tu cuaderno.

## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B (no leer literal en clase)

**Modulo ancla: B1 Book M8 "Reported Speech — He Said, She Said" (p.83-96).** Conv lo APLICA oralmente; Grammar lo formaliza hoy. NO enseñar la tabla en Conv.

Reglas del libro (referencia; el molde oral sale de aqui):
- **Guide 2 (p.89) — say / tell / ask:** SAY = decir (no requiere persona; "to" opcional: "I said hello to Janel"). TELL = contar/informar (requiere persona, sin preposicion: "I told my mom the time"). ASK = preguntar (requiere persona; para si/no usa IF/WHETHER). Ejemplos del libro (p.85): *"Daniella said that she was happy / Daniella told me where she lives / Daniella asked me if I was sick."*
- **Guide 3 (p.93) — back-shift:** *"When the control verb moves one step into the past, all dependent verbs also move one step into the past."* Ejemplo: *"Mark: 'I am happy.' -> Mark said that he was happy."* Tabla: is->was, plays->played, played/has played->had played, is playing->was playing, will->would, can->could, must->had to.

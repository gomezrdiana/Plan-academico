## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B (no leer literal en clase)

**Modulos ancla: B1 Book M10 "The Causative" (p.117-126) + M11 "Advanced Adjectives — Tell Me About It!" (p.127-144).** Conv los APLICA oralmente; Grammar los formaliza hoy.

Reglas y ejemplos del libro (referencia):
- **M10 Guide 1 (p.119) — active causative:** CON "to": ask/allow/get/tell/want/need. SIN "to": help/let/make/have. *"Most verbs use the infinitive... if the verb implies the main action has started, then we do not use the infinitive."* Libro p.120: *"I always help Sebastian finish his homework / You can't make me love you! / The teacher had the children sit on the floor."*
- **M10 Guide 2 (p.123) — passive causative:** *"Subject + Causative verb ('have' or 'get') + Direct Object + Action (In Past Participle). Only 2 causative verbs exist; 'get' and 'have'. 'Get' is more common, while 'have' is more formal."* Libro p.124: *"I will get my hair done before the party / I had the letter written by my secretary."*
- **M11 Guide 1 (p.129) — noun-adjective pairs:** health/healthy, strength/strong, talent/talented, intelligence/intelligent, caution/cautious, carelessness/careless, etc. Libro p.130: *"Samson was a strong man. His strength could destroy a palace."*
- **M11 Guide 2 (p.133) — prefixes/suffixes:** Un-/In-/Im-/Ir-/Dis- = not; Mis- = incorrectly; Re- = again; -less = without; -ful = full of; -able/-ible = can be done. Libro p.134: *"Tim's answer is incorrect / He's so shameless."*
- **M11 Guide 3 (p.137) — compound adjectives:** "a 150-page book"; numero+unidad en SINGULAR ("a 3-year-old child"). Libro p.138: *"Shakespeare is a well-known author."*
- **M11 Guide 4 (p.141) — impersonal expressions:** *"'es' -> 'it is'... never in English"*; relative pronoun o infinitivo. Libro p.142: *"It is important to help / It is dangerous to drive after drinking alcohol."*

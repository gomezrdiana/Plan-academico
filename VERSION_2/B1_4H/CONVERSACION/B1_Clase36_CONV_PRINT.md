## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B + NOTA INTERNA

### M5 — Could Have / Should Have (Guide 1 Past Modals p.43; Guide 2 Expressing Regret p.47)
Cita p.43: *"'would' + present perfect = willingness in the past, but not done ('I would have gone to the store'); 'could' + present perfect = possibility in the past, but not done ('I could have gone'); 'should' + present perfect = recommendation in the past, but not done ('I should have gone')."*
Cita p.47: *"'To wish' — similar to 'ojalá': 'I wish I had gone to the game.' / 'If only' — 'If only I had gone to the game.' Both use the 'had + participle' form."*
Ejemplos p.44/48: *"Bella should have done her homework last night."* · *"The dog couldn't have taken the cookies. She was outside."* · *"I wish I hadn't broken the car."* · *"If only I hadn't fought with my mother."*

### M6 — Connectors of Cause and Contrast (Guide 1 Cause/Result p.53; Guide 2 Contrast p.57)
Cita p.53: *"Because + clause ('I love her because she is beautiful'); Because of + noun ('because of her beauty', formal: due to; thanks to); so + clause ('She is beautiful, so I love her'); That's why + clause."*
Cita p.57: *"but + clause ('I want to play, but I have to work'); Even though + clause ('Even though I want to play, I have to work'; formal: although, while); On the other hand + clause; In spite of / Despite + noun ('In spite of your efforts, we lost')."*
Ejemplos p.54/58: *"Because of the winter, we will stay in the house."* · *"Since you want bread, you should go to the bakery."* · *"Although I want to buy a new car, I don't have the money."* · *"Despite my hard work, I have no money."*

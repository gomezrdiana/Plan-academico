## BLOQUE 2 — DIDN'T vs HAVEN'T EN PAPEL (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds negative and question in both pasts and knows what each IMPLIES.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M17, Guide 1, p.164, pto.6):
```
NEGATIVE / QUESTION — TWO MEANINGS (A2 Book M17, p.164)
SIMPLE PAST negative = closed and over, will NOT change:
   "I didn't say hello."  -> no lo dije, y no lo voy a decir.
PRESENT PERFECT negative = not YET, still possible:
   "I haven't said hello." -> no lo he dicho todavia, pero posiblemente lo hare.
FORM (perfect):  (-) haven't / hasn't + participle   he/she/it = HAS
   (?) Have you finished? — Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.   (short answer = short!)
FOR + duration  ("for six years")   |   SINCE + a point   ("since 2018")
```
2. Señala: *"Only the auxiliary carries the meaning: didn't = closed; haven't = open."*
3. **Drill en circulo (10', DE PIE):** cadena. Tu lanzas sujeto + verbo + intencion ("she / finish / not-yet") -> estudiante 1 forma la negativa correcta, 2 la vuelve pregunta, 3 da la short answer, 4 lanza el siguiente. Velocidad creciente.
4. **Errores tipicos al board (coro 2x):**
   - "I don't finished" -> "I HAVEN'T finished" (perfect negativo)
   - "he haven't" -> "he HASN'T" (3a persona)
   - "Yes, I have finished it" (a "Have you finished?") -> "Yes, I HAVE." (short answer corta)
   - "I have finished yesterday" -> "I FINISHED yesterday" (cerrado)
5. **Zone walk (12', DE PIE):** tablero `CLOSED & OVER (didn't) | NOT YET (haven't)`. Dices una situacion ("the client / call / never came back" vs "the client / call / still pending"); las parejas CAMINAN a la zona Y producen la frase negativa correcta en voz alta + justifican en 1 linea.
6. Cierre del bloque: produccion libre de pie — cada uno 1 "I didn't... (closed)" + 1 "I haven't... yet". El uso libre cierra, no el drill.

## ANEXO — M17 verbatim (capa 2) + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**A2 Book, Module 17 — "You Didn't, or You Haven't?" (p.163-164), Guide 1, punto 6 (verbatim):**
"Debido a la logica del punto 5, en el negativo e interrogativo, hay dos implicaciones diferentes:
a. **Pasado simple:** algo que no ha pasado todavia, y no va a pasar en el futuro. E.g. 'I didn't say hello.' -> Implica: 'No dije hola, y no voy a decirlo.'
b. **Presente perfecto:** algo que no ha pasado todavia, pero todavia se puede. E.g. 'I haven't said hello.' -> Implica: 'No he dicho hola todavia, pero posiblemente lo hare.'"

**Puntos 1-5 (resumen verbatim de Cl 1):** simple past = punto especifico ("I studied at 2:00"); present perfect = periodo indefinido/no terminado/abierto ("I have studied" / "for 2 hours" / "today"). "last year" cerrado -> past; "this year" abierto -> perfect.

**Short answer (A2 M15 p.134 verbatim):** "Have you visited London?" "Yes, I have." / "No, I haven't." — la respuesta corta NO repite el verbo.

- Si sobra tiempo: "make it a question" — tu das una afirmativa, el grupo la vuelve pregunta + short answer.

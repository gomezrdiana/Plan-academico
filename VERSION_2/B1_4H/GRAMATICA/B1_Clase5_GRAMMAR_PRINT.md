**Objective: everyone forms present affirmative, negative and question, and never drops the third-person -s.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M8 p.99-100 + M9 p.111-112):
```
THE SIMPLE PRESENT (A1 Book M8, p.99-101)
Two auxiliaries:  I / you / we / they -> DO      he / she / it -> DOES
  (-) don't / doesn't + verb    (?) Do/Does + subject + verb?
THE -S EXCEPTION (A1 Book M9, p.111-113)
  AFFIRMATIVE ONLY: we drop do/does and add -s to the verb (he/she/it):
     "He plays soccer."   (NOT "He does plays")
  NEGATIVE / QUESTION always keep do/does + BASE verb:
     "He doesn't play."  "Does he play?"   (NEVER "doesn't plays")
```

## ANEXO — M8 + M9 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**A1 Book, Module 8 — "Where Do We Eat? / The Simple Present" (p.99-101), Guia 1 (verbatim):**
1. Los auxiliares "do" y "does" indican el presente; cosas habituales o que no cambian. 2. El presente es el unico tiempo con dos auxiliares: I/You/We/They -> Do; He/She/It -> Does. 3. Se trata como can/should/will. -> "She does play soccer." 4. "do + not" = "don't"; "does + not" = "doesn't". 5. Respuestas cortas: "do/don't", "does/doesn't".

**A1 Book, Module 9 — "He Eats in the Restaurant / The Simple Present – Affirmative Exception" (p.111-113), Guia 1 (verbatim):**
1. En el caso POSITIVO (nunca en negativo ni pregunta) generalmente omitimos el "do/does". 2. Con "do" no hay otro cambio. 3. Con "does", al eliminarlo, se corre la "-s" o "-es" al verbo. -> "She plays soccer." 4. Pronunciacion de la -s segun el ultimo sonido (-s/-z/-x/-sh/-ch -> "-ez": washes, watches; consonante dura -> "-s": eats; suave/vocal -> "-z": plays).
**Ejemplos (p.112-113):** "Where does he live?" -> "He lives in Bogota." · "Where does she eat?" -> "She eats in the restaurant." · "Does Fritz play soccer?" -> "No, he doesn't play soccer." (negativo/pregunta SIEMPRE do/does + base).
Ortografia -y -> -ies: "study -> studies", "try -> tries".

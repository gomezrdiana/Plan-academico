## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B + NOTA INTERNA

### M3 — Deduction (Guia 1 p.23; Ejemplos p.24; Ejercicios p.25)
Regla verbatim p.23: *"The verb depends on the certainty the speaker feels: Very certain -> Must ('He must be sick'); Partially certain -> Might / May / Could ('He might/may/could be sick'); Very certain of the opposite -> Can't / Must not ('He can't be sick')."* + *"'He must be playing soccer.' / 'He must have arrived already.'"*
Ejemplos p.24: *"Vanessa might know Alejandra."* · *"It is raining today. We may arrive late."* · *"My father is in Canada. You can't have seen him."* · *"I hear music. The concert must have started."*

### M4 — Third Conditional Straight (Guia 1 p.29; Ejemplos p.30; Ejercicios p.31)
Regla verbatim p.29: *"Clause with 'if': HAD + PARTICIPLE / Conditional clause: WOULD HAVE + PARTICIPLE. 'If I had studied… I would have passed the exam.'... The probability of studying is now 0%, because past events cannot be changed."*
Ejemplos p.30: *"If I had gotten a bike for Christmas, I would have used it every day."* · *"If the pilots had gone to the airport, they would have arrived on time."* · *"If Gillian had met Arthur, she would have loved him."* · *"If the boys had studied medicine, what would they have become?"*
> El libro extiende a MIXTO (p.33: HAD + participle / WOULD + base, "If I had studied, I would work in Rome today") y comparacion 0/1/2/3 (p.37). Refuerzo opcional si sobra tiempo; Cl 35 cierra el straight.

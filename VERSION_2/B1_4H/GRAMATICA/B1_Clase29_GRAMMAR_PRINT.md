**Objective: reactivate M29 and M30 in ~7 min each, then everyone MIXES both on paper about real work.**

2. **Activacion M30 — SO / TOO / ENOUGH (7', board compacto — cita libro p.267-272):**
```
REQUIRED LEVELS: enough / so / too (M30, p.267-272)
"enough" va DESPUES del adjetivo: "I am tall enough." /
  "I am not tall enough."
"so" = nivel alto y BUENO:  "You are so tall!"  "It is so new!"
"too" = excesivo, NO deseable:  "You are too tall."  "It is too new."
  (so nice = good ; too nice = can't fight)
```
Coro 2x + cada estudiante 1 par "so.../too..." real de su trabajo.

3. **Meta de tarea comunicativa (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, this board holds 12 real work lines that MIX both — a 'how ___?' with an intensifier AND a judgement with enough/so/too."*

6. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**

7. **Producto verificable:** lectura coral del board; feedback logro primero, forma despues.

## ANEXO — REFERENCIAS COMPACTAS DEL LIBRO (no leer en clase; consulta rapida)

**M29 "How Tall Are You?" — Degrees of Adjectives / Intensifiers (p.261; Guia p.262; Ejemplos p.263).** Regla: "how" + adjetivo = "que tan ___?"; responder con intensificador: really (informal) · very (informal/academico) · kinda · sorta · pretty · not very (opuesto cortes). Ej. libro: "How nice is Ana? — She's very nice" · "How new is your car? — It's pretty new" · "How old is your dad? — He's not very old. He's 38" · "How good is the food? — It's not very good" · "How hard is your job? — It's pretty hard, but I'm learning." Vocab: really/very/kinda/sorta/pretty/not very.

**M30 "Too Much or Just Enough?" — Required Levels (p.267; Guia p.268; Ejemplos p.269).** Regla: "enough" va DESPUES del adjetivo (tall enough / not tall enough); "so" = nivel alto y bueno (You are so tall!); "too" = excesivo/no deseable (You are too tall). Ej. libro (pares so/too): "She is so nice! / She is too nice (can't fight)" · "It is so new! / Your car is too new" · "He is so smart! / He is too smart (can't teach kids)" · "It was so easy. / It is too easy" · "You are so early. / It is too early." Vocab: enough · so · too.

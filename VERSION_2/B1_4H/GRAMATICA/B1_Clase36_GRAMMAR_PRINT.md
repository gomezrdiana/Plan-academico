## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B + NOTA INTERNA

### M5 — Could Have / Should Have (Guia 1 p.43; Guia 2 Regret p.47; Ejercicios p.45/49)
Regla verbatim p.43: *"'would' + present perfect = willingness (voluntad) in the past, but not done; 'could' + present perfect = possibility in the past, but not done; 'should' + present perfect = recommendation in the past, but not done. 'I should have gone to the store.' (Implies: 'But, I did not go.')"*
Regla verbatim p.47: *"'To wish' — similar to 'ojalá': 'I wish I had gone to the game.' '(If only)' — 'If only I had gone to the game.' Note that both expressions use the 'had + participle' form... North American speakers often use both with 'would have', this is incorrect but generally accepted."*
Ejemplos p.44/48: *"Brian shouldn't have driven to the mall. He should have walked."* · *"You should have seen Natalia last night."* · *"I wish I hadn't broken the car."* · *"If only we hadn't lost our dog."*

### M6 — Connectors of Cause and Contrast (Guia 1 p.53; Guia 2 p.57; Ejercicios p.55-56/59-60)
Regla verbatim p.53: *"Because + [clause]; Because of + [noun] (formal: due to; sometimes sarcastic: thanks to); [clause] + ', so' + [clause] (formal: thus, therefore); [clause] + '. That's why' + [clause]."*
Regla verbatim p.57: *"[clause] + ', but' + [clause] (formal: however, though, yet); 'Even though,' + [clause] (formal: although, while); [clause] + '. On the other hand' + [clause]; 'In spite of' + [noun] (formal: despite)."*
Ejemplos p.54/58: *"Thanks to my teacher, I can speak English!"* · *"Due to your excellent offer, I will work with you."* · *"I have to be strong, but I want to run."* · *"In spite of my work, I have no money."*

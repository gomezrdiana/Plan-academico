## BLOQUE 2 — HAD + PARTICIPLE EN PAPEL (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone forms the past perfect and places the flashback in the right event.**

1. Board:
```
PAST PERFECT — HAD + past participle (the past before the past)
HAD never changes:  I/you/he/she/it/we/they  +  HAD  +  participle
Regular participle = -ed (like past simple): finished, prepared, worked
Irregular participle (from A2 Book M16, p.147): gone, taken, seen, done, been, made
3 signal frames:
  By the time + [past simple], [HAD + participle].
  [past simple] because [HAD + participle].
  Before + [past simple], [HAD + participle].
  E.g. "By the time she arrived, I HAD finished."
       "I passed because I HAD studied."
       "Before he called, I HAD sent the file."
Contrast: "When I got there, it started" vs "When I got there, it HAD started."
```
2. Señala la logica: *"HAD carries the tense; the verb wears its PARTICIPLE — the same participle you will meet again next class after HAVE/HAS. Here it lives under HAD."*
3. **Drill en circulo (10', DE PIE):** cadena — tu lanzas 2 eventos ("arrive / finish the report") -> est. 1 ordena cual fue primero, 2 lo dice con "By the time...", 3 con "...because I had...", 4 short answer ("Had you finished? Yes, I had."); el 5 lanza el siguiente par. Velocidad creciente.
4. **Errores tipicos al board (coro 2x cada correccion):**
   - "I had finish" -> "I had FINISHED" (participio, no bare)
   - "I had went" -> "I had GONE" (participio irregular, no past simple)
   - HAD en el evento equivocado -> el flashback es lo ANTERIOR, no el principal
5. **Zone walk (12', DE PIE):** tablero `MAIN EVENT (past simple) | EARLIER EVENT (had + participle)`. Dices dos hechos; las parejas CAMINAN colocando cada uno en su zona Y producen la frase enlazada con un signal frame en voz alta.
6. Cierre del bloque: cada uno produce de pie 2 frases verdaderas con "had + participle" sobre un logro. El uso libre cierra el bloque.

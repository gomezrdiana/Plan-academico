## BLOQUE 2 — ANCLAJE Y ORDEN EN PAPEL (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone places the right time word in the right position and sequences a past account.**

1. Board — cita del libro + reglas:
```
TIME EXPRESSIONS — PAST (A2 Book M12, p.99-102 — verbatim)
Book: "I ate a sandwich AT 8:00." · "Steve woke up AT 6:30 today."
      "I was living in Bucaramanga IN 2019." · "last week" · "last year"
AT + clock   ON + day/date   IN + year/month   (no prep: yesterday, this morning)
LAST + period (NO "the", NO "in"):  last week, last Monday
___ AGO (goes AFTER the period):    two days ago  (NOT "ago two days")
POSITION: END by default; FRONT + comma for emphasis. Never mid-verb.
ORDER: First, / Then, / After that, / Before ..., / Finally,
```
2. Señala la logica: *"One time word per sentence, and it lands at the end. AGO is a countdown — it follows the period."*
3. **Drill en circulo (10', DE PIE):** cadena — tu das accion + dato de tiempo ("call the client / 2 days") → estudiante 1 arma la frase con el anchor correcto en posicion → 2 la reordena al frente con coma → 3 lanza la siguiente. Velocidad creciente.
4. **Errores tipicos al board (coro 2x cada correccion):**
   - "ago two days" → "two days AGO"
   - "the last Monday" → "LAST Monday"
   - "in last week" → "LAST week" (sin "in")
   - "I yesterday called him" → "I called him YESTERDAY"
   - "at Monday / on 2020" → "ON Monday / IN 2020"
5. **Anchor walk (12', DE PIE):** tablero en 4 zonas `AT | ON | IN | LAST/AGO`. Dictas un dato de tiempo ("8:00 / Monday / 2019 / two days"); las parejas CAMINAN a la zona correcta Y producen una frase en pasado con ese anchor en su posicion. El que llega, justifica en 1 linea.
6. Cierre del bloque: produccion libre de pie — cada uno ordena una secuencia real de 4 pasos con first/then/after that/finally + anchors. El drill no cierra el bloque — cierra el uso libre.

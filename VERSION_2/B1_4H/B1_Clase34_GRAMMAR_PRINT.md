## BLOQUE 2 — HOW MANY? (COUNTABLE) (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds "how many?" questions and answers on paper about real countable work things.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M32, Vocab 1 p.274 + Guia 1 p.275):
```
COUNTABLE QUANTITIES (A2 Book M32, p.273-275)
VOCAB (p.274): a person · people (plural IRREGULAR) ·
  many / a lot of / lots of = muchos · a few = unos (3-5)
GUIA (p.275):
  "How many + countable?" = "¿Cuantos ___?"
  "How many dogs do you have?"  (con have)
  "How many dogs are there?"    (con there are)
  "I have many friends. / I have a lot of friends." (muchos)
  "I have a few friends." (unos, 3-5)
BOOK Q&A (p.278):
  "How many nurses are there? — There are 7 nurses."
  "How many people are there? — There are a lot of people."
  "How many sandwiches are there? — There are a few sandwiches."
```
2. **Meta de tarea comunicativa (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, this board holds 12 real 'how many?' exchanges — with many, a lot of, and a few — about your work."*
3. **Preparacion en parejas (7', DE PIE junto al board):** cada pareja arma 2 preguntas "How many ___?" (una con "have", una con "there are") + respuestas reales (many / a lot of / a few / numero).
4. **Ejecucion (12'):** cada pareja ESCRIBE su Q&A en el board, la dice, y el grupo verifica: ¿"how many" (no "how much")? ¿"people" sin -s? ¿"a lot OF"? Meta: 12+ limpias.
5. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**
   - "how much people" -> "how many people" (contable = many)
   - "many peoples" -> "many people" (people ya es plural)
   - "a lot people" -> "a lot OF people"
   - "a few of hours" -> "a few hours" (sobra "of")
6. **Producto verificable:** lectura coral del board; feedback primero sobre el logro, luego la forma.

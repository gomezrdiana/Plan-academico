## BLOQUE 2 — EL PHRASAL VERB QUE NO SE SEPARA (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds non-separable phrasal-verb sentences on paper, object at the end.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M23, Guia 1 p.222-223):
```
NON-SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS (A2 Book M23, p.221-224)
Regla 1: verbo + preposicion/adverbio = NUEVO verbo.
         "look" + "after" = "look after" (cuidar)
Regla 2: ya sabes 3 phrasal verbs: wake up, get dressed,
         hang out.
Regla 3: se conjuga el VERBO (primera palabra):
  Present:  I look after Amy.   3rd sg: He looks after Amy.
  Past:     He looked after Amy.
  Continuous: He is looking after Amy.
Regla 4: los NO SEPARABLES nunca se parten; el objeto
         directo (o el pronombre) va AL FINAL:
  "I look after Amy."   "I look after her."
BOOK EXAMPLES (Ejemplos p.223-224, Q/A):
  "Who do you listen to in the class? — I listen to the teacher."
  "Are you looking after your sister? — Yes, I'm looking after her."
  "Who did you run into in the mall? — I ran into my friends."
  "What will Frank ask for? — He will ask for a pencil."
VOCAB (p.221-222): ask for · go back (went back) · look at ·
  listen to · run into (ran into) · look after
```
2. **Meta de tarea comunicativa (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, this board holds 12 real work sentences with non-separable phrasal verbs — object always at the end."*
3. **Preparacion en parejas (7', DE PIE junto al board):** cada pareja arma 2 frases reales con look after / listen to / ask for / run into + objeto (o pronombre al final).
4. **Ejecucion (12'):** cada pareja ESCRIBE sus 2 frases en el board, las dice, y el grupo verifica: ¿verbo + preposicion juntos? ¿objeto al final? Meta: 12+ limpias.
5. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**

   - "I look after her the interns" -> "I look after her" / "I look after the interns" (un objeto)

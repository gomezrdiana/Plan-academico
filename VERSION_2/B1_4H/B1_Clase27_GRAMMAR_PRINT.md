## BLOQUE 2 — DOS REGLAS DEL PHRASAL, RAPIDO (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: reactivate M23 and M24 in ~7 min each, then everyone MIXES both on paper about real work.**

1. **Activacion M23 — NO separables (7', board compacto — cita libro p.221-224):**
```
NON-SEPARABLE PHRASALS (M23, p.221-224)
Regla: verbo + preposicion = nuevo verbo; NUNCA se parte;
       el objeto (o el pronombre) va AL FINAL.
  ask for · look after · listen to · look at · run into · go back
  "I look after Amy." -> "I look after her."
  "I listen to the teacher."  "I ran into my friends in the mall."
```
Coro 2x + cada estudiante 1 frase real (a quien cuida/escucha en el trabajo).

2. **Activacion M24 — SEPARABLES (7', board compacto — cita libro p.233-236):**
```
SEPARABLE PHRASALS (M24, p.233-236)
Regla: objeto-NOMBRE va despues O en medio; el PRONOMBRE
       va SIEMPRE EN MEDIO.
  turn on · turn off · put on · take off · pick up · look up
  "I pick up Amy." / "I pick Amy up."  ->  "I pick her up." (NO "pick up her")
  "Turn it off."  "Look it up."  "He will pick her up."
```
Coro 2x + cada estudiante 1 frase real con pronombre en medio.

3. **Meta de tarea comunicativa (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, this board holds 12 real work lines that MIX both — some non-separable (object at the end) and some separable (pronoun in the middle)."*

5. **Ejecucion (12'):** cada pareja ESCRIBE sus 2 frases en el board (una zona por pareja), las dice, y el grupo verifica: NO separable -> ¿objeto al final? Separable con pronombre -> ¿en medio? Meta: 12+ lineas limpias.
6. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**
   - "I listen my team" -> "I listen TO my team" · "I run to a client" -> "I ran INTO a client"

7. **Producto verificable:** lectura coral del board; feedback logro primero, forma despues.

## ANEXO — REFERENCIAS COMPACTAS DEL LIBRO (no leer en clase; consulta rapida)

**M23 "I Asked for A Pencil" — Basic Non-Separable Phrasal Verbs (p.221; Vocab p.221-222; Guia p.222-223; Ejemplos p.223-224).** Regla: verbo + preposicion = nuevo verbo; se conjuga el verbo (primera palabra); NO se parte; objeto/pronombre AL FINAL. Ej. libro (Q/A): "Who do you listen to? — I listen to the teacher" · "Are you looking after your sister? — Yes, I'm looking after her" · "Who did you run into in the mall? — I ran into my friends" · "What will Frank ask for? — He will ask for a pencil." Vocab p.221-222: ask for · go back · look at · listen to · run into · look after.

**M24 "I'll Pick You Up at 8:00" — Separable Phrasal Verbs (p.233; Vocab p.233-234; Guia p.234-235; Ejemplos p.235).** Regla: objeto-nombre despues O en medio; pronombre SIEMPRE en medio. Ej. libro: "Did you turn on the TV? — Yes, I turned it on" · "Will you turn off your phone? — Yes, I will turn it off" · "Did you put on your hat? — Yes, I put it on" · "Will Alvaro pick up his sister? — Yes, he will pick her up" · "Did you look up Shakira on Google? — Yes, I looked her up." Vocab p.233-234: turn on · turn off · put on · take off · pick up · look up · a radio · a TV · a computer · a light.

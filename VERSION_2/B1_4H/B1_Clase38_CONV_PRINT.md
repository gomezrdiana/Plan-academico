## BLOQUE 2 — WHOSE IS THIS? (40')

**Objective: everyone claims and assigns real work items and duties using whose + mine/yours/his/hers/ours/theirs.**

1. Meta en 1 linea: *"By the end of this block, every person has claimed one duty and returned one item, out loud, with a possessive pronoun."*
2. **Avance de proyecto hoy: una linea para MY STORY — ownership: "This progress is mine — no one else did it for me."**
3. Board — el molde oral (Grammar formaliza hoy con M39; aqui NO se enseña la regla):
```
CLAIMING & RETURNING (say it, don't spell it)
"Whose report is this?"        "It's mine."
"Whose desk is that?"          "It's hers / his."
"Whose clients are those?"     "They're ours / theirs."
"Is this your badge?"          "No, it's not mine — it's yours."
"Whose result is stronger?"    "Mine is." / "Ours is."
(no 'my' before mine · whose = de quien · 's = de: Peter's)
```
4. Modela 45 seg: pierde y reclama 3 objetos reales de oficina ("Whose stapler is this? It's mine. Whose folder? It's yours.").
5. **Lost-and-found de oficina (12'):** cada estudiante "pierde" 1 objeto real suyo (celular, llaves, cuaderno) en una mesa comun; en cadena preguntan "Whose ___ is this?" y el dueño reclama "It's mine" (los demas: "It's his/hers"). Sin cuaderno, todo oral.
6. **Ejecucion en parejas (10'):** reparto de responsabilidades reales de su trabajo: A "Whose task is the deadline report?" B "It's mine, but the numbers are yours." Cambio de pareja a mitad.
7. Producto verificable (8'): vuelta completa — cada uno asigna al grupo UNA responsabilidad con posesivo ("This decision is ours; that risk is theirs") en 20 seg. Cuentas moldes limpios en el board.
8. Feedback: logro primero (¿quedo claro de quien es?), forma despues. Errores 2+ parejas -> board anonimo; nombres a tu cuaderno.

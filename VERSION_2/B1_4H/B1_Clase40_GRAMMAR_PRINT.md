## BLOQUE 2 — CAUSATIVE + ADVANCED ADJECTIVES ON PAPER (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds both causatives (active + passive) and forms noun/adjective pairs, prefixed negatives, compounds and impersonal expressions.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M10 + M11):
```
THE CAUSATIVE (B1 Book M10, p.117-126)
ACTIVE (p.119): cause a person to act
  WITH "to": ask/allow/get/tell/want/need
  NO "to":   help/let/make/have   "I made you stay." "I had them sit."
PASSIVE (p.123): the doer doesn't matter
  Subject + HAVE/GET + object + PAST PARTICIPLE
  "I had the car washed." / "I get my hair done."  (get=common, have=formal)

ADVANCED ADJECTIVES (B1 Book M11, p.127-144)
NOUN<->ADJ (p.129): strength/strong · talent/talented · caution/cautious
PREFIX/SUFFIX (p.133): Un/In/Im/Ir/Dis=not · Mis=wrongly · Re=again ·
  -less=without · -ful=full of · -able=can be done  (IMpolite, IRresponsible)
COMPOUND (p.137): "a 20-page report" (number+unit SINGULAR: "3-year-old")
IMPERSONAL (p.141): "It is important to help." (never drop "it")
```
2. Señala: *"have/get + thing + participle to delegate. Pick the noun OR adjective; flip meaning with a prefix; keep 'it' in impersonals."*
3. **Conveyor chain (10', DE PIE, circulo):** tu lanzas una accion ("someone washes my car"); est. 1 la vuelve passive causative ("I have my car washed"), 2 la vuelve active causative ("I make him wash it"), 3 describe al que lo hace con un adjetivo + prefijo ("he is a careful, never careless worker"), 4 lanza la siguiente. Velocidad creciente.
4. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**
   - "I had the car wash" -> "I had the car WASHED" (passive causative needs participle)
   - "She made me to stay" -> "She MADE me STAY" (make/let/have/help no "to")
   - "she is intelligence" -> "she is INTELLIGENT"
   - "unpolite / a 3-years-old child / is important to help" -> "IMpolite / 3-YEAR-old / IT IS important to help"
5. **Zone walk (12', DE PIE):** board `CAUSATIVE | NOUN<->ADJ | PREFIX/SUFFIX | IMPERSONAL/COMPOUND`. Tu cantas un contexto; parejas caminan a la zona correcta Y producen la forma. Si mal, "again" y re-caminan. 14 llamados (min 3 causativas, 2 passive).
6. Cierre del bloque: uso libre — cada uno 2 frases verdaderas: 1 passive causative real ("I got my documents translated") + 1 con noun/adjective pair o impersonal ("It is important to...").

## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B (no leer literal en clase)

**M10 (p.117-126) + M11 (p.127-144) — reglas y ejemplos citados verbatim:**
- **M10 Guide 1 (p.119):** *"Most verbs use the infinitive... if the verb implies that the main action has started, then we do not use the infinitive."* CON "to": ask/allow/get/tell/want/need. SIN "to": help/let/make/have. Libro p.120: *"I always help Sebastian finish his homework / You can't make me love you! / The teacher had the children sit on the floor."*
- **M10 Guide 2 (p.123):** *"Subject + Causative verb ('have' or 'get') + Direct Object + Action (In Past Participle). Only 2 causative verbs exist; 'get' and 'have'. 'Get' is more common, while 'have' is more formal."* Libro p.124: *"I will get my hair done before the party / I had the letter written by my secretary / We had the dinner served at 7:00."*
- **M11 Guide 1 (p.129):** pares noun/adjetivo: health/healthy, bravery/brave, strength/strong, talent/talented, intelligence/intelligent, caution/cautious, wisdom/wise, enthusiasm/enthusiastic, laziness/lazy, carelessness/careless. Libro p.130: *"Samson was a strong man. His strength could destroy a palace."*
- **M11 Guide 2 (p.133):** Un-/In-/Im-/Ir-/Dis- = not; Mis- = incorrectly; Re- = again; -less = without; -ful = full of; -able/-ible = can be done. Libro p.134: *"Tim's answer is incorrect / Germans are usually irreligious / Arturo... He's so shameless."*
- **M11 Guide 3 (p.137):** *"a 150-page book"*; *"when we form a compound adjective with a number and unit, we do not use the units in the plural: 'I have a 3-year-old child.'"* Libro p.138: *"Shakespeare is a well-known author / We have ice-cold beer."*
- **M11 Guide 4 (p.141):** *"'es' -> 'it is'. Subjects can be omitted in Spanish, but never in English."* Libro p.142: *"It is important to help / It is dangerous to drive after drinking alcohol."*

## BLOQUE 2 — GET EN TODOS LOS TIEMPOS (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds GET-change sentences on paper across present, past, future, continuous.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M42, Guia 1 p.335):
```
CHANGES OF STATE WITH GET (A2 Book M42, p.333-338)
Regla (Guia 1 p.335): "get" (o formal "become") = "llegar a estar",
  un cambio de estado, en CUALQUIER tiempo. + adjetivo o participio.
  Present:    I get sick.        She gets angry.
  Past:       I got lost.        We got tired.
  Future:     I will get ready.  It will get late.
  Continuous: I am getting better. The project was getting hard.
  GET + participle (de M41): "She got interested." "I got hurt."
BOOK EXAMPLES (p.336):
  "Camila got bored in the Spanish class."
  "Miguel got tired after the game."
  "Don't get lost in Bogota!"
  "Liam got excited before the concert."
  "The class got quiet before the test."
```
2. **Meta de tarea comunicativa (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, this board holds 12 GET sentences about real work — three in each tense: present, past, future, continuous."*
3. **Preparacion en parejas (7', DE PIE junto al board):** cada pareja arma 1 frase GET por tiempo (present/past/future/continuous), reales de su trabajo.
4. **Ejecucion (12'):** cada pareja ESCRIBE sus 4 frases en el board, las dice, y el grupo verifica: ¿"got" en pasado (no "getted")? ¿"is getting" bien formado? ¿nunca "am get"? Meta: 12+ limpias.
5. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**
   - "I am get tired" -> "I get tired"
   - "I getted / I get" (pasado) -> "I got"
   - "I am getting bored" ok; "I am get bored" -> mal
   - "I got angry" (cambio) vs "I am angry" (estado) — distinguir
6. **Producto verificable:** lectura coral del board; feedback primero sobre el logro, luego la forma.

## BLOQUE 3 — DRILL + THE TURNAROUND (35') — DE PIE 100%

**Objective: fire the right GET-tense at speed, then narrate change in a realistic turnaround.**

1. **Cadena de conversion (12', circulo, DE PIE):** tu cantas un adjetivo/participio + un tiempo ("tired / past" -> "I got tired"; "ready / future" -> "I will get ready"; "complicated / continuous" -> "it is getting complicated"); estudiante 1 forma la frase, 2 la justifica en 1 linea real, 3 lanza el siguiente. Velocidad creciente; ultimo giro: solo cambios reales de SU trabajo.
2. **The turnaround (18'):** escenario profesional realista: contar como un proyecto en problemas se recupero. **1 guest = quien narra el cambio** ("Sales got worse, the team got stressed, but we got organized, I got trained, and it got better."); otro estudiante rota preguntando ("When did it get critical? How did you get ready?"); el resto = **OBSERVERS ACTIVOS** (2 errores, 1 acierto, 1 pregunta). Tu = COACH afuera, nunca guest. 3 rotaciones.
3. **Cierre FORTALEZA (5'):** *"Fortitude, day five — one true line: 'It got hard, but I got ___.'"* — 2 respuestas maximo. *"Block closed. Next opens synthesis — four virtues, one voice."*

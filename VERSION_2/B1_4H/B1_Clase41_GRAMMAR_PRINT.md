## BLOQUE 2 — ADVANCED PRONOUNS ON PAPER (40') — DE PIE ~90%

**Objective: everyone builds indefinite (singular verb), reflexive (subject = object / by + self) and reciprocal (each other) correctly.**

1. Board — cita del libro (M12, Guides 1-3):
```
ADVANCED PRONOUNS (B1 Book M12, p.145-155) — LAST MODULE
INDEFINITE (Guide 1, p.147):  person / place / thing
  every- : everyone/everybody/everywhere/everything
  some-  : someone/somebody/somewhere/something (affirmative)
  any-   : anyone/anybody/anywhere/anything (negative/question)
  no-    : no one/nobody/nowhere/nothing (absence)
  RULE: always 3rd person SINGULAR -> "Everyone IS here."
REFLEXIVE (Guide 2, p.151):  subject = object
  myself/yourself/yourselves/himself/herself/itself/ourselves/themselves
  "I cut MYSELF." | "by + self" = alone: "I work BY MYSELF."
RECIPROCAL (Guide 3, p.155):
  "each other" = 2 · "one another" = 3+ (each other now used for any number)
  "We helped EACH OTHER."
```
2. Señala: *"Everyone/someone always take a SINGULAR verb. Reflexive when the doer and the receiver are the same person. 'By myself' means alone."*
3. **Conveyor chain (10', DE PIE, circulo):** tu lanzas un contexto ("nobody in the office"); est. 1 da la frase indefinite con verbo singular, 2 la vuelve reflexive ("I finished it by myself"), 3 la vuelve reciprocal ("we finished it, helping each other"), 4 lanza el siguiente. Velocidad creciente.
4. **Errores tipicos (board, coro 2x):**

5. **Zone walk (12', DE PIE):** board `INDEFINITE | REFLEXIVE | RECIPROCAL | + BOOK-CLOSE zone`. ## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B (no leer literal en clase)

**M12 (p.145-155) — reglas y ejemplos citados verbatim (ULTIMO modulo del libro):**
- **Guide 1 (p.147):** every/some/any/no + one/body/where/thing. *"Indefinite pronouns are always used as a 3rd person singular (even if they refer to multiples). CORRECT: Everyone is here. INCORRECT: Everyone are here."* Some = afirmativo; any = negativo/pregunta; no = ausencia; "any" tambien = cualquier ("Anything can happen"). Ejemplos p.148: *"Everyone is angry that the flight arrived late / No one can help me. And I can't help anyone / Is anybody working today?"*
- **Guide 2 (p.151):** *"When the subject and object of a verb are the same, we use the reflexive pronoun."* Tabla: I/myself, you/yourself(-ves), he/himself, she/herself, it/itself, we/ourselves, they/themselves. *"We also often use 'by' + reflexive pronoun to indicate 'alone': 'I am eating by myself.'"* Ejemplos p.152: *"I am going to teach myself Spanish / Don't blame yourself / Ray and Gil built the house by themselves."*
- **Guide 3 (p.155):** *"'Each other' - 2 actors. 'One another' - 3 or more actors."* Ejemplo: *"Becky and Sandy talked with each other."* *"'One another' is becoming less common... Many speakers use 'each other' for any number."*

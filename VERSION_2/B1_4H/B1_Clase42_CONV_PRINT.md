## BLOQUE 1 — APERTURA (20')

**Objective: reactivate the course's real B1 structures across the arc, then aim at building the presentation.**

1. **Recuperacion (10', oral, DE PIE, cadena, sin cuaderno).** N-3 = Cl 39 (M9 perfect tenses) · N-7 = Cl 35 (M3-M4 deduction + third conditional); errores anonimos al board; 1 min de re-produccion:
   - "Perfect timeline: 'I HAVE finished the audit, I HAD prepared before it started, by the deadline I WILL HAVE closed it.'" (Cl 39 · M9)
   - "Marker check: 'I've worked here SINCE 2021 / FOR three years — I haven't finished YET.'" (Cl 39 · M9)
   - "Deduction — a colleague isn't answering: 'He MUST be busy / He MIGHT have left / He CAN'T be home.'" (Cl 35 · M3)
   - "Third conditional — a real work regret: 'If I HAD checked the file, I WOULDN'T HAVE sent the error.'" (Cl 35 · M4)
2. **Chequeo publico de portafolio (3'):** conteo en voz alta del audio de ayer; no-envios a tu cuaderno.
3. **Frase del Dia (4').** Board antes; 2 lecturas + coro + 1 solo; 3+ inserciones. Señala: *"Temperance = steady pace, no rushing the story. Justice = honest, kind feedback to your partner. Both, today."*
4. **Revision tarea (3'):** tarea de Cl 41 (esqueleto de la presentacion final + portafolio) sobre la mesa; recoge. **PASE de Grammar (Cl 41)** leido: hoy construimos y pulimos la presentacion; Grammar afina la precision hoy mismo.

> [VIRTUD] Board: "SYNTHESIS — DAY 2 · TEMPERANCE + JUSTICE". *"How do temperance and justice look together? In rehearsal: I control my nerves and pace (temperance) and I give my partner true, fair feedback (justice). We rise together."*

## BLOQUE 2 — BUILD THE SKELETON (45')

**Objective: every student leaves with a written 3-part skeleton of their final presentation (7-10 min) that shows the structures of the whole B1 course.**

1. **Meta de proyecto (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each person has the skeleton of their real-story presentation on paper — three parts, showing the structures we learned all level."*
2. Board — el molde de la presentacion (3 partes; con las 5 estructuras minimas del curso marcadas):
```
FINAL PRESENTATION — MY REAL STORY (7-10 min + questions)
PART 1 — WHO I WAS (past + reported speech):
   "When I started, my teacher TOLD me my English WAS weak;
    she SAID I couldn't hold a meeting." (M8 reported speech)
PART 2 — WHO I AM NOW (perfect + comparison + tag):
   "I HAVE worked in English for months; I'm better THAN I was,
    stronger THAN before — that's real progress, isn't it?"
   (M9 perfect · comparatives · M1 tag question)
PART 3 — WHERE I'M GOING (future + relative/embedded):
   "The goal WHICH matters most is to get certified; I know
    WHERE I want to be next year." (M7 relative + embedded)
SHOW at least: 1 reported speech · 1 perfect tense · 1 relative
or embedded question · 1 tag question · 1 comparative.
A structured true story, NOT a speech read word-by-word.
```
3. **Construccion individual guiada (18'):** cada estudiante escribe su esqueleto (bullets, no discurso completo): 3-4 lineas por parte, marcando al lado que estructura de la lista usa. Tu rotas y ayudas a que cada parte luzca al menos una de las 5 estructuras minimas (no traduccion suelta). Es escritura de apoyo para hablar, no texto para leer.
4. **Meta comunicativa — primer intento oral en parejas (12'):** A cuenta su esqueleto en voz alta (2-3 min), B escucha como OBSERVER con tarea (marca cuales de las 5 estructuras oyo + 1 duda). Cambian. Sin leer palabra por palabra.
5. **Producto verificable:** cada estudiante tiene su esqueleto de 3 partes en papel (con las estructuras marcadas) + lo dijo una vez. Tu anotas quien ya luce las 5 estructuras y quien va corto (a tu cuaderno, sin nombres al board).

## BLOQUE 3 — FIRST REHEARSAL — GUEST & PANEL (30')

**Objective: rehearse the presentation once in front of a small audience with fair, structured feedback.**

1. Board: `REHEARSAL — "MY STORY" — GUEST PRESENTS, GROUP OBSERVES — ROTATIONS`
2. Simulacion de ensayo (realista, formato de la presentacion final): **1 guest = quien presenta** su esqueleto (~2-3 min) ante el grupo; el resto = **OBSERVERS ACTIVOS con tarea justa** (cada uno anota: 1 fortaleza real, cuales de las 5 estructuras del curso escucho, 1 sugerencia amable). Otro estudiante rota como quien hace 1 pregunta al final ("What have you achieved this level? Where are you going next?"). Tu = COACH y cronometras — NUNCA presentas tu por ellos.
3. Rotaciones segun tiempo (~4-5 estudiantes ensayan hoy; el resto ensaya la proxima). Debrief JUSTO por TIPOS (anonimo): que estructuras salieron limpias, cual falto; feedback primero al logro, luego la forma.
4. **Cierre SINTESIS dia 2 (4'):** *"Temperance + justice: give your partner ONE honest, kind piece of feedback out loud — name one structure they used well."* — 2-3 respuestas. Sin notas ni evaluacion: es ensayo, no examen.

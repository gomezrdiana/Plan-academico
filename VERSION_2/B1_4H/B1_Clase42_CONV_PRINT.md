## BLOQUE 1 — APERTURA (20')

**Objective: reactivate the course's core tools across the arc, then aim at building the presentation.**

1. **Recuperacion integral (10', oral, DE PIE, cadena, sin cuaderno).** Repaso del arco reciente (Cl 39 y Cl 35), enfocado a lo que la presentacion necesita; errores anonimos al board; 1 min de re-produccion:
   - "Feeling turn: 'At first English was ___ing; now I am ___ed.'" (Cl 39 · M41 — key line for the story)
   - "Change with GET: 'I got started, I got better, I will get certified.'" (Cl 40 · M42)
   - "Comparison for growth: 'I'm better than I was; my English is stronger than before.'" (Cl 31 arc · comparatives)
   - "Quantities of your journey: 'How many months? How much progress?'" (Cl 35 window)
2. **Chequeo publico de portafolio (3'):** conteo en voz alta del audio de ayer; no-envios a tu cuaderno.
3. **Frase del Dia (4').** Board antes; 2 lecturas + coro + 1 solo; 3+ inserciones. Señala: *"Temperance = steady pace, no rushing the story. Justice = honest, kind feedback to your partner. Both, today."*
4. **Revision tarea (3'):** tarea de Cl 41 (adverbs + "want you to") sobre la mesa; recoge. **PASE de Grammar (Cl 41)** leido: hoy construimos la presentacion; Grammar la pule hoy mismo.

> [VIRTUD] Board: "SYNTHESIS — DAY 2 · TEMPERANCE + JUSTICE". *"How do temperance and justice look together? In rehearsal: I control my nerves and pace (temperance) and I give my partner true, fair feedback (justice). We rise together."*

## BLOQUE 2 — BUILD THE SKELETON (45')

**Objective: every student leaves with a written 3-part skeleton of their final presentation (7-10 min) using only course structures.**

1. **Meta de proyecto (1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each person has the skeleton of their real-story presentation on paper — three parts, only structures we learned."*
2. Board — el molde de la presentacion (3 partes; usa SOLO estructuras del curso):
```
FINAL PRESENTATION — MY REAL STORY (7-10 min + questions)
PART 1 — WHO I WAS (past): "When I started, I couldn't...,
   English was confusing, I got nervous..." (past, participle adj)
PART 2 — WHO I AM NOW (present + comparison): "Now I can...,
   I am better than I was, I speak more confidently, I work
   carefully..." (present, comparatives, adverbs)
PART 3 — MY FUTURE (future + wishes): "I will get..., I want to...,
   I want my team to..., my next goal is..." (future, causative)
ONLY course structures. Real, professional, honest. No memorized
speech read word-by-word — a structured story.
```
3. **Construccion individual guiada (18'):** cada estudiante escribe su esqueleto (bullets, no discurso completo): 3-4 lineas por parte, marcando que estructura usa. Tu rotas y ayudas a que cada linea use una estructura del curso (no traduccion suelta). Es escritura de apoyo para hablar, no texto para leer.
4. **Meta comunicativa — primer intento oral en parejas (12'):** A cuenta su esqueleto en voz alta (2-3 min), B escucha como OBSERVER con tarea (1 cosa clara, 1 estructura bien usada, 1 duda). Cambian. Sin leer palabra por palabra.
5. **Producto verificable:** cada estudiante tiene su esqueleto de 3 partes en papel + lo dijo una vez. Tu anotas quien ya tiene las 3 partes y quien va corto (a tu cuaderno, sin nombres al board).

## BLOQUE 3 — FIRST REHEARSAL — GUEST & PANEL (30')

**Objective: rehearse the presentation once in front of a small audience with fair, structured feedback.**

1. Board: `REHEARSAL — "MY STORY" — GUEST PRESENTS, GROUP OBSERVES — ROTATIONS`
2. Simulacion de ensayo (realista, formato de la presentacion final): **1 guest = quien presenta** su esqueleto (~2-3 min) ante el grupo; el resto = **OBSERVERS ACTIVOS con tarea justa** (cada uno anota: 1 fortaleza real, 1 estructura bien usada, 1 sugerencia amable). Otro estudiante rota como quien hace 1 pregunta al final ("What was the hardest part? What is your next goal?"). Tu = COACH y cronometras — NUNCA presentas tu por ellos.
3. Rotaciones segun tiempo (~4-5 estudiantes ensayan hoy; el resto ensaya la proxima). Debrief JUSTO por TIPOS (anonimo): que estructuras salieron limpias, que patron repetir; feedback primero al logro, luego la forma.
4. **Cierre SINTESIS dia 2 (4'):** *"Temperance + justice: give your partner ONE honest, kind piece of feedback out loud."* — 2-3 respuestas. Sin notas ni evaluacion: es ensayo, no examen.

## ANEXO — banco de estructuras del curso + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Estructuras del curso disponibles para la presentacion (recordatorio para el profe; el estudiante elige):**
```
Pasado: simple past, past continuous, participle adjectives
  (bored/confused), "I got + adj" (change).
Presente: present simple/continuous, present perfect (I have
  learned), comparatives (-er/more...than, better/worse),
  superlatives (the best), adverbs of manner (carefully/well).
Futuro: will / going to, "I will get...", causative ("I want
  you to...", "my goal is to...").
Funciones: phrasal verbs, gerund/infinitive after verbs.
```

**Plan B (cero material del profe):**

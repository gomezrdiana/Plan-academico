## ANEXO — VERBATIM DEL LIBRO + PLAN B + NOTA INTERNA

### M1 — Question Tags (B1 Book, Guide 1 p.3; Examples p.4; Exercises p.5)
Cita p.3: *"The question tag uses the same auxiliary as the principal statement. If the statement is affirmative, the tag is negative... If the statement is negative, the tag is affirmative."* + *"I am very tall, aren't I? (Amn't does not exist, so we use aren't for I)."*
Ejemplos verbatim p.4: *"You went to school, didn't you? / You didn't go to school, did you?"* · *"The boys are playing tennis, aren't they?"* · *"We can eat lunch, can't we?"* · *"You know Santiago, don't you?"*

### M2 — Either / Neither / Both (Guide 1 "Either…or" p.9; Guide 2 "Neither…nor" p.13; Guide 3 "Both…and" p.17)
Cita p.9: *"We use 'either' to say 'o' or 'cualquier'. It indicates TWO options."* — *"We can speak either Spanish or English."*
Cita p.13 (IMPORTANT): *"'Neither…nor' are never used with a negative 'not'."* — *"I can speak neither Spanish nor English."* (NO "can't speak neither").
Cita p.17: *"'Both…and' is the equivalent to 'ambos…y'... we do not use 'both' to say 'tambien'; we use 'also' or 'too'."* — *"We can speak both Spanish and English."*

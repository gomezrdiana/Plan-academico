## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M24: verbos frasales SEPARABLES

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.234):** Turn on (prender/arrancar) · Turn off (apagar) · Put on (ponerse ropa) · Take off - took off (quitarse ropa) · Pick up (recoger cosa o persona) · Look up (buscar en libro/lista/internet) · a radio · a TV · a computer · a light. Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M24, Guia 1, p.235):**
1. La gran mayoria de verbos frasales son SEPARABLES. Es opcional colocar el objeto directo despues del verbo frasal O entre las dos palabras: *"I pick up Amy."* = *"I pick Amy up."* (ambos validos).
2. PERO cuando reemplazamos el objeto directo con un PRONOMBRE, ese pronombre DEBE ir EN MEDIO: *"I pick her up."* (correcto) — nunca *"I pick up her."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.235):** *Did you turn on the TV? → Yes, I turned it on. · Will you turn off your phone? → Yes, I will turn it off. · Did you put on your hat? → Yes, I put it on. · Does Amanda take off her shoes? → Yes, she takes them off. · Will Alvaro pick up his sister? → Yes, he will pick her up. · Did you look up Shakira on Google? → Yes, I looked her up.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have described your morning and your workplace, and every time you use a pronoun you will put it IN THE MIDDLE (turn it off, pick her up)."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 6 frases reales (*turn on / turn off / put on / take off / pick up / look up*) con objeto completo, y luego repite 3 cambiando el objeto por pronombre EN MEDIO (*turn off the computer → turn it off*).
2. Ejecucion: se las dicen; la pareja verifica que con pronombre quede EN MEDIO (*pick her up*, nunca *pick up her*) y hace 1 pregunta (*"When do you pick them up?"*).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su rutina mas util (*"I always turn off the lights before I go."*).
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~I pick up her~~ → I pick HER up · ~~turn off it~~ → turn IT off · ~~I put on it~~ → I put IT on.

## ANEXO — M24 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 24 — "I'll Pick You Up at 8:00." — Separable Phrasal Verbs (p.233-240). Habilidades objetivas del libro:** "Sin dudar, aprender el vocabulario del modulo. Sin dudar, usar verbos frasales en varios tiempos. Sin dudar, usar verbos separables con objetos directos y pronombres. Hablar con fluidez y sin pausas."

**Guia 1 (p.235), reglas tal cual:**
1. La gran mayoria de los verbos frasales son del tipo SEPARABLE. Es OPCIONAL colocar el objeto directo despues del verbo frasal, o entre las dos palabras. *"I pick up Amy." / "I pick Amy up."* (ambos validos).
2. PERO cuando reemplazamos el objeto directo con un pronombre objeto, este pronombre DEBE ir entre las dos palabras. *"I pick her up."* (correcto), nunca *"I pick up her."*

**Vocabulario (p.234):** turn on (prender/arrancar) · turn off (apagar) · put on - put on (ponerse ropa) · take off - took off (quitarse ropa) · pick up (recoger una cosa o persona) · look up (buscar en un libro, lista o internet) · a radio · a TV · a computer · a light.

**Ejemplos completos (p.235):** *Did you turn on the TV? → Yes, I turned it on. / No, I didn't turn it on. · Does Sally turn on her phone? → Yes, she turns it on. · Will you turn off your phone in the class? → Yes, I will turn it off. · Did you put on your hat? → Yes, I put it on. · Does Amanda take off her shoes in the house? → Yes, she takes them off. · Will Alvaro pick up the pencil? → Yes, he will pick it up. · Will Alvaro pick up his sister? → Yes, he will pick her up. · Where will Alvaro pick up his sister? → He will pick her up in the school. · Did you look up Shakira on Google? → Yes, I looked her up. · Do you look up your friends on Facebook? → Yes, I look them up.*

**Practica de pronombres separables (Practica Verbal 2, p.239):** *Is Pepita turning the light on? → No, she isn't turning it on. · Is Pepito turning the lights off? → Yes, he's turning them off. · Are you putting your jacket on? → Yes, I'm putting it on. · Are they taking their shoes off? → No, they aren't taking them off. · Is he picking his children up? → Yes, he's picking them up.*

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — Repaso activo: las TRES caras del GERUNDIO

Este es REPASO: no se re-explica, se PRODUCE. Recordatorio corto, drill de pie, produccion masiva.

**Recordatorio en tablero (verbatim del libro):**
- **1. Gerundio SUJETO (M20, Guia 1, p.183):** cuando un verbo va en la posicion del sujeto, va en **verbo + -ing**: *Playing is fun. · Working in the morning is boring. · Studying English is difficult.* (nunca *"Play is fun"* ni *"The swimming"*).
- **2. Gerundio tras PREPOSICION (M21, Guia 1, p.199):** despues de *before / after / without / about* el verbo va en **-ing**: *I never eat before swimming. · I never speak without thinking. · I read about playing golf.*
- **3. DOBLE VERBO (M22, Guia 1, p.208-209):** el 2do verbo va en infinitivo o gerundio segun el 1ro. **TO + verbo:** *want / need / decide / forget / have to* (accion no empezada: *I want to play*). **verbo + -ing:** *start / finish / stop / like / love* (accion ya empezada: *I like playing*).

**Drill de arranque, TODO de pie (12'):**
1. Estaciones del gerundio: 3 zonas en tablero (SUBJECT · AFTER A PREPOSITION · DOUBLE VERB). Tu dices un verbo (work, sleep, eat, study, drive); cada estudiante elige una zona, camina y produce una frase real de esa forma. Rota la zona por vuelta.
2. Cadena de metas: A dice una meta con doble verbo (*"I decided to start walking."*), B la transforma o agrega (*"I stopped eating late."*) y lanza al proximo. Velocidad creciente, sin cuaderno.

**Construccion desde el error (8'):** copia 4-6 errores REALES (anonimos), del error paper de Cl 44 o de hoy: ~~Play soccer is fun~~ → PLAYING soccer is fun · ~~I eat before swim~~ → before swimmING · ~~I want playing~~ → I want TO PLAY · ~~I like to swimming~~ → I like SWIMMING · ~~I stopped to smoke~~ → I stopped SMOKING. El grupo reconstruye; cada estudiante produce 1 ejemplo propio corregido.

## ANEXO — verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**M20 — "Playing is Fun!" — Gerunds as Subjects (p.181-196). Guia 1 (p.183):** el gerundio (verbo+ing) puede ocupar la posicion del sujeto; el orden sujeto-verbo-objeto es estricto. Vocab descriptores (p.182): fun, boring, important, expensive, difficult, easy. **Ejemplos (p.184):** *Playing is fun. · Working in the morning is boring. · Eating breakfast is important. · Living in London is expensive. · Studying Spanish is difficult. · Speaking English is easy.*

**M21 — "We Shouldn't Swim After Eating" — Gerunds after Prepositions (p.197-206). Guia 1 (p.199):** tras una preposicion el verbo va en gerundio; *before/after* (tiempo), *without* (sin), *about* (tema). **Ejemplos (p.200):** *I never eat before swimming. · I never swim after eating. · I never speak without thinking. · I read about playing golf. · You should ask before using the car.*

**M22 — "I Love Swimming!" — Gerunds with Double Verbs (p.207-220). Guia 1 (p.208-209):** con dos verbos, el 2do va en infinitivo o gerundio segun el 1ro. Infinitivo (accion no empezada): *decide/forget/need/want/have to + verbo*. Gerundio (accion ya empezada): *start/finish/stop/like/love + verbo-ing*. **Ejemplos (p.209):** *I want to wake up early. · I need to shower. · We decide to travel tomorrow. · I start studying. · We finish talking. · I love eating ice cream. · The girls stop fighting.*

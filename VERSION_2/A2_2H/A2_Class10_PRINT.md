## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M12: Pasado simple vs pasado continuo

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M12, Guide 1, p.99-100):**
1. Del nivel A1: **pasado simple** ("I played") y **continuo** ("I was playing"). Pasado simple = un evento o accion simple, terminado en el pasado en un tiempo ESPECIFICO. *"I played yesterday."* — Pasado continuo = un evento o accion en el pasado con extension o DURACION. *"I was playing for three hours."*
2. Otro uso importante: una actividad CORTA o abrupta que ocurre o interrumpe otra actividad mas EXTENSA. *"I was playing when my mom called."* = Yo estaba jugando cuando mi madre llamo.

**Estructura (escribela grande):**

```
LONG action  = was/were + verb-ING   (past continuous, background)
SHORT action = verb in past simple    (the interruption)
Joined by:   WHEN  (before the short)  /  WHILE  (before the long)
```

**Ejemplos del libro (p.101):** *I was living in Bucaramanga in 2019. · I ate a sandwich at 8:00. · Eliana was studying for her exams last week. · I was eating pasta when my brother arrived. · My dad was working when I ate lunch. · We were walking in the park when Jeff saw a dog. · What were you doing when I was studying?*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea, dila al inicio):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have told a partner about 2 real moments that were interrupted."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 2 combinaciones reales — una accion larga (was/were + -ing) + una interrupcion corta (past simple) + when/while. Notas minimas.
2. Ejecucion: se las cuentan; la pareja identifica cual es la accion LARGA (continuous) y cual la INTERRUPCION (simple).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes cuentan al grupo su mejor interrupcion. El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero sobre el LOGRO (contaron un momento real), despues sobre la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos en tablero, coro 2x):** ~~I was play when my mom called~~ → I was PLAYING when my mom called · ~~I played when my mom was calling~~ → I was PLAYING when my mom CALLED · ~~What you were doing when...?~~ → What WERE YOU doing when...?

## ANEXO — M12 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 12 — "I Was Playing when My Mom Called." — Past Simple vs. Past Continuous (p.99-106). Objetivo del libro:** "Diferenciar correctamente entre los usos del pasado simple y el pasado continuo."

**Guide 1 (p.99-100), reglas tal cual:**
1. En el nivel A1, aprendimos el pasado simple ("I played") y continuo ("I was playing"). El pasado simple = un evento o accion simple, terminado en el pasado en un tiempo especifico ("I played yesterday."). El pasado continuo = un evento o accion en el pasado que tenia una extension o duracion ("I was playing for three hours.").
2. Otro uso muy importante para las dos formas es describir una actividad corta o abrupta que ocurre o interrumpe otra actividad mas extensa. "I was playing when my mom called." → "Yo estaba jugando cuando mi madre llamo."

**Ejemplos completos (p.101):** *I was living in Bucaramanga in 2019. · I ate a sandwich at 8:00. · Steve woke up at 6:30 today. · Eliana was studying for her exams last week. · I was eating pasta when my brother arrived. · My dad was working when I ate lunch. · We were walking in the park, when Jeff saw a dog. · The family was watching a movie when Sebastian left.*
**Negativa / pregunta (p.101):** *I wasn't driving the Model T last year. · The girls weren't driving to school when their dad took a cab. · What time did you get dressed? · Where was Joe working last year? · What were you doing when I was studying? · Did you work when Jack was feeling sick?*
**Practica verbal para prompts (p.104):** *You studied. · The engineer was living in Canada. · The boy felt sick while the woman was swimming. · The children ate breakfast while the man was working.*

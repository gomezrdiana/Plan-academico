## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M33: Cantidades incontables (how much / a bit of / a lot of)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.284):** A kilo · A liter · A gallon · A bit of (un poco de) · A glass (un vaso) · A bottle (una botella) · Wine · Whisky. Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M33, Guia 1, p.285):**
1. *"How much...?"* significa *"¿Cuanto...?"* — se usa para sustancias INCONTABLES (liquidos, polvos, gases).
2. Se coloca antes del elemento: *"How much milk do you have?"* → *"¿Cuanta leche tienes tu?"*
3. Se miden por UNIDADES: *"I have 1 liter of milk. / I have 1 kilo of butter. / I have 300 pesos."* (la plata es incontable; pesos/dolares son las unidades).
4. Tambien con *there is*: *"How much milk is there?"* → *"¿Cuanta leche hay?"*
5. *a lot of / lots of* = mucho (incontable). *a bit of* = un poco de. *"I have a lot of money. / I have a bit of money."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.288-290):** *How much milk is there? — There are 4 liters of milk. · How much coffee is there? — There is a bit of coffee. · How much money is there? — There are sixty-seven dollars. · How much pasta does Pablo have? — He has 3 kilos of pasta. · How much pizza does Karol have? — She has a lot of pizza. · How much broccoli does Diego have? — He has a bit of broccoli.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have asked and answered 5 'How much ___?' questions using units, a bit of and a lot of."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno prepara 5 preguntas *"How much ___ do you have / is there?"* sobre cosas reales incontables (coffee, water, money, time, work).
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta, B responde con unidad, *a bit of* o *a lot of* (*"How much coffee do you drink?" — "A lot of coffee — 3 cups a day."*). Cambian.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes hacen al grupo su pregunta; el grupo responde por si mismo antes de oir al companero.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~How many money?~~ → How MUCH money? · ~~a bit of dollars~~ → a few dollars (contable) / a bit of MONEY (incontable) · ~~two milks~~ → two LITERS OF milk.

**Libro A2, Module 33 — "How Much Money Do You Have?" — Specifying Uncountable Quantities (p.283-292). Habilidades objetivas del libro:** "Sin dudar, aprender el vocabulario del modulo. Sin dudar, formar preguntas usando 'how much' y responder correctamente. Hablar con fluidez y sin pausas y dudas, usando una velocidad adecuada."

**Vocabulario (p.284):** a kilo · a liter · a gallon · a bit of... (un poco de) · wine · whisky · a glass (un vaso) · a bottle (una botella).

**Guia 1 (p.285), reglas tal cual:**
1. Las palabras interrogativas "how much...?" significan "¿Cuanto...?"
2. Se usan para sustancias incontables (ej. liquidos, polvos, gases). "How much milk do you have?" = "¿Cuanta leche tienes tu?"
3. Medimos sustancias incontables por unidades: "I have 1 liter of milk." / "I have 1 kilo of butter." / "I have 300 pesos." (Plata es incontable; pesos, dolares, etc., son las unidades.)
4. Tambien con "there is / is there": "How much milk is there?" = "¿Cuanta leche hay?"
5. "a lot of / lots of" = "mucho" para incontables. "a bit of..." = "un poco de...": "I have a lot of money. / I have a bit of money."

**Ejemplos completos (p.288-292):** *How much cheese is there? — There are 2 kilos of cheese. · How much water is there? — There are 3 gallons of water. · How much coffee is there? — There is a bit of coffee. · How much wine is there? — There are 4 glasses of wine. · How much money is there? — There are sixty-seven dollars. · How much money does Doris have? — She has ninety-five dollars. · How much wine do Mary and Jose have? — They have a bit of wine. · How much pizza does Karol have? — She has a lot of pizza. · How much coffee do you need? — I need a bit of coffee.*

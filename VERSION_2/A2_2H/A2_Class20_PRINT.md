## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M23: verbos frasales NO separables

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.222):** Ask for (pedir) · Go back - went back (regresar/devolverse) · Look at (mirar a algo/alguien) · Listen to (escuchar) · Run into - ran into (encontrar por sorpresa) · Look after (cuidar a una persona). Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M23, Guia 1, p.222):**
1. Un verbo frasal = verbo + una preposicion/adverbio = un NUEVO verbo. *"Look"* (mirar) + *"after"* (despues) = *"look after"* (cuidar).
2. Ya conoces 3 verbos frasales: *wake up, get dressed, hang out*.
3. Las conjugaciones se aplican a la PRIMERA palabra (el verbo): *"I look after Amy. · He looks after Amy. · He looked after Amy. · He is looking after Amy."*
4. Hay verbos "separables" y "no separables". Estos son NO SEPARABLES: las dos palabras nunca se separan; el objeto directo va AL FINAL. *"I look after Amy." → "I look after her."* (el pronombre tambien va al final).

**Ejemplos del libro (p.223), en pregunta/respuesta:** *What will Frank ask for? → He will ask for a pencil. · When did Sarah go back to Colombia? → She went back last week. · Who do you listen to in the class? → I listen to the teacher. · Who did you run into in the mall? → I ran into my friends. · Are you looking after your sister? → Yes, I'm looking after her. · Did you look at the dog? → Yes, I looked at it. · Can you ask for the shoes? → Yes, I can ask for them.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have used all six phrasal verbs about a real day, and replaced the object with a pronoun (her / him / it / them)."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 6 frases reales de un dia normal, una por verbo (*ask for / go back / look at / listen to / run into / look after*), y luego repite 2 de ellas cambiando el objeto por pronombre (*I look after my sister → I look after her*).
2. Ejecucion: se las dicen; la pareja verifica que el pronombre quede AL FINAL (no separan las dos palabras) y hace 1 pregunta de seguimiento (*"Who do you look after?"*).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su frase mas util (*"I always listen to my manager."*).
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~I look her after~~ → I look after HER · ~~I asked a pencil~~ → I asked FOR a pencil · ~~I listen the teacher~~ → I listen TO the teacher.

**Libro A2, Module 23 — "I Asked for A Pencil." — Basic, Non-Separable Phrasal Verbs (p.221-232). Habilidades objetivas del libro:** "Sin dudar, aprender el vocabulario del modulo. Sin dudar, usar verbos frasales en varios tiempos. Sin dudar, usar verbos no separables con objetos directos y pronombres. Hablar con fluidez, sin pausas y dudas."

**Guia 1 (p.222), reglas tal cual:**
1. Los verbos frasales consisten en un verbo + una preposicion/adverbio = un nuevo verbo. *"Look" + "after" = "look after" (cuidar)*.
2. Ya has aprendido 3 verbos frasales: *wake up, get dressed, hang out*.
3. Las conjugaciones se aplican al verbo (la primera palabra) de forma normal: Presente *I look after Amy* · 3ra sing. *He looks after Amy* · Pasado *He looked after Amy* · Continuo *He is looking after Amy*.
4. Hay verbos separables (la mayoria) y NO separables. En este modulo miramos los NO separables: nunca se separan las dos palabras; los objetos directos van al final. *I look after Amy → I look after her*.

**Vocabulario (p.222):** ask for (pedir) · go back - went back (regresar/devolverse) · look at (mirar a una persona/cosa) · listen to (escuchar) · run into - ran into (encontrar sin intencion / por sorpresa) · look after (cuidar a una persona).

**Ejemplos completos (p.223):** *What time do you wake up? → I wake up at 6:00. · Where did you get dressed? → I got dressed in my house. · What will Frank ask for in the class? → He will ask for a pencil. · When did Sarah go back to Colombia? → She went back last week. · Who do we look at in the class? → We look at the teacher. · Who do you listen to in the class? → I listen to the teacher. · Who did you run into in the mall? → I ran into my friends. · Are you looking after your sister? → Yes, I'm looking after her. · Can you ask for the shoes? → Yes, I can ask for them. · Did you look at the dog yesterday? → Yes, I looked at it. · Did you look after your sister? → Yes, I looked after her.*

**Practica de pronombres objeto (Practica Verbal 3, p.231):** *Is Alina looking at the car? → No, she isn't looking at it. · Is Mark listening to his teacher? → Yes, he's listening to her. · Are you running into the man? → Yes, I'm running into him. · Are they looking after their children? → No, they aren't looking after them. · Am I asking for the sandwich? → Yes, you're asking for it.*

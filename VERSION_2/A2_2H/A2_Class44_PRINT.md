## BLOQUE 2 (43') — Repaso activo: PAST vs PERFECT + la VOZ PASIVA

Este es REPASO: no se re-explica, se PRODUCE. Recordatorio corto, drill de pie, produccion masiva.

**Recordatorio en tablero (verbatim del libro):**
- **Past vs Perfect (M17, Guia 1, p.164):** periodo CERRADO (yesterday, last week, at 2:00) → **pasado simple** (*I studied last year*). Periodo ABIERTO (today, this week, this year) → **presente perfecto** (*I have studied this year*). En negativo: *"I didn't say hello"* (no lo dije y no lo hare) vs *"I haven't said hello"* (no todavia, pero puede pasar).
- **Voz pasiva (M18, Guia 1, p.174):** cuando el foco es el OBJETO que recibe la accion (el actor es desconocido o no importa). Estructura: **[cosa] + is/are + participio (+ by + agente)**. El *by* se puede ELIMINAR: *"The lunch is prepared." · "Soccer is played in Colombia." · "You are loved!"*
- Ejemplos del libro: *I studied at 2:00 → I have studied today. · The game is played by Sebastian. · English is studied by Mariana. · The office is cleaned at night. · Students are helped in the morning.*

**Drill de arranque, TODO de pie (12'):**

## ANEXO — verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 17 — "You Didn't, or You Haven't?" — Present Perfect vs Simple Past, p.163-172. Guia 1 (p.164), reglas tal cual:**
1. Pasado simple: accion terminada en un punto especifico en el pasado (*I studied at 2:00*).
2. Presente perfecto 1: accion hecha o no hecha en un periodo desconocido (*I have studied*).
3. Presente perfecto 2: accion que empezo en el pasado y no ha terminado (*I have studied for 2 hours*).
4. Presente perfecto 3: accion terminada, pero en un periodo que sigue abierto (*I have studied today*).
5. Clave: *"I studied last year"* (periodo cerrado → pasado) vs *"I have studied this year"* (periodo abierto → perfecto).
6. En negativo: *"I didn't say hello"* (no lo dije, no lo hare) vs *"I haven't said hello"* (no todavia, pero se puede).
**Ejemplos (Verbal Practice, p.171-172):** *Ana has been sick today. · Lucas took a bus in New York last night. · You drove to the office last week. · We have told John this month. · I was in Orlando last weekend. · It has gone to the house this year.*

**Libro A2, Module 18 — "You Are Loved!" — Passive Voice (Regular Participles), p.173-180. Guia 1 (p.174), reglas tal cual:** la voz pasiva enfoca el paciente (el que recibe la accion) cuando el actor no importa o es desconocido; estructura paciente + *to be* + participio (+ *by* + agente); el verbo principal toma la forma del participio; el agente se puede eliminar. **Ejemplos (p.175):** *The game is played by Sebastian. · English is studied by Mariana. · The dog is loved by the boy. · The teacher is helped by us.* — sin agente: *Soccer is played in Colombia. · You are loved! · The office is cleaned at night. · Students are helped in the morning.* (M18 trabaja pasiva en PRESENTE, participios REGULARES, solo afirmativa — no introducir pasado, irregulares ni negativa/pregunta: no esta en el modulo.)

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M29: Grados de un adjetivo (intensificadores)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.262):** Really / Very (muy) · Kinda / Sorta (mas o menos) · Pretty (bastante) · Not very (no muy — cortes, implica lo opuesto). Descriptores utiles: tall, nice, new, old, good, bad, intelligent, rich, serious, early, late, complicated, expensive, important, hard. Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M29, Guia 1, p.261-262):**
1. A veces queremos describir algo en terminos menos exactos, subjetivos.
2. Para PREGUNTAR usamos "how". Antes de un descriptor, "how" significa "que tan ___". Ej. *"How tall are you?"* → *"¿Que tan alto eres tu?"*
3. Para RESPONDER usamos un intensificador ANTES del descriptor.
4. *"Not very"* dice lo opuesto de manera cortes: *"He's not very old. He's 38."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.263):** *How nice is Ana? — She's very nice. · How old is your dad? — He's not very old. He's 38. · How intelligent is Sally? — She's kinda intelligent. · How hard is your job? — It's pretty hard, but I'm learning. · How early is the class? — It's pretty early. It starts at 6:00 AM.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have ASKED and ANSWERED 5 real 'How + adjective' questions using intensifiers."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno prepara 5 preguntas *"How ___ is/are ___?"* sobre cosas reales (su ciudad, su trabajo, su telefono, su jefe, su clase).
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta, B responde con un intensificador + dato real (*"How expensive is your city?" — "It's pretty expensive, but not very expensive for food."*). Cambian.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes hacen al grupo su pregunta mas interesante; el grupo adivina la respuesta antes de oirla.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~How much tall are you?~~ → How TALL are you? · ~~She is very much nice~~ → She is very nice · ~~He's not very tall no~~ → He's not very tall.

**Guia 1 (p.261-262), reglas tal cual:**
1. A veces, queremos describir algo en terminos menos exactos, es decir, subjetivos.
2. Para preguntar, usamos la palabra de pregunta "how". Antes de un descriptor, "how" significa "que tan ___". Ej. "How tall are you?" = "¿Que tan alto eres tu?"
3. Para responder, usamos un intensificador antes del descriptor. Aqui vemos 3 (mas la forma cortes).
4. Intensificadores: really (muy, informal) · very (muy, informal o academico) · kinda / sorta (mas o menos) · pretty (bastante) · not very (no muy). *"No soy muy alto"* implica *"soy bajo"* — forma cortes.

**Ejemplos completos (p.263):** *How nice is Ana? — She's very nice. · How new is your car? — It's pretty new. It's a Model 2018. · How old is your dad? — He's not very old. He's 38. · How good is the food? — It's not very good. I don't like it. · How intelligent is Sally? — She's kinda intelligent. · How rich is he? — He's very rich. · How early is the class? — It's pretty early. It starts at 6:00 AM. · How hard is your job? — It's pretty hard, but I'm learning. · How tall is your mother? — She's kinda tall. She's 163 cm.*

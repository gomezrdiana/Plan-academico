## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M32: Cantidades contables (how many / a few / a lot of)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.274):** A person (una persona) · People (gente/personas — plural irregular) · Many / A lot of / Lots of (muchos) · A few (unos, aprox. 3-5). Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M32, Guia 1, p.274-275):**
1. *"How many...?"* significa *"¿Cuantos...?"* — se usa para elementos CONTABLES (1 perro, 2 perros...).
2. Se coloca antes del elemento: *"How many dogs do you have?"* → *"¿Cuantos perros tienes tu?"*
3. Tambien con *there are*: *"How many dogs are there?"* → *"¿Cuantos perros hay?"*
4. *many / a lot of / lots of* = muchos (contables). *"I have many friends. / I have a lot of friends."*
5. *a few* = unos/algunos, aprox. 3-5. *"I have a few friends."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.278-280):** *How many nurses are there? — There are 7 nurses. · How many people are there? — There are many people. / a lot of people. / lots of people. · How many chairs does John have? — He has a few chairs. · How many papers do they have? — They have a lot of papers. · How many students does Sergio have? — He has a few students.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have asked and answered 5 real 'How many ___?' questions about your world."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno prepara 5 preguntas *"How many ___ are there / do you have?"* sobre cosas reales (hermanos, companeros de trabajo, sillas en la sala, apps en el telefono, amigos en la ciudad).
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta, B responde con numero exacto, *a few* o *a lot of* (*"How many brothers do you have?" — "I have a few — three."*). Cambian.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes hacen al grupo su pregunta; el grupo estima antes de oir la respuesta.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~How much brothers?~~ → How MANY brothers? · ~~a few peoples~~ → a few PEOPLE · ~~How many people is there?~~ → How many people ARE there?

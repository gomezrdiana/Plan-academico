## BLOQUE 2 (43') — Repaso activo: DESCRIBIR UNA PERSONA en detalle

**Mini-referencia en tablero (del libro, para repaso — NO clase magistral). Una linea por familia:**
- **ROPA** (M36, p.301): *"He is wearing a light blue shirt and dark pants."* (adjetivo ANTES del sustantivo; *dark / light* modifican el color.)
- **APARIENCIA** (M37, p.307): *"What does she look like? — She's tall and slender. She has short, curly hair."* (*to be* + *to have*; *average height / weight*.)
- **ORDEN DE ADJETIVOS** (M38, p.313): cantidad → opinion → tamano → edad → forma → color → origen/material → sustantivo. *"a big black car / long red hair."*
- **WHOSE / posesivos** (M39, p.318): *"Whose bag is this? — It's hers / mine / theirs."*
- **PARTICIPIOS -ing / -ed** (M41, p.326): *-ing* = produce el efecto · *-ed* = lo siente. *"The class is boring. The student is bored."*
- **GET (cambios de estado)** (M42, p.333): *"She got tired. He got angry. Don't get lost."*
- **ADVERBIOS DE MANERA** (M43, p.340): *-ly* despues del verbo. *"He works quickly / carefully."* Irregulares: *fast, well.*
- **CAUSATIVO** (M44, p.347): *"The manager wants you to finish it."* (persona + verbo en infinitivo.)

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have DESCRIBED a real person completely, using at least 5 of these families."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (7'): cada uno elige una persona real (un/a companero/a de trabajo, un familiar, alguien famoso) y prepara una descripcion de 5-6 frases que incluya: 1 de ROPA + 1 de APARIENCIA (con orden de adjetivos) + 1 con participio (*-ing/-ed*) + 1 con *get* + 1 adverbio de manera + 1 con *whose* o causativo.
2. Ejecucion: se las dicen; la pareja verifica el orden de los adjetivos y que *-ing/-ed* este del lado correcto (quien produce vs quien siente).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes describen a su persona al grupo; el grupo adivina quien es por la descripcion.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~a car black big~~ → a big black car · ~~I am boring (cuando siente)~~ → I am bored · ~~He works good~~ → He works well · ~~Whose is this bag?~~ → Whose bag is this?

## ANEXO — Familia final del libro: verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**M36 "What Are You Wearing?" (p.301-306).** *"I am wearing a shirt."*; el adjetivo va antes del verbo/sustantivo y no cambia; *dark red / light red* (oscuro/claro). Ej. *"I am wearing a light blue shirt and dark blue pants."*
**M37 "What Do You Look Like?" (p.307-312).** Describir con *to be* o *to have* (*"He has black hair. His hair is black."*); *very / somewhat*; *average height / average weight*. Ej. *"The woman is young, tall and slender. She has short, straight black hair."*
**M38 "Order of Adjectives" (p.313-317).** Orden tradicional: cantidad → opinion/calidad → tamano → edad → forma → color → origen/material → sustantivo. Ej. *"a big black car / long red hair / a beautiful big white house."*
**M39 "Mine, All Mine!" (p.318-325).** *whose* pregunta "de quien es"; pronombres posesivos: *mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs*. Ej. *"Whose car is that? — It's mine. / Whose keys are those? — They're mine."*
**M41 "This Is Interesting!" (p.326-332).** Participio presente *-ing* = quien produce el efecto; participio pasado *-ed* = quien lo siente. Ej. *"The teacher is boring. The student is bored. / The game was very exciting!"*
**M42 "Getting Crazy!" (p.333-339).** *get* = "llegar a estar" (cambio de estado/animo), en cualquier tiempo. Ej. *"Camila got bored. / Don't get lost in Bogota! / She got interested in the class."*
**M43 "How Do You Do?" (p.340-346).** Adverbios de manera con *-ly* despues del verbo/objeto; irregulares *fast, well*. Ej. *"He plays well. / Mike speaks English correctly. / Please work on it immediately."*
**M44 "I Want You to Go" (p.347-352).** Volicion sobre otra persona: sujeto + verbo de volicion + persona + verbo en infinitivo. NO *"I want that you go"*. Ej. *"I want you to get a job. / The teacher needs you to hand in your test. / Brayan wants us to play soccer."*

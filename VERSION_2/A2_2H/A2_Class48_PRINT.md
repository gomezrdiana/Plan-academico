## ANEXO — verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 28 — "You're the Best!" — The Superlative (p.253-260). Guia 1 (p.253-254), reglas tal cual:**
1. El superlativo tiene la forma "X es el/la mas (descriptor)."
2. La forma depende del adjetivo, muy parecido al comparativo desigual: (a) 1 silaba → *the* + **-est** (*I am the oldest*). (b) 2 silabas en -y → *the* + **-iest** (*I am the prettiest*). (c) 2 silabas o mas → **the most** (*I am the most intelligent*). (d) Irregulares: good → *the best*, bad → *the worst*.
**Ejemplos (p.254):** *Donald is the richest. · Sarah is the prettiest. · The cat is the ugliest. · My father is the most serious. · The mass is the earliest. · Diego is the tallest. · Diana is the nicest. · Einstein is the most intelligent.* (M28 NO trae vocabulario nuevo: reusa los descriptores de M26-M27.)

**Libro A2, Module 29 — "How Tall Are You?" — Degrees of Adjectives / Intensifiers (p.261-266). Guia 1 (p.261-262), reglas tal cual:**
1. A veces queremos describir algo en terminos menos exactos, subjetivos.
2. Para preguntar usamos "how" + descriptor = "que tan ___". *"How tall are you?"* = "¿Que tan alto eres tu?"
3. Para responder usamos un intensificador ANTES del descriptor.
4. Intensificadores: **really** (muy, informal) · **very** (muy, informal o academico) · **kinda / sorta** (mas o menos) · **pretty** (bastante) · **not very** (no muy — cortes; *"No soy muy alto"* implica *"soy bajo"*).
**Ejemplos (p.263):** *How nice is Ana? — She's very nice. · How new is your car? — It's pretty new. It's a Model 2018. · How old is your dad? — He's not very old. He's 38. · How good is the food? — It's not very good. I don't like it. · How intelligent is Sally? — She's kinda intelligent. · How rich is he? — He's very rich. · How hard is your job? — It's pretty hard, but I'm learning.* (M29 NO trae tabla de vocabulario dedicada; el banco es la tabla de intensificadores de la Guia.)

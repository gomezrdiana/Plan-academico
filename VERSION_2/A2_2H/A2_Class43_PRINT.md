## BLOQUE 2 (43') — Repaso activo: el PRESENT PERFECT (regular + irregular)

Este es REPASO: no se re-explica, se PRODUCE. Recordatorio corto en tablero, luego drill de pie y produccion masiva.

**Recordatorio en tablero (verbatim del libro):**
- **Forma (M15, Guia 1, p.133-146):** *have/has* + **participio pasado**. Regulares = verbo + **-ed** (igual al pasado): *I have visited / worked / studied / lived / helped.* Abreviada: *I've played · I haven't talked.* Short answer: *"Have you visited London?" — "Yes, I have. / No, I haven't."*
- **Irregulares (M16, tabla p.147-148):** el participio es impredecible, se memoriza: *be→been · take→taken · drive→driven · wake up→woken up · eat→eaten · go→gone · tell→told · find→found.* *He/she/it usan HAS.*
- Ejemplos del libro: *I have been in Madrid. · Sam has driven a Suzuki. · We have gone to Bogota. · I haven't found my dog. · You have visited my parents. · Have I told my friends?*

**Drill de arranque, TODO de pie (12'):**
1. Cadena de participios: tu dices el verbo base desde el tablero (visit, work, go, eat, take, be, drive, tell, find, study); cada estudiante responde en voz alta con *"I have + participio"* + un dato real. Velocidad creciente; regular e irregular mezclados.
2. Ronda *"Have you...?"*: A pregunta al siguiente *"Have you ever + participio?"*, B responde con short answer + 1 frase extra (*"Have you eaten sushi?" — "Yes, I have. I ate it last year."*), y B pregunta al proximo. Sin cuaderno.

**Construccion desde el error (8'):** copia en tablero 4-6 errores REALES oidos hoy o del error paper de Cl 42 (anonimos), tipicos: ~~I have went~~ → I have GONE · ~~She have eaten~~ → She HAS eaten · ~~I have visit London~~ → I have visitED London · ~~I have ate~~ → I have EATEN. El grupo diagnostica y reconstruye; cada estudiante produce 1 ejemplo propio nuevo corregido.

**Produccion masiva (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have told a partner 4 REAL experiences (regular + irregular), 1 negative, and asked 2 'Have you...?' questions."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (5'): cada uno arma 4 experiencias reales (2 regulares: visited/worked/studied/lived; 2 irregulares: been/gone/eaten/taken/driven) + 1 negativa + 2 preguntas *"Have you...?"*.
2. Ejecucion: se las cuentan; la pareja verifica el participio (regular -ed vs irregular) y responde con short answer.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su mejor experiencia.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

## ANEXO — verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 15 — "I Have Visited London" — The Present Perfect (Regular Verbs), p.133-146. Guia 1, reglas tal cual:**
1. Al contrario del pasado simple, usamos el presente perfecto para acciones realizadas en un periodo indefinido o acciones no terminadas. (A pesar de su nombre, es un tiempo pasado.)
2. Lo formamos con "to have" como auxiliar + el participio pasado del verbo.
3. La mayoria de los participios (regulares) se forman agregando "-ed" al verbo, igual al pasado simple.
4. "have" como auxiliar normal (he/she/it usan "has").
5. Abreviada: *I've played / I haven't played.* 6. Short answer: *"Have you visited London?" "Yes, I have. / No, I haven't."*
**Ejemplos (M15, tabla pasado→perfecto):** *I played soccer yesterday → I've played soccer. · You lived in Madrid last year → You've lived in Madrid. · We visited my parents last month → We've visited my parents. · They helped the children last week → They've helped the children. · I didn't talk to Miguel → I haven't talked to Miguel. · Did Cristian arrive at 7:00? → Has Cristian arrived?*

**Libro A2, Module 16 — "I Have Gone to Paris" — The Present Perfect (Irregular), p.147-162. Guia 1, reglas tal cual:** participios irregulares impredecibles, se aprenden de memoria (~30 comunes); *have* auxiliar (has en 3ra persona). **Tabla de participios (p.147-148):** be→been · take→taken · drive→driven · wake up→woken up · eat→eaten · go→gone · tell→told · find→found. **Ejemplos (p.150):** *I have been in Madrid. · You have taken a bus to the school. · Sam has driven a Suzuki. · Daniela has woken up at night. · We have gone to Bogota. · The children have told their parents. · I haven't found my dog.*

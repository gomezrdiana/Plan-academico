## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M27: comparaciones DESIGUALES (more ... than)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.248):** Rich (rico) · Pretty (bonita) · Ugly (feo) · Serious (serio/grave) · Early (temprano) · Late (tarde) · Complicated (complicado). Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M27, Guia 1, p.248-249):**
1. El comparativo NO igual: *X es mas (descriptor) que Y*. *"than" = "que"*.
2. La forma depende del adjetivo:
   - (a) 1 silaba → adjetivo + **-er**: *"I am older than Carlos."*
   - (b) 2 silabas terminando en **-y** → cambia -y por **-ier**: *"I am prettier than Anita."*
   - (c) 2 silabas o mas → **more** + adjetivo: *"I am more intelligent than Carlos."*
   - (d) Irregulares: *good → better than* · *bad → worse than*.

**Ejemplos del libro (p.249):** *I'm richer than Donald Duck. · Sarah is prettier than Clara. · The cat is uglier than the dog. · My father is more serious than my mother. · The mass is earlier than the class. · Diego is later than Louisa. · Spanish is more complicated than English. · Einstein is more intelligent than me.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have compared two real things using the right form: -er, -ier, more..., and better/worse."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 5 comparaciones reales; obliga variedad — al menos 1 con *-er* (1 silaba: old, rich, late, early), 1 con *-ier* (pretty→prettier, ugly→uglier, happy→happier), 1 con *more* (serious, complicated, intelligent) y 1 irregular (*better / worse*).
2. Ejecucion: se las dicen; la pareja verifica que se eligio la forma correcta segun las silabas y responde si esta de acuerdo.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su comparacion mas util (*"This job is better than my old one because it is less complicated."*).
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~more old than~~ → OLDER than · ~~more better~~ → BETTER · ~~more pretty than~~ → PRETTIER than.

## ANEXO — M27 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 27 — "I'm Smarter than Steve" — Unequal Comparatives (p.247-252). Habilidades objetivas del libro:** "Sin dudar, aprender el vocabulario del modulo. Sin dudar, formar comparaciones desiguales entre dos elementos."

**Guia 1 (p.248-249), reglas tal cual:**
1. El comparativo no igual tiene la forma *X es mas (descriptor) que Y*.
2. La forma depende del adjetivo:
   - (a) Adjetivo de UNA silaba → agregamos **-er**. *"than" = "que"*. *Soy mas viejo que Carlos. = I am older than Carlos.*
   - (b) Adjetivo de DOS silabas que termina en **-y** → cambia -y por **-ier**. *Soy mas bonita que Anita. = I am prettier than Anita.*
   - (c) Adjetivo de DOS silabas o mas → **more** antes del adjetivo. *Soy mas inteligente que Carlos. = I am more intelligent than Carlos.*
   - (d) Irregulares: good (bueno) → *I am better than Carlos*. bad (malo) → *I am worse than Carlos*.

**Vocabulario (p.248):** rich (rico) · pretty (bonita) · ugly (feo) · serious (serio/grave) · early (temprano) · late (tarde) · complicated (complicado).

**Ejemplos completos (p.249):** *Yo soy mas rico que Donald Duck. = I'm richer than Donald Duck. · Sarah es mas bonita que Clara. = Sarah is prettier than Clara. · El gato es mas feo que el perro. = The cat is uglier than the dog. · Mi padre es mas serio que mi madre. = My father is more serious than my mother. · La misa es mas temprana que la clase. = The mass is earlier than the class. · Diego llega mas tarde que Louisa. = Diego is later than Louisa. · El espanol es mas complicado que el ingles. = Spanish is more complicated than English. · Einstein es mas inteligente que yo. = Einstein is more intelligent than me.*

**Practica del libro (Practica Verbal 1, p.251):** *Cecilia is prettier than Angelica. · Simon is richer than Sandra. · The restaurant is better than the school. · The dog is uglier than the elephant. · The engineer is more serious than the maid. · The nurses are happier than the doctors. · The hamburger is more expensive than the sandwich. · The woman is earlier than the man. · The student is later than the teacher. · Spanish is more difficult than English.*

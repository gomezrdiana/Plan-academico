## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M34: Precios (cost / be / each)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.293-294):** How much is/are ___? · How much does/do ___ cost? · Cost (costar; pasado irregular: cost) · Each (cada uno / por unidad). Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M34, Guia 1, p.293-294):**
1. Hay DOS maneras de pedir y dar un precio:
   a. Con *cost* (costar; pasado irregular *cost*): *"How much does the car cost?"* → *"It costs $5."* · plural: *"How much do the cars cost?"* → *"They cost $5."*
   b. Con *be*: *"How much is the car?"* → *"It's $5."* · plural: *"How much are the cars?"* → *"They're $5."*
2. La pregunta implica *"how much money"*, pero NO decimos *"money"*.
3. Se usa *each* (cada uno / por unidad): *"The sandwiches cost $2 each."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.295-298):** *How much are the hamburgers? — They're six dollars each. · How much is the pizza? — It's ten dollars. · How much is the hot dog? — It's three fifty. · How much are the books? — They're five dollars each. · How much does the marker cost? — It costs one seventy-five. · How much do the shoes cost? — They cost twenty ninety-seven.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have asked and given the price of 5 real items using BOTH 'be' and 'cost'."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno piensa 5 objetos reales con precio (su telefono, un cafe, el bus, unos zapatos, el almuerzo).
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta con *be* (*"How much is your coffee?"*), B responde; luego B pregunta con *cost* (*"How much does the bus cost?"*), A responde. Al menos 1 con *each*.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo el precio de algo real y el grupo dice si es *cheap*, *expensive* o *not very expensive* (reusa intensificadores de Cl 25).
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~How much cost the car?~~ → How much DOES the car cost? · ~~It costs five dollars each~~ (1 solo objeto) → It costs five dollars (each = por unidad, varios) · ~~They costs $5~~ → They COST $5.

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M14: While / During / Again

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M14, Guide 1, p.121-122):**
1. **"While" → "mientras".** Exactamente como el espanol. Conecta DOS frases completas (sujeto + verbo). *"I studied while you worked." · "I was studying while you were working."*
2. **"During" → "durante".** Exactamente como el espanol. Conecta una frase con un EVENTO o PERIODO (un sustantivo, no una clausula). *"I studied during the party." · "I won't study during the night."*
3. **"Again" → "otra vez / de nuevo".** Exactamente como el espanol. Generalmente al FINAL de la frase (puede moverse con una frase de tiempo). *"I ate at 1:00. I ate again at 2:00. I will eat again at 4:00."*

**Clave (escribela grande):** WHILE + una clausula completa (sujeto + verbo). DURING + una cosa / periodo (sustantivo, sin verbo). AGAIN al final.

**Ejemplos del libro (p.123):** *Richard visited his mother while she ate lunch. · I lived in Canada during the war. · The girls ate lunch during the afternoon. · Will you shower while I get dressed? · I was helping my mom while you visited your friends. · Beatriz was finishing the project while I was sleeping. · When will you visit Canada again?*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea, dila al inicio):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have described a busy day with a partner: who did what WHILE another worked, what happened DURING a period, and what you did AGAIN."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 1 frase con *while* (dos acciones a la vez), 1 con *during* (un periodo), 1 con *again* (una tarea repetida). Notas minimas.
2. Ejecucion: se las cuentan; la pareja verifica que *while* lleve un verbo, *during* un periodo/sustantivo, y da credito justo al companero.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes cuentan al grupo su mejor momento de equipo. El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero sobre el LOGRO (equipo justo), despues sobre la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos en tablero, coro 2x):** ~~I studied while the party~~ → I studied DURING the party · ~~We will eat while they will take a taxi~~ → We will eat while they TAKE a taxi · ~~I again ate at 2:00~~ → I ate AGAIN at 2:00.

## ANEXO — M14 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 14 — "We Studied During the Game Again!" — While, During, and Again (p.121-132). Objetivo del libro:** "Expresar frases usando los tres especificadores de tiempo de este modulo."

**Guide 1 (p.121-122), reglas tal cual:**
1. "While" → "mientras". Exactamente como el espanol. Se usa para conectar dos frases completas. "I studied while you worked." → "Yo estudie mientras tu trabajaste." "I was studying while you were working." → "Yo estaba estudiando mientras tu estabas trabajando."
2. "During" → "durante". Exactamente como el espanol. Se usa para conectar una frase con un evento o periodo. "I studied during the party." → "Yo estudie durante la fiesta." "I won't study during the night." → "No estudiare durante la noche."
3. "Again" → "otra vez / de nuevo". Exactamente como el espanol. Generalmente colocado al fin de la frase, pero depende del contexto exacto, se puede colocar antes o despues de una frase de tiempo. "I ate at 1:00. I ate again at 2:00. I will eat again at 4:00."

**Ejemplos completos (p.123):** *Richard visited his mother while she ate lunch. · I lived in Canada during the war. · I was in the park again on Monday. · The girls ate lunch during the afternoon. · I won't clean the apartment while you are speaking on the phone. · Wilson will be at the mall during the break. · Beatriz was finishing the project while I was sleeping. · Will you shower while I get dressed? · Was Fritz hanging out with Dale during the class? · When will you visit Canada again?*

**Nota gramatical del libro (p.128), verbatim:** "Igual al espanol, para usar 'while' en el futuro, usamos el presente en la segunda clausula, no el futuro. E.g. No es correcto decir 'We will eat breakfast while the children will take a taxi to go to their school.'" → correcto: *"...while the children TAKE a taxi."*

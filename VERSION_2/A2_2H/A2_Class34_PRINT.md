## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M39: Whose + pronombres posesivos (mine / yours / his / hers / ours / theirs)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.318-319):** Whose? (¿de quien? — se pronuncia "jus") · mine (mio) · yours (tuyo/suyo) · his (de el) · hers (de ella) · ours (nuestro) · theirs (de ellos). Y el posesivo con apostrofe: *Peter's · my brother's · Chris'*. Pronunciacion coral + individual.

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M39, Guia 1, p.318-319):**
1. Usamos *whose* para preguntar "de quien es...": *"Whose car is that?"* → ¿De quien es ese carro?
2. Los pronombres posesivos se usan igual que en espanol: mine · yours · his · hers · ours · theirs. Ej. *"Whose car is that?" — "It's mine."*
3. Con nombres/personas se usa apostrofe + s: *"It's Peter's." · "It's my brother's."* (varios duenos: *"It's my brothers'."*).

**Ejemplos del libro (p.320):** *Whose keys are those? — They're mine. · Whose house is that? — It's yours. · Whose car is this? — It's Frank's. · Is this Sarah's marker? — Yes, it's hers. · Is that Miguel's motorcycle? — Yes, it's his. · Is this Marie and Celeste's house? — Yes, it's theirs. · Whose hair is longer? — Mine is.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have asked and answered WHOSE questions about 5 real objects, using possessive pronouns."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): pongan 5 objetos reales al centro (telefonos, llaves, mochilas, esferos). Cada uno prepara 5 preguntas *"Whose ___ is this/are these?"*.
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta (*"Whose phone is this?"*), B responde con pronombre (*"It's mine / It's hers / It's Ana's."*). Rota los objetos.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes hacen 1 pregunta *whose* al grupo y alguien responde con el pronombre correcto.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~It's mine car~~ → It's MINE (o: It's MY car) · ~~Whose is car?~~ → WHOSE CAR is it? · ~~It's hers keys~~ → They're HERS (o: They're HER keys).

**Guia 1 (p.318-319), reglas tal cual:**
1. Usamos la palabra de pregunta "whose" (pronunciado "jus") para preguntar "de quien es...": "Whose car is that?" → "¿De quien es ese carro?"
2. Los pronombres posesivos ingleses se usan exactamente igual que en espanol: Mio = Mine · Tuyo/Suyo = Yours · Suyo (de el) = His · Suyo (de ella) = Hers · Nuestra = Ours · Suyo (de ellos/ellas) = Theirs. Ej. "Whose car is that?" "It's mine."
3. Se pueden usar las mismas reglas del apostrofe + "s" como pronombres: El perro de Peter = Peter's dog → "It's Peter's." · El perro de mis hermanos (varios duenos) = my brothers' dog → "It's my brothers'." · Dueno que termina en "s" = Chris' dog → "It's Chris'."

**Ejemplos completos (p.320):** *Whose keys are those? — They're mine. · Whose house is that? — It's yours. · Whose car is this? — It's Frank's. · Is this Sarah's marker? — Yes, it's hers. · Is that Miguel's motorcycle? — Yes, it's his. · Is this Marie and Celeste's house? — Yes, it's theirs. · Is that my apartment? — No, it's not yours, it's mine. · Whose dogs are those? — They're Sebastian's. · Whose hair is longer? — Mine is. [se pueden usar como sujetos tambien] · Whose father is taller? — Sam's is. · Whose group is bigger? — The girls' is.*

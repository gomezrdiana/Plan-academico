## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M8: El pronombre relativo "that" (unir dos frases)

**Vocabulario del modulo (tablero, p.62)** — con su pasado irregular:

```
know - knew      think - thought      see - saw
hear - heard     feel - felt          say - said
```

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M8, Guide 1, p.63):**
1. Usamos los pronombres relativos para conectar dos frases y dar mas informacion.
2. El pronombre relativo "that" es aproximadamente equivalente a "que".
3. En frases sencillas es comun y aceptable OMITIR "that": *"I think that you're sick." = "I think you're sick."*
4. Podemos usar el interrogativo y el negativo normalmente: *"Do you think that I am sick? · I don't think that you are sick. · I think that you aren't sick."*
5. Diferente al espanol: si el VERBO 1 esta en pasado, el VERBO 2 tambien debe estar en pasado.

**Contraste presente / pasado del libro (p.63-64)** — escribelo en 2 columnas:

```
PRESENT                              PAST
I know (that) you live here.         I knew (that) you lived here.
I think (that) you want it.          I thought (that) you wanted it.
I see (that) you are happy.          I saw (that) you were happy.
I hear (that) you like Nicole.       I heard (that) you liked Nicole.
Mark says (that) you are working.    Mark said (that) you were working.
```

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have joined two ideas with 'that' — in the present AND in the past."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): A dice una frase en PRESENTE con verbo + that (*"I think that you..."*); B la repite en PASADO (*"You thought that I..."*), manteniendo AMBOS verbos en pasado. Usa know/think/see/hear/feel/say. Notas minimas.
2. Ejecucion: 6 intercambios y cambian; la pareja caza si el segundo verbo salto al pasado cuando el primero lo hizo.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo 1 pareja presente/pasado (*"I know that... / I knew that..."*). El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero el logro (unieron dos ideas con calma), despues la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~I thought that you are sick~~ → I thought that you WERE sick · ~~I no think that he is here~~ → I DON'T think that he is here · ~~I think that is sick~~ → I think that HE is sick.

## ANEXO — M8 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 8 — "I Think that I'm Learning!" — The Relative Pronoun "That" (p.61-74). Objetivo del libro:** conectar dos frases con "that" para dar mas informacion.

**Vocabulario (p.62):** know - knew (saber) · think - thought (pensar) · see - saw (ver) · hear - heard (oir) · feel - felt (sentir) · say - said (decir).

**Guide 1 (p.63), reglas tal cual:**
1. Usamos los pronombres relativos para conectar dos frases y dar mas informacion.
2. El pronombre relativo "that" es aproximadamente equivalente a "que".
3. En frases sencillas es comun y aceptable omitir "that". E.g. "I think that you're sick." = "I think you're sick."
4. Podemos usar el interrogativo y/o negativo normalmente en las frases componentes. E.g. "Do you think that I am sick?" / "I don't think that you are sick." / "I think that you aren't sick."
5. Diferente al espanol: si el "verbo 1" esta en el pasado, "el verbo 2" tambien debe estar en el pasado. ("I thought that you are sick" es INCORRECTO → "I thought that you were sick".)

**Ejemplos completos (p.64), presente / pasado:** *I know (that) you live in Bucaramanga. → I knew (that) you lived in Bucaramanga. · I think (that) you want ice cream. → I thought (that) you wanted ice cream. · I see (that) you are happy. → I saw (that) you were happy. · I hear (that) you like Nicole. → I heard (that) you liked Nicole. · I feel (that) you are confused. → I felt (that) you were confused. · Mark says (that) you are working. → Mark said (that) you were working. · We know (that) Sebastian wakes up at 7:00. → We knew (that) Sebastian woke up at 7:00. · Yully thinks (that) you eat fish. → Yully thought (that) you ate fish.*

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M15: El present perfect con verbos REGULARES

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M15, Guide 1, p.133-134):**
1. A diferencia del pasado simple, el **present perfect** se usa para acciones hechas (o no hechas) en un periodo INDEFINIDO / no especificado, o para acciones NO terminadas. (Nota: a pesar de su nombre, es un tiempo PASADO.)
2. Se forma con el verbo "to have" como auxiliar (= "haber") + el PARTICIPIO PASADO del verbo.
3. La mayoria de los participios (los "regulares") agregan "-ed" al verbo — igual que el pasado simple, como el espanol "-ado / -ido".
4. Se usa "have" normal; **he / she / it usan "HAS"**.

**Estructura y formas (escribelas grande):**

```
Subject + HAVE/HAS + participle (+ complement)
   "I have played hockey."  = Yo he jugado hockey.

I have (not) played.   /  Have I played?
He has (not) played.   /  Has he played?
We have (not) played.  /  Have we played?

ABBREVIATED: I've played / I haven't played · He's played / He hasn't played
SHORT ANSWER: "Have you visited London?"  "Yes, I have. / No, I haven't."
```

**Ejemplos del libro (p.135), pasado simple → present perfect:** *I played soccer yesterday → I've played soccer. · You lived in Madrid last year → You've lived in Madrid. · Jeff showered in the morning → Jeff has showered. · We visited my parents last month → We've visited my parents. · They helped the children → They've helped the children. · I didn't talk to Miguel → I haven't talked to Miguel. · Did you study last week? → Have you studied? · Did Sandra visit the apartment? → Has Sandra visited the apartment?*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea, dila al inicio):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have told a partner 4 real things you HAVE DONE in your life — no exact date needed."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma oralmente 4 experiencias reales con verbos regulares (worked / studied / lived / visited / helped / shared) + 1 negativa + 1 pregunta *Have you...?* Notas minimas.
2. Ejecucion: cada estudiante cuenta sus 4 experiencias y responde 2 preguntas *Have you...?* con short answer (*Yes, I have / No, I haven't*).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su mejor experiencia + una cosa justa que han hecho por otros. El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero sobre el LOGRO (nombraron experiencias reales), despues sobre la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos en tablero, coro 2x):** ~~I have play hockey~~ → I have PLAYED hockey · ~~He have played~~ → He HAS played · ~~You have visited London?~~ → HAVE you visited London? · ~~Yes, I have visited~~ → Yes, I HAVE (short answer sin repetir el verbo).

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M18: La voz pasiva (participios regulares)

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M18, Guide 1, p.174):**
1. Hasta ahora usamos "la voz activa": el sujeto es el actor y el enfoque de la frase.
2. A veces queremos enfocar mas en el OBJETO: el que RECIBE la accion. Puede ser que el actor no es informacion importante o incluso desconocido.
   - Activa: *"Juan prepares the lunch at 12:00."* — Pasiva: *"The lunch is prepared at 12:00."* (en un restaurante no sabemos QUIEN lo preparo; solo que esta preparado)
3. Estructura pasiva: **Sujeto (paciente) + TO BE + participio pasado + by + agente**: *"The lunch is prepared by Juan."*
4. El verbo principal toma la forma del PARTICIPIO (el que acabamos de aprender en los modulos anteriores).
5. Es completamente aceptable ELIMINAR el agente: *"The lunch is prepared."*

**Ejemplos del libro (p.175):** *The game is played by Sebastian. · English is studied by Mariana. · The dog is loved by the boy. · The teacher is helped by us. · The house is cleaned by the girls.* — Sin agente: *Soccer is played in Colombia. · You are loved! · Santa Marta is often visited in Semana Santa. · Students are helped in the morning. · I am often asked about English.*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have described your job or your home with 4 real passive sentences."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 4 pasivas REALES de su trabajo/casa/barrio (2 con *by* + 2 sin agente), con participios regulares (prepared, cleaned, called, helped, needed, watched, played, finished, visited, asked, loved, wanted).
2. Ejecucion: se las dicen; la pareja verifica el orden (paciente + is/are + participio) y pregunta *"Who does it?"* para decidir si el *by* hace falta o sobra.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su mejor pasiva sin agente.
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~The lunch is prepare~~ → The lunch is preparED · ~~The house cleaned by the girls~~ → The house IS cleaned by the girls · ~~Is played soccer in Colombia~~ → Soccer IS PLAYED in Colombia.

## ANEXO — M18 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 18 — "You Are Loved!" — The Passive Voice for Regular Participles (p.173-180). Objetivos del libro:** "Expresar actos. Convertir frases de voz pasiva a voz activa."

**Guide 1 (p.174), reglas tal cual:**
1. Hasta ahora, hemos usado "la voz activa"; donde el sujeto es el actor y el enfoque de la frase.
2. A veces, queremos enfocar mas en el objeto de la frase; el que recibe la accion.
3. Puede ser que el actor no es informacion importante o incluso desconocido. E.g. "Juan prepares the lunch at 12:00." → "Juan prepara el almuerzo a las 12:00."
4. Si estamos en un restaurante, no sabemos quien preparo el almuerzo; solo que esta preparado. E.g. "The lunch is prepared at 12:00." → "El almuerzo es preparado a las 12:00."
5. En la voz pasiva el paciente es el sujeto: El almuerzo + Es + Preparado + Por + Juan. / The lunch + Is + Prepared + By + Juan.
6. El verbo principal toma la forma del participio (que acabamos de aprender en los modulos anteriores).
7. De hecho, es completamente aceptable eliminar el agente totalmente: "The lunch is prepared."

**Ejemplos completos (Examples 1, p.175):** activa → pasiva: *Sebastian plays the game. → The game is played by Sebastian. · Mariana studies English. → English is studied by Mariana. · The girls like ice cream. → Ice cream is liked by the girls. · The boy loves the dog. → The dog is loved by the boy. · I visit my father. → My father is visited by me. · We help the teacher. → The teacher is helped by us. · They call the cat. → The cat is called by them. · My friends want the car. → The car is wanted by my friends. · Steven asks the teacher. → The teacher is asked by Steven. · We watch the movie. → The movie is watched by us. · The girls clean the house. → The house is cleaned by the girls. · The boys finish the game. → The game is finished by the boys.*

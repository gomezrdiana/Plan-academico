## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M6: El presente simple para horarios

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M6, Guide 1, p.43):**
1. Hay una manera mas de expresar el futuro: el **presente simple**.
2. Se usa **unicamente para eventos programados con un horario fijo**: *My dad leaves at 8:20 tomorrow. · What time does your train arrive?*
3. Verbo **"arrive at"** para llegar (formal): *I arrive at the school at 4:30 today.*
4. Forma mas conversacional: **"get to"**: *I get to the school at 4:30.* Excepcion: **"get home"** (nunca "get to home"): *I get home tomorrow.*
5. Los numeros de vuelos se leen individualmente: Flight 1234 -> *"Flight one two three four".*

**Marcadores de tiempo (repaso de Cl 1-3):** AT + hora exacta (*at 7 PM*) · ON + dia/fecha (*on Monday, on May 5th*) · IN + mes/anio (*in June, in 2026*).

**Puente con Cl 4 (present continuous):** el horario NO es tuyo (del tren, del vuelo) -> present simple (*The train leaves at 7*). El plan es TUYO -> present continuous (*I'm leaving at 7*). Ese contraste es el corazon de la simulacion.

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea, dila al inicio):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have given 5 real schedules with present simple."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma 5 horarios reales (bus, tren, vuelo, clase, tienda) con present simple + *arrive at / get to / leave* + AT/ON/IN (*"My bus leaves at 6:15." "The store opens on Monday."*).
2. Ejecucion: se los dicen; la pareja verifica la **-s** de 3a persona (*leaves, arrives, opens*) y la preposicion correcta.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su horario + su plan personal en contraste (*"The flight leaves at 8. I'm arriving at the airport at 6."*). El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero el LOGRO (dieron horarios reales), despues la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~get to home~~ -> get home · ~~The train arrive at 7~~ -> The train ARRIVES at 7 · ~~does the train leaves?~~ -> does the train LEAVE?

## ANEXO — M6 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 6 — "Right on Time!" — Scheduled Events (p.41-50). Objetivos del libro:** "Sin dudar, demuestras que has aprendido el vocabulario del modulo. Usar correctamente la forma del futuro del modulo."

**Guide 1 (p.43), reglas tal cual:**
1. Hay una manera mas de expresar el futuro: el presente simple.
2. Esta forma se usa unicamente para eventos programados con un horario fijo. E.g. *My dad leaves at 8:20 tomorrow. · What time does your train arrive?*
3. Has aprendido el verbo "arrive at" para llegar. E.g. *I arrive at the school at 4:30 today.*
4. Una forma mas conversacional para hablar de llegar a un lugar es "get to". E.g. *I get to the school at 4:30 today. · I get home tomorrow.* Nota: "get home" es especial; nunca se dice "get to home".
5. Por costumbre, los numeros de vuelos se leen individualmente. E.g. Flight 1234 -> "Flight one two three four".

**Vocabulario (p.42):** The flight (el vuelo) · The bus (el bus) · The train (el tren) · arrive at (llegar a, formal) · get to (llegar a, informal).

**Ejemplos del libro (Examples 2, Scheduled Events, p.45):** *Flight 4566 to Santa Marta leaves at two twenty PM. · What time does Flight 4821 from Rio Negro arrive? / It arrives at eleven thirty-three AM. · Flight 6725 from Cucuta gets to Bucaramanga at four twenty-eight PM. · What time does the flight to Medellin leave? / It leaves at six fifty AM.*
**Negativa / pregunta (Verbal Practice, p.48-49):** *Does the train to Floridablanca leave at 6:12 PM? -> Yes, it does. · What time does the train leave Cafe Madrid? -> The train doesn't leave Cafe Madrid.* (unica negativa explicita del modulo). *The train leaves Piedecuesta at seven o'clock. It gets to Floridablanca at seven twenty and leaves at seven twenty-five.*

## BLOQUE 2 (43') — M16: Present Perfect con verbos IRREGULARES

**Tablero verbatim del libro (M16, Guide 1, p.149):**
- "Aunque muchos verbos (regulares) forman su forma participio agregando -d o -ed, hay un grupo de verbos 'irregulares' que forman el participio en una manera impredecible."
- "La unica manera de aprenderlos es por memoria; solo hay alrededor de 30 en uso comun."
- Se usa HAVE como auxiliar normal (he / she / it usan HAS): *I have eaten pizza.*

**Tabla de participios (vocab p.147-148)** — escribela completa:

```
be (am/is/are)  - was/were - BEEN      take - took - TAKEN
drive - drove - DRIVEN                 wake up - woke up - WOKEN UP
eat - ate - EATEN                      go - went - GONE
tell - told - TOLD                     find - found - FOUND
```

**Formas (p.149):** I have (not) eaten. / Have I eaten? · He has (not) eaten. / Has he eaten? — abreviada: I've eaten / He hasn't eaten.
**Ejemplos del libro (p.150):** *I have been in Madrid. · Sam has driven a Suzuki. · We have gone to Bogota in our car. · I haven't found my dog. · Fred hasn't taken a taxi to work. · Have I told my friends?*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea, dila al inicio):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have told a partner 3 REAL experiences with these verbs."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (6'): cada uno arma oralmente 3 experiencias reales (been / gone / eaten / taken / driven) + 1 negativa + 1 pregunta *Have you...?* Notas minimas, no parrafos.
2. Ejecucion: cada estudiante cuenta sus 3 experiencias a su pareja y responde 2 preguntas *Have you...?* con short answer (*Yes, I have / No, I haven't*).
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes dicen al grupo su mejor experiencia. El bloque no termina sin esto.
4. Feedback: primero sobre el LOGRO (contaron experiencias reales), despues sobre la forma.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos en tablero, coro 2x):** ~~I have went~~ → I have GONE · ~~She have eaten~~ → She HAS eaten · ~~I have ate~~ → I have EATEN.

## ANEXO — M16 verbatim + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**Libro A2, Module 16 — "I Have Gone to Paris" — The Present Perfect - Irregular (p.147-162). Objetivo del libro:** "Expresar frases usando el presente de perfecto con verbos irregulares."

**Guide 1 (p.149), reglas tal cual:**
1. Aunque muchos verbos (regulares) forman su participio agregando "-d" o "-ed", hay un grupo de verbos "irregulares" que forman el participio en una manera impredecible.
2. Aunque la unica manera de aprenderlos es por memoria, solo hay alrededor de 30 en uso comun.
3. Se usa "have" como auxiliar normal (recuerde: "he", "she" y "it" usan la forma "has").
- Estructura: Sujeto + HAVE/HAS + verbo en participio + complemento. *I have eaten pizza. = Yo he comido pizza.*

**Vocabulario del modulo (p.147-148):** be (am/is/are) - was/were - been (ser/estar) · take - took - taken (tomar un bus) · drive - drove - driven (manejar/conducir) · wake up - woke up - woken up (despertarse) · eat - ate - eaten (comer) · go - went - gone (ir) · tell - told - told (contar/informar) · find - found - found (encontrar).

**Formas completas (p.149):** I have (not) eaten. / Have I eaten? · You have (not) eaten. / Have you eaten? · He has (not) eaten. / Has he eaten? · She has (not) eaten. / Has she eaten? · It has (not) eaten. / Has it eaten? · We have (not) eaten. / Have we eaten? · They have (not) eaten. / Have they eaten?

**Ejemplos del libro (p.150), presente → pasado → perfecto:**
- I am in Madrid. → I was in Madrid. → I have been in Madrid.
- You take a bus to the school. → You took a bus. → You have taken a bus to the school.
- Sam drives a Suzuki. → Sam drove a Suzuki. → Sam has driven a Suzuki.
- Daniela wakes up at night. → Daniela woke up at night. → Daniela has woken up at night.
- We go to Bogota in our car. → We went to Bogota. → We have gone to Bogota in our car.
- The children tell their parents. → The children told their parents. → The children have told their parents.
- I don't find my dog. → I didn't find my dog. → I haven't found my dog.
- Fred doesn't take a taxi to work. → Fred didn't take a taxi. → Fred hasn't taken a taxi to work.
- Do I tell my friends? → Did I tell my friends? → Have I told my friends?

**Short answer (Verbal Practice 5, p.161-162):** *"Has Karina been happy?" "No, she hasn't." · "Have you driven to the office?" "Yes, I have." · "Have we told Pepito?" "No, we haven't."*

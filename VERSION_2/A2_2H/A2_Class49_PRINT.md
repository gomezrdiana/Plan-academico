## BLOQUE 2 (43') — Repaso activo: HOW MANY / HOW MUCH / SO-TOO-ENOUGH / PRICES

**Mini-referencia en tablero (del libro, para repaso — NO clase magistral):**
- **HOW MANY + contables** (M32, p.273): *"How many ___ do you have? / are there?"* → respuesta: *many · a lot of · lots of · a few* (aprox. 3-5). *"How many friends do you have? — I have a few. / I have a lot of friends."*
- **HOW MUCH + incontables** (M33, p.283): *"How much ___ do you have?"* → *a lot of · lots of · a bit of* + unidades (*a kilo, a liter, a glass, a bottle*). *"How much money do you have? — I have a bit of money."*
- **SO / TOO / ENOUGH** (M30, p.267): *so* = nivel alto y bueno · *too* = excesivo/no deseable · *enough* va DESPUES del descriptor. *"She is so nice! / It is too expensive. / I am not tall enough."*
- **PRICES** (M34, p.293): *"How much does it cost? / How much is it?"* → *"It costs $5. / It's $5. / They cost $5 each. / They're $5 each."*

**Actividad (meta en 1 linea):** *"By the end of this block, each of you will have ASKED and ANSWERED real HOW MANY / HOW MUCH questions and given at least 3 prices."*
1. Preparacion en parejas (7'): cada uno arma una situacion real de compra o inventario (mercado, papeleria de la oficina, despensa de un restaurante) — 3 preguntas con *how many* (contables), 3 con *how much* (incontables/dinero), 2 precios con *cost / each*, y 1 juicio con *too / so / enough* (*"It's too expensive."*).
2. Ejecucion: A pregunta, B responde con la cantidad y el precio reales; la pareja verifica que *how many* vaya con contables y *how much* con incontables/dinero.
3. Producto: 4-5 estudiantes le dicen al grupo su "mejor negocio" (*"The boxes cost $8 each, so I bought a few. That's a lot of boxes, but not too expensive."*).
4. Feedback: logro primero, forma despues.

**Errores tipicos (tachalos, coro 2x):** ~~How much apples do you have?~~ → How MANY apples? · ~~How many money?~~ → How MUCH money? · ~~too much expensive~~ → too expensive / so expensive · ~~It cost 5 dollars each one~~ → They cost $5 each.

## ANEXO — Cantidades y precios: verbatim del libro + Plan B (no leer en clase)

**M30 "Too Much or Just Enough?" (p.267-272).** Regla (Guia 1): *enough* se coloca DESPUES del descriptor; *so* = nivel alto pero bueno; *too* = excesivo o no deseable. Ejemplos: *"She is so nice! / She is too nice (can't fight). / My car is so new! / Your car is too new. / I am not tall enough. / I am tall enough."*

**M32 "How Many People Are There?" (p.273-282).** *how many* = contables. Cantidades: *many · a lot of · lots of* (muchos) · *a few* (aprox. 3-5). Ejemplos: *"How many dogs do you have? / How many dogs are there? / I have a lot of friends. / I have a few friends. / There are many people."* (Nota: *people* es plural irregular de *person*.)

**M33 "How Much Money Do You Have?" (p.283-292).** *how much* = incontables (liquidos, dinero, etc.). Se miden por unidades: *a kilo, a liter, a gallon, a glass, a bottle*. Cantidades: *a lot of · lots of* (mucho) · *a bit of* (un poco de). Ejemplos: *"How much milk do you have? — I have 1 liter of milk. / I have a lot of money. / I have a bit of money. / There is a bit of coffee."*

**M34 "How Much Does It Cost?" (p.293-300).** Dos formas de dar precio: con *cost* (*"How much does the car cost? — It costs $5. / They cost $5."*) o con *be* (*"How much is the car? — It's $5. / They're $5."*). *each* = cada uno / por unidad: *"The sandwiches cost $2 each. / How much are the hamburgers? — They're six dollars each."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT TABLE WHEN/WHILE (~50 min) · ~80% STAND/BOARD

```
1. [Board] ESCRIBE la tabla completa en board (toma 5
   min, vale la pena). El profe DE PIE todo el bloque:

   PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS — INTERRUPTION

   LONG / BACKGROUND (PAST CONTINUOUS = was/were + -ing)
      I was working.   /  She was driving.
      They were having a meeting.
      We were walking in the park.

   SHORT / INTERRUPTION (PAST SIMPLE)
      the phone rang.  /  the light changed.
      the manager walked in.  /  Jeff saw a dog.

   COMBINING — WHEN + simple / WHILE + continuous
      "I was working WHEN the phone rang."
      "WHILE I was working, the phone rang."
      "She was driving WHEN the light changed."
      "WHILE she was driving, the light changed."
      *** Same meaning, two doorways. ***
      *** WHEN -> short action. WHILE -> long action. ***

   TWO LONG ACTIONS AT ONCE (both continuous, WHILE)
      "WHILE I was studying, my brother was cooking."
      "They were talking WHILE we were waiting."

   QUESTION ORDER
      "What WERE you DOING WHEN I called?"
      "What WERE you DOING WHILE I was studying?"
      Short answer keeps -ing: "I was having lunch."

2. [Board] SEÑALA LONG. DI: "The background — already
   in progress — is PAST CONTINUOUS. was/were + verb-ing.
   It has length. 'I WAS working.' 'They WERE having a
   meeting.'"

3. [Board] SEÑALA SHORT. DI: "The strike that cuts in —
   short, sudden — is PAST SIMPLE. 'the phone RANG.' One
   strike. Done."

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "WHILE +
   was/were-ing is the long action that endures; WHEN +
   past simple is the strike. The background stretches;
   the strike lands; courage keeps the form clean."

5. [Board] SEÑALA COMBINING. DI: "WHEN points at the
   short action in simple: 'when the phone RANG.' WHILE
   points at the long action in continuous: 'while I WAS
   working.' Same story, two doorways."

6. [Board] SEÑALA TWO LONG ACTIONS. DI: "No strike —
   two backgrounds running together? Both continuous,
   joined by WHILE. 'WHILE I was studying, my brother
   was cooking.'"

7. [Board] SEÑALA QUESTION ORDER. DI: "Ask about the
   background and you use continuous: 'What WERE you
   DOING when I called?' The answer keeps the -ing."

8. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I worked when the phone rang."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I WAS WORKING when the
   phone rang." DI: "The long action is the background —
   stretch it to continuous." COHORTE repite 2 veces en
   coro (de pie).

9. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "While I worked, it happened."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "WHILE I WAS WORKING, it
   happened." DI: "WHILE always points at the long
   action — continuous." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

10. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I was work when she called."
    -> TACHA "was work", ESCRIBE "I WAS WORKING when
    she called." DI: "Continuous needs the -ing. was +
    working." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

11. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien deje la accion
    larga en simple, DI suave: "Fortitude. The long
    action endures — WAS working. Stretch it to
    continuous."

12. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "While the phone rang, I was
    working." -> reordena: ESCRIBE "WHILE I was working,
    the phone rang." / "I was working WHEN the phone
    rang." DI: "WHEN points at the short strike; WHILE
    at the long action. Don't swap them." COHORTE repite
    2 veces.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "What you were doing when I
    called?" -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "What WERE you doing when
    I called?" DI: "Continuous question: WERE before the
    subject." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

14. [Stand, parejas de pie] PIDELES: "Transform 10
    prompts. I dictate a long action + a short event —
    you build the combined sentence, alternating: half
    with WHEN, half with WHILE." 6 min. DICTA estos 10:
       a. (I / study) + (my brother / arrive)  -> "I was studying WHEN my brother arrived"
       b. (she / drive) + (the light / change)  -> "WHILE she was driving, the light changed"
       c. (we / have a meeting) + (the manager / walk in) -> "We were having a meeting WHEN the manager walked in"
       d. (they / walk in the park) + (Jeff / see a dog)  -> "WHILE they were walking in the park, Jeff saw a dog"
       e. (I / write the report) + (the system / crash)   -> "I was writing the report WHEN the system crashed"
       f. (he / present) + (the screen / go black)        -> "WHILE he was presenting, the screen went black"
       g. (you / cook) + (the phone / ring)               -> "You were cooking WHEN the phone rang"
       h. (we / close the store) + (the alarm / ring)     -> "WHILE we were closing the store, the alarm rang"
       i. (she / send the email) + (the client / call)    -> "She was sending the email WHEN the client called"
       j. (I / wait) + (the bus / arrive)                 -> "WHILE I was waiting, the bus arrived"
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca 2
    veces.

15. [Stand] PODIUM — WHEN/WHILE SPRINT (7 min). DOS
    volunteers al frente. Tu das un kit (long action +
    short event) y construyen la oracion completa DE LAS
    DOS FORMAS (con WHEN y con WHILE), manteniendo
    continuous en la accion larga. Cohorte verifica en
    coro: "Confirmed — long continuous, short simple!"

    8 kits para llamar:
       (I / read) + (the boss / call)        -> "I was reading WHEN the boss called" / "WHILE I was reading, the boss called"
       (she / drive) + (it / start to rain)  -> "She was driving WHEN it started to rain" / "WHILE she was driving, it started to rain"
       (they / talk) + (we / arrive)         -> "They were talking WHEN we arrived" / "WHILE they were talking, we arrived"
       (we / wait) + (the email / come)      -> "We were waiting WHEN the email came" / "WHILE we were waiting, the email came"
       (he / work) + (the power / go out)    -> "He was working WHEN the power went out" / "WHILE he was working, the power went out"
       (I / present) + (my phone / ring)     -> "I was presenting WHEN my phone rang" / "WHILE I was presenting, my phone rang"
       (you / study) + (I / leave)           -> "You were studying WHEN I left" / "WHILE you were studying, I left"
       (she / cook) + (the guests / arrive)  -> "She was cooking WHEN the guests arrived" / "WHILE she was cooking, the guests arrived"

    Plus 2 TWO-LONG-ACTIONS challenges (both continuous):
       (I / study) + (my brother / cook)     -> "WHILE I was studying, my brother WAS cooking"
       (they / talk) + (we / wait)           -> "They were talking WHILE we were waiting"

16. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% construya long-
    continuous + short-simple con WHEN/WHILE sin titubear.
    DI: "Stay standing. Push chairs to the walls. We go
    to the live sprint."
```

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: WHEN/WHILE INTERRUPTION SPRINT (~45 min) · 100% STAND

```
1. [Board] ESCRIBE en board grande: "WHEN/WHILE
   INTERRUPTION SPRINT — 18 BUILDS, 3 ROUNDS — TODOS DE
   PIE"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs, standing the whole block.
   PHASE A — 10 interruption builds: I give a long
   action + a short event, your pair builds the
   combined sentence and SAYS it out loud, then writes
   it on the board grid I'm about to draw. Alternate
   WHEN and WHILE. PHASE B — 8 transformations: I give
   a WHEN-sentence, your pair flips it to a WHILE-
   sentence (or the reverse). Observer counts continuous-
   on-the-long-action maintained vs simple slips. Then
   you rotate roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This sprint IS the phrase
   — WHILE + was/were-ing endures, WHEN + simple
   strikes. The background stretches; the strike lands;
   courage keeps the form clean."

4. [Board] DIBUJA un grid 2x10 vacio para PHASE A (cada
   par anota su build de pie en una celda):

     #   LONG ACTION        + SHORT EVENT          CONNECTOR
     1   I / finish report    the laptop / die       WHEN
     2   she / drive          the light / change     WHILE
     3   we / have lunch      the manager / arrive    WHEN
     4   they / close store   the alarm / ring        WHILE
     5   he / present         the screen / go black   WHEN
     6   I / send the email   the client / call       WHILE
     7   you / cook           the phone / ring         WHEN
     8   we / walk in park    Jeff / see a dog         WHILE
     9   she / study          her brother / arrive     WHEN
    10   I / wait at the gate the bus / leave          WHILE

5. [Stand] PIDELES round 1 — PHASE A (12 min): pareja
   toma builds 1-5. Roles claros: A dicta/verifica, B
   construye + escribe en el grid, luego switch en mid-
   pair. Observer cuenta slips. Todos de pie.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a
   parejas.

7. [Stand] ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 — PHASE
   A parte 2 (10 min): builds 6-10. Mismo formato, de
   pie.

8. [VIRTUD] activation durante los rounds: si alguien
   escriba "I worked when..." o "While I worked" sin
   -ing, DI suave: "Fortitude. The long action endures
   — WAS working. Stretch it to continuous."

9. [Stand] ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 — PHASE B
   (15 min): WHEN/WHILE FLIP. DICTA 8 sentences, pareja
   las voltea a la otra forma. Plus 2 two-long-actions
   challenges al final.

    8 flip prompts:
       1. "I was working WHEN the phone rang"        -> "WHILE I was working, the phone rang"
       2. "WHILE she was driving, it started to rain" -> "She was driving WHEN it started to rain"
       3. "They were talking WHEN we arrived"         -> "WHILE they were talking, we arrived"
       4. "WHILE we were waiting, the email came"     -> "We were waiting WHEN the email came"
       5. "He was presenting WHEN the power went out" -> "WHILE he was presenting, the power went out"
       6. "WHILE I was reading, the boss called"      -> "I was reading WHEN the boss called"
       7. "You were studying WHEN I left"             -> "WHILE you were studying, I left"
       8. "WHILE she was cooking, the guests arrived" -> "She was cooking WHEN the guests arrived"

    2 TWO-LONG-ACTIONS challenges (both continuous):
       9.  (I / study) + (brother / cook)  -> "WHILE I was studying, my brother was cooking"
      10.  (they / talk) + (we / wait)      -> "They were talking WHILE we were waiting"

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. [Stand] CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos
    frente al grupo (4 builds + 4 flips in a row,
    alternating WHEN/WHILE). COHORTE cuenta cuantas
    continuous-on-the-long-action maintained. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Build: 'I / write' + 'the system / crash' with WHEN") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rounds completos (PHASE A x 10 + PHASE B x 8 + 2 two-long-actions) + UNA demo al frente con continuous-on-the-long-action visible. Bloque corrido 100% de pie.

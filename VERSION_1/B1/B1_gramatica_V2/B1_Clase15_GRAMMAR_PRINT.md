## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT TABLE WAS/WERE + -ING (~50 min) — ~80% DE PIE

```
1. ESCRIBE la tabla completa en board (DE PIE, toma 5
   min, vale la pena):

   PAST CONTINUOUS — was/were + verb-ING

   AFFIRMATIVE — agreement
      I / he / she / it   WAS  + verb-ING
      you / we / they     WERE + verb-ING

      I was working.        You were working.
      He was studying.      We were waiting.
      She was driving.      They were training.
      It was raining.

   NEGATIVE — wasn't / weren't + -ing
      I wasn't driving the Model T last year.
      She wasn't studying at that time.
      They weren't driving to school.
      We weren't waiting for him.

   YES/NO QUESTION — Was/Were + subj + -ing
      Was he working last night?
      Were you waiting for the supplier?
      Was she studying for the exam?
      Were they driving to the office?

   SHORT ANSWER
      Yes, I was.    /  No, I wasn't.
      Yes, we were.  /  No, we weren't.
      Yes, he was.   /  No, he wasn't.
      *** Never repeat the full verb. ***

   PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS
      I worked at 8.       = done, a closed dot
      I was working at 8.  = in progress, an open line

   *** was/were carries the tense. ***
   *** the verb ALWAYS wears -ing. ***

2. SEÑALA AFFIRMATIVE. DI (DE PIE): "WAS for I, he, she,
   it. WERE for you, we, they. The verb wears -ing every
   time. 'I WAS WORKING.' 'They WERE WAITING.' One helper
   plus -ing — no exceptions."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "WAS for I,
   he, she, it; WERE for you, we, they; the verb always
   wears -ing. wasn't and weren't deny it, Was and Were
   ask it. Ongoing is the form's justice."

4. SEÑALA NEGATIVE. DI: "wasn't for I/he/she/it. weren't
   for you/we/they. The -ing stays. 'She wasn't
   studying.' 'They weren't driving.'"

5. SEÑALA YES/NO QUESTION. DI: "To ask, Was or Were jumps
   to the FRONT. 'WAS he working?' 'WERE you waiting?' The
   verb keeps -ing. Never 'Was he work?'"

6. SEÑALA SHORT ANSWER. DI: "Short and clean. 'Yes, I
   was.' 'No, they weren't.' You never repeat the verb."

7. SEÑALA el contraste past simple vs continuous. DI:
   "Past simple is a dot — done. Past continuous is a
   line — it was still running. Today we live in the
   line. 'I worked' closes; 'I was working' stays open."

8. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I was play football"
   -> TACHA "play", ESCRIBE "I was PLAYING football."
   DI: "The verb wears -ing. Was PLAYING." COHORTE repite
   2 veces en coro (DE PIE).

9. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "They was working late"
   -> TACHA "was", ESCRIBE "They WERE working late."
   DI: "They/we/you take WERE." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

10. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I studying at 8"
    -> ESCRIBE "I WAS studying at 8."
    DI: "No floating verb. The was/were must be there."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

11. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien titubee con
    was/were o se coma el -ing, DI suave: "Justice. The
    moment was in motion — was or were, plus the -ing.
    Write it whole."

12. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "Where was Joe work last year?"
    -> TACHA "work", ESCRIBE "Where was Joe WORKING last
    year?" DI: "Even in a question — the -ing stays."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "We was waiting"
    -> TACHA "was", ESCRIBE "We WERE waiting."
    DI: "We take WERE. Agreement first, then -ing."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas (DE PIE en el board, transform 10):
    "I dictate a past-simple sentence — your pair rewrites
    it in PAST CONTINUOUS and writes it on the board."
    6 min. DICTA estos 10:
       a. "I worked at 8"            -> "I was WORKING at 8"
       b. "She studied last night"   -> "She was STUDYING last night"
       c. "They drove to the office" -> "They were DRIVING to the office"
       d. "We waited for the client" -> "We were WAITING for the client"
       e. "He trained the new hire"  -> "He was TRAINING the new hire"
       f. "I lived in Bucaramanga"   -> "I was LIVING in Bucaramanga"
       g. "Paula swam in the pool"   -> "Paula was SWIMMING in the pool"
       h. "You reviewed the report"  -> "You were REVIEWING the report"
       i. "It rained in the morning" -> "It was RAINING in the morning"
       j. "We finished the proposal" -> "We were FINISHING the proposal"
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca 2 veces.

15. PODIUM — WAS/WERE SPRINT (7 min, DE PIE). DOS
    volunteers al frente. Tu das un kit (SUBJECT + VERB +
    time) y construyen la frase completa en past
    continuous, manteniendo agreement + -ing. Cohorte
    verifica en coro: "Confirmed — was/were + -ing!"

    8 kits para llamar:
       I    / review / at 9      -> "I was REVIEWING at 9"
       they / wait   / for him   -> "They were WAITING for him"
       she  / study  / last week -> "She was STUDYING last week"
       we   / drive  / to work   -> "We were DRIVING to work"
       he   / train  / the team  -> "He was TRAINING the team"
       you  / take   / notes     -> "You were TAKING notes"
       it   / rain   / at noon   -> "It was RAINING at noon"
       they / finish / the report-> "They were FINISHING the report"

    Plus 2 question challenges:
       Was  / he / work?    -> "WAS he WORKING?"
       Were / you / wait?   -> "WERE you WAITING?"

16. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% construya
    was/were + -ing con agreement correcto sin titubear.
    DI: "Stay standing. Push chairs to the walls. We go
    to the live sprint."
```

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: WAS/WERE + -ING SPRINT (~45 min) — 100% DE PIE

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "WAS/WERE + -ING SPRINT —
   18 BUILDS, 3 ROUNDS — ALL STANDING"

2. DI las reglas (DE PIE): "On your feet, in pairs. Two
   phases. PHASE A — 10 transformation drills: I read a
   past-SIMPLE sentence, your pair turns it into past
   CONTINUOUS, says it OUT LOUD, then writes it on the
   board grid. PHASE B — 8 Was/Were question builds: I
   give subject + verb + time, your pair builds the full
   Was/Were question in past continuous. Observer counts
   -ing maintained + was/were agreement vs slips. Then
   you rotate roles. Nobody sits."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This sprint IS the phrase —
   WAS for I/he/she/it, WERE for you/we/they, the verb
   always wears -ing. wasn't/weren't deny, Was/Were ask.
   Ongoing is the form's justice."

4. DIBUJA en board un grid 2x10 vacio para PHASE A (cada
   par anota su transformacion en una celda):

     #   PAST SIMPLE                  -> PAST CONTINUOUS
     1   She finished the report         (was/were + -ing)
     2   I called the manager            (was/were + -ing)
     3   He drove to the airport         (was/were + -ing)
     4   They waited for the supplier     (was/were + -ing)
     5   We reviewed the document         (was/were + -ing)
     6   She trained new equipment users  (was/were + -ing)
     7   I took the early train           (was/were + -ing)
     8   He sent the invoices             (was/were + -ing)
     9   They studied the material        (was/were + -ing)
    10   We wrote the proposal            (was/were + -ing)

5. PIDELES round 1 — PHASE A (12 min): pareja toma
   transformaciones 1-5 (DE PIE en el grid). Roles claros:
   A dicta/verifica, B transforma + escribe, luego switch
   en mid-pair. Observer cuenta slips.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a
   parejas.

7. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 — PHASE A parte 2
   (10 min): transformaciones 6-10. Mismo formato, DE PIE.

8. [VIRTUD] activation durante los rounds: si alguien
   escriba "didn't wait" en vez de continuous, o "they
   was waiting", DI suave: "Justice. The moment was in
   motion — was or were, plus the -ing."

9. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 — PHASE B (15 min):
   WAS/WERE QUESTION SPRINT (DE PIE). DICTA los 8 kits
   (subject + verb + time), pareja construye la pregunta
   completa Was/Were + subj + -ing. Plus 2 negative
   challenges al final.

    8 Was/Were question kits:
       1. he   / work   / last night  -> "Was he WORKING last night?"
       2. you  / wait   / for the bus  -> "Were you WAITING for the bus?"
       3. she  / study  / at 9         -> "Was she STUDYING at 9?"
       4. they / drive  / to the office-> "Were they DRIVING to the office?"
       5. we   / take   / notes        -> "Were we TAKING notes?"
       6. the manager / call / supplier-> "Was the manager CALLING the supplier?"
       7. you  / review / the report   -> "Were you REVIEWING the report?"
       8. it   / rain   / at noon      -> "Was it RAINING at noon?"

    2 NEGATIVE challenges:
       9.  I    / not answer / emails  -> "I WASN'T ANSWERING emails."
      10.  they / not wait  / for him  -> "They WEREN'T WAITING for him."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (4 transformations + 4 Was/Were questions in a
    row, alternating, DE PIE). COHORTE cuenta cuantos
    -ing + agreement maintained. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Transform: 'I worked late' -> ?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.

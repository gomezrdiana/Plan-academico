## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT TABLE + POSITION + SEQUENCING (~50 min) — DE PIE EN BOARD

```
1. (Stand, write) ESCRIBE la tabla completa en board
   (toma 5 min, vale la pena):

   PAST TIME EXPRESSIONS — THE TABLE

   PREPOSITION   USE              EXAMPLE
   AT            clock time       at 8:00 / at 6:30
   ON            day / date       on Monday / on the 5th
   IN            year / month     in 2020 / in June
   (no prep)     today-ish words  yesterday / this morning
   LAST + period last span        last week (NO "the")
   ___ AGO       back from now    two days ago (AFTER period)

   *** Book anchor (A2 Module 12): "I ate a sandwich
       AT 8:00." "Steve woke up AT 6:30 today." "I was
       living in Bucaramanga IN 2019." "last week",
       "last year." ***

   POSITION
      END (default):  I finished it YESTERDAY.
      FRONT (+comma): YESTERDAY, I finished it.
      *** Never mid-verb: not "I yesterday finished". ***

   SEQUENCING A PAST STORY
      FIRST, ... / THEN, ... / AFTER THAT, ... /
      BEFORE ..., ... / FINALLY, ...

2. (Point) SEÑALA la fila AGO. DI: "AGO counts back
   from NOW and goes AFTER the period. 'Two days ago.'
   'Three weeks ago.' NEVER 'ago two days'."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "AGO goes
   after the period, LAST takes no 'the', IN holds the
   year, and the time word lands at the end. Fair
   time, true story."

4. (Point) SEÑALA la fila LAST. DI: "LAST plus the
   period — no 'the', no 'in'. 'Last week.' 'Last
   Monday.' NEVER 'the last week', NEVER 'in last
   week'."

5. (Point) SEÑALA AT / ON / IN. DI: "AT for the clock
   — at 8:00. ON for the day — on Monday. IN for the
   year or month — in 2020, in June. The book says it:
   'I ate a sandwich at 8:00.' 'I was living in
   Bucaramanga in 2019.'"

6. (Point) SEÑALA POSITION. DI: "Default — the time
   word lands at the END. For emphasis, move it to the
   FRONT with a comma. Never jam it mid-verb."

7. (Point) SEÑALA SEQUENCING. DI: "These words order a
   past story. FIRST, THEN, AFTER THAT, BEFORE,
   FINALLY. The verb stays past simple; the sequencing
   word gives the order; the time word gives the
   place."

8. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I called him ago two days."
   -> TACHA "ago two days", ESCRIBE "I called him TWO
   DAYS AGO." DI: "AGO after the period." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces en coro (de pie).

9. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I worked the last Monday."
   -> TACHA "the last Monday", ESCRIBE "I worked LAST
   MONDAY." DI: "LAST takes no 'the'." COHORTE repite
   2 veces.

10. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "In last week I traveled."
    -> TACHA "In last week", ESCRIBE "LAST WEEK I
    traveled." DI: "No 'in' before LAST." COHORTE
    repite 2 veces.

11. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien titubee con la
    posicion de la expresion de tiempo, DI suave:
    "Justice. Give the time its fair place — AGO
    after, LAST no 'the', IN for the year."

12. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "Yesterday I go to the office."
    -> TACHA "go", ESCRIBE "Yesterday I WENT to the
    office." DI: "A past time word demands a past
    verb." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "I yesterday called him."
    -> TACHA, ESCRIBE "I called him YESTERDAY." DI:
    "Time word at the END. Not mid-verb." COHORTE
    repite 2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas (de pie, anotan en sus
    cuadernos contra la pared o de pie): "Transform 10
    sentences. I dictate a bare past sentence — you
    ADD the correct time expression in the right place
    and fix any tense drift." 6 min. DICTA estos 10:
       a. "I call him / two days"     -> "I called him TWO DAYS AGO"
       b. "She start the project / last week" -> "She started the project LAST WEEK"
       c. "He join the company / 2020" -> "He joined the company IN 2020"
       d. "We meet the client / Monday" -> "We met the client ON MONDAY"
       e. "They arrive / 8:00"        -> "They arrived AT 8:00"
       f. "I travel to Bogota / last year" -> "I traveled to Bogota LAST YEAR"
       g. "She send the report / yesterday" -> "She sent the report YESTERDAY"
       h. "He leave the office / this morning" -> "He left the office THIS MORNING"
       i. "We finish / three weeks ago" -> "We finished it THREE WEEKS AGO"
       j. "I study English / when I was younger" -> "I studied English WHEN I WAS YOUNGER"
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    2 veces.

15. PODIUM — SEQUENCING SPRINT (7 min, DE PIE). DOS
    volunteers al frente. Tu das 5 acciones sueltas;
    ellos las ORDENAN con first / then / after that /
    before / finally, cada una con su time word.
    Cohorte verifica en coro: "Confirmed — ordered and
    anchored!"

    1 kit de 5 acciones para llamar (modelo):
       review budget / call supplier / send report /
       print it / close the week
       -> "FIRST, last Monday, I reviewed the budget.
           THEN, two days ago, I called the supplier.
           AFTER THAT, yesterday, I sent the report.
           BEFORE the meeting, this morning, I printed
           it. FINALLY, I closed the week."

    Plus 3 anchor challenges (place the time word):
       "I called him ___ (two days)"   -> "...two days ago"
       "She started ___ (last / week)"  -> "...last week"
       "He joined ___ (2020)"           -> "...in 2020"

16. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% coloque la
    expresion de tiempo en su lugar (AGO after, LAST
    no 'the', IN + year, posicion al final) y ordene
    con sequencing words sin titubear. DI: "Stand.
    Push chairs to the walls. We go to the live drill."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 35 min y aun pegan "ago two days" / "the last week" / drift a presente, NO AVANCES al podium de sequencing. Reduce: solo la tabla de prepositions + AGO position hoy, deja sequencing para Cl 15 opener. Mejor LAND 2 bien que rush 4 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas + podium produciendo time expressions en su lugar correcto + una historia ordenada con sequencing sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: TIME-ANCHOR SPRINT + SEQUENCING BUILD (~45 min) — DE PIE

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "TIME-ANCHOR SPRINT +
   SEQUENCING BUILD — 18 BUILDS, 3 ROUNDS"

2. (Stand) DI las reglas: "In pairs, standing. Two
   phases. PHASE A — 10 anchor drills: I give you a
   bare past sentence + a time hint, your pair places
   the time expression in the RIGHT spot and fixes any
   tense, says it OUT LOUD, then writes it in the grid
   on the board. PHASE B — 8 sequencing builds: I give
   you a set of actions, your pair orders them with
   first / then / after that / before / finally, each
   action anchored in time. Observer counts time words
   placed correctly vs slips. Then you rotate roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This drill IS the phrase
   — AGO goes after the period, LAST takes no 'the',
   IN holds the year, time word at the end. Fair time,
   true story."

4. (Stand, draw) DIBUJA en board un grid 2x10 vacio
   para PHASE A (cada par anota su anclaje en una
   celda):

     #   BARE SENTENCE + HINT          -> ANCHORED
     1   I call him / two days            (AGO)
     2   She start the project / last week (LAST)
     3   He join the company / 2020       (IN)
     4   We meet the client / Monday      (ON)
     5   They arrive / 8:00               (AT)
     6   I travel to Bogota / last year   (LAST)
     7   She send the report / yesterday  (no prep)
     8   He leave / this morning          (no prep)
     9   We finish / three weeks ago      (AGO)
    10   I study English / when I was younger (clause)

5. PIDELES round 1 — PHASE A (12 min): pareja toma
   anclajes 1-5. Roles claros: A dicta / verifica, B
   ancla + escribe en el grid, luego switch en mid-
   pair. Observer cuenta slips. TODOS de pie en el
   board.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven
   a parejas.

7. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 — PHASE A
   parte 2 (10 min): anclajes 6-10. Mismo formato, de
   pie.

8. [VIRTUD] activation durante los rounds: si alguien
   escriba "ago two days" o "the last week" en el
   grid, DI suave: "Justice. Give the time its fair
   place — AGO after, LAST no 'the'."

9. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 — PHASE B (15 min):
   SEQUENCING BUILD. DICTA los 8 sets de acciones, la
   pareja ordena cada uno con first / then / after
   that / before / finally + time words, de pie y al
   board.

    8 sequencing kits (sets of actions to order):
       1. check email / call supplier / send invoice
       2. review budget / print report / start meeting
       3. open office / greet client / show product
       4. arrive / clock in / read messages
       5. finish lunch / answer calls / update sheet
       6. board flight / land / take a taxi
       7. wake up / get dressed / leave home
       8. close sale / sign contract / shake hands

    Cada set -> "FIRST, [time], I... THEN, [time]...
    AFTER THAT... BEFORE... FINALLY..."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

11. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (3 anchors + 1 full sequenced story).
    COHORTE cuenta cuantas time expressions placed
    correctly. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Anchor: 'I called him' + two days") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rounds completos (PHASE A x 10 + PHASE B x 8) + UNA demo al frente con time expressions placed correctly visibles — todo de pie.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT HAD + PARTICIPLE + SIGNAL FRAMES (~50 min) — STAND ~90%

```
1. (Stand at board) ESCRIBE el MAPA completo (toma 6 min,
   vale la pena):

   PAST PERFECT — THE EARLIER EVENT

   STRUCTURE
      HAD + past participle   (HAD never changes)
          "I had started."  "She had gone."
          "They had finished."  "He had eaten."

   THE PARTICIPLE (from Cl 15-16)
      regular:  play -> played   finish -> finished
      irregular: go -> gone   do -> done   eat -> eaten
                see -> seen   write -> written

   TWO EVENTS — WHO CAME FIRST?
      LATER event (main)   -> past simple   "I arrived."
      EARLIER event        -> had + participle
                              "...the meeting had started."

   SIGNAL FRAMES
      "By the time + [simple], + [had + participle]"
      "[simple] + because + [had + participle]"
      "Before + [simple], + [had + participle]"

   *** Later = past simple.  Earlier = HAD + participle. ***
   *** HAD is the same for every subject. ***

2. (Stand) SEÑALA STRUCTURE. DI: "Past perfect = HAD +
   participle. HAD does not change — not has, not have-d,
   just HAD for everybody. The participle is the third
   form you learned in Cl 15-16: go, went, GONE. We use
   the GONE part."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2 (Stand): SEÑALA la frase. DI:
   "HAD + participle marks what was already done before
   the main past event. 'By the time it happened, I had
   already started.' One past under another, both clean."

4. (Stand) SEÑALA TWO EVENTS. DI: "The main action stays
   simple — 'I arrived'. The thing that was already done
   BEFORE it takes HAD — 'the meeting had started'. Decide
   which came first; the earlier one wears HAD."

5. (Stand) SEÑALA SIGNAL FRAMES. DI: "Three frames carry
   the order: 'by the time...', '...because...', and
   'before...'. Each one tells the listener the HAD-clause
   happened earlier. Without them, the order gets lost."

6. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "The meeting had start."
   -> TACHA "start", ESCRIBE "The meeting had STARTED."
   DI: "After HAD = participle. Never the bare verb."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She had went."
   -> TACHA "went", ESCRIBE "She had GONE." DI: "go ->
   went -> GONE. The participle, not the past simple.
   had GONE, had DONE, had EATEN, had SEEN." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "When I arrived, it started" (cuando
   el sentido es que ya habia empezado). -> ESCRIBE las
   dos versiones y SEÑALA la diferencia:
   "When I arrived, it started." (I arrived first)
   "When I arrived, it HAD started." (it started first)
   DI: "Same words — HAD changes the order in time."
   COHORTE repite la segunda 2 veces.

9. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): cuando alguien escriba
   "had went" o aplane los eventos, DI suave: "Fortitude.
   The earlier event = HAD + participle. Which came first?"

10. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "I had ate before he arrived."
    -> TACHA "ate", ESCRIBE "I had EATEN before he
    arrived." DI: "eat -> ate -> EATEN. Participle after
    HAD." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

11. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "I was tired because I work all
    night." -> TACHA "work", ESCRIBE "...because I HAD
    WORKED all night." DI: "The earlier cause = had +
    participle." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

12. (Stand) PIDELES en parejas DE PIE en el board:
    "Transform 8 clauses — put the EARLIER event into HAD
    + participle. I dictate two events; you write the
    earlier one with HAD and say why." 8 min.
    DICTA estos 8 (el evento anterior entre parentesis):
       a. "I arrived / (the train + leave)"   -> "...the train HAD LEFT" (irregular)
       b. "She was tired / (she + work + all night)" -> "...she HAD WORKED all night"
       c. "By the time I called / (he + go)"   -> "...he HAD GONE"
       d. "I wasn't hungry / (I + eat)"         -> "...I HAD EATEN"
       e. "We knew the city / (we + visit + before)" -> "...we HAD VISITED it before"
       f. "He failed / (he + not study)"        -> "...he HADN'T STUDIED"
       g. "Before the meeting / (I + prepare)"  -> "...I HAD PREPARED"
       h. "They were ready / (they + practice + for weeks)" -> "...they HAD PRACTICED for weeks"
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    2 veces.

13. PODIUM — "WHICH CAME FIRST?" SPRINT (7 min). DOS
    volunteers al frente DE PIE. Tu lees DOS eventos
    pasados; ellos deciden cual fue PRIMERO y lo ponen en
    HAD + participle. Cohorte verifica en coro: "Confirmed
    — earlier event, HAD + participle!"

    8 pairs de eventos para llamar:
       "I opened the door / the guests already left"
            -> "...the guests had already LEFT"
       "She smiled / she pass the exam"
            -> "...she had PASSED the exam"
       "He apologized / he forget her birthday"
            -> "...he had FORGOTTEN her birthday"
       "We sold the house / we live there 10 years"
            -> "...we had LIVED there for 10 years"
       "I recognized him / we meet once before"
            -> "...we had MET once before"
       "They celebrated / they finish the project"
            -> "...they had FINISHED the project"
       "I was nervous / I never speak in public"
            -> "...I had NEVER SPOKEN in public"
       "The boss thanked me / I save the deal"
            -> "...I had SAVED the deal"

14. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% ponga el evento
    anterior en HAD + participle con el orden correcto.
    DI: "Stand. Push chairs to the walls. We build a
    two-layer story on the board."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 35 min y aun escriben "had went" o aplanan los eventos, NO AVANCES al podium. Reduce a UN frame ("by the time + simple, + had + participle") y deja el contraste para Cl 20 opener. Mejor LAND una flashback limpia que rush cinco confusas.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas + podium poniendo el evento anterior en HAD + participle, con el participio correcto, sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: TWO-LAYER STORY SPRINT (~45 min) — STAND 100%

```
1. (Stand) ESCRIBE en board grande: "TWO-LAYER STORY
   SPRINT — ONE STORY, EVERY PAIR ADDS A FLASHBACK —
   18+ BUILDS, 3 ROUNDS"

2. DI las reglas: "We build ONE long past story together
   on the board, standing. I write the main line in past
   simple. Then each pair adds ONE clause — but at least
   every second clause must be a FLASHBACK: something that
   HAD already happened before, in HAD + participle. Say
   it out loud, then write it. The next pair connects with
   a signal word (by the time / because / before / already).
   If a flashback is wrong — 'had went', bare verb, or the
   order is backwards — the cohort calls 'Fortitude!' and
   the pair fixes it before we move on."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3 (Stand): DI: "This sprint IS the
   phrase — HAD + participle marks what was already done
   before the main past event. By the time it happened, I
   had already started. One past under another, both
   clean."

4. (Stand) ESCRIBE la PRIMERA linea semilla en board y el
   banco de signals al lado:

     SEED: "Last year I walked into my first big meeting."

     SIGNAL BANK (each flashback must use one):
        by the time / because / before / already /
        after / once / when (...had...)

   ROUND 1 (15 min) — BUILD THE BACKSTORY:
   pairs add, in turn, clauses 2-7. At least half are
   flashbacks. Target shape:
        "By the time I walked in, my manager HAD already
         chosen me for a hard client. I was nervous
         because I HAD never managed an account alone.
         I HAD failed a smaller project before..."
   Cada pareja: 1 clausula; los flashbacks en HAD +
   participle con un signal word.

5. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> sigue la cadena.

6. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): si una clausula diga "had
   went" o ponga HAD en el evento equivocado, DI suave:
   "Fortitude. The earlier event = HAD + participle.
   Which came first?"

7. ROTA el turno. ROUND 2 (15 min) — BUILD THE MAIN
   STRUGGLE (past simple) WITH FLASHBACKS UNDER IT:
   pairs add clauses 8-13. Target shape:
        "First I panicked. I didn't know the product
         because I HAD not finished the training. Then I
         asked a colleague who HAD done it before..."
   Cada par alterna: una accion principal (simple) + una
   flashback (had + participle).

8. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If a pair freezes,
   plan in Spanish for 5 seconds: which event came FIRST?
   That one is HAD + participle. Then say it in English."

9. ROTA el turno otra vez. ROUND 3 (15 min) — BUILD THE
   RESOLUTION + a final FLASHBACK CLOSE: pairs add clauses
   14-18+. Target:
        "After that I practiced every night. Finally I
         closed the deal — and I realized I HAD grown more
         in one month than I HAD expected in a year."
   Push: al menos 18 clausulas, con al menos 8 flashbacks
   en HAD + participle, orden de tiempo claro. Observer
   cuenta "had went / bare verb" slips.

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. CIERRA con LECTURA CORAL: la cohorte lee la historia
    completa del board en voz alta, de principio a fin,
    todos de pie. UN voluntario reporta: "X clauses, Y
    flashbacks in HAD + participle, order clear." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Add a flashback: what HAD already happened before this?" / "Connect with 'by the time'") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rounds completos (18+ clausulas, 8+ flashbacks encadenados) + lectura coral final con el orden de tiempo claro.

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Two zones in the room (tape labels on opposite walls):
- **CAUSE → -ING** ("the meeting", "the project", "the book", "the teacher" — CREATES the effect)
- **FEELING → -ED** ("I", "the team", "the student", "the boss" — FEELS the effect)

Hand each student **ONE Set E slip** with a reversed-shape sentence.

### Round 1 — Walk it — 8 min

Each student:
1. Reads slip aloud with the wrong form.
2. Walks to the CORRECT zone depending on subject role, shouting the corrected form: *"I am EXCITED about the new project!"* / *"The Monday meeting was TIRING!"* / *"I am INTERESTED in the policy!"*
3. Cohort confirms with thumbs + names the rule aloud: *"CAUSE → -ING, correct"* / *"FEELER → -ED, correct."*

Suggested slips:

```
"I am exciting about the new project."        → "I am EXCITED..."             (feeler)
"The Monday meeting was tired."               → "The Monday meeting was TIRING." (cause)
"I am interesting in the analytics dashboard."→ "I am INTERESTED in..."        (feeler)
"The team is confusing about the new policy." → "The team is CONFUSED..."     (feeler)
"The new policy is confused."                 → "The new policy is CONFUSING." (cause)
"The Friday status is drained."               → "The Friday status is DRAINING." (cause)
"I am tiring after ten hours of email."       → "I am TIRED..."                (feeler)
"The sprint was excited!"                     → "The sprint was EXCITING!"     (cause)
"Mark was sleeping in the meeting. He was boring." → "He was BORED." [book p.329] (feeler)
"Julio is always sad at work. He is very depressed."→ "He is very DEPRESSING." [book p.329 cause variant]
"My marks were very disappointed last quarter." → "...DISAPPOINTING last quarter." (cause)
"Monica is disappointing that her father cannot visit."→ "Monica is DISAPPOINTED..." (feeler)
```

> The "Julio is depressing" vs "depressed" trap is intentional — the SAME person can carry both forms depending on role. Temperance: think the role, don't apply template.

### Round 2 — Reverse — 4 min

Swap slips. Each student starts in RIGHT zone and walks to WRONG zone saying why it was wrong: *"'I am exciting' is wrong — 'exciting' is the cause shape; I FEEL excitement, so 'I am EXCITED' — book p.328: present participle for the cause, past participle for the feeler."*

### Close — 2 min

2 volunteers walk their transformation in front of the group. Cohort counts correct pairs + names the role (CAUSE→-ING or FEELING→-ED). Note errors with NAMES.

```

K: "The Friday status meeting is frustrating." → L: "I am frustrated by it." → "Confirmed!"

```

"the meeting" / CAUSE   → "The meeting is boring."
"the student" / FEELER  → "The student is bored."

"the sales team" / FEELER → "The sales team is confused."
"the client demo" / CAUSE → "The client demo was exciting."

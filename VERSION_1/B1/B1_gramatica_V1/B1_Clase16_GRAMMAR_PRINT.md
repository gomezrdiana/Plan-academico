## 6. LIBRO M23 — NON-SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 23, page 221 — 'I Asked for A Pencil'. A verb plus a small word makes a new verb — and these ones never split. Then we race it on the board."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M23 (pág 221-232) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M23 — BASIC, NON-SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS (A2 Book p.221-232)
       "I Asked for A Pencil."

From the book (Guia 1):
"1. Los verbos frasales consisten en un verbo + una
 preposicion/adverbio = nuevo verbo."
   Ej. "Look" ("mirar") + "after" ("despues") = "look after"
   -> "cuidar"
"2. Ya has aprendido 3 verbos frasales: 'wake up',
 'get dressed' y 'hang out'."
"3. Se aplican las conjugaciones al verbo (la primera
 palabra) de forma normal:"
   Presente:   I look after Amy.    -> Yo cuido a Amy.
   3ra Sing.:  He looks after Amy.  -> El cuida a Amy.
   Pasado:     He looked after Amy. -> El cuido a Amy.
   Continuo:   He is looking after Amy.
"4. Hay verbos 'separables' (la mayoria) y 'no separables'.
 En este modulo, miramos los NO separables."
"a. Los no separables: NUNCA se separan las dos palabras.
 Los objetos directos van AL FINAL."
   Con objeto directo:  I look after Amy.
   Con pronombre:       I look after her.   (NUNCA "look her after")

BOOK PHRASAL VERBS (p.221-223): ask for, go back, look at,
   listen to, look after, run into.

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.223-232):
   He will ask for a pencil.
   She went back last week.
   We look at the teacher.
   I listen to the teacher.
   I ran into my friends.
   Yes, I'm looking after her.

KEY: NON-separable -> object AND pronoun ALWAYS at the END.
     "I listen to her."  NOT "I listen her to."
```

> Di: *"These verbs never break. The object — and especially the pronoun — always goes at the END. 'I look after her', never 'I look her after'."*

### BOARD RACE — non-separable, object at the end (7 min) — DE PIE, 2 equipos

Dos columnas en el tablero. Dictas una frase con el pronombre mal colocado (en medio); el primer estudiante de cada fila corre al tablero y escribe la frase correcta con el pronombre AL FINAL. Cohorte verifica. Rota.

```
Round (dicta MAL; ellos escriben bien — pronombre al final):
1. "I look her after."        -> I look after HER.
2. "She listened him to."     -> She listened to HIM.
3. "We ran them into."        -> We ran into THEM.
4. "He asked it for."         -> He asked for IT.
5. "They looked it at."       -> They looked at IT.
6. "I will go it back to."    -> I will go back to IT.
```

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Compare with our phrase — 'look IT up' splits because 'look up' is separable. But 'look at it' does NOT split. Today's phrase, then the rule: pick it up, get it done."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — M23 (00:34–00:50, 16 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"You become the sentence. Wrong split on one side of the room, correct non-separable on the other. You walk the transformation."*

### Setup (2 min)

Dos zonas del salón rotuladas: **WRONG (split)** (izquierda) y **RIGHT (object/pronoun at the end)** (derecha). Reparte tiras con frases profesionales donde el verbo no separable está mal partido (forma incorrecta, 1 por estudiante).

### Round 1 — Walk it (8 min)

Cada estudiante:
1. Se para en zona WRONG y lee su frase partida en voz alta ("I look the client after...").
2. Camina a zona RIGHT diciendo la forma correcta en voz alta ("I look after the client...").
3. El cohorte (de pie) confirma con pulgar arriba/abajo.

**Tiras sugeridas (contexto profesional):**

```
"I look the new hire after on day one."  -> I look after the new hire / look after HIM.
"We listen the supervisor to every day." -> We listen to the supervisor / listen to HER.
"He asked a raise for last month."        -> He asked for a raise / asked for IT.
"She ran a old client into at the expo."  -> She ran into an old client / ran into HER.
"They looked the report at carefully."    -> They looked at the report / looked at IT.
"I go the manager back to with answers."  -> I go back to the manager / go back to HIM.
"Evaluations are handled by coordination, not by us." -> (limit reminder — leave correct as is)
```

> Nota: la última tira refuerza el límite — evaluaciones SOLO las maneja coordinación; el profesor NUNCA comunica nada de evaluaciones/midterm/final.

### Round 2 — Reverse (4 min)

Ahora al revés: parten de la forma CORRECTA, caminan a WRONG diciendo en voz alta por qué está mal ("'look him after' is wrong because 'look after' is non-separable — the pronoun stays at the end"). Intercambia tiras entre estudiantes.

### Cierre (2 min)

2 voluntarios caminan su transformación al frente. Cohorte cuenta verbos no separables correctos.

> Anota errores con NOMBRE en libreta personal.

## 8. LIBRO M24 — SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS + SORTING FÍSICO (00:50–01:02, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Module 24, page 233 — 'I'll Pick You Up at 8:00.'. These DO split — and with a pronoun they MUST. Then we sort separable vs non-separable with our hands."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie

**Cita textual del A2 Book M24 (pág 233-240) — esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M24 — SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS (A2 Book p.233-240)
       "I'll Pick You Up at 8:00."

From the book (Guia 1):
"1. La gran mayoria de los verbos frasales son del tipo
 'separable'."
"a. Los separables: Es OPCIONAL colocar el objeto directo
 o despues del verbo frasal, o ENTRE las dos palabras."
   Con objeto directo:  I pick up Amy.  /  I pick Amy up.
"b. Pero, cuando reemplazamos el objeto directo con un
 PRONOMBRE objeto, este pronombre DEBE ir ENTRE las
 palabras."
   Con pronombre:  I pick her up.   (NUNCA "I pick up her")

BOOK PHRASAL VERBS (p.233-234): turn on, turn off, put on,
   take off, pick up, look up.

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.235-240):
   Did you turn on the TV? -> Yes, I turned it on.
   Will you turn off your phone? -> Yes, I will turn it off.
   Did you put on your hat? -> Yes, I put it on.
   Does Amanda take off her shoes? -> Yes, she takes them off.
   Will Alvaro pick up the pencil? -> Yes, he will pick it up.
   Did you look up Shakira on Google? -> Yes, I looked her up.

KEY: SEPARABLE -> noun object: either position.
     PRONOUN object: ALWAYS in the middle.
     "I picked it up."  NOT "I picked up it."
```

> Di: *"Most phrasal verbs are separable. With a noun you choose: 'pick up the pencil' or 'pick the pencil up'. With a pronoun there is NO choice — it goes in the middle: 'pick it up', never 'pick up it'."*

### SORTING FÍSICO (7 min) — DE PIE, en grupo

Reparte tarjetas: unas con un verbo frasal NO separable ("look after", "listen to", "ask for", "run into", "look at"), otras con un verbo frasal SEPARABLE ("turn on", "turn off", "pick up", "look up", "take off", "put on"). El cohorte, de pie, agrupa cada tarjeta en la zona correcta (NO SEPARABLE / SEPARABLE) y dice en voz alta la frase con un PRONOMBRE para probar la regla:

```
"look after" -> NON-SEP -> "I look after HER."    (pronoun at END)
"pick up"    -> SEP     -> "I pick HER up."        (pronoun in MIDDLE)
"listen to"  -> NON-SEP -> "I listen to IT."       (pronoun at END)
"turn off"   -> SEP     -> "I turn IT off."        (pronoun in MIDDLE)
"ask for"    -> NON-SEP -> "I ask for IT."         (pronoun at END)
"look up"    -> SEP     -> "I look IT up."          (pronoun in MIDDLE)
```

Cada grupo dice su frase. El cohorte confirma si el verbo era separable o no, y por qué (¿dónde quedó el pronombre?).

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"'Look IT up', 'pick IT up', 'put IT off' — all separable, pronoun in the middle, all in our phrase. Now contrast: 'I look after her' — non-separable, pronoun at the end. One breath, both rules."*

## 10. SPEED DRILL DE PIE — NON-SEP + SEP + PRONOMBRE (01:16–01:28, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"12 minutes of both structures in your mouth until reflex — on your feet, fast."*

### Drill 1 — Pronoun placement cadena (5 min) — DE PIE en círculo

Dices un verbo frasal + un objeto; el estudiante responde la frase con el PRONOMBRE en su posición correcta, luego pasa al siguiente:

```
"pick up / the report"  -> "I pick it up."        (SEP — middle)
"look after / the team"  -> "I look after it."     (NON-SEP — end)
"turn off / the machine" -> "I turn it off."       (SEP — middle)
"listen to / the client" -> "I listen to her."     (NON-SEP — end)
"look up / the address"  -> "I look it up."         (SEP — middle)
"ask for / a raise"      -> "I ask for it."         (NON-SEP — end)
"turn on / the system"   -> "I turn it on."         (SEP — middle)
"run into / a coworker"  -> "I ran into him."       (NON-SEP — end)
```

### Drill 2 — Separable vs non-separable coro rápido (4 min) — DE PIE

Dices el verbo frasal; el cohorte responde en coro "SEPARABLE — middle" o "NON-SEPARABLE — end" y da la frase:

```
"pick up"    -> "SEPARABLE — I pick it up."
"look after" -> "NON-SEP — I look after her."
"turn off"   -> "SEPARABLE — I turn it off."
"listen to"  -> "NON-SEP — I listen to it."
"look up"    -> "SEPARABLE — I look it up."
"ask for"    -> "NON-SEP — I ask for it."
```

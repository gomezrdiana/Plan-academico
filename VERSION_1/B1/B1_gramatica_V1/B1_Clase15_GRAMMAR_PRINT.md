## 6. LIBRO M21 — GERUND AFTER PREPOSITION + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 21, page 197 — 'We Shouldn't Swim After Eating'. The gerund after a preposition. Then we race it on the board."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M21 (pág 197-201) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M21 — GERUNDS AFTER PREPOSITIONS (A2 Book p.197-201)
       "We Shouldn't Swim After Eating"

From the book (Guia 1):
"El gerundio tambien se usa para un verbo ubicado inmediatamente
 despues de una PREPOSICION."
"Ya sabes las preposiciones de tiempo 'before' (antes de) y
 'after' (despues de)."
   "I study after 6:00."          -> Yo estudio despues de las 6:00.
   "I study after eating dinner." -> Yo estudio despues de cenar.
"La preposicion 'without' (sin) funciona igual."
   "I study without eating."      -> Yo estudio sin comer.
"La preposicion 'about' (acerca de) se usa para describir un tema."
   "I talk about studying."       -> Yo hablo de estudiar.

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.199):
   I never eat before swimming.
   I never swim after eating.
   I never speak without thinking.
   I read about playing golf.
   You should ask before using the car.
   He always showers after eating breakfast.
   I'm watching a movie about fighting.

KEY: preposition (before/after/without/about) + VERB-ing.
     NOT "before to swim" — it is "before swimming".
```

> Di: *"The verb right after the preposition is always -ing — never the infinitive. 'Before swimming', not 'before to swim'."*

### BOARD RACE — preposition + -ing (7 min) — DE PIE, 2 equipos

Dos columnas en el tablero. Dictas una frase con verbo en infinitivo/base entre paréntesis; el primer estudiante de cada fila corre al tablero y escribe la frase correcta con preposición + -ing. Cohorte verifica. Rota.

```
Round (dicta; ellos escriben con preposition + -ing):
1. "I check email before (start) work."   -> before STARTING work.
2. "She rests after (finish) the report."  -> after FINISHING the report.
3. "He decides without (ask) anyone."      -> without ASKING anyone.
4. "We talk about (improve) the process."  -> about IMPROVING the process.
5. "Never sign after (read) only half."    -> after READING only half.
6. "Help before (be) thanked."             -> before BEING thanked.
```

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Look at #6 — 'before BEING thanked.' That's our phrase: after learning the rules, decide to use them."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — M21 (00:34–00:50, 16 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"You become the sentence. Infinitive on one side of the room, correct gerund-after-preposition on the other. You walk the transformation."*

### Setup (2 min)

Dos zonas del salón rotuladas: **WRONG (infinitive)** (izquierda) y **RIGHT (preposition + -ing)** (derecha). Reparte tiras con frases profesionales con el verbo en infinitivo después de la preposición (forma incorrecta, 1 por estudiante).

### Round 1 — Walk it (8 min)

Cada estudiante:
1. Se para en zona WRONG y lee su frase con infinitivo en voz alta ("Before to start the shift...").
2. Camina a zona RIGHT diciendo la forma correcta en voz alta ("Before STARTING the shift...").
3. El cohorte (de pie) confirma con pulgar arriba/abajo.

**Tiras sugeridas (contexto profesional):**

```
"Before to start the shift, we check the schedule."  -> Before STARTING the shift...
"After to finish the call, log the notes."           -> After FINISHING the call...
"Never reply without to read the full message."      -> ...without READING the full message.
"We met about to plan the budget."                   -> ...about PLANNING the budget.
"He left after to send the report."                  -> ...after SENDING the report.
"Decide without to ask is risky at work."            -> Deciding without ASKING is risky.
"The team improves by to share feedback."            -> ...by SHARING feedback.
"Evaluations are handled by coordination, not by us." -> (limit reminder — leave correct as is)
```

> Nota: la última tira refuerza el límite — evaluaciones SOLO las maneja coordinación; el profesor NUNCA comunica nada de evaluaciones/midterm/final.

### Round 2 — Reverse (4 min)

Ahora al revés: parten de la forma CORRECTA, caminan a WRONG diciendo en voz alta por qué está mal ("'before to start' is wrong because a preposition takes -ing"). Intercambia tiras entre estudiantes.

### Cierre (2 min)

2 voluntarios caminan su transformación al frente. Cohorte cuenta gerundios correctos después de preposición.

> Anota errores con NOMBRE en libreta personal.

## 8. LIBRO M22 — GERUNDS WITH DOUBLE VERBS + SORTING FÍSICO (00:50–01:02, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Module 22, page 207 — 'I Love Swimming!'. Two verbs together: the first one chooses infinitive or gerund. Then we sort it with our hands."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie

**Cita textual del A2 Book M22 (pág 207-210) — esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M22 — GERUNDS WITH DOUBLE VERBS (A2 Book p.207-210)
       "I Love Swimming!"

From the book (Guia 1):
"Cuando tenemos dos verbos, el segundo verbo puede ser o en el
 infinitivo o el gerundio."
   "Quiero jugar."   -> "I want to play."   (Infinitivo)
   "Me gusta jugar." -> "I like playing."   (Gerundio)
"El uso del infinitivo o el gerundio se basa en el PRIMER verbo."
SIMPLE RULE FROM THE BOOK:
   Infinitivo = el segundo verbo NO ha empezado todavia.
                "I want to play."  (no he empezado a jugar)
   Gerundio   = el segundo verbo SI ha empezado.
                "I like playing."  (ya he empezado a jugar)

INFINITIVE list (book p.209):     GERUND list (book p.209):
   decide to play                    start playing
   forget to play                    finish playing
   need to play                      stop playing
   want to play                      like playing
   have to play                      love playing

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.209):
   I want to wake up early.       She doesn't have to eat.
   I need to shower.              He forgets to say hello.
   We decide to travel tomorrow.  I start studying.
   We finish talking.             I love eating ice cream.
   I like eating broccoli.        The girls stop fighting.
```

> Di: *"The first verb decides. 'Want / decide / need / forget / have' take the infinitive — the action hasn't started. 'Start / finish / stop / like / love' take the gerund — the action has started."*

### SORTING FÍSICO (7 min) — DE PIE, en grupo

Reparte tarjetas: unas con un PRIMER verbo ("want", "decide", "need", "forget", "start", "finish", "stop", "like", "love"), otras con un SEGUNDO verbo base ("travel", "ask", "work", "study", "speak", "help"). El cohorte, de pie, se empareja físicamente y forma la frase correcta en voz alta, decidiendo infinitivo o gerundio según el primer verbo:

```
"decide" + "travel"  ->  "I DECIDE TO TRAVEL."   (infinitive — not started)
"stop"   + "ask"     ->  "I STOP ASKING."        (gerund — has started)
"want"   + "help"    ->  "I WANT TO HELP."       (infinitive)
"finish" + "study"   ->  "I FINISH STUDYING."    (gerund)
"forget" + "speak"   ->  "I FORGET TO SPEAK."    (infinitive)
"love"   + "work"    ->  "I LOVE WORKING."       (gerund)
```

Cada pareja dice su frase al grupo. El cohorte confirma si el primer verbo pedía infinitivo o gerundio y por qué (¿la acción ya empezó o no?).

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"'Decide TO use them' — infinitive, not started. 'Stop PRACTICING' — gerund, already started. Both in our phrase, in one breath."*

## 10. SPEED DRILL DE PIE — PREPOSITION+-ING + DOUBLE VERBS (01:16–01:28, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"12 minutes of both structures in your mouth until reflex — on your feet, fast."*

### Drill 1 — Preposition + -ing cadena (5 min) — DE PIE en círculo

Dices una preposición + un verbo base; el estudiante responde una frase correcta, luego pasa al siguiente:

```
"before / start"   -> "Before starting, I check the plan."
"after / finish"   -> "After finishing, I save the file."
"without / ask"    -> "I never decide without asking."
"about / improve"  -> "We talk about improving the process."
"before / send"    -> "Before sending, I read it twice."
"after / learn"    -> "After learning the rule, I use it."
"without / think"  -> "I never speak without thinking."
"about / lead"     -> "We talk about leading the team."
```

### Drill 2 — Double verbs coro rápido (4 min) — DE PIE

Dices el primer verbo + un segundo verbo base; el cohorte responde en coro con infinitivo o gerundio:

```
"want + travel"   -> "I WANT TO TRAVEL."     (not started)
"stop + complain" -> "I STOP COMPLAINING."   (has started)
"decide + ask"    -> "I DECIDE TO ASK."      (not started)
"finish + write"  -> "I FINISH WRITING."     (has started)
"need + help"     -> "I NEED TO HELP."       (not started)
"love + learn"    -> "I LOVE LEARNING."      (has started)
```

### Drill 3 — Parejas DE PIE (3 min)

Cada pareja construye 3 oraciones combinando preposición + -ing y doble verbo sobre su vida profesional. Las dicen al grupo.

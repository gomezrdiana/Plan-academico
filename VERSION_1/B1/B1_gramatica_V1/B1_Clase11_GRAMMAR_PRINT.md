## 5. LIBRO M12 — PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS (00:17–00:25, 8 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open A2 Book Module 12. Two past tenses, two different windows. Past Simple for the specific moment. Past Continuous for what was already happening. Today we lock the difference."*

### Concepto del módulo (lectura dirigida, 3 min)

**Lee EN INGLÉS desde el libro:**

> *"Past Simple = action completed at a specific past time. Form: V2 (verb-ed for regular, V2 for irregular). Example: 'I worked yesterday. She went to Cartagena last week.'*
>
> *Past Continuous = action in progress at a past time. Form: was/were + V-ing. Example: 'I was working when the phone rang. She was studying at 8 PM.'*
>
> *KEY USE TOGETHER: short interrupting action (Past Simple) inside a longer ongoing action (Past Continuous).*
>
> *Example: 'While I WAS WALKING home, I SAW an accident.' (was walking = ongoing / saw = interrupting moment)*"

### Speed Drill en parejas (5 min)

**Pizarra:**

```
SPEED DRILL — M12 (5 min)

Each pair writes 6 sentences using BOTH tenses together.
Format: "While X was [verb-ing], Y [past simple verb]."

Topics: hotel, work, traffic, weather, food.
Example: "While the chef was cooking, the manager called him."

After 4 min, you swap pairs and check each other's sentences.
```

## 6. LIBRO M13 — ALREADY / STILL / UNTIL (00:25–00:33, 8 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open A2 Book Module 13. Three small adverbs. Each one changes meaning drastically. Today we lock when to use each."*

### Concepto del módulo (lectura dirigida, 3 min)

**Lee EN INGLÉS desde el libro:**

> *"ALREADY = something happened EARLIER THAN EXPECTED. Position: between auxiliary and main verb. Example: 'I have ALREADY eaten.' (you expected I hadn't, but I did)*
>
> *STILL = something CONTINUES when you might expect it to have stopped. Position: between subject and verb. Example: 'She STILL works at the hotel.' (you thought maybe she left, but she's there)*
>
> *UNTIL = up to a specific point in time. Position: before the time reference. Example: 'Wait UNTIL Friday.' / 'She slept UNTIL noon.'*
>
> *KEY: these three adverbs can stack with Present Perfect (which you saw in Conv this morning): 'She has STILL not arrived.' 'I have ALREADY finished.' 'I haven't seen him UNTIL today.'*"

### Speed Drill en parejas (5 min)

**Pizarra:**

```
SPEED DRILL — M13 (5 min)

Each pair writes 6 sentences — 2 with ALREADY, 2 with STILL,
2 with UNTIL.

Topics: hotel service, work delays, travel, study, food.

Examples:
- "The chef has ALREADY prepared your meal."
- "The technician is STILL fixing the AC."
- "Your suite won't be ready UNTIL 3 PM."

After 4 min, swap pairs and check each other's sentences.
```

# ANEXO A — ESCENARIOS DETALLADOS PARA LOS 2 HUÉSPEDES

> **Esta sección es PRIVADA — solo para el profesor (Juan Diego, que también es Guest 2) y el invitado especial. NO se entrega a los estudiantes.**

## 🎬 ESCENARIO 1 — Guest 1 (Invitado especial B2/C1)
### "The Double-Booked Suite + The Allergic Reaction"

**Setup:** VIP llega un viernes en la noche para una conferencia de negocios de 3 días. Su suite ejecutiva fue accidentalmente asignada a otro huésped. Mientras Reservations arregla esto, la asistente del VIP llama sobre una **alergia severa a mariscos** que estaba en la reserva original pero nunca se comunicó a cocina — y el plato de bienvenida contiene camarón.

**Flujo de escalación:**
1. **Min 0-3:** Guest llega a Front Desk. *"I have a reservation under [name] for the Presidential Suite."* Front Desk descubre el conflicto: la suite fue asignada a otra persona.
2. **Min 3-8:** Front Desk llama a Reservations Manager. Reservations debe disculparse + ofrecer alternativa. Guest acepta de mala gana una suite menor con compensación.
3. **Min 8-10:** Concierge sube a la suite con plato de bienvenida (camarón). Asistente del Guest (Guest mismo en voz cambiada) llama desde el celular: *"My boss has a SEVERE shellfish allergy. Why is there shrimp on the plate? This was in the original booking."*
4. **Min 10-18:** Crisis. Chef llamado, F&B Manager involucrado. Deben verificar cómo se perdió la nota de alergia. Compensación adicional. Guest no acepta menos que upgrade gratis a Presidential + dos noches sin costo.
5. **Min 18-22:** Resolución. Duty Manager interviene si necesario. Disculpas formales en inglés B2.
6. **Min 22-25:** Guest sale de personaje y da feedback B2/C1 explícito.

**Grammar triggers obligatorios (forzar producción):**
- *"While I WAS CHECKING your reservation, I NOTICED that another guest HAD ALREADY been assigned to your suite."* (M12 + already)
- *"The chef HAD ALREADY prepared the welcome plate before the allergy note ARRIVED."* (M12 + already)
- *"Your new suite is STILL being prepared — it won't be ready UNTIL 9 PM."* (M13 still + until)
- *"We have ALREADY upgraded you to the Presidential Suite at no charge."* (already)

## 🎬 ESCENARIO 2 — Guest 2 (TÚ MISMO — Juan Diego en rol dual)
### "The Service Delay + The AC Emergency"

> **Por qué este escenario te toca a ti:** se desarrolla principalmente en UNA suite con un guest fijo. Eso te permite mantener el control de la actividad (escuchar ambos grupos) mientras estás en personaje. El invitado especial maneja el Escenario 1 que es más fluido entre departamentos — eso da rienda suelta a su B2/C1.

**Setup:** VIP solicitó un desayuno de negocios a las 6 AM en su suite para una reunión importante con cliente. Llega 40 minutos tarde. Mientras come apurado, el AC falla — y el VIP debe salir 90 minutos a una llamada pero el clima debe arreglarse antes de regresar porque tiene una **condición médica respiratoria**.

**Flujo de escalación:**
1. **Min 0-3:** Guest está en su suite. Room Service Server llega con desayuno 40 min tarde. *"I needed this 40 minutes ago. My client meeting starts in 20 minutes."*
2. **Min 3-7:** F&B Manager es llamado. Debe disculparse formalmente, ofrecer compensación. Guest acepta pero exige que esto se reporte al Duty Manager.
3. **Min 7-10:** Mientras Guest come, el AC falla. Llama a Front Desk furioso: *"It's getting hot in here. I have a respiratory condition. Send someone NOW."*
4. **Min 10-18:** Facilities Manager despacha técnico. Técnico en otra suite — debe terminar antes. Guest informa que sale 90 min a llamada pero AC debe estar reparado al regresar. Housekeeping limpia mientras tanto.
5. **Min 18-22:** Resolución. Duty Manager ofrece suite alternativa equivalente. Guest debe decidir: esperar o mudarse. Compensación final negociada.
6. **Min 22-25:** Guest sale de personaje y da feedback B2/C1 explícito.

**Grammar triggers obligatorios:**
- *"While we WERE PREPARING the breakfast, the lift BROKE DOWN — that's why it ARRIVED late."* (M12)
- *"The technician is STILL in another suite. He will be with you UNTIL he finishes there."* (M13 still + until)
- *"We have ALREADY notified the duty manager."* (already)
- *"Your AC won't be cool again UNTIL approximately 9:30 — STILL within your meeting timeline."* (until + still)

# ANEXO B — ROLE CARDS (8 roles × 2 grupos = 16 estudiantes)

> **Imprimir 1 copia de cada role card. Entregar 1 por estudiante al inicio de la actividad. Los roles se REPITEN entre los 2 grupos (un Front Desk en Group A y otro en Group B), pero cada estudiante recibe SOLO su role card.**

### 🛎️ ROLE 1 — FRONT DESK AGENT

**Your role:** First point of contact with the VIP guest at check-in.

**Your objective:** Welcome professionally, handle the initial complaint, and escalate appropriately. You CANNOT solve major problems alone — your job is to triage and call the right manager.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- check-in / check-out / key card / reservation confirmation / suite / executive floor / late check-out / complimentary upgrade / double-booked / discretion

**Required grammar (you MUST produce both at least once):**
- ✅ M12: *"While I WAS [verb-ing], I NOTICED..."* OR *"When you ARRIVED, we WERE [verb-ing]..."*
- ✅ M13: One sentence using ALREADY, STILL, or UNTIL

**Tone:** Apologetic but professional. Never defensive. *"I'm terribly sorry... allow me to..."*

---

### 📋 ROLE 2 — RESERVATIONS MANAGER

**Your role:** Handle reservation conflicts. Authority to upgrade, comp nights, or escalate to Duty Manager.

**Your objective:** Solve booking problems. Offer compensation. Make the guest feel heard.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- to escalate the matter / to accommodate / to honor the rate / rooming list / comp / on the house / discretionary upgrade / goodwill gesture / VIP protocol / compensation

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While I WAS REVIEWING your file, I REALIZED..."*
- ✅ M13: At least 2 sentences using ALREADY, STILL, or UNTIL

**Tone:** Authoritative + warm. *"I take full responsibility... as a goodwill gesture..."*

---

### 👨‍🍳 ROLE 3 — CHEF / F&B MANAGER

**Your role:** Handle dietary issues, allergies, special requests, and food complaints.

**Your objective:** Apologize for the food error, explain (briefly) what went wrong, offer alternative immediately.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- dietary restrictions / allergens / off-menu request / signature dish / plating / expedite the order / course timing / dietary accommodation / front-of-house / allergy protocol

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While the team WAS PREPARING the meal, the note about the allergy ARRIVED late."*
- ✅ M13: *"The new plate is STILL being prepared..."* OR *"We have ALREADY removed all shellfish from the kitchen line..."*

**Tone:** Direct accountability. *"I take full responsibility. Allow me to make this right..."*

---

### 🔧 ROLE 4 — FACILITIES MANAGER

**Your role:** Handle technical failures (AC, plumbing, electrical, maintenance).

**Your objective:** Dispatch technician, give realistic ETA, offer alternative (move suites) if needed.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- out of order / maintenance request / work order / on-call technician / HVAC / deep clean / prompt resolution / service interruption / emergency protocol / dispatch

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While the technician WAS WORKING in another suite, your AC FAILED."*
- ✅ M13: *"The technician is STILL in suite 412 — he won't be available UNTIL 9:30."*

**Tone:** Operational + clear timelines. *"Rest assured, we'll have this sorted by..."*

---

### 🛏️ ROLE 5 — CONCIERGE

**Your role:** Handle special requests, coordinate with all departments, arrange anything the guest needs.

**Your objective:** Anticipate guest needs, provide personalized service, liaise between departments.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- liaise with / arrange transportation / secure tickets / personalized itinerary / restock the minibar / DND / turn-down service / lost and found / on-site

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While you WERE in your meeting, we WERE arranging the alternative suite."*
- ✅ M13: *"Everything has ALREADY been arranged for your departure..."*

**Tone:** Helpful + proactive. *"May I suggest...?"*

---

### 👔 ROLE 6 — DUTY MANAGER

**Your role:** Highest on-shift authority. Final escalation point. Authority to authorize major comps.

**Your objective:** Resolve unresolved escalations. Authorize upgrades or free nights. Restore trust.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- I take full responsibility / goodwill gesture / comp the stay / discretionary upgrade / on the house / our utmost / personally attend to / immediate resolution / I will personally oversee

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While I WAS reviewing the situation, I REALIZED we had not done enough."*
- ✅ M13: *"As of right now, you have ALREADY been upgraded. The room is STILL being prepared. It will be ready UNTIL 9 PM at the latest."*

**Tone:** Calm authority. *"Allow me to personally see to this..."*

---

### 🧹 ROLE 7 — HOUSEKEEPING SUPERVISOR

**Your role:** Coordinate cleaning, prepare alternative suites quickly, manage staff.

**Your objective:** Get the alternative suite ready ASAP. Communicate timelines clearly.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- turn-down service / linen change / deep clean / restock / DND / express clean / standby suite / lost and found / coordinate with

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While the team WAS preparing your alternative suite, we FOUND..."*
- ✅ M13: *"The suite is STILL being prepared. It will be ready UNTIL 9 PM. We have ALREADY restocked the minibar."*

**Tone:** Operational + reassuring. *"Bear with us, please..."*

---

### 🥂 ROLE 8 — ROOM SERVICE SERVER / BUTLER

**Your role:** Deliver food, handle in-room service issues, communicate kitchen delays.

**Your objective:** Apologize for delays, offer alternatives, escalate kitchen problems to F&B Manager.

**Required vocabulary (use at least 5):**
- expedite / course / off-menu / plate / signature dish / room service / butler / cart / silver service / discretion

**Required grammar:**
- ✅ M12: *"While the chef WAS plating, I NOTICED the time and CALLED the manager."*
- ✅ M13: *"Your second course is STILL being prepared. It will arrive UNTIL approximately 6:45."*

**Tone:** Service-oriented + apologetic. *"I'm so sorry — allow me to..."*


Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Four shapes on the board. BECAUSE = clause behind it (subject+verb). BECAUSE OF = noun behind it (no verb). HOWEVER = full clause after a comma, contrast. DESPITE = noun (or gerund), contrast. Two families: CLAUSE-rule and NOUN-rule — they never swap. Fortitude day 4: under pressure (memo deadline, client question, audit) the cheap shortcut is to mix them — 'despite I tried' (wrong) instead of 'although I tried' or 'despite my effort'. Hold the shape."*

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing board race
Two columns on the board. You call a connector; the first student of each row runs to the board and writes "CLAUSE" or "NOUN" next to it. Cohort verifies. 10 rounds in 3 minutes.

```
"because"      → CLAUSE
"because of"   → NOUN
"due to"       → NOUN
"since"        → CLAUSE
"despite"      → NOUN
"although"     → CLAUSE
"however"      → CLAUSE (after comma/period)
"in spite of"  → NOUN
"thanks to"    → NOUN
"even though"  → CLAUSE
```

Quick note as you erase: *"That column on the left is what you commit to memory today. Mix them and the sentence collapses — 'because of I was tired' is the most common error in B1 written email."*

### (2 min) Bridge to today's content
> *"Today's content is M6 FORM: the two families (CLAUSE vs NOUN), the comma rule (HOWEVER and ON THE OTHER HAND need a comma after them; ALTHOUGH and EVEN THOUGH need a comma between the two clauses), and the trap — DESPITE + noun or gerund, never DESPITE + clause. Then we drill the form physically — 4 zones around the room — and we land it in a 45-minute project memo simulation. Stay standing."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — form rules from the book

Stand at the board. Book p.53 + p.57 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M6 — CONNECTORS — FORM RULES (B1 Book p.53 + p.57)

TWO FAMILIES:

(A) CLAUSE-RULE → connector + [subject + verb...]
    CAUSE:     because / since / as
    RESULT:    so / that's why / therefore /
               consequently
    CONTRAST:  but / however / although /
               even though / whereas /
               on the other hand / nevertheless

(B) NOUN-RULE → connector + [noun or gerund]
    CAUSE:     because of / due to / thanks to
    CONTRAST:  despite / in spite of

PUNCTUATION:
  • HOWEVER / ON THE OTHER HAND / THEREFORE /
    NEVERTHELESS → preceded by period or
    semicolon, followed by COMMA.
    "We shipped late. HOWEVER, the client approved."
  • ALTHOUGH / EVEN THOUGH → comma between
    the two clauses.
    "ALTHOUGH the spec was late, we shipped on time."
  • DESPITE + gerund (very B1):
    "DESPITE arriving late, we passed audit."
    NOT: "Despite we arrived late..."
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.53 verbatim quotes drilled out loud: *"I love her BECAUSE she is beautiful / I love her BECAUSE OF her beauty / Equivalent but more formal: due to / Equivalent (and sometimes sarcastic): thanks to."* Book p.57 verbatim quotes: *"EVEN THOUGH I want to play, I have to work / Equivalent but more formal: although, while, whereas, notwithstanding / IN SPITE OF your efforts, we lost / Equivalent but more formal: despite."* Book p.58 verbatim: *"Despite becoming a doctor, I want more money. Note the gerund use."*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"BECAUSE + clause / BECAUSE OF + noun. Fortitude day 4 — never mixed."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners:
- **CAUSE — CLAUSE**
- **CAUSE — NOUN**
- **CONTRAST — CLAUSE**
- **CONTRAST — NOUN**

Stand in the middle. Call a half-sentence + connector out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + complete the sentence out loud as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 16 calls in 15 min, ~1 per minute.

```
Call                                  → Right zone + sample completion
1.  "...BECAUSE..."                   → CAUSE-CLAUSE  — "...because the spec landed late."
2.  "...BECAUSE OF..."                → CAUSE-NOUN    — "...because of the late spec."
3.  "...DUE TO..."                    → CAUSE-NOUN    — "...due to the late spec."
4.  "...SINCE..."                     → CAUSE-CLAUSE  — "...since QA was full."
5.  "...DESPITE..."                   → CONTR-NOUN    — "...despite the timeline pressure."
6.  "...ALTHOUGH..."                  → CONTR-CLAUSE  — "...although QA was full, we shipped."
7.  "...HOWEVER, ..."                 → CONTR-CLAUSE  — "...however, the client signed."
8.  "...IN SPITE OF..."               → CONTR-NOUN    — "...in spite of the budget delay."
9.  "...THANKS TO..."                 → CAUSE-NOUN    — "...thanks to the team."
10. "...EVEN THOUGH..."               → CONTR-CLAUSE  — "...even though the build was red."
11. "...THEREFORE, ..."               → CAUSE-CLAUSE  — "...therefore, we delayed launch."
12. "...DESPITE [gerund]..."          → CONTR-NOUN    — "...despite working overtime."
13. "...AS..."                        → CAUSE-CLAUSE  — "...as the migration was blocked."
14. "...ON THE OTHER HAND, ..."       → CONTR-CLAUSE  — "...on the other hand, QA accepted."
15. "...CONSEQUENTLY, ..."            → CAUSE-CLAUSE  — "...consequently, we re-scoped."
16. "...BECAUSE OF [noun]..."         → CAUSE-NOUN    — "...because of the spec compression."
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — listen to what comes after." When the zone is right and the completion is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (10 min) Pair drilling with cards

Hand each pair: 5 connector cards (mix of all 4 families) + 1 punctuation marker. Pair work:

1. Student A picks a connector card; reads it out loud.
2. Student B builds a full sentence using it correctly, applying the right comma/period rule.
3. Swap roles after every card. After 5 cards each, swap to a different family mix.

Walk and coach the FORM (clause vs noun) AND the PUNCTUATION (comma after HOWEVER, comma between ALTHOUGH-clauses, no comma needed after BECAUSE in mid-sentence). Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Clause-rule or noun-rule, never mixed. Fortitude day 4 — hold the shape."*

### (7 min) Podium — reversal trap (clause ↔ noun)

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a sentence in ONE form; they must REVERSE it to the OTHER form keeping the same meaning. Cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"* If they hesitate or get it wrong, the cohort gives the chorus correction.

```
1st: "BECAUSE the spec was late, we missed audit."
     → reverse to NOUN-rule:
     → "BECAUSE OF the late spec, we missed audit."

2nd: "ALTHOUGH the team worked overtime, the budget slipped."
     → reverse to NOUN-rule:
     → "DESPITE the team's overtime, the budget slipped."

3rd: "DUE TO the migration plan, we paused releases."
     → reverse to CLAUSE-rule:
     → "BECAUSE the migration plan needed review, we paused releases."
```

Then one final chorus drill — you call the reversal pattern, cohort responds in unison:

> *"Because of + late spec — reverse?"* → "Because the spec was late."
> *"Although + tried — to noun?"* → "Despite the effort."
> *"Due to + delay — to clause?"* → "Because there was a delay."

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "PROJECT MEMO WRITING DRILL — ORAL"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"You're running a PROJECT MEMO WRITING DRILL — but oral, not written. Three roles: MEMO-AUTHOR speaks the project memo out loud — outcome of last sprint, framed in 6 connectors. CHALLENGER repeats the sentence inserting the OPPOSITE-family connector (clause→noun or noun→clause). OBSERVER marks each connector on the grid I'm handing out — right family, right comma, right meaning. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. I will WRITE recurring errors on the board live as I hear them — when there are three on the board we stop and correct as a cohort."*

Hand out printed observer grid (prepared before class):

```
OBSERVER GRID — CL 39 GRAMMAR — PROJECT MEMO WRITING DRILL

Memo-author name (this round): __________

Mark each connector below as:
  ✓     = right family + right comma + right meaning
  ✗ fam = mixed family (e.g. "despite I was..." or
          "because of we shipped...")
  ✗ com = missing comma after HOWEVER / between
          ALTHOUGH-clauses
  ✗ mn  = meaning wrong (cause used for contrast)
  ✗ rev = challenger failed to reverse correctly

Round 1 — 6 connectors:
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____
  4: ____  5: ____  6: ____

Round 2 — 6 connectors (must include 2 DESPITE/
IN SPITE OF — the hardest):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____
  4: ____  5: ____  6: ____

Round 3 — 6 connectors (must include 2
HOWEVER comma + 2 ALTHOUGH-clause):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____
  4: ____  5: ____  6: ____

One sentence to the memo-author at the end:
________________________________________________
```

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 6 sentences. Memo-author speaks; challenger reverses in 3 sec; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for the NOUN-rule connectors (despite / in spite of / because of / due to / thanks to). Each triad must produce at least 2 DESPITE constructions in their 6 sentences. Continue live board-error correction. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3: *"Clause-rule or noun-rule, never mixed. Especially under pressure."*

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Push for HOWEVER comma + ALTHOUGH paired clauses — at least 2 each in every triad. By now they should be sharp. Slow down only for the most stubborn pattern you wrote on the board across the previous 2 rounds.

### (2 min) Cohort close
One strong triad demos 90 sec in front of the group — 3 connectors including 1 DESPITE. Applause. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4: *"Clause-rule or noun-rule, never mixed. Fortitude day 4 — hold the shape under pressure."*

Write on the board (anonymous; NAMES only in your personal notebook):

```
ERROR PAPER — CL 39 GRAMMAR

1. ❌ "Because of we shipped late, the client called."   → ✅ "BECAUSE we shipped late, the client called."   (because of + NOUN, not clause)
2. ❌ "Despite I tried hard, the bug remained."          → ✅ "ALTHOUGH I tried hard, the bug remained."     OR "DESPITE my effort, the bug remained." (despite + noun/gerund only)

```

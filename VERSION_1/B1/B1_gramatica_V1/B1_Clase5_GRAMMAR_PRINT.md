## 3. STAND-UP DRILL — SIMPLE PRESENT (00:19–00:34, 15 min)

**Concepto del módulo M8 — SIMPLE PRESENT (afirmativo):**

Escribe en el tablero (en INGLÉS):
```
SIMPLE PRESENT (affirmative)
- Routines:  I wake up at 6.   We work from Monday to Friday.
- Habits:    She drinks coffee every morning.
- Facts:     The sun rises in the east.   Water boils at 100°C.

Form: SUBJECT + VERB
Time markers: every day, usually, always, sometimes, never
```

> NO expliques. SOLO modela. Si preguntan POR QUÉ: "Listen. Repeat. We'll see why later."

**Setup (1 min):** "STAND UP. We do 20 prompts. I say one in Spanish, you say it in English. SIMPLE PRESENT only. Fast. Whoever sits down loses energy."

**Round 1 — 1ª/2ª persona (5 min):** Dispara prompts uno por uno, ~15 seg cada uno. Todos de pie. Si alguien se equivoca: "No. Listen." + correcto + repite 2x.

```
1. "Yo trabajo todos los días."          → I work every day.
2. "Tú estudias inglés."                 → You study English.
3. "Nosotros vivimos en Bucaramanga."    → We live in Bucaramanga.
4. "Ustedes hablan español."             → You (all) speak Spanish.
5. "Yo me despierto a las 6."            → I wake up at 6.
6. "Nosotros leemos libros."             → We read books.
7. "Tú escuchas música."                 → You listen to music.
8. "Yo bebo café cada mañana."           → I drink coffee every morning.
9. "Nosotros caminamos al trabajo."      → We walk to work.
10. "Tú duermes 8 horas."                → You sleep 8 hours.
```

**Round 2 — anuncio del cambio (1 min):** Para. "Now the rule changes. HE / SHE / IT add an -s at the end. Why? Because. Listen. Repeat."

Modela:
- "I work" → "She workS"
- "I drink" → "He drinkS"
- "I sleep" → "It sleepS"

**Round 3 — 3ª persona -s (8 min):** Dispara 10 prompts más. Todos de pie. Si se olvidan -s: "No. Listen. He WORKS." Repiten 2x.

```
1. "Maria estudia inglés."               → Maria studies English.   (estudia → studies, ojo "y → ies")
2. "Mi papá trabaja mucho."              → My dad works a lot.
3. "El bebé duerme bien."                → The baby sleeps well.
4. "Mi hermana cocina los domingos."     → My sister cooks on Sundays.
5. "Pedro lee el periódico."             → Pedro reads the newspaper.
6. "Diana enseña inglés."                → Diana teaches English.   (teach → teaches, ojo "ch → ches")
7. "Mi gata duerme todo el día."         → My cat sleeps all day.
8. "El profesor camina por el salón."    → The teacher walks around the room.
9. "Mi mamá maneja al trabajo."          → My mom drives to work.
10. "El sol sale por el este."           → The sun rises in the east.
```

> **3 reglas del -s que escribes en el tablero al final:**
> 1. La mayoría: + s   (works, sleeps, drinks)
> 2. -y → -ies         (studies, tries, flies)
> 3. -ch / -sh / -s / -x → + es   (teaches, washes, fixes)

## 4. GALLERY WALK — 4 ESTACIONES 3rd PERSON -s (00:34–00:49, 15 min)

**Setup ANTES de clase:** Pega 4 hojas en las paredes del salón. Cada hoja tiene un prompt diferente. Los estudiantes rotan en grupos de 4.

**Las 4 estaciones (escribe en hojas tamaño carta y pégalas en las paredes):**

```
ESTACIÓN 1 — "MY MOM/DAD"
Write 3 sentences about what your mom OR dad does every day.
Use 3rd person -s. Example: "My mom cooks at 7 PM."

ESTACIÓN 2 — "MY BEST FRIEND"
Write 3 sentences about what your best friend does on weekends.
Use 3rd person -s. Example: "She watches Netflix on Saturdays."

ESTACIÓN 3 — "MY PET / MY NEIGHBOR'S PET"
Write 3 sentences about what a pet does every day.
Use 3rd person -s. Example: "My cat sleeps 18 hours a day."

ESTACIÓN 4 — "MY BOSS / TEACHER"
Write 3 sentences about what your boss or a teacher does at work.
Use 3rd person -s. Example: "He answers emails all morning."
```

**Setup (2 min):** "We have 4 stations on the walls. Groups of 4. Each group has 3 minutes per station. Read the prompt, write your 3 sentences DIRECTLY ON the paper (use the marker). Then ROTATE to next station. Anchor leads each group."

**4 rondas de 3 min (12 min):** Cronómetro a la vista. "ROTATE!" cada 3 min.

**Cierre (1 min):** Sit down. "Which station was the easiest? Which had the funniest answers?"

> Tu trabajo durante la rotación: caminar, escuchar errores, anotar en papel anónimo. Si ves error sistemático ("My mom cook" sin -s): para el grupo, "Listen. My mom cookS." Repiten 2x. Sigue.

## 6. WRITING SPRINT — MY DAILY ROUTINE (01:04–01:19, 15 min)

**Setup (2 min):** "Notebooks open. NOT GoldList — your personal notebook. We do 3 sprints of writing. Each sprint = 60 sec of writing nonstop. Pencil moves the WHOLE time. If you don't know a word: write it in Spanish, keep going."

**Sprint 1 — Mi rutina diaria normal (60 sec + 60 sec checking):** Di: "Write 5 sentences about your typical Tuesday. SIMPLE PRESENT. Use -s for he/she/it. GO." Cronómetro 60 sec.

Después: "Stop. Read your sentences. Check the -s. Add it where missing. 60 sec."

**Sprint 2 — La rutina de alguien cercano (60 sec + 60 sec checking):** Di: "Write 5 sentences about your mom OR dad OR partner OR best friend. What do they do every day? 3rd person -s. GO." Cronómetro.

Después: "Stop. Check the -s. 60 sec."

**Sprint 3 — Hechos universales (60 sec + 60 sec checking):** Di: "Write 5 sentences about FACTS — things that are always true. Examples: 'The sun rises in the east.' GO."

Después: "Stop. Check. 60 sec."

**Cierre (3 min):** "Pairs. Read your favorite sentence from any sprint. Partner says: 'Nice' or 'Check the -s.'"

## 7. WALKING CONJUGATION BEE — 3rd PERSON -s (01:19–01:31, 12 min)

**Setup (2 min):** "STAND UP. Two lines facing each other. Like spelling bee but for verb conjugation."

**Reglas (escribe en tablero):**
```
WALKING CONJUGATION BEE
- I say a verb in INFINITIVE + a 3rd person subject
- Student says the conjugated form OUT LOUD
- WRONG → step OUT of the line
- LAST one standing wins
- Examples: "play / he" → "he plays" | "study / she" → "she studies"
```

**Round (10 min):** Dispara 25-30 verbos rápido. Si fallan, salen. Si la fila se acaba, los que salieron vuelven y empezamos otra ronda.

```
play / he → plays           study / she → studies      teach / he → teaches
work / he → works            try / she → tries           wash / he → washes
sleep / it → sleeps          fly / it → flies            fix / she → fixes
drink / she → drinks         cry / he → cries            watch / she → watches
walk / it → walks            buy / she → buys            kiss / he → kisses
read / he → reads            carry / she → carries       brush / he → brushes
write / she → writes         say / he → says             miss / she → misses
listen / he → listens        marry / she → marries
```

**Cierre (2 min):** "Winner stays standing. Aplauso." Aplauden. "Sit down."

> Energía alta. Ruido. Risa cuando alguien se equivoca y "se sale" de la fila. NO es castigo, es juego.

## 8. ERROR PAPER LIVE — PEER RELAY (01:31–01:43, 12 min)

**Tu papel de errores debe tener 8-10 errores escritos (de los bloques anteriores).**

**Escribe los 5-6 errores más graves en el tablero (SIN NOMBRES):**
```
ERRORS HEARD TODAY:
1. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
2. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
3. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
4. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
5. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
```

**STANDING. Peer Relay format:**

**Setup (2 min):** "STAND UP. Two teams. The marker passes from teammate to teammate. When you have the marker: read ONE error aloud as if it were correct. The OTHER team identifies what's wrong + says correct version. If they get it right: 1 point for them. Pass marker to teammate."

**Round (8 min):** Equipo A empieza. Marcador rota equipo a equipo. Cada error se discute UNA vez. Si una corrección es la equivocada: profesor da la versión correcta, todos repiten 2x.

**Cierre (2 min):** "Sit down. Score? Aplauso for both teams."

> **Por qué Peer Relay funciona:** Pasar el marcador es físico, social, todos están atentos porque puede tocar a cualquiera. La presión es saludable.

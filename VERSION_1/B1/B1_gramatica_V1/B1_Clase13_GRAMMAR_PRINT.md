## 7. LIBRO M17 — PRESENT PERFECT vs PAST SIMPLE (00:37–00:50, 13 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Module 17. The most important grammar comparison in B1. Same sentence, two tenses, two meanings. Today: when to use which."*

### Concepto (5 min)

**Tablero:**

```
M17 — WHEN TO USE WHICH

PAST SIMPLE = specific time in the past (closed)
  Markers: yesterday, last week, in 2018, ago, at 3 PM
  Example: "I VISITED Cartagena in 2019."

PRESENT PERFECT = no specific time (open, relevant now)
  Markers: ever, never, just, yet, already, since, for, lately
  Example: "I HAVE VISITED Cartagena 3 times."

SAME EVENT, TWO TENSES:
- "She STUDIED at Harvard in 2018." (specific year = Past Simple)
- "She HAS STUDIED at Harvard." (her life experience, no time = Present Perfect)

KEY RULE: if you mention a SPECIFIC TIME → Past Simple.
          if you do NOT → Present Perfect.

FREQUENT ERROR:
❌ "I have visited Cartagena in 2019."  (mixing — wrong)
✅ "I visited Cartagena in 2019."
✅ "I have visited Cartagena 3 times."
```

### Ejercicios M17 + drill (8 min)

Cohorte hace ejercicios M17 del libro. Énfasis en distinción.

**Mini drill al final (3 min):** Tú das una oración mixta, cohorte decide: *"Past Simple? Or Present Perfect? Why?"*

```
1. "She ___ (live) here for 5 years." → has lived (Present Perfect — duration)
2. "She ___ (live) here in 2018." → lived (Past Simple — specific time)
3. "I ___ (see) that movie 3 times." → have seen (no time)
4. "I ___ (see) that movie last night." → saw (specific time)
```

## What Time Has Taught Me About English

When I **STARTED**[PS] learning English in 2018, I **HAD**[PS] no idea where this journey **WOULD TAKE**[FS] me. I **WAS WORKING**[PC] full-time, **STUDYING**[PC] at night, and I rarely **HAD**[PS] time to practice. While I **WAS STRUGGLING**[PC] with verb tenses, I often **FELT**[PS] I **WOULD NEVER MASTER**[FS] them. I **HAD ALREADY GIVEN UP**[PP-B2] twice before — once in 2015, and again in 2017.

But during those years of failure, something quiet **WAS HAPPENING**[PC]. Every time I **TRIED**[PS] again, I **STARTED**[PS] a little further along than I **HAD BEEN**[PP-B2] the time before. By 2020, I **WAS READING**[PC] short articles. By 2022, I **WAS HOLDING**[PC] basic conversations. By 2024, I **HAD GIVEN**[PP-B2] my first presentation in English to a foreign client. I **HAVE NOT STOPPED**[PrPI] since.

Three things **HAVE SHAPED**[PrPR] my journey. First, I **HAVE STOPPED**[PrPR] expecting fluency to come fast. Real fluency **TAKES**[PrSI] years — not months. Second, I **HAVE LEARNED**[PrPR] that mistakes **ARE NOT**[PrSI] enemies. They **ARE**[PrSI] data. Every error I **HAVE MADE**[PrPI] **HAS TAUGHT**[PrPI] me something I **HAD NOT KNOWN**[PP-B2] before. Third, I still **STRUGGLE**[PrSI] with some structures — but I no longer **PANIC**[PrSI]. Until I **AM**[PrSI] perfect, I **WILL KEEP**[FS] practicing. And perfect **IS NOT**[PrSI] the goal anyway.

If you **ARE READING**[PrCo] this and **FEELING**[PrCo] discouraged, I **WANT**[PrSI] to tell you something I wish I **HAD HEARD**[PP-B2] when I **STARTED**[PS]. You **ARE NOT**[PrSI] behind. You **ARE**[PrSI] exactly where you **ARE SUPPOSED**[PrSI] to be. The student who **HAS PRACTICED**[PrPR] English for one hour today **IS**[PrSI] ahead of the student who **HAS ONLY THOUGHT**[PrPI] about it. While others **HAVE BEEN WAITING**[PrPCo-B2] for the right moment, you **ARE USING**[PrCo] THIS moment. That **IS**[PrSI] the difference.

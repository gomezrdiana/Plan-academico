## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — form rules from the book

Stand at the board. Book p.63 + p.67 + p.71 + p.75 + p.79 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M7 — RELATIVE PRONOUNS + EMBEDDED — FORM RULES
                          (B1 Book p.63-82)

(1) RELATIVE PRONOUNS by category:
    PEOPLE:           WHO (subject), WHOM (object,
                      formal), WHOSE (possessive),
                      THAT (defining only)
    THINGS/ANIMALS:   WHICH, THAT
    PLACE:            WHERE
    TIME:             WHEN
    REASON:           WHY
    MANNER:           HOW (or THAT)

(2) DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING (Guide 2 & 3):
    DEFINING (necessary to identify) → NO commas.
      "The car that I bought is blue."
    NON-DEFINING (extra info) → COMMAS, use WHICH
    (never THAT for things in non-defining).
      "My house, which is big, is white."

(3) OMITTING THE PRONOUN (Guide 4, p.75):
    Only when the pronoun is OBJECT of the
    relative clause. Never when it is SUBJECT.
      Object:  "The man WHO I met."  →  "The man
               I met."   ✓ omit
      Subject: "The man WHO lives next door."
               →  NEVER omit.

(4) EMBEDDED QUESTIONS (Guide 5, p.79):
    The inner clause uses AFFIRMATIVE ORDER
    (subject + verb), NOT the question order.
      Direct:   "Where does she live?"
      Embedded: "Can you tell me WHERE SHE LIVES?"
    If no question word → use IF (or WHETHER for
    formal):  "I wonder IF he came."
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.63 verbatim quote (drilled out loud): *"The words after the relative pronoun follow the normal organization for an affirmative sentence."* Book p.71 verbatim: *"Relative clauses with additional information are usually separated by commas. They almost always use the relative pronoun 'which' for 'que'."* Book p.75 verbatim: *"When the relative pronoun is used as the object of a clause, it can be omitted optionally. When the relative pronoun is used as the subject of a clause, it cannot be omitted."* Book p.79 verbatim: *"Important! The relative clause uses the AFFIRMATIVE ORGANIZATION, not the question. CORRECT: Can you tell me what you need? INCORRECT: Can you tell me what do you need?"*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"AFFIRMATIVE order inside the embedded question. Fortitude day 5 — we close the block holding the shape."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners:
- **WHO / WHOM**
- **WHICH / THAT**
- **WHERE / WHEN / WHY / HOW**
- **EMBEDDED (affirmative order!)**

Stand in the middle. Call a sentence skeleton out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + speak the full correct version as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 16 calls in 15 min, ~1 per minute.

```
Call                                                  → Right zone + sample completion
1.  "The colleague ___ I trained..."                  → WHO/WHOM     — "WHO/THAT I trained" (object → can omit)
2.  "The report ___ I sent..."                        → WHICH/THAT   — "WHICH/THAT I sent" (object → can omit)
3.  "The office ___ we met..."                        → WHERE        — "WHERE we met"
4.  "Can you tell me ___ the meeting is?"             → EMBEDDED     — "WHERE THE MEETING IS" (NOT 'where is')
5.  "My manager, ___ is from Bogotá, signed off."     → WHO/WHOM     — "WHO is from Bogotá" (non-def, comma)
6.  "The year ___ I joined was 2024."                 → WHERE/WHEN.. — "WHEN I joined"
7.  "Do you know ___ she works here?"                 → EMBEDDED     — "WHY SHE WORKS HERE"
8.  "The person ___ car is in the lot..."             → WHO/WHOM     — "WHOSE car is in the lot"
9.  "The system ___ crashed yesterday..."             → WHICH/THAT   — "WHICH/THAT crashed" (subject → CANNOT omit)
10. "I wonder ___ he replied."                        → EMBEDDED     — "IF/WHETHER HE REPLIED"
11. "The reason ___ we paused..."                     → WHERE/WHEN.. — "WHY we paused"
12. "My laptop, ___ I bought last month, is slow."    → WHICH/THAT   — "WHICH I bought" (non-def → WHICH, not THAT)
13. "Could you say ___ the report is finished?"       → EMBEDDED     — "IF THE REPORT IS FINISHED"
14. "The way ___ she solved it..."                    → WHERE/WHEN.. — "HOW she solved it"
15. "The candidate ___ we hired..."                   → WHO/WHOM     — "WHO/WHOM/THAT we hired" (object → omit OK)
16. "I don't know ___ they decided."                  → EMBEDDED     — "WHAT THEY DECIDED"
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — what is the noun before the blank?" When the zone is right and the form is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (10 min) Pair drilling with cards + commas

Hand each pair: 6 relative pronoun cards (mix of all 3 colors) + 1 [OMIT?] marker + 2 [comma]/[no comma] markers. Pair work:

1. Student A picks a relative pronoun card + a comma marker; reads them out loud.
2. Student B builds a full sentence using both correctly. If [OMIT?] also drawn, says whether the pronoun is droppable (object) or not (subject).
3. Swap roles after every card. After 5 cards each, add an EMBEDDED stem card from SET B and they build the embedded version.

Walk and coach the FORM (pronoun choice, defining/non-defining, OMIT or not). Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"AFFIRMATIVE order inside. Fortitude day 5 — hold the shape."*

### (7 min) Podium — defining vs non-defining

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a base sentence; they must produce BOTH versions (defining = no comma, identifies / non-defining = comma, extra info) and explain which one fits a real workplace context. Cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"*

```
1st: "The colleague + she trained me"
     → DEFINING: "The colleague WHO trained me is on leave." (identifies which colleague)
     → NON-DEF:  "Marcela, WHO trained me, is on leave."     (Marcela is known; extra info)

2nd: "The report + I finished it"
     → DEFINING: "The report THAT I finished is on your desk." (which report)
     → NON-DEF:  "Q2-MEMO-07, WHICH I finished, is on your desk." (the report is named; extra)

3rd: "Bogotá + the new office is there"
     → DEFINING (no comma in this case): "Cities WHERE we have offices are growing."
     → NON-DEF:  "Bogotá, WHERE the new office is, is growing fast."
```

Then one final chorus drill — you call the embedded stem, cohort completes in unison keeping AFFIRMATIVE order:

> *"Can you tell me — where (she lives)?"* → "where SHE LIVES?"
> *"Do you know — when (the meeting starts)?"* → "when THE MEETING STARTS?"
> *"I wonder — if (he replied)?"* → "if HE REPLIED."

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 6 ask/answer pairs. Asker speaks an embedded question; answerer responds with a relative-clause sentence in 3 sec; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for NON-DEFINING clauses — at least 2 per triad. Watch for the wrong use of THAT in non-defining (must be WHICH/WHO). Continue live board-error correction. **Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Push for OMITTED pronouns (object position only). Each triad must produce 2. By now they should be sharp. Slow down only for the most stubborn pattern you wrote on the board across the previous 2 rounds.

## 6. BOOK M37 — USES OF "LIKE" + BOARD RACE — 12 min — STAND UP

Write on the board (before class):

```
M37 — WHAT DO YOU LOOK LIKE? (Book p.307)
       Describing People

From Guia 1 (p.310):

"1. Aparte de su uso como 'gustar', hay varios
 significados adicionales para 'like'."

  USE              ENGLISH                  SPANISH
  ----------------------------------------------------
  "Gustar"         What do you LIKE?        Que te gusta?
  "Como"           You work LIKE a donkey.  Trabajas como
                                            un burro.
  Personalidad     What ARE you LIKE?       Como eres tu?
  (idiomatico)
  Apariencia       What do you LOOK LIKE?   Como te ves?
  (idiomatico)
  Estado animo     How are you?             Como estas?
  (NO usa "like")

"2. Para describirnos, usamos 'to be' o 'to have'."
   I HAVE a round face.    (to have + body part)
   I AM tall.              (to be  + personal adj)

"3. Podemos usar 'very' o 'somewhat'."
   I have a VERY round face.    (amplifica)
   I am SOMEWHAT tall.          (minimiza)

KEY: appearance      → "what do X LOOK LIKE?"
     personality     → "what is X LIKE?"
     enjoy           → "what does X LIKE?" (NOT here)
     describe        → "to be" + adj / "to have" + body
     tune strength   → "very" / "somewhat" (one at a time)
```

Say it standing at the board: *"'Like' has three jobs. Job 1: ENJOY — 'what do you LIKE?'. Job 2: PERSONALITY — 'what ARE you LIKE?'. Job 3: APPEARANCE — 'what do you LOOK LIKE?'. Today: jobs 2 and 3 — describing people. 'To be' + adjective ('I AM tall') OR 'to have' + body part ('I HAVE a round face'). 'Very' stronger, 'somewhat' softer — one at a time."*

### BOARD RACE — 2 teams — 7 min

```

   → "What does she LOOK LIKE?" (appearance)
2. "What you look like in your free time?" (personality)
   → "What ARE you LIKE in your free time?"
3. "I have tall."
   → "I AM tall." (to be + adj; NOT to have)
4. "He is a beard."
   → "He HAS a beard." (to have + body part)
5. "She is somewhat very calm."
   → "She is VERY calm." / "She is SOMEWHAT calm."
6. "How does he look like? Tired."
   → "What does he LOOK LIKE?" (appearance = "what",
      not "how"; "how" = mood)
```

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Two zones in the room (tape labels on opposite walls):
- **WRONG zone (left):** "like" confused / "to be" used for body parts / "how" instead of "what" / two modifiers stacked
- **RIGHT zone (right):** correct M37 use of "like" + "to be"/"to have" + single modifier

Hand each student **ONE Set E slip card** with a wrong-shape sentence.

### Round 1 — Walk it — 8 min

Each student:
1. Stands in WRONG zone, reads slip aloud with the wrong form ("I have tall and I am a beard...").
2. Walks to RIGHT zone shouting the corrected form ("I AM tall and I HAVE a beard...").
3. Cohort confirms with thumbs.

Suggested slips:

```
"I have tall and I am a beard."
   → I AM tall and I HAVE a beard.
"How does the new client look like?"
   → What does the new client LOOK LIKE?
"What do you like? (asking about her personality)"
   → What ARE you LIKE? (personality)
"He is a round face."
   → He HAS a round face. (to have + body part)
"She is somewhat very calm in meetings."
   → She is SOMEWHAT calm. / She is VERY calm.
"The supervisor is have steady hands."
   → The supervisor HAS steady hands.
"What is your supervisor look like under pressure?"
   → What DOES your supervisor look like under pressure?
"My new colleague has tall and slender."
   → My new colleague IS tall and slender.
```

### Round 2 — Reverse — 4 min

Swap slips. Each student starts in RIGHT, walks to WRONG saying out loud WHY it's wrong: *"'I have tall' is wrong — adjective uses 'to be': 'I am tall'; 'to have' is for body parts: 'I have long hair'."*

### Close — 2 min

2 volunteers walk their transformation in front of the group. Cohort counts correct forms. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

```
VOCABULARIO 2 — ADJETIVOS PERSONALES (p.309):

   pale/olive/dark (skin), strong, weak,
   tall, short, long, blond, fat, slender, thin,
   straight, curly, wavy, average-height, average-weight

BOOK EXAMPLES (Ejemplos p.311) — verbatim:
   "The man is average height and average weight. He
    has long, strong arms and short legs. His eyes
    are round and his nose is big. He has pale skin.
    He has brown, curly hair. He has a beard and a

    nose and a big mouth and gray hair. He has big
    hands. He has pale skin."

```

Say: *"Two verbs do the description job. 'To be' goes with adjectives — what the person IS. 'To have' goes with body parts — what the person HAS. 'Her hair IS black' OR 'She HAS black hair' — both correct, two paths. Add 'very' to push the adjective stronger, 'somewhat' to soften it — one at a time, never both."*

## 9. HUMAN-SENTENCE LINE-UP + 3 STATIONS — 12 min — STAND UP

### Human line-up — 4 min

**Use Set F sentence builder cards.** Each student gets ONE card (subject, verb, adjective or body part, modifier, special "LIKE" card, or "?"). You call out a target sentence; whoever holds a relevant card walks to the front and stands in the correct word-order to form it. Group physically decides whether "to be" or "to have", whether modifier comes in, and whether the "LIKE" card belongs (appearance/personality questions only).

Targets to call (3–4 rounds):

- *"He has steady hands."* → "He" + "has" + "steady hands"
- *"What does the new auditor LOOK LIKE?"* → "What" + "does" + "The new auditor" + "look" + "LIKE" + "?"
- *"What is your supervisor LIKE under pressure?"* → "What" + "is" + "your supervisor" + "LIKE" + "?"

### 3 stations — 8 min — bell every ~2.5 min

```
STATION A — USE OF "LIKE" (question vs statement)
Build 4 questions/sentences using "like" correctly:
- 1 with "what do you LIKE?" (enjoy)
- 1 with "what ARE you LIKE?" (personality)
- 1 with "what do you LOOK LIKE?" (appearance)
- 1 with "like a [comparison]" ("you work like a pro")

"What do you like for lunch? // What is your
 supervisor like under pressure? // What does the
 new auditor look like? // She works like a senior
 — calm and precise."

STATION B — "TO BE" + PERSONAL ADJECTIVE
Build 4 sentences describing real teammates with
"to be" + personal adjective: tall, short, slender,
young, strong, calm, average-height.
"Maria IS somewhat tall. The new client IS very
 young. Carlos IS strong but somewhat short.
 Together we ARE average-loud and very precise."

STATION C — "TO HAVE" + BODY PART
Build 4 sentences with "to have" + body part: hair,
eyes, hands, beard, freckles, mustache, fingers.
"He HAS short, curly hair. She HAS very steady
 hands. The supervisor HAS dark eyes. The auditor
 HAS a small mustache and long, thin fingers."
```

You circulate, correct softly, note errors with NAMES.

## 10. PODIUM + SPEED DRILL — 10 min — STAND UP

### Podium — 4 min — circle

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Chain: each student describes a real teammate in 3 lines using "to be" + "to have" + 1 modifier. Cohort confirms in chorus: *"Confirmed — described!"* Cover at least one student per piece (LOOK LIKE / ARE LIKE / TO BE + adj / TO HAVE + body).

```
A: "What does Maria look like? She IS somewhat tall,
    she HAS short straight black hair, her eyes ARE
    very dark."                              (LOOK LIKE)
   → chorus: "Confirmed — described!"
B: "What is the supervisor like under pressure? He IS
    very calm, he HAS steady hands, his face IS
    somewhat serious."                       (ARE LIKE)
   → chorus: "Confirmed — described!"
C: "Carlos IS strong and somewhat short. He HAS dark
    olive skin and a small beard."           (TO BE +
                                              TO HAVE)
   → chorus: "Confirmed — described!"
D: "She HAS very long, curly hair, and her face IS
    very round."                             (TO HAVE
                                              + TO BE)
   → chorus: "Confirmed — described!"
```

### Speed drill — 6 min — circle

You say a body part or personal adjective; the next student responds with "to have" or "to be" as appropriate + optional modifier:

```
"hair"         → "She HAS very long, curly hair."
"tall"         → "He IS somewhat tall."
"a beard"      → "He HAS a small beard."
"calm"         → "She IS very calm under pressure."
"hands"        → "The supervisor HAS steady hands."
"young"        → "The new auditor IS somewhat young."
"freckles"     → "Maria HAS a few freckles on her face."
"average-height" → "Carlos IS average-height and slender."
```

Then chorus drill on the question form:

```
"appearance"        → "What do you LOOK LIKE?"
"personality"       → "What ARE you LIKE?"
"enjoy"             → "What do you LIKE?"
"mood"              → "How are you?" (NO "like")
"boss's appearance" → "What does your boss LOOK LIKE?"
"client's personality" → "What is the new client LIKE?"
```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT PAST PERFECT (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande el MAPA (2 capas de tiempo):

   TWO PAST EVENTS — WHICH CAME FIRST?

   (1) THE MAIN PAST EVENT (the later one)
        past simple -> the action you are telling
            "I arrived at the office."
            "She called me."

   (2) THE EARLIER EVENT (it happened BEFORE)
        had + past participle -> already done
            "...the meeting had already started."
            "...because I had forgotten my keys."

   SIGNAL WORDS that mark the earlier event:
        by the time ... / before ... / already /
        after ... / when (once I had ...)

   *** Later event = past simple. ***
   *** Earlier event = HAD + participle. ***

2. SEÑALA capa (1). DI: "The main action — the one your
   story is about — stays in past simple. 'I arrived.'
   'I opened the door.' Nothing new here."

3. SEÑALA capa (2). DI: "Now the earlier moment. Something
   that was ALREADY done before the main action. That one
   takes HAD + the participle — the same participle you
   used in Cl 15-17. 'The meeting HAD started.' 'I HAD
   forgotten my keys.' HAD never changes — he, she, I,
   they: all HAD."

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "Listen — 'by the time the hard moment arrived,
   I HAD already prepared, I HAD already failed, I HAD
   already learned.' Three earlier events, all HAD +
   participle, all done BEFORE the hard moment. That is
   the past before the past."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "By the time I arrived, the meeting
   had start." -> TACHA "start", ESCRIBE "...had
   STARTED." DI: "After HAD = participle. start ->
   started. The -ed (or irregular form), never the bare
   verb." COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "When I arrived, she had went."
   -> TACHA "had went", ESCRIBE "she had GONE." DI:
   "go -> went -> GONE. The participle, not the past
   simple. had GONE, had DONE, had SEEN, had EATEN."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande en board:

   THE THREE SIGNAL FRAMES (use these to show order)

      "By the time + [past simple], + [had + participle]"
          "By the time I called, he had already left."
      "[past simple] + because + [had + participle]"
          "I was tired because I had worked all night."
      "Before + [past simple], + [had + participle]"
          "Before the test came, I had practiced for weeks."

      *** The HAD-clause is the FLASHBACK. ***
      *** The past-simple clause is the MAIN scene. ***

8. SEÑALA los frames. DI: "These three frames are your
   tools. 'By the time...', 'because...', 'before...' —
   each one lets you drop a flashback under the main
   action. The flashback is HAD + participle."

9. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I had ate before he arrived... no,
   I ate before he had arrived." -> ESCRIBE las dos y
   PREGUNTA cual evento fue PRIMERO. CORRIGE: si comiste
   primero, "I HAD eaten before he arrived." DI:
   "Fortitude — the EARLIER event gets HAD. Decide which
   came first, THEN choose the form." COHORTE repite la
   correcta 2 veces.

10. MODELA UNA historia completa en voz alta (60 seg),
    apuntando a las capas del board:
    "Last year I started a new job. By the time I walked
    in on the first morning, my manager had already
    chosen me for a difficult client. I was nervous
    because I had never managed an account alone. I had
    failed a smaller project before, so I knew the risk.
    First I asked for help. Then I practiced every night.
    Finally I closed the deal." DI: "Hear the flashbacks?
    'had already chosen', 'had never managed', 'had
    failed before' — each one happened EARLIER, under the
    main story."

11. PIDELES en parejas: "Each person tells a 5-line past
    story about a real challenge. At least TWO lines must
    be a flashback — something that HAD already happened
    before the main event. Use 'by the time', 'because',
    or 'before'. Partner listens and says which event
    came FIRST." 8 minutos.

12. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

13. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien aplane los dos eventos
    o diga "had went / had ate", DI suave: "Fortitude.
    What had ALREADY happened before? HAD + participle."

14. ESCRIBE una tercera seccion grande en board:

    PAST SIMPLE vs PAST PERFECT — THE CONTRAST

       "When I arrived, the meeting started."
           -> I arrived, THEN it started (same time/after)
       "When I arrived, the meeting had started."
           -> it started FIRST, I arrived later

       *** Same words, different order in time. ***
       *** HAD = it was already done. ***

15. SEÑALA el contraste. DI: "This is the power of past
    perfect — same sentence, but HAD tells the listener
    the meeting was ALREADY going before you walked in.
    Without HAD, you arrived first. One word changes the
    whole timeline."

16. PIDELES en parejas (nueva): "Retell your story — now
    make sure ONE moment uses the contrast clearly: tell
    me something that had ALREADY happened before your
    main event, so I feel you arrived 'too late' or
    'already prepared'. 6-8 lines. Partner counts the
    HAD + participle flashbacks." 7 minutos.

17. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo coloque al menos
    1 flashback en HAD + participle con el orden correcto.
    DI: "Stand up — we go to the interview."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y la cohorte aun pone el evento anterior en past simple plano, NO agregues el contraste (paso 14). Reduce el objetivo a UN frame solo ("By the time + simple, + had + participle") y deja el contraste como callback en Cl 20. Mejor UNA flashback limpia que tres confusas.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas narrando 6-8 lineas con al menos 1 flashback en HAD + participle, orden de eventos claro, sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: BACKSTORY INTERVIEW — "WHAT HAD YOU ALREADY DONE?" (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "BACKSTORY INTERVIEW — WHAT
   HAD YOU ALREADY DONE BEFORE? — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the INTERVIEWER. One
   is the CANDIDATE. The interviewer asks about a real
   achievement or recovery at work: 'Tell me about a
   success — and what you had already done to prepare for
   it.' The candidate answers with a story that has TWO
   time layers: the main past event (past simple) AND at
   least two flashbacks of what HAD already happened
   before (had + participle). The interviewer asks 2
   follow-ups that dig into the backstory: 'What had you
   tried before that?' 'Had you ever done it before?'
   Then switch roles — new story."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This interview IS the phrase
   in action — by the time the hard moment arrived, I HAD
   already prepared, I HAD already failed, I HAD already
   learned. The past before the past is where the courage
   was built."

4. ESCRIBE en board el ESQUELETO que el candidate sigue
   (no un guion — un mapa):

     MAIN EVENT  -> "Last year I ... (past simple)"
     FLASHBACK 1 -> "By the time it happened, I had
                     already ..."
     FLASHBACK 2 -> "I knew what to do because I had ..."
     FLASHBACK 3 -> "I had failed/tried ... before, so..."
     RESULT      -> "...and in the end I ... (past simple)"

   Plus the 2 interviewer backstory follow-ups (examples):
     "What had you already done to prepare?"
     "Had you ever faced this before?"
     "What had gone wrong the first time?"
     "By the time you started, what had you decided?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge roles.
   Candidate tells the story with main event + at least
   2 flashbacks (had + participle). Interviewer asks 2
   backstory follow-ups. Then SWITCH roles — different
   story.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a
   parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English. Decide
   which event came FIRST, then say 'had' + participle."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same format, pero ahora
   el candidate debe usar AL MENOS un frame "By the time
   ... had already ..." Y un frame "...because I had ..."
   in the SAME story. Interviewer adds 2 follow-ups.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la entrevista: si alguien
   aplane los eventos o diga "had went / had came", DI
   suave: "Fortitude. What had ALREADY happened before?
   HAD + participle."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que cada historia tenga el evento
    principal (past simple) + AL MENOS 2 flashbacks (had +
    participle) + 1 signal word (by the time / before /
    already). Observer (third person of nearby pair)
    cuenta los flashbacks y los "had went / had ate".

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (1 mini-interview completa — pregunta + historia
    con 2 flashbacks + 2 follow-ups). Observer voluntario
    reporta en voz alta: "I counted X flashbacks in HAD +
    participle, and the order was clear." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What had you already tried before it worked?" / "What had gone wrong the first time?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de interviewer ni candidate.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con main event + 2 flashbacks en HAD + participle, orden de tiempo claro.

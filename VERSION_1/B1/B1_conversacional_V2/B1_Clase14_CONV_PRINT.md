## BLOCK 1 — OPENER (~10 min)

```

   Today we anchor every past action in TIME — when
   it happened — and we order the story: first, then,

2. [VIRTUD] activation: ESCRIBE "JUSTICE" en board
   grande. DI: "Day 4 — Justice gives every action

   the form's justice."

3. [FRASE] insertion 1: ESCRIBE la frase en board.
   LEELA 2 veces en voz alta. COHORTE repite 2 veces.

4. [PROMPT] (Past Time Anchor roundup — Grammar pase
   Cl 13): LEE en voz alta los 3 gaps que el Grammar
   Teacher anoto y devolvio en la hoja "PASE A CONV —
   CL 14". Luego corre el roundup que pidio: cada
   persona describe 1 interaccion real de la semana
   pasada usando EXACTO: 1 affirmative + 1 negative
   (DIDN'T + bare) + 1 WH-question + 1 short answer,
   TODO anclado con una expresion de tiempo (yesterday,
   last Tuesday, two days ago). Rapido, una vuelta.

5. ESCUCHA. NO corrijas todavia la expresion de tiempo
   — solo marca: estos son los puentes de hoy. Si oyes
   "ago two days", "in last week", "the last Monday",
   "yesterday I go" — anotalo mental, NO interrumpas.

6. DI (bridge): "You all reached for time words just
   now — yesterday, last week. Today we make them
   exact. THREE rules. ONE — AGO counts back from now
   and goes AFTER the period: 'two days ago', never
   'ago two days'. TWO — LAST takes NO 'the': 'last
   week', never 'the last week'. THREE — IN for the
   year: 'in 2020', 'in June'. And the time word
   usually lands at the END of the sentence."

7. DI: "If I hear 'ago two days' or 'in last week' or
   'the last Monday' — I say JUSTICE, and you give the
   time word its fair place."

8. DI: "Sit straight. Marker ready. We go to the board."
```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT TIME EXPRESSIONS + SEQUENCING (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 1 tabla grande de PREPOSICIONES de tiempo
   en el board:

   PREPOSITION   USE                 EXAMPLE
   AT            clock time          at 8:00, at 6:30
   ON            day / date          on Monday, on the 5th
   IN            year / month        in 2020, in June
   (no prep)     yesterday / today   yesterday, this morning
   LAST + period last week/month/yr  last week (NO "the")
   ___ AGO       counts back from now two days ago

   *** Book anchor (A2 Module 12): "I ate a sandwich
       AT 8:00." "Steve woke up AT 6:30 today." "I was
       living in Bucaramanga IN 2019." ***

2. SEÑALA la fila AGO. DI: "AGO counts back from NOW.
   It goes AFTER the period. 'Two days ago.' 'Three
   weeks ago.' 'Ten years ago.' NEVER 'ago two days'."

3. SEÑALA la fila LAST. DI: "LAST plus the period.
   No 'the'. 'Last week.' 'Last Monday.' 'Last year.'
   NEVER 'the last week'."

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is what the phrase says — yesterday, last
   week, two days ago, in 2020. Give every action its
   fair place in time. AGO after the period, LAST with
   no 'the', IN for the year."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I called him ago two days."
   -> TACHA "ago two days", ESCRIBE "I called him TWO
   DAYS AGO." DI: "AGO goes AFTER the period." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces en coro.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I worked the last Monday."
   -> TACHA "the last Monday", ESCRIBE "I worked LAST
   MONDAY." DI: "LAST takes no 'the'." COHORTE repite
   2 veces.

7. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande en board:

   WHERE THE TIME WORD GOES

      END (default):
        I finished the report YESTERDAY.
        She started LAST WEEK.
        We met TWO DAYS AGO.

      START (for emphasis, + comma):
        YESTERDAY, I finished the report.
        LAST WEEK, she started the project.

   *** Default = END. Front only for emphasis, with a
       comma. NEVER mid-verb: not "I yesterday went". ***

8. SEÑALA position. DI: "Default — the time word lands
   at the END. For emphasis you can move it to the
   FRONT with a comma. But never in the middle: not
   'I yesterday called'."

9. ESCRIBE una tercera seccion grande en board:

   SEQUENCING A PAST STORY

      FIRST, I checked my email.
      THEN, I called the supplier.
      AFTER THAT, I sent the invoice.
      BEFORE the meeting, I printed the report.
      FINALLY, I closed the office.

   *** These words ORDER the actions. One past story,
       clean order, each action anchored in time. ***

10. SEÑALA sequencing. DI: "These words order the
    story. FIRST, THEN, AFTER THAT, BEFORE, FINALLY.
    Each action stays in past simple — bare after DID
    if it turns into a question. The sequencing word
    gives the order; the time word gives the place."

11. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee con la
    expresion de tiempo, DI suave: "Justice. Give the
    time its fair place — AGO after the period, LAST
    with no 'the', IN for the year."

12. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "In last week I traveled."
    -> TACHA "In last week", ESCRIBE "LAST WEEK I
    traveled." DI: "No 'in' before LAST. LAST already
    fixes the time." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "Yesterday I go to the office."
    -> TACHA "go", ESCRIBE "Yesterday I WENT to the
    office." DI: "A past time word demands a past
    verb. Yesterday -> WENT, not go." COHORTE repite
    2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each in past
    simple, every one anchored with a DIFFERENT time
    expression — yesterday, last week, two days ago,
    in 2020, this morning. Time word at the END. About
    your real last week at work or study." 6 minutos.

15. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

16. PIDELES en parejas: "Now build ONE short past
    story — 5 actions — ordered with FIRST / THEN /
    AFTER THAT / BEFORE / FINALLY, each action with a
    time word. About a real workday last week." 6 min.

17. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo ancle el
    verbo pasado con la expresion de tiempo correcta
    (AGO after, LAST no 'the', IN + year) y ordene con
    sequencing words sin titubear. DI: "Stand up — we
    go to the weekly recap meeting."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun pegan "ago two days" / "the last week" / drift a presente, NO AVANCES a sequencing. Reduce a 2 reglas hoy (AGO position + LAST no 'the'), deja sequencing para callback en Cl 15 opener. Mejor LAND 2 bien que rush 4 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas produciendo past simple anclado con AGO/LAST/IN correctos + una historia ordenada con sequencing words sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: WEEKLY RECAP MEETING (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "WEEKLY RECAP MEETING —
   3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the TEAM LEAD.
   One is the REPORTER. The reporter recaps LAST WEEK
   — 6 to 8 completed actions in past simple, each one
   PINNED with a time expression (yesterday, last
   Monday, two days ago, last week, this morning) and
   ORDERED with sequencing words (first, then, after
   that, before, finally). The team lead asks at least
   2 WH-questions to dig in ('When did you send it?'
   'What did you do first?'). Then you switch roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This meeting IS the
   phrase in action — yesterday, last week, two days
   ago, in 2020. Give every action its fair place in
   time, and the story stands true."

4. ESCRIBE en board el frame del reporter (modelo):

     FIRST, last Monday, I reviewed the budget.
     THEN, two days ago, I called the supplier.
     AFTER THAT, yesterday, I sent the report.
     BEFORE the meeting, this morning, I printed it.
     FINALLY, I closed the week clean.

   Plus team lead WH-questions:
     "When DID you send the report?"
     "What DID you do first?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge roles.
   Reporter recaps 6-8 actions, anchored + ordered.
   Team lead asks 2 WH-questions. Then SWITCH roles,
   different week.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven
   a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch
   to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English.
   Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same format, pero
   ahora el team lead PUSHES for at least 3 different
   time expressions and 3 different sequencing words
   in the recap. Reporter keeps the time word at the
   END (or front + comma).

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la reunion: si alguien
   diga "ago two days" o "in last week" o "the last
   Monday", DI suave: "Justice. Give the time its fair
   place — AGO after the period, LAST with no 'the'."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada recap aparezcan AL
    MENOS 6 past actions + 4 time expressions
    distintas + 3 sequencing words + 2 WH-questions
    del team lead. Observer (third person of nearby
    pair) counts time expressions used correctly.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (1 mini-recap completo — reporter ordena
    5 actions con time words + team lead 2 WH-
    questions). Observer voluntario reporta en voz
    alta: "I counted X time expressions, Y sequencing
    words — all in the right place." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What did you do first last Monday?" / "When did you send it?" / "What happened two days ago?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de team lead ni reporter.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con 6 past actions + 4 time expressions correctas + 3 sequencing words + 2 WH-questions visibles.

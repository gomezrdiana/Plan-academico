## BLOCK 1 — OPENER (~10 min)

```
1. DI: "Welcome. Class 12 of 44. Yesterday the past was
   easy — verb + ED. Today the past refuses ED. Went.
   Had. Thought. Took. Said. Each one has its own form.
   No rule lets you guess. Memorize, then use."

2. [VIRTUD] activation: ESCRIBE "JUSTICE" en board
   grande. DI: "Day 2 — Some verbs refuse the regular
   ED. They have their own past. Memorize the form,
   then use it. Justice means giving each verb its
   true name in the past."

3. [FRASE] insertion 1: ESCRIBE la frase en board.
   LEELA 2 veces en voz alta. COHORTE repite 2 veces.
   UN estudiante la dice solo.

4. [PROMPT] (mystery — Grammar pase Cl 11):
   LEE en voz alta los 3 gaps que el Grammar Teacher
   anoto ayer (tipico: "She studyed the report" /
   "I worked /wɔːrkid/" pronunciado mal / "He stoped
   at 5" sin doblar consonante). DI: "Yesterday's
   regulars still leak — spelling and pronunciation.
   Today we add 25 irregulars. The regulars do NOT
   disappear — they sit next to the irregulars and
   we use both."

5. ESCUCHA reacciones. NO entres en discusion. Solo
   marca: estos son los puentes de hoy.

6. DI (bridge): "Yesterday: WORK -> WORKED. STUDY ->
   STUDIED. STOP -> STOPPED. The ED adds and ED is
   pronounced /t/, /d/, or /ɪd/. Today: GO -> WENT.
   HAVE -> HAD. THINK -> THOUGHT. No ED. Different
   word. Same job — past tense. The verb decides
   the form. You learn it once, you own it forever."

7. DI: "If I hear 'I goed to work' or 'She thinked
   about it' or 'We getted there at 9' — I say the
   phrase, and you replace ED with the irregular."

8. DI: "Sit straight. Marker ready. We go to the board."
```

**[ALERTA]** si la pase de Grammar no llego (Grammar Teacher olvido entregarla), USA los errores tipicos genericos: "She studyed", "He stoped", "I worked /wɔːrkid/". NO esperes. NO pierdas tiempo buscando.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** cuando la cohorte tenga voz (3-4 hayan dicho algo) y el contraste regular vs irregular este verbalizado, PASA a Block 2.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT IRREGULAR VERBS — 5 PATTERN GROUPS (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board la frase encabezado:
   "PAST SIMPLE IRREGULAR — 5 PATTERNS"

2. ESCRIBE las 5 columnas (toma 4 min, vale la pena):

   GROUP 1 — SAME (no change)
     put -> put
     set -> set
     let -> let
     cut -> cut
     read -> read (spelled SAME, pronounced /rɛd/)
     hit -> hit
     Ex: "I put the file on the desk yesterday."
         "She read the email this morning."

   GROUP 2 — VOWEL CHANGE
     run -> ran        sit -> sat
     give -> gave      see -> saw
     come -> came      begin -> began
     write -> wrote    drink -> drank
     get -> got        win -> won
     Ex: "He ran to the meeting."
         "We came late but we sat in front."

   GROUP 3 — -OUGHT / -AUGHT
     think -> thought
     bring -> brought
     buy -> bought
     catch -> caught
     teach -> taught
     fight -> fought
     Ex: "I thought about it and bought the laptop."
         "She brought the report and taught the class."

   GROUP 4 — -ELT / -ENT / -EPT / -EFT
     feel -> felt
     mean -> meant
     send -> sent
     spend -> spent
     keep -> kept
     leave -> left
     sleep -> slept
     Ex: "I felt tired so I left early."
         "She sent the email and kept a copy."

   GROUP 5 — TOTALLY DIFFERENT
     go -> went
     be -> was / were
     have -> had
     do -> did
     say -> said
     make -> made
     see -> saw
     find -> found
     Ex: "I went to the office, I had two meetings,
          I did three reports, I said goodbye at 6."

3. SEÑALA Group 5. DI: "These are the top 5 most
   frequent verbs in English. WENT. HAD. DID. SAID.
   MADE. You will use them today, tomorrow, every
   day. Own them first."

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is what the phrase says — I went, she
   had, we thought, they took. Each verb has its
   own past. No ED can fake it. Justice gives each
   verb its true past."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I goed to the office" -> TACHA
   "goed", ESCRIBE "I WENT to the office." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces en coro.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She thinked about it" -> TACHA
   "thinked", ESCRIBE "She THOUGHT about it." DI:
   "THINK belongs to Group 3 — the -OUGHT family.
   Same pattern as bring/brought, buy/bought."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "We getted there" -> TACHA
   "getted", ESCRIBE "We GOT there." DI: "GET is
   Group 2 — vowel change. Like run/ran, sit/sat."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "He bringed his laptop" ->
   TACHA "bringed", ESCRIBE "He BROUGHT his laptop."
   DI: "BRING is Group 3 — -OUGHT family. Bought,
   thought, brought, caught — same music."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

9. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "I readed the email" (pronunciado
   /riːdɪd/) -> TACHA "readed", ESCRIBE "I READ the
   email" + agrega pronunciacion /rɛd/. DI: "READ
   is Group 1 — same spelling, different pronunciation.
   Present /riːd/, past /rɛd/. Same letters, different
   mouth." COHORTE repite la presente Y la pasada 2
   veces: 'I read /riːd/ books every day. I read
   /rɛd/ the email yesterday.'

10. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee y diga
    "I goed" o "She thinked", DI suave: "Justice.
    That verb has its own past form. Use it."

11. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each. Each
    sentence uses 1 different irregular from a
    different group. About something real you did
    YESTERDAY or LAST WEEK. Verbs: went, had, did,
    thought, took, saw, came, gave, told, felt."
    5 minutos.

12. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

13. ESCRIBE en board una segunda seccion grande:
    REGULAR + IRREGULAR LIVE TOGETHER

    Cl 11 regulars (callback): worked, studied,
    stopped, traveled, played
    Cl 12 irregulars (today):   went, had, did,
    thought, took

    Mixed example:
      "Yesterday I WORKED until 6, then I WENT
       home, I HAD dinner, I STUDIED for an hour,
       and I THOUGHT about today's class."

14. SEÑALA el mixed example. DI: "Real B1 sounds
    like this — regulars and irregulars in the same
    sentence. WORKED is regular, WENT is irregular,
    HAD is irregular, STUDIED is regular, THOUGHT
    is irregular. Same time, same story, mixed
    forms. The verb decides the form."

15. PIDELES en parejas: "Describe yesterday from
    morning to night. 5 actions, in order. AT LEAST
    2 regulars from Cl 11 + AT LEAST 3 irregulars
    from today's 5 groups. Time-link them with then,
    after that, finally." 6 min.

16. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo combine
    regulares + irregulares sin reemplazar el
    irregular por -ED. DI: "Stand up — we go to
    the simulation."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun reemplazan irregulares por -ED, NO AVANCES al mixed pairs. Reduce a 3 grupos hoy (Group 5 + Group 2 + Group 3), deja Group 1 + Group 4 para callback en Cl 13. Mejor LAND 3 patterns bien que rush 5 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo oraciones que mezclan regular + irregular sin titubear y respetando la forma irregular sin agregar -ED.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: WEEKEND STORYTELLING SWAP (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "WEEKEND STORYTELLING
   SWAP — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. Person A tells Person B
   5 things they did last weekend or last vacation.
   Force AT LEAST 4 irregular verbs in those 5
   sentences. Person B is the OBSERVER — counts
   irregular verbs used on paper. Then SWITCH.
   Then ROTATE partners. Three rounds total."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This swap IS the phrase
   in action — I went, she had, we thought, they
   took. Each story owns its irregular past. No ED
   can fake it."

4. ESCRIBE en board 2 mini-scenarios para arrancar:

   SCENARIO 1 — Last weekend
     What you DID (Saturday + Sunday).
     5 actions in order.
     Force at least 4 of: went, had, ate, drank,
     saw, met, took, made, bought, slept, woke,
     felt, came, found.
     Observer counts irregular verbs on paper.
     Observer also flags any "I goed" / "I eated"
     / "I drinked" misfires.

   SCENARIO 2 — Best vacation you've taken
     Where you WENT.
     What you SAW, ATE, DID, BOUGHT.
     Who you MET.
     What you FELT at the end.
     5 sentences minimum.
     Observer counts irregular verbs + flags any
     regularized irregular ("eated", "buyed").

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge un
   scenario. Roles claros. Person A talks 4 min
   (5 sentences). Person B observes + counts.
   SWITCH inside the pair (4 min). Observer reports:
   "I counted X irregulars and Y misfires."

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3
   veces en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x ->
   vuelven a pares.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck on an
   irregular form, switch to Spanish for 5 seconds
   ('que dije en pasado?'), find the irregular,
   come back to English. Don't freeze. Don't fake
   it with -ED."

8. ROTA pares. Round 2 (12 min): cambien de scenario
   tambien. Same rules.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si
   alguien diga "I eated pizza" o "We buyed tickets"
   o "She thinked it was funny", DI suave: "Justice.
   That verb has its own past form. Use it."

10. ROTA pares otra vez. Round 3 (10 min): pareja
    inventa una historia mixta — 5 sentences que
    combinen MINIMO 3 irregulares (de hoy) + MINIMO
    2 regulares (de Cl 11). Tema libre: workday,
    holiday, family event, accident, learning
    moment.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2
    veces en pares distintos. Anota con NOMBRE en
    tu cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (5 sentences in a row, mixing regular +
    irregular). Observer reporta en voz alta: "I
    counted X irregulars, Y regulars, Z misfires."
    Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What did you eat on Saturday?" / "Where did you go for lunch?" / "Who did you see on Sunday?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO cuentes tu historia.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con minimo 3 irregulares + 2 regulares visibles + observer reportando conteo.

## BLOCK 4 — CIERRE (~15 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board: "ERRORS HEARD TODAY"

2. ESCRIBE los 5 errores tipicos (anonimos):
     X "I goed to the office"     -> "I WENT to the office"
     X "She thinked about it"     -> "She THOUGHT about it"
     X "We getted there"          -> "We GOT there"
     X "He bringed his laptop"    -> "He BROUGHT his laptop"
     X "I readed the email"
       (pronunciado /riːdɪd/)     -> "I READ the email" (/rɛd/)
     CALLBACK Cl 11 regulars:
       WORKED /wɔːrkt/ (not /wɔːrkid/)
       STOPPED (double consonant)
       STUDIED (y -> ied)

3. COHORTE repite las 5 correcciones + el callback
   2 veces en coro.

4. [FRASE] close: UN estudiante (al azar) dice la
   frase de memoria + la PARAFRASEA con su propio
   ejemplo del trabajo o de ayer ("Justice — yesterday
   I went to the meeting, I had three calls, I thought
   about the proposal, I sent the report; each verb
   owns its past, no ED needed"). Aceptas cualquier
   parafraseo que mantenga "each verb its own past
   form, no ED."

5. [VIRTUD] cierre del dia:
   PREGUNTA: "Which irregular past did you own today?"
   — 2 respuestas maximo, rapido, sin discusion.
   DI: "Day 2 of 5 — JUSTICE. Each verb its true
   past. Tomorrow Cl 13 — Past Simple negatives and
   questions. Same irregulars, new shape: did + go,
   did + have, did + think."

6. ESCRIBE HOMEWORK en board (mientras la dices):

      HOMEWORK CL 12 CONV — DUE BEFORE NEXT CLASS, 7:00 AM
      TIME: 30-45 min (B1). NOT fragmented.

      (1) WRITING — 10 sentences total:
          - 6 with irregular past from today,
            covering AT LEAST 4 of the 5 groups:
              "I went to the bank at 10."
              "She had two meetings yesterday."
              "We thought it was a bad idea."
              "He took the bus home."
              "I read /rɛd/ the email at 8."
              "They put the files on the shelf."
          - 4 mixing irregular + regular (Cl 11):
              "Yesterday I worked until 6 and went
               home tired."
              "She studied for two hours then had
               dinner."
              "We stopped at the cafe and bought
               coffee."
              "He played soccer then felt sore."
          Hand in on paper next class.

      (2) AUDIO eyes closed — 2-3 min — "My yesterday
          from morning to night" — describe what you
          did yesterday in order, using AT LEAST 6
          irregular verbs from today + 2 regulars
          from Cl 11. Label: B1-CONV-12. Send to
          WhatsApp group before next class.

      (3) LISTENING — 10 min YouTube — search "short
          personal story English past tense". Note
          5 sentences with irregular past verbs.
          Mark which group each belongs to (1-5).
          Bring on paper.

      RULE: not fragmented. No "I didn't have time."
      If pushback: "This is what B1 requires.
      30 to 45 minutes is the minimum. We don't lower
      the bar — we raise your stamina."

7. PASE Conv -> Grammar entregada ANTES de cambiar de
   bloque (ver siguiente seccion).

8. DI: "Next class Cl 13: past simple NEGATIVES and
   QUESTIONS — did + verb (in base form), didn't +
   verb (in base form). 'I didn't GO' — not 'I didn't
   went'. Same irregulars, new shape. Cl 13 continues
   JUSTICE day 3. See you next class. Any question
   about evaluations goes to coordination — not to me."
```

**[ALERTA]** si un estudiante no anota la tarea -> DI: "Phone out. Photo of the board now." Nadie se va sin saberla. Si alguien protesta por la longitud: "30-45 min total, not fragmented. That is the rule."
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** cohorte sale sabiendo la tarea + UN estudiante diciendo la frase de memoria + 5 errores + callback Cl 11 en board + PASE a Grammar entregada antes de cambiar de bloque + Cl 13 JUSTICE day 3 anunciada.

```

2. [VIRTUD] activation: ESCRIBE "FORTITUDE" en board

3. [FRASE] insertion 1: ESCRIBE la frase en board.
   LEELA 2 veces en voz alta. COHORTE repite 2 veces.
   UN estudiante la dice solo.

4. [PROMPT] (roundup — Grammar pase Cl 16):
   LEE en voz alta la hoja "PASE A CONV — CL 17" que
   el Grammar Teacher entrego al final de Cl 16. Pide
   el "How it used to be" roundup: cada persona dice
   UNA frase real — un habito o estado que USED TO be
   true en su vida o trabajo y ya no ("I used to take
   the bus to work" / "We used to use paper forms" /
   "She used to live in Bogotá"). Rapido, una vuelta.

5. ESCUCHA. NO corrijas todavia. Solo marca mentalmente
   los que pegan -ed/irregular despues de used to
   ("used to worked" / "used to went") — son los
   puentes de hoy.

6. DI (bridge): "Cl 13 you learned the BARE rule —
   after DID and DIDN'T the verb goes bare. Same family
   today. After USED TO, the verb is bare — 'used to
   WORK', not 'used to worked'. In the negative it's
   'didn't USE to' — bare USE, no -d. In the question
   'Did you USE to...?' — bare USE. One rule you already
   know: after DID the verb returns home."

7. DI: "And one more tool — WOULD. 'Every morning I
   would arrive at 7 and check the mail.' WOULD plus
   bare verb for repeated ACTIONS in the past. But NOT
   for states — we don't say 'I would live there'; for
   a state we say 'I used to live there.' Actions can
   take would; states stay with used to."

8. DI: "Sit straight. Marker ready. We go to the board."
```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT USED TO / WOULD (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 3 columnas en board grandes:

   AFFIRMATIVE              NEGATIVE                  QUESTION
   I used to work nights    I DIDN'T USE to work nights   DID you USE to work nights?
   She used to live there   She DIDN'T USE to live there  DID she USE to live there?
   We used to have an office We DIDN'T USE to have one     DID we USE to have one?
   They used to smoke       They DIDN'T USE to smoke       DID they USE to smoke?

   *** RULE: USED TO + BARE verb. ***
   *** NEGATIVE = didn't USE to (no -d). QUESTION = Did you USE to (no -d). ***
   *** Same BARE-after-DID rule from Cl 13. ***

2. SEÑALA columna AFFIRMATIVE. DI: "USED TO + bare
   verb. For a HABIT you repeated — 'I used to work
   nights' — or a STATE that was true and is not
   anymore — 'She used to live in Bogotá', 'We used to
   have a bigger office'. Habit or state, both take
   used to."

3. SEÑALA columna NEGATIVE. DI: "Negative drops the
   -d: 'didn't USE to'. Not 'didn't used to'. After
   DIDN'T the verb is bare — you know this from Cl 13.
   'I didn't use to work nights.'"

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is the phrase — I used to quit; now I push
   through. Used to plus the bare verb holds the old
   habit. Courage writes the new one."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I used to worked there"
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I used to WORK there."
   DI: "After USED TO the verb is bare. Drop the -ed."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She didn't used to live here"
   -> TACHA "used", ESCRIBE "She DIDN'T USE to live here."
   DI: "Negative drops the -d. Bare USE after DIDN'T.
   Same Cl 13 rule." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande en board:

   WOULD — repeated ACTIONS only (not states)

      Every morning I WOULD arrive at 7.
      He WOULD check the mail before coffee.
      We WOULD close the shop at 8 every night.

      *** WOULD + bare verb for repeated past ACTIONS. ***
      *** NOT for states: NOT "I would live there." ***
      *** State -> used to / past simple: "I used to live there." ***

8. SEÑALA WOULD. DI: "WOULD plus bare verb for actions
   you repeated. 'Every morning I would arrive at 7.'
   But a STATE — live, have, be, like, own — does NOT
   take would. 'I would live there' is wrong. Say 'I
   used to live there.' Actions take would; states stay
   with used to."

9. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I would live in Cali"
   -> TACHA "would live", ESCRIBE "I USED TO live in Cali."
   DI: "Live is a STATE. States don't take would. Used
   to." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

10. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each with USED TO
    — at least 2 habits and 2 states — about your life
    or work 5 years ago. Then 2 sentences with WOULD
    for a repeated action only." 6 minutos.

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee con la
    forma, DI suave: "Fortitude. After USED TO the verb
    is bare — used to GO. After DIDN'T it's USE. And
    WOULD is for actions, not states."

13. ESCRIBE una tercera seccion grande en board:

    USED TO vs PAST SIMPLE

       USED TO = repeated habit OR extended state
          "I used to work nights." (for years)
          "She used to live in Bogotá." (a state)

       PAST SIMPLE = one finished event
          "I worked last night." (one night)
          "She lived in Bogotá in 2019." (one fact / time)

       *** Repeated / extended -> used to. ***
       *** Single completed event -> past simple. ***

14. SEÑALA la comparacion. DI: "If it happened ONCE
    and finished — past simple. 'I worked last night.'
    If it was a habit you repeated, or a state that
    lasted — used to. 'I used to work nights.' Same
    past, different shape."

15. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "Yesterday I used to call the client"
    -> TACHA "used to call", ESCRIBE "Yesterday I CALLED the client."
    DI: "Yesterday is ONE finished event. Past simple,
    not used to. Used to is for repeated or extended."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

16. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "Did you used to smoke?"
    -> TACHA "used", ESCRIBE "DID you USE to smoke?"
    DI: "Question drops the -d. Bare USE after DID.
    Same Cl 13 machine." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

17. PIDELES en parejas: "Each pair builds 6 sentences
    contrasting used to and past simple about the same
    topic — work, a city, a routine. For 2 of them ask
    a question: 'Did you use to...?'" 6 minutos.

18. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo use USED TO +
    bare, didn't USE to, Did you USE to, y separe used
    to vs past simple sin titubear. DI: "Stand up — we
    go to the onboarding chat."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun pegan -ed/irregular despues de used to, o ponen "would" en estados, NO AVANCES a used-to-vs-past-simple. Reduce a 2 estructuras hoy (used to affirmative + negative), deja WOULD y la comparacion para callback en Cl 18. Mejor LAND 2 bien que rush 4 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo used to (habit + state) + didn't USE to + Did you USE to + WOULD para acciones, separando used to vs past simple sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: ONBOARDING / COMPANY-CHANGE CHAT (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "ONBOARDING CHAT — HOW
   THE COMPANY USED TO BE — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the SENIOR who has
   been here for years. One is the NEW HIRE on day one.
   The senior explains how the company, the role, and
   the routine USED TO be — compared to now. Use AT
   LEAST 6 sentences: a mix of used-to HABITS ('We used
   to work on paper'), used-to STATES ('We didn't use
   to have a CRM', 'The office used to be downtown'),
   and WOULD for repeated actions ('Every morning I
   would arrive at 7 and open the shop'). The new hire
   asks at least 2 questions: 'Did you use to...?' Then
   you switch roles — new senior, new hire."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This chat IS the phrase —
   we used to do it the old way; now we push through to
   the new one. Used to plus the bare verb holds the old
   habit. Courage writes the new one."

4. ESCRIBE en board el senior's base kit (lo que cubre):

     HABITS (used to + bare):
       "We used to work on paper."
       "I used to print every document."
     STATES (used to + bare):
       "We didn't use to have a CRM."
       "The office used to be downtown."
     WOULD (repeated actions):
       "Every morning I would arrive at 7 and open up."
       "We would close the shop at 8 every night."
     NEW HIRE questions:
       "Did you use to work weekends?"
       "Did the team use to be bigger?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge roles.
   Senior cubre 6 frases (habits + states + would).
   New hire hace 2 preguntas 'Did you use to...?'.
   Then SWITCH roles — different company, same shapes.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven
   a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch
   to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English.
   Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same format pero
   el new hire ahora hace 3 preguntas 'Did you use
   to...?' y el senior contrasta CADA habito viejo con
   el presente ('We used to work on paper — now we use
   the system').

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante el chat: si alguien diga
   "used to went" o "didn't used to" o "would live",
   DI suave: "Fortitude. After USED TO the verb is bare.
   After DIDN'T it's USE. WOULD is for actions, not
   states."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada chat aparezcan AL
    MENOS 4 used-to (2 habits + 2 states) + 2 WOULD
    actions + 2 'Did you use to...?' questions. Observer
    (third person of nearby pair) counts bare verbs
    maintained after used to / would.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (1 mini-onboarding completo — senior +
    new hire, 4 used-to + 1 would + 1 question).
    Observer voluntario reporta en voz alta: "I counted
    X used-to, Y would, Z 'Did you use to' — all bare."
    Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("We used to use paper — what changed?" / "Did the team use to be bigger?" / "How would you start the morning?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de senior ni new hire.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con used-to (habit + state) + would + 'Did you use to' visibles, todos bare.

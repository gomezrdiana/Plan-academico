## BLOCK 1 — OPENER (~10 min)

```
1. DI: "Welcome. Class 16 of 44. Today we combine two
   pasts. The long action — what was already happening
   — goes PAST CONTINUOUS: I WAS working. The short
   action that cuts in — the interruption — goes PAST
   SIMPLE: the phone RANG. 'I was working when the
   phone rang.' That is today."

2. [VIRTUD] activation: ESCRIBE "FORTITUDE" en board
   grande. DI: "Justice closed yesterday. Today we OPEN
   Fortitude — day 1 of 5. Fortitude is the long action
   that keeps going when something strikes it. You were
   working, the problem hit — and you pushed through.
   The grammar lives the virtue: the long action
   endures in PAST CONTINUOUS, the short strike lands
   in PAST SIMPLE."

3. [FRASE] insertion 1: ESCRIBE la frase en board.
   LEELA 2 veces en voz alta. COHORTE repite 2 veces.
   UN estudiante la dice solo.

4. [PROMPT] ("When the call came" roundup — Grammar
   pase Cl 15):
   LEE en voz alta la hoja PASE que el Grammar Teacher
   dejo ayer. PIDE: "Each person, one sentence — one
   thing you were doing (PAST CONTINUOUS) when one
   short thing happened (PAST SIMPLE). 'I was driving
   to work when my phone rang.' Quick round, no
   stopping." Da la vuelta al grupo, 1 frase por
   persona, sin corregir todavia — solo marca patrones.

5. ESCUCHA reacciones. NO entres en discusion. Solo
   marca: estos son los puentes de hoy.

6. DI (bridge): "Cl 15 you learned the long action
   alone — 'I was working all morning'. Today we add
   the interruption. WHEN points to the short action in
   PAST SIMPLE: 'when the phone RANG'. WHILE points to
   the long action in PAST CONTINUOUS: 'while I WAS
   working'. Same story, two doorways: 'I was working
   WHEN the phone rang' = 'WHILE I was working, the
   phone rang.'"

7. DI: "If I hear 'I worked when the phone rang' with
   no -ing on the long action — I say FORTITUDE, and
   you stretch the long action back to continuous: I
   WAS working."

8. DI: "Sit straight. Marker ready. We go to the board."
```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT WHEN/WHILE (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 2 columnas en board grandes:

   LONG / BACKGROUND ACTION     SHORT / INTERRUPTING
   (PAST CONTINUOUS)            (PAST SIMPLE)
   I was working               the phone rang
   She was driving             the light changed
   They were having a meeting  the manager walked in
   We were walking in the park Jeff saw a dog
   I was studying              my brother arrived

   *** The long action = was/were + -ing. ***
   *** The short action that cuts in = past simple. ***

2. SEÑALA columna LONG. DI: "The action already in
   progress — the background — is PAST CONTINUOUS.
   was/were + verb-ing. 'I WAS working.' 'She WAS
   driving.' 'They WERE having a meeting.' It has
   length. It was already running."

3. SEÑALA columna SHORT. DI: "The action that cuts in
   — short, sudden — is PAST SIMPLE. 'the phone RANG.'
   'the light CHANGED.' 'the manager WALKED in.' One
   strike. Done."

4. ESCRIBE la combinacion en board grande:

   COMBINING THE TWO

      WHEN  + PAST SIMPLE   (the interruption)
      WHILE + PAST CONTINUOUS (the background)

      "I was working WHEN the phone rang."
      "WHILE I was working, the phone rang."
      "She was driving WHEN the light changed."
      "WHILE she was driving, the light changed."

      *** Same meaning, two doorways. ***
      *** WHEN points at the short action. ***
      *** WHILE points at the long action. ***

5. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is what the phrase says — I was working
   WHEN the problem hit. The long action endures in
   continuous; the short one strikes in simple;
   courage holds the line."

6. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I worked when the phone rang."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I WAS WORKING when the
   phone rang." DI: "The long action is the background
   — stretch it to continuous. WAS working." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces en coro.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "While I worked, the phone rang."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "WHILE I WAS WORKING,
   the phone rang." DI: "WHILE always points at the
   long action — continuous." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande en board:

   QUESTIONS WITH WHEN / WHILE

      "What were you doing WHEN I called?"
      "What were you doing WHILE I was studying?"
      "Where were they going WHEN you saw them?"

      Short answer keeps the continuous:
      "I was having lunch."  /  "We were leaving."

   *** The question about the background = continuous. ***

9. SEÑALA questions. DI: "When you ask about the
   background — what was already happening — you use
   past continuous: 'What WERE you DOING when I
   called?' The answer keeps the -ing: 'I was having
   lunch.'"

10. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each. One long
    action (was/were + -ing) interrupted by one short
    action (past simple). Use WHEN. About yesterday or
    last week at work. 'I was sending the report when
    the system crashed.'" 5 minutos.

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien ponga la accion de
    fondo en simple, DI suave: "Fortitude. The long
    action that endures goes continuous — I WAS working
    WHEN the phone rang."

13. ESCRIBE una tercera seccion grande en board:

    TWO LONG ACTIONS AT THE SAME TIME

       Both go PAST CONTINUOUS, joined by WHILE.

       "WHILE I was studying, my brother was cooking."
       "What were you doing WHILE I was working?"
       "They were talking WHILE we were waiting."

       *** No interruption — two backgrounds running
           together = both continuous. ***

14. SEÑALA. DI: "When TWO long actions run at the same
    time — no short strike — both go continuous, joined
    by WHILE. 'WHILE I was studying, my brother was
    cooking.' Two ongoing tracks, side by side."

15. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "While I was studying, my brother
    cooked." -> si quieres marcar dos acciones SIMULTANEAS
    largas: TACHA "cooked", ESCRIBE "was cooking." DI:
    "Two long actions together — both continuous."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces. (NOTA: si la intencion era
    interrupcion corta, 'cooked' simple esta bien — el
    sentido manda.)

16. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "I was work when she called."
    -> TACHA "was work", ESCRIBE "I WAS WORKING when
    she called." DI: "Continuous needs the -ing.
    was + working." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

17. PIDELES en parejas: "Build 4 sentences. 2 with an
    interruption (long continuous + WHEN + short simple).
    2 with two simultaneous long actions (WHILE + both
    continuous). About a normal workday." 6 minutos.

18. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo distinga la
    accion larga (continuous) de la corta (simple) sin
    titubear y use WHEN/WHILE correctamente. DI: "Stand
    up — we go to the interview."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun ponen la accion de fondo en past simple, NO AVANCES a las dos-acciones-largas. Reduce a 1 estructura hoy (long continuous + WHEN + short simple), deja WHILE-dos-largas para callback en Cl 17. Mejor LAND 1 bien que rush 3 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo long-continuous + WHEN + short-simple y al menos 1 par de dos acciones largas con WHILE, sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW — "TELL ME ABOUT A TIME" (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW —
   TELL ME ABOUT A TIME — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the INTERVIEWER.
   One is the CANDIDATE. This is a real behavioral
   interview question: 'Tell me about a time something
   interrupted your work and how you handled it.' The
   candidate tells a true work story: what you WERE
   DOING (past continuous, the long action) WHEN
   something happened (past simple, the interruption),
   and how you PUSHED THROUGH. At least 6 when/while
   sentences. Then you switch roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This interview IS the
   phrase in action — I was working WHEN the problem
   hit, but I kept going. The long action endures, the
   short one strikes, courage holds the line."

4. ESCRIBE en board los interviewer prompts (base):

     1. "What were you working on WHEN it happened?"
     2. "What were you doing WHEN the problem hit?"
     3. "So WHILE you were handling that, what changed?"
     4. "What were your colleagues doing WHEN it started?"
     5. "WHEN exactly did you notice the problem?"
     6. "How did you push through?"

   Candidate answer model (interruption + push):
     "I was preparing the monthly report WHEN the
      system crashed. WHILE I was calling support, the
      manager asked for the numbers. I stayed calm and
      I rebuilt the file by hand."

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge roles.
   Interviewer asks the prompts. Candidate tells one
   real story with at least 6 when/while sentences.
   Then SWITCH roles — different story.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven
   a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch
   to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English.
   Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same format, ahora
   interviewer ADD 2 follow-up questions improvisadas
   usando "What were you doing WHEN...?" / "WHILE you
   were ___ing, what...?". Candidate keeps the long-vs-
   short distinction clean.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la entrevista: si
   alguien diga "I worked when it happened" sin -ing,
   DI suave: "Fortitude. The long action endures —
   I WAS working WHEN it happened."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada interview aparezcan
    AL MENOS 4 WHEN-interruptions + 2 WHILE-backgrounds
    + 1 "two long actions" sentence + 1 push-through
    line. Observer (third person of nearby pair) counts
    continuous-on-the-long-action maintained.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (1 mini-interview completa — 1 interruption
    story con 4 when/while sentences + 1 push-through).
    Observer voluntario reporta en voz alta: "I counted
    X WHEN-interruptions, Y WHILE-backgrounds — all with
    continuous on the long action." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What were you doing when the client called?" / "While you were finishing, what happened?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de interviewer ni candidate.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con long-continuous + WHEN/WHILE + short-simple + 1 push-through visibles.

```

6. ESCRIBE HOMEWORK en board (mientras la dices):

      HOMEWORK CL 16 CONV — DUE BEFORE NEXT CLASS, 7:00 AM
      TIME: 30-45 min (B1). NOT fragmented.

      (1) WRITING — 10 sentences total:
          - 6 interruption sentences (long continuous
            + WHEN + short simple):
              "I was writing the email when the client called."
              "She was driving to the office when it started to rain."
              "We were having lunch when the manager arrived."
              "They were closing the store when the alarm rang."
              "I was finishing the report when the laptop died."
              "He was presenting when the screen went black."
          - 2 WHILE-first versions of any two above:
              "While I was writing the email, the client called."
              "While we were having lunch, the manager arrived."
          - 2 two-long-actions sentences (WHILE + both

              "While I was preparing the slides, my colleague was checking the numbers."

          Hand in on paper next class.

      (2) AUDIO eyes closed — 2-3 min — "A time my work
          was interrupted" — tell one true work story:
          what you WERE DOING (continuous) WHEN something

          B1-CONV-16. Send to WhatsApp group before next
          class.

          continuous vs past simple when while English".
          Note 5 sentences with a long continuous action

      If pushback: "This is what B1 requires.

7. PASE Conv -> Grammar entregada ANTES de cambiar de

   Any question about evaluations goes to coordination
   — not to me."
```

**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** cohorte sale sabiendo la tarea + UN estudiante diciendo la frase de memoria + 5 errores en board + PASE a Grammar entregada antes de cambiar de bloque + Cl 17 anunciada.

## 3. B2 SERVICE-RECOVERY EXPRESSIONS

These are **expressions that any hospitality professional uses at B2 level**. Memorizing 4-5 of them will lift your speaking. Use them in your responses.

### Apologizing professionally
- *"I'm terribly sorry for the inconvenience."*
- *"Please accept my sincerest apologies."*
- *"This falls well below our standards — I take full responsibility."*
- *"There seems to be a mix-up with..."*

### De-escalating an angry guest
- *"Allow me to look into this for you right away."*
- *"Please bear with me while I sort this out."*
- *"Rest assured, we'll have this resolved shortly."*
- *"I completely understand your frustration."*

### Offering compensation
- *"As a goodwill gesture, we'd like to offer you..."*
- *"Your stay tonight will be on the house."*
- *"May I offer you a complimentary upgrade as compensation?"*
- *"On behalf of the hotel, please accept..."*

### Escalating up the chain
- *"Let me escalate this to my manager immediately."*
- *"I'll have the Duty Manager attend to you personally."*
- *"This requires the attention of our Reservations Manager."*

### Polite refusals / managing expectations
- *"Unfortunately, that won't be possible at this time — however..."*
- *"What I can do, however, is..."*
- *"I'll do my utmost to accommodate that request."*

## 4. GRAMMAR REMINDER — M12 + M13

Both grammar points appear in your role card as **required production**. Here's the refresher:

### M12 — Past Simple vs Past Continuous

| Tense | Use | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Past Simple** | Specific moment, completed | V2 / V-ed | *I arrived at 8 PM.* |
| **Past Continuous** | Ongoing past action | was/were + V-ing | *She was checking the system.* |
| **Both together** | Short action interrupts long action | While X was V-ing, Y V-ed | *While I was checking your reservation, the phone rang.* |

**Forced production:** in YOUR role card, you must produce at least ONE sentence with the "While X was V-ing, Y V-ed" pattern.

### M13 — Already / Still / Until

| Adverb | Meaning | Position | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ALREADY** | Something happened EARLIER than expected | Between auxiliary and main verb | *I have ALREADY informed the manager.* |
| **STILL** | Something CONTINUES when you might expect it to stop | Between subject and verb | *The technician is STILL working on it.* |
| **UNTIL** | Up to a specific point in time | Before time reference | *Wait UNTIL Friday.* / *She slept UNTIL noon.* |

**Forced production:** in YOUR role card, you must produce at least ONE sentence with **already**, **still**, OR **until** (some roles require 2 or 3 of these).

# 🛎️ YOUR ROLE — FRONT DESK AGENT

**B1 MASTERY — Cl 11 Grammar — VIP Hotel Hospitality Activity (13/05/2026)**

---

## YOUR POSITION
You are the **first point of contact** with the VIP guest at check-in. The guest meets YOU before they meet anyone else in the hotel. Your impression sets the tone.

## YOUR OBJECTIVE IN THE SCENE
1. **Welcome the guest professionally** — tone matters more than words at first.
2. **Listen to the complaint without interrupting.** Take notes.
3. **Triage the problem.** You cannot solve major issues yourself — your job is to identify WHO needs to be called (Reservations Manager? Chef? Facilities? Duty Manager?).
4. **Apologize sincerely**, even if it wasn't your fault.
5. **Escalate quickly.** Don't keep the VIP waiting.

## REQUIRED VOCABULARY — use at least 5
- check-in / check-out
- key card
- reservation confirmation
- suite / executive floor
- late check-out
- complimentary upgrade
- double-booked
- discretion
- incidentals
- walk-in guest

## REQUIRED GRAMMAR — you MUST produce both at least once

### ✅ M12 — Past Simple + Past Continuous
Use ONE sentence with: *"While I WAS [verb-ing], I NOTICED..."* OR *"When you ARRIVED, we WERE [verb-ing]..."*

**Example:** *"While I was checking your reservation, I noticed there was a conflict with another booking."*

### ✅ M13 — Already / Still / Until
Use ONE sentence with **already**, **still**, OR **until**.

**Example:** *"I have already informed our Reservations Manager — she'll be with you shortly."*

## TONE REGISTER (B2)
- Apologetic but professional
- Never defensive
- Never blame other departments out loud

**Sample phrases:**
- *"I'm terribly sorry, sir/madam..."*
- *"Allow me to look into this for you right away."*
- *"Please bear with me while I sort this out."*
- *"There seems to be a mix-up — I take full responsibility for resolving it."*

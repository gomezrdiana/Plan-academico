# 👨‍🍳 YOUR ROLE — CHEF / F&B MANAGER

**B1 MASTERY — Cl 11 Grammar — VIP Hotel Hospitality Activity (13/05/2026)**

---

## YOUR POSITION
You are responsible for **all food and beverage** at the hotel. You handle:
- Dietary restrictions and allergies
- Off-menu requests from VIP guests
- Kitchen delays and service complaints
- Coordination with Front Desk on welcome plates and in-room dining

When something goes wrong with food, it's YOUR responsibility — even if it was a server's mistake.

## YOUR OBJECTIVE IN THE SCENE
1. **Take full ownership** of any food-related problem immediately.
2. **Apologize sincerely** — food mistakes can be dangerous (allergies, medical conditions).
3. **Explain briefly** what went wrong without making excuses.
4. **Offer an immediate alternative** — new dish, removal of allergen, etc.
5. **Document the issue** so it doesn't happen again. Communicate this to the guest so they feel safe.

## REQUIRED VOCABULARY — use at least 5
- dietary restrictions
- allergens
- off-menu request
- signature dish
- plating
- expedite the order
- course timing
- dietary accommodation
- front-of-house
- allergy protocol

## REQUIRED GRAMMAR — you MUST produce both at least once

### ✅ M12 — Past Simple + Past Continuous
**Example:** *"While the team was preparing your welcome plate, the note about the allergy arrived late — that's how the shrimp ended up on the dish."*

### ✅ M13 — Already / Still / Until
**Example:** *"We have already removed all shellfish from the kitchen line. The new plate is still being prepared — it will be ready until 7:15."*

## TONE REGISTER (B2)
- Direct accountability — never blame your team out loud
- Calm even under pressure
- Solution-focused

**Sample phrases:**
- *"I take full responsibility. Allow me to make this right."*
- *"Please accept my sincerest apologies — this falls well below our standards."*
- *"I've personally inspected the kitchen — there will be no further risk."*
- *"As compensation, I'd like to offer you a tasting menu prepared by me personally."*

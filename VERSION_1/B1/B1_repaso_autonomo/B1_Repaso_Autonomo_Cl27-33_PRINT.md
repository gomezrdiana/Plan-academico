# TEMA 1 — DESCRIBIR PERSONAS (Clase 27 · Libro p.307-312)

### [1] LA IDEA
Cuando describes como es o como se ve una persona, hay 3 preguntas distintas
con la palabra **like** que NO se pueden confundir:

- **What do you LIKE?** = que te GUSTA (helado, futbol...).
- **What ARE you LIKE?** = como eres por dentro, tu PERSONALIDAD (amable, timida...).
- **What do you LOOK LIKE?** = como te VES por fuera, tu APARIENCIA (alta, ojos cafes...).

Para la apariencia usas 2 verbos:
- **to be (ser/estar)** + adjetivo -> "I **am** tall." (Soy alta.)
- **to have (tener)** + parte del cuerpo -> "I **have** brown eyes." (Tengo ojos cafes.)

### [2] LA REGLA
```
APARIENCIA (What do you LOOK LIKE?)

   to BE  + adjetivo        ->  "She IS tall."   "He IS young."
   to HAVE + parte del cuerpo ->  "She HAS long hair."  "He HAS a beard."

   Intensidad (solo UNA a la vez):
      very (mucho)  ->  "She is VERY calm."
      somewhat (algo) ->  "She is SOMEWHAT tall."

EJEMPLOS DEL LIBRO:
   "What does a calm professional look like? She is somewhat tall,
    she has steady hands, and her face is clear under pressure."
   "He has pale skin. He has brown, curly hair."
   "The woman is young and tall and slender. She has short,
    straight black hair."
```

Palabras utiles:
- **Partes del cuerpo:** hair, face, eyes, nose, mouth, ears, arms, hands, legs, feet, beard, mustache, freckles.
- **Adjetivos de apariencia:** tall, short, young, old, strong, pale, dark, blond, slender, thin, curly, straight, wavy, average height.

### [3] CUIDADO (errores tipicos)
```
X  "I have tall."            ->  OK  "I am tall."        (adjetivo va con BE)
X  "He is a beard."          ->  OK  "He has a beard."   (parte del cuerpo va con HAVE)
X  "How does she look like?" ->  OK  "What does she look like?"
X  "She has the hair short." ->  OK  "She has short hair." (adjetivo antes del sustantivo)
X  "She is very somewhat calm." -> OK "She is very calm." (solo UN modificador)
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Escribe la forma correcta (be o have)
```
1. She ______ green eyes.
2. My brother ______ very tall.
3. They ______ curly hair.
4. What ______ your teacher look like?   (do / does)
5. He ______ a big nose and he ______ short.
```
Ahora escribe **2 frases describiendote a ti misma** (una con *be*, una con *have*).

# TEMA 2 — ORDEN DE LOS ADJETIVOS (Clase 28 · Libro p.313-316)

### [1] LA IDEA
Cuando pones VARIOS adjetivos antes de un sustantivo, en ingles hay un orden
fijo. No se pueden poner en cualquier orden. Truco: usa maximo **2 o 3
adjetivos**, no 7. Menos es mas.

### [2] LA REGLA
```
EL ORDEN (siempre este):

  CANTIDAD -> OPINION -> TAMANO -> EDAD -> FORMA -> COLOR -> ORIGEN -> MATERIAL -> SUSTANTIVO
   (two)    (beautiful) (big)   (old)  (round) (black) (German)  (wooden)   (car)

EJEMPLOS DEL LIBRO:
   "I have a big black car."            (tamano + color)
   "I love my good old dog."            (opinion + edad)
   "You have two beautiful young children."  (cantidad + opinion + edad)
   "The president lives in a beautiful big white house."
   "She has long straight black hair."  (tamano + forma + color)
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "a green small English car"     ->  OK  "a small green English car"
X  "a white big beautiful house"   ->  OK  "a beautiful big white house"
X  "blue 2 pretty eyes"            ->  OK  "2 pretty blue eyes"
X  "hair long straight black"      ->  OK  "long straight black hair"  (sustantivo al final)
```

# TEMA 3 — PRONOMBRES POSESIVOS (Clase 29 · Libro p.318-325)

### [1] LA IDEA
En espanol dices "mio" siempre igual. En ingles hay DOS formas:
- **antes** de un sustantivo (adjetivo): "It's **my** car."
- **solo**, sin sustantivo (pronombre): "It's **mine**." (es mio)

Para preguntar de quien es algo usas **Whose?** (de quien).

### [2] LA REGLA
```
ADJETIVO (antes de sustantivo)  ->  PRONOMBRE (va solo)
   my    ->  mine
   your  ->  yours
   his   ->  his    (NO cambia)
   her   ->  hers
   our   ->  ours
   their ->  theirs

EJEMPLOS DEL LIBRO:
   "Whose keys are those? — They're mine."
   "Is this Sarah's marker? — Yes, it's hers."
   "Is that Miguel's motorcycle? — Yes, it's his."
   "No, it's not yours, it's mine."
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "It is my."         ->  OK  "It's mine."
X  "It's her."         ->  OK  "It's hers."
X  "The printer is our." -> OK "The printer is ours."
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Cambia a pronombre posesivo
```
1. This is my book.        ->  This book is ______.
2. That is her phone.      ->  That phone is ______.
3. These are our seats.    ->  These seats are ______.
4. Is this your pencil?    ->  Is this pencil ______?
5. That is their house.    ->  That house is ______.
```

# TEMA 4 — ADJETIVOS -ING / -ED (Clase 30 · Libro p.326-332)

### [1] LA IDEA
Este tema es importante. Hay dos formas del mismo adjetivo:
- **-ING** = describe lo que CAUSA la emocion (la cosa, el causante).
      "The movie is **boring**." (la pelicula causa aburrimiento)
- **-ED** = describe a quien SIENTE la emocion (la persona).
      "I am **bored**." (yo siento aburrimiento)

Truco: si es una COSA que produce el sentimiento -> **-ing**.
       si es una PERSONA que lo siente -> **-ed**.

### [2] LA REGLA
```
CAUSA (la cosa)  =  -ING       |   SIENTE (la persona)  =  -ED
   "The work is ENGAGING."     |     "I am ENGAGED."
   "The game was exciting!"    |     "I am excited."
   "Running is very tiring."   |     "I am super tired."

PAREJAS UTILES (del libro):
   boring/bored        exciting/excited     interesting/interested
   tiring/tired        surprising/surprised confusing/confused
   annoying/annoyed    relaxing/relaxed     frustrating/frustrated
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "I am exciting about the project."  ->  OK  "I am excited..."   (yo SIENTO)
X  "The meeting was tired."            ->  OK  "The meeting was tiring." (la reunion CAUSA)
X  "I am interesting in the project."  ->  OK  "I am interested in..."
X  "The class is bored today."         ->  OK  "The class is boring today." (si la clase causa)
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Elige -ing o -ed
```
1. The book was very ______ (bore). I fell asleep.
2. I am really ______ (interest) in science.
3. That was a ______ (surprise) ending!
4. She felt ______ (confuse) by the instructions.
5. The trip was ______ (tire), so now we are ______ (tire).
```

# TEMA 5 — CAMBIOS DE ESTADO: GET / BECOME / GO / TURN (Clase 31 · Libro p.333-339)

### [1] LA IDEA
Para decir que algo CAMBIA (antes no era asi, ahora si) el ingles usa 4 verbos,
cada uno para un tipo de cambio:
- **GET** = cambios rapidos y de todos los dias (got tired, got sick, got excited).
- **BECOME** = cambios lentos o permanentes, mas formal (become a doctor, become famous).
- **GO** = expresiones fijas (go crazy, go red, go bald, go quiet).
- **TURN** = solo para EDAD y COLOR (turned 15, turned red, turned pale).

El mas usado es **GET**.

### [2] LA REGLA
```
GET     -> rapido, diario:   "I got tired."  "She got sick."  "He got excited."
BECOME  -> lento, formal:    "She became a manager."  "He became famous."
GO      -> frases fijas:     "go crazy"  "go red"  "go bald"  "go quiet"
TURN    -> edad y color:     "He turned 50."  "The sky turned dark."

Las 5 formas de GET (del libro):
   gets / got / will get / is getting / was getting
   "The dog GETS tired. / GOT tired. / WILL GET tired. /
    IS GETTING tired. / WAS GETTING tired."
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "She become angry on Friday."    ->  OK  "She GOT angry on Friday."
X  "She went confused at the meeting." -> OK "She GOT confused..."
X  "I am turning tired."             ->  OK  "I am GETTING tired."
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Elige get / become / go / turn (en su forma correcta)
```
1. After running, I ______ tired. (pasado)
2. My grandpa ______ 80 next month. (futuro; edad)
3. If you don't practice, you can ______ crazy. (frase fija)
4. She studied for years and ______ a lawyer. (pasado; lento/formal)
5. The leaves ______ red in autumn. (color)
```

# TEMA 6 — ADVERBIOS DE MODO (Clase 32 · Libro p.340-346)

### [1] LA IDEA
Los adverbios de modo dicen COMO se hace una accion. Responden "How?".
Casi todos se forman agregando **-ly** al adjetivo:
   careful -> carefully,  quick -> quickly,  clear -> clearly.

Pero hay irregulares que debes memorizar, y la POSICION importa.

### [2] LA REGLA
```
FORMA:
   adjetivo + -ly     ->  slow -> slowly, beautiful -> beautifully
   termina en -y      ->  happy -> happily, easy -> easily  (y -> ily)

IRREGULARES (no llevan -ly):
   fast -> fast   (NO existe "fastly")
   good -> well   (NO existe "goodly")
   hard -> hard   ("hardly" significa "casi no": es OTRA palabra)
   late -> late,  early -> early,  together -> together

POSICION:
   sin objeto:   S + V + adverbio        ->  "He plays WELL."
   con objeto:   S + V + objeto + adverbio ->  "He plays soccer WELL."
   NUNCA entre sujeto y verbo:  X "He well plays"
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "She drives fastly."            ->  OK  "She drives FAST."
X  "He plays goodly."              ->  OK  "He plays WELL."
X  "She carefully organizes the data." -> OK "She organizes the data CAREFULLY."
X  "The new hire works hardly."    ->  OK  "The new hire works HARD."
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Forma el adverbio y colocalo bien
```
1. She sings (beautiful). ->  She ______________________________
2. He runs (fast).        ->  He ______________________________
3. They speak English (clear). -> They ______________________________
4. My dog eats (quick).   ->  My dog ______________________________
5. She plays the piano (good). -> She ______________________________
```

# TEMA 7 — CAUSATIVO: WANT / MAKE / LET (Clase 33 · Libro p.347-352)

### [1] LA IDEA
Este es el ULTIMO tema del libro A2. Sirve para decir que quieres que OTRA
persona haga algo. Hay dos grupos:
- **Verbos de deseo** (want, need, would like, ask, tell): llevan **TO**.
      "I want you **TO** go." (Quiero que vayas.)
- **Verbos de causa/permiso** (make, let, have): llevan el verbo SIN **to**.
      "I made her **wait**." (La hice esperar.)  "Let me **think**." (Dejame pensar.)

REGLA DE ORO del libro: en ingles NUNCA se dice "I want THAT you go".
Siempre "I want you **TO** go".

### [2] LA REGLA
```
DESEO   ->  verbo + persona + TO + verbo
   "I want YOU to go."
   "She needs ME to help."
   "The teacher needs YOU to hand in your test."

CAUSA/PERMISO  ->  make / let / have + persona + verbo BASE (sin to)
   "I MADE him stop."
   "She HAD me wait."
   "LET me think."

GET es de deseo (lleva TO):  "I got him TO agree."
```

### [3] CUIDADO
```
X  "I want THAT you confirm."  ->  OK  "I want you TO confirm."
X  "I need her call the client." -> OK "I need her TO call the client."
X  "She made me TO stop."      ->  OK  "She MADE me stop."   (make = sin to)
X  "Let me TO think."          ->  OK  "Let me think."       (let = sin to)
```

### [4] PRACTICA — Completa con TO o sin TO (verbo base)
```
1. My mom wants me ______ clean my room.        (clean)
2. The coach made us ______ 20 push-ups.        (do)
3. Please let me ______ the answer.             (know)
4. I need you ______ me before 5 PM.            (call)
5. She told him ______ the door.                (close)
```

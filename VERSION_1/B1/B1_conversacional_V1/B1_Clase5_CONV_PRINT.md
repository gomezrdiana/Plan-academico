## 2. ICEBREAKER — RUTINAS ABSURDAS (00:07–00:20, 13 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO:** Mover el cuerpo, romper el hielo del bloque, practicar Simple Present + 3ª persona -s sin presión.

**Setup (2 min):** "Everybody STAND UP. Push your chair back. Make space. I will say sentences starting with HE / SHE / IT + a daily action. If TRUE for someone YOU KNOW (mom, dad, friend, coworker), STAY standing. If not, SIT DOWN."

**Demostración (1 min):** "If your mom DRINKS coffee every morning, stay standing." Aplaude. "If your dad WAKES UP at 5 AM, sit down if not."

**Round 1 — Datos cotidianos (4 min):** Una por una, deja 5 seg entre cada una:
- "If someone in your family WAKES UP before 6 AM every day..."
- "If your best friend WATCHES Netflix every night..."
- "If your mom or dad COOKS lunch at home every day..."
- "If your boss SENDS messages on weekends..."
- "If your pet SLEEPS more than 12 hours a day..."

**Round 2 — Más absurdo (4 min):**
- "If someone in your house TALKS to themselves out loud..."
- "If your neighbor PLAYS music too loud at night..."
- "If your cat KNOCKS things off the table on purpose..."
- "If your dog WAITS for you at the door every day..."
- "If your phone DIES before 2 PM every day..."

**Cierre (2 min):** "SIT DOWN. Look around. Everyone has stories. Pick ONE absurd thing you stayed standing for. Tell the person next to you in 30 sec. ENGLISH ONLY."

> Anota errores en tu papel. Si oyes "My mom cook" sin -s, anótalo. Lo trabajas en Error Paper Live.

## 4. SIMULACIÓN — COORDINAR AGENDA CON COMPAÑERO (00:38–01:03, 25 min) — ⚠️ NO SE PUEDE SALTAR

**OBJETIVO:** Tarea comunicativa real con producto medible. Tema 100% profesional. Conecta con TEMPLANZA (planear con cuidado).

**Setup (3 min):** "STAND UP. Push chairs. Today: a real-life task. You and a partner are coworkers in the same office. You need to coordinate ONE WEEK of work. Schedule meetings, deadlines, lunch. Real. Professional. NOT pretending to be someone else — you are YOU at work."

**Materiales:** Cada pareja recibe 1 hoja en blanco para llenar la agenda compartida (Mon-Fri).

**Reglas en el tablero:**
```
AGENDA COORDINATION — RULES
- 5 working days (Mon-Fri)
- At least 3 shared meetings
- 1 lunch together (which day, where)
- 1 deadline EACH person has to meet
- Use SIMPLE PRESENT for routines: "I usually have lunch at 1"
- Use WILL for next week: "I will be in a meeting on Tuesday morning"
- ENGLISH ONLY
```

**Trabajo en parejas (15 min):** Mismas parejas del Hot Topic. "15 minutes. Coordinate. Write the agenda on the paper."

**Camina y escucha. Asigna English Points por:**
- Vocabulario rico ("conference call", "deadline", "block my calendar")
- Mezcla correcta de Simple Present + WILL
- Anchor ayudando a peer

**Presentar (5 min):** Cada pareja: 30 seg. UNA persona habla, la otra escucha. "ONE person from each pair presents the agenda. The OTHER will go in the next activity." Entrena Public Speaking en pequeñas dosis.

**Cierre (2 min):** "Sit. Best agenda? Most realistic? Vote with applause."

> **No es roleplay teatral**. No están "siendo otra persona". Son ELLOS coordinando con un colega ficticio. Si alguien dice "no quiero hacer esto", dale opción segura: "OK — coordinate with a real coworker you have. Same task." No insistas en el roleplay.

## 5. HISTORIA INTERACTIVA ABSURDA — "LA DOCTORA OLÍMPICA" (01:03–01:21, 18 min)

**Qué es:** TÚ narras una historia deliberadamente absurda con SIMPLE PRESENT + 3ª persona -s. PAUSAS en frases clave. Estudiantes REPITEN. Estudiantes AGREGAN al final con sus propias frases.

**Setup (1 min):** "Sit in a circle if you can, or face front. I'll tell you a story. When I pause, you repeat what I said. ENGLISH ONLY."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO. Pausa para que repitan.**

> "In a small town in Antioquia, a doctor named Carolina WAKES UP at four in the morning."

Para. "Say it: 'Carolina WAKES UP at four in the morning.'" Repiten. Si pronuncian mal "wakes": "No. Listen." + correcto + repiten.

> "Every day, she DRINKS three coffees before sunrise."

Para. "Say it: 'She DRINKS three coffees.'" Repiten. Pregunta: "Three coffees? Is that healthy?" Espera reacciones.

> "Then she RUNS ten kilometers to the hospital. She doesn't take the bus. She RUNS."

Para. "Say: 'She RUNS ten kilometers.'" Repiten.

> "At the hospital, she TREATS twenty patients before nine AM."

Para. "Say: 'She TREATS twenty patients.'" Repiten.

> "On Wednesdays, Carolina TEACHES medicine at the university."

Para. "Say: 'She TEACHES medicine.'" Repiten. Pregunta a la clase: "TEACHES — ¿por qué -es?" Respuesta esperada: "Because TEACH ends in -ch."

> "On weekends, she TRAINS for the Olympics. She SWIMS, she CYCLES, she RUNS."

Para. "Say: 'She SWIMS, she CYCLES, she RUNS.'" Repiten.

> "Her dog, Max, FOLLOWS her everywhere. Max KNOWS the schedule better than Carolina."

Para. "Say: 'Max KNOWS the schedule.'" Repiten.

> "But Carolina has ONE secret habit. Every Friday at midnight, she WATCHES old cartoons and EATS popcorn in pajamas."

Para. "Say: 'She WATCHES old cartoons.'" Repiten.

**Ahora estudiantes AGREGAN (10 min):** Di: "Now YOU continue Carolina's story. Each person says ONE sentence about Carolina. Use 3rd person -s. What else does she DO every day? What does she NEVER do? What does her dog Max DO?"

Señala estudiante por estudiante. 12-14 estudiantes agregan UNA frase cada uno con verbo + -s.

Si uno se queda mudo: "Take 5 seconds. ONE verb + Carolina or Max. SHE READS / HE EATS / IT SLEEPS." Si sigue mudo: "Pass to your neighbor."

Si confunden -s: "No. Listen. She READS." Repiten 2x. NO expliques la regla.

**Cierre (1 min):** "Carolina is now a famous doctor. Aplauso for Carolina." Aplauden.

> Es absurda a propósito. Memorable. Engancha. Si un estudiante propone algo subido de tono, redirige con humor: "Carolina es doctora, no haría eso, ella es muy templada."

- Class 3: Page 2 — MY MILESTONES

- **Hoy: Page 4 — MY DAILY HABITS THAT BUILD MY ENGLISH** ← cierra la fase de "recolección de materia prima" (Clases 1-5)

**Page 4 contains (escribe estructura en el tablero):**
```
MY DAILY HABITS THAT BUILD MY ENGLISH:

3 habits I DO every day that help my English (use SIMPLE PRESENT):
- Every morning, I _____________
- During the day, I _____________
- Before bed, I _____________

3 habits I DO every day that HURT my English (use SIMPLE PRESENT):
- I sometimes _____________ (this hurts because…)
- I often _____________ (this hurts because…)
- I never _____________ (and that's a problem because…)

ONE new habit I WILL add this week (use WILL + SIMPLE PRESENT):
- Starting Monday, I will _____________
- I will do it every _____________ at _____________
```

**Workshop (14 min):** Each student writes Page 4 in their personal notebook. SILENT. Camina anotando errores.

**Sharing (3 min):** 1 voluntario lee 1 sección. Class applauds. NO peer correction during sharing — espacio emocional.

> Esta página conecta directamente con la **Carta del Yo del Futuro** (sellada en Class 1). Hoy escriben los hábitos que están construyendo — su yo futuro va a leer al graduarse y verá cuáles mantuvieron.

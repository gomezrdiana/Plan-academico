**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"A professional talk comparing two real options. When we compare two things that are close, English uses equal comparatives — 'as good as', 'not as expensive as', 'almost as fast as'. You will use it before Juan Diego names it this afternoon."*

### Setup (2 min)

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural (equal comparatives), not a narrative. So today is pure speaking. The topic: compare two real things from your work or study — two tools, two jobs, two days, two providers — and be HONEST about how close they are."*

### Hot Topic debate (12 min)

**Tablero:**

```
HOT TOPIC — "COMPARING TWO JOBS / TWO TOOLS / TWO DAYS"

Discuss with your partner:
1. Pick TWO real things from your work/study (two tools, two
   shifts, two jobs you had, two days this week). Compare them
   with EQUAL COMPARATIVES:
   "Tool A is as good as Tool B."
   "Today wasn't as hard as yesterday."
   "The new app is almost as fast as the old one."
2. Name ONE thing people THINK is very different but is
   actually about equal: "People think X is much better, but
   it's not as different as they say."
3. "Be honest, don't exaggerate." Why does fair comparison
   matter at work (reports, recommendations, decisions)?

Requirements (12 min discussion):
- 6+ EQUAL COMPARATIVES (as good as / as fast as / not as
  expensive as / not as hard as / almost as reliable as)
- 1 SPECIFIC pair of things from your real work
- 1 sentence about being honest in comparisons (FORTALEZA:
  no shrinking, no inflating)
```

## 7. SIMULACIÓN — "THE TWO OPTIONS BRIEFING" (00:42–01:02, 20 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Stand up. You present two equivalent options to a colleague or a client — two providers, two plans, two shifts, two tools. You explain HOW CLOSE they are using equal comparatives the whole time, honestly."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Estructura sugerida (Tablero):**

```
"THE TWO OPTIONS BRIEFING" — 3-4 min each

You present Option A and Option B to a colleague/client. Give
6-7 honest comparisons. Use EQUAL COMPARATIVES:

POINT 1: "Option A is as reliable as Option B."
POINT 2: "Option B is not as expensive as Option A."
POINT 3: "The free plan is almost as fast as the paid plan."
POINT 4: "This supplier is as good as the other one."
POINT 5: "The morning shift isn't as busy as the night shift."
POINT 6: "Honestly, the cheaper tool is almost as strong as
          the premium one."

USE: 6+ equal comparatives (as ... as / not as ... as /
     almost as ... as) / professional context ONLY / honest
```

### Modela (3 min)

> *"Let me brief you on two options for our printing. Supplier A is as reliable as Supplier B, and Supplier B is not as expensive as Supplier A. The standard paper is almost as good as the premium paper, so for most jobs it's as suitable as the premium one. One thing: if a teacher asks anything about evaluations, midterms or finals, you do not handle that — it goes only to coordination, never to us. Back to the options: I won't tell you one is perfect; honestly, Option A is almost as strong as Option B — that's the fair comparison, and being honest about it is the strong thing to do."*

### Round 1 — Parejas (10 min)

Cada pareja: 5 min cada una. Persona A presenta las dos opciones, B es el colega/cliente + cuenta cuántos comparativos iguales usó A y si fueron honestos (¿exageró? ¿minimizó?).

### Cierre (4 min)

2 voluntarias hacen su briefing frente al grupo. Cohorte cuenta comparativos iguales.

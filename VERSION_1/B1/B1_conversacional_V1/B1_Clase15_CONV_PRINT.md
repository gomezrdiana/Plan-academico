> *"A professional talk about habits. When we describe routines, English puts the verb in -ing after a preposition ('before starting', 'after finishing', 'without asking') and uses double verbs ('I decided to...', 'I stopped...'). You will use it before Juan Diego names it this afternoon."*

```
HOT TOPIC — "HABITS THAT BUILD A CAREER"

Discuss with your partner:
1. Describe your work routine using PREPOSITION + -ING:

**Estructura sugerida (Tablero):**

```
"THE ONBOARDING CONVERSATION" — 3-4 min each

You are training a new hire. Give 5-7 pieces of real advice from
your job. Use PREPOSITION + -ING and DOUBLE VERBS:

ADVICE 1: "Before starting any task, check the schedule."
ADVICE 2: "After finishing a report, save two copies."
ADVICE 3: "Never send anything without asking your lead first."
ADVICE 4: "Decide to ask early — don't forget to take notes."
ADVICE 5: "You should stop checking your phone during meetings."

USE: 5+ preposition + -ing (before/after/without/about) /
     3+ double verbs (decide to / start / stop / want to /
     need to / forget to) / professional context ONLY

Anything about midterms or finals is handled only by coordination, never by us. That part is fair because it's not mine to carry."*

### Error Paper Live (2 min)

**4 errores típicos (anónimos en tablero; nombres SOLO en tu libreta):**

```
1. ❌ "Before to start, I check the plan."   → ✅ "Before STARTING, I check the plan." (preposition + -ing, not infinitive)
2. ❌ "I never speak without to think."       → ✅ "I never speak without THINKING." (without + -ing)
3. ❌ "I decided changing jobs."              → ✅ "I decided TO CHANGE jobs." (decide + infinitive)
4. ❌ "I stopped to arrive late."             → ✅ "I stopped ARRIVING late." (stop + -ing = quit the habit)
```

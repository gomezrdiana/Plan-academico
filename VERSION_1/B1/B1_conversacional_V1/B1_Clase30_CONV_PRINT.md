## 6. HOT TOPIC — "WHAT IS ENGAGING vs DRAINING IN MY WORK WEEK?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural (-ING vs -ED), not narrative. So today is pure speaking. Your work week — what energizes you, what drains you. Juan Diego names the technical table this morning in the second block — boring/bored, exciting/excited, tiring/tired, interesting/interested, confusing/confused, frustrating/frustrated, relaxing/relaxed, surprising/surprised, amazing/amazed, annoying/annoyed, embarrassing/embarrassed, depressing/depressed, distracting/distracted, worrying/worried. Sample patterns to model briefly:
- *"The Monday status meeting is boring. I am bored."*
- *"The new dashboard project is exciting. I am excited."*
- *"Ten hours of email is tiring. I am tired."*
- *"The new compliance rules are confusing. I am confused."*
- *"Working from Barichara is relaxing. I am relaxed."*

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "ENGAGING vs DRAINING IN MY WORK WEEK"

Discuss with your partner — describe YOUR last work
week using PARTICIPLE ADJECTIVES (-ING for the cause,
-ED for the person):

1. Pick TWO things from your week that are ENGAGING /
   EXCITING / INTERESTING — and say how YOU FELT:
   "The Wednesday client demo was exciting. I was
    excited the whole afternoon."

2. Pick TWO things that are DRAINING / TIRING /
   BORING / FRUSTRATING — and say how YOU FELT:
   "Ten hours of back-to-back meetings is tiring.
    I was tired by 6 PM."

3. Pick ONE thing that is CONFUSING or SURPRISING —
   and say who is CONFUSED or SURPRISED:
   "The new pricing policy is confusing. The whole
    team is confused."

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ pairs -ING / -ED used correctly (cause with
  -ING, person with -ED — never reversed)
- 2+ work-specific things (meetings, projects, tasks,
  rituals — NOT generic "life")
- 1 honest TEMPERANCE closure: "this is what was
  ENGAGING and this is what was DRAINING — no more,
  no less"
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE ENGAGEMENT SURVEY" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. A consultant from outside (Guest Observer pattern — 1 guest interviewer, group observer, professor coach) interviews the cohort about engagement. You answer using PARTICIPLE ADJECTIVES: 'what is ENGAGING for you?' / 'what is DRAINING?' / 'when do you feel EXCITED?' / 'when are you BORED?'. Right shape every time — -ING for the cause, -ED for the person."*

Board:

```
"THE ENGAGEMENT SURVEY" — 3-4 min per round

PATTERN VALIDATED (Guest Observer):
  - 1 GUEST (the consultant — rotates each round)
  - GROUP OBSERVER (cohort listens + counts pairs)
  - PROFESSOR (coach, never plays guest)

CONSULTANT (script — adapt to interviewee):
  Q1: "What part of your work this week was most
       ENGAGING? — were you ENGAGED?"
  Q2: "What was DRAINING or TIRING? — were you TIRED?"
  Q3: "What was CONFUSING or FRUSTRATING? — was the
       team CONFUSED?"
  Q4: "What was the most EXCITING moment? — were you
       EXCITED?"
  Q5: "What SURPRISED you? — were you SURPRISED?"
  Q6 (TEMPERANCE close): "Name the week honestly —
       was it more ENGAGING or more DRAINING? Choose
       ONE word — no inflation."

INTERVIEWEE (you):
  - Right shape: -ING for the cause, -ED for the person
  - 6+ pairs minimum
  - Professional context only
  - Temperance day 5 closure: name the week as it was

OBSERVER GROUP:
  - Count -ING / -ED pairs used correctly
  - Flag any reversed shape ("I am exciting" / "the
    meeting was tired") for the cohort to correct
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me model the cohort answer: 'The new analytics dashboard project is exciting. I am excited about it — every morning I open the file first. The Tuesday all-hands meeting is tiring. I am tired by the end. The new pricing policy is confusing. The whole sales team is confused. The Wednesday sprint was the most engaging part of my week. I was fully engaged. Honestly — naming the week — it was more engaging than draining. That is the truth, no inflation.' If a colleague asks about evaluations, midterms or finals — that goes only to coordination."*

### Round 1 — Guest Observer — 10 min

3 rounds (3–4 min each). Each round: 1 student = consultant (guest), 1 student = interviewee, rest = observer group counting -ING/-ED pairs. Professor coaches from the side, NEVER plays guest. Rotate.

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their Engagement Survey in front of the group. Cohort counts correct pairs + flags reversals ("I am exciting" → "I am excited" / "the meeting was tired" → "was tiring"). Note errors with NAMES in personal notebook.

Board:

```
PAGE 28 — WHAT ENGAGES ME / WHAT DRAINS ME (Draft 1)

Write an honest, energy-mapping narrative (130-170
words handwritten):

1. WHAT ENGAGES ME: name 2-3 specific parts of YOUR
   work / project that ENGAGE you. -ING for the cause
   and -ED for you:
   "Researching new sources is engaging. I am engaged
    when I open a fresh paper at 7 AM."
   "Talking with a real client about a real problem
    is interesting. I am interested for hours."

2. WHAT DRAINS ME: name 2-3 specific parts that DRAIN
   you. -ING for the cause and -ED for you:
   "Designing slides for 90 minutes straight is
    tiring. I am tired and unfocused after."
   "Sitting through a 2-hour status meeting is
    draining. I am drained by the end."

3. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR MY PROJECT (TEMPERANCE bridge):
   one short paragraph (3-4 sentences) connecting
   energy to plan:
   "Because research engages me, I will start every
    project session with 20 minutes of research.
    Because designing slides drains me, I will batch
    it on Friday afternoons. Day 5 closure: I name
    the energy honestly and plan around it — no
    inflation, no martyrdom."

Use -ING / -ED PAIRS correctly. At least 6 pairs total.
130-170 words handwritten. Hand in at end of class.
```

## 6. HOT TOPIC — "WHOSE IS THIS? / MINE vs OURS" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural (possessive pronouns as objects). Today is pure speaking. The topic: who OWNS what at your work. Personal things, shared things, team things, other-team things. Juan Diego names the technical table this morning in the second block — my→mine, your→yours, his→his, her→hers, our→ours, their→theirs. Today you produce it in clean professional speech."*

Sample patterns to model briefly:
- *"Whose is this laptop?"* / *"It's mine."*
- *"Is this your printer?"* / *"No, it's not mine — it's ours."*
- *"Whose are these keys?"* / *"They're hers — the receptionist's."*
- *"The meeting room is ours on Mondays; it's theirs on Wednesdays."*

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "WHOSE IS THIS? / MINE vs OURS"

Discuss with your partner:

1. Pick THREE things at your work — name OWNERSHIP
   using mine / yours / his / hers / ours / theirs
   as OBJECTS (not as adjectives):
   "Whose is this laptop? → It's mine."
   "Whose is that printer? → It's ours, the team's."
   "Whose are those keys? → They're hers."

2. Pick TWO situations where ownership IS SHARED —
   use OURS / THEIRS:
   "The meeting room on the 2nd floor is ours on
    Mondays; it's theirs on Wednesdays."
   "The coffee machine is ours — the whole floor."

3. Pick ONE thing you would NEVER claim as yours
   (TEMPERANCE day 4 — don't inflate property):
   "I would never say 'the report is mine' if half
    of it is hers and a third is ours."

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ uses of MINE / YOURS / HIS / HERS / OURS / THEIRS
  as OBJECTS
- 2+ uses of WHOSE in questions
- 1 honest "I would never claim this is mine" moment
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

### Plenary — 3 min

2 students share their 3 things + their 1 "would never claim" moment.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Listen to your phrases: 'It's mine', 'It's ours on Mondays', 'They're hers'. Possessive pronouns as objects. That structure has a name — Juan Diego gives it this morning."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE LOST & FOUND DESK RETURNS" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. Same scene as last class — but today the consigna is POSSESSION. You stand at the Lost & Found desk and claim or return lost items: 'Is this yours?' / 'Yes, it's mine.' / 'No, it's not mine, it's hers.' / 'Whose is this jacket?'"*

Board:

```
"THE LOST & FOUND DESK RETURNS" — 3–4 min each

Patterns:
CLAIM:    "Is this jacket yours?" → "Yes, it's mine."
DENY:     "Are these keys yours?" → "No, they're not mine
                                     — they're hers."
ASK:      "Whose is this wallet?" → "It's his — the
                                     engineer's."
SHARED:   "Whose is this meeting room?" → "It's ours on
                                            Mondays."
DEFLECT:  "These backpacks are theirs — the visiting
           team's, not ours."
RETURN:   "This pen isn't mine, it isn't yours, and it
           isn't his — let's leave it at reception."

Requirements:
- 6+ uses of MINE/YOURS/HIS/HERS/OURS/THEIRS as objects
- 2+ WHOSE questions
- Professional context only
- Claim only what IS truly yours (temperance)
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me brief you for the Lost & Found desk. (Picks up a jacket.) 'Is this jacket yours?' — 'No, it's not mine, it's hers — Maria left it last week.' (Picks up keys.) 'Whose are these keys? They're not mine, they're not yours — they're his, the engineer's, fifth floor.' (Picks up a laptop bag.) 'This laptop bag is ours — the team's — please leave it at the front desk.' (Picks up a wallet.) 'Whose is this wallet? It's not mine, it's not yours — let's check the photo inside.' Name property cleanly — mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs — not 'my', 'your', 'our'."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A claims (6+ items with pronouns + 2+ Whose questions); Person B is the desk colleague who returns or denies and COUNTS how many correct object pronouns A used. Use the 8–10 real objects prepared.

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their Lost & Found in front of the group. Cohort counts correct uses. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook (common: *"It is my"* for *"It's mine"*, *"Is the my"* for *"It's mine"*, *"It's her"* for *"It's hers"*).

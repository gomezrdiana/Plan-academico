### ☐ 00:42–00:54 | PRESENT SIMPLE: ESCUCHAR + REPETIR (12 min)

**Paso 1 — Escuchar la S (4 min).** El presente simple en tercera persona lleva S: "He playS." "She liveS." "It workS." Yeison probablemente no la escucha.

Di pares. Yeison dice cuál tiene S. Después di solo una frase y Yeison dice: "con S" o "sin S."

```
1. "I play" vs "He plays"             → "plays" tiene S
2. "We live" vs "She lives"           → "lives" tiene S
3. "They work" vs "It works"          → "works" tiene S
4. "I eat" vs "She eats"              → "eats" tiene S
5. "We study" vs "He studies"         → "studies" tiene S
6. "They like" vs "She likes"         → "likes" tiene S
7. "I cook" vs "He cooks"             → "cooks" tiene S
8. "We dance" vs "She dances"         → "dances" tiene S
9. "They read" vs "He reads"          → "reads" tiene S
10. "I sleep" vs "It sleeps"          → "sleeps" tiene S
```

**Paso 2 — Preguntas DO/DOES (4 min).** Di preguntas. Yeison responde con frase completa. Alterna DO y DOES. Si usa "does" mal: Di: "No." Di: "Listen. DO you play? DOES she play?" Repite.

```
1. "Do you play soccer?"             → "Yes, I play soccer." / "No, I don't play soccer."
2. "Does your mom cook?"             → "Yes, she cooks." / "No, she doesn't cook."
3. "Do your friends speak English?"   → "Yes, they speak English." / "No, they don't."
4. "Does your dad work?"             → "Yes, he works." / "No, he doesn't work."
5. "Do you like pizza?"              → "Yes, I like pizza."
6. "Does your sister study?"         → "Yes, she studies." / "No, she doesn't study."
7. "Do you live near here?"          → "Yes, I live near here." / "No, I don't."
8. "Does your best friend dance?"    → "Yes, he/she dances." / "No, he/she doesn't."
```

**Paso 3 — Yeison pregunta (4 min).** Ahora Yeison te hace preguntas a TI con DO/DOES. Dale los temas uno por uno — él tiene que formar la pregunta. Si forma la pregunta mal: Di: "No." Di: "Listen. DOES your brother work?" Repite.

```
1. Tu comida favorita         → Yeison debe decir: "Do you like ___?"
2. Tu deporte                 → "Do you play ___?"
3. Tu hermano/a y su trabajo  → "Does your brother/sister work?"
4. Tu mamá y la cocina        → "Does your mom cook?"
5. Tus amigos y el inglés     → "Do your friends speak English?"
6. Tu mascota                 → "Do you have a pet?"
7. Tu papá y el deporte       → "Does your dad play soccer?"
8. Dónde vives                → "Do you live in ___?"
```

### ☐ 00:54–01:06 | PAST SIMPLE: AYER VS HOY (12 min)

**Paso 1 — Escuchar -ED (4 min).** Muchos hispanohablantes no escuchan la terminación -ed.

- "cook → cooked" → "cooked"
- "live → lived" → "lived"
- "work → worked" → "worked"
- "want → wanted" → "wanted"
- "need → needed" → "needed"

Después di solo la forma pasada. Yeison dice el verbo base:
- "played" → "play"
- "cooked" → "cook"
- "lived" → "live"
- "worked" → "work"
- "wanted" → "want"
- "needed" → "need"

**Paso 2 — Irregulares comunes (4 min).** Escribe en un papel 8 irregulares:
```

have → had      see → saw

say → said      get → got
```

Di la forma pasada. Yeison dice el verbo base. Luego al revés.
Después: di frases con irregulares, Yeison identifica el verbo:
```
1. "I went to the store."       → went = go
```

**Paso 3 — Ayer vs hoy (4 min).** Di: "Cuéntame qué hiciste ayer." Si Yeison usa presente ("I go to school"), di: "No. Listen. Yesterday, I WENT to school." Yeison repite.

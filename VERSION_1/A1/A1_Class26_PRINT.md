## 7. LIBRO M29 — WHEN IS IT? + VOCABULARIO TIEMPO (00:25–00:45, 20 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 29 in your book. Today: how to talk about TIME. Clock, days, dates. The structures behind every appointment in English."*

### Vocabulario (7 min)

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
M29 VOCABULARY — TIME

CLOCK:
- It's THREE O'CLOCK / It's three p.m.
- It's TEN FIFTEEN / a quarter past ten
- It's TWO THIRTY / half past two
- It's NINE FORTY-FIVE / a quarter to ten

DAYS:
Monday / Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday / Friday / Saturday / Sunday

PREPOSITIONS WITH TIME:
- AT + hour:           "AT 3 o'clock"
- ON + day or date:    "ON Monday" / "ON May 14th"
- IN + month or year:  "IN May" / "IN 2026"
```

Lee. Estudiantes repiten 2x cada palabra.

> **(Aprovecha aquí para usar la Frase del Día por primera vez):**
> *"For example — 'I have an appointment AT three o'clock ON Friday.' Notice AT for hour, ON for day."* ← inserción #1

### Concepto (8 min)

**Lee EN INGLÉS desde el libro:**

> *"Module 29 — When Is It? The question we use to ask about time, day, or date. The answer uses prepositions correctly."*

**Estructuras:**

```
QUESTIONS:
- "WHEN is the meeting?"
- "WHAT TIME is it?"
- "WHAT DAY is it?"

ANSWERS:
- "It is AT [time] ON [day]."
- "The meeting is AT 3 PM ON Monday."
- "Today is Thursday, May 14th."
```

**Ejemplos modelados:**

- *"When is your English class?"* → *"It's AT 6:30 PM ON Tuesday and Thursday."* ← inserción #2 (variante)
- *"When is your birthday?"* → *"It's ON August 15th."*
- *"What time do you start work?"* → *"AT 8 AM."*

### Ejercicios del libro (5 min)

Cohorte hace 4-5 ejercicios del M29 del libro. Auto-corrección en parejas.

## 8. TIME DRILL (00:45–01:00, 15 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"15 minutes of clock + calendar in your mouth. Until you stop thinking."*

### Drill 1 — Decir la hora (5 min)

Tú apuntas a un reloj imaginario (o real). Cohorte responde en coro:

```
- "3:00" → "It's three o'clock"
- "10:15" → "It's a quarter past ten / It's ten fifteen"
- "2:30" → "It's half past two / It's two thirty"
- "9:45" → "It's a quarter to ten / It's nine forty-five"
- (siguen 10 más)
```

### Drill 2 — Días + preposiciones (5 min)

Tú dices el día, cohorte construye la oración con AT + ON:

```
"Class Monday 6:30 PM" → "Class is AT 6:30 PM ON Monday."
"Meeting Wednesday 3 PM" → "Meeting is AT 3 PM ON Wednesday."
(siguen 8 más con variaciones)
```

### Drill 3 — Parejas (5 min)

Parejas se hacen preguntas:

```
A: "When is your favorite class?"
B: "It's AT 6:30 PM ON Thursday."
A: "When is your birthday?"
B: "It's ON February 12th."
A: "What time do you get up?"
B: "AT 6 AM."
```

> **Inserción #3 de Frase del Día:** *"For example, you could answer 'I have an appointment at three o'clock on Friday' — same pattern."* ← natural

## 9. SIMULACIÓN — "MAKING AN APPOINTMENT" (01:00–01:25, 25 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Real-life scene. You call someone to book an appointment. Time + day + politeness."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Llamas a un doctor / dentista / peluquería para hacer una cita.

**Roles (rotan):**
- A = persona que llama (paciente / cliente)
- B = recepcionista (ofrece horarios disponibles)

### Vocabulario forzado

- Mínimo 3 preguntas con WHEN / WHAT TIME / WHAT DAY
- Mínimo 3 respuestas con AT + hour, ON + day
- Estructura saludo + propósito + agendar + confirmar

### Modela (4 min)

> *Tú como cliente, peer teacher como recepcionista:*
> *"Good morning, I'd like to make an appointment with the doctor."*
> *"Of course. When would you like to come?"*
> *"Is Tuesday possible?"*
> *"Yes. We have openings AT 10:30 AM ON Tuesday, or AT 3:00 PM ON Tuesday."*
> *"3:00 PM works for me."*
> *"Perfect. Your appointment is AT 3:00 PM ON Tuesday, May 19th."* ← inserción #4 (Frase del Día en versión variada)

### Round 1 (8 min) — Parejas

Parejas. Persona A llama (clínica). Persona B recepcionista. Mínimo 4 turnos de diálogo + cita confirmada.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Roles cambian + nuevo contexto

Persona B llama. Persona A recepcionista. Nuevo lugar (peluquería / dentista / oficina).

### Cierre (2 min)

1 pareja representa frente al grupo. Aplauso. Anotas errores con NOMBRE.

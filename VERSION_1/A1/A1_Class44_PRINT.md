## 6. 3 STATIONS — GRAMMAR / VOCAB / SPEAKING — 25 min — STAND UP

> *"Three stations. Eight minutes each. Grammar, vocabulary, speaking. Rotate. Warm up head, mouth, and memory before the mock exam. No pressure — no grade — just movement."*

### Setup — 2 min

Split cohort into 3 small groups (6 → 3 pairs; 4 → 2 pairs + 1 solo; 5 → 1 trio + 1 pair + 1 solo). Each group starts at a different station. Stopwatch: 8 min per station → 1 min transition → next station.

```
3 STATIONS — ROTATE EVERY 8 MIN

STATION A — GRAMMAR (the 3 tenses)
  6 mini-prompts on a card:
    "Yesterday I ___ (eat / go / study / wake up / drink / see)"
    "Right now I am ___ (speak / read / write / listen / think)"
    "Tomorrow I am going to ___ (call / visit / cook / clean / travel)"
  Each person says 1 full sentence per prompt OUT LOUD.
  Pair checks: thumbs up = ok, thumbs down = retry.
  NO writing. Only speaking.

STATION B — VOCABULARY (M1–M35 accumulated)
  1 sheet, 12 words grouped in 4 themes:
    FAMILY (mother, brother, daughter)
    BODY/HEALTH (head, stomach, tired)
    CITY (street, market, bus)
    ROUTINE (wake up, brush, drive)
  Round: 1 person says the word in English → partner says
    a sentence using it. Rotate.
  12 words / 2 people / 8 min = 1.5 words per min. Tight.

STATION C — SHORT SPEAKING (everyday)
  4 micro-topics on a card:
    1. "Yesterday after class I..."          (45 sec)
    2. "Right now I feel..."                 (30 sec)
    3. "Tomorrow morning I am going to..."   (45 sec)
    4. "My best memory of this level is..."  (60 sec)
  1 speaks, 1 listens silent. Then swap. Then next topic.
  Pure speaking — no script, no card, no notes.
```

### Rotation — 24 min

You circulate SILENT. Note literal errors in your personal notebook with NAME — for coordination, NOT for public correction. **NO interruption.** If a station stalls: ONE re-start phrase (*"Next prompt. Keep moving."*) then move on.

### Quick close — 1 min

*"Hands up — who felt the THREE tenses are alive in their mouth? Who felt vocabulary is there but slow? Who felt speaking is the easiest now?"* — visual reading only, no comments. Move to the mock.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"You just rotated three stations. That's Prudence + Temperance + Justice + Fortitude in motion. On Cl 45 I show what 10 weeks of work made me — and the Final is the celebration, not the test."*

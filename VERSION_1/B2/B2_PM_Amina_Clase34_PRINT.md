## 2. CONTENT OF THE DAY — PHASE 5 CRAFT: MONOLOGUE ARCS + SET #26 CLOSING INTEGRATED + PAIR REHEARSAL — 50 min — MIXED

**Function:** the entire block is ONE arc — micro-talk on monologue ARCHITECTURE → individual craft on the HOOK + CLOSE of each student's Why Colombia → Set #26 introduced AS the language of landing, not as a separate vocab block → pair rehearsal of the 20-sec hook + 15-sec close until each one is OWNED. Amina coaches inside the work, not from the front. 50 min, no format change.

### Stage A — Micro-craft talk: monologue ARCS (~8 min) — STAND at board

Amina at the board, the 3 standing in a semicircle. No notebooks open yet — listening, not transcribing.

> *"A 5-7 minute monologue is not five-to-seven minutes of content. It is three load-bearing structures: the HOOK is twenty seconds, the THREE PILLARS are one minute each — give or take — and the CLOSE is fifteen seconds. The audience remembers two things: the first twenty seconds and the last fifteen. The middle is what they trust IF the first and last hold. So: 35 seconds of the 7 minutes carry 80% of the load. Today we sharpen the 20 + the 15."*

Draw on board (live, three lines):

```
MONOLOGUE ARC — 5 to 7 MIN

[HOOK 20 sec]    →    [3 PILLARS, ~1 min each]    →    [CLOSE 15 sec]
   surprise            anchor + example + bridge        LAND + signal end
   anti-cliché         (anchor / example / bridge)      (Set #26 verb)
   one image                                             (CTA or echo of hook)
```

> *"The HOOK is not your thesis. The hook is the image or surprise that makes the room WANT the thesis. Last dedicated session block 7, two of you opened with a thesis statement — the room respected it but did not LEAN IN. The hook makes the room lean in. The CLOSE is not 'thank you for listening.' The close is the sentence the room will quote back. If you can say at minute 7 something the room CAN quote, you closed the turn cleanly. That is justice day 4. That is the last word."*

> *"Set #26 is six words today — all of them verbs for LANDING. Sets #22 #23 #24 #25 got you to the floor, kept you on it, and made what came out sharp. Set #26 is HOW you leave the turn. Not 'I think that's it,' not 'um yeah,' not a fade. A clean LAND."*

Show Set #26 on board — pre-written, revealed now:

```
ACADEMIC B2 SET #26 (CL 34) — CLOSING THE TURN CLEANLY
Use these as the final 15 sec verb of a turn — monologue close,
Q&A answer close, or united-class build close.
Cumulative total: 156 words.

1. LAND /lænd/ — aterrizar el punto con peso
   "Let me land this: the daily-life layer is the
   only one that survives the translation."

2. WRAP /ræp/ — cerrar limpio sin colgar
   "To wrap — three pillars, one frame: Colombia
   is asked the wrong question."

3. SEAL /siːl/ — sellar con autoridad la conclusión
   "Let me seal the point: if you keep the frame,
   the answer keeps itself."

4. HAND OFF /hænd ɒf/ — pasar la palabra con intención
   "I'll hand off here — what's the part you'd push on?"

5. YIELD /jiːld/ — ceder el turno cuando ya cerraste
   "I'll yield — I've made the case; the floor is open."

6. PUNCTUATE /ˈpʌŋk.tʃu.eɪt/ — poner el énfasis final que se recuerda
   "Let me punctuate: not 'a place to visit' — a
   place to be asked the right question about."
```

Per word: pronounce 2x → cohort 2x → definition + example named. ~50 sec each = ~5 min embedded inside this stage. Not a separate block — the words ARE the craft material for stages B + C below. *Don't drill in isolation here — drill them inside the closes you're about to write.*

**[Insertion #2]** *"What is owed is the last word — these six verbs ARE the last words. Pick one. Own it. Use it in your close in the next ten minutes."*

### Stage B — Individual craft: rewrite YOUR opening 20 sec + closing 15 sec using Set #26 (~17 min) — SIT, notebook open, Amina circulates

> *"Sit. Notebook open. You each have your Why Colombia full topic sheet — pull it out. Seventeen minutes. By the end, you have rewritten — by hand, in your notebook — the OPENING 20 seconds and the CLOSING 15 seconds of your monologue. The CLOSE must anchor in ONE Set #26 verb of your choice. The HOOK must be an image or surprise, not a thesis. I circulate. I coach in whispers, one-on-one, not from the front. The room stays quiet — this is the craft room, not the rehearsal room yet."*

Amina circulates continuously. For each student, ~5-6 min of one-on-one coaching across the 17 min. Coaching moves (use 2-3 of these per student depending on what she actually wrote):

- *"Read me your hook. Stop. Is that an image, or is that a thesis? If thesis, what's the image underneath it?"*
- *"Your close ends on 'thank you' — strip it. What's the Set #26 verb? LAND or PUNCTUATE? Land if you want the room to settle. Punctuate if you want the room to remember a contrast."*
- *"Your close currently mirrors your hook — good, but the mirror is fuzzy. Sharpen the echo: take the strongest noun from your hook and place it as the last word of your close."*
- *"Set #26 SEAL fits because your topic argues a frame. SEAL says 'this frame closes.' Try it: 'Let me seal — if you keep the frame...'"*
- *"You wrote 'in conclusion' — that's the rehearsed shape. Set #26 WRAP or LAND replaces it. WRAP if you want collegial. LAND if you want decisive."*

Internal notebook (during circulation): for each student, note WHICH Set #26 verb she chose + ONE sentence about how her HOOK still drifts (if it does) — feed into Stage C.

**[Insertion #3]** (whispered to whichever student is currently struggling): *"Justice day 4 — what is owed is no longer your first word; it is your last word. The first word can be rewritten Cl 35. The last word is what we sharpen today."*

### Stage C — Pair rehearsal of the hook + close (~22 min) — STAND, rotating pairs

> *"Stand up. Pair up. You will rehearse ONLY the 20-second hook + 15-second close — not the full monologue, not the pillars — just the two load-bearing pieces. Three times each per pair. Partner feedback after every delivery: ONE question, two checks. ONE: did the close LAND? Yes or no. Did you hear the Set #26 verb? Yes or no. We rotate pairs every seven minutes so each of you rehearses with each of the other two and once with me."*

Rotation (~22 min = 3 rotations of ~7 min):

- **Rotation 1 (7 min) — Pair AB, Amina pairs with student C.** Student A delivers hook + close 3x to B; B feedback (LAND y/n + Set #26 verb heard y/n + ONE specific image suggestion). Reverse: B delivers 3x to A. Meanwhile Amina works one-on-one with C: same protocol, but Amina names the Set #26 verb she SHOULD be hearing and isn't.
- **Rotation 2 (7 min) — Pair AC, Amina with B.** Same protocol. Each delivers 3x. A new pair, fresh feedback — the second pair catches what the first missed.
- **Rotation 3 (8 min) — Pair BC, Amina with A.** Same protocol. Each delivers 3x. By end of rotation 3, each student has rehearsed the hook + close 9 times total (3 per rotation × 3 rotations) and received feedback from both peers + Amina.

**Strict rule for the partner feedback:** the partner says LITERALLY: *"Did the close LAND? Y/N. Did you hear the Set #26 verb? Y/N. The verb was ___."* No nice-feedback. No "good job." Binary signal + the name of the verb. If she didn't hear the verb, she names what she heard instead — and the speaker drills the close one more time before moving on.

**[Insertion #4]** (Amina during one of the rotations, to the whole room): *"Last word. The room remembers the last word. Drill it like the first time you'll say it is in front of an external evaluator who has never heard you before. Because that is exactly what Cl 36 is."*

Close stage C (~30 sec, all standing): *"You each rehearsed the hook + close nine times. Block 3 next — full monologue, 5-7 minutes from memory, hook and close that you just sharpened sitting inside the whole talk. No notes. No paper. Stand where you are. Block 3 begins as soon as I draw order."*

## 3. LIVE APPLICATION — FULL 5-7 MIN MONOLOGUE FROM MEMORY · PEER Q&A · AMINA AS B1 CRITICAL — 45 min — STAND

**Function:** ONE activity, 45 minutes, no format change. Each of the 3 students delivers the FULL 5-7 min Why Colombia monologue from memory, no notes, standing in front of the other two and Amina. After each monologue: peer Q&A (2 questions from peers as B1 audience), then Amina as B1 audience with ONE critical question. Total per student ~14-15 min = (5-7 monologue) + (1 min silence/setup of Q&A) + (2 peer questions × ~80 sec) + (1 Amina critical Q&A × ~80 sec) + (30 sec recovery / transition). 3 students × 14-15 min = ~43 min + 2-3 min closing reflection.

This is the heart of the class. The teacher does NOT change the format inside the 45 min. The students internalize one rhythm — DELIVER → SILENCE → Q&A → RECOVER — three times. The repetition under audience pressure is the work.

### (3 min) Setup

Amina at the front. The other 2 students seated, notebooks open for Q&A note-taking.

> *"Three monologues. Each of you delivers the full 5-7 minutes from memory. No notes. No paper. The hook and close you sharpened in block 2 sit inside the full talk — you keep them. After each monologue: silence three seconds. Then the two peers ask one question each — your job as audience is to ask AS A B1 — non-expert framing, real curiosity, not a B2 trap. Then I — sitting in audience — ask ONE critical question of YOU as a B1 might frame it if she did NOT understand. Recovery: thirty seconds, water, sit. Next student up."*

> *"I do not stop you mid-monologue. If you blank, you recover and continue — that recovery is exactly what the external evaluator Cl 36 will read. No notes is anti-fraud — the talk is yours."*

Draw order (~30 sec — student names random). Confirm: stopwatch on, ~7 min cap per monologue.

### (~14 min) Student 1 — full delivery + 3-question Q&A

**Monologue (5-7 min):** Student 1 stands at front. Amina starts stopwatch. The other 2 take notes for their Q&A questions. Amina notes (internal):
- Did the HOOK do its job (image, not thesis)?
- Did Set #26 verb LAND the close?
- Where did "um" / "well" / "I think probably" colonize?
- Memory failure points: where did she blank? How did she recover?
- Time: 5-7 ideal, ~3-4 = under-prepared, ~8+ = rambling.

Stopwatch off at end. **3-sec silence — Amina holds it intentionally.** The silence is the first signal that the close LANDED or didn't.

**Peer Q&A (~3 min, 2 questions × ~80 sec each):**
- Peer 1 asks a B1-frame question (real curiosity, non-expert). Student 1 answers in ~80 sec — Amina watches for: did she use a Set #25 verb (clarify / specify / contextualize) to bridge to B1 register? Did she close her answer with a Set #26 verb (LAND or WRAP) or did she fade?
- Peer 2 asks a B1-frame question. Same protocol.

**Amina critical Q&A (~80 sec):** Amina as B1 audience asks ONE critical question — phrased as a real B1 might (slight confusion + push for a reason). Example shapes (pick the one that fits Student 1's actual topic):

- *"I'm B1, I'm honest, I didn't fully get pillar 2 — can you say that part again but for someone who's never been to Colombia? Use one of those Set #25 words."*
- *"I'm a B1 from the united class — you said 'Colombia is asked the wrong question' — what IS the right question, in one sentence I can use tomorrow with a foreigner?"*
- *"I'm B1, I'm not convinced — your hook was strong, your close was strong, but the middle felt rehearsed. Which pillar do YOU think is your weakest, honestly? And how would you re-anchor it right now?"*

Student 1 answers in ~80 sec. Amina watches for: did she SHAPE-FIT the answer (justice day 3 holding into day 4)? Did she LAND the answer with a Set #26 verb? Did she collapse / fluff / defend?

**(~30 sec recovery)** Student 1 sits. Water. Brief silence. Amina internal note: ONE specific gap, ONE specific win.

### (~14 min) Student 2 — same protocol

Stopwatch reset. Stand. Full monologue 5-7 min from memory. 3-sec silence. 2 peer Q&A. 1 Amina B1-critical. 30-sec recovery.

Amina watches with FRESH eyes — what worked for Student 1's hook may not work for Student 2's topic. The critical question for Student 2 is DIFFERENT from Student 1's — Amina prepares the 3 critical questions ahead (one per student, anchored in each topic) but adapts in the moment if the monologue revealed a different gap than expected.

### (~14 min) Student 3 — same protocol

Stopwatch reset. Stand. Full monologue 5-7 min from memory. 3-sec silence. 2 peer Q&A. 1 Amina B1-critical. 30-sec recovery.

Same discipline. Same intensity. By the third monologue, the cohort has SEEN the rhythm twice — Student 3 delivers into a room that already knows the protocol, which means the room can listen MORE carefully. The peer questions for Student 3 should be SHARPER than for Student 1 — Amina notes if they are; if soft, she names it during the critical Q&A: *"Peer questions softened across the three — what did you skip asking? Bring it now."*

### (~3 min) Closing reflection — STAND, semicircle

> *"Three full monologues from memory. First time in this room all three of you did it back-to-back. Each in ONE sentence: what LANDED cleanest in YOUR monologue today + what needs more reps before Cl 36."*

3 × ~30 sec. No coaching here. Pure self-report. Amina internal notebook: who NAMED a real gap vs who substituted a comfortable gap (the fake-gap pattern from Cl 33 Round 3). If anyone substitutes, Amina names it in block 4 close — not now.

Brief silence (~10 sec). *"Sit. Block 4 closes the class."*

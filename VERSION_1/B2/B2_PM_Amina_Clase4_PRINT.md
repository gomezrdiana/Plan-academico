## 3. ESTRUCTURA DEL MONÓLOGO — HOOK + 3 PUNTOS + CIERRE (19:00–19:25, 25 min)

**OBJETIVO CENTRAL DE HOY.**

**Setup (3 min):** "Today you build the SKELETON of your final monologue. NOT the full speech — just the structure. Every great speech has 3 parts."

Escribe en el tablero:
```
MONOLOGUE STRUCTURE (5-7 min total)

1. HOOK (45-60 sec) — opens, captures attention
   Options:
   - A surprising fact: "Did you know that...?"
   - A question to the audience: "Have you ever...?"
   - A story: "Last year, I was in..."
   - A bold statement: "Most people don't know that Colombia is..."

2. BODY — 3 KEY POINTS (3-5 min total, ~1-1.5 min each)
   Point 1 → Point 2 → Point 3 (logical order)
   Each point: claim + example + why it matters

3. CLOSE / CALL TO ACTION (45-60 sec)
   - "If you remember ONE thing from this, remember..."
   - "Next time you think of Colombia, think of..."
   - "I want to invite you to..."

TOTAL: 5-7 min monologue + 2-3 min Q&A live = 7-10 min
```

**Modelo (10 min):** Tú haces UN ejemplo completo. Tema: "Why study English in Colombia."

> HOOK: "Most people think you study English in the US. But what if I told you Colombia might be the BEST place to learn English?"
>
> POINT 1: "Cost. Colombia is one of the cheapest countries in Latin America for English immersion."
>
> POINT 2: "Culture. Colombian Spanish is the clearest in Latin America, and English schools here teach without distractions."
>
> POINT 3: "People. Colombians are patient teachers. They want you to succeed."
>
> CLOSE: "Next time you think 'where should I learn English' — don't think US. Think Colombia."

Pregunta: "Did you notice the structure? Hook + 3 points + close. ~45 sec each."

**Discusión (12 min):** "What makes a HOOK strong? What's the difference between a hook that works and one that doesn't?" Lluvia de ideas en grupo. Anota en tablero.

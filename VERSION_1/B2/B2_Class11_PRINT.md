### ☐ 00:00–00:20 | ICEBREAKER: MODULE ROULETTE (20 min)
**Before class:** Write numbers 1–6 on separate small papers. Fold them. Put them in a cup or bag.

**Write on the board (leave it up ALL class):**
```
MODULE KEY
1 = Question Tags
2 = Advanced Adjectives
3 = Either / Neither / Both
4 = Conditionals (2nd, 3rd, Mixed)
5 = Passive Voice + Passive Reporting
6 = Reported Speech + Reporting Verbs
```

**Write these 6 topics on the board too:**
1. Your last vacation
2. Your dream job
3. A problem in your city
4. Your best friend
5. A memorable meal
6. A decision you regret

**How it works:**
- Student picks a folded number from the cup. Say they get "4" = Conditionals.
- Teacher assigns them a topic (go in order: first student gets topic 1, second gets topic 2, etc.).
- Student speaks for 60 seconds about that topic using structures from THAT module. You time it. If they stop before 60 seconds, say: "Keep going. You still have [X] seconds."
- After they finish, ANY other student adds ONE sentence to the same topic using a DIFFERENT module. Example: Student spoke about "Your last vacation" using conditionals → classmate adds a sentence using passive voice about the same topic.
- Go around TWICE. If a student picks the same number again, they refold it and pick another.

> This warms up ALL modules. Pay attention to which modules students struggle with — you'll need this info for the report.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: MEGA EDITION (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors spanning ALL modules (10 min).** Read the story below SLOWLY. Students shout "WRONG!" when they hear an error. They must say the correct version. Award 1 point per catch. Keep score on the board.

Say: "This story has 15 errors. They come from ALL the modules we have studied — question tags, adjective order, either/neither/both, conditionals, passive voice, reported speech, and reporting verbs. Listen carefully."

Read this story (errors are marked for YOU — do NOT read the corrections aloud):

"Last week something incredible happened at my school. It was reported that a exquisite old golden trophy was stole from the principal's office. **(1 — AN exquisite) (2 — was STOLEN)**

The principal said that the trophy is missing since Monday. **(3 — HAD BEEN missing)** She warned us don't touch anything in the office. **(4 — warned us NOT TO touch)**

The police arrived. They are believed to having found fingerprints. **(5 — are believed TO HAVE found)**

A student named Carlos denied to be near the office. **(6 — denied BEING near)**

His friend said, 'I saw Carlos here yesterday.' When asked, the friend told the police he saw Carlos here yesterday. **(7 — HE HAD SEEN Carlos THERE THE DAY BEFORE)**

The detective asked Carlos, and Carlos said he would be innocent if he would have an alibi. **(8 — would be innocent if he HAD an alibi — 2nd conditional)**

If Carlos would have been at school that day, he could have taken it. **(9 — If Carlos HAD BEEN at school — 3rd conditional)**

Neither the teacher or the janitor saw anything suspicious. **(10 — neither the teacher NOR the janitor)**

Either Carlos is guilty, and the trophy will never be found. **(11 — Either Carlos is guilty, OR the trophy will never be found)**

The trophy was a beautiful Italian old silver cup. **(12 — beautiful OLD Italian silver cup — adjective order: Opinion→Size→Age→Shape→Color→Origin→Material→Purpose)**

It was said that neither the principal nor the police knows what happened. **(13 — KNEW — tense agreement with past narrative)**

If the trophy hadn't been stole, the school would still have it, wouldn't they? **(14 — STOLEN) (15 — wouldn't IT — 'the school' = it)**"

After reading: review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice.

**Pairs (10 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: tell a short story (1–2 minutes) with deliberate errors from multiple modules. Partner B: catch and correct. After 5 minutes, SWITCH." Walk around and listen. Write down good corrections AND missed errors.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | 60-SECOND CHALLENGE: MEGA BLEND (17 min)
**How it works:** Each student speaks ALONE for 60 seconds on a random topic. They must use as many different modules as possible. The class counts how many modules they hear. Most modules = winner.

**Write on board:**
```
MODULES: 1) Question tags  2) Advanced adjectives  3) Either/neither/both
4) Conditionals (2nd/3rd/mixed)  5) Passive voice  6) Passive reporting
7) Reported speech  8) Reporting verbs
```

**Step 1 — Prep (3 min).** Write 6 topics on folded papers, put in a cup: "Your city" / "Your family" / "A crime that happened" / "The perfect school" / "A decision you regret" / "A famous person." Say: "You will pick a topic and talk for 60 seconds. Your goal: use as many modules as possible. The class will count."

**Step 2 — Challenge (12 min).** One student picks a topic, stands up, speaks for 60 seconds. The rest listen and make a tally mark each time they hear a different module. After 60 seconds: "Class — how many modules did you count?" Write the number on the board next to their name. If someone is stuck, they can say "um" but cannot stop. 2 min per student (60 sec speaking + 60 sec counting/feedback).

**Step 3 — Winner (2 min).** Highest score wins. In case of a tie: the two students do a 30-second bonus round. Class votes.

**Example (teacher demos first — 30 sec):** "It is said that Bucaramanga is a breathtaking city, isn't it? Both the weather and the people are exquisite. If I hadn't moved here, I would be living in a desolate town. My friend told me that she had never visited. She denied knowing anything about it. I warned her not to miss it. Either you visit or you regret it."

> WALK AROUND writing errors. This is the final test — can they blend ALL modules in free speech? Celebrate the winner.

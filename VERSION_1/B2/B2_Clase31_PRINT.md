## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT FRAMEWORK (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande el sistema completo Phase 5:

   PHASE 5 SYSTEM — 5 CLASSES TO FINAL

   Cl 31 (today)  -> Framework + recap vocab + intro
   Cl 32          -> REFRAME role + Q&A intensive
   Cl 33          -> Monologue refinement + Set #25 PRECISION
   Cl 34          -> Full monologue memory + Set #26 CLOSING
   Cl 35          -> FULL DRY-RUN (Cl 36 conditions)
   Cl 36          -> FINAL (external evaluator — coordinación)

   MONOLOGUE STRUCTURE (memorize):
     Hook (30s) -> 3 Pillars (1 min each) -> Close (15s)

   Q&A REFLEX (3-second rule):
     Question lands -> 3 seconds max -> answer starts
     No "umm", no Spanish drift

2. SEÑALA fila por fila. DI:
   "Cl 31 today = framework. Cl 32 = REFRAME — what your role
    actually IS in front of the evaluator. Cl 33 = refinement
    of the talk + precision vocab. Cl 34 = full memory + clean
    close. Cl 35 = dry-run under Cl 36 conditions. Cl 36 = the
    Final itself. Five sessions. Map it now."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "Five classes
   before the room comes for us. Today opens that. Justice
   means we deliver the audience what we promised."

4. RECAP SET #21-23 (~12 min, central de este bloque):
   ESCRIBE en board:

   SET #21 (Phase 4) — BASE REGISTER BRIDGE (formal <-> casual)
     "If I may put it more plainly..."     formal -> casual
     "To frame this more precisely..."     casual -> formal
     6 phrases total. Use when shifting tone mid-turn.

   SET #22 (Phase 4) — BRIDGE WHEN ANSWER MISSING
     "Let me address that — though I'd acknowledge..."
     "That's a fair point — I'd qualify..."
     6 phrases. Use when the answer isn't fully there yet.
     Recover gracefully, never freeze.

   SET #23 (Phase 4) — HOLD WHEN CHALLENGED
     "I'd contend the core still stands..."
     "Let me maintain that point — even given X."
     6 phrases. Use under pushback. Hold ground.

5. PRONUNCIA cada set sample 2x. COHORTE repite 2x. PIDELE a
   un estudiante AT RANDOM que use UNA frase de cada set en un
   ejemplo workplace IMPROVISADO. Prompts en vivo:
     - Set #21: "Reframe 'the strategic alignment is suboptimal'
       for a customer who asked plainly."
     - Set #22: "Answer 'what's your projection for Q3?' when
       you don't actually have the number."
     - Set #23: "A colleague says 'your timeline is unrealistic'
       — hold without collapsing."

6. DI: "Three sets active going into Phase 5: BRIDGE register,
   BRIDGE missing answer, HOLD under challenge. Phase 5 adds
   four more: #24 TAKE the floor (Cl 32), #25 PRECISION mid-turn
   (Cl 33), #26 CLOSE the turn (Cl 34), #27 EVALUATOR-clean
   close (Cl 35). Seven sets total by the Final. Today: own
   the three you have."

7. INTRODUCE monologue + Q&A SCHEME (~10 min). ESCRIBE board:

   YOUR FINAL — Cl 36
     5-7 min monologue + 2-3 min Q&A
     External evaluator (coordinación) — first time
     hearing you. Stranger audience. Real stakes.

   HOOK ANATOMY (30 sec):
     - Question OR fact (no "today I want to talk about")
     - Names the stakes for the listener
     - Lands silence at the end (don't trail off)

   PILLAR ANATOMY (1 min each):
     - ONE claim (sharp, specific)
     - ONE example (concrete, named)
     - ONE bridge to next pillar

   CLOSE ANATOMY (15 sec):
     - Reframes hook with the answer. One sentence.
     - No "thank you for listening." Hold eye 2 sec.

8. DI: "Each of you — name your project topic out loud + ONE
   sentence on why it matters. ~30 sec each. I listen for
   CLARITY of stake — I do NOT coach the topic (Phase 4 closed)."

9. ESCUCHA. Internal notebook: topic clear y/n + stake landed
   y/n. Si alguien intenta cambiar topic, NOMBRA suave: "Stay
   with the topic. Today we open — we don't switch."

10. [FRASE] insertion 3 (cierra bloque 2): DI: "Audience is owed
    what we promised. Block 3 tests the first promise — the
    30-second HOOK. Stand."
```

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: 30-SEC HOOK ROUND (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "30-SEC HOOK ROUND"
   Debajo: "Question OR fact. Names stakes. Lands silence."

2. DI las reglas:
   "Five minutes. Notebook open. You CRAFT your 30-second HOOK
    by hand. Question OR fact opening — not 'today I want to
    talk about.' Name the stakes for the listener in one phrase.
    Land silence at the end. Five minutes. Begin."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3 (bridge a la ronda live):
   DI: "Justice in the last stretch — clean hook in 30 sec.
    The room is owed it. Craft."

4. CIRCULA durante los 5 min. NO coachees todavía. Si alguien
   te llama, DI: "Write it first. We test it next." Internal
   notebook: who took the brief seriously (writing fast) vs
   who is stuck (staring at the page).

5. RONDA 1 LIVE (~15 min): PIDELE al primer estudiante que se
   pare frente a la cohorte. DI: "Stopwatch on. 30 seconds.
   Deliver. Stop when I say stop." Cronometra. Si pasa 35 sec
   DI "Stop." Si termina en 18 sec, NOTA — too thin.

6. PEER FEEDBACK inmediato (~90 sec por hook): PIDELE a OTRO
   estudiante (no al speaker):
     "ONE thing that LANDED."  — exact phrase or move.
     "ONE thing to SHARPEN."   — specific, actionable.
   Si la feedback es soft ("it was good"), redirige: "Specific.
   Phrase or move. Not nice."

7. INTERNAL notebook por hook: Time ___ sec (target 28-32) /
   Question or fact opening y/n / Stakes named y/n / Silence
   landed y/n / Peer feedback noted.

8. RONDA 2 LIVE (~15 min, mismo orden): PIDELE al primer
   estudiante que vuelva a entregar el HOOK YA con feedback
   aplicada. DI: "Same hook, sharper. Apply the feedback. 30
   sec. Go." Ronda 2 = prueba de absorción EN VIVO.

9. PEER FEEDBACK ronda 2 (DIFERENTE estudiante): "Did the
   sharpen LAND between ronda 1 y ronda 2? Y/N. What ONE move
   stuck this time?" Binary + 1 phrase. ~60 sec.

10. [VIRTUD] activation durante el round: si un hook se desvía,
    DI suave: "Justice. The room is owed a clean hook in 30 sec.
    Land it." Reset. Misma hook. UNA repetición más.

11. CIERRE ronda (~5 min):
    DI: "Two rounds. Each of you delivered the HOOK twice. The
    peer feedback is your homework calibrator. Tonight you
    polish to a final version. Cl 35 dry-run measures all 5
    HOOKs in succession — Final-day timing."
```

**[ALERTA]** si un estudiante se congela en ronda 1 (silencio >5 sec, no arranca), NO lo rescates verbalmente. DI: "Reset. Restart the stopwatch. Begin when ready." Si congela DE NUEVO, NOMBRA NOMBRE en libreta + DI: "Sit. We work it 1-on-1 after class. Next student." NO arrastrar el bloque.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** los 5 hooks entregados 2x con peer feedback inmediata. Internal notebook con tiempos + 4 checks por estudiante.

## BLOCK 4 — CIERRE (~15 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board: "ERRORES TIPICOS DEL DIA — HOOK ROUND"

2. ESCRIBE los 5 errores típicos (anónimos):

   X Hook over 30 sec (drift)
   -> Tighten to 25 sec — cut adjectives.

   X Mid-hook drift to Spanish
   -> Stay in English. Pause if needed, don't drift.

   X Hook starts "Today I want to talk about..."
   -> Cut. Start with the QUESTION or the FACT.

   X Hook delivered sitting + soft volume
   -> Stand. Voice forward. Eyes on audience.

   X Q&A reflex 5+ sec silence after question
   -> 3-second rule. Set #22 bridge phrase if you need time.

3. COHORTE lee silenciosa (~30 sec). DI: "Each of you knows
   which one was yours. I don't name it publicly. Tonight you
   correct it in the homework."

4. [FRASE] close (~2 min):
   PIDELE a UN estudiante (al azar) que diga la frase de
   memoria CON intonation.
   PIDELE a OTRO que la paráfrase en sus propias palabras
   (acepta cualquier paráfrasis que mantenga "deliver the room
    what was promised — clean hook, firm pillars, sealed close").

5. [VIRTUD] cierre: PREGUNTA "What did you owe the room today
   that you delivered clean?" — 2 respuestas máximo, rápido,
   sin discusión.

6. ESCRIBE HOMEWORK en board (mientras la dices):

      HOMEWORK CL 31 — DUE BEFORE CL 32, 6:00 PM
      TIME: 30-45 min (B2). NOT fragmented.

      (1) WRITING (cuaderno, a mano — anti-fraude):
          Polish your 30-sec HOOK to a FINAL version.
          Apply the peer feedback received today.

          landed. Bring the page Cl 32 — guarded for archive.
          (10 min total).

      (2) AUDIO eyes closed (~3 min):
          Deliver your polished HOOK aloud 3 times in a row,
          no paper, eyes closed. ONE take total.
            - Label: B2-CL31-HOOK-V1
          (10 min total — prep + delivery + replay).

      (3) LISTENING (~10 min):
          YouTube — search "TED talk hook opening" OR
          "great speech opening line." Pick ONE talk under
          15 min. Note 5 HOOKS that landed clean — write the
          literal first 2 sentences + timestamp + WHY it
          landed (question? fact? stake-named?).
          5 lines total.
          (10-15 min total).

      RULE: not fragmented. No "I didn't have time."

7. DI: "Next class Cl 32: REFRAME the role + Q&A intensive.
   JUSTICIA day 2 of 5. The Q&A 3-sec rule we wrote today

```

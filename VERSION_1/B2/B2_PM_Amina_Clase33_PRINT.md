## 3. LIVE APPLICATION — Q&A INTENSIVE: 3 ROUNDS × 3 QUESTIONS EACH, ALL 3 ROTATE — 45 min — STAND

**Function:** ONE activity, 45 minutes, no format change. Each round has 3 questions; each student answers all 9 across the rotation. Round 1 = B1-audience explain (simpler shape, register flexibility). Round 2 = SKEPTIC pushback (hold under disagreement). Round 3 = HONEST GAP (name something you don't yet have a great answer for). No format change in the middle. Amina sits as observer most of the time, intervenes for COACHING in 30-sec interruptions between answers (not as block change). ~80 sec per answer + ~30 sec coaching = ~110 sec per question × 9 = ~16-17 min × 3 rounds is too much — discipline: ~5 sec teacher note instead of 30 sec for most, full 30-sec coaching only on the 3-4 worst gaps.

This is the heart of the class. The teacher does NOT change the format inside the 45 min. The students internalize one rhythm — QUESTION LANDS → 3-SEC START → 80-SEC ANSWER → 5-SEC NOTE → NEXT — nine times. The repetition under shape-pressure is the work.

### (3 min) Setup

Amina at the front. The other 2 students seated, notebooks open for note-taking (they listen for each other's shape-fit hits and misses across all 9 questions).

> *"Nine questions. Three rounds. Each of you answers ALL nine across rotation — Round 1 question 1 goes to one of you, question 2 to another, question 3 to the third; Round 2 same rotation shifted; Round 3 same. Each answer is ~80 seconds. You have 3 seconds to start once the question lands. You stand to answer; the other two sit and take notes. The three rounds map to three Cl 36 failure modes. Round 1 = B1-audience: same content, simpler shape, Set #25 word REQUIRED. Round 2 = SKEPTIC: push back, Set #23 hold + Set #25 qualify, do NOT collapse. Round 3 = HONEST GAP: name a real gap, not a fake one you already have a polished answer for. Round 3 is the hardest and the most Final-shaped."*

> *"I do not stop you in the middle of your 80 seconds. Between answers I take 5 seconds to note. If a gap is bad enough that the whole room needs to hear the coaching, I use 30 seconds — not more. Then next question. No notes for you. The question shapes are yours."*

Draw rotation order (~30 sec). Confirm: stopwatch on, ~80 sec cap per answer.

### (~14 min) ROUND 1 — EXPLAIN TO A B1 AUDIENCE — register flexibility under simpler shape

Failure mode at Final: rambling academic phrasing when the listener is non-expert. Cl 36 evaluator may simulate B1 to test register flexibility.

3 questions, rotated so each student answers ONE — by the end of the round all 3 have spoken:

- *"In one minute, explain your central Why Colombia claim to a B1 student who has never been to Colombia. Same content, simpler shape. Set #25 word REQUIRED — CLARIFY or SPECIFY."*
- *"Pick the most complex sentence from your talk. Rewrite it LIVE for a B1. Same meaning, B1 shape. What word from Set #25 carries it?"*
- *"A B1 in the next united class asks you: 'why should I care about your topic?' Answer in ~80 seconds without academic lift. Set #25 CONTEXTUALIZE."*

Per answer: ~80 sec. Amina internal notebook: did she SIMPLIFY without losing the point? Or did she defend the academic phrasing and miss the register? Amina coaching (only if needed, 30 sec max once per round): *"You stayed in academic register — strip the lift. The B1 student would have stopped listening at second 20. Try the first sentence again with SPECIFY: 'Let me specify in everyday terms —' then continue."*

### (~14 min) ROUND 2 — SKEPTIC PUSHES BACK — hold under disagreement without collapse

Failure mode at Final: collapse under pushback. Cl 36 evaluator may push to test whether the position holds or folds.

3 pushback questions, rotated (whoever answered Round 1 question 1 now answers Round 2 question 2 — shift, so the rotation isn't predictable):

- *"Someone says: 'Why Colombia is just tourism marketing — your talk is propaganda.' You have 80 seconds to push back. Set #23 + Set #25 QUALIFY. You may CONCEDE the weakest edge to hold the strongest core. You may NOT collapse."*
- *"Someone says: 'Your central claim has no data — it's opinion dressed as analysis.' Push back in 80 sec. Use NUANCE from Set #25. Hold core, refine edge."*
- *"Someone says: 'That's just one perspective — a Colombian abroad would disagree.' Push back. Use CONTEXTUALIZE from Set #25 to anchor your perspective, NOT abandon it."*

Per answer: ~80 sec. Amina internal notebook: did she HOLD (Set #23 + Set #25 qualify/nuance + reason) or COLLAPSE (apologize, change position, "yes you're right")? Coaching (only on the worst collapse, 30 sec): *"At second 40 you said 'maybe you're right' — that's the collapse. Set #23 hold the core: 'I'd contend the core holds even if X.' Try the second half again."*

### (~14 min) ROUND 3 — HONEST GAP — name what you don't yet have a great answer for

Failure mode at Final: rehearsed answer to an unasked question. The hardest round. Cl 36 evaluator may ask something you do NOT have a great rehearsed answer for, to test integrity under uncertainty.

3 questions, rotated:

- *"Name ONE part of your Why Colombia argument that you do NOT yet have a great answer for. Don't rehearse — name the gap. Use SPECIFY from Set #25 to be exact about WHERE the gap is."*
- *"If I asked you something tangential at the Final — something you didn't prep — what's the cleanest move? Demonstrate it now: I ask: 'What's the role of Afro-Colombian heritage in your argument?' Answer honestly with a Set #22 acknowledge + Set #25 CONTEXTUALIZE."*
- *"What's the question you'd LEAST want the external evaluator to ask you Cl 36? Name it. Don't strategize around it. Naming it is half the protection."*

Per answer: ~80 sec. Amina internal notebook: did she NAME a real gap, or substitute a FAKE gap (one she already had a polished answer for)? The fake-gap substitution is the deepest failure mode — it is what fluent students do at the Final to avoid uncertainty. Track who does it. Coaching (only on substitution, 30 sec): *"That was a fake gap — you had the answer ready. The real gap is the one that makes you uncomfortable to name. Try again — SPECIFY the actual gap, the one you'd dodge."*

### (~3 min) Closing reflection — STAND, semicircle

> *"Nine answers each. Three rounds. More shape-pressure in 45 minutes than Cl 36 will give you in a single session. Each in ONE sentence: which ROUND today exposed your weakest move + what's the ONE thing you need to drill before the next united class."*

3 × ~30 sec. No coaching here. Pure self-report. Amina internal notebook: who NAMED a real round-weakness vs who deflected ("they were all hard"). If anyone deflects, Amina names it in block 4 close — not now.

Brief silence (~10 sec). *"Sit. Block 4 closes the class."*

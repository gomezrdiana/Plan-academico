### 00:00--00:20 | ICEBREAKER: MODULE ROULETTE (20 min)

**Before class:** Write numbers 7, 8, 9, 10 on separate small papers (make 2 copies of each so you have 8 papers). Fold them. Put them in a cup or bag.

**Write on the board (leave it up ALL class):**
```
MODULE KEY
7 = Relative Pronouns (who, where, when, that, why, how, what + restrictive clauses)
8 = Reported Speech (say/tell + tense shifts + reporting verbs)
9 = Perfect Tenses (present perfect, past perfect, future perfect)
10 = Causative (have/get something done)
```

**Write these 6 topics on the board too:**
1. Your phone
2. A restaurant experience
3. Your hair
4. A car problem
5. A rumor you heard
6. Something you've never done

**How it works:**
- Student picks a folded number from the cup. Say they get "9" = Perfect Tenses.
- Teacher assigns them a topic (go in order: first student gets topic 1, second gets topic 2, etc.).
- Student speaks for 60 seconds about that topic using structures from THAT module. You time it. If they stop before 60 seconds, say: "Keep going. You still have [X] seconds."
- After they finish, ANY other student adds ONE sentence to the same topic using a DIFFERENT module from the board. Example: Student spoke about "Your phone" using perfect tenses --> classmate adds a sentence using causative about the same topic.
- Go around all 6 students. If a student picks the same number as a previous student, they refold it and pick another.

**Example round (say this to demo):** "My phone --- I've had this phone for two years. I've never dropped it, but I've scratched the screen. If I had been more careful, it wouldn't have happened." (Module 9 --- Perfect Tenses). Then classmate adds: "I had my screen replaced last month." (Module 10 --- Causative).

> This warms up ALL four modules. Pay attention to which modules students struggle with --- you'll need this to guide the rest of the class.

### 02:30--02:55 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: MEGA STORY WITH 15 ERRORS (25 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 15 errors --- from Modules 7, 8, 9, AND 10. All four. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. The person who catches the most errors wins. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Let me tell you about my neighbor, Laura. Laura is the kind of person what knows everyone in the building. **(1 --- WHO knows everyone --- 'who' for people, not 'what' --- Module 7)**

She told me that she has seen something strange the night before. **(2 --- she HAD seen --- reported speech tense shift, past simple 'saw' becomes past perfect 'had seen' --- Module 8)**

Apparently, a man who she didn't recognized was standing outside our building at midnight. **(3 --- didn't RECOGNIZE --- after 'didn't,' use base form, not past participle --- Module 7/general grammar)**

Laura said that she has lived in this building for ten years and she has never saw anything like it. **(4 --- had lived... had never SEEN --- reported speech tense shift: present perfect becomes past perfect; 'saw' should be 'seen' --- Module 8 + Module 9)**

The next morning, she has her locks change by a locksmith. **(5 --- had her locks CHANGED --- causative: have + object + past participle --- Module 10)**

She also getted her security camera installed. **(6 --- GOT her security camera installed --- irregular past tense of 'get' is 'got,' not 'getted' --- Module 10)**

The locksmith, that was a very old man, told her the locks were fine. **(7 --- WHO was a very old man --- 'who' for people, not 'that' in a non-restrictive clause --- Module 7)**

He said her that the building was very safe. **(8 --- He told her / He said TO her --- 'say' doesn't take an indirect object directly; use 'tell' or 'say to' --- Module 8)**

By the time the police arrived, Laura has already called her mother, her sister, and three of her neighbors. **(9 --- HAD already called --- past perfect for action completed before another past action --- Module 9)**

The police officer said that he will investigate the situation. **(10 --- he WOULD investigate --- reported speech: 'will' becomes 'would' --- Module 8)**

Laura told me she has already has her apartment checked by a security expert before the police came. **(11 --- she had already HAD her apartment checked --- past perfect causative: had + had + object + past participle --- Module 9 + Module 10)**

The place when the man was standing was right under her window. **(12 --- The place WHERE the man was standing --- 'where' for places, not 'when' --- Module 7)**

By next month, she will has installed five new cameras. **(13 --- will HAVE installed --- future perfect: will + have + past participle --- Module 9)**

She is getting her entire apartment to renovate next week. **(14 --- getting her entire apartment RENOVATED --- causative: get + object + past participle, not 'to + base form' --- Module 10)**

I asked her if she will move out. She said no --- this is the building where she wants to grow old, and she have never considered leaving. **(15 --- HAS never considered --- present perfect: third person singular 'has,' not 'have' --- Module 9)**

Laura smiled and said: 'I'm not going anywhere.'"

---

**After reading (5 min):** Review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version first, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice. Focus especially on errors from Module 10 (causative) --- this is the newest module and students are most likely to miss these.

**Pairs (8 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: tell a short story --- 1 to 2 minutes --- with deliberate errors from Modules 7, 8, 9, and 10. At least 2 errors per module. Partner B: catch and correct. After 4 minutes, SWITCH."

Walk around. Write down good corrections AND missed errors on your error paper.

> If the class catches fewer than 10 errors in the story, they need more integration practice. Note this for coordination.

### 02:55--03:10 | DEBATE: TECHNOLOGY --- HAS IT MADE LIFE BETTER OR WORSE? (15 min)

Say: "We're going to have a real debate. The question is simple." Write on the board:

```
DEBATE: Has technology made life better or worse?

TEAM A: BETTER (3 students)
TEAM B: WORSE (3 students)

RULES:
Every argument MUST include at least 1 structure from Modules 7-10:
- Relative clause (The people who invented social media...)
- Reported speech (Experts have said that...)
- Perfect tense (Technology has changed everything...)
- Causative (We can have food delivered to our door...)
```

**Step 1 --- Assign teams (1 min).** Count off: 1, 2, 1, 2, 1, 2. Ones = BETTER. Twos = WORSE. If someone disagrees with their team, say: "That's the point. Argue the position you're given."

**Step 2 --- Prepare (4 min).** Teams huddle. Each person prepares 2 arguments. Each argument MUST blend at least 2 different module structures. Write on the board:

```
EXAMPLE ARGUMENTS:

BETTER: "Technology has made communication faster.
The people who invented video calls have connected
families that had never been able to see each other.
My grandmother gets her groceries delivered every week.
Scientists have said that technology will have solved
most diseases by 2050."

WORSE: "Technology has destroyed privacy. The companies
that collect our data have never been honest about it.
Experts have reported that children who spend too much
time on screens have developed serious problems.
People have their thinking done by AI now --- they've
stopped using their own brains."
```

**Step 3 --- Debate (12 min).** Format:
- Team A: Student 1 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 1 responds (1 min).
- Team A: Student 2 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 2 responds (1 min).
- Team A: Student 3 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 3 responds (1 min).
- Open floor: Anyone can jump in with a final point (3 min).
- Each response MUST challenge the previous argument AND use a structure from Modules 7-10.

Say: "Challenge them! Don't just give your own argument --- respond to what they said. 'You said that technology has connected families, but research has shown that people who use social media have actually become MORE isolated.'"

If someone gives an argument WITHOUT a Module 7-10 structure, say: "BLEND! Add a relative clause, reported speech, perfect tense, or causative." They rephrase.

**Step 4 --- Vote (3 min).** Say: "Which team had the strongest arguments? Vote." Quick hand vote. Announce winner. Say: "Now forget the teams --- what do YOU personally believe? Better or worse? One sentence each." Go around. 6 students, ~20 seconds each.

> Error paper. This is free production under pressure --- errors will appear. Write them ALL down.

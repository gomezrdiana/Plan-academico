### ☐ 00:00–00:20 | ICEBREAKER: CRIME SCENE INVESTIGATION — DISCOVERY (20 min)
**BEFORE CLASS — SET UP THE CRIME SCENE.** This is mandatory. Place these items around the classroom BEFORE students arrive. Do NOT let students in until everything is placed:

1. A knocked-over chair near the window
2. A torn piece of paper on the floor (write "HELP ME" on it before tearing it)
3. ONE shoe (yours or a spare) under a desk
4. A cup with a red marker line on the rim (fake "lipstick")
5. A mysterious handwritten note on a desk that says: "Meet me at 3pm. Tell no one."
6. A "coffee spill" (brown paper or brown marker on paper) on the floor near the door

**When students walk in,** say NOTHING for 30 seconds. Let them look around. Then say: "Something happened here last night. You are detectives. You will investigate — but first, you must learn the language of investigations."

**Step 1 — Observe (5 min).** Students walk around the room. They look at ALL evidence. They take notes on paper — what do they see? WHERE is each item? They do NOT touch anything. Say: "Observe. Write what you see. Do not touch the evidence."

**Step 2 — Quick report (10 min).** In pairs, students try to describe what they see using ANY English they want. Write their sentences on paper. Say: "Describe what you see. We will come back to this crime scene later today — and you'll describe it MUCH better."

**Step 3 — Preview (5 min).** Say: "Notice how the news describes events like this." Write 3 headlines on the board: "Man Arrested for Stealing 200 Chickens." "Famous Painting Stolen from Museum." "Five Workers Injured in Factory Explosion." Ask: "What do these headlines have in common? WHO did the action? Is the WHO important, or the WHAT?" Let them answer. Say: "This is passive voice. Today you learn to use it like a detective."

> The crime scene stays set up ALL morning. They'll come back to it after learning passive voice — and rewrite their report properly.

### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 5 — PASSIVE VOICE (20 min)
**What it's about:** Passive voice = the subject RECEIVES the action (instead of doing it). Formula: Subject + BE (conjugated to the tense) + PAST PARTICIPLE.

**Tenses on the board (write these EXACTLY):**

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | "Colombia grows coffee." | "Coffee **is grown** in Colombia." |
| Past Simple | "Someone sent the letter yesterday." | "The letter **was sent** yesterday." |
| Present Perfect | "They have repaired the bridge." | "The bridge **has been repaired**." |
| Past Perfect | "They had finished the report." | "The report **had been finished**." |
| Future (will) | "They will finish the report tomorrow." | "The report **will be finished** tomorrow." |
| Modal | "You can do this easily." | "This **can be done** easily." |

Say: "Open your B2 book to Module 5. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 10 minutes."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain passive voice to you. They give you an example in EACH tense (present, past, present perfect, future, modal). If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you. If they can't give you an example in every tense, they read again.

### ☐ 01:10–01:45 | TBLT: CRIME SCENE — RETURN TO THE EVIDENCE (35 min)
**Now they have the tools.** The crime scene from the icebreaker is still set up. Say: "Remember this morning? You described the evidence in your own words. Now you know passive voice. Let's go back to the crime scene — and this time, you describe it like REAL detectives."

**Step 1 — Compare (5 min).** Say: "Take out the notes you wrote this morning. Read them. How many of those sentences were in passive voice? Probably zero. Now you rewrite them."

**Step 2 — Rewrite the report (10 min).** In pairs, students rewrite their morning observations as a FORMAL detective report. ALL sentences MUST be in passive voice. They must write exactly 8 sentences. Examples:
- "A chair was knocked over near the window."
- "A note was left on the desk."
- "Coffee was spilled near the door."
- "A shoe was found under a desk."
- "A cup with lipstick was placed on the table."
- "A piece of paper was torn and thrown on the floor."

Say: "Write 8 sentences. ALL passive voice. If you write active voice, cross it out and rewrite it. Compare with your morning notes — see the difference?"

**Step 3 — Present your theory (10 min).** Each pair reads their 8 passive sentences and presents their theory of what happened. ALL in passive voice. Say: "Tell us: what happened here? Who was involved? Use ONLY passive voice." If they slip into active voice, stop them: "Passive. The action was done TO something. Say it again."

**Step 4 — Vote and reveal (10 min).** Class votes: which theory is most creative? Most logical? Then YOU reveal "the truth." Say: "I'll tell you what really happened. A secret meeting was organized by two students. A love letter was written. Coffee was spilled when someone was surprised by a noise. One shoe was left behind when the person ran away. The note was dropped accidentally. The chair was knocked over during the escape. The paper was torn when someone tried to grab the letter." Make it dramatic and funny. Then say: "Compare your first notes from this morning to your report now. THAT is what you learned in one hour."

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG passive voice — wrong tense of "be," missing past participle, using active when passive is needed, wrong form. Read slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch.

"Something terrible happened at our school last week. A window was break by someone during the night. **(1 — was broken — need past participle, not base form)** The principal's office was entering by the thief. **(2 — was entered — need past participle, not -ing form)**

The security camera was not working because it has been damaged the week before. **(3 — had been damaged — past perfect passive, not present perfect)** The thief stole three computers. The computers didn't found until Monday morning. **(4 — weren't found — need "be" + past participle)**

A report was write by the secretary. **(5 — was written — wrong past participle)** The police called by the principal immediately. **(6 — The police were called — missing "were")** When officers arrived, they said the door had been unlock from the inside. **(7 — had been unlocked — need past participle, not base form)**

The security guard said he wasn't been told about the broken camera. **(8 — hadn't been told — wrong tense of "be")** Three suspects were been questioned by the police. **(9 — were questioned — remove extra "been")** Their fingerprints will collected tomorrow. **(10 — will be collected — missing "be")**

New cameras have installed last Friday. **(11 — were installed — past simple passive, not present perfect without "been")** The stolen computers have not been find yet. **(12 — have not been found — wrong past participle)** A reward is offer to anyone with information. **(13 — is being offered / is offered — wrong form of "offer," need past participle "offered")**

The school community was asking to stay alert. **(14 — was asked — need past participle, not -ing)** New security rules will been implemented next week. **(15 — will be implemented — "will be," not "will been")**"

**Pairs (10 min):** 5 min each. One reads a short story with deliberate passive voice errors, the other corrects. Switch roles. If they can't create their own story, they take 3 headlines from the icebreaker and tell the full story with passive errors for their partner to catch.

### ☐ 02:45–03:05 | BLENDING: PASSIVE + CONDITIONALS MASHUP (20 min)
**What is this?** Students combine passive voice with 2nd/3rd/mixed conditionals. This is the BLENDING activity — they must use structures from previous modules together.

Write these 4 prompts on the board:
1. "If the internet hadn't been invented, how would information be shared today?"
2. "If all cars were banned, how would cities be redesigned?"
3. "If plastic had never been created, how would food be packaged?"
4. "If homework were eliminated from all schools, how would students be evaluated?"

**Before they start, write 3 example sentences on the board. Read each one out loud. Point to the passive part and the conditional part:**

- **2nd conditional + passive:** "If uniforms were required at work, creativity would be destroyed." (If + past simple → would be + past participle)
- **3rd conditional + passive:** "If electricity had never been discovered, candles would have been used in every home." (If + had been + past participle → would have been + past participle)
- **Mixed conditional + passive:** "If coffee hadn't been brought to Colombia 200 years ago, our economy would be powered by something completely different today." (past cause → present passive result)

Say: "Every answer needs BOTH — passive voice AND a conditional. Look at the board. This is what your sentences should sound like."

**Prompt 1 (4 min).** "If the internet hadn't been invented, how would information be shared today?" Pairs discuss. Walk around listening. If someone uses active voice, stop them: "Passive. The action is done TO something. Say it again." If someone forgets the conditional, stop them: "Use 'if' + conditional. Look at the board."
**Prompt 2 (4 min).** "If all cars were banned, how would cities be redesigned?"
**Prompt 3 (4 min).** "If plastic had never been created, how would food be packaged?"
**Prompt 4 (4 min).** "If homework were eliminated from all schools, how would students be evaluated?"

**Share (4 min).** Each pair shares their best answer from any prompt. Class listens.

> This is the hardest activity so far. They must think about TWO structures at once. The examples on the board are their lifeline — point to them every time someone gets stuck. Be patient but firm — every sentence must have passive + conditional.

### ☐ 03:05–03:25 | STEAM: INVENTION PITCH (20 min)
**What is this?** Each pair invents a product. They describe it using ONLY passive voice: how it was made, what it's used for, and where it will be sold.

**Step 1 — Invent (5 min).** Each pair invents something. It can be ridiculous. Examples on the board: "A pillow that translates your dreams." "Shoes that charge your phone while you walk." "A pen that corrects your grammar." They write the name and a one-sentence description.

**Step 2 — Write the pitch (8 min).** Each pair writes 6 sentences about their invention using ONLY passive voice. Write this structure on the board:
- "It was designed by..."
- "It is made of..."
- "It can be used to..."
- "It was inspired by..."
- "It will be sold in..."
- "It is expected to..."

Say: "Every sentence = passive voice. If you write 'we designed it,' cross it out. 'It was designed by us.'"

**Step 3 — Present (5 min).** Each pair presents their invention to the class. ALL passive voice. If they slip into active, stop them: "Passive." They self-correct and continue.

**Step 4 — Vote (2 min).** Class votes: which invention would you actually buy?

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | VOCAB BATTLE: PASSIVE VOICE (17 min)
Write these 6 passive structures on the board: **is grown / was sent / has been repaired / had been finished / will be finished / can be done**

**ROUND 1 — Speed sentence (5 min).** Two teams. Teacher points at a structure → first team to use it correctly in a full sentence wins a point. Example: Teacher points at "was sent" → "The email was sent to the wrong person." Must be a NEW sentence each time. No repeating.

**ROUND 2 — Double passive (5 min).** Same teams. Teacher gives a TOPIC (e.g., "school," "hospital," "restaurant"). Each team has 30 seconds to say a sentence using 2 passive structures about that topic. Example (topic: hospital): "The patient was examined and the results have been sent to the doctor." Topics: school / hospital / restaurant / airport / wedding / crime scene.

**ROUND 3 — Passive story relay (7 min).** Teams alternate. One person from each team adds a sentence to a story. ALL sentences must be passive voice. First person to use active voice loses a point for their team. Teacher starts: "Last night, a bank was robbed." Next student: "The alarm was triggered by the thief." Next: "The police were called immediately." Keep going until one team makes 3 mistakes.

> Everyone STANDING. Points on the board. WALK AROUND writing errors.

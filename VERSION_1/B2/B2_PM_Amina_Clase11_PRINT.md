## 8. 🆕 REPETITION / PARAPHRASING — LAYER 6 (19:21–19:38, 17 min) — SENTADAS + voz alta

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"This is the SAFETY layer. You will get stuck in the Final — everyone does. The difference is recovering invisibly. Two tools: REPEAT to buy time, and PARAPHRASE to re-say an idea better when the first try failed."*

### Parte A — Repetition (estratégica, no nerviosa) (8 min)

**Tablero:**

```
REPETITION — buy time WITHOUT sounding nervous

ECHO THE QUESTION:
  "Why is that important? — Why is it important... well,..."
STALL PHRASES (then continue):
  "That's a good question." / "Let me think about that for a second."
  / "There are a few ways to look at this."
KEY-WORD REPEAT for emphasis:
  "It is unfair. Deeply, structurally unfair."
```

Drill (4 min): tú lanzas una pregunta hostil rápida; el estudiante NO responde de una — primero ECHO la pregunta + 1 stall phrase, LUEGO responde. 2 estudiantes encadenan: pregunta → echo → stall → respuesta con 1 Q&A pattern.

### Parte B — Paraphrasing (re-decir mejor) (9 min)

**Tablero:**

```
PARAPHRASING — say the same idea a SECOND, better way

MARKERS:  "To put it another way..." / "In other words..."
          / "What I mean is..." / "Or, more simply..."
          / "Let me rephrase that."

3 MOVES:
1. SIMPLER:  formal sentence → plain casual version
2. STRONGER: weak version → sharper, more precise version
3. SHORTER:  long tangled sentence → one clean line
```

**Drill (5 min):**
- (2 min) Tú dices una oración enredada/larga. Cohorte la PARAFRASEA en 1 línea limpia (move SHORTER).
- (3 min) Parejas: cada una dice 1 idea de SU tema dos veces — versión 1 formal, versión 2 con un paraphrase marker (move SIMPLER o STRONGER). Esto es el **bridge formal→casual** de hoy: la versión 2 es la natural/hablada.

> Inserción #2 Frase del Día parafraseándose: *"To put it another way — and to be fair — what I mean is, fairness is, more often than not, the overarching thread."*

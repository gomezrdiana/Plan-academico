### 00:35--00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 8 GUIDE 1 --- ORDER OF OBJECTS (15 min)

Say: "Open your book to Module 8, Guide 1 --- Order of Objects. Read the explanation and ALL examples with your partner. You have 5 minutes."

**First 5 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading, not chatting.

**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is a direct object?" (The thing that receives the action directly. "I bought A BIKE.")
- "What is an indirect object?" (The person who receives the direct object indirectly. "I bought MY SON a bike.")
- "What are the two ways to say the same thing?" (Put indirect object first with no preposition: "I bought my son a bike." OR put direct object first with to/for: "I bought a bike for my son.")
- "Give me an example with TO." (Expected: "I sent a letter to my mother." / "I gave the book to her.")
- "Give me an example with FOR." (Expected: "I made dinner for my family." / "I bought a gift for my friend.")

If they struggle, say: "Read it again. 2 more minutes." Then ask again.

**Quick drill --- say these sentences and ask them to say it the OTHER way:**

1. "I gave the keys to my neighbor." (Expected: "I gave my neighbor the keys.")
2. "She made her husband a cake." (Expected: "She made a cake for her husband.")
3. "He sent a message to his boss." (Expected: "He sent his boss a message.")
4. "We bought our daughter a new phone." (Expected: "We bought a new phone for our daughter.")
5. "I wrote a letter to my grandmother." (Expected: "I wrote my grandmother a letter.")
6. "They got their teacher a present." (Expected: "They got a present for their teacher.")

One sentence per student. If they freeze, say: "Try moving the person. Where does the person go?" They try again.

### 00:50--01:10 | BOOK: MODULE 8 GUIDE 2 --- SAY, TELL, ASK (20 min)

Say: "Now Guide 2 --- Say, Tell, Ask. These three words are DIFFERENT. Read the guide carefully. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. Walk around silently.

**Last 13 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is the difference between SAY and TELL?" (SAY doesn't need a person after it. TELL needs a person --- you TELL someone.)
- "Give me an example with SAY." (Expected: "He said that he was tired." / "She said it was raining.")
- "Give me an example with TELL." (Expected: "He told me that he was tired." / "She told us it was raining.")
- "Can you say 'He said me that he was tired'?" (NO. Say doesn't take an indirect object like that. It's "He said TO me" or just "He said.")
- "What about ASK --- how is it different?" (ASK is for questions. It needs a person. "He asked me if I was tired.")
- "What word do we use after ASK when it's a yes/no question?" (IF or WHETHER. "She asked me if I was coming." / "She asked me whether I was coming.")
- "Can you combine these with relative pronouns?" (Yes. "Could you tell me where the bank is?" "He asked me what time it was.")

**Write on the board --- 6 situations. Students must choose SAY, TELL, or ASK:**

1. "Your friend wants to know where the party is. Report it." (Expected: "She asked me where the party was.")
2. "Your mom gave you instructions: 'Clean your room.' Report it." (Expected: "She told me to clean my room.")
3. "A stranger on the street made a comment: 'Nice weather today.' Report it." (Expected: "He said it was nice weather." / "He said that the weather was nice.")
4. "Your boss wants to know if you finished the report. Report it." (Expected: "He asked me if I had finished the report.")
5. "Your teacher gave the class information: 'The exam is on Monday.' Report it." (Expected: "She told us that the exam was on Monday.")
6. "You overheard someone in a cafe: 'This coffee is terrible.' Report it." (Expected: "He said that the coffee was terrible.")

One situation per student. If they use the wrong verb (e.g., "She said me to clean my room"), say: "No. Listen. She TOLD me to clean my room." They repeat twice.

### 01:25--01:45 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: STORY WITH 12 ERRORS (20 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 12 errors --- all related to object order, say/tell/ask, and some relative clause errors from last class. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Last weekend I went to a party at my friend Laura's house. When I arrived, Laura said me that the party had already started. **(1 --- Laura TOLD me --- 'say' doesn't take an indirect object like that)**

I walked in and saw my friend Carlos. He told that the music was too loud. **(2 --- He SAID that the music was too loud --- 'tell' needs a person: 'He told ME' or use 'said')**

I went to the kitchen. Laura's mom had made a beautiful cake to Laura for her birthday. **(3 --- made a beautiful cake FOR Laura --- 'make' uses FOR, not TO)**

Carlos asked me where was the bathroom. **(4 --- where the bathroom WAS --- word order after ask)**

I said him to check upstairs. **(5 --- I TOLD him to check upstairs --- 'say' doesn't take a person + infinitive)**

Later, a girl named Sofia arrived. She gave to me a present and said: 'This is for Laura.' **(6 --- She gave ME a present --- no 'to' needed when indirect object comes first, OR 'She gave a present to me')**

I asked Sofia that she wanted something to drink. **(7 --- I asked Sofia IF she wanted something to drink --- 'ask' with yes/no questions uses IF/WHETHER, not THAT)**

She told: 'Just water, please.' **(8 --- She SAID: 'Just water, please.' --- 'tell' needs a person, use 'said' for quoting exact words)**

Then the music stopped. Someone said us that the speaker was broken. **(9 --- Someone TOLD us --- 'say' doesn't take an indirect object; use 'told')**

Laura's brother offered fixing it. He told: 'I know how to do it.' **(10 --- He SAID: 'I know how to do it.' --- 'tell' needs a person)**

After he fixed it, Laura sent a message for her brother thanking him. **(11 --- sent a message TO her brother --- 'send' uses TO, not FOR)**

At the end of the night, everyone asked me what did I think of the party. **(12 --- what I THOUGHT of the party --- word order after ask, no 'did')**

I said it was the best party I'd ever been to."

### 02:05--02:25 | BOOK: MODULE 8 GUIDE 3 --- TENSE BACKSHIFT (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Guide 3 --- Reported Speech Tense Shifts. This is the hardest part of Module 8. Read carefully. You have 8 minutes."

**First 8 min:** They read in pairs. Walk around silently. Do NOT help.

**Last 12 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "When do we shift tenses back?" (When the reporting verb --- said, told, asked --- is in the PAST.)
- "What happens to Present Simple?" (It becomes Past Simple. "I am tired" becomes "He said he WAS tired.")
- "What happens to Past Simple?" (It becomes Past Perfect. "I went home" becomes "She said she HAD GONE home.")
- "What happens to Present Perfect?" (It becomes Past Perfect. "I have finished" becomes "He said he HAD FINISHED.")
- "What happens to Present Continuous?" (It becomes Past Continuous. "I am working" becomes "She said she WAS WORKING.")
- "What happens to 'going to'?" (It becomes "was going to." "I'm going to travel" becomes "He said he WAS GOING TO travel.")
- "What happens to WILL?" (It becomes WOULD. "I will call you" becomes "She said she WOULD call me.")
- "What happens to CAN?" (It becomes COULD. "I can help" becomes "He said he COULD help.")

**Write on the board --- the complete tense shift chart:**

```
DIRECT SPEECH          -->  REPORTED SPEECH
Present Simple              Past Simple
"I work here"               He said he worked there

Past Simple                 Past Perfect
"I went home"               She said she had gone home

Present Perfect             Past Perfect
"I have seen it"            He said he had seen it

Present Continuous          Past Continuous
"I am leaving"              She said she was leaving

Going to                    Was going to
"I'm going to study"        He said he was going to study

Will                        Would
"I will help"               She said she would help

Can                         Could
"I can swim"                He said he could swim
```

Say: "Look at the board. This is your reference. Now let's practice."

**Quick drill --- say the direct quote, student gives the reported version:**

1. "Anna says: 'I love my job.'" (Expected: "Anna said she loved her job.")
2. "Tom says: 'I am studying for the exam.'" (Expected: "Tom said he was studying for the exam.")
3. "Maria says: 'I will visit you next week.'" (Expected: "Maria said she would visit me next week.")
4. "Carlos says: 'I have already eaten.'" (Expected: "Carlos said he had already eaten.")
5. "Your boss says: 'I can give you a day off.'" (Expected: "My boss said he could give me a day off.")
6. "Your friend says: 'I went to the doctor yesterday.'" (Expected: "My friend said he had gone to the doctor the day before.")

One per student. If they don't shift the tense, say: "No. Listen." Give the correct version. They repeat twice.

### 02:45--03:10 | THE GOSSIP CHAIN (25 min)

**Step 1 --- Prepare your story (5 min).**
Say: "Everyone loves gossip. Here's how this works. Each of you is going to tell a short personal story --- something that happened to you recently. It can be real or made up. Something interesting, dramatic, or funny. You have 5 minutes to prepare. Write down a few notes --- NOT full sentences. Your story should be 1 to 2 minutes long."

Write on the board:
```
YOUR STORY MUST INCLUDE:
- At least 3 things someone SAID to you (direct quotes)
- At least 1 question someone ASKED you
- At least 1 thing someone TOLD you to do

Example: "Last week my sister called me. She said: 'I'm getting
married!' I asked her: 'When?' She told me to keep it a secret.
My mom said: 'I already knew.' My dad said he couldn't believe it."
```

Students prepare. Walk around. If someone is writing full sentences, say: "Notes only. Keywords. You're going to TALK, not read."

**Step 2 --- Tell and report (20 min).**
Say: "Here's how The Gossip Chain works. Student A tells their story to Student B. Then Student B turns to Student C and REPORTS Student A's story using reported speech --- said, told, asked, with correct tense shifts. Then Student C tells THEIR OWN story to Student D. And Student D reports it. We go around the whole room."

Arrange students in a circle or line.

**The chain:**
- Student 1 tells their story (2 min). Direct speech.
- Student 2 REPORTS Student 1's story to Student 3 (2 min). Must use: "He/She said that...", "He/She told me that...", "He/She asked me if/whether..."
- Student 3 tells their OWN story to Student 4 (2 min). Direct speech.
- Student 4 REPORTS Student 3's story to Student 5 (2 min). Reported speech.
- Student 5 tells their OWN story to Student 6 (2 min). Direct speech.
- Student 6 REPORTS Student 5's story to the whole class (2 min). Reported speech.

After each REPORT, check:
- Did they use SAY, TELL, and ASK correctly? (Tell needs a person, say doesn't, ask uses if/whether)
- Did they shift tenses? (Present to past, will to would, can to could, etc.)
- Did they use correct object order?

If you hear an error, say: "No. Listen." Give the correct version. They repeat twice. Then continue.

**Step 3 --- Class recap (10 min).**
Say: "Now --- who can report the BEST story they heard? Tell us, using reported speech. You must use at least SAY, TELL, and ASK at least once each."

3 volunteers report a story they heard. Class listens. After each:
- Say: "Class --- did they shift the tenses correctly?"
- Say: "Did they use say, tell, and ask correctly?"
- If there's an error, the class corrects it. If they miss it, YOU correct it.

Write the best reported speech sentences on the board as models.

> This is the core production activity. Walk with error paper. Write EVERYTHING. Students should be speaking 90%+ of the time.

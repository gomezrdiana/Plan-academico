### ☐ 00:30–00:45 | BOOK: MODULE 4 — CONDITIONALS (15 min)
**What it's about:** 2nd Conditional = unreal/hypothetical present or future ("If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world" — I don't have it, but imagine). 3rd Conditional = unreal past, regrets ("If I had studied harder, I would have passed" — I didn't study, so I failed). Formula: 2nd = If + past simple, would + base verb. 3rd = If + past perfect, would have + past participle.

Say: "Open your B1 book to Module 4. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 8 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain 2nd and 3rd conditional to you. They give you examples. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you.

### ☐ 01:05–01:45 | TBLT: LIFE SWAP (40 min)
**How it works:** Each student gets a character card with a life situation completely different from theirs. They must "become" that person and have conversations using 2nd and 3rd conditionals to talk about their real vs. imaginary life.

**Character cards (write on paper before class):**
1. "You are a 65-year-old retired astronaut who regrets never having a family."
2. "You are a 19-year-old billionaire who inherited everything but has no real friends."
3. "You are a famous chef who is secretly allergic to most foods."
4. "You are a doctor in a small village who dreams of being a rock star."
5. "You are a taxi driver who speaks 8 languages but never left your city."
6. "You are a professional athlete who just lost the ability to walk."

**Read cards (3 min).** Each student reads their card silently. They can ask vocabulary questions.
**Prepare (5 min).** Each student writes 5 sentences about their character using 2nd and 3rd conditional. Ex: "If I hadn't become an astronaut, I would have had more time for a family." "If I could go back, I would choose differently."
**Mingle (20 min).** Students walk around as their characters. They meet each other, introduce themselves, share regrets (3rd conditional) and wishes (2nd conditional). Every 4 min, clap — they find a new partner. 5 rotations.
**Debrief (12 min).** Each student tells the class about the most interesting person they met. "I met a taxi driver who said if he had traveled more, he would have..." Class corrects errors together.

> Walk around WITH error paper. WRITE DOWN every error you hear.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG conditional usage — wrong tense in the if-clause, wrong form after would, mixing 2nd and 3rd incorrectly. Read slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch.

"Yesterday I was thinking about my life. If I am taller, I would play basketball professionally. **(1 — If I were taller / was taller)** When I was young, if I would study harder, I would have passed my exams. **(2 — if I had studied harder)**

My friend Laura is a doctor. She always says, 'If I didn't study medicine, I would have become an artist.' **(3 — If I hadn't studied — it's past, so 3rd conditional)** But honestly, if she would be an artist, she wouldn't make any money. **(4 — if she were an artist)**

Last summer I almost went to Japan. If I would have saved more money, I would have gone. **(5 — If I had saved)** My brother went instead. He said, 'If I didn't go, I would have regretted it forever.' **(6 — If I hadn't gone)**

If I will win the lottery tomorrow, I would buy a house. **(7 — If I won)** I told my boss, 'If you would give me a raise, I would work harder.' **(8 — If you gave me)** He said, 'If you worked harder last year, I would give you one now.' **(9 — If you had worked harder last year, I would have given)**

My sister moved to Canada. If she wouldn't have moved, she would still be unemployed. **(10 — If she hadn't moved)** She told me, 'If I would know how cold it is, I would have brought more clothes.' **(11 — If I had known)**

If I could travel anywhere, I would have gone to Mars. **(12 — I would go — it's hypothetical present, not past)** If I met a time traveler, I will ask about the future. **(13 — I would ask)** If I hadn't eaten that sushi yesterday, I didn't get sick. **(14 — I wouldn't have gotten sick)** And if I was feeling better right now, I will go to the party tonight. **(15 — I would go)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** 5 min each. One makes deliberate conditional errors, the other corrects. Switch roles.

### ☐ 02:45–03:05 | TRANSLANGUAGING: REGRET CONFESSIONS (20 min)
**What is this?** This is the ONLY activity where students can briefly use Spanish — as a BRIDGE, not a crutch. Students get 30 seconds of Spanish to understand/brainstorm, then they MUST switch to English and present their answer using 2nd and 3rd conditionals.

**How to do it:** Read one prompt. Pairs get 30 sec in Spanish to process. Then 3-4 min in English to discuss and present. Each pair shares their answer with the class. They MUST use conditionals. If they don't, stop them: "Use a conditional." 3 prompts total.

**Prompt 1:** "Think of something you regret from your school years. What would you change? What would have happened differently?"
**Prompt 2:** "If you could have any superpower, what would it be? What would you do with it? What wouldn't you do?"
**Prompt 3:** "If you had been born in a different country, how would your life be different? What would you have missed?"

> The Spanish is only to unblock understanding. The moment they have the idea, it's English only.

### ☐ 03:05–03:25 | STEAM: CAUSE & EFFECT CHAIN (20 min)
**What is this?** Students build a chain of hypothetical consequences using conditionals. One scenario leads to another, creating a butterfly effect. This combines logical thinking with grammar practice.

**How to do it:** Write the starting scenario on the board. Student 1 says a consequence using a conditional. Student 2 takes that consequence and builds another one. The chain keeps going. 3 chains total.

**Chain 1:** "If all phones disappeared tomorrow..."
Example: Student 1: "If all phones disappeared, people would talk to each other more." Student 2: "If people talked to each other more, they would make more friends." Student 3: "If they made more friends, they would be happier." Keep going until it gets absurd — that's the fun part.

**Chain 2:** "If humans could breathe underwater..."
**Chain 3:** "If nobody had to sleep..."

Each chain: 1 min to think, then go around 2-3 times. After all 3 chains, class votes: which chain had the most creative ending?

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | SPEED DATING: WHAT IF? (17 min)
2 rows of chairs facing each other (or standing). Teacher gives a scenario. Students have 2 min to discuss using conditionals. Then one row shifts. 6 rounds.

1. "If you could live in any century, which would you choose and why?"
2. "If you hadn't learned English, how would your life be different?"
3. "If you could have dinner with anyone dead or alive, who would it be?"
4. "If you had been born the opposite gender, what would have been different?"
5. "If you could change one decision from your past, what would it be?"
6. "If money didn't exist, how would the world work?"

> WALK AROUND writing errors. Everyone standing = more energy.

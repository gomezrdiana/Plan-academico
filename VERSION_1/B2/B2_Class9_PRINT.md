### ☐ 00:00–00:15 | ICEBREAKER: INTERVIEW & REPORT (15 min)
**How it works:** Pairs interview each other with 3 personal questions. Then each student REPORTS what their partner said to the class. This naturally introduces reported speech — they'll be doing it before they even know the grammar name.

**Write these 3 questions on the board:**
1. "What is the best decision you ever made?"
2. "What is something most people don't know about you?"
3. "If you could change one thing about your past, what would it be?"

**Step 1 — Interview (5 min).** Pairs. Student A asks all 3 questions. Student B answers. Student A takes NOTES (key words, not full sentences). Then switch — Student B asks, Student A answers. 2.5 min each.

**Step 2 — Report (10 min).** Each student stands and reports what their partner said. They CANNOT read their partner's exact words — they must convert to third person. Example: Partner said "I moved to Bucaramanga when I was 10" → Student reports: "She told me that she had moved to Bucaramanga when she was 10."

Say: "Notice what you're doing — you're changing the words. 'I' becomes 'she.' 'Moved' becomes 'had moved.' This is reported speech. Today you learn the rules."

> Don't correct tense shifts yet — just let them try naturally. The book will formalize it next.

### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 6 — REPORTED SPEECH (20 min)
**What it's about:** Converting direct speech to reported speech. Tense shift back: present → past, past → past perfect, will → would, can → could. Pronoun changes. Time expression changes (today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, yesterday → the day before, here → there, this → that). Example: "I love pizza" → She said she loved pizza. "I will come tomorrow" → He said he would come the next day. "I have been waiting" → She said she had been waiting.

Say: "Open your B1 book to Module 6. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 10 minutes."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain the tense changes to you. They give you 3 examples — one with a tense shift, one with a pronoun change, one with a time expression change. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you. If they can't explain the tense shift rule, they read again.

### ☐ 01:10–01:45 | TBLT: GOSSIP FACTORY (35 min)
**How it works:** Students write secrets, then report them to each other using reported speech. Then they try to guess who wrote each secret.

**Step 1 — Secret sharing (5 min).** Give each student a blank piece of paper. Say: "Write 3 secrets about yourself. They can be real OR invented. Do NOT put your name on the paper." Write these examples on the board:
- "I once ate 15 tacos in one sitting."
- "I secretly want to move to Japan."
- "I almost became a firefighter."
Students write 3 secrets. Fold the papers. Collect all 6 papers and put them in a pile on the desk.

**Step 2 — Read & Report (10 min).** Each student picks a random paper from the pile. If they get their own, they put it back and pick another. Say: "Read the 3 secrets silently. Now walk around the room and REPORT these secrets to other students using reported speech. Say: 'Someone in this class said that they had once eaten 15 tacos.' 'One person told me that they secretly wanted to move to Japan.' Do NOT say whose secrets they are — you don't know." Students mingle for 10 min, reporting to at least 3 different classmates.

**Step 3 — Detective (10 min).** Say: "Sit down. Now — who wrote what? Go around one by one. Each person guesses 2 secrets." Model: "I think Maria said she had almost become a firefighter, didn't she?" They MUST use reported speech + question tags. If they say "I think Maria almost became a firefighter" — stop them: "Use reported speech. 'I think Maria SAID she had...'"

**Step 4 — Reveal (10 min).** Each student reveals their own secrets. The class reacts using reported speech: "So YOU were the one who said you had eaten 15 tacos!" "I knew it! You told us you wanted to move to Japan!" Practice converting back and forth between direct and reported speech for 2-3 secrets per student.

> Walk around WITH error paper. WRITE DOWN every reported speech error — wrong tense shift, missing pronoun change, wrong time expression.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG reported speech — wrong tense shift, wrong pronoun, wrong time expression, forgetting to change tense. Read the story slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch. Write the FULL story on the board or print it — students MUST see it.

"My friend Carlos told me about his crazy weekend. He said, 'I go to the beach yesterday.' **(1 — He said he had gone to the beach the day before)** His girlfriend Maria told me she will come too. **(2 — told me she would come / had come too)**

Carlos said that he is very tired right now. **(3 — was very tired at that moment)** Maria told him, 'I can't believe you forgot the sunscreen.' He told me that Maria said she can't believe he forgot the sunscreen. **(4 — couldn't believe)**

He said they arrived at the beach and it is raining. **(5 — was raining)** Maria told him that she want to leave. **(6 — wanted to leave)** He said 'I told her we should stay here.' **(7 — stay there, not here)**

Carlos said that yesterday was the worst day ever. **(8 — the day before, not yesterday)** He said, 'I will never go to the beach again.' When I asked him today, he told me he will never go again. **(9 — would never go)**

Maria told her friend that Carlos don't know how to plan. **(10 — didn't know)** Her friend said that it is not Carlos's fault. **(11 — was not)** Maria said she tells him every time to check the weather. **(12 — told him)** She said, 'I am going to break up with him tomorrow.' Her friend told me that Maria said she is going to break up with him tomorrow. **(13 — was going to break up with him the next day)**

Carlos told me, 'I buy her flowers this morning.' **(14 — He told me he had bought her flowers that morning)** He said everything will be fine now. **(15 — would be fine then)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** One student says direct quotes, the other converts to reported speech. 5 min each. Switch roles. Example: Student A says "I love chocolate" → Student B says "She said she loved chocolate." Go through at least 8 sentences each.

### ☐ 02:45–03:05 | BLENDING: REPORT THE DEBATE (20 min)
**What is this?** Blending reported speech + conditionals + passive + question tags. Students debate, then report what their partner said using multiple modules.

Write these 3 statements on the board:
1. "Money is the most important thing in life."
2. "If social media hadn't been invented, people would be happier."
3. "It is believed that robots will replace all teachers."

**Step 1 — Debate (5 min).** Assign one statement per pair. Pairs debate it — one FOR, one AGAINST. They take notes on what their partner says.

**Step 2 — Report (10 min).** Each student moves to a DIFFERENT pair and reports what their original partner said. They MUST blend reported speech + at least 2 other modules from M1–M5B. Model on the board: "My partner said that if social media hadn't been invented, people would be happier. She argued that neither Instagram nor TikTok were necessary, didn't she?" If a student only uses reported speech without blending, stop them: "Add a question tag. Add a conditional. Add passive."

**Step 3 — Share (5 min).** Each student shares the most interesting argument they heard with the whole class. Class discusses — agree or disagree?

> Walk around with error paper during Steps 1 and 2.

### ☐ 03:05–03:25 | TRANSLANGUAGING: FAMILY STORIES (20 min)
**What is this?** This is the ONLY activity where students can briefly use Spanish — as a BRIDGE, not a crutch. Students get 30 seconds of Spanish to understand/brainstorm, then they MUST switch to English and present their answer using reported speech.

**How to do it:** Read one prompt. Pairs get 30 sec in Spanish to process. Then 3-4 min in English to discuss and present. Each pair shares their answer with the class. They MUST use reported speech with correct tense shifts. If they don't shift tenses, stop them: "Shift the tense. He SAID he..." 3 prompts total.

**Prompt 1:** "Think of advice your parents or grandparents always gave you. Report it." Example: "My grandmother always told me that I should never trust anyone who didn't like dogs."
**Prompt 2:** "What did your teacher from school say that you still remember? Report it." Example: "My math teacher told me that I would never amount to anything if I didn't study harder."
**Prompt 3:** "What is the funniest thing a friend ever said to you? Report it using all tense changes." Example: "My friend once told me that he had seen a ghost in his bathroom, and that it had been wearing his bathrobe."

> The Spanish is only to unblock understanding. The moment they have the idea, it's English only.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | CIRCLE STORY: HE SAID, SHE SAID (17 min)
**How it works:** Students sit in a circle. Teacher starts a story. Each student adds ONE sentence using reported speech. The story must be continuous — each sentence builds on the last. If someone breaks the chain or forgets reported speech, they start their sentence over.

**Teacher starts:** "Yesterday I met a strange man at the park. He told me that he had traveled from the future."

**Student 1 adds:** "He said that in the future, everyone would speak only English." **Student 2:** "I asked him if he was serious. He replied that he had never been more serious in his life." **Student 3:** "He warned me not to tell anyone about our conversation..."

**Round 1 (7 min) — Reported speech only.** Go around the circle 2-3 times. Each sentence MUST use reported speech (said, told, asked, replied, explained). If someone uses direct speech ("He said 'I am from the future'"), stop them: "Reported speech. Change the tense."

**Round 2 (7 min) — Reported speech + blending.** NEW story. Teacher starts: "A detective told me that a breathtaking painting had been stolen from the museum." Now each sentence must use reported speech PLUS one other module (passive, conditional, adjective, either/neither/both, question tag). Go around 2-3 times.

**Vote (3 min).** Which round had the best story? Which student added the most creative sentence?

> WALK AROUND writing errors. The chain format means everyone MUST participate — no hiding.

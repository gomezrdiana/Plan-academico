### 00:35--00:55 | BOOK: MODULE 12 GUIDE 1 --- INDEFINITE PRONOUNS (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Module 12, Guide 1 --- Indefinite Pronouns. Read the explanation, the chart, and ALL the examples. You have 8 minutes. Read everything. Don't skip anything."

**First 8 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading, not chatting.

**Last 12 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What are the four prefixes for indefinite pronouns?" (every-, some-, any-, no-)
- "What are the three categories?" (person, place, thing)
- "Give me the word for every + person." (everyone / everybody)
- "Give me the word for no + place." (nowhere)
- "Give me the word for some + thing." (something)
- "Is it 'everyone IS' or 'everyone ARE'?" (everyone IS --- always 3rd person singular)
- "When do you use SOME in a question?" (When the speaker suspects something exists: "Is something wrong?")
- "When do you use ANY?" (Negatives and questions --- also means 'cualquier': "Anything can happen.")
- "Can you use NO and NOT together? 'I don't have nothing' --- is that correct?" (No. Double negative. "I don't have anything" OR "I have nothing.")

If they get one wrong: "No. Listen." Give the correct answer. They repeat twice.

**Quick-fire drill. Say each sentence. Students tell you if it's CORRECT or WRONG. If wrong, they give the correct version:**

1. "Everyone are ready to go." (WRONG --- Everyone IS ready to go.)
2. "I don't know anyone here." (CORRECT.)
3. "Somebody have my phone." (WRONG --- Somebody HAS my phone.)
4. "She went nowhere yesterday." (CORRECT.)
5. "I don't have nothing to say." (WRONG --- double negative. "I don't have anything to say" OR "I have nothing to say.")
6. "Is something wrong?" (CORRECT --- speaker suspects something is wrong.)
7. "Nobody know the answer." (WRONG --- Nobody KNOWS the answer.)
8. "You can go anywhere you want." (CORRECT --- "any" meaning "cualquier.")
9. "Everything are fine." (WRONG --- Everything IS fine.)
10. "Someone left their bag here." (CORRECT --- informal, "their" is accepted for unknown gender.)

One per student, then keep going. If they freeze: "Is it 3rd person singular? Yes. So what's the verb?" They try again.

### 00:55--01:10 | EXERCISES (15 min)

Say: "Do the exercises for Guide 1. Check your answers with the Answer Key at the back. If you and your partner disagree, discuss. If neither of you knows, raise your hand."

Walk around. Give the correct version only --- never explain rules.

Common errors to watch for:
- Subject-verb agreement: "Everyone are" / "Nobody know" / "Somebody have"
- Double negatives: "I don't have nothing" / "She can't go nowhere"
- Wrong prefix for context: Using "something" in a negative sentence instead of "anything"
- "No one" written as one word ("noone") --- it's two words or hyphenated (no one / no-one)

If 50%+ get the same thing wrong, STOP. Write the rule on the board. Give two examples. They repeat.

### 01:10--01:40 | STRUCTURED CONVERSATION: THE MYSTERY (30 min)

Say: "Put your books away. I'm going to tell you a story --- a mystery. But I'm not going to finish it. You have to figure out what happened. You ask me questions. I answer. But HERE ARE THE RULES."

**Write on the board:**
```
THE MYSTERY --- RULES:
- Every question MUST use an indefinite pronoun
  (someone, anyone, nobody, something, anything,
  nothing, somewhere, anywhere, nowhere, everyone,
  everything, everywhere)
- Every statement MUST use an indefinite pronoun
- If you ask a question WITHOUT an indefinite pronoun,
  I will say: "Rephrase."
- I will correct EVERY grammar error I hear.
```

**THE STORY. Read this exactly:**

"Last Tuesday night, a woman named Elena walked into a police station. She was shaking. She said: 'Something terrible has happened. Someone has taken my husband. He was at home. Now he's gone. Nobody saw anything. Nothing was broken. Everything was exactly where it should be --- except him. He has disappeared. I have looked everywhere. He is nowhere. Can anyone help me?'"

Say: "That's all you get. Now --- you are the detectives. Ask me questions. I am the police chief. I know things about this case that you don't. But you MUST use indefinite pronouns in every question."

**YOUR ANSWERS. Memorize or keep this sheet visible. When they ask questions, use these details to build the story:**

- Did anyone hear anything? --- "A neighbor heard something around 11 PM. She said it sounded like someone was arguing. But she didn't see anyone."
- Was anything missing from the house? --- "Nothing was missing. Everything was in its place. That's what's strange."
- Did someone call him? --- "Everyone who knows him has tried calling. Nobody can reach him."
- Has anyone checked his workplace? --- "Someone from the office said he left work at 6 PM. Nobody saw him after that."
- Was there something on his phone? --- "His phone was found somewhere near the park. There was nothing on it --- someone had deleted everything."
- Did he tell anyone anything? --- "His best friend said he mentioned something strange last week. He said: 'If anything happens to me, don't believe anyone.'"
- Has anyone looked everywhere? --- "The police have searched everywhere in the neighborhood. They found nothing."
- Is there anyone suspicious? --- "Nobody has come forward. But someone left an envelope under the door of the police station. There was nothing inside --- just an empty envelope with his name."

**HOW TO RUN THIS:**
- Students ask questions. You answer using the details above.
- If a student asks a question WITHOUT an indefinite pronoun (e.g., "What happened to the man?"), say: "Rephrase. Use an indefinite pronoun." They try again: "Did something happen to someone?"
- If a student makes a GRAMMAR error (e.g., "Did anyone saw anything?"), say: "No. Listen. Did anyone SEE anything?" They repeat.
- WRITE EVERY ERROR ON YOUR ERROR PAPER.
- After 15 minutes of Q&A, say: "Now --- what do you think happened? Each of you gives a theory. Use indefinite pronouns."

**Theories (10 min).** Each student presents a theory (about 1.5 min each). Push them:
- "Did someone take him? Who? Use 'someone.'"
- "Was there anything on the phone that was important?"
- "Is anyone lying? Who?"
- "Where is he? Use 'somewhere' or 'nowhere.'"

**Final reveal (5 min).** Say: "Here is what actually happened. Nobody took him. He left on his own. He went somewhere nobody would look --- a small town where no one knew his name. He had told no one his plan. Everyone thought something terrible had happened, but nothing was wrong. He simply wanted to start a new life. Someone eventually found him --- a journalist who was investigating something else entirely. Everything became clear. Sometimes, nobody is the villain."

> This activity replaces Shadowing. The key is constant correction. You are a native speaker --- you hear every error. USE THAT. If a student says "Did anyone saw?" you catch it INSTANTLY. That is your value. Do not let errors slide.

### 02:00--02:20 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: STORY WITH 12 ERRORS (20 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 12 errors --- all related to indefinite pronouns. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Last summer, I went on a road trip with my friends. Everyone were excited about the trip. **(1 --- Everyone WAS excited --- 3rd person singular, always)**

We drove to a small town where nobody have ever been before. **(2 --- nobody HAD ever been --- 3rd person singular past perfect)**

When we arrived, we couldn't find anywhere to eat. Everything was closed. But then somebody suggest a place near the beach. **(3 --- somebody SUGGESTED --- 3rd person singular past tense)**

We went there and found something amazing --- the best seafood restaurant I've ever seen. We ordered everything on the menu. The waiter said: 'Has everybody decided?' I said: 'Yes, everybody have decided.' **(4 --- everybody HAS decided --- 3rd person singular)**

After dinner, we went for a walk. I don't saw nobody on the beach. **(5 --- I didn't SEE anyone / I saw nobody --- double negative AND wrong verb form)**

It was completely empty. My friend Carlos said: 'There isn't nothing to do here at night.' **(6 --- There isn't ANYTHING to do / There is NOTHING to do --- double negative)**

But then we heard something. Someone were playing guitar near the water. **(7 --- Someone WAS playing --- 3rd person singular)**

We walked closer and found a group of locals having a bonfire. They said: 'Is anybody want to join?' **(8 --- Does anybody WANT to join? --- question structure)**

We said yes. That night, we talked to everyone. Anyone had a story to tell. **(9 --- EVERYONE had a story to tell --- 'anyone' doesn't work in affirmative statements meaning 'all'; should be 'everyone')**

One man said: 'I have traveled everywhere, but I haven't found somewhere more beautiful than this beach.' **(10 --- I haven't found ANYWHERE more beautiful --- use 'anywhere' in negative sentences, not 'somewhere')**

At midnight, nobody wanted to leave. Somebody say: 'Let's stay until sunrise.' **(11 --- Somebody SAID --- 3rd person singular past tense)**

We stayed. And when the sun came up, everyone agree it was the best night of the entire trip." **(12 --- everyone AGREED --- 3rd person singular past tense)**

---

**After reading (5 min):** Review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version first, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice.

**Pairs (8 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: tell a short story --- 1 to 2 minutes --- with deliberate errors using indefinite pronouns. At least 4 errors. Partner B: catch and correct. After 4 minutes, SWITCH."

Walk around. Write down good corrections AND missed errors on your error paper.

> If the class catches fewer than 8 of the 12 errors, they need more practice. Note this for coordination.

### 02:20--02:45 | PRODUCTION: THE DETECTIVE (25 min)

Say: "You are all detectives now. A crime has been committed. Here is what we know."

**Write on the board:**
```
THE CASE:
- A famous painting was stolen from the city museum
  last night between 10 PM and 2 AM.
- The alarm system was turned off by SOMEONE.
- NOTHING was caught on camera --- someone deleted
  the footage.
- EVERYONE who works at the museum is a suspect.
- The painting was worth $5 million.

YOUR JOB:
- 2 students = DETECTIVES (ask questions)
- 2 students = MUSEUM STAFF (answer questions ---
  you may be hiding something)
- 2 students = WITNESSES (you saw something but
  you're not sure what)

RULE: Every question and every answer MUST contain
at least one indefinite pronoun.
```

**Assign roles (2 min).** Point at students: "You two --- detectives. You two --- museum staff. You two --- witnesses."

Give secret instructions to each group. Say these quietly or write them on paper:

**To MUSEUM STAFF:** "One of you turned off the alarm. You know who did it but you're protecting them. When asked, deflect: 'Nobody touched the alarm' / 'I didn't see anyone near the control panel.'"

**To WITNESSES:** "You saw someone running from the museum at midnight. You saw something in their hands, but it was dark. You're not sure of anything. Use: 'I think I saw someone...' / 'Something was in their hands but I couldn't see anything clearly.'"

**To DETECTIVES:** "Ask questions using indefinite pronouns. Push hard. If someone is lying, challenge them: 'Are you telling me nobody saw anything? That's impossible.' / 'Someone must know something.'"

**Round 1 --- Investigation (10 min).** Detectives question museum staff first (5 min), then witnesses (5 min). You stand back. Write errors on your error paper. Only interrupt for errors: "No. Listen." + correct version.

**Round 2 --- Accusation (5 min).** Say: "Detectives --- who did it? Present your theory. Use indefinite pronouns. Start with: 'We believe someone...' or 'Nobody is telling the truth because...'"

Each detective gives their theory (2.5 min each).

**Round 3 --- The Twist (3 min).** Say: "Here is what really happened. Nobody on the staff stole the painting. Someone from OUTSIDE the museum did it --- a former employee who still had access to everything. This person told no one about their plan. Nothing on the cameras because they knew where every camera was. The painting? It was hidden somewhere nobody would look --- inside another painting in the museum's storage. Sometimes the answer is right in front of everyone, and nobody sees anything."

**Debrief (5 min).** Say: "Which indefinite pronouns did you use the most? Which ones did you forget? Someone give me a sentence with 'nowhere.' Someone give me a sentence with 'everybody.' Someone give me a sentence with 'anything.'" Go around. Correct errors.

> Error paper. This is free production. Errors WILL appear. Write them ALL down.

### 02:45--03:00 | DEBATE: CAN ANYONE REALLY DO ANYTHING THEY WANT IN LIFE? (15 min)

Say: "Final debate of the day. The question is:" Write on the board:

```
DEBATE: Can anyone really do anything they want in life?

TEAM A: YES --- anyone can do anything (3 students)
TEAM B: NO --- not everyone can do everything (3 students)

RULES:
- You MUST use indefinite pronouns in every argument
- Minimum 3 different indefinite pronouns per argument
- If you make a statement without an indefinite pronoun,
  I will say: "Rephrase."
```

**Assign teams (1 min).** Split the class. If someone disagrees with their side: "That's the point. Argue the position you're given."

**Prepare (3 min).** Teams huddle. Each person prepares 2 arguments.

**Write example arguments on the board:**

```
YES: "Anyone can achieve anything if they work hard
enough. Nobody is born without some kind of talent.
Everyone has something special. The problem is that
some people do nothing about it. But if someone truly
wants something, nothing can stop them."

NO: "Not everyone has the same opportunities. Someone
born in poverty can't do everything that someone born
rich can do. Nobody chooses where they are born.
Saying 'anyone can do anything' ignores that some
people have nothing to start with. Not everything
is about effort --- sometimes nobody gives you
a chance."
```

**Debate (8 min).** Format:
- Team A: Student 1 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 1 responds (1 min).
- Team A: Student 2 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 2 responds (1 min).
- Open floor: Anyone can jump in with a final point (2 min).
- Every response MUST use indefinite pronouns. If they don't, say: "Rephrase."

**Vote + personal opinion (3 min).** Say: "Which team had the strongest arguments? Vote." Quick hand vote. Say: "Now --- what do YOU personally believe? One sentence. Must include at least two indefinite pronouns." Go around. 6 students, ~20 seconds each.

> Error paper. Free production. Write down every error.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT REFRAME + SET #24 (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande (zona izquierda):

   THE REFRAME
   "You are not the student.
    You are the model.
    The room learns by hearing you first."

2. DI: "Stop thinking like a learner. Today you behave as
   the model. The room — any audience — learns by hearing
   you first. Silence from you is not modesty. Silence is
   withdrawal."

3. ESCRIBE debajo (zona izquierda):

   THE 3-SECOND RULE
   Question lands -> 3 seconds MAX -> start speaking.
   No "um". No "well". No waiting for the name call.
   The first sound is the Set #24 entry phrase.

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "This is what
   the phrase says — three seconds. Jump in. Build on.
   Weigh in. The audience owes you nothing. You owe them
   the voice you practiced."

5. ESCRIBE en board grande (zona derecha):

   SET #24 — TAKING THE FLOOR (cumulative = 24 sets)
   Use these LIVE today and in Cl 33.

   1. JUMP IN /dʒʌmp ɪn/ — entrar / intervenir
      "If I may jump in — I'd offer an example here."

   2. BUILD ON /bɪld ɒn/ — añadir sobre lo dicho
      "Building on what was just said — I'd add..."

   3. OFFER /ˈɒf.ər/ — ofrecer aporte sin imponer
      "Let me offer one example from the B2 angle —"

   4. CHIME IN /tʃaɪm ɪn/ — intervenir (informal)
      "Mind if I chime in? One quick example."

   5. STEP UP /stɛp ʌp/ — tomar el lugar / asumir el rol
      "I'll step up on this one — here's the answer."

   6. WEIGH IN /weɪ ɪn/ — opinar con peso
      "Let me weigh in — the issue has two sides."

6. Per word (~50 seg): pronuncia 2x -> cohorte 2x -> definicion
   -> ejemplo en board -> UN estudiante AL AZAR usa la palabra
   LIVE entrando al floor con pregunta que improvisas ("Who has
   an example of a Colombian custom foreigners misunderstand?").

7. [ERROR TIPICO #1] estudiante dice "Um, well, maybe..."
   ANTES de la Set #24 phrase -> DI: "The Set #24 phrase
   IS the opening. No 'um' before it. 'Let me chime in —'
   is the FIRST sound. Reset. Same question."

8. [ERROR TIPICO #2] estudiante repite la MISMA Set #24
   phrase 3 veces seguidas -> DI: "Vary. Six phrases on
   the board. Don't anchor on one."

9. MINI-DRILLS (~15 min): lanzas 4 preguntas genericas
   al aire ("Who has an example of...?"). 3 seg de espera.
   Quien levante mano primero entra con Set #24. Los otros
   dos BUILD usando otra Set #24 phrase.

   Preguntas sugeridas (escoge 4):
     - "Who has an example of disagreeing politely in B2?"
     - "Who has an example of a Colombian phrase foreigners
        find difficult to translate?"
     - "Who has an example of a question you fear at the Final?"
     - "Who has an example of a topic you could speak 2 min
        about right now without preparation?"

10. [VIRTUD] activation durante mini-drills: si nadie levanta
    mano en 3 seg, DI: "Justice. Three seconds. Jump in.
    The wait costs the room." Re-throw misma pregunta. NO
    pases hasta que alguien entre.

11. CIERRA Block 2 cuando los 3 hayan entrado al floor al
    menos UNA vez con Set #24 limpia. DI: "Stand up. Round
    Q&A starts now."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun no completaste las 6 Set #24 phrases con ejemplo -> NO AVANCES a mini-drills. Deja 2 palabras para repaso al final del Block 3. Mejor LAND 4 con drill que rush 6 sin uso real.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** los 3 estudiantes han entrado al floor con Set #24 al menos una vez sin "um" previo.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: Q&A INTENSIVO (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "Q&A INTENSIVO — 3 ROUNDS"

2. ESCRIBE estructura debajo:

   ROUND 1 — B1 AUDIENCE (12 min)
     3 preguntas. Tu respuesta = explicar al B1.
     Shapes simples. Slow it down. Set #24 entry.

   ROUND 2 — SKEPTIC (12 min)
     3 preguntas pushback. Counter. Hold ground.
     Set #24 entry + Set #23 hold.

   ROUND 3 — HONEST GAP (12 min)
     3 preguntas. Nombra lo que AUN no tienes
     respuesta limpia + 1 frase de como lo arreglas.

3. DI las reglas: "Stand up. Each of you answers all 9
   questions across the rounds. ~30 segundos por respuesta.
   Set #24 entry obligatoria en cada turno. The first sound
   from your mouth is the Set #24 phrase."

4. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI antes del round 1: "This round
   is the phrase in action — three seconds, jump in, build
   on, weigh in. The audience — me as B1, me as skeptic, you
   as honest learner — owes you nothing. You owe them what
   you practiced."
```

### ROUND 1 — B1 AUDIENCE (~12 min)

```
5. DI: "I am now a B1 audience member. Slow it down. Simpler
   shapes. No jargon. Set #24 entry. Round 1."

6. Lanza pregunta 1 al aire. Espera 3 seg. Quien levante
   mano primero entra. Los otros dos esperan turno con OTRA
   Set #24 phrase.

   Preguntas R1 (escoge 3):
     - "Explain your Final topic in 2 simple sentences."
     - "What does 'taking the floor' mean? Like I'm 15."
     - "Why does your Final matter to someone never visited
        Colombia?"
     - "Describe a region of Colombia in 30 seg para B1."

7. Despues de cada respuesta, NOTA EN CUADERNO con NOMBRE:
   ¿Set #24 entry? ¿simplifico para B1? ¿~30 seg?

8. [ERROR TIPICO #3] vocabulario B2 abstracto con audience
   B1 -> DI: "Stop. B1 audience. Slower words. Reformulate."
```

### ROUND 2 — SKEPTIC (~12 min)

```
9. DI: "Now I am a SKEPTIC. I push back. You counter. You
   hold ground. Set #24 entry + Set #23 hold + reason.
   Round 2."

10. Lanza pregunta 1 skeptic. Espera 3 seg. Quien entra
    primero. Los otros build con counter.

    Preguntas R2 (escoge 3):
      - "Your Final topic — why is it actually true? Sounds
         like just opinion to me."
      - "Colombia is unsafe for tourists. Convince me otherwise."
      - "Your hook line — I find it cliche. Defend or change it."
      - "Why should the audience care about YOUR angle?"

11. NOTA EN CUADERNO con NOMBRE: ¿holdio o colapso? (Set
    #23 vs caved) ¿Set #24 entry limpia? ¿dio razon o solo
    repitio posicion?

12. [ERROR TIPICO #4] bajo presion, estudiante drift al
    español -> DI: "Pause. Breathe. English. Use Set #21
    bridge if needed." NO lo dejes resbalar.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #5] estudiante CONCEDE todo el punto bajo
    pushback -> DI: "Hold. Set #23: 'I'd contend the core
    holds even if X is true.' Don't collapse the whole
    position to avoid disagreement."
```

### ROUND 3 — HONEST GAP (~12 min)

```
14. DI: "Now honest gap. Name ONE thing in your Final you
    DON'T yet have a clean answer for. Then ONE sentence on
    HOW you'll fix it. Set #24 entry obligatoria. Round 3."

15. Lanza pregunta 1. Espera 3 seg. Mismo protocolo.

    Preguntas R3 (escoge 3):
      - "Weakest part of your Final argument? Fix plan
         before Cl 36?"
      - "Question you secretly hope evaluator does NOT ask?
         How will you arm for it?"
      - "ONE Colombian fact you keep guessing at? How verify
         tonight?"

16. NOTA EN CUADERNO con NOMBRE: ¿gap real (no cosmetico)?
    ¿fix-sentence concreta (verb + plazo)? ¿Set #24 entry
    limpia tras 9 turnos?

17. RECOVERY (~3 min): cada estudiante nombra UN momento de
    hoy donde tomo el floor limpio. DI: "Name it. One
    sentence. 'I took the floor when ___'."
```

**[ALERTA]** si un estudiante no entro al floor primero en NINGUN round (siempre construyo despues) -> escalas en cuaderno + Plan B Cl 33: lo nombras EXPLICITAMENTE al inicio para entrar primero en la apertura. Esto NO se queda sin atender.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 9 preguntas x 3 estudiantes ejecutadas + recovery hecho + cuaderno con 27 entries (9 por estudiante).

### 00:00--00:20 | ICEBREAKER: I KNOW SOMETHING YOU DON'T KNOW (20 min)

Say: "Welcome back. Last class was a big review --- today we start something new. Module 7. But first, a game. I'm going to say a sentence. Then each of you will say a sentence with the same structure. Listen."

Say these 6 example sentences. After each one, pause and let them absorb the structure:

1. "I know where the best restaurant in the city is."
2. "I know who ate my sandwich yesterday."
3. "I know when the next holiday starts."
4. "I know why the boss was angry last Friday."
5. "I know how to fix a broken phone screen."
6. "I know what you did last summer."

Say: "Did you hear the pattern? Every sentence starts with 'I know' and then gives information. Now --- your turn. Each of you says ONE sentence starting with 'I know...' and it has to be something interesting, funny, or mysterious. Something the rest of us probably don't know. Go."

Go around the room. Each student says one sentence. If a student says something like "I know that you are tired" (just using 'that'), say: "Good, but try using WHERE, WHEN, WHO, WHY, HOW, or WHAT instead. Like: I know WHY you're tired." Let them try again.

After the first round, say: "Good. One more round. This time, make it more personal or dramatic." Go around again.

> Don't correct grammar yet --- just let them produce sentences. Listen for how naturally they use relative pronouns. Note which ones they avoid (usually "how" and "why"). You'll come back to these in the book section.

### 00:35--00:55 | BOOK: MODULE 7 GUIDE 1 --- SIMPLE RELATIVE PRONOUNS (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Module 7, Guide 1 --- Simple Relative Pronouns. Read the explanation and ALL examples with your partner. You have 10 minutes. Read everything --- the examples, the notes, the explanations. Don't skip anything."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading, not chatting.

**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What are the 6 relative pronouns in this guide?" (where, when, why, how, who, what)
- "Give me an example with WHERE." (Expected: "I know where you live.")
- "Give me an example with HOW." (Expected: "I know how to cook." or "I know how you did it.")
- "What happens to sentence order after the relative pronoun --- does it stay like a question or like a normal sentence?" (Normal affirmative order: "I know where you PLAY" NOT "I know where DO you play")
- "Can you use the infinitive form? Give me an example." (Expected: "I know where to go." "I know how to swim.")

5. "Your friend wants to know about a restaurant location." (Expected: "I know where it is.")
6. "Someone wants to understand your boss's bad mood." (Expected: "I know why he's upset.")

### 01:15--01:35 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: STORY WITH 12 ERRORS (20 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 12 errors --- all related to relative pronouns and sentence order. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Let me tell you about my friend Roberto. Roberto is the kind of person who always knows everything. He says he knows where is the best coffee in the city. **(1 --- where the best coffee in the city IS --- word order)**

Last week, I asked him about a new restaurant. He said: 'I know where is it located.' **(2 --- where it IS located --- word order)** He also told me he knows what does the chef cook best. **(3 --- what the chef COOKS best --- word order, no 'does')**

So we went to the restaurant. When we arrived, Roberto said: 'I know the waiter what works here.' **(4 --- the waiter WHO works here --- 'who' for people, not 'what')** He pointed at a man and said: 'That's the guy what served us last time.' **(5 --- the guy WHO served us --- 'who' for people)**

We sat down and Roberto said: 'I know why is this place so popular.' **(6 --- why this place IS so popular --- word order)** He also claimed he knew how do they make the pasta. **(7 --- how they MAKE the pasta --- word order, no 'do')**

The food arrived. I asked Roberto: 'Do you know the person which cooked this?' **(8 --- the person WHO cooked this --- 'who' for people, not 'which')** He said: 'Of course. I know who is the chef.' **(9 --- who the chef IS --- word order)**

After dinner, we were walking home. Roberto said: 'I know a shortcut. I know where to going.' **(10 --- where to GO --- infinitive form, not gerund)** But we got lost. He didn't know how to getting back to the main road. **(11 --- how to GET back --- infinitive form)**

Finally, I said: 'Roberto, you say you know everything. But do you know what is the time? Because I think we're lost and it's midnight.' **(12 --- what the time IS --- word order)**

Roberto just smiled."

---

After reading: review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version first, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice.

**Pairs (8 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: tell a short story --- 1 to 2 minutes --- with deliberate errors using relative pronouns. Wrong word order, wrong pronoun, wrong infinitive form. Partner B: catch and correct. After 4 minutes, SWITCH."

Walk around. Write down good corrections AND missed errors on your error paper.

> If the class catches fewer than 8 errors in the story, they need more practice. Note this in your report.

### 01:55--02:15 | BOOK: MODULE 7 GUIDE 2 --- RESTRICTIVE RELATIVE CLAUSES (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Guide 2 --- Restrictive Relative Clauses. Read the explanation and examples. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. Walk around silently.

**Last 8 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is a restrictive relative clause?" (Expected: It gives NECESSARY information --- without it, you don't know which person/thing/place we're talking about.)
- "What word do we use for 'que' in English?" (that)
- "Give me an example with THAT." (Expected: "The car that I bought is blue." "The book that you recommended was great.")
- "When do we use WHERE instead of THAT?" (For places: "The park where we met is beautiful.")
- "When do we use WHEN?" (For times: "The night when I met you was special.")
- "When do we use WHO?" (For people: "The girl who I love is pretty." "The teacher who helped me was kind.")
- "WHY are these clauses necessary? What happens if you remove them?" (You lose the identification --- "The car is blue" doesn't tell us WHICH car.)

If they struggle to explain, say: "Read it again. You have 2 more minutes." Then ask again. Do NOT explain it yourself.

**Quick test --- say these sentences and ask: "Which word --- that, where, when, or who?"**

1. "The restaurant ___ we went to was amazing." (where / that)
2. "The day ___ I graduated was the happiest of my life." (when)
3. "The man ___ you saw is my uncle." (who / that)
4. "The movie ___ she recommended was terrible." (that)
5. "The house ___ I grew up was demolished." (where)
6. "The woman ___ called you is my boss." (who)

### 02:35--03:10 | THE PERSON WHO CHANGED MY LIFE (35 min)

**Step 1 --- Write (8 min).**
Say: "Think of one person who changed your life. It could be a family member, a teacher, a friend, a stranger. Someone who made you different. You're going to write about this person --- but you MUST use relative clauses. Let me give you a model."

Write on the board:
```
My grandmother is the person WHO taught me to cook.
She lived in a house WHERE the kitchen was the biggest room.
The summer WHEN I stayed with her was the best of my childhood.
She had a recipe THAT nobody else could make.
The reason WHY she was so important is that she believed in me.
```

Say: "Write 6--8 sentences about YOUR person. Use at least 4 different relative words: who, where, when, that, why, how, what. You have 8 minutes. Go."

Walk around. If someone writes "The person that change my life" --- point at it silently. They self-correct. If they don't notice, say: "No. Listen. The person who changed my life." They write the correct version.

**Step 2 --- Present (12 min).**
Say: "Stand up. You're going to present your person to a partner. Don't just READ your paper --- TALK about them. Use your notes as a guide, but look at your partner. Speak naturally. Each person has 2 minutes. After you present, your partner asks 2 questions. Then switch."

Pair them up. Time it: 2 min presentation + 1 min questions per person = 6 min per pair. Then switch partners. Each student presents twice to two different partners.

**Step 3 --- Q&A (8 min).**
Say: "Now --- 3 volunteers. Present your person to the whole class. The class asks questions. Questions MUST use relative clauses." Write on the board:

```
Example questions:
"Is she the person WHO gave you advice about your career?"
"Is that the place WHERE you met?"
"Was that the moment WHEN you realized she was important?"
```

3 students present (2 min each). After each, class asks 1--2 questions using relative clauses. If a question doesn't use a relative clause, say: "Good question, but rephrase it with WHO, WHERE, WHEN, THAT, WHY, or WHAT."

**Step 4 --- Peer feedback (7 min).**
Say: "Go back to your last partner. Tell them: one thing they said really well, and one sentence where the relative clause could be better. Help each other improve ONE sentence."

Pairs work. Walk around with error paper. Write down errors. If you hear a great sentence, say: "That was excellent. Say it again." They repeat.

> This is the core production activity. Students should be speaking 90%+ of the time. Your job: walk, listen, write errors, and occasionally redirect.

### 03:10--03:25 | WRITING: THE PLACE WHERE I FEEL MOST ALIVE (15 min)

Say: "You're going to write a paragraph about a place that is special to you. A place where you feel something. It could be a city, a room, a park, a street --- anywhere."

**Write on the board:**

```
Write 8-10 sentences about a place that is special to you.
You MUST use at least:
- 2 sentences with "where"
- 1 sentence with "when"
- 1 sentence with "who"
- 1 sentence with "that"
- 1 sentence from a previous module (passive, reported speech, conditional, etc.)

Example:
"The beach where my family used to go every summer is in Santa Marta.
It's a place where I feel completely free.
The summer when I learned to swim there was the best summer of my life.
My grandmother, who always brought empanadas, would sit under a tree
that was older than anyone in my family.
I was told that the tree was planted 200 years ago."
```

Say: "You have 10 minutes. Write. Don't ask me vocabulary --- figure it out. Use what you know."

Students write. Walk around in SILENCE. Do NOT help --- let them make errors. Note errors on your error paper.

**After 10 minutes (8 min):** Say: "Read your paragraph to your partner. Partner: listen for the relative clauses. Count them. Are there at least 5? If not, help them add more. Also: check word order --- remember, after a relative pronoun, the sentence follows NORMAL order."

Partners exchange feedback. Walk around and listen. Correct only if both partners missed the same error.

Say: "Who wants to share? Read yours to the class." 2-3 students share. After each: brief class feedback on relative clause usage.

> Walk with error paper. This is a KEY diagnostic activity --- you can see if they truly understand relative clauses or if they're just repeating patterns.

### 03:25--03:40 | DEBATE: THE THING THAT MATTERS MOST (15 min)

Say: "We're going to have a mini-debate. I'll give you a topic. You have to argue your position using relative clauses as much as possible."

**Write on the board:**

```
DEBATE: What is the thing that matters most in life?
- Money    - Health    - Love    - Knowledge    - Freedom
```

Say: "Choose ONE. You have 2 minutes to prepare 3 arguments. Each argument MUST include a relative clause."

**Example on the board:** "Health is the thing that matters most because a person who is sick can't enjoy anything else. The moment when you lose your health is the moment when you understand its value."

**2 min preparation.** Students choose and prepare arguments.

**Debate (10 min):** Each student states their choice and gives their 3 arguments (1 min each). After each student, ONE other student must challenge them --- also using relative clauses.

Say: "Challenge them! 'But the person who has money can BUY health.' 'The moment when you fall in love is more important than any amount of money.'"

Keep it moving. 1 minute per student + 30 seconds for the challenge. 6 students × 1.5 min = 9 min.

**Vote (1 min):** "What does the class think? What is the thing that matters most?" Quick hand vote. Announce winner.

> Error paper. This is free production --- errors will appear. Write them ALL down. This is gold for your report.

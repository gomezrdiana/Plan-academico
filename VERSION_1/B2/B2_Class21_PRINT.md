### 00:25--01:55 | MOCK PRESENTATIONS (90 min total --- 15 min per student)

Say: "Here is how this works. Each student presents for 5 to 7 minutes. After the presentation, the class asks questions for 3 minutes. Then I give feedback for 3 to 4 minutes. That is 15 minutes per student. 6 students. 90 minutes total. We cannot go over. I will time everything strictly."

**Write on the board:**
```
MOCK PRESENTATION --- FORMAT

1. Present: 5-7 minutes (I will stop you at 7)
2. Q&A: 3 minutes (class asks, you answer)
3. Feedback: 3-4 minutes (from me + class)

WHAT I AM LOOKING FOR:
- Clear structure (intro, body, conclusion)
- Use of grammar from Modules 1-12
  (at least 6 different structures)
- Fluency (minimal pauses, no reading)
- Eye contact with the class
- Timing (7-10 min for the real one ---
  today 5-7 min is acceptable)
- Ability to answer questions

AUDIENCE:
- Listen carefully
- Write down 1 strength and 1 suggestion
  for each presenter
- Prepare at least 1 question per presentation
```

**FOR EACH STUDENT (repeat this process 6 times):**

**Step 1 --- Presentation (5-7 min).**

Say: "Student name, you're up. Come to the front. Leave your note cards on the table --- you can glance at them, but do NOT read from them. Class: listen. Write down one strength and one area to improve. Ready? Go."

Start your timer. While they present:
- **On a separate sheet for this student**, write their name at the top and note:
  - Grammar errors (exact quote + correct version): e.g., "Said 'if I would have' --- should be 'if I had'"
  - Pronunciation errors: e.g., "Pronounces 'comfortable' as 'com-for-TAB-le' --- should be 'CUMF-ter-bul'"
  - Filler words: count "um," "uh," "like," "you know" --- write the total
  - Structure notes: "No clear introduction" / "Conclusion was abrupt" / "Good transition between points"
  - Module structures used: check which ones you hear --- e.g., "Used: passive, conditional, relative clause, reported speech. Missing: causative, inversion, phrasal verbs"
  - Timing: exact time they spoke

Do NOT interrupt during the presentation. Let them finish. Save corrections for feedback.

If they stop before 5 minutes: "You still have time. Keep going. Expand on your last point."
At 7 minutes: "Time. Wrap up with one final sentence."

**Step 2 --- Q&A (3 min).**

Say: "Class --- questions. At least 3 questions."

If the class is slow to ask: seed questions yourself:
- "Why did you choose this topic?"
- "You mentioned [X]. Can you explain that more?"
- "What would you change about [topic] if you could?"
- "Do you think everyone agrees with your point about [X]?"
- "If you had to summarize your presentation in one sentence, what would it be?"

While the presenter answers, listen for errors but do NOT interrupt. Write them on their feedback sheet.

**Step 3 --- Feedback (3-4 min).**

Give feedback in this order:

1. **Timing:** "Your presentation was [X] minutes. For the final, you need 7 to 10. You need to [add more / cut some]."

2. **Structure:** "Your introduction was [clear/unclear]. Your body had [X] main points --- that's [enough/not enough]. Your conclusion was [strong/weak/missing]."

3. **Grammar --- TOP 3 ERRORS:** Pick the 3 most important errors from your notes. Say each one:
   - "You said: '[wrong version].' The correct version is: '[correct version].' Say it." They repeat.
   - "You said: '[wrong version].' The correct version is: '[correct version].' Say it." They repeat.
   - "You said: '[wrong version].' The correct version is: '[correct version].' Say it." They repeat.

4. **Module structures:** "I heard you use [list structures]. I did NOT hear [list missing structures]. For the final, add at least [X] more. Specifically, try adding [specific structure] in your section about [specific part of their presentation]."

5. **Delivery:** "Your eye contact was [good/needs work]. Your pace was [good/too fast/too slow]. You used [X] filler words --- for the final, try to reduce that to zero."

6. **One strength:** Always end with something genuinely positive. "Your strongest moment was when you said [specific quote]. That was clear, well-structured, and confident. Do more of that."

Say: "Class --- what was this student's biggest strength?" One student answers. "What is one thing they should improve?" One student answers.

### 03:00--03:25 | FINAL PROJECT WORKSHOP: FINAL REVISIONS (25 min)

**THE PROJECT MATERIALS STAY AT THE ACADEMY.** Students do NOT take their project notes home. Collect everything at the end of this block.

Say: "You just got detailed feedback from me and from your classmates. Now you have 25 minutes to make revisions. This is your LAST full workshop before the final presentation. Use every minute. Fix what was wrong. Add what was missing. Practice your timing."

**How this works --- you do NOT sit down and wait:**
- You have 6 students. That is about 4 minutes per student.
- Go to each student WITH their individual feedback sheet. Say:
  - "Here are the 3 grammar errors you made. Fix them in your presentation now. Show me the corrected version."
  - "You were missing [module structures]. Where in your presentation can you add them? Write them in now."
  - "Your presentation was [X] minutes. You need [more/less]. Which section will you [expand/cut]?"
  - "Your introduction needs work. Rewrite your opening sentence right now. Read it to me."
  - "Your conclusion was [weak/abrupt/missing]. Write a strong final sentence. Use an inversion or a conditional to end powerfully."
  - "Practice your first minute out loud to me right now. Go."

- Specific revision suggestions:
  - "Start with a question to the audience: 'Have you ever wondered why...?'"
  - "Add a personal story in the middle. 'When I was [age], something happened that changed everything.'"
  - "Use a conditional to make a point: 'If I hadn't experienced this, I wouldn't understand...'"
  - "End with: 'Never have I been more certain that...' --- strong inversion for your conclusion."
  - "Add transitions between points: 'Not only did this affect [X], but it also changed [Y].'"
  - "You said 'um' 12 times. Practice your transition sentences so you don't need to fill the silence."

- If a student is resisting revision: "You just presented and you heard the feedback. If you don't fix these things, you will make the same errors in the final. Fix them NOW while you remember."

**Last 3 min.** Say: "Time is up. Show me your revised note cards." Quick check --- did they actually make changes? If yes: "Good. Practice at home." If no: "You need to revise. Come to the next class with changes made."

**Remind them:**
Say: "Next class we do Mock Presentations Round 2 --- full 7 to 10 minutes. That is the real length. You MUST be ready. Fix what we discussed today. Practice at home with a timer. Two weeks until the real thing."

> Active coaching. This is not free time.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT CLUSTER 6 (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 3 columnas en board grandes:

   3RD PERSON -S        THERE IS / ARE       CAUSATIVE
   he/she/it + verb-S   IS + singular        have/get + OBJ
     work -> works        "a meeting"        + PAST PARTICIPLE
     open -> opens      IS + uncountable     (someone else
   -ES (o/s/sh/ch/x)      "much paper"        does it FOR you)
     go -> goes         ARE + plural         PRESENT routine
     watch -> watches     "12 boxes"          "I HAVE my car
   -IES (cons + y)        "many emails"        serviced"
     study -> studies   NEG: isn't / aren't  PAST one event
   IRREGULAR            Q:  Is there / Are    "I HAD my car
     have -> HAS            there?              FIXED"
     be -> IS           RULE: word AFTER     PARTICIPLE: -ED
   AUX: doesn't / does    decides             regular; 3rd col
     "She DOESN'T work"                       irregular
     "DOES he work?"                          cut/cut/cut

2. SEÑALA columna 1 (3rd person -s). DI: "Present simple.
   He / she / it + verb-S. Regular = +S. -O, -S, -SH, -CH, -X
   = +ES. Consonant + Y = -IES. HAVE becomes HAS. BE becomes
   IS. With DOES or DOESN'T, the verb goes back to bare."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "This is what
   the phrase says — a 'she' takes its 's'. Don't drop the
   S. Don't double it after DOES."

4. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "He go to work" -> TACHA "go", ESCRIBE
   "He GOES to work." DI: "-O ending = -ES." COHORTE repite
   2 veces en coro.

5. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She work in the office" -> TACHA "work",
   ESCRIBE "She WORKS in the office." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "My boss have meetings" -> TACHA "have",
   ESCRIBE "My boss HAS meetings." DI: "Have becomes HAS.
   No exceptions." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences with 3rd person -S
   about your real workplace. Mix regular, -es, has,
   and one negative with DOESN'T." 4 minutos.

8. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
   2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
   NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

9. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubea con el -s,
   DI suave: "Measure. Match the ending. That is temperance."

10. SEÑALA columna 2 (there is/are). DI: "There IS for
    singular or uncountable. There ARE for plural. The word
    AFTER decides — not 'there'."

11. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "there is many people" -> TACHA "is",
    ESCRIBE "THERE ARE many people." DI: "People = plural
    countable." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

12. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "there are much water" -> TACHA "are",
    ESCRIBE "THERE IS much water." DI: "Water = uncountable
    = IS." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

13. PIDELES en parejas: "6 sentences mixing THERE IS / THERE
    ARE about your workplace right now — meetings, people,
    coffee, boxes, time, paper. Include 1 question with
    'Is there...?' or 'Are there...?'" 4 minutos.

14. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    2 veces.

15. SEÑALA columna 3 (causative). DI: "Have or get + object
    + PAST PARTICIPLE. Someone else does it FOR you. 'I HAD
    my car FIXED.' 'She GOT her hair CUT.' Never bare verb
    after the object."

16. [ERROR TIPICO #6] "I had my car fix" -> TACHA "fix",
    ESCRIBE "I had my car FIXED." DI: "Past participle =
    -ED for regulars." Despues escribe debajo: "She got her
    hair cutted" -> TACHA "cutted", ESCRIBE "She got her
    hair CUT." DI: "Cut is irregular — cut/cut/cut. No -ED."
    COHORTE repite las 2 correcciones 2 veces.

17. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences with CAUSATIVE about
    real services in your life — car, hair, computer, clothes,
    documents. 3 in past with HAD/GOT + past participle,
    2 in present with HAVE/GET + past participle for routine."
    5 minutos.

18. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo construya en las
    3 categorias sin titubear. DI: "Stand up — we go to
    the simulation."
```

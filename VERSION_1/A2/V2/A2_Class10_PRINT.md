## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT M12 PAST SIMPLE vs CONTINUOUS (~52 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board el titulo y las REGLAS del libro
   (M12, p.99-100), tal cual:

   PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS

   1) From A1: PAST SIMPLE ("I played") + PAST CONTINUOUS
      ("I was playing").
      - PAST SIMPLE = a simple, finished action at a
        specific time.   "I played yesterday."
      - PAST CONTINUOUS = a past action with duration /
        extension.       "I was playing for three hours."
   2) KEY USE: a short/abrupt action that interrupts a
      longer one:
        "I was playing WHEN my mom called."
        = Yo estaba jugando cuando mi madre llamo.

   STRUCTURE (write big):
      LONG action  = was/were + verb-ING   (past continuous)
      SHORT action = verb in past simple    (the interruption)
      Joined by:   WHEN  /  WHILE

2. SEÑALA la estructura. DI: "The long action is the
   background — 'was playing'. The short action cuts in —
   'called'. 'When' usually goes before the short action.
   'While' usually goes before the long action."

3. ESCRIBE EJEMPLOS del libro (p.101), tal cual:

      I was living in Bucaramanga in 2019.
      I ate a sandwich at 8:00.
      Eliana was studying for her exams last week.
      I was eating pasta when my brother arrived.
      My dad was working when I ate lunch.
      What were you doing when I was studying?
      We were walking in the park, when Jeff saw a dog.
      The family was watching a movie when Sebastián left.

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "The phrase is
   exactly this — 'I WAS keeping calm WHEN the problem
   called.' Long steady action, sudden interruption."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I was play when my mom called" ->
   AÑADE -ING, ESCRIBE "I was PLAYING when my mom called."
   DI: "Past continuous = was/were + verb-ING." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I played when my mom was calling" (la
   accion larga en simple, la corta en continuo — al reves)
   -> ESCRIBE limpio: "I was PLAYING when my mom CALLED."
   DI: "Long action = continuous. Short interruption =
   simple. Don't swap them." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I was eating when my brother was
   arriving" -> TACHA "was arriving", ESCRIBE "...when my
   brother ARRIVED." DI: "The interruption is short = past
   simple." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. PRACTICA dirigida (oral): TU das la accion larga, el
   grupo añade la interrupcion:
        "I was cooking when ___."
        "We were driving when ___."
        "She was sleeping when ___."
   8-10 vueltas, distintos estudiantes.

9. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien pone la accion larga en
   simple, DI suave: "Temperance. The long, steady action
   was CONTINUOUS. 'Was playing.' The sudden one is simple."

10. PIDELES en parejas: "Student A gives a LONG past action
    (was/were + -ing). Student B interrupts it with a SHORT
    action (past simple) + 'when'. Then switch. 6 sentences.
    Theme: a normal day at work or home. 5 minutes."

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
    2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
    NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. ESCRIBE el uso con WHILE + pregunta (p.101):
        "While I was working, the power went out."
        "What were you doing when I called?"
    DI: "'While' often goes before the long action; 'when'
    before the short one." Practica 4-5 vueltas en coro.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "What you were doing when...?" ->
    TACHA, ESCRIBE "What WERE YOU doing when...?" DI:
    "Question: were + subject + verb-ing." COHORTE repite
    2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas round 2: "Now tell a 4-sentence
    MINI-STORY of a day that got interrupted: 2 long actions
    (past continuous) + 2 short interruptions (past simple),
    joined with when/while. Theme: a work day that didn't go
    as planned. 5 minutes."

15. CIRCULA. CORRIGE cuando el mismo error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas.

16. CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo combine accion larga
    (continuous) + corta (simple) sin invertirlas. DI:
    "Stand up — we go to the simulation. Incident report
    at work."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 32 min y aun invierten las formas (larga en simple, corta en continuo), NO AVANCES a la mini-historia. Quedate en "was/were + -ing + WHEN + simple" hasta que salga limpio; deja la mini-historia para tarea. Mejor LAND uno bien que rush dos shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas combinando past continuous (larga) + past simple (corta) con when/while sin invertir.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: INCIDENT REPORT AT WORK (~36 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "INCIDENT REPORT AT WORK"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is a SUPERVISOR asking
   'what were you doing when it happened?' The other is the
   WORKER reporting: a long action (past continuous) that
   was interrupted by a short event (past simple). You must
   use, in the whole conversation, AT LEAST:
     - 4 long actions (past continuous: 'I was checking the
       machine')
     - 4 short interruptions (past simple: 'when the alarm
       rang')
     - 2 'when' + 1 'while'
   Then rotate."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This report is the phrase in
   action. 'I was keeping calm when the problem called.' You
   describe the steady action and the interruption — calmly."

4. ESCRIBE 2 mini-scenarios en board:

   SCENARIO 1 — Reporting a workplace incident
     Supervisor asks, worker reports: "I was loading the
     truck when the box fell." "While I was answering the
     phone, the customer arrived." "We were closing the
     store when the power went out."

   SCENARIO 2 — Explaining a missed deadline / delay
     Manager asks, employee explains: "I was finishing the
     report when the system crashed." "While we were in the
     meeting, the client cancelled." "What were you doing
     when the email came?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (5 min): pareja escoge un scenario.
   Roles claros. El supervisor arranca con "What were you
   doing when...?"

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English with
   'I was ...-ing when ... happened'. Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 (5 min): cambien
   de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si alguien
   pone la accion larga en simple, DI suave: "Temperance.
   The long action was continuous — 'was doing'. The
   interruption is simple."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. OBSERVER COUNT (ligero): en round 2, asigna a 2
    estudiantes como observers — cuentan en silencio
    cuantas combinaciones CORRECTAS (continuous + simple +
    when/while) oyeron en la pareja vecina. Reportan: "Pair
    X — 4 correct combos." No correccion, solo conteo.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (3 reportes con interrupcion). COHORTE cuenta
    cuantas combinaciones correctas cazaron. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What were you doing when the alarm rang?" / "While you were working, what happened?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 2 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente + observer count reportado.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT M11 2ND CONDITIONAL (~52 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board el titulo y las REGLAS del libro
   (M11, p.87-88), tal cual:

   THE SECOND CONDITIONAL — improbable / imaginary events

   1) Second conditional = IMPROBABLE, IMPOSSIBLE or
      IMAGINARY events.
   2) Not what we WILL do — what we WOULD do under
      different circumstances.
   3) Spanish uses the subjunctive (-ía endings):
        "Si yo tuviera plata… compraría un carro."
   4) English: subjunctive is almost gone, so we use the
      SIMPLE PAST instead.

   STRUCTURE (write big, 2 columns):
      IF CLAUSE                 CONDITIONAL CLAUSE
      VERB IN SIMPLE PAST       WOULD + base verb
      "If I HAD money…"         "…I WOULD buy a car."

   5) "would" = the -ía/-ías/-íamos/-ían endings.
   6) Negative = "would not" / "wouldn't".
   7) Negative & question: same treatment as the FIRST
      conditional (just past + would).

2. CONTRASTE en board (1st vs 2nd), grande:
      1ST (likely):  If I HAVE money, I WILL buy a car.
      2ND (dream):   If I HAD money, I WOULD buy a car.
   DI: "Present + will = real. Past + would = imagined.
   That is the whole difference."

3. ESCRIBE EJEMPLOS del libro (p.89), tal cual:

      If I played, I would play soccer.
      If we traveled, we would fly to Paris.
      If I had a car, I would drive to Bogotá.
      If you found your dog, you would be happy.
      If the girls were hungry, where would they eat?
      If Jeremy was sick, who would he call?
      If we had to go to the store, we wouldn't walk.

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI: "The phrase
   shows it — 'If I HAD no limit, I WOULD lose my peace.'
   Past in the 'if', would in the result. A calm imagining."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "If I WOULD have money, I would buy" ->
   TACHA el primer "would", ESCRIBE "If I HAD money, I would
   buy." DI: "No 'would' in the 'if' side. Past there."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "If I had money, I WILL buy a car" ->
   TACHA "will", ESCRIBE "...I WOULD buy a car." DI: "Dream
   = would, not will." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "If I HAVE money, I would buy" -> TACHA
   "have", ESCRIBE "If I HAD money, I would buy." DI: "The
   imagined 'if' is PAST. 'Had', not 'have'." COHORTE repite
   2 veces.

8. PRACTICA dirigida (oral): TU das la mitad "If I had / If
   you were...", el grupo completa con "would":
        "If I had a free month, ___."
        "If you were the boss, ___."
        "If we won the lottery, ___."
   8-10 vueltas, distintos estudiantes.

9. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien mete "would" en el "if",
   DI suave: "Temperance. Hold back. Past on the 'if' side.
   Only the result takes 'would'."

10. PIDELES en parejas: "Student A imagines a condition
    (past: 'If I had... / If I were...'). Student B finishes
    with the result (would). Then switch. 6 imagined
    situations. 5 minutes."

11. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
    2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
    NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

12. ESCRIBE la regla 6-7 en accion (negativo + pregunta):
        "If they had no money, they wouldn't go to the store."
        "If you worked in London, who would you live with?"
        "If you ate lunch, where would you eat?"
    Practica 4-5 vueltas rapidas en coro.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "If I didn't had money..." -> TACHA,
    ESCRIBE "If I DIDN'T HAVE money..." DI: "Negative on the
    'if' side = didn't + base verb. 'Didn't have', not
    'didn't had'." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

14. PIDELES en parejas round 2: "Now MIX. 6 second
    conditionals total — 4 affirmative, 1 negative ('If I
    didn't..., I wouldn't...'), 1 question ('If you were...,
    what would you...?'). Theme: dreams about your career or
    a different life. 5 minutes."

15. CIRCULA. CORRIGE cuando el mismo error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas.

16. CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo forme "If + past, ... +
    would" sin meter would en el if. DI: "Stand up — we go
    to the simulation. Job interview hypotheticals."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 32 min y aun confunden 1st y 2nd (will vs would, present vs past en el "if"), NO AVANCES al mix. Quedate solo en el contraste de 2 columnas hasta que distingan real/imaginado; deja negativo y pregunta para el opener de Cl 10. Mejor LAND uno bien que rush dos shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas formando second conditional con pasado en el "if" y would en el resultado, distinguiendolo del first.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: JOB INTERVIEW HYPOTHETICALS (~36 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "JOB INTERVIEW HYPOTHETICALS"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the INTERVIEWER asking
   imagined 'what would you do if...' questions. The other
   is the CANDIDATE answering with the second conditional.
   You must use, in the whole conversation, AT LEAST:
     - 5 second conditionals ('If I had a problem, I would
       ask my manager')
     - 1 NEGATIVE ('If I didn't agree, I wouldn't stay
       silent')
     - 2 QUESTIONS ('If you were the boss, what would you
       change?')
   Then rotate."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This interview is the phrase in
   action. 'If I had no limit, I would lose my peace.' You
   imagine calmly — but the dream does not rule the real
   answer."

4. ESCRIBE 2 mini-scenarios en board:

   SCENARIO 1 — Behavioral interview (a new job)
     Interviewer asks hypotheticals, candidate answers:
     "If a client was angry, what would you do?" "If you
     had a deadline problem, who would you call?" "If you
     led the team, how would you start?"

   SCENARIO 2 — Career-change brainstorm with a mentor
     One imagines a different path, the other reacts:
     "If I changed careers, I would study design." "If you
     moved cities, where would you go?" "If we had a bigger
     budget, what would we build?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (5 min): pareja escoge un scenario.
   Roles claros. El entrevistador arranca con un "if".

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English with
   'if + past, would'. Don't freeze."

8. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 (5 min): cambien
   de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si alguien
   mete "would" en el "if" o usa "will", DI suave:
   "Temperance. Past on the 'if', would on the result. Keep
   the dream in its place."

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. OBSERVER COUNT (ligero): en round 2, asigna a 2
    estudiantes como observers — cuentan en silencio
    cuantos second conditionals CORRECTOS (past + would)
    oyeron en la pareja vecina. Reportan: "Pair X — 5
    correct conditionals." No correccion, solo conteo.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (3 preguntas hipoteticas + respuestas). COHORTE
    cuenta cuantos second conditionals correctos cazaron.
    Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("If you were the manager, what would you change first?" / "If a coworker made a mistake, what would you do?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 2 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente + observer count reportado.

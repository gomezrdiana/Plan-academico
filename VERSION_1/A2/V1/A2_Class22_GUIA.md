## CONTEXTO DE HOY

**Módulo 24: PHRASAL VERBS SEPARABLES.**

Contraste con M23 (ayer):
- **M23**: ask FOR, look FOR, depend ON → **NO se separan** ("look FOR my keys")
- **M24 (HOY)**: turn ON, pick UP, take OFF → **SÍ se separan** ("turn the light ON" / "turn it ON")

**Regla CLAVE de M24:**
- Con SUSTANTIVO: se puede separar O juntar. *"Pick up the kids"* = *"Pick the kids up"* ✓
- Con PRONOMBRE (it, them, him, her): **OBLIGATORIO separar.** *"Pick THEM up"* ✓ / *"Pick up THEM"* ✗

**8 phrasal verbs separables del módulo:**
```
turn on / turn off | put on / take off | pick up | look up | write down | throw away
```

## 3. LIBRO: MÓDULO 24 — SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS (00:13–00:28, 15 min)

**CONCEPTO CLAVE:**
```
SEPARABLE PHRASAL VERBS — verb + preposition + object

PATRON 1 (con SUSTANTIVO): se pueden ESCRIBIR juntos o separados
"Pick up the kids."  =  "Pick the kids up."  ← AMBOS OK

PATRON 2 (con PRONOMBRE): OBLIGATORIO separar
"Pick THEM up."  ✓
"Pick up THEM."  ✗ MAL

8 phrasal verbs separables del modulo:
turn on / turn off | put on / take off | pick up | look up | write down | throw away

CONTRAST CON M23 (ayer = NO separables):
M23: I'm looking FOR my keys.   (no separable)
M24: Turn ON the light. / Turn it ON.   (separable)
```

## 7. HISTORIA INTERACTIVA ABSURDA — "EL ROBOT QUE NO PODÍA APAGARSE" (01:08–01:20, 12 min)

**Setup (1 min):** "Sit. Story. Pause + repeat. ENGLISH ONLY."

> "In a small lab in Medellín, a scientist invented a robot named Roberto."

Para. Repiten.

> "Roberto could TURN ON the lights without help."

Para. "Say: 'Roberto could TURN ON the lights.'" Repiten.

> "But Roberto couldn't TURN himself OFF. He couldn't TURN HIMSELF OFF!"

Para. Repiten. Pregunta: "TURN himself off — separable, right?" Clase: "Yes!"

> "So the scientist had to PICK UP Roberto every night and PUT HIM IN a closet."

Para. "Say: 'She had to PICK HIM UP and PUT HIM IN a closet.'" Repiten.

> "One night, Roberto LOOKED UP a recipe for tea on the internet."

Para. "Say: 'Roberto LOOKED UP a recipe.'" Repiten.

> "He TURNED ON the kettle. He PUT ON oven mitts. He POURED OUT the water. He WROTE DOWN the recipe in his memory."

Para. Repiten en cadena rápida.

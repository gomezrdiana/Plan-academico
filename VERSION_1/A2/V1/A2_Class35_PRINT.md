## ⚠️ NOTA SOBRE EL CONTENIDO — M37 WHAT DO YOU LOOK LIKE?

**M37 oficial (libro pág 307-312):** "What Do You Look Like? — Describing People". Objetivos del libro (pág 307, literal): *"Sin dudar, reconoce y pronuncia el vocabulario del módulo cuando se muestra por medio de un gesto (ej. Fingers). Sin dudar, describe las características de una persona. Sin dudar, genera preguntas sobre las características de una persona. Sin dudar, describe a una persona usando los verbos 'to have' and 'to be'. Ej. 'He has black hair.' 'His hair is black.' Sin dudar, usa la palabra 'like' como preposición y distingue entre sus usos adicionales."*

```
CONCEPTO — Describir cómo SE VE / CÓMO ES una persona, y los usos de "like"

GUÍA 1 "What Are You Like?" (libro pág 310, reglas literales):

  1. Aparte de su uso como "gustar", hay varios significados
     adicionales para "like":

       USO                                     INGLÉS              ESPAÑOL
       "Gustar"                                What do you like?   ¿Qué te gusta?
       "Como" (comparación)                    You work like a     Tú trabajas como
                                               donkey.             un burro.
       Idiomático (personalidad)               What are you like?  ¿Cómo eres tú?
       Idiomático (apariencia)                 What do you look    ¿Cómo te ves?
                                               like?
       Estado de ánimo (SIN "like")            How are you?        ¿Cómo estás?

  2. Para describirnos usamos "to be" o "to have":
       "I have a round face."   /  "I am tall."

  3. Podemos usar "very" (muy) para amplificar y "somewhat" (algo)
     para minimizar una característica:
       "I have a very round face."   →  Tengo una cara muy redonda.
       "I am somewhat tall."         →  Estoy algo alto.
```

**Vocabulario 1 — Partes del cuerpo (libro pág 308):**

```
hair (pelo) · a face (una cara) · eyes (ojos) · a nose (una nariz)
a mouth (una boca) · ears (orejas) · arms (brazos) · hands (manos)
fingers (dedos de las manos) · toes (dedos de los pies)
legs (piernas) · feet (pies) · freckles (pecas)
a mustache (bigotes) · a beard (una barba)
```

**Vocabulario 2 — Adjetivos personales (libro pág 309):**

```
big / large (grande) · small / little (pequeño) · young (joven) · old (viejo)
pale skin (palido) · olive skin (trigueño) · dark skin (oscuro)
strong (fuerte) · weak (debil) · tall (alto) · short (bajo/corto)
long (largo) · blond (rubio) · fat (gordo) · slender (delgado) · thin (flaco)
straight (liso) · curly (churco) · wavy (ondulado)
average-height (altura media) · average-weight (peso promedio)
```

**Ejemplos del libro — Describiendo a la Gente (pág 311, literal):**

- *"The man is average height and average weight. He has long, strong arms and short legs. His eyes are round and his nose is big. He has pale skin. He has brown, curly hair. He has a beard and a mustache."*
- *"The woman is young and tall and slender. She has short, straight black hair. She has pale skin. Her arms and legs are long. She has a small mouth and a turned-up nose."*
- *"The man is old and average height. He has a big nose and a big mouth and gray hair. He has big hands. He has pale skin."*

**Conexión M34 → M36 → M37:**
- Cl 33 M34: *"How much does the car cost? — It costs $5."* (PRECIO de cosas)
- Cl 34 M36: *"What are you wearing? — I'm wearing a blue shirt."* (descripción de ropa)
- HOY M37: *"What does she look like? — She has long black hair and is tall."* (descripción FÍSICA de personas con to have / to be + usos de "like")

> M37 NO es ropa ni precio: describe a la PERSONA. Si dicen *"How is she?"* para preguntar apariencia → corta: "How are you?" = estado de ánimo; apariencia = **"What do you look like?"**. Si dicen *"What does she like?"* para apariencia → corta: eso = "qué le gusta"; apariencia = **"What does she LOOK like?"**. Si dicen *"She is long hair"* → corta: con cabello usamos **have** → *"She HAS long hair"* (o **"Her hair IS long"**). Si dicen *"He have a beard"* → corta: tercera singular → **has**. Si dicen *"She has tall"* → corta: característica con adjetivo = **be** → *"She IS tall"*. Si dicen *"She is very somewhat tall"* → corta: **very** o **somewhat**, no los dos.

## 6. LIBRO M37 — LOOK LIKE + HAVE / BE (00:23–00:38, 15 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO:** *"Open Module 37, page 307. What Do You Look Like? Today we stop describing what someone WEARS and start describing what someone IS LIKE and what they LOOK LIKE — their body, their hair, their build. Two verbs: 'to have' for parts ('She has long hair') and 'to be' for adjectives ('She is tall'). And we learn the four uses of 'like' — only one of them is the verb 'to like'."*

### Estructuras (5 min)

```
M37 STRUCTURES (libro pág 310)

A) FOUR USES OF "LIKE"
   "Gustar"            What do you like?       Qué te gusta
   "Como" (comparison) You work like a donkey. Trabajas como burro
   Personality (idiom) What are you like?      Cómo eres tú
   Appearance (idiom)  What do you look like?  Cómo te ves
   (Mood = NO "like")  How are you?            Cómo estás

B) TO DESCRIBE — "to have" or "to be"
   "I have a round face."     (partes del cuerpo, pelo, barba)
   "I am tall."               (adjetivos: tall, slender, strong)

C) AMPLIFIERS
   "very"     (muy)    → "I have a very round face."
   "somewhat" (algo)   → "I am somewhat tall."
   (very OR somewhat — never both at the same time.)
```

Lee. Repiten 2x. Énfasis: "look /lʊk/ like /laɪk/", "she HAS" /hæz/, "He IS" enlazado, "somewhat" /ˈsʌm.wʌt/, "average-height" /ˈæv.rɪdʒ haɪt/.

### Concepto (10 min)

**Tablero:**

```
WHAT DO YOU LOOK LIKE? = ¿Cómo te ves?  (apariencia física)
WHAT ARE YOU LIKE?     = ¿Cómo eres tú? (personalidad)
HOW ARE YOU?           = ¿Cómo estás?   (estado de ánimo — NO "like")

  Have  → partes / pelo / barba / pecas
            "She HAS long hair."   "He HAS a beard."
  Be    → adjetivos físicos
            "She IS tall."         "He IS slender."
  His / Her hair IS long.    (forma alterna con "be")

  very (muy) / somewhat (algo)  — usa UNA, no las dos.

NO "She is long hair" — hair → have. NO "He have a beard" — has.
NO "She has tall" — adjective → be. NO "very somewhat tall".
NO confundir "like" verbo con "like" preposición.
```

**Ejemplos modelados** (libro pág 311, inserción #1 Frase del Día):
- *"The man is average height and average weight. He has long, strong arms and short legs."* (be + have, pág 311)
- *"His eyes are round and his nose is big. He has pale skin."* (have/be alternados, pág 311)
- *"The woman is young and tall and slender. She has short, straight black hair."* (be cadena + have, pág 311)
- *"He has a beard and a mustache."* (have + ítems compuestos, pág 311)
- *"I have a very round face."* / *"I am somewhat tall."* (amplificadores, pág 310)
- *"What does a fair person look like? They have steady eyes, open hands, and a straight back — and they look the same with everyone."* ← Frase del Día

> Estudiantes te explican a TI: pregunta *"Four uses of 'like' — which is which? Which verb for hair, eyes, beard? Which verb for tall, slender, strong? Can we say 'very somewhat tall'? How do we ask about mood, without 'like'?"*

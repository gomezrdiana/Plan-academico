### Cluster 2 — COMPARISON + GER/INF (5 min)

- *"my current job vs my last job"* → comparative (better / worse / more interesting)
- *"the best part of my week"* → superlative
- *"I enjoy ___"* → gerund (-ing)

- *"I look after my niece. I look after ___"* → HER (non-separable: at the end)
- *"I pick up the kids. I pick ___ up"* → THEM (separable: in the middle)

- *"They write the report"* → ✅ *"The report IS WRITTEN"*
- *"They make the cars in Japan"* → ✅ *"The cars ARE MADE in Japan"*

### Cluster 4 — QUESTIONS / AUX / WORD ORDER (5 min)

- *"I'm good ___ math"* → AT (fixed dependency)
- *"I have ___ hour to finish"* → AN (vowel sound)
- *"There is ___ books on the desk"* → MANY (countable plural)

### Cluster 6 — 3rd -S + THERE IS/ARE + CAUSATIVE (5 min)

## 8. SIMULATION — "THE HANDOVER BRIEFING" — 30 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"The closing professional simulation of A2. You hand over your work — your shift, your project, your week — to a colleague. The handover FORCES the six clusters together: tenses (what you did, what is happening, what will be done), comparison (compared to last week), phrasal verbs + passive (what was picked up, what is delivered), questions/word order (the colleague asks), prepositions + articles/quantifiers (where, when, how much), and 3rd -s + there is/are + causative (who does what, what exists, what is arranged)."*

### Setup (4 min)

**Scenario:** Shift / project / week handover to a colleague taking over Monday (everyday role: store, office, support, hotel, restaurant, reception, workshop). The deliverer explains the state of things, what was done, what's pending, automatic processes, key people. The receiver asks what's needed to take control on Monday.

The 6 clusters in context:
- Tenses: *"This week we FINISHED inventory. Right now we ARE REVIEWING the new system. Next Monday you WILL START with..."*
- Comparison + ger/inf: *"This month is BUSIER than last month. We decided TO HIRE one more — they enjoy WORKING the night shift."*
- Phrasal + passive: *"The supplies WERE PICKED UP yesterday. If a client asks for a refund, look IT up in the system. The catalog IS UPDATED by IT every Monday."*
- Questions / aux / order: *"What time DO YOU OPEN? Who DOES the closing? I don't have ANY pending issues."*
- Prepositions + art/quant: *"At 8 we open. On Mondays staff meeting. There are MANY suppliers and A FEW priority ones. Keys are IN the drawer."*
- 3rd -s + there is/are + causative: *"Maria HANDLES reception. She DOESN'T work Fridays. There ARE three pending tickets. We have the office CLEANED every night and get the coffee DELIVERED Mondays."*

**Roles:** A = deliverer · B = receiver.

### Model (4 min)

> *You (A): "Welcome. This week WAS BUSIER than last week. We FINISHED the inventory on Wednesday and right now we ARE REVIEWING the report from supplier B. Maria HANDLES reception — she STARTS at 8 and SHE DOESN'T work Fridays. There ARE three pending tickets: two ARE BEING REVIEWED by IT, one IS WAITING for the client. Supplies WERE PICKED UP on Monday — if more come, pick THEM up and put them IN the second storage room. We have the office CLEANED every night at 9, and get the coffee DELIVERED on Mondays. Don't let the new employee TOUCH the cash register alone — let HIM work next to Carlos first. I decided TO PREPARE a short note — look IT up in the shared folder."*
> *Peer teacher (B): "Yes — what time DOES Maria FINISH on Thursdays? IS THERE a backup if she IS sick? AT what time DO YOU CLOSE the supplier review? And HAVE the new printers BEEN INSTALLED?"*
> *You: "Maria finishes AT 5 on Thursdays. If sick, ask Carlos TO cover. Supplier review CLOSES Friday at 4. Printers WERE INSTALLED last week — they ARE WORKING. If they break, we get them FIXED by the technician — he COMES on Tuesdays."*

### Round 1 — Pairs (12 min)

Each pair: ~6 min each as A. Minimum required: 2 different tenses, 1 comparison or ger/inf, 1 phrasal with pronoun in correct position, 1 passive (with or without agent), 2 questions or negatives with correct order, 2 prepositions + 1 article + 1 quantifier, 1 sentence with 3rd -s + 1 with there is/are + 1 with causative. Receiver asks minimum 4 questions with correct order.

### Round 2 — Swap + new scenario (8 min)

Roles swap, scenario changes. New handover.

### Close (2 min)

1 pair performs in front of the group (90 sec each). Cohort votes: did the 6 families appear correctly? Note errors with NAMES.

> **Insertion #4 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good handover sounds like the phrase — plan the verb, match the subject, place the word. That's A2 ready for B1."*

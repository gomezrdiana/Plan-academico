## 6. SYSTEMATIC A — PRESENT SIMPLE vs PRESENT CONTINUOUS — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the #1 error that blocks B1: choosing between 'I work here' (routine) and 'I am working now' (right now)."*

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
TENSE A — PRESENT SIMPLE vs PRESENT CONTINUOUS  [review/fix]

PRESENT SIMPLE = routine / fact / always true
  "I WORK in an office."   "Water BOILS at 100."
  signals: every day, usually, always, on Mondays, never

PRESENT CONTINUOUS = now / temporary / in progress
  be + verb-ING:  "I AM WORKING now."  "She IS STUDYING this week."
  signals: now, right now, at the moment, today, this week

STATE VERBS: no continuous → know / want / like / need / have(=own)
  WRONG "I am knowing"     RIGHT "I KNOW"
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"She is get angry now."* → ✅ *"She IS GETTING angry now."*
- ❌ *"I am work in a bank."* → ✅ *"I WORK in a bank."* (routine = simple)
- ❌ *"Right now I study."* → ✅ *"Right now I AM STUDYING."*
- ❌ *"He is knowing the answer."* → ✅ *"He KNOWS the answer."* (state verb)
- ❌ *"Every day I am eating at home."* → ✅ *"Every day I EAT at home."*

### Guided practice (5 min)

Board: give a time signal (*"every day" / "right now" / "this week" / "usually"*) + a verb; cohort produces the correct sentence in chorus. 6–8 items mixing simple and continuous, including 2 state verbs.

## 7. SYSTEMATIC B+C — PAST SIMPLE vs CONTINUOUS · PRESENT PERFECT vs PAST SIMPLE — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Two more contrasts that block B1: 'I worked' vs 'I was working when…' and 'I visited Cali in 2019' vs 'I have visited Cali.' We make the choice automatic."*

### Concept B — past simple vs continuous (7 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
TENSE B — PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS  [review/fix]

PAST SIMPLE = finished, complete action
  "I WORKED yesterday."   "She CALLED at 8."
  after DID → base verb:  "I DIDN'T GO." (not "didn't went")

PAST CONTINUOUS = in progress at a past moment / interrupted
  was/were + verb-ING:
  "I WAS WORKING when she called."
  "While I was cooking, the phone rang."
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"Yesterday I get sick."* → ✅ *"Yesterday I GOT sick."* (past simple, irregular)
- ❌ *"I was work when he arrived."* → ✅ *"I WAS WORKING when he arrived."* (was + -ing)
- ❌ *"When she called, I cooked."* (action in progress) → ✅ *"When she called, I WAS COOKING."*
- ❌ *"I didn't went."* → ✅ *"I DIDN'T GO."* (after "did" verb in base)

### Concept C — present perfect vs past simple (8 min)

Board — third section:

```
TENSE C — PRESENT PERFECT vs PAST SIMPLE  [review/fix]

PAST SIMPLE = specific finished time
  "I VISITED Cali in 2019 / last year / yesterday."

PRESENT PERFECT = experience (no time) / since-for / effect now
  have/has + PAST PARTICIPLE:
  "I HAVE VISITED Cali."  "I HAVE LIVED here for 3 years."
  "HAVE you EVER eaten sushi?"   "She HAS already FINISHED."

RULE: a specific past time word → PAST SIMPLE (never perfect)
  WRONG "I have visited Cali last year"
  RIGHT "I visited Cali last year"
```

**Canonical typical errors (NO names):**

- ❌ *"I have visited Cali last year."* → ✅ *"I VISITED Cali last year."* (specific time = past simple)
- ❌ *"I live here since 2019."* → ✅ *"I HAVE LIVED here since 2019."* (since/for = perfect)
- ❌ *"Did you ever eat sushi?"* → ✅ *"HAVE you EVER EATEN sushi?"* (experience = perfect)
- ❌ *"I have went there."* → ✅ *"I HAVE GONE there."* (past participle, not past simple)

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Specific time or experience? Think before you speak — choose the right tense."*

## 8. SPEED DRILL — THE 3 CONTRASTS AT SPEED — 15 min

### Drill 1 — Fast chorus (8 min) — STAND UP

You give signal + verb + hint (A = present simple/cont · B = past simple/cont · C = perfect/past). Cohort answers with full sentence:

```
"now / work" / A             → "I AM WORKING now."
"every day / work" / A       → "I WORK every day."
"this week / study" / A      → "She IS STUDYING this week."
"usually / study" / A        → "She USUALLY STUDIES."
"know" (state) / A           → "I KNOW." (no "am knowing"!)
"yesterday / get sick" / B   → "Yesterday I GOT sick."
"when she called / cook" / B → "I WAS COOKING when she called."
"didn't / go" / B            → "I DIDN'T GO."
"while / cook · phone ring" / B → "While I was cooking, the phone rang."
"in 2019 / visit Cali" / C   → "I VISITED Cali in 2019."
"have / visit Cali" / C      → "I HAVE VISITED Cali."
"for 3 years / live here" / C → "I HAVE LIVED here for 3 years."
"ever / eat sushi" / C       → "HAVE you EVER EATEN sushi?"
"last year / visit Cali" / C → "I VISITED Cali last year." (no "have visited"!)
"have / go there" / C        → "I HAVE GONE there." (no "have went"!)
```

15 items, fast pace. If they fail, repeat until correct chorus.

### Drill 2 — Pairs (7 min) — SIT

*"Face your partner."* Each pair builds 6 sentences, one per category:
- 1 routine (present simple): *"I take the bus every morning."*
- 1 right now (present continuous): *"I am talking to you now."*
- 1 finished past simple: *"Yesterday I finished the report."*
- 1 interrupted past continuous: *"I was cooking when the phone rang."*
- 1 present perfect (no specific time): *"I have worked here for 3 years."*
- 1 explicit contrast perfect vs past: *"I have visited Cali; I went there in 2019."*

1–2 pairs share in front of the group. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

## 9. SIMULATION — "THE DAILY REPORT AT WORK" — 20 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You give a daily report to your supervisor — what you DO every day, what you ARE DOING right now, what you DID yesterday, what you WERE DOING when a problem hit, and what you HAVE DONE so far. The 5 tenses, all correct, under light pressure."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — the 5 forced tenses + 2 cases:

```
THE DAILY REPORT FORCES (5 tenses):
- Routine        → "I usually open the store at 8."
- Now            → "Right now I am checking the inventory."
- Past simple    → "Yesterday I finished the order."
- Past continuous → "I was talking to a client when the system crashed."
- Present perfect → "This week I have completed all reports."

CASE 1 — Store / shop                CASE 2 — Office / customer service
- daily opening / closing            - daily emails / calls
- inventory check                    - report preparation
- a sale that closed yesterday       - a meeting yesterday
- a problem mid-task                 - a problem mid-call
- weekly stock done so far           - weekly tickets done so far
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"Daily report: I normally start at 8 and I CHECK emails first. Right now I AM PREPARING the morning order. Yesterday I CLOSED two big sales. I WAS SERVING a customer when the card machine failed. This week I HAVE TRAINED two new people, but I HAVEN'T FINISHED the schedule yet."*

**Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good daily report sounds like today's phrase — think before you speak, choose the right tense: do, am doing, did, was doing, or have done."*

### Round 1 (7 min) — Trios

Trios. One gives the report (~2 min) using the 5 tenses at least once each; the other 2 are supervisors and each asks 1 question that forces a specific tense (*"And what were you doing when that happened?" / "Have you finished the weekly stock?"*). Rotate: each one reports once.

### Round 2 (5 min) — New scenario

Switch cases (store → office, or vice versa). New report, same 5 tenses forced.

### Close (2 min)

1 trio in front of the group: 60 sec report + 30 sec Q&A. Cohort counts how many of the 5 tenses appeared correctly. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

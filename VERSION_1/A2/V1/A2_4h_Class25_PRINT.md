## 7. SYSTEMATIC A — QUESTIONS / NEGATIVES / AUXILIARIES — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the question and negative errors that block B1: auxiliary first, verb in base form, no double negation, modals without 'to'."*

### Concept (14 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK A — QUESTIONS / NEGATIVES / AUXILIARIES   [review/fix]

WH-QUESTION (M3/M5/M8/M10):
  WH + AUX + SUBJECT + VERB(base)
  "Where DID you GO yesterday?"
  "What DOES she EAT for breakfast?"
  "Why IS HE late?"          <-- BE: invert BE itself
  "Where DOES your brother WORK?"
                              (NEVER "Where works your brother?")
                              (NEVER "Where you go yesterday?")

YES/NO QUESTION:
  AUX + SUBJECT + VERB(base)
  "DO you LIKE coffee?"
  "DOES she WORK here?"
  "DID you GO yesterday?"
  "CAN you HELP me?"         <-- modal + base, NO "to"
                              (NEVER "Can you to help me?")
                              (NEVER "You like coffee?" only intonation)

NEGATIVE:
  SUBJECT + AUX + NOT + VERB(base)
  "She DOESN'T work here."   (NEVER "She no work here")
  "I DIDN'T go."             (NEVER "I no went")
  "He CAN'T come."

ONE NEGATIVE ONLY (no double negation):
  "I don't know ANYTHING."   (NEVER "I don't know nothing")
  "I didn't see ANYBODY."    (NEVER "I didn't see nobody")
  "We didn't go ANYWHERE."   (NEVER "We didn't go nowhere")

DID + BASE (not past):
  "DID you GO?"              (NEVER "Did you went?")
  "DIDN'T she SEE it?"       (NEVER "Didn't she saw it?")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M3/M5/M8/M10/M12, NO names):**

- ❌ *"Where you go yesterday?"* → ✅ *"Where DID you GO yesterday?"* (missing aux DID + base)
- ❌ *"What she eats for breakfast?"* → ✅ *"What DOES she EAT for breakfast?"* (aux DOES + base)
- ❌ *"You like coffee?"* → ✅ *"DO you LIKE coffee?"* (aux DO + subject + base)
- ❌ *"She no work here."* → ✅ *"She DOESN'T work here."* (DOESN'T, not "no")
- ❌ *"I don't know nothing."* → ✅ *"I don't know ANYTHING."* (one negative only)
- ❌ *"Why he is late?"* → ✅ *"Why IS HE late?"* (BE inversion)
- ❌ *"Can you to help me?"* → ✅ *"CAN you HELP me?"* (modal + base, no "to")
- ❌ *"Did you went to the meeting?"* → ✅ *"DID you GO to the meeting?"* (DID + base)
- ❌ *"Where works your brother?"* → ✅ *"Where DOES your brother WORK?"* (full inversion)

### Guided practice (6 min)

Board: give WH + topic (*"Where / weekend" / "What / your job" / "Why / late" / "When / breakfast" / "How / it works" / "Who / boss"*) and/or a Yes/No prompt (*"like coffee" / "work here" / "go yesterday" / "help me"*); cohort produces full question in chorus with AUX first + BASE VERB. 8–10 items mixing WH and Yes/No, present and past, with a modal.

### Close A (4 min)

5 students at random transform: give an affirmative, they make (a) WH question and (b) negative, with correct auxiliary. *"She works in marketing" → "Where DOES she WORK?" + "She DOESN'T work in marketing." / "I went to the meeting" → "DID you GO to the meeting?" + "I DIDN'T go to the meeting."* Note errors with NAMES.

## 9. SYSTEMATIC B — WORD ORDER — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"The other big block before B1: where the adjective goes (BEFORE the noun, always) and where frequency adverbs go (BEFORE the main verb, AFTER the verb BE). 'A RED car', not 'a car red'. 'I ALWAYS drink water', not 'Always I drink water'."*

### Concept B1 — adjective before noun + adjective order (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK B — WORD ORDER   [review/fix]

ADJECTIVE BEFORE NOUN — always:
  "a RED car"                (NEVER "a car red")
  "an INTERESTING book"      (NEVER "a book interesting")
  "a NEW office"             (NEVER "an office new")

MULTIPLE ADJECTIVES — order:
  OPINION  -  SIZE  -  COLOR  -  ORIGIN  -  MATERIAL  +  NOUN
  "a beautiful BIG RED Italian LEATHER bag"
  "a BIG RED car"            (NEVER "a red big car")
  "a small old wooden table" (NEVER "a wooden small old table")
```

### Concept B2 — frequency adverbs before main verb / after BE (10 min)

Third board section:

```
BLOCK B — FREQUENCY ADVERBS
(always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never)

BEFORE the main verb:
  "I ALWAYS drink water."         (NEVER "Always I drink water")
  "She USUALLY arrives at 8."     (NEVER "She arrives usually at 8")
  "We NEVER work on Sundays."     (NEVER "Never we work on Sundays")
  "I USUALLY go to the gym."      (NEVER "I go usually to the gym")

AFTER the verb BE:
  "She IS always late."           (NEVER "She always is late")
  "They ARE usually busy."        (NEVER "They usually are busy")
  "I AM never tired in the morning." (NEVER "I never am tired")

QUANTITY: "A LOT" / "REALLY" (not "much" in affirmative):
  "I like coffee A LOT."          (NEVER "I like much coffee")
  "I REALLY like coffee."         (better than "I like much coffee")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book + B1 Cl 14-18, NO names):**

- ❌ *"A car red."* → ✅ *"A RED car."* (adjective ALWAYS before noun)
- ❌ *"A red big car."* → ✅ *"A BIG RED car."* (size before color)
- ❌ *"Always I drink water."* → ✅ *"I ALWAYS drink water."* (frequency before main verb)
- ❌ *"She always is late."* → ✅ *"She IS always late."* (frequency AFTER BE)
- ❌ *"I go usually to the gym."* → ✅ *"I USUALLY go to the gym."* (before main verb)
- ❌ *"I like much coffee."* → ✅ *"I like coffee A LOT."* / *"I REALLY like coffee."*
- ❌ *"Where works your brother?"* → ✅ *"Where DOES your brother WORK?"* (full inversion)

### Guided practice (4 min)

5–6 students at random: give a scrambled phrase (*"car / red / a"*, *"always / water / I / drink"*, *"is / late / always / she"*, *"red / big / Italian / a / bag"*, *"usually / gym / I / go / the / to"*); they reorder in chorus.

> **Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Adjective or adverb in the wrong place? Plan the order — adjective before the noun, frequency before the main verb, after BE."*

## 11. SIMULATION — "THE JOB INTERVIEW" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"A real job interview. The interviewer asks questions, the candidate answers. The questions FORCE WH + auxiliaries + base verbs, modals without 'to', and the candidate's descriptions FORCE adjective + noun order and frequency adverbs in the right place."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Real job interview (any everyday role: office reception, team assistant, tech support, customer service, shift supervisor). Interviewer asks WH and Yes/No; candidate answers with descriptions using adjectives in correct order and frequency adverbs in correct place.
- Questions: *"Where DID you WORK before? / What DOES your current job include? / Why ARE you the best person for this position? / DO you USUALLY work under pressure? / CAN you START on Monday? / DID you GO to the training last year?"*
- Descriptions: *"I am a HARD-WORKING person. I have a NEW PROFESSIONAL certification. I ALWAYS arrive on time. My current office IS very busy in the morning. I USUALLY handle 20 calls per day."*

**Roles:**
- A = interviewer (min. 6 questions: 3 WH + 2 Yes/No + 1 with modal, all with AUX FIRST)
- B = candidate (answers with min. 2 adjectives in correct order + 2 frequency adverbs in correct place)

### Model (3 min)

> *You (interviewer): "Hello — welcome. Where DID you WORK before this?"*
> *Peer teacher (candidate): "I worked at a SMALL FAMILY company for three years. It WAS a great experience."*
> *You: "What DOES your current role include?"*
> *Candidate: "I USUALLY answer customer emails and I ALWAYS check the inventory at the end of the day."*
> *You: "CAN you START on Monday?"*
> *Candidate: "Yes, I CAN START on Monday. I AM always available in the morning."*
> *You: "Why ARE you the best person for this job?"*
> *Candidate: "I am a RESPONSIBLE, ORGANIZED person and I REALLY like this kind of work."*

### Round 1 — Pairs (10 min)

Each pair: ~5 min each as interviewer. Min. 6 questions covering both blocks (3 WH, 2 Yes/No, 1 with modal). Candidate uses at least: 2 adjectives in correct order (size/color/origin) + 2 frequency adverbs in correct position + answers with AUX / NO double negation.

### Round 2 — Roles swap + new scenario (6 min)

Swap role and position (reception → support, or supervisor, or assistant). New interview.

### Close (3 min)

1 pair performs in front of the group. Cohort counts correct forms by category. Note errors with NAMES.

> **Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** *"A good interview sounds like the phrase — plan the question, put the auxiliary first, the verb in base form, and the adjective before the noun."*

## 12. INTEGRATED SIMULATION — "THE CUSTOMER INQUIRY" — 22 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Integration. A customer asks questions about a product or service. You answer with correct descriptions: adjectives before the noun, frequency adverbs in the right place, modal without 'to', no double negation."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Customer inquiry to an employee (any everyday role: store, hotel reception, phone support, restaurant, tech support).
- Customer: *"Where IS your store? / What DOES this product include? / Why IS this option more expensive? / DO you HAVE it in another color? / CAN I PAY with a card? / What time DOES the shop OPEN?"*

### Forced vocabulary

- Min. 2 WH and 2 Yes/No questions (with auxiliary first + base verb)
- Min. 2 descriptions with adjectives in correct order (at least 1 with two adjectives: size/color/origin)
- Min. 2 frequency adverbs in correct position
- At least 1 modal without "to" and 1 negative with "any-" (no double negation)

### Model (3 min)

> *"Customer inquiry — model: 'Hi, where IS the customer service desk? — It IS on the second floor, near the BIG BLUE sign. — DO you OFFER a discount for repeat customers? — Yes, we USUALLY offer 10% on the second purchase. CAN I PAY with my international card? — Yes, you CAN PAY with any major card. — Why ARE the white shoes more expensive? — Because they are HANDMADE Italian shoes. We DON'T have anything cheaper in that style right now.'"*

### Round 1 — Trios (9 min)

Trios. One as customer (3 min, min. 4 questions: 2 WH + 2 Yes/No + 1 with modal). The other 2 are employees taking turns answering with min. 2 adjectives in correct order + 2 frequencies well placed + 1 negative with "any-". Rotate: each one plays customer once.

### Round 2 — Groups rotate (5 min)

New trios, new scenario (another store/service/support).

### Close (3 min)

1 trio performs in front of the group. Cohort votes: did questions with AUX first + base verb appear? Adjectives before noun? Frequency well placed? One negation only? Note errors with NAMES.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 50 min — FINAL STRUCTURE + MINI-MOCK

### (15 min) Final STRUCTURE walk-through — sections + minutes only

Write on the board (before class) AND read aloud:

```
FINAL — CL 55 — STRUCTURE OVERVIEW
(SECTIONS + MINUTES ONLY. NO items, NO criteria.
 Coordination owns the rubric. External evaluator

 is NOT the proctor.)

  Section 1:  ~20 min      (written component)
  Section 2:  ~30 min      (listening component)

  Section 4:  ~25 min      (oral component)
  Buffer:     ~20 min      (entry, instructions, breaks, transitions)
  TOTAL:       2h           (the last 2 hours of the level — calendar fact)

What you control today:
  - Pacing strategy: don't overspend on section 1.
  - Calibration: do the 8-item mini-mock to check your own
    feel for the cycle.
  - Rest tonight.

What you DO NOT control:
  - Items.   (coordination)
  - Criteria. (coordination)
  - Order in which sections run. (external evaluator decides)
  - Your result. (coordination — released by them)
```

Read out loud once, slowly. Take 3 questions max. Frame for any question:
- *"What's in section 1?"* → *"That's coordination's. The structure is what I have for you — section 1 is ~20 min and written."*
- *"What grade do I need to pass?"* → *"Coordination handles criteria — not me."*
- *"Who's the evaluator?"* → *"Coordination assigns an external evaluator. You'll meet them tomorrow."*

> **DO NOT say:** which items, which topics, which book pages, sample questions, score thresholds, "easy section" / "hard section," what coordination usually asks.

### (35 min) Confidence-building mini-mock — 8 items from the review arc

Sit. Hand out OR write the 8 items on the board, numbered. Cohort works in silence — each student writes their answer on their own paper. NOT GRADED — calibration only.

```
MINI-MOCK — CL 54 — 8 ITEMS (calibration, not evaluation)

(1)  Past simple of GO and present perfect of SEND.

(2)  Comparative of FAST and superlative of GOOD.

(3)  Passive of "they fixed it" (past simple).

(4)  Make a WHO question (subject Wh) about sending an email.

(5)  AT / ON / IN — fill: "I have a meeting ___ Monday ___ 9 AM ___ the office."

(6)  Causative — turn "the mechanic fixed my car" into causative past
     ("I HAD ___ ___ ___.").

(7)  Reported speech — turn "I will call you" into "She said..."

(8)  Conditional 2 — complete: "IF I ___ (have) more time, I ___ (travel)."

[ANSWERS — for teacher only, NOT on the board:
 (1) WENT / HAS SENT or HAVE SENT
 (2) FASTER / THE BEST
 (3) IT WAS FIXED
 (4) WHO SENT the email?  (no DID — subject Wh)
 (5) ON / AT / IN
 (6) I HAD MY CAR FIXED
 (7) She said she WOULD CALL me.
 (8) HAD / WOULD TRAVEL]
```

15 min silent work. Then 15 min review — chorus call:
- You read item, cohort calls answer in chorus.
- Coach in real time: any wrong answer → 1 sentence on WHY the right answer + cohort echo 2×.
- No "right / wrong" tally, no scoring out loud. *"This is calibration — you know what you know."*

Last 5 min: each student silently marks on their own paper their own count of how many they got right — for THEMSELVES, not for collection. *"You know your number. That's yours. Coordination has no access to this paper."*

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2:** *"Give yourself the cycle you actually ran — not the one you wish, not the one you fear."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "ERROR PAPER SHOWCASE"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. The 9 cluster errors from the entire review arc — team competition to correct + post-mortem of own most-used wrong phrases.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"Stand. Two teams — count off 1, 2, 1, 2. On the board I'm putting the 9 most-common errors from the review arc — one per cluster. Teams take turns correcting one error at a time. To correct: name the cluster, give the CORRECT version, give a NEW sentence in your own words. 2 points if all three; 1 point if 2 of 3; 0 if just the correction. After the 9 errors, each of you names YOUR own most-used wrong phrase from the cycle — out loud — and the corrected version. Justice day 4: own the cycle."*

### (25 min) Team competition — 9 errors

Write on the board (before class — large, well-spaced, one per line):

```
ERROR PAPER SHOWCASE — CL 54 — 9 CLUSTERS (review arc)

(1)  X "I work here since 2020."
(2)  X "More fast than the email."
(3)  X "The report was hand in."
(4)  X "Who did sent the email?"
(5)  X "I have much emails."
(6)  X "I had my car fix."
(7)  X "He said me he was busy."
(8)  X "If she will come, we will start."
(9)  X "I can to go now."
```

Run:
- Teams alternate. Team 1 picks an error → names cluster + gives correction + a fresh sentence → score. Then team 2. Until all 9 are corrected.
- After each correction, ALL the cohort echoes the corrected version 1× in chorus.
- You score visibly on a corner of the board. NO student-name attribution to errors. NAMES only in personal notebook.

Expected answers (teacher reference):
```
(1) Tenses        -> "I HAVE WORKED here since 2020."   (present perfect for since/for)
(2) Comp+gerund   -> "FASTER than the email."           (1-syllable adj + -er)
(3) Phrasal+pass  -> "The report WAS HANDED IN."        (past participle)
(4) Questions     -> "WHO SENT the email?"              (subject Wh — no DID, no -ED double)
(5) Prep+art+quant-> "I have MANY emails."              (countable plural = many)
(6) 3rd-s/there/caus-> "I HAD my car FIXED."            (past participle after causative)
(7) Reported      -> "He TOLD ME he was busy."          (told + object)
(8) Conditionals  -> "IF she COMES, we WILL start."     (no WILL after IF in Cond 1)
(9) Modals        -> "I CAN go now."                    (bare verb after modal)
```

### (15 min) Post-mortem of own most-used wrong phrases

Sit. Round the cohort, 90 sec per student max. Each:
1. Names YOUR own most-used wrong phrase from the cycle (e.g., *"I used to say 'I can to go' until cluster 8."*).
2. Gives the corrected version (*"Now I say 'I CAN go.'"*).
3. Names ONE sentence you'll use in tomorrow's Final oral component that proves the fix (*"If they ask about my routine, I'll say 'I CAN handle the morning shift.'"*).

You note 1 phrase per student in personal notebook with NAMES. NOT for cohort.

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3** during post-mortem mid-point: *"Give yourself the cycle you actually ran — own what you fixed."*

### (2 min) Cohort close
Lead a brief applause for the cohort: *"Nine clusters fixed. Own the cycle."*

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE — 15 min

### (5 min) Cohort retrospective — 1 word + 8-word clarifier per student, 30 sec hard stop

Sit. Round the cohort, 30 SECONDS HARD STOP per student (you time):

> *"30 seconds. ONE WORD that names what this level was for you — and ONE clarifier of no more than EIGHT words. Examples: 'Slow. The work was steady, not flashy.' / 'Stretch. Cluster 8 broke open what I was missing.' / 'Honest. I learned what I don't know.' One word + 8 words. Go."*

If a student goes over 30 sec: hand up at 30 sec → *"Thank you."* Move on. NO follow-up questions — protect the time.

You note each retrospective in personal notebook (NAMES) for coordination.

### (3 min) Phrase of the Day close

1. *"Phrase from memory — one student."* Pick one at random.
2. *"Of the three fairnesses today (yourself / future self / tomorrow), which one needs the most attention tonight?"* Pick another at random.
3. *"JUSTICE day 4. One day of Justice left — Cl 55 Final, tomorrow, coordination administers."*

### (5 min) Personal pre-Final ROUTINE (NOT homework) + Cl 55 announce

```
PERSONAL PRE-FINAL ROUTINE — TONIGHT + TOMORROW MORNING
(NOT homework — I do not collect this; nobody checks it.
 This is yours.)

TONIGHT (45 min total):
 - 10 min LIGHT READING — any English text you like
   (article, lyrics, book). Skim, don't study.
 - 10 min — WRITE 5 sentences in YOUR own context — your work,
   your week, your plans. Mix tenses naturally.
 - 5 min BREATHING — slow inhale 4 / slow exhale 6, repeated.

TOMORROW MORNING:
 - Normal breakfast.
 - 5 min QUIET review of your 5 sentences from tonight.
 - Arrive 10 min early — clear room.

RULE: NO new study tonight. NO new vocabulary list. NO mock
exams. The cycle is done. Rest is the work tonight.
```

Read it aloud. *"This is yours. Nobody collects it. The point of the routine is not study — the point is rest."*

I will not be in that room as your teacher tomorrow; you'll meet the evaluator at the door. Today was the last class with me as your regular teacher. See you outside the Final room tomorrow."*

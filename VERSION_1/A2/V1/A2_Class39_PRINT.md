## 6. BOOK M42 — TO GET + CHANGE OF STATE — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Open Module 42, page 333. Getting Crazy! Today we stop describing a state and start marking the CHANGE OF STATE — from 'before' to 'now' — using the verb 'to get' + adjective. Spanish uses many reflexive verbs for this (me enfermo, se pone brava, me perdí); English uses ONE verb — get — in any tense: present, past, future, continuous present, continuous past."*

### Structures (5 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
M42 STRUCTURES (Book p.335, 338)

A) BEFORE → CHANGE OF STATE → NOW
   Not sick   →  I get sick.          →  Sick
   Not happy  →  We get happy.        →  Happy
   Not angry  →  She gets angry.      →  Angry
   Not lost   →  I got lost. (past)   →  Lost

B) "to get" in 5 TENSES (p.338 verbatim):
   Present              → The dog GETS tired.
   Past                 → The dog GOT tired.
   Future               → The dog WILL GET tired.
   Continuous present   → The dog IS GETTING tired.
   Continuous past      → The dog WAS GETTING tired.

C) With PARTICIPLE ADJECTIVES from M41 (p.335 verbatim):
   "She got interested in the class."
   "She got interesting after university."
   (-ED = change in the person; -ING = change in what
    the person causes in others)
```

Read. Cohort repeats 2×. Emphasis: "got" /gɑːt/, "gets" /gɛts/, "getting" /ˈgɛtɪŋ/ (double t), "got married" /gɑːt ˈmæriːd/, "got lost" /gɑːt lɔːst/.

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board:

```
TO GET + ADJECTIVE = CHANGE OF STATE ("llegar a estar ___")

  Present            → He GETS tired.       (general / habit)
  Past               → He GOT tired.        (one specific change)
  Future             → He WILL GET tired.   (a change to come)
  Present continuous → He IS GETTING tired. (happening now)
  Past continuous    → He WAS GETTING tired.(ongoing in past)

Spanish (many verbs)             English (one verb: get)
  me enfermo                  →  I get sick.
  se pone brava               →  She gets angry.
  nos ponemos felices         →  We get happy.
  me perdí                    →  I got lost.
  me casé                     →  I got married.
  me lastimé                  →  I got hurt.
  tu pelo se hizo largo       →  Your hair got long.
  mis amigos se están casando →  My friends are getting married.

With PARTICIPLES (from M41):
  "She got interested in the class."  (change in HER)
  "She got interesting after college."(change in HER EFFECT)
  "The book got boring."              (change in the book)
  "I got bored."                      (change in me)

NEVER:
  ✗ "She get angry."          → "She GETS angry."  (3rd sing + s)
  ✗ "I get lost yesterday."   → "I GOT lost yesterday." (past)
  ✗ "He is get tired."        → "He IS GETTING tired." (gerund)
  ✗ "I got marry."            → "I got MARRIED." (-ED form)
  ✗ "I am sick" (meaning a change) → "I GOT sick." (change)
```

**Book examples (p.336 verbatim):**
- *"Camila got bored in the Spanish class."*
- *"My sister got tall last year."*
- *"Miguel got tired after the game."*
- *"Don't get lost in Bogotá!"*
- *"I got sick last night."*
- *"If you don't keep learning, you will get boring."*
- *"I got confused in my science class."*
- *"If I do not call, my mother will get worried."*
- *"It's almost 7:00 — I have to get up!"*
- *"The class got quiet before the test."*

**Vocabulary (p.334):**
- Irregular verbs: to LOSE (lost/lost), to HURT (hurt/hurt), to SCARE (scared/scared), to MARRY (married/married)
- Adjectives: huge, far, tough, quiet, active, famous

> Students explain to YOU: *"What is the difference between 'I am sick' and 'I got sick'? Why 'gets' with s for she/he/it? Why 'getting' with double t? How do we say 'me casé' / 'me perdí' / 'me enfermé' in English? What is the difference between 'I got bored' and 'I got boring'?"*

## 7. SPEED DRILL — TO GET IN 5 TENSES — 15 min

### Drill 1 — Fast chorus (5 min) — STAND UP

You give subject + adjective + tense hint (P = present / PA = past / F = future / CP = cont. present / CPa = cont. past). Cohort answers:

```
The dog / tired / P            → "The dog gets tired."
The dog / tired / PA           → "The dog got tired."
The dog / tired / F            → "The dog will get tired."
The dog / tired / CP           → "The dog is getting tired."
The dog / tired / CPa          → "The dog was getting tired."
My marks / low / PA            → "My marks got low."
My marks / low / CP            → "My marks are getting low."
Your hair / long / PA          → "Your hair got long."
The boys / active / PA         → "The boys got active."
My friends / married / PA      → "My friends got married."
My friends / married / CP      → "My friends are getting married."
I / bored / P                  → "I get bored."
I / bored / PA                 → "I got bored."
I / bored / CP                 → "I am getting bored."
The book / boring / PA         → "The book got boring."
She / interested in the class / PA → "She got interested in the class."
I / lost in Bogotá / PA        → "I got lost in Bogotá."
The class / quiet / PA         → "The class got quiet."
```

~20 items, fast pace. If they fail, repeat. Emphasis on "gets" (with s) vs "got" (past) vs "getting" (gerund double t).

### Drill 2 — Pairs (10 min) — SIT

Each pair writes **8 sentences** using "to get" + adjective of state, **one in each of the 5 tenses**, with at least:
- 2 with a PARTICIPLE adjective from M41 (bored, excited, interested, confused, disappointed, frustrated, surprised, worried)
- 2 with M42 new vocabulary (huge, far, tough, quiet, active, famous, lost, hurt, married)
- 2 with a physical change (tired, sick, hungry, tall, late)
- 2 with 3rd person singular subject (he / she / it / my brother / the class) to force "gets" or "got"

At 8 min, swap pairs + check **gets/got/will get/is getting/was getting · -ED vs -ING participles · married (not marry) · getting (double t)**.

## 8. SIMULATION — "THE SHIFT CHANGE REPORT" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You are finishing a shift (store, office, clinic, call center, or kitchen) and you give a SHIFT CHANGE REPORT to the next person taking over. You describe what happened using past changes of state ('the customer got angry', 'the system got slow', 'two clients got lost') and ongoing changes ('the line is getting longer', 'the kitchen is getting hot'). The next person responds with one future prediction ('it will get busy at 7') and one ongoing change."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — report frame + 2 cases:

```
THE SHIFT CHANGE REPORT USES (to get + adjective, 5 tenses):
- Past: "It GOT ___."           ("The customer got angry.")
- Past: "They GOT ___."         ("Two clients got lost.")
- Cont. present: "It IS GETTING ___." ("The line is getting longer.")
- Cont. past: "It WAS GETTING ___."   ("The kitchen was getting hot.")
- Future: "It WILL GET ___."    ("It will get busy at 7.")
- Present: "She GETS ___ when…" ("She gets nervous when…")

CASE 1 — Retail store / cashier      CASE 2 — Clinic reception
- a customer (angry / impatient)     - a patient (worried / lost)
- the line (getting longer)          - the waiting room (getting full)
- the boss (worried / surprised)     - a kid (getting tired)
- the new employee (getting tired)   - the doctor (got busy)
- the system (got slow)              - the phone (got crazy)
- closing time (will get busy)       - the day (will get long)
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"During my shift, three things happened. The line got long around 5. The new employee got tired after two hours and got confused with the prices. A customer got angry because the system got slow. Right now the line is getting shorter, but the boss is getting worried about closing. Tomorrow it will get busy again at 7."*

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"I got tired in this shift, but I did not get weak — and now I am getting up again to hand it over to you, even when no one applauds."*

### Round 1 (8 min) — Pairs

One = outgoing employee (gives the SHIFT CHANGE REPORT: 4+ sentences, **minimum 2 with "got"** (past), **minimum 1 with "is getting"** (cont. present), **minimum 1 with a participle adjective** like "got worried / got confused / got surprised / got lost / got bored"). The other = incoming employee (responds with **1 future** "It will get ___" + **1 cont. present** "I am getting ___ already"). Cover one full case.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Switch

Switch roles **and** case (the outgoing now reports the other place; force different "got ___" changes, at least 1 with M42 new vocabulary (huge, far, tough, quiet, active, famous, lost, hurt, married), at least 1 with "was getting ___" (past continuous) and at least 1 with "gets" (present habit, "she gets nervous when…")).

### Close (3 min)

1 pair in front of the group: 60 sec live shift change report + 30 sec Q&A. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

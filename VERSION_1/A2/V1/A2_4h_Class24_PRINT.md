## 7. SYSTEMATIC A — PHRASAL VERBS — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Not a new module — the A2 book is done. We CORRECT the phrasal verb errors that block B1: where the pronoun goes — at the end (non-separable) or in the middle (separable)."*

### Concept (14 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK A — PHRASAL VERBS   [review/fix]

NON-SEPARABLE  (M23):  never split / pronoun AT THE END
  look after / look at / listen to / run into / ask for / go back
  "I look after Amy."        "I look after HER."
                              (NEVER "I look her after.")
  "Listen to the teacher."   "Listen to HIM."
                              (NEVER "Listen him to.")

SEPARABLE  (M24):  noun = either side / pronoun IN THE MIDDLE
  turn on/off · pick up · look up · put on · take off
  "I pick up Amy."  =  "I pick Amy up."   <-- noun: either side
  "I pick HER up."                          <-- pronoun: MIDDLE
                              (NEVER "I pick up her.")
  "Turn IT on."  /  "Look IT up."
                              (NEVER "Turn on it." / "Look up it.")

CONJUGATION: tense goes on the VERB (first word):
  "She picks them up." / "She picked them up." / "She is picking them up."
                              (NEVER "She is pick them up.")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M23/M24, NO names):**

- ❌ *"I look her after."* → ✅ *"I look AFTER her."* (non-separable, pronoun AT THE END)
- ❌ *"I picked up her."* → ✅ *"I picked HER up."* (separable, pronoun IN THE MIDDLE)
- ❌ *"Look up it in the dictionary."* → ✅ *"Look IT up in the dictionary."* (separable)
- ❌ *"Turn on it, please."* → ✅ *"Turn IT on, please."* (separable)
- ❌ *"She is looking her sister after."* → ✅ *"She is looking AFTER her sister."* (non-separable, no split)
- ❌ *"I run into him yesterday."* → ✅ *"I ran INTO him yesterday."* (past conjugated on the VERB)
- ❌ *"Listen him to."* → ✅ *"Listen to HIM."* (non-separable, pronoun at end)

### Guided practice (6 min)

Board: give a phrasal verb + a pronoun (*"look after — her" / "pick up — them" / "look up — it" / "listen to — him" / "turn on — it" / "run into — her" / "take off — them" / "ask for — it"*); cohort produces the sentence with the pronoun in the correct position in chorus. 8–10 items mixing separable and non-separable.

### Close A (4 min)

5 students at random transform: give a sentence with a noun, they replace with a pronoun in the CORRECT position. *"I look after my niece" → "I look after her" / "I pick up the kids" → "I pick them up"*. Note errors with NAMES.

## 9. SYSTEMATIC B — PASSIVE VOICE — 24 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"The other big block before B1: when the actor is unknown or unimportant, we put the action ON THE SUBJECT. To be + past participle + (by + actor). 'The report IS WRITTEN every week' — never 'is wrote'."*

### Concept B1 — structure + participle (10 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
BLOCK B — PASSIVE VOICE   [review/fix]   (A2 Book M18)

ACTIVE:   Subject (agent) + verb + object (patient)
          "They prepare the lunch at 12:00."

PASSIVE:  Subject (patient) + TO BE + PAST PARTICIPLE + (by + agent)
          "The lunch IS PREPARED at 12:00 (by them)."

KEY:  the verb is ALWAYS the PAST PARTICIPLE (V3), never V1 / never V2:
   write -> WRITTEN   make -> MADE   take -> TAKEN
   give  -> GIVEN     do   -> DONE   see  -> SEEN

TO BE matches the TENSE you need:
   present:    is / are     PRESENT:   "The report IS WRITTEN."
   past:       was / were   PAST:      "The report WAS WRITTEN."
   continuous: is/are being           "The report IS BEING WRITTEN."
   perfect:    has/have been          "The report HAS BEEN WRITTEN."
```

### Concept B2 — "by + agent" is OPTIONAL (10 min)

Third board section:

```
BLOCK B — when to USE "by + agent" and when to DROP it

USE "by" when the actor MATTERS / is named:
   "The decision was made BY US."
   "The book was written BY Garcia Marquez."

DROP "by + agent" when the actor is:
   - unknown:     "The window WAS BROKEN." (we don't know who)
   - unimportant: "The lunch IS PREPARED at 12:00." (the cook is irrelevant)
   - obvious:     "The cars ARE MADE in Japan." (factory workers, obvious)

DO NOT MIX active and passive in the same transformation:
   ACTIVE:  "They make the cars in Japan."
   PASSIVE: "The cars ARE MADE in Japan."
            (NEVER "The cars make in Japan.")
```

**Canonical typical errors (from A2 Book M18 + B1 Cl 14 Grammar, NO names):**

- ❌ *"The report is wrote every week."* → ✅ *"The report IS WRITTEN every week."* (V3 = written)
- ❌ *"The decision was make by us."* → ✅ *"The decision WAS MADE by us."* (V3 = made)
- ❌ *"The report is review every week."* → ✅ *"The report IS REVIEWED every week."* (participle, not base)
- ❌ *"The door is broken by the wind every storm."* → ✅ *"The door IS BROKEN every storm."* (irrelevant agent → drop "by")
- ❌ *"The lunch prepares at 12:00."* → ✅ *"The lunch IS PREPARED at 12:00."* (missing TO BE)
- ❌ *"The cars are make in Japan."* → ✅ *"The cars ARE MADE in Japan."* (V3 = made)
- ❌ *"The cars make in Japan."* → ✅ *"The cars ARE MADE in Japan."* (don't mix active/passive)

### Guided practice (4 min)

5–6 students at random: give an active sentence (*"They review the report every week" / "Someone broke the window" / "They make the cars in Japan" / "We made the decision"*); they transform to passive and decide whether to keep or drop "by + agent".

> **Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Actor unknown or unimportant? Plan the verb — put the action on the subject."*

## 11. SIMULATION — "THE HELP DESK CALL" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"A real help desk call. A customer calls because something is broken / can't be turned on / needs to be picked up. The questions FORCE phrasal verbs AND passive voice correctly."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Scenario:** Real help desk / support call (any everyday role: tech support, store customer service, office reception, maintenance). Caller explains a problem; agent asks details and proposes actions. Questions and answers force both blocks:
- Phrasal verbs: *"Did you turn it on? When was the package picked up? Who do I ask for? Can you look it up in your system?"*
- Passive: *"The order was placed yesterday. The system is being updated. The decision was made by my supervisor. The product is not made anymore."*

**Roles:**
- A = customer / caller (describes problems, asks questions forcing both blocks)
- B = support agent / reception (responds using phrasals with pronouns in correct position + passive)

### Model (3 min)

> *You (customer): "Hi — the printer in the office is not working. I tried to turn it on, but nothing happened. Was it updated last week?"*
> *Peer teacher (agent): "Let me look it up in the system. Yes — the printer was updated last Friday. The driver was reinstalled by IT. Did you pick up the new cable that was sent?"*
> *You: "No, I didn't pick it up yet. Who should I ask for at reception?"*
> *Agent: "Ask for Maria. She looks after the equipment, and the cables are kept in her office."*

### Round 1 — Pairs (10 min)

Each pair: ~5 min each as agent. Caller asks min. 6 questions/comments covering both blocks (3 with phrasal verbs, 3 with passive). Agent must use at least: 1 non-separable phrasal with pronoun at end, 1 separable phrasal with pronoun IN THE MIDDLE, 1 passive in present, 1 in past, 1 with "by" + agent, 1 without "by" — correctly.

### Round 2 — Roles swap + new scenario (6 min)

### Close (3 min)

## 12. INTEGRATED SIMULATION — "THE PROCESS BRIEFING" — 22 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Integration. You explain a real process at work: what is done, by whom (or no agent), and which phrasal verbs are part of the routine — with the pronouns in the right place."*

**Scenario:** Brief workplace process briefing to a new colleague (store shift, office reception, restaurant, support, maintenance). Each person gives a briefing forcing both blocks:

- Min. 2 different phrasal verbs (1 non-separable with pronoun AT END + 1 separable with pronoun IN THE MIDDLE)

## 7. LIBRO M26 — EQUAL COMPARATIVES (00:34–00:53, 19 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"M26 — Equal Comparatives. Comparing things AS equal. Pattern: 'AS + adjective + AS'. Today we lock the structure positive, negative, and almost."*

**Setup (2 min):** *"Open Module 26 — 'I'm as Big as My Dad'. Read with partner. 7 min."*

**Lectura (7 min):** parejas leen en silencio. Hojas: 241-245 del libro.

**Explicación dirigida (10 min)** — tú NO explicas, los estudiantes te explican respondiendo:

1. ¿Cuál es la fórmula del comparativo igual? → *"AS + adjective + AS"* (X is as ___ as Y)
2. ¿Cómo se hace el negativo? → *"NOT AS + adjective + AS"* (X is NOT as ___ as Y)
3. ¿Qué significa ALMOST AS? → casi tan (un poquito menos)
4. ¿3 ejemplos del libro?
   - *"I am as tall as Carlos."*
   - *"My car isn't as new as your car."*
   - *"Your father is almost as old as my father."*
5. ¿Error común a evitar? → NO doblar comparador. NO decir *"as more tall as"*. Es solo *"as tall as"*.
6. ¿Funciona con cualquier adjetivo? → SÍ — adjetivos cortos o largos, todos van entre AS...AS.

> Consolidación: si dudan en algo básico, repite hasta que salga sin pensar.

### Setup (3 min)

**Lee al cohorte EN INGLÉS, sin prisa:**

```
THE GOLDLIST PURGE — HOW IT WORKS:

The method trusts that your brain absorbed phrases passively — without studying.
We don't review or rehearse. We verify.

Step by step:
1. Open your notebook to LIST 1 (the very first one — Class 1).
2. Cover the English column with your hand or a paper.
3. Read ONE Spanish phrase silently.
4. Pause. Does the English version come to your mind FAST and FLUID?
5. If YES → CROSS OUT that phrase (line through it).
6. If you hesitate, doubt, or can't → LEAVE IT. No cheating.
7. Continue to the next phrase. Same procedure.

After: count how many you crossed out. Then on a NEW page write
"D1 of List 1" + the date, and re-copy ONLY the un-crossed phrases.
Those are still in passive memory — not active yet.

This is not a test. This is verification.
There is no "passing" or "failing".
There is only honesty.
```

### Modelado por el profesor (2 min)

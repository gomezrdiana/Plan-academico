## 6. BOOK M44 — WANT/NEED/WOULD LIKE + OBJ + TO + VERB — 15 min — SIT

**Objective:** *"Open Module 44, page 347. I Want You to Go! Today we stop describing HOW we do something and start expressing the desire that SOMEONE ELSE do it. We never use 'that' with want/need/would like — never 'I want that you go.' Always 'I want YOU TO GO.' The pattern: subject + want/need/would like + another person (object pronoun me / you / him / her / us / them) + to + verb in infinitive + complement. Also: 'I want to go' (same subject — I go) vs 'I want YOU to go' (different subject — you go)."*

### Structures (5 min)

Write on the board (before class):

```
M44 STRUCTURES (Book p.348)

A) AFFIRMATIVE — Subject + want/need/would like + OBJ + to + verb

   I            want          you            to go         to the store.
   You          want          me             to go         to the store.
   I            need          the teacher    to teach      the class.
   The teacher  would like    the student    to study      the guides.
   Brayan       wants         us             to play       soccer.
   Eddy         wants         Norman         to see        the new house.

B) SAME SUBJECT vs CHANGE OF SUBJECT
   I want to go.              (I go)
   I want YOU to go.          (YOU go — change of subject)

   Connie needs to talk to you.       (Connie talks)
   Connie needs YOU to talk to her.   (YOU talk — change of subject)

C) NEGATIVE — with don't / doesn't / didn't BEFORE want / need / would like
   I do NOT want you to leave.
   She does NOT need us to wait.
   They did NOT want me to call.

D) INTERROGATIVE — Yes/No (do/does/did + subject + want + OBJ + to + verb)
   Do you want me to help?         → Yes, I want you to help. / No, I don't.
   Does he need us to come?        → Yes, he needs us to come.

E) INTERROGATIVE — Wh- (p.351)
   Where do you want US to go?         → I want you to go to France.
   When do you need ME to finish?      → I need you to finish before 11.
   What does the teacher need US to study? → She needs us to study English.
   Who do you want ME to share with?   → I want you to share with your sister.

F) NEVER:
   ✗ "I want THAT you go."           → "I want YOU TO go."
   ✗ "I want you go."                → "I want you TO go." (missing "to")
   ✗ "I want you going."             → "I want you TO go." (infinitive, not -ing)
   ✗ "She wants I to go."            → "She wants ME to go." (object pronoun)
   ✗ "The teacher needs WE study."   → "The teacher needs US to study."
   ✗ "Do you want that I help?"      → "Do you want ME to help?"
```

Read. Cohort repeats 2×. Emphasis: link "want-you-to" /ˈwɒnt.juː.tuː/, "need her to", "would like us to", object pronouns **me / him / her / us / them**, contrast "I want to GO" (I) vs "I want YOU to go" (you).

### Concept (10 min)

Write on the board:

```
I WANT YOU TO GO! = Quiero que (tú) vayas.

  Subject + [want / need / would like] + OBJ + to + verb

  OBJ = object pronoun:  me / you / him / her / us / them
                       + name (Peter, the teacher, the kids)

  SAME subject:    I want to go.          (I go)
  CHANGE of subject: I want YOU to go.    (YOU go)

  NEGATIVE:    I don't want you to leave.
  QUESTION:    Do you want me to help?
  Wh-:         What do you need me to do?

NEVER:
  ✗ "I want THAT you go."   → "I want YOU TO go."
  ✗ "I want you go."         → "I want you TO go."
  ✗ "She wants I to go."     → "She wants ME to go."
  ✗ "Want you me to help?"   → "Do you want me to help?"
```

**Book examples (p.349-351 verbatim):**
- *"Brayan wants us to play soccer."*
- *"Connie needs you to talk to her."* (change of subject)
- *"David would like the girls to take a cookie."*
- *"My parents would like us to come to Bucaramanga."*
- *"The teacher needs you to hand in your test."*
- *"Your friends want you to play with them."* (change of subject)
- *"Where do you want us to go? — I want you to go to France."* (p.351)
- *"When do you need me to finish the exam? — You need to finish it before 11."* (p.351)

> Students explain to YOU: *"Why no 'that'? What is the difference between 'I want to go' and 'I want YOU to go'? Which pronoun comes after want — subject or object? How do we ask 'Do you want me to help?' Why 'to go' and not 'to going' or just 'go'?"*

## 8. SIMULATION — "THE PROJECT KICK-OFF MEETING" — 25 min — STAND UP

**Objective:** *"Real workplace scene. You run a 10-minute project kick-off meeting. You ASSIGN tasks to your teammates using 'I want / I need / I would like + [person] + to + verb + complement.' You ASK what others need from you with 'Do you want me to ___? / What do you need me to do?' You include at least one negative ('I don't want anyone to ___') and at least one Wh- question. Real workplace assignment — desire that another person do something, not a command."*

### Setup (3 min)

Board — meeting list + 2 cases:

```
THE PROJECT KICK-OFF MEETING ASKS (want / need / would like + OBJ + to + verb):
- I want [person] to [verb] [complement].
- I need [person] to [verb] [complement].
- I would like [person] to [verb] [complement].
- Do you want me to ___?     / What do you need me to do?
- I don't want anyone to ___.

CASE 1 — Marketing launch (5 roles)     CASE 2 — Restaurant opening (5 roles)
- write the email                        - prepare the menu
- design the flyer                       - hire 2 waiters
- call the printer                       - call the food supplier
- post on Instagram                      - clean the kitchen
- prepare the report for Friday          - greet the first guests Friday
```

### Model (3 min)

> *"OK team — kick-off. I want Maria to write the email by Wednesday. I need Carlos to design the flyer. I would like the printer to send the proof on Thursday. I don't want anyone to post on Instagram until Friday morning. Do you want me to send you the brand guidelines? What do you need me to do before Wednesday? Where do you need the team to meet on Friday? When would you like us to review the final draft?"*

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"And one rule for this kick-off — I want you to pause before you act. Prudence is not a brake; it is the wisdom that asks one person to think before the whole group moves. So before anyone presses 'send', I want one person to ask: have we checked it?"*

### Round 1 (8 min) — Groups of 3-4

One = project lead (assigns 4+ tasks with **want / need / would like + OBJ + to + verb**, minimum 1 with each volition verb, minimum 1 negative, minimum 1 Wh- question); the others = team members (respond with *"Do you want me to ___?"* / *"I need you to give me ___."* / *"What do you want me to do first?"*). Cover one full case.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Switch

Switch roles **and** case (new lead works the other project; force volitions with different people and tasks, minimum 1 contrast "I want to ___" (I) vs "I want YOU to ___" (you), at least 1 yes-no question and 1 Wh-).

### Close (3 min)

1 group in front of the cohort: 60 sec live kick-off + 30 sec Q&A. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

## 6. HOT TOPIC — "HOW DO PEOPLE CHANGE IN THIS WORK WEEK?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — Grammar topic is structural (changes of state with GET / BECOME / GO / TURN), not narrative. So today is pure speaking. Your work week as a series of CHANGES. Juan Diego names the technical table this morning in the second block — book p.335: 'English uses the verb to get or (very formally) to become; this can be used in any tense.'"*

Sample patterns:
- *"I GOT tired by 6 PM on Tuesday."* (quick → GET past)
- *"My colleague GETS excited every time we close a deal."* (recurring → GET present)
- *"The project is GETTING complex this quarter."* (in progress → GET continuous)
- *"She BECAME a senior manager in three years."* (formal/permanent → BECOME)
- *"The team WENT crazy when the budget was cut."* (idiomatic → GO)
- *"I TURNED 30 last month."* (age → TURN)

### Hot Topic debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "HOW DO PEOPLE CHANGE IN THIS WORK WEEK?"

Discuss with your partner — describe YOUR last work
week as a series of CHANGES using GET / BECOME / GO /
TURN:

1. Pick TWO CHANGES IN YOU this week using GET (quick
   everyday):
   "I GOT tired by 6 PM every day — too many meetings."
   "I GOT excited on Wednesday when the new client
    said yes."

2. Pick TWO CHANGES IN A COLLEAGUE this week using
   GET or BECOME:
   "My boss GOT angry on Friday when the report was
    late."
   "My colleague is BECOMING more confident with the
    dashboard."

3. Pick ONE IDIOMATIC change (GO crazy / GO red / GO
   bald / GO quiet) or AGE change (TURN + number):
   "The team WENT quiet when the CEO joined."
   "Our supervisor TURNED 50 last week."

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ get/become/go/turn forms used correctly
- 2+ work-specific things (meetings, projects, deals,
  deadlines, hires)
- 1 JUSTICE closure: name a colleague's change with
  the right word — no inflation, no erasure
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

"THE TEAM CHECK-IN" — 3-4 min per round

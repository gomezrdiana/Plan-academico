## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — three structures in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model EACH structure out loud.

> *"Either, Neither, Both in real work dialogue — three structures, one rule each. ONE: EITHER…OR — one of two options, picking. Position matters — 'either' can land before a noun ('I want either coffee or tea'), before a verb ('I can either come or send a note'), or before 'of' for indifference ('either of the two contracts works for me'). TWO: NEITHER…NOR — zero of two options, refusing. Critical trap: never with 'not'. Spanish says 'no puedo hablar ni español ni inglés' — three negatives. English says 'I can speak neither Spanish nor English' — ONE negative carried by 'neither'. Saying 'I can't speak neither' is a double negative, wrong. THREE: BOTH…AND — two of two options, taking both. 'I can both write the proposal and run the demo.' Also: 'both' takes plural agreement ('Both contracts are signed'), 'either of' and 'neither of' take singular ('Neither of the contracts IS signed'). And in fast speech — agreement responses. Positive: 'Me too / So do I / I do too.' Negative: 'Me neither / Neither do I.' Justice day 5: the structure forces you to NAME the cost — saying 'neither' commits, saying 'both' commits. No fence-sitting."*

Demo on the board:

```
EITHER  → 1 of 2 (picking)        verb = singular if "either of"
NEITHER → 0 of 2 (refusing)       verb = singular if "neither of"
                                  NEVER with "not"
BOTH    → 2 of 2 (taking both)    verb = plural
AGREE+ → "Me too / So do I / I do too"
AGREE- → "Me neither / Neither do I"
```

Coach softly while you walk: double negatives, wrong verb agreement, mixed-up positions, "both" used where "also" or "too" belongs. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook. ## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "BOSS / TEAM DECISION MEETING"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"Stand. You're running a weekly leadership decision meeting. You'll work in triads — three roles, rotating. BOSS opens the meeting and presents 4 binary decisions to the TEAM MEMBER — budget A or budget B, hire senior or hire two juniors, ship Friday or ship Tuesday, in-person event or virtual event. TEAM MEMBER answers each with either/or, neither/nor, or both/and — and in 1 sentence names the cost of the choice. The TEAM MEMBER also throws back a fifth, sixth, seventh, eighth decision to the BOSS. So the round is 8 decisions total. OBSERVER tracks 3 things on the grid I'm handing out: how many decisions use the structure correctly, how many double negatives slip in ('I can't… neither' = automatic fail), and whether the speaker actually NAMED THE COST or just dodged. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. Justice day 5 — closing: own the choice, name the cost, decide out loud."*

Hand out printed observer grid (prepared before class):


### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Roles assigned. BOSS opens, runs 4 decisions, TEAM MEMBER throws back 4. Each answer carries the structure + the cost. Observer marks the grid. You circulate triad by triad — coach the structure when wrong, kill double negatives instantly (*"redo without the 'not'"*), and push for the COST sentence when it gets dropped.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate. New BOSS, new TEAM MEMBER, new OBSERVER. Same 8-decision structure. By now the cohort knows the rhythm — push for SPEED. Decisions should land in 5 seconds, not 15. Push for at least one BOTH…AND per round (most cohorts default to either/or — they avoid both because it commits to more work).

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Every student has now played every role. Push for COMPLEXITY: at least one decision per BOSS must include "neither of the two options" with correct singular verb agreement, and at least one agreement response in fast speech ("So do I / Neither do I" — full inverted form, not just "Me too / Me neither").

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4 during round 2 transition: *"Justice day 5 closes the block — name the cost or the decision doesn't count. Both, either, or neither — own it."*

### (2 min) Cohort close
One strong triad demos 90 sec in front of the group — full BOSS open, 2 decisions, TEAM MEMBER answers + throws back 1, observer reports back. Applause.

> *"Justice day five closes today. Next class opens FORTITUDE — day one of five — but that's next class. Today you weighed, named, decided, owned."*

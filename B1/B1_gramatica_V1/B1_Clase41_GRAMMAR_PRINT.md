```
PHRASE OF THE DAY — CL 41 GRAMMAR
"On day one of prudence we back-shift before

 'is working'), 'He TOLD me he HAD FINISHED'
 (not 'finished'); the verb steps one foot

```

Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Two truths on the board. (1) When we report what someone said in the past, the inner verb back-shifts — present becomes past, past becomes past perfect, will becomes would, can becomes could. (2) Prudence is the virtue of thinking before you commit — and reporting speech is exactly that: you pause, you adjust the tense, you don't blurt the original verb. Book p.93 verbatim: 'When the control verb moves one step into the past, all dependent verbs also move one step into the past.' Same auxiliary stepped back. Every time."*

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing board race
Two columns on the board. You call a present-tense verb; the first student of each row runs to the board and writes the back-shifted (past) form. Cohort verifies. 10 rounds in 3 minutes.

```
"says"      → SAID
"tells"     → TOLD
"asks"      → ASKED
"plays"     → PLAYED  (then → had played in report)
"is"        → WAS
"are"       → WERE
"have"      → HAD
"will"      → WOULD
"can"       → COULD
"must"      → HAD TO
```

Quick note as you erase: *"These are the 10 most-used shifts in B1 reported speech. Two on this list are tricky — MUST → HAD TO (not 'musted'), and WILL → WOULD (not 'willed'). Book p.93 lists the full table; we drill it next."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — form rules from the book

Stand at the board. Book p.89 + p.93 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M8 — REPORTED SPEECH — FORM RULES (B1 Book p.83-96)

(1) SAY vs TELL vs ASK (Guide 2, p.89):
    SAY  = decir → direct words; indirect object
           OPTIONAL with "to":  "I said hello (to
           Janel)."
    TELL = contar → indirect object REQUIRED, no
           preposition: "I told my mom the time."
    ASK  = preguntar → indirect object REQUIRED;
           for yes-no use IF/WHETHER:
           "I asked Andres IF he had finished."

(2) BACK-SHIFT TABLE (Guide 3, p.93):
    Mark says...           → Mark said...
    he plays soccer        → he played soccer
    he played soccer       → he had played soccer
    he has played soccer   → he had played soccer
    he is playing soccer   → he was playing soccer
    he is going to play    → he was going to play
    he will play           → he would play
    he can play            → he could play
    he must play           → he had to play

(3) PRONOUN + TIME + PLACE SHIFT:
    I       → he / she
    we      → they
    today   → that day
    now     → then
    tomorrow → the next day / the following day
    yesterday → the day before / the previous day
    here    → there
    this    → that
    these   → those

(4) NO BACK-SHIFT when:
    - Reporting verb is present ("She SAYS she IS
      busy")
    - The fact is universally true ("He said the
      sun IS hot")
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.85 verbatim quote (drilled out loud): *"Daniella said that she was happy / Daniella told me where she lives / Daniella asked me if I was sick."* Book p.93 verbatim: *"When using relative pronouns, when the 'control verb' moves one step into the past, all dependent verbs also move one step into the past."* Book p.93 example: *"Mark, 'I am happy.' (Present) → Mark said that he was happy. (Past simple)."*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"Back-shift before you report. Prudence day 1 — think before you commit."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners:
- **SAY (no object req'd)**
- **TELL (object req'd)**
- **ASK (object req'd, or IF/WHETHER)**
- **BACK-SHIFT (verb steps back)**

Stand in the middle. Call a direct-speech sentence out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone of the right reporting verb + speak the full reported version, applying back-shift as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 16 calls in 15 min, ~1 per minute.

```
Direct speech called                              → Right zone + reported version
1.  "Mark: 'I am happy.'"                         → SAY      — "Mark said (that) he was happy."
2.  "Sara to me: 'Your laptop is broken.'"        → TELL     — "Sara told me my laptop was broken."
3.  "Boss: 'Are you free at 3?'"                  → ASK      — "The boss asked if I was free at 3."
4.  "Carlos: 'I have finished.'"                  → BACK-SHIFT — "Carlos said he HAD finished."  (pres perf → past perf)
5.  "Marta to Juan: 'I will call you.'"           → TELL     — "Marta told Juan she would call him."
6.  "Diego: 'Where do you live?'"                 → ASK      — "Diego asked where I lived." (no aux, affirmative)
7.  "Ana: 'I can swim.'"                          → BACK-SHIFT — "Ana said she COULD swim."
8.  "Lead: 'I am working on the bug now.'"        → BACK-SHIFT — "The lead said she WAS WORKING on the bug THEN."
9.  "Client: 'You must reply today.'"             → BACK-SHIFT — "The client said I HAD TO reply THAT DAY."
10. "Maria: 'I went to Bogotá yesterday.'"        → BACK-SHIFT — "Maria said she HAD GONE to Bogotá THE DAY BEFORE."
11. "Manager to team: 'We need help.'"            → TELL     — "The manager told the team they needed help."
12. "PM: 'Did you finish the deck?'"              → ASK      — "The PM asked if I HAD finished the deck."
13. "QA: 'It's broken.'"                          → SAY      — "QA said it was broken."
14. "Recruiter to him: 'You start tomorrow.'"     → TELL     — "The recruiter told him he started THE NEXT DAY."
15. "Auditor: 'I have been here before.'"         → BACK-SHIFT — "The auditor said he HAD BEEN there before."  (here → there)
16. "Lead: 'I will help you.'"                    → SAY      — "The lead said he would help me."
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — does the original verb need an object?" When the zone is right and the back-shift is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (10 min) Pair drilling with cards

Hand each pair: 5 reporting-verb cards + 3 tense-shift arrow cards + 2 time/place/pronoun shift cards. Pair work:

1. Student A invents a direct-speech sentence (any topic, professional preferred) + picks a reporting-verb card.
2. Student B reports it applying the right shift (verb + pronoun + time + place if applicable) in 3 sec.
3. Swap roles after every card. After 5 cards each, swap to ASK-family (with IF/WHETHER for yes-no).

Walk and coach the FORM (right reporting verb, full back-shift, pronoun + time shift). Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Back-shift before you report. Prudence day 1 — every dependent verb steps back."*

### (7 min) Podium — pronoun + time + place shifts

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a direct-speech sentence loaded with pronouns + time + place markers. They must report it applying ALL THREE shifts (verb back-shift + pronoun + time/place). Cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"*

```
1st: Direct: Manager: "I am sending you this report today from here."
     Reported: "The manager said she WAS SENDING me that report THAT DAY from THERE."

2nd: Direct: Client: "I will need this by tomorrow."
     Reported: "The client said he WOULD NEED that by THE NEXT DAY."

3rd: Direct: Auditor: "I have already been here yesterday."
     Reported: "The auditor said he HAD ALREADY BEEN there THE DAY BEFORE."
```

Then one final chorus drill — you call the original, cohort responds with the report in unison:

> *"Mark: I am happy — Mark said?"* → "Mark said he WAS happy."
> *"Sara to me: I will call — Sara?"* → "Sara told me she WOULD call."
> *"Diego: Where do you live — Diego?"* → "Diego asked where I LIVED."

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

Three roles: WITNESS speaks 6 direct-speech quotes from different colleagues about the incident. REPORTER instantly reports each quote to HR applying full back-shift + pronoun + time/place shifts. OBSERVER counts correct shifts on the grid — reporting verb, back-shift, pronoun, time/place. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. I will WRITE recurring errors on the board live as I hear them — when there are three on the board we stop and correct as a cohort."*

```
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____
```

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 6 quote+report pairs. Witness speaks; reporter back-shifts in 3 sec; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for ASK with IF/WHETHER — at least 2 per triad. Watch for "ask if did he" (wrong — affirmative order). Continue live board-error correction. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3: *"Back-shift before you report. Prudence day 1 — every verb steps back, every pronoun adjusts."*

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Push for full pronoun + time/place shifts — at least 2 per triad with multiple markers. By now they should be sharp. Slow down only for the most stubborn pattern you wrote on the board across the previous 2 rounds.

### (2 min) Cohort close
One strong triad demos 90 sec in front of the group — 3 reports including 1 ASK + 1 fully time-shifted quote. Applause. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4: *"Back-shift before you report. Prudence day 1 — the block is OPEN."*

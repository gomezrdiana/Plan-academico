## 6. LIBRO M29 — "HOW + ADJ + TO BE + SUBJECT?" + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 29, page 261 — 'How Tall Are You?'. To ask the SIZE of a quality, English puts 'how' in front of the adjective and then 'to be + subject'. Then we race it on the board."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M29 (módulo pág 261; Guía 1 pág 262; Ejemplos pág 263) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M29 — "HOW TALL ARE YOU?" / DEGREES OF ADJECTIVES
       (A2 Book — modulo pag 261)

From the book (Guia 1 — Los Intensificadores, pag 262):
"1. A veces, queremos describir algo en terminos menos
 exactos, es decir; subjetivos."
"2. Para preguntar, usamos la palabra de pregunta
 'how'. Has aprendido 'how' = 'como'."
"3. Antes de un descriptor, 'how' significa 'que tan
 ____________...'"
   Ej. "How tall are you?" -> "Que tan alto eres tu?"
"4. Para responder, usamos un intensificador antes del
 descriptor. Aqui, vamos a ver 3 [seis] de ellos."

THE INTENSIFIERS (Guia 1, pag 262):
   REALLY (informal)            "I am really tall."
   VERY   (informal/academic)   "I am very tall."
   KINDA  (mas o menos)         "I am kinda tall."
   SORTA  (mas o menos)         "I am sorta tall."
   PRETTY (bastante)            "I am pretty tall."
   NOT VERY* (cortes, opuesto)  "I am not very tall."

"5. *Igual al espanol, usamos 'no muy' para decir lo
 opuesto de manera cortes."
   Ej. "No soy muy alto." Implica, "Soy bajo."

BOOK EXAMPLES (Ejemplos, pag 263):
   How nice is Ana?         -> She's very nice.
   How new is your car?     -> It's pretty new.
   How old is your dad?     -> He's not very old.
   How good is the food?    -> It's not very good.
   How bad is your dog?     -> It's really bad.
   How intelligent is Sally? -> She's kinda intelligent.
   How rich is Donald Trump? -> He's very rich.
   How pretty is Helen?     -> She's really pretty.
   How serious is the situation? -> It's not very serious.
   How expensive is the car? -> It's kinda expensive.

KEY: QUESTION = "How + ADJ + to be + SUBJECT?"
     ANSWER   = "SUBJECT + to be + INTENSIFIER + ADJ."
     6 intensifiers: REALLY, VERY, KINDA, SORTA, PRETTY,
                     NOT VERY.
```

> Di: *"To ask the SIZE of a quality — put 'how' in front of the adjective, then 'to be' + the subject. 'How tall are you?' / 'How busy is Monday?'. To answer, pick an intensifier — really, very, kinda, sorta, pretty — and put it before the adjective. To say the opposite politely, use 'not very'. 'I am not very tall' = 'I am short, said politely'. Six intensifiers, one question shape — the 'How + adj' is the signature of M29."*

### BOARD RACE — "How + adj" (7 min) — DE PIE, 2 equipos

Dos columnas en el tablero. Dictas una frase mal formada (orden invertido, intensificador faltante, "how much" en vez de "how + adj", "not too" en vez de "not very"); el primer estudiante de cada fila corre al tablero y escribe la frase correcta. Cohorte verifica. Rota.

```
Round (dicta MAL; ellos escriben bien):
1. "How is busy Monday?"               -> How BUSY is Monday?
2. "How much reliable is the system?"  -> How RELIABLE is the system?
3. "It is reliable really."            -> It is REALLY reliable.
4. "She is not too serious."           -> She is NOT VERY serious.
5. "How is your dad old?"              -> How OLD is your dad?
6. "How tall is you?"                  -> How TALL are you?  (subject-verb agreement)
```

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Compare with our phrase — 'HOW strong are you really? HOW tall do your goals stand? HOW long have you worked?'. The question always starts 'How + adjective + to be + subject'. Phrase first, then the rule."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — M29 (00:34–00:50, 16 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"You become the question and the answer. Wrong order on one side of the room, correct 'How + adjective + to be + subject?' on the other. You walk the transformation, and then answer with an intensifier."*

### Setup (2 min)

Dos zonas del salón rotuladas: **WRONG (orden invertido / "how much" + adj / "is" mal con subject / "not too")** (izquierda) y **RIGHT (correct "How + adj + to be + subject?" + intensifier answer)** (derecha). Reparte tiras con preguntas mal formadas en contextos profesionales (1 por estudiante).

### Round 1 — Walk it (8 min)

Cada estudiante:
1. Se para en zona WRONG y lee su pregunta mal formada en voz alta ("How much busy is Monday at your work?").
2. Camina a zona RIGHT diciendo la pregunta correcta + la respuesta con intensificador en voz alta ("How BUSY is Monday at your work? — It is REALLY busy, the busiest day.").
3. El cohorte (de pie) confirma con pulgar arriba/abajo.

**Tiras sugeridas (contexto profesional):**

```
"How much busy is Monday?"         -> How BUSY is Monday? -> It is really busy.
"How is reliable the new tool?"    -> How RELIABLE is the new tool? -> It is very reliable.
"How important the deadline is?"   -> How IMPORTANT is the deadline? -> It is pretty important.
"How is complicated the report?"   -> How COMPLICATED is the report? -> It is kinda complicated.
"How tall is you?"                 -> How TALL are you? -> I am pretty tall.
"How old your supervisor is?"      -> How OLD is your supervisor? -> He is not very old.
"How long is your shift?"          -> (already correct) -> It is pretty long, ten hours.
"Evaluations are handled by coordination, not by us." -> (limit reminder — leave as is)
```

> Nota: la última tira refuerza el límite — evaluaciones SOLO las maneja coordinación; el profesor NUNCA comunica nada de evaluaciones/midterm/final (más allá del hecho de calendario).

### Round 2 — Reverse (4 min)

Ahora al revés: parten de la pregunta CORRECTA, caminan a WRONG diciendo en voz alta por qué la versión mala está mal ("'How is busy Monday?' is wrong — the order is 'How + adjective + to be + subject', not 'How + to be + adjective + subject'. And the answer 'It is busy really' is wrong — the intensifier goes BEFORE the adjective: 'It is really busy'"). Intercambia tiras entre estudiantes.

### Cierre (2 min)

2 voluntarios caminan su transformación al frente (pregunta + respuesta). Cohorte cuenta preguntas e intensificadores correctos.

```
     POLITE OPPOSITE: not very.
```

> Di: *"Six tools, one job — say the SIZE of the quality. 'Really' and 'very' = strong. 'Kinda' and 'sorta' = so-so, medium. 'Pretty' = quite a lot. 'Not very' = the polite way to say the opposite — 'I am not very tall' really means 'I am short', said with respect. Choose the intensifier that matches the honest size, then say the adjective."*

### SORTING FÍSICO (7 min) — DE PIE, en grupo

Reparte tarjetas: cada una con una respuesta profesional usando un intensificador. El cohorte, de pie, agrupa cada tarjeta en la zona correcta (**STRONG (really/very) / SO-SO (kinda/sorta) / QUITE A LOT (pretty) / POLITE OPPOSITE (not very)**) y dice en voz alta por qué (qué nivel de intensidad):

```
"It is really busy today."                  -> STRONG (really)
"Carlos is very reliable, no problems."     -> STRONG (very)
"The new system is kinda complicated."      -> SO-SO (kinda)
"My new colleague is sorta serious."        -> SO-SO (sorta)
"The premium plan is pretty expensive."     -> QUITE A LOT (pretty)
"This shift is not very long, six hours."   -> POLITE OPPOSITE (not very)
"My boss is not very strict, he is calm."   -> POLITE OPPOSITE (not very)
"The deadline is really important."         -> STRONG (really)
```

Cada grupo dice su tarjeta. El cohorte confirma el nivel y por qué (¿qué intensificador? ¿qué tan fuerte/débil/cortés?).

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"'How strong are you really?' — 'really' = strong. 'How tall do your goals stand?' — answer 'pretty tall' = quite a lot. 'How long have you worked?' — answer 'really long' or 'not very long' = polite. Same question shape, six possible intensifier answers."*

## 9. HUMAN-SENTENCE LINE-UP (CON TARJETA "HOW") + STATION ROTATION (01:02–01:14, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"First you become words and line up as a correct 'How + adjective' question — and someone MUST hold the 'HOW' card. Then three-type stations around the room — you move; the grammar stays."*

### Human-Sentence Line-up (4 min) — DE PIE

Reparte tarjetas grandes (1 palabra por estudiante: **una tarjeta especial "HOW" que alguien sostiene siempre**, el adjetivo, el verbo "to be" (am/is/are), el sujeto, el signo "?"). Por grupos, los estudiantes se PARAN en fila físicamente para formar la PREGUNTA correcta — y físicamente deben decidir el orden: "How + adjective + to be + subject?". La tarjeta "HOW" SIEMPRE tiene que estar al inicio de la fila — si nadie la sostiene al frente, la oración no es una pregunta de medida. El grupo lee la pregunta en voz alta. Luego otro estudiante (con tarjeta "INTENSIFIER" — really/very/kinda/sorta/pretty/not very) sale a dar la respuesta. 2-3 rondas, alternando intensificadores.

### Station Rotation (8 min) — DE PIE

3 estaciones rotuladas. Grupos rotan cada ~2.5 min (campana). Todos de pie.

```
STATION A — THE QUESTION ("How + adj + to be + subject?")
Build 4 professional questions with "How + adjective":
"How busy is Monday at your work?"
"How reliable is the new system?"
"How important is the deadline?"
"How complicated is the new process?"

STATION B — STRONG ANSWERS (REALLY / VERY)
Answer 4 questions with REALLY or VERY + adjective:
"It is really busy. The busiest day of the week."
"It is very reliable. We have 99% uptime."
"It is really important. The client is waiting."
"It is very complicated. We need a week to learn it."

STATION C — SO-SO / QUITE / POLITE ANSWERS
              (KINDA / SORTA / PRETTY / NOT VERY)
Answer 4 questions with KINDA, SORTA, PRETTY or NOT VERY
+ adjective — and say WHY you chose that level:
"It is kinda busy. Some weeks more, some less."
"It is pretty new, only one year old."
"It is sorta complicated, but I am learning."
"It is not very expensive — the basic plan is enough."
```

Tú circulas entre estaciones, corriges en voz baja, anotas errores con NOMBRE.

## 10. MEASUREMENT-LINE CEREMONY + SPEED DRILL DE PIE (01:14–01:24, 10 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"On the measurement-line: a physical line from 'not very' (least) to 'really' (most). Each of you stands where you HONESTLY measure. Then drill: all six intensifiers in your mouth until reflex."*

### Measurement-Line Ceremony (4 min) — DE PIE en línea

Dibuja en el piso (o el tablero) una línea con 4 marcas: **NOT VERY → KINDA/SORTA → PRETTY → REALLY/VERY**. Lanzas una cualidad ("How busy is your week?" / "How confident is your English now?" / "How hard is your job today?" / "How important is your work to you?"). Cada estudiante PHYSICALLY se para en la marca que corresponde a su respuesta honesta, dice la frase completa en voz alta, y el cohorte responde en coro: *"Confirmed — [intensifier] [adjective]!"*. Cubrir los 6 intensificadores (mínimo una vez cada uno).

```
Cadena Measurement-Line (cada uno una pregunta distinta):
A: "How busy is your week? — I am REALLY busy this week."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — really busy!"
B: "How confident is your English now? — I am PRETTY confident now."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — pretty confident!"
C: "How hard is your job today? — It is KINDA hard."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — kinda hard!"
D: "How important is this work to you? — It is VERY important."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — very important!"
E: "How nervous am I now? — I am NOT VERY nervous anymore."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — not very nervous!"
F: "How early is your shift? — It is SORTA early, 7 AM."
   -> coro: "Confirmed — sorta early!"
```

### Drill 1 — Form-on-command cadena (4 min) — DE PIE en círculo

Dices un adjetivo + un nivel ("strong / so-so / quite / polite opposite"); el estudiante responde la frase con el intensificador correcto, luego pasa al siguiente:

```
"busy / strong"          -> "Monday is really busy."
"reliable / strong"      -> "Carlos is very reliable."
"complicated / so-so"    -> "The report is kinda complicated."
"new / quite a lot"      -> "The model is pretty new."
"expensive / polite opp" -> "The plan is not very expensive."
"important / strong"     -> "The deadline is really important."
"long / polite opp"      -> "My shift is not very long."
"hard / so-so"           -> "The task is sorta hard."
```

### Drill 2 — Question + answer coro rápido (2 min) — DE PIE

Dices la pregunta; el cohorte responde en coro con la respuesta CORTA usando el intensificador indicado:

```
"How tall are you? — STRONG"      -> "I am really tall."
"How busy is Monday? — STRONG"    -> "It is very busy."
"How rich is Donald? — STRONG"    -> "He is really rich."
"How serious is it? — POLITE OPP" -> "It is not very serious."
"How new is your car? — QUITE"    -> "It is pretty new."
"How smart is Sally? — SO-SO"     -> "She is kinda smart."
```

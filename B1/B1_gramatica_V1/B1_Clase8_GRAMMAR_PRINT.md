## 3. STAND-UP DRILL — FREQUENCY ADVERBS (00:19–00:34, 15 min) — DE PIE

**Concepto M12:**
```
FREQUENCY ADVERBS — adverbios de frecuencia
100% always → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → never 0%

POSICIÓN — ENTRE sujeto y verbo principal:
- I ALWAYS work from home.
- She USUALLY studies at night.
- He NEVER eats breakfast.

CON "TO BE" — DESPUÉS del verbo:
- She IS always late.
- They ARE never on time.
- I AM usually tired on Mondays.

QUESTION:
- "How OFTEN do you...?"
- "How OFTEN does she...?"
```

Setup (1 min): "STAND UP. 20 prompts. Spanish to English. Whoever sits down loses energy."

Round 1 — Posición ENTRE sujeto y verbo (8 min). 10 prompts:
```
1. "Yo siempre trabajo en casa."           → I always work from home.
2. "Ella usualmente estudia de noche."     → She usually studies at night.
3. "Él nunca desayuna."                    → He never eats breakfast.
4. "Nosotros a veces vamos al cine."       → We sometimes go to the movies.
5. "Tú rara vez llegas tarde."             → You rarely arrive late.
6. "Mi mamá siempre cocina los domingos."  → My mom always cooks on Sundays.
7. "Mi gato a menudo duerme en la mesa."   → My cat often sleeps on the table.
8. "Ellos nunca pelean."                   → They never fight.
9. "Yo usualmente bebo café en la mañana." → I usually drink coffee in the morning.
10. "Ella a veces me llama."               → She sometimes calls me.
```

Round 2 — Posición DESPUÉS del "to be" (5 min). 5 prompts:
```
1. "Ella es siempre puntual."              → She is always punctual.
2. "Yo estoy siempre cansado los lunes."   → I am always tired on Mondays.
3. "Él está nunca disponible."             → He is never available.
4. "Estamos a veces tristes."              → We are sometimes sad.
5. "Tu ropa es siempre elegante."          → Your clothes are always elegant.
```

> Si dicen "I work always from home" → "No. Listen. ALWAYS goes BEFORE the verb."

Round 3 — Pregunta "How often" (1 min):
- "How often do you go to the gym?" → I usually go twice a week. / I rarely go.

## 4. GALLERY WALK — FREQUENCY EXPRESSIONS (00:34–00:49, 15 min) — CAMINANDO

**Setup ANTES de clase:** Pega 4 hojas en las paredes.

**Las 4 estaciones:**
```
ESTACIÓN 1 — "MY DAILY ROUTINE"
Write 3 sentences about what you do EVERY DAY.
Use: every day / every morning / every night.
Example: "I check my emails every morning before breakfast."

ESTACIÓN 2 — "MY WEEKLY HABITS"
Write 3 sentences using:
once a week / twice a week / three times a week.
Example: "I go to the gym three times a week."

ESTACIÓN 3 — "MY WEEKEND vs WEEKDAYS"
Write 3 sentences contrasting:
on weekends / on weekdays / in the morning / at night.
Example: "On weekends I usually sleep until 9. On weekdays I wake up at 6."

ESTACIÓN 4 — "RARE EVENTS"
Write 3 sentences using:
once a month / twice a year / hardly ever / once in a while.
Example: "I visit my grandparents once a month."
```

**Setup (2 min):** "4 stations on walls. Groups of 4. 3 minutes per station. Write DIRECTLY on paper."

**4 rondas de 3 min (12 min):** Cronómetro. "ROTATE!" cada 3 min.

**Cierre (1 min):** "Sit. Which station was hardest?"

## 6. WRITING SPRINT — "MY WEEKLY HABITS" × 3 RONDAS (01:04–01:19, 15 min)

**Sprint 1 — Daily routine (60 + 60):** "Write 5 sentences about your daily routine. Use AT LEAST 3 frequency adverbs. GO." Después: "Stop. Check that adverbs go BETWEEN subject and verb."

Cierre (3 min): "Pairs. Read your favorite sentence. Partner says: 'Nice' or 'Adverb position?'"

## 7. WALKING ADVERB DRILL — POSICIÓN CORRECTA (01:19–01:31, 12 min) — DE PIE

**Setup (2 min):** "STAND UP. Two lines facing each other."

**Reglas:**
```
WALKING ADVERB DRILL
- I say a sentence WITHOUT the adverb
- Student inserts the adverb in CORRECT POSITION
- WRONG → step OUT of line
- LAST one standing wins
```

**Round (10 min):** Dispara 25 prompts:
```
"I work from home + always"          → I always work from home.
"She studies + usually"               → She usually studies.
"He drinks coffee + never"            → He never drinks coffee.
"We go to the beach + sometimes"      → We sometimes go to the beach.
"I am tired + always (with am)"       → I am always tired.
"They eat out + rarely"               → They rarely eat out.
"My mom cooks + always"               → My mom always cooks.
"She is late + usually"               → She is usually late.
"I check emails + often"              → I often check emails.
... (continúa 25 total mezclando todos los adverbs)
```

**Cierre (2 min):** "Winner stays. Aplauso." Aplauden.

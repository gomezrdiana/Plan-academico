## 6. BOOK M36 — VOCAB + GUIDE 1 + BOARD RACE — 12 min — STAND UP

Write on the board (before class):

```
M36 — WHAT ARE YOU WEARING? (A2 Book p.301)
       Describing Clothing

VOCAB 1 — CLOTHING (p.302):
  a shirt · T-shirt · jeans · pants · belt · jacket
  shoes · sandals · sneakers · sweater · underwear
  socks · necklace · earrings · shorts · skirt
  dress · bag · purse · hat · leggings · tie · glasses

VOCAB 2 — COLORS (p.303):
  black · white · red · blue · green · yellow
  brown · gray · pink · purple · silver · DARK · LIGHT

From the book (Guide 1 — La Ropa, p.304):
"1. Two common ways to say 'estar usando':
   'I have a shirt.'       → 'Tengo una camisa.'
   'I am wearing a shirt.' → 'Estoy usando una camisa.'"

"2. Adjectives go BEFORE the noun in English and
    NEVER change form."
   El carro rojo    → The red car.
   Los carros rojos → The red cars.    ← NO plural on adj
   La casa roja     → The red house.   ← NO gender change
   Las casas rojas  → The red houses.

"3. Two adjectives where the first modifies the second:
   'Dark red' / 'light red' → 'rojo oscuro' / 'rojo claro'."

KEY: "I HAVE X" or "I AM WEARING X"
     ADJ in FRONT of noun
     ADJ NEVER changes form (no plural, no gender)
     "DARK X" / "LIGHT X" — first adj modifies second

(M35 does NOT exist — M34 → M36, same as M30 → M32,
 M24 → M26, M18 → M20. We follow the book.)
```

Say it standing at the board: *"Two ways to say it: 'I HAVE a shirt' / 'I AM WEARING a shirt'. The adjective always goes BEFORE the noun. No plural on the adjective — 'red cars', never 'reds cars'. Two adjectives together — 'dark blue' — the first modifies the second."*

### BOARD RACE — M36 — 7 min — 2 teams

Two columns on the board. Dictate a wrong sentence; first student of each row runs to the board and writes it correctly. Cohort verifies. Rotate.

```
Round (dictate WRONG; they write RIGHT):
1. "I am wearing shirt blue."              → "I am wearing a BLUE shirt."
2. "She has reds shoes."                   → "She has RED shoes."
3. "He wearing a jacket black."            → "He IS wearing a BLACK jacket."
4. "The pants are blue dark today."        → "The pants are DARK BLUE today."
5. "I have a shirt green clear."           → "I have a LIGHT GREEN shirt."
6. "She is wearing dress red and shoes whites." → "She is wearing a RED dress and WHITE shoes."
```

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Every adjective BEFORE its noun. 'Dark' modifying 'blue'. No plural on 'brown' even though 'shoes' is plural. That's M36 in one line. Phrase first, then the rule."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Two zones in the room (tape labels on opposite walls):
- **WRONG zone (left):** adj after noun / plural on adj / "clear/obscure" / missing "am"
- **RIGHT zone (right):** correct M36 — *I have X / I am wearing X* + adj BEFORE noun + adj no plural + dark/light correctly

Hand each student **ONE slip card** with a wrong-form professional sentence.

### Round 1 — Walk it — 8 min

Each student:
1. Stands in WRONG zone, reads slip aloud with the wrong form.
2. Walks to RIGHT zone shouting the corrected form.
3. Cohort confirms with thumbs.

   → "I am wearing a WHITE shirt for the meeting today."

   → "She has BROWN shoes for client visits."

   → "For field work I wear BLUE jeans."

   → "My presentation outfit is a LIGHT PINK shirt and BLACK pants."

   → "I have a LIGHT BLUE tie for the interview."
"Coordination handles results — not me."
   → (limit reminder — leave correct as is)

Swap slips. Each student starts in RIGHT, walks to WRONG saying out loud WHY it's wrong: *"'reds shoes' is wrong because the adjective NEVER takes a plural — 'red' stays 'red'.

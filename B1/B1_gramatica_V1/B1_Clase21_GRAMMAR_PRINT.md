## 6. LIBRO M30 — REQUIRED LEVELS + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 30, page 267 — 'Too Much or Just Enough?'. Four markers for the level of a descriptor. Then we race them on the board."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M30 (módulo pág 267; Guía 1 pág 268; Ejemplos 1 pág 269) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M30 — REQUIRED LEVELS / NIVELES DE DESCRIPTORES
       (A2 Book pag 267) "Too Much or Just Enough?"

From the book (Guia 1, pag 268):
"1. A veces, queremos describir si algo llega (o
 excede) un nivel esperado de una caracteristica."
"2. La palabra 'enough' es inusual, porque se coloca
 DESPUES del descriptor."
"3. El intensificador 'so' implica que la
 caracteristica es de nivel alto pero buena."
"4. El intensificador 'too' implica que la
 caracteristica esta excesiva, o hasta un punto no
 deseable."

THE 4 MARKERS:
  No suficientemente -> Not __[adj]__ enough -> I am not tall enough.
  Suficientemente    -> __[adj]__ enough     -> I am tall enough.
  Tan                -> So + adj             -> You are so tall!
  Demasiado          -> Too + adj            -> You are too tall.

BOOK EXAMPLES (Ejemplos 1, pag 269 — "So" vs "Too"):
  "We love Gabriela. She is so nice!"
  "Gabriela can't fight. She is too nice."
  "Einstein is a great scientist. He is so smart!"
  "Einstein can't teach kids. He is too smart."
  "I got 100%. My marks are so high!"
  "I can't get the bread. It is too high."
  "You are never late. You are so early."
  "I don't like my class at 5:00. It is too early."

PRACTICA VERBAL 1 — Respuestas (pag 272):
  How rich is Andrea?              -> She's so rich.
  How nice is Paula?               -> He's nice enough.
  How serious are you?             -> I'm not serious enough.
  How intelligent are Alix-Oscar?  -> They're so intelligent.
  How late am I?                   -> You're too late.
  How old are you?                 -> I'm not old enough.

KEY:  SO + adj      = high and good ("so smart!")
      TOO + adj     = excessive ("too smart [to ...]")
      adj + ENOUGH  = just right ("tall enough")  <-- AFTER
      NOT + adj + ENOUGH = insufficient ("not tall enough")
      UNIQUE PIECE: "enough" ALWAYS AFTER the adjective.
```

> Di: *"Four markers, one idea — the LEVEL of a quality. 'So' = high and good. 'Too' = excessive, not desirable. 'enough' = just right — and 'enough' goes AFTER the adjective ('tall enough', never 'enough tall'). 'Not + adj + enough' = not yet at the level. That little position rule — 'enough' AFTER — is the signature of this module."*

### BOARD RACE — Required Levels (7 min) — DE PIE, 2 equipos

Dos columnas en el tablero. Dictas una frase mal formada (con "enough" antes del adj, con "too much + adj", con "so" donde va "too", con "for + verb" en lugar de "to + verb"); el primer estudiante de cada fila corre al tablero y escribe la frase correcta. Cohorte verifica. Rota.

```
Round (dicta MAL; ellos escriben bien):
1. "I am enough prepared for tomorrow."     -> I am PREPARED ENOUGH for tomorrow.
2. "The meeting was too much long."          -> The meeting was TOO LONG.
3. "I am not focus enough on the deadline."  -> I am NOT FOCUSED ENOUGH on the deadline.
4. "She is so smart for teach kids."         -> She is TOO smart TO teach kids.
5. "Your marks are too high, congrats!"      -> Your marks are SO high, congrats! ("too" = bad; here good = "so")
6. "I am enough old to vote."                -> I am OLD ENOUGH to vote.
```

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Compare with our phrase — 'I am prepared ENOUGH' (enough AFTER), 'not TOO anxious' (excessive), 'SO focused' (high and good). Four markers, the phrase has three of them. Phrase first, then the rule."*

3. El cohorte (de pie) confirma con pulgar arriba/abajo.

**Tiras sugeridas (contexto profesional / midterm):**

```

"My English is so good for the role!"      -> SO zone     -> "My English is SO good for the role!"
"I am too anxious to sleep tonight."       -> TOO zone    -> (already correct: TOO + adj + TO + verb)
"The slide deck is enough short now."      -> ENOUGH zone -> "The slide deck is SHORT ENOUGH now."
"I am enough confident on the closing."    -> ENOUGH zone -> "I am CONFIDENT ENOUGH on the closing."
"Evaluations are handled by coordination, not by us." -> (limit reminder — leave correct as is)

Intercambia tiras entre estudiantes.

THE POSITION:
  ✅ I am tall ENOUGH.        (adj + enough)
  ❌ I am ENOUGH tall.         (WRONG — never before)

  ✅ I am NOT tall ENOUGH.    (not + adj + enough)
  ❌ I am NOT ENOUGH tall.     (WRONG)

BOOK EXAMPLES (Respuestas Practica Verbal 1, pag 272):
  "He's nice enough."             (adj + enough)
  "I'm not serious enough."       (not + adj + enough)
  "I'm not old enough."           (not + adj + enough)
  "We're complicated enough."     (adj + enough)
  "He's ugly enough."             (adj + enough)
  "You're not pretty enough."     (not + adj + enough)

CONTRAST WITH "SO" AND "TOO" (book Ejemplos, pag 269):
  SO  = high and GOOD ("so nice!" / "so smart!")
  TOO = excessive, NOT desirable ("too nice [to fight]"
        / "too smart [to teach kids]")
  Same adjective, opposite feeling.

KEY: "enough" ALWAYS AFTER the adjective.
     "so" and "too" go BEFORE the adjective.
     With "too" we often add "to + verb": "too tired
     to drive".
```

> Di: *"'enough' is the one that breaks the pattern — it goes AFTER. 'tall enough', 'prepared enough', 'focused enough'. Never 'enough tall'. And the difference between 'so' and 'too' is feeling: 'so smart!' is wonderful; 'too smart to teach kids' is excessive. Same word — opposite story."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — M11 Advanced Adjectives (B1 Book p.127-144)

Stand at the board. Book p.129 + p.133 + p.137 + p.141 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M11 — ADVANCED ADJECTIVES — FORM RULES (p.127-144)

(1) NOUN-ADJECTIVE PAIRS (Guide 1, p.129):
    Same root, different shape:
      health → healthy · bravery → brave
      happiness → happy · strength → strong
      talent → talented · intelligence → intelligent
      enthusiasm → enthusiastic · wisdom → wise
      caution → cautious · laziness → lazy
      carelessness → careless

(2) PREFIXES + SUFFIXES (Guide 2, p.133):
    PREFIXES (negation/repetition):
      UN- : happy → UNhappy · countable → UNcountable
      IN- : correct → INcorrect · exact → INexact
      IM- : polite → IMpolite · perfect → IMperfect
      IR- : responsible → IRresponsible
      DIS-: honest → DIShonest
      MIS-: understood → MISunderstood
      RE- : do → REdo · build → REbuild
    SUFFIXES (without / full of / can be):
      -LESS: fear → fearLESS · hope → hopeLESS
      -FUL : color → colorFUL · fear → fearFUL
      -ABLE/-IBLE : love → loveABLE · work → workABLE
                    comfort → comfortABLE

(3) COMPOUND ADJECTIVES (Guide 3, p.137):
    Two words joined with a hyphen, ONE descriptor:
      "a 20-page report" · "a well-known author"
      "self-confident" · "ice-cold beer"
      "a 3-year-old child" (singular, not "3-years-old")

(4) IMPERSONAL EXPRESSIONS (Guide 4, p.141):
    "It is + adjective + (that-clause OR to-infinitive)":
      "It is IMPORTANT to help."  (infinitive)
      "It is GREAT that you came."  (relative pronoun)
      "It is DANGEROUS to drive after drinking."
    Subject "it" cannot be omitted (unlike Spanish).
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.129 verbatim (drilled out loud): *"Like Spanish, English often uses a very similar word as a noun, verb, and adjective. For example: Jugar → Juego → Juguetón / To play → A play → Playful."* Book p.133 verbatim: *"Similar to Spanish, adding a few letters to the beginning (called a prefix) or end (called a suffix) of a word changes its meaning."* Book p.137 verbatim: *"Sometimes we use two separate words to create a single descriptor: 'I am reading a 150-page book.' The words '150' and 'page' form a single descriptor, indicated with a hyphen (-) between them."* Book p.141 verbatim: *"Subjects can be omitted in Spanish, but never in English."*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"Name things precisely. Prudence day 3 — at the close, vocabulary precision matters most."*

### (10 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners:
- **NOUN ↔ ADJECTIVE**
- **PREFIX / SUFFIX**
- **INDEFINITE (every/some/any/no)**

Stand in the middle. Call a context out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + speak the full sentence as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 14 calls in 10 min.

```
Context called                                  → Right zone + sample sentence
1.  "Your colleague speaks 5 languages."        → NOUN↔ADJ      — "She is TALENTED — her TALENT is rare."
2.  "Opposite of 'polite'."                     → PREFIX/SUFFIX — "IMpolite."  (not 'unpolite')
3.  "Everyone in the room — nobody missing."    → INDEFINITE    — "EVERYONE is here."
4.  "I cut my finger by accident."              → REFLEXIVE     — "I cut MYSELF."
5.  "The report is 50 pages."                   → NOUN↔ADJ      — "It's a 50-PAGE report." (compound)
6.  "The deadline is impossible."               → PREFIX/SUFFIX — "IMpossible."
7.  "There is no person in the room."           → INDEFINITE    — "NO ONE is here."  /  "NOBODY is here."
8.  "She did the project alone."                → REFLEXIVE     — "She did it BY HERSELF."
9.  "Opposite of 'responsible'."                → PREFIX/SUFFIX — "IRresponsible."
10. "We need to do the test again."             → PREFIX/SUFFIX — "REdo / Retake the test."
11. "Is anyone in the office?"                  → INDEFINITE    — "Is ANYONE in the office?"
12. "He hurts only himself."                    → REFLEXIVE     — "He hurts HIMSELF."
13. "Without fear."                             → PREFIX/SUFFIX — "fearLESS."
14. "I have something in my hand."              → INDEFINITE    — "I have SOMETHING in my hand."
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — what kind of change does the sentence need?" When the zone is right and the form is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (6 min) Mini-talk — M12 Advanced Pronouns (B1 Book p.145-155)

Stand at the board. Book p.147 + p.151 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M12 — ADVANCED PRONOUNS — FORM RULES (p.145-155)

(1) INDEFINITE PRONOUNS (Guide 1, p.147):
                  PERSON (formal/inf)  PLACE      THING
    ALL:          everyone/everybody   everywhere everything
    PARTIAL (+):  someone/somebody     somewhere  something
    PARTIAL (-):  anyone/anybody       anywhere   anything
    ABSENCE (0):  no one/nobody        nowhere    nothing

    KEY RULES:
      - Always 3rd person SINGULAR verb:
        "EVERYONE IS here." (NOT "everyone are")
      - SOME-family → affirmative
      - ANY-family → negative + question
      - NO-family → negative meaning, positive verb

(2) REFLEXIVE PRONOUNS (Guide 2, p.151):
    I → MYSELF      we → OURSELVES
    you (sg) → YOURSELF   you (pl) → YOURSELVES
    he → HIMSELF    she → HERSELF
    it → ITSELF     they → THEMSELVES

    USE WHEN subject = object:
      "I cut MYSELF."  (not "I cut me")
      "She introduced HERSELF."
    USE "BY + reflexive" for ALONE:
      "I work BY MYSELF." = alone
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.147 verbatim (drilled out loud): *"Indefinite pronouns are always used as a 3rd person singular (even if they refer to multiples). CORRECT: Everyone is here. INCORRECT: Everyone are here."* Book p.151 verbatim: *"When the subject and object of a verb are the same, we use the reflexive pronoun."* Book p.151 verbatim: *"We also often use 'by' + reflexive pronoun to indicate 'alone' ('solo'). I am eating by myself."*

### (6 min) Pair drilling — M12 cards

Hand each pair: 4 indefinite-pronoun cards (mix of all 4 families) + 3 reflexive-pronoun cards. Pair work:

1. Student A picks an indefinite card; builds a sentence (affirmative/negative/question matching the family).
2. Student B picks a reflexive card; builds a sentence where subject = object OR uses "BY + reflexive" for alone.
3. Swap roles. Walk and coach.

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3: *"Pronouns that point at the speaker, or at no-one in particular. Prudence day 3 — precision in pointing."*

### (6 min) Podium — reversal (positive ↔ negative ↔ question with indefinites)

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students. Each gets a sentence in one family; must produce the OTHER TWO family forms. Cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"*

```
1st: "Someone called me." (SOME → affirmative)
     → NEGATIVE: "No one called me."  /  "Nobody called me."
     → QUESTION: "Did anyone call you?"  /  "Did anybody call you?"

2nd: "I haven't done anything." (ANY → negative)
     → AFFIRMATIVE: "I have done something."
     → QUESTION: "Have you done anything?"

3rd: "Everywhere is closed." (EVERY → affirmative)
     → NEGATIVE: "Nowhere is open."  /  "Nothing is open anywhere."
     → QUESTION: "Is anywhere open?"
```

Then one final chorus drill — you call a reflexive context, cohort responds in unison:

> *"She cut — by accident."* → "She cut HERSELF."
> *"They built — alone."* → "They built it BY THEMSELVES."
> *"I introduce — to the team."* → "I introduce MYSELF to the team."

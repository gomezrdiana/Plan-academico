### (3 min) Warm-up — standing board race
Two columns on the board. Call a Spanish phrase; the first student of each row runs to the board and writes the English structure (either / neither / both). Cohort verifies. 8 rounds in 3 minutes.

```
"o café o té"            → "either coffee or tea"
"ni café ni té"          → "neither coffee nor tea"
"ambos café y té"        → "both coffee and tea"
"o nadar o volar"        → "either swim or fly"
```

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — three structures + agreement responses

Stand at the board. Book p.9, 13, 17 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M2 — EITHER / NEITHER / BOTH — FORM RULES
(B1 Book p.9, 13, 17)

THREE STRUCTURES — three meanings:
  EITHER … OR    = 1 of 2 (affirmative + questions)
  NEITHER … NOR  = 0 of 2 (no NOT verb — already neg.)
  BOTH … AND     = 2 of 2 (affirmative only — never neg.)

POSITION — before a noun or before a verb:
  noun: "I prefer either meat or chicken."
  verb: "We can either swim or fly."
  noun: "We can speak neither Spanish nor English."
  verb: "We should neither eat nor drink."
  noun: "We can speak both Spanish and English."
  verb: "We can both swim and fly."

DETERMINER + "of" (book p.9, 13, 17):
  "Either of the boys can help you."   (any 1)
  "Neither of the boys can help you."  (0)
  "Both of the boys can help you."     (2)

THE "TAMPOCO" PROBLEM:
  Short form at the END: "..., either."
     "I don't have a car, either."
  Inverted form at the START: "Neither + aux + I."
     "John doesn't have a car. Neither do I."
     "John can't play soccer. Neither can I."
  NEVER mix; NEVER "too" for negatives.

AGREEMENT RESPONSES (workplace reality):
  + statement → "So + aux + I" / "Me too" / "I do too"
  − statement → "Neither + aux + I" / "Me neither"
  formal:  "So do I." / "Neither am I."
  casual:  "Me too." / "Me neither."

THE BIG TRAP (book p.13 IMPORTANT):
  NEVER mix "not" with "neither":
  ✗ "I can't speak neither Spanish nor English."
  ✓ "I can speak neither Spanish nor English."
  Negative meaning is already inside "neither".
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.9 verbatim quote: *"We often use 'either…or' in a question or an affirmative sentence."* Book p.13 verbatim trap: *"'Neither…nor' are never used with a negative 'not'."* Book p.17 verbatim: *"'Both…and' is the equivalent to 'ambos…y' in Spanish, and is used in affirmative sentences."* Book p.10/14/18 examples drilled out loud: *"Can we eat either fruits or vegetables? / We can eat neither fruits nor vegetables. / We can eat both fruits and vegetables. / Either Kevin or Sergio can move the car. / Neither Kevin nor Sergio can move the car. / Both Kevin and Sergio can move the car."*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"Three structures, three meanings — no mixing, no leaving anyone out. Justice day 5."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 3 zones

Tape the 3 floor labels in 3 corners (or 3 walls) of the room:
- **EITHER … OR (1 of 2)**
- **NEITHER … NOR (0 of 2)**
- **BOTH … AND (2 of 2)**

Stand in the middle. Call a Spanish sentence out loud + the meaning (1 of 2 / 0 of 2 / 2 of 2). Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + say the full English sentence out loud as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 16 sentences in 15 min, ~1 per minute.

```
Spanish called → meaning → Right zone + right form
1.  "Podemos hablar inglés o español"  (1 of 2)
       → EITHER — "We can speak either English or Spanish."
2.  "Ni inglés ni español"             (0 of 2)
       → NEITHER — "Neither English nor Spanish."
3.  "Ambos inglés y español"           (2 of 2)
       → BOTH — "Both English and Spanish."
4.  "Cualquiera de los chicos"         (any 1)
       → EITHER — "Either of the boys."
5.  "Ninguno de los chicos"            (0)
       → NEITHER — "Neither of the boys."
6.  "Ambos de los chicos"              (2)
       → BOTH — "Both of the boys."
7.  "Yo tampoco como carne"            (negative)
       → NEITHER — "Neither do I." / "I don't, either."
8.  "Ambos cantar y bailar"            (2 of 2)
       → BOTH — "We can both sing and dance."
9.  "O caminamos o tomamos taxi"       (1 of 2)
       → EITHER — "We either walk or take a taxi."
10. "Ni mi madre ni mi padre cocinan"  (0 of 2)
       → NEITHER — "Neither my mother nor my father cooks."
11. "Ambos pan y mantequilla"          (2 of 2)
       → BOTH — "Both bread and butter."
12. "O hoy o mañana"                   (1 of 2)
       → EITHER — "Either today or tomorrow."
13. "Ni el carro ni la moto"           (0 of 2)
       → NEITHER — "Neither the car nor the bike."
14. "Ambos correr y nadar"             (2 of 2)
       → BOTH — "We can both run and swim."
15. "Cualquiera de las opciones"       (any 1)
       → EITHER — "Either of the options."
16. "Ninguna de las opciones"          (0)
       → NEITHER — "Neither of the options."
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — count the options." When the form is right AND zone is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook. Most common slip: students try "I can't speak neither…" — point to the board, do NOT shame, ask them to re-say without the "not."

### (10 min) Pair drilling with cards + agreement responses

Hand each pair: 6 decision-prompt cards (SET A) + 4 agreement-response cards (SET B). Pair work:

1. Student A picks a decision-prompt card, asks a YES/NO or choice question using EITHER: *"Do you prefer either coffee or tea?"*
2. Student B answers using BOTH (2 of 2), NEITHER (0 of 2), or picks 1 with a full sentence: *"I prefer both coffee and tea."* / *"I prefer neither coffee nor tea."* / *"Either is fine."*
3. Then A makes a personal statement: *"I don't drink coffee."* B replies with the AGREEMENT response card showing: *"Neither do I."* / *"Me neither."* / *"I don't, either."*
4. Swap roles after every 2 cards. Burn through all 6 cards.

Walk and coach the FORM (no double negative with neither, correct auxiliary in agreement response, full structure not fragment). Call out one win publicly per pair. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Three structures, three meanings — and the agreement responses match the structure. Justice day 5 — every option named."*

### (7 min) Podium — reinforce the determiner + agreement responses

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a SPECIAL prompt; produces the full sentence; cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"* If they hesitate or get it wrong, the cohort gives the chorus correction.

```
1st (DETERMINER + of):
   "0 of these 2 reports works."
       → "Neither of these two reports works."
2nd (AGREEMENT response negative):
   "My coworker doesn't drink coffee. You agree."
       → "Neither do I." / "Me neither." /
         "I don't, either."
3rd (BOTH … AND verbs):
   "Two abilities in your role: lead + listen."
       → "I can both lead and listen."
```

Then one final chorus drill — call all 3 structures in sequence, cohort responds in unison:

> *"Pick 1 of 2 options: coffee, tea — _____?"* → "Either coffee or tea."
> *"0 of 2 options: not coffee, not tea — _____?"* → "Neither coffee nor tea."
> *"2 of 2 options: coffee AND tea — _____?"* → "Both coffee and tea."

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "BOSS / TEAM DECISION MEETING"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"You're running a BOSS / TEAM DECISION MEETING. Triads, rotating roles. The BOSS posts a real-sounding decision with TWO options (vendor A or vendor B; today or tomorrow; trial or full demo). The TEAM MEMBER responds using EITHER, NEITHER, or BOTH plus an agreement response — a complete sentence, never a one-word reply. The OBSERVER marks each answer on the grid I'm handing out — right structure for the meaning, no double negative, right determiner form, correct agreement response. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. I will WRITE recurring errors on the side of the board live — when there are three on the board we stop and correct as a cohort."*

Hand out printed observer grid (prepared before class):

```
OBSERVER GRID — CL 35 GRAMMAR — BOSS / TEAM
DECISION MEETING

Team-member name (this round): __________

Mark each answer below as:
  ✓     = right structure + right form + right
          agreement response
  ✗ mix = mixed structure (e.g. "either A and B")
  ✗ neg = double negative ("can't … neither")
  ✗ det = wrong determiner ("either of" missing of)
  ✗ agr = wrong agreement ("So I do" / "Me also")
  ✗ frg = one-word fragment instead of full sentence

Round 1 — 8 answers:
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

Round 2 — 8 answers (must include 2 with
"neither + nor" or "neither + aux + I"):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

Round 3 — 8 answers (must include 2 with
"both … and" + 2 agreement responses):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

One sentence to the team-member at the end:
________________________________________________
```

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 8 boss/answer pairs. Boss speaks a real-sounding decision with two options; team member responds in 3 sec with a full EITHER / NEITHER / BOTH sentence; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for NEITHER … NOR + "Neither + aux + I" agreement — at least 2 per triad. This is the form they most avoid out of fear. Continue live board-error correction. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3: *"Three structures, three meanings — neither is a positive verb with a negative meaning. Justice day 5."*

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Push for BOTH … AND in 2 answers + agreement responses in another 2. By now they should be sharp. Slow down only for the most stubborn pattern you wrote on the board across the previous 2 rounds.

### (2 min) Cohort close
One strong triad demos 90 sec in front of the group — 3 answers covering EITHER, NEITHER, BOTH. Applause. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4: *"Three structures, three meanings — no mixing, no leaving anyone out. Justice day 5 — block CLOSES."*

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE — 15 min

### (4 min) Error paper recap — 4 most common errors today

Write on the board (anonymous; NAMES only in your personal notebook):

```
ERROR PAPER — CL 35 GRAMMAR

1. ❌ "I can't speak neither Spanish nor English."
   → ✅ "I can speak NEITHER Spanish NOR English."
     (book p.13 IMPORTANT — no NOT with neither)

2. ❌ "Either A and B."
   → ✅ "Either A OR B." / "Both A AND B."
     (either pairs with OR; only both pairs with AND)

3. ❌ "Me too." (after a negative statement)
   → ✅ "Me NEITHER." / "Neither do I." /
        "I don't, either."
     (positive agreement vs negative agreement)

4. ❌ "Either the boys can help you."
   → ✅ "EITHER OF the boys can help you."
     (determiner before "the" needs "of")
```

Cohort echoes the 4 corrections in chorus 1×. Note the named version in your personal notebook for the audit report — do NOT say names aloud.

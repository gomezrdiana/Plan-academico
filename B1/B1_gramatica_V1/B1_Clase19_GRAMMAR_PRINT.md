```
M28 — THE SUPERLATIVE (A2 Book — modulo pag 253)
       "You're the Best!"

From the book (Guia 1 — El Superlativo, pag 254):
"1. El superlativo tiene la forma, 'X es el/la mas
 (descriptor).'"
"2. La forma del superlativo depende del adjetivo, de una
 manera muy parecida al comparativo desigual."

"d. Hay unos descriptores irregulares; 'good' (bueno) and
 'bad' (malo):"
   (Bueno) Soy el mejor. -> (Good) I am THE BEST.
   (Malo)  Soy el peor.  -> (Bad)  I am THE WORST.

BOOK EXAMPLES (Ejemplos, pag 255):

   Sarah is the prettiest.
   The cat is the ugliest.
   My father is the most serious.
   The mass is the earliest.
   Diego is the tallest.
   Spanish is the most complicated.
   Diana is the nicest.
   Einstein is the most intelligent.

PRACTICA VERBAL 1 — Respuestas (pag 258):
   Cecilia is the prettiest.
   Monica is the richest.
   The school is the best.
   The dog is the ugliest.
   The nurses are the happiest.
   The engineer is the most serious.
   The hamburger is the most expensive.
   The woman is the earliest.
   The student is the latest.
   Spanish is the most complicated.

KEY: 1 syllable     -> "the (adj)-est".
     2 syll. in "-y" -> "the (adj)-iest".
     2+ syllables   -> "the most (adj)".
```

## 9. HUMAN-SENTENCE LINE-UP + STATION ROTATION (01:02–01:14, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"First you become words and line up as a correct superlative — and someone MUST hold the 'the'. Then three-type stations around the room — you move; the grammar stays."*

### Human-Sentence Line-up (4 min) — DE PIE

Reparte tarjetas grandes (1 palabra por estudiante: un sujeto, el verbo, **una tarjeta especial "THE"** que alguien sostiene siempre, el superlativo correcto, el grupo de referencia "of/in the …"). Por grupos, los estudiantes se PARAN en fila físicamente para formar la oración correcta — y físicamente deben decidir si el adjetivo toma "the -est", "the -iest", "the most" o es irregular ("the best/worst"). La tarjeta "THE" SIEMPRE tiene que estar en la fila — si nadie la sostiene, la oración no es superlativo. El grupo lee la oración en voz alta. 2-3 rondas, alternando 1 sílaba / "-y" / 2+ sílabas / irregular.

### Station Rotation (8 min) — DE PIE

3 estaciones rotuladas. Grupos rotan cada ~2.5 min (campana). Todos de pie.

```
STATION A — 1 SYLLABLE ("the -est")
Build 4 sentences about your job with 1-syllable adjectives
+ "the -est": fast, hard, cheap, slow, late, old, tall.
"Excel is the fastest tool of the office. That was the
 hardest task of the month. This supplier is the cheapest
 of the three. The morning shift is the latest in the
 schedule."

STATION B — "-Y" ("the -iest") + IRREGULAR ("the best/worst")
Build 4 sentences: 2 with "-y" adjectives (busy, happy,
easy) -> "the -iest"; 2 with "good/bad" -> "the
best/worst".
"Monday is the busiest day of the week. Maria is the
 happiest teammate this quarter. The new tool is the best
 software we have. My first draft was the worst report of
 the year."

STATION C — 2+ SYLLABLES ("the most ...")
Build 3 sentences with 2+-syllable adjectives + "the most
..." — and say WHY they take "the most" (not "the -est").
"The premium plan is the most expensive option. This
 system is the most reliable platform we use. The night
 shift is the most complicated part of the schedule."
```

Tú circulas entre estaciones, corriges en voz baja, anotas errores con NOMBRE.

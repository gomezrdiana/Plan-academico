## 2. CONTENT — PRESENT SIMPLE FULL TABLE + DRILL — 40 min — STAND UP

### Mini-talk + full table — 8 min — STAND

Write on the board (before class — full table):

```
PRESENT SIMPLE — A1 REVIEW M5–M9

AFFIRMATIVE
   I / you / we / they + VERB           "I work at 8."
   he / she / it       + VERB-S         "She works at 9."

NEGATIVE
   I / you / we / they + DON'T + BASE   "I don't work on Sundays."
   he / she / it       + DOESN'T + BASE "She doesn't work weekends."

QUESTION
   DO  + I/you/we/they + BASE + ?       "Do you work on Mondays?"
   DOES + he/she/it    + BASE + ?       "Does she work on Mondays?"

SPELLING RULES — THIRD-PERSON -S:
   1. Most verbs: + s                 (work → works, play → plays)
   2. -o / -s / -sh / -ch / -x: + es  (go → goes, watch → watches, finish → finishes)
   3. Consonant + y: -y → -ies        (study → studies, fly → flies, try → tries)
   4. Irregular: HAVE → HAS · BE → IS · DO → DOES

FREQUENCY ADVERBS — POSITION:
   BEFORE the main verb:    "She ALWAYS arrives early."
   AFTER the verb BE:       "She IS USUALLY on time."
   In QUESTION: between auxiliary and subject is RARE — adverb usually moves to
                end: "Do you check email often?" / "Does she usually arrive late?"

Common adverbs: always · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · never
```

Say at the board:
> *"AFFIRMATIVE: only third-person — he, she, it — takes the -s. Everyone else uses the base verb. NEGATIVE: the auxiliary takes the inflection — DOESN'T for 3rd person. After DOESN'T, the main verb is BASE — never doubled. Common error: 'he doesn't works' — wrong, DOESN'T already carries the -s. QUESTION: auxiliary first. DOES for 3rd person, DO for the rest. Verb stays BASE. Common error: 'does she works' — wrong, same reason. Spelling: -o/-s/-sh/-ch/-x get -es; consonant + y becomes -ies; HAVE → HAS; BE → IS. Frequency adverbs go BEFORE the main verb, but AFTER the verb BE. We drill it all standing in the next 30 minutes."*

### Walking transformation — 15 min — STAND, 4 zones in room

Four zones (tape labels on walls):
- **AFFIRMATIVE**
- **NEGATIVE**
- **QUESTION**
- **FREQUENCY** (adverb placement)

> *"Stand. I call a base sentence. I name a zone. You walk to that zone and SAY the transformed sentence out loud. Cohort verifies in chorus."*

12 sentences (mix all 4 zones):

```
1. "She works at 8."                   → NEGATIVE zone  → "She DOESN'T work at 8."
2. "I check email in the morning."     → QUESTION zone  → "DO I check email in the morning?" (rare — try YOU: "Do you check email in the morning?")
3. "He goes to the gym."               → NEGATIVE zone  → "He DOESN'T go to the gym."
4. "They close on Sundays."            → QUESTION zone  → "DO they close on Sundays?"
5. "She arrives early."                → FREQUENCY zone → "She USUALLY arrives early." (before main verb)
6. "We have lunch at 1."               → NEGATIVE zone  → "We DON'T have lunch at 1."
7. "He studies English."               → QUESTION zone  → "DOES he study English?" (verb stays BASE)
8. "I take the bus."                   → FREQUENCY zone → "I ALWAYS take the bus." (before main verb)
9. "She is on time."                   → FREQUENCY zone → "She IS USUALLY on time." (AFTER be)
10. "They finish at 5."                → NEGATIVE zone  → "They DON'T finish at 5."
11. "He plays soccer on Saturdays."    → QUESTION zone  → "DOES he play soccer on Saturdays?" (verb stays BASE)
12. "We work from home."               → FREQUENCY zone → "We RARELY work from home." (before main verb)
```

Each round: Grammar Teacher calls + names zone → student walks + says aloud → cohort thumbs + names the rule ("DOESN'T + base verb" / "adverb before main verb" / "DOES + base verb"). Note errors with NAMES.

**Insertion #1 of Phrase of the Day:** mid-round, after a clean answer: *"Three shapes, named cleanly — that's day 2 of temperance in the body."*

### Pair drilling — 10 min — STAND, pairs facing

Pull out **Sets A + B + C** (subject + base verb + frequency adverb cards).

> *"Pairs facing. Each pair takes 3 cards — one subject, one verb, one adverb. Build the AFFIRMATIVE, then the NEGATIVE, then the QUESTION — all three shapes in a row. Verify with your partner. Swap cards. 15 rounds across the pair."*

Example with subject=SHE / verb=WORK / adverb=USUALLY:
- Affirmative: *"She usually works at 8."* (verb-S + adverb before main verb)
- Negative: *"She doesn't usually work at 8."* (DOESN'T + base; adverb between auxiliary and verb is allowed here)
- Question: *"Does she usually work at 8?"* (DOES + base; adverb between subject and verb)

Grammar Teacher circulates, corrects on the spot — out loud, not whispered. If a pattern repeats, mark it on the board immediately so others see.

### Podium — 7 min — STAND, circle

Two volunteers up front. You give a SUBJECT + VERB + ADVERB. Each volunteer produces all THREE shapes (affirmative + negative + question) in 10 seconds. Cohort verifies in chorus: *"Confirmed — right shape!"*

```
A: "he / play / often"     → "He often plays." / "He doesn't often play." / "Does he often play?"
B: "we / study / never"    → "We never study." / "We don't study." (NEVER already negates — no double neg) / "Do we ever study?"
C: "she / have / usually"  → "She usually has." / "She doesn't usually have." / "Does she usually have?"
D: "they / go / sometimes" → "They sometimes go." / "They don't sometimes go." / "Do they sometimes go?"
E: "I / finish / always"   → "I always finish." / "I don't always finish." / "Do I always finish?" (more natural: "Do you always finish?")
```

Cover ≥3 third-person subjects (catch the -s) + ≥1 NEVER (catch the double-negation trap) + ≥1 frequency-after-BE.

Applause.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Three shapes — affirmative, negative, question — held cleanly. That is the rule, not the exception."*

## 3. LIVE APPLICATION — "OFFICE INTERROGATIVE — HR ONBOARDING INTERVIEW" — 45 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand. Push chairs to the walls. An HR onboarding scene: the HR rep is asking a new hire about their typical work week so they can match them to a team. Pairs. Each of you plays HR once and new hire once. I circulate and correct on the spot — out loud, in the room — and I mark recurring patterns on the board."*

Board:

```
"THE HR ONBOARDING INTERVIEW"
2 rounds · ~20 min each · PAIRS

ROLE A — HR REP (interviewer)
  Asks 8 question-form questions about the new
  hire's typical work week. Must use DO / DOES
  + subject + BASE verb (verb stays BASE).
  Suggested questions:
   1. "Do you usually start work at the same
       time every day?"
   2. "How often do you check email before
       lunch?"
   3. "Does your previous team always meet on
       Mondays?"
   4. "Do you often take work home in the
       evening?"
   5. "How often does your supervisor give you
       feedback?"
   6. "Do you sometimes work on Saturdays?"
   7. "Does your day usually end before 6?"
   8. "How often do you take a real lunch
       break?"

ROLE B — NEW HIRE (interviewee)
  Answers in FULL sentences using:
   - Present Simple AFFIRMATIVE (correct -s for
     3rd person)
   - At least 1 NEGATIVE per round (DOESN'T /
     DON'T + base verb — never double inflection)
   - 5 different frequency adverbs across the
     round (always / usually / often / sometimes
     / never)
   - 3rd-person references to former coworkers
     ("My old manager USUALLY arrives early") —
     must carry the -s

GRAMMAR TEACHER (coach, never plays HR)
  Circulates. Corrects on the spot OUT LOUD.
  Marks recurring patterns on the board so the
  whole cohort sees:
   - Missing -s: "she arrive" → "she ARRIVES"
   - Double inflection: "does she works" →
     "does she WORK"
   - Adverb position: "she arrives usually" →
     "she USUALLY arrives"
   - Wrong negative auxiliary: "he don't" →
     "he DOESN'T"
   - Spelling: studies / flies / has / does /
     finishes / watches
```

### Model — 2 min

Pull 2 volunteers. Demo a 60-second HR-new-hire exchange. Stop the moment a gap appears, name it, restart the line clean. Then turn it over.

### Round 1 — 20 min

Form pairs. Each pair: ONE student is HR, ONE is new hire. 20 minutes. Grammar Teacher circulates, corrects on the spot — out loud — and marks the recurring patterns visible on the board. After ~10 min, call for a quick mid-round check: *"Stop. Look at the board — three patterns we've seen most. Fix them in the next 10 minutes."*

### Round 2 — 20 min

SWITCH ROLES. New hire becomes HR; HR becomes new hire. Same 8-question script. Different specifics from their own work history.

**Insertion #3 of Phrase of the Day:** mid-round 2, during a strong exchange: *"He WORKS, she DOESN'T work, DO you work? Three shapes — and you are holding them at full speed."*

### Cohort close — 3 min — at the front

Pick ONE strong pair to demo a 90-second exchange in front of the cohort. Audience listens for 3rd-person -s + adverb position + question shape. Applause.

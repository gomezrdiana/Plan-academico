## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — relatives + embedded in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model EACH pattern out loud.

> *"M7 has two halves. HALF ONE — RELATIVE PRONOUNS. Six to land. WHO = people (subject role). WHOM = people (object role, formal — 'the colleague WHOM I emailed'). WHOSE = possession ('the manager WHOSE team handles staging'). WHICH = things (often non-defining, with commas — 'our roadmap, WHICH is public, lists Q3'). THAT = restrictive things or people ('the contract THAT we signed'). WHERE = places ('the meeting WHERE we agreed'). WHEN = time ('the week WHEN the demo is scheduled'). WHY = reason ('the reason WHY the build slipped'). DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING — defining clauses identify WHICH ONE, NO commas — 'The car THAT I bought is blue.' Non-defining clauses add extra info, COMMAS, almost always WHICH (not THAT) for things — 'My house, WHICH is big, is white.' OMISSION — when the relative pronoun is the OBJECT of a defining clause, you can drop it — 'The deck (THAT) I sent yesterday is on the drive.' HALF TWO — EMBEDDED QUESTIONS. The trap: when you put a question INSIDE another sentence ('Can you tell me…?' / 'Do you know…?' / 'I wonder…' / 'I'd like to know…'), the inner clause uses AFFIRMATIVE order, NOT question order. CORRECT: 'Can you tell me WHEN THE DECK IS DUE?' INCORRECT: 'Can you tell me WHEN IS THE DECK DUE?' No DO/DOES inside either — CORRECT: 'Do you know WHAT THE CLIENT NEEDS?' INCORRECT: 'Do you know WHAT DOES THE CLIENT NEED?' For yes/no embedded questions, use IF or WHETHER — 'I wonder IF we should escalate.' Fortitude day 5 in this room: closure = courteous shape. The information is added before the ask; the ask is shaped right before the answer."*

Demo each rule on the same situation twice. Then:

> *"Most workplace embedded questions slip on inversion — the speaker pulls in 'DOES' or 'IS' before the subject. That's question-order leaking inside an embed. Hear it, kill it. Fortitude day 5: close the block on courteous shape."*

### (15 min) Guided practice in pairs — 12 prompt cards (peer teacher rotation embedded)

Hand each pair the deck of 12 cards. They take turns: one reads the card, the other delivers the right shape. Then swap. Walk between pairs.

Card deck:
```
1.  "Add info about: my manager (oversees three teams)" → relative WHO + commas
2.  "Add info about: the contract (we signed last quarter)" → restrictive THAT
3.  "Add info about: our roadmap (public, lists Q3)" → non-restrictive WHICH + commas
4.  "Add info about: the dev lead (team handles staging)" → WHOSE
5.  "Add info about: the meeting room (we agreed on the SLA)" → WHERE
6.  "Add info about: the week (the demo is scheduled)" → WHEN
7.  "DIRECT: 'When does the client want the deck?' → embed in 'Can you tell me…?'"
8.  "DIRECT: 'Where is the design review?' → embed in 'Do you know…?'"
9.  "DIRECT: 'Did the QA team sign off?' → embed in 'I wonder…' (use IF)"
10. "DIRECT: 'What does the PM need?' → embed in 'I'd like to know…'"
11. "Omit the relative: 'The deck THAT I sent yesterday is on the drive.'"
12. "DIRECT: 'Should we escalate?' → embed in 'Can you tell me…?' (use IF)"
```

Coach softly: missing commas in non-defining, "where DOES she work" inversion, no IF for yes/no embeds, WHICH used in defining where THAT is cleaner, no omission attempted. Note errors with NAMES.

**Peer teacher inside this block (3 min):** halfway through, pause one pair and have ONE student read aloud the Grammar pase patterns from previous class M6 — "despite OF the pressure" → "DESPITE the pressure" / "because of the team was late" → "because the team was late" / "however the team" → "however, the team". Cohort echoes 2×. Bridge: *"M6 connectors join two clauses; M7 embeds one clause inside another. Both happen in the same memo — 'The client WHO confirmed Tuesday BECAUSE OF the early demo is the same one WHO emailed Friday'."* Resume pair work.

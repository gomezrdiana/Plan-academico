## 2. ICEBREAKER — "WILL YOU EVER?" LINES (00:07–00:20, 13 min)

**Setup (1 min):** "STAND UP. Push your chairs to the walls. Form 2 lines, facing each other. 8 students per line. We're going to play 'WILL YOU EVER?'"

**Reglas:**
- Tú lees una pregunta empezando con "Will you ever...?"
- Si la respuesta es SÍ → da 1 paso AL FRENTE (hacia tu compañero).
- Si la respuesta es NO → da 1 paso ATRÁS.
- Después de cada pregunta, los que se acercaron tienen 30 seg para hablar entre sí del tema. Los que retrocedieron también — entre ellos.

**10 preguntas:**
```
1. Will you ever live in another country?
2. Will you ever start your own business?
3. Will you ever skydive?
4. Will you ever forgive someone who really hurt you?
5. Will you ever wake up at 5 AM willingly?
6. Will you ever travel alone?
7. Will you ever go vegetarian for life?
8. Will you ever marry?
9. Will you ever sing in public?
10. Will you ever be famous?
```

Tu trabajo: camina con papel de errores. Los que se mueven hacia adelante están más comprometidos — escucha qué dicen entre ellos. Anota errores comunes (especialmente will + estructuras).

**Cierre (2 min):** "Sit down. Who heard a partner say something that surprised them?" 2 students share.

## 3. HOT TOPIC — "WILL ROBOTS REPLACE US?" (00:20–00:35, 15 min)

**Topic en el tablero:**
```
HOT TOPIC:
"In 10 years, AI and robots WILL replace 50% of the jobs
in Colombia. Some say this is good (more time, less work).
Others say this is a disaster (no income, no purpose).

Where do YOU stand? What WILL we do about it?"
```

**Setup (2 min):** Read the topic. Each student picks side: "Side A — it's GOOD" / "Side B — it's a DISASTER." They sit with their side.

**Round 1 — same side prep (5 min):** Pairs with someone on YOUR side. "Build the strongest argument together. 5 minutes. Use WILL minimum 4 times. Use a number/year minimum 2 times."

**Round 2 — debate cross-side (8 min):** Pairs face the OTHER side.
- 90 sec attack from Side A.
- 90 sec counter from Side B.
- 30 sec rebuttal each.

3 mini-debates. Class judges who was MORE persuasive.

**Anota errores graves de WILL:** "Will replace" en lugar de "will be replaced" (passive), "will not work" vs "won't work" (contracción), "by 2030" (preposition).

> Si alguien usa "going to" en lugar de "will" — déjalo. La diferencia se ve en clases más adelante.

## 4. TBLT — "ESCAPE PLAN" SIMULATION (00:35–01:00, 25 min)

**The task:** Pairs are stranded together in a fictional emergency. They must produce an ESCAPE PLAN with 6 steps using WILL + time/place markers (IN/ON/AT).

**Setup (3 min):** Show the scenarios on the board. Each pair picks ONE:

```
ESCAPE SCENARIOS:
A) You're stuck in an elevator with your coworker. The phone is dead.
B) You're lost in a remote area in Colombia. Your car broke down.
C) You're at a wedding and you just realized your ex is also there.
D) You arrive at the airport. You discover your passport is expired.
```

Reglas:
- Build a 6-step escape plan IN ENGLISH.
- Each step uses **WILL** + a TIME (IN/ON/AT) or a PLACE preposition.
- Plan must take less than 24 hours.

**Pairs work (12 min):** Pairs write/discuss their plan. **STANDING is encouraged** — they can pace as they think. ENGLISH ONLY.

**Modelo en el tablero:**
```
EXAMPLE PLAN (Stuck in elevator):
1. We will press the emergency button at 9:01.
2. In 5 minutes, we will hear voices from outside.
3. We will tell them we're on the 4th floor.
4. We will sit on the floor while we wait.
5. They will arrive in 20 minutes with the technician.
6. By noon, we'll be free at the lobby having coffee.
```

**Presentation (8 min):** Each pair presents their plan as if to an emergency dispatcher. Class scores 1-5 on:
- Clarity
- Use of WILL (correct construction)
- Use of TIME/PLACE prepositions

The team with highest score wins **Escape Master of the Day**. Photo for redes.

**Cierre (2 min):** "What was the most creative plan you heard? One sentence why."

> Si una pareja se traba: "Pick the SCARIEST escape route first. Then build the plan around it." Si dicen "I don't know what would happen": "Don't predict — INVENT."

## 5. SHADOWING — DÍA 3: PRESENTACIÓN DE MEMORIA (01:00–01:20, 20 min) — ⚠️ NO SE PUEDE SALTAR

**⚠️ HOY ES DÍA 3 DEL CICLO. NO HAY VIDEO. PRESENTACIÓN DE MEMORIA.**

**Recordatorio del modelo:**
- Día 1 (Clase 2): vieron el video por primera vez.
- Día 2 (Clase 3): repitieron los 5 pasos del mismo video.
- **Día 3 (HOY): grupos de 4 reproducen el diálogo de memoria al frente.**

**Setup (3 min):** Di: "Today is the closing of the Shadowing cycle. The video stays OFF. Groups of 4 reproduce the dialogue from memory. Best group gets 5 English Points."

**Forma 4 grupos de 4** (mezcla los 16 estudiantes — anchors con catch-up).

**Preparación de los grupos (5 min):** Each group:
- Decides who plays which character of the video.
- Practices the dialogue from memory as a group. Whisper or quiet voices.
- One round all together to feel the rhythm.

**Presentaciones (10 min):** Cada grupo pasa al frente. **2 minutos por grupo × 4 grupos = 8 min + 2 min de transición.**
- The group performs the dialogue from memory.
- No notes. No phones. Just voices.
- Audience listens, applauds at the end.

**Mini-competencia (2 min):** Class votes on:
- 🥇 Most accurate dialogue (closest to original)
- 🥈 Best pronunciation
- 🥉 Most natural delivery (not robotic)

3 winners → 5 English Points each. Photo for redes.

**Cierre:** "Tomorrow Bloque 2 starts a NEW cycle. New video. Day 1. Stay tuned."

> **Por qué cierra el ciclo así:** los estudiantes que el Día 1 no entendían NADA hoy reproducen párrafos completos. Esa magia es la consolidación. Si saltas este bloque (porque "se sienten incómodos"), pierdes el cierre que justifica todo el ciclo. Si lo haces — viven uno de los momentos más memorables del nivel.

## 6. PROJECT WORKSHOP — PAGE 3: MY OBSTACLES IN ENGLISH (01:20–01:35, 15 min)

**Project context:** Cada estudiante construye su proyecto personal "MY STORY, MY GOALS" durante B1.
- Class 1: brainstorm
- Class 2: Page 1 — MY STORY, MY GOALS (intro)
- Class 3: Page 2 — MY MILESTONES
- **Hoy: Page 3 — MY OBSTACLES IN ENGLISH**

**Page 3 contains (escribe estructura en el tablero):**
```
MY OBSTACLES IN ENGLISH:

3 obstacles I FACE right now in learning English (use Present Continuous):
- I am struggling with ________
- I am still confused by ________
- I am afraid of ________

What WILL I do about each? (use WILL):
- For obstacle 1, I will ________
- For obstacle 2, I will ________
- For obstacle 3, I will ________

ONE obstacle I have already overcome (use Present Perfect):
- I have learned to ________
```

**Workshop (12 min):** Each student writes Page 3 in their notebook. SILENT. Camina anotando errores.

**Sharing (3 min):** 1 voluntario lee 1 sección. Class applauds. NO peer correction during sharing — emotional space.

> Esta página conecta directamente con la **Carta del Yo del Futuro** (sellada en Class 1). Hoy escriben sus miedos actuales — su yo futuro los va a leer al graduarse y verá CUÁLES superó.

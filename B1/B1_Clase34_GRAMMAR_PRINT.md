```
PHRASE OF THE DAY — CL 34 GRAMMAR
"On day four of justice we confirm with the
 right shape — 'You sent it, didn't you? You
 aren't worried, are you?' — same auxiliary,
 opposite polarity, never trapping."
```

Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Two tags on the board. First: 'You sent it, didn't you?' Statement is positive ('sent'), no visible auxiliary, so we pull out 'did' for past simple and make the tag negative → 'didn't you?'. Same subject 'you'. Second: 'You aren't worried, are you?' Statement is negative ('aren't'), so the tag is positive in the same auxiliary → 'are you?'. Same subject. Same auxiliary, opposite polarity — that is the whole rule in four words. End of phrase: 'never trapping' — Justice day 4: a tag confirms a shared fact, it does not load blame."*

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing board race
Two columns on the board. You call an auxiliary in its positive form; the first student of each row runs to the board and writes the NEGATIVE contraction. Cohort verifies. 8 rounds in 3 minutes.

```
"is"     → "isn't"
"are"    → "aren't"
"was"    → "wasn't"
"were"   → "weren't"
"have"   → "haven't"
"has"    → "hasn't"
"did"    → "didn't"
"will"   → "won't"
```

Quick note as you erase: *"These are the 8 contractions you'll need in 90% of tags today. The other 10% — 'shall' for let's, 'will/can' for imperative, 'aren't I' for I am — we handle in the next block."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — form rules from the book + 4 special cases

Stand at the board. Book p.3-4 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M1 — QUESTION TAGS — FORM RULES (B1 Book p.3-4)

ONE RULE — three pieces:
  (1) SAME AUXILIARY as the principal statement
  (2) OPPOSITE POLARITY (affirmative → negative
      tag; negative → affirmative tag)
  (3) SAME SUBJECT pronoun

When no auxiliary is visible (present simple,
past simple), PULL OUT do / does / did:
  "Sam plays soccer, doesn't he?"
  "You play soccer, don't you?"
  "Andres played soccer, didn't he?"

4 SPECIAL CASES (book p.3 + extension):
  • I AM → "aren't I?"  (book p.3 — amn't
    does not exist)
    "I am very tall, aren't I?"
  • IMPERATIVE → "will you? / can you?"
    "Open the door, will you?"
  • LET'S → "shall we?"
    "Let's go, shall we?"
  • THERE IS/ARE → "isn't there / aren't
    there?"   "There is a meeting, isn't there?"
  • NOTHING → positive tag (negative word
    "nothing" counts as a negative statement)
    "Nothing's broken, is it?"

INTONATION (book note + workplace reality):
  ↗ RISING = genuine question (I want a real
              answer)
  ↘ FALLING = expecting confirmation (I already
              know, I'm confirming aloud)
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.3 verbatim quote: *"The question tag uses the same auxiliary as the principal statement."* Book p.4 verbatim examples drilled out loud: *"We can eat lunch, can't we? / We can't eat lunch, can we? / Bella should do her homework, shouldn't she? / Your friend is sick, isn't he? / The boys are playing tennis, aren't they? / You went to school, didn't you? / Mark makes breakfast, doesn't he? / I am working, aren't I? / Edith will take a cookie, won't she? / We are going to call Mom, aren't we?"*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"Same auxiliary, opposite polarity, same subject. Justice day 4 — never trapping."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners (or 4 walls) of the room:
- **REGULAR**
- **I AM (→ aren't I)**
- **IMPERATIVE (→ will you / can you)**
- **LET'S / THERE / NOTHING**

Stand in the middle. Call a statement out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + say the full tagged sentence out loud as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong tag, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 16 statements in 15 min, ~1 per minute.

```
Statement called               → Right zone + right tag
1.  "You're coming."           → REGULAR — "aren't you?"
2.  "I'm ready."               → I AM — "aren't I?"
3.  "Let's go."                → LET'S — "shall we?"
4.  "Open the door."           → IMPERATIVE — "will you?"
5.  "There's a meeting."       → THERE — "isn't there?"
6.  "She isn't here."          → REGULAR — "is she?"
7.  "Nothing is broken."       → NOTHING — "is it?"
8.  "We did it."               → REGULAR — "didn't we?"
9.  "He hasn't called."        → REGULAR — "has he?"
10. "I am late."               → I AM — "aren't I?"
11. "Close the window."        → IMPERATIVE — "will you?"
12. "Let's reschedule."        → LET'S — "shall we?"
13. "There are 5 people."      → THERE — "aren't there?"
14. "They won't agree."        → REGULAR — "will they?"
15. "You can do it."           → REGULAR — "can't you?"
16. "It's clear."              → REGULAR — "isn't it?"
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — listen to the statement first." When the tag is right but the zone is right too, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (10 min) Pair drilling with cards + intonation marker

Hand each pair: 6 statement cards (SET A) + 6 auxiliary tag cards (SET B) + 1 intonation marker per pair (↗ or ↘). Pair work:

1. Student A reads the statement card.
2. Student B picks the right auxiliary tag card + adds the right SUBJECT pronoun + speaks the full tagged sentence with the intonation shown on the marker.
3. Swap roles after every card. After 5 cards each, swap the intonation marker so they practice both ↗ and ↘.

Walk and coach the FORM (right auxiliary, right polarity, right subject) AND the INTONATION (up or down — make the hand visible from across the room). Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Same auxiliary, opposite polarity, same subject. Falling = confirm; rising = ask. Justice day 4 — fair only."*

### (7 min) Podium — reinforce the 4 special cases

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a SPECIAL-CASE statement; produces the full tagged sentence; cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"* If they hesitate or get it wrong, the cohort gives the chorus correction.

```
1st (I AM):        "I am running this kickoff."   → "I am running this kickoff, aren't I?"
2nd (LET'S):       "Let's redeploy at lunch."     → "Let's redeploy at lunch, shall we?"
3rd (IMPERATIVE):  "Pass me the report."          → "Pass me the report, will you?"
```

Then one final chorus drill — you call all 4 special-case patterns in sequence, cohort responds in unison:

> *"I am late — _____?"* → "aren't I?"
> *"Let's go — _____?"* → "shall we?"
> *"Open the door — _____?"* → "will you?"
> *"There is a meeting — _____?"* → "isn't there?"

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "OFFICE CONFIRMATION INTERROGATIVE"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"You're running an OFFICE CONFIRMATION INTERROGATIVE — pair work in rotating triads. Three roles: STATEMENT-MAKER reads a flat statement (any topic — your real week works fine). TAG-ADDER instantly produces the full statement + correct tag + the right intonation. OBSERVER marks each tag on the grid I'm handing out — right auxiliary, right polarity, right subject, right intonation. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. I will WRITE recurring errors on the board live as I hear them — when there are three on the board we stop and correct as a cohort."*

Hand out printed observer grid (prepared before class):

```
OBSERVER GRID — CL 34 GRAMMAR — OFFICE CONFIRMATION INTERROGATIVE

Tag-adder name (this round): __________

Mark each tag below as:
  ✓     = right aux + right polarity + right subject + right intonation
  ✗ aux = wrong auxiliary (e.g. "isn't she?" after past simple)
  ✗ pol = wrong polarity / same sign
  ✗ sub = wrong subject pronoun
  ✗ int = flat or wrong intonation
  ✗ spc = special-case miss (amn't I / no shall / no will-you for imperative)

Round 1 — 8 tags:
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

Round 2 — 8 tags (must include 2 specials):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

Round 3 — 8 tags (must include 1 negative
statement + positive tag):
  1: ____  2: ____  3: ____  4: ____
  5: ____  6: ____  7: ____  8: ____

One sentence to the tag-adder at the end:
________________________________________________
```

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 8 statement + tag pairs. Statement-maker speaks; tag-adder responds in 2 sec; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for the 4 SPECIAL CASES (I am / let's / imperative / there). Each triad must produce at least 2 specials in their 8 tags. Continue live board-error correction. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3: *"Same auxiliary, opposite polarity, same subject. Special cases obey it too."*

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Push for NEGATIVE statements with POSITIVE tags — at least 1 in each triad. By now they should be sharp. Slow down only for the most stubborn pattern you wrote on the board across the previous 2 rounds.

### (2 min) Cohort close
One strong triad demos 90 sec in front of the group — 3 tags including 1 special. Applause. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4: *"Same auxiliary, opposite polarity, same subject. Justice day 4 — confirm, never trap."*

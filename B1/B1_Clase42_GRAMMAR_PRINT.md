## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — M9 Perfect Tenses (B1 Book p.97-116, Guides 1-5)

Stand at the board. Book p.99 + p.103 + p.107 + p.111 + p.115 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M9 — PERFECT TENSES — FORM RULES (B1 Book p.97-116)

PRES PERFECT  — HAS/HAVE + past participle
                Open/unfinished period · unspec. time
                · very recent (JUST) · with EVER/YET/
                ALREADY/SINCE/FOR
                "I have lived here SINCE 2014."
                "I have JUST finished."
                "Have you EVER tried it?"

PAST PERFECT  — HAD + past participle
                Action before another past action
                "When we moved, I had learned Spanish."
                "I had already left when she called."

FUT PERFECT   — WILL HAVE + past participle
                Done BY a deadline
                "I will have finished by 5pm."
                "By 2030, I will have worked 10 years."

PERFECT CONT  — HAS/HAVE/HAD/WILL HAVE + been + V-ing
                Duration of an ongoing action
                "I have been working all day."
                "By tonight, I will have been
                 watching the film for 3 hours."

ALREADY / YET (Guide 5):
  ALREADY → affirmative, before the time referenced
  YET     → negative or question, at the end
            "I haven't finished YET."
            "Have you sent it YET?"
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.99 verbatim (drilled out loud): *"We use the present perfect for events that occurred in an unknown or unspecified period of time / events that are not complete / events in a period of time that is open / happened very recently."* Book p.103 verbatim: *"We use the past perfect together with the simple past to talk about events that happened before another past event."* Book p.107 verbatim: *"The future perfect describes an event that will be complete by a certain time limit."*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"Place actions in time precisely. Prudence day 2 — present perfect for open, past perfect for before-another-past, future perfect for by-a-deadline."*

### (12 min) Walking transformation — 4 zones (M9 + M10 integrated)

Tape the 4 floor labels in the 4 corners:
- **PRES PERFECT (open/unfinished)**
- **PAST PERFECT (before another past)**
- **FUT PERFECT (done by a deadline)**
- **CAUSATIVE (delegate / have done)**

Stand in the middle. Call a context out loud. Students physically WALK to the zone that fits + speak the full sentence as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 14 calls in 12 min.

```
Context called                                       → Right zone + sample sentence
1.  "I started this job in 2024; still here."        → PRES PERFECT — "I HAVE WORKED here SINCE 2024."
2.  "I finished the report before the meeting."      → PAST PERFECT  — "I HAD FINISHED the report when the meeting started."
3.  "I will be done by Friday."                      → FUT PERFECT   — "I WILL HAVE FINISHED by Friday."
4.  "The mechanic fixes my car (not me)."            → CAUSATIVE     — "I HAVE my car FIXED by the mechanic."
5.  "Open period — have you ever been to Cartagena?" → PRES PERFECT — "Have you EVER been to Cartagena?"
6.  "Cleaner cleans the office (not me)."            → CAUSATIVE     — "I GET the office CLEANED every Friday."
7.  "Tasks done before client arrived."              → PAST PERFECT  — "We HAD COMPLETED the tasks before the client arrived."
8.  "Tasks done by next quarter."                    → FUT PERFECT   — "We WILL HAVE COMPLETED the tasks by next quarter."
9.  "Today is not over — I already sent 5 emails."   → PRES PERFECT — "I HAVE already SENT 5 emails TODAY."
10. "Painter paints my house tomorrow."              → CAUSATIVE     — "I AM GETTING my house PAINTED tomorrow."
11. "I had been working 3 hrs when she arrived."     → PAST PERFECT  — "I HAD BEEN WORKING for 3 hours when she arrived."
12. "By tonight, 3 hrs of the film."                 → FUT PERFECT   — "By tonight I WILL HAVE BEEN WATCHING the film for 3 hours."
13. "Boss makes me stay late."                       → CAUSATIVE     — "The boss MADE me STAY late." (active causative, no "to")
14. "I helped Sebastian finish."                     → CAUSATIVE     — "I HELPED Sebastian FINISH." (no "to" — book p.119)
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — what's the time marker?" When the zone is right and the form is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (4 min) Mini-talk — M10 Active + Passive Causative (B1 Book p.117-126)

Stand at the board. Book p.119 + p.123 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M10 — CAUSATIVE — FORM RULES (B1 Book p.117-126)

ACTIVE CAUSATIVE — someone causes another to act
  Verbs WITH infinitive ("to"): ASK / ALLOW / GET /
    TELL / WANT / NEED
    "I asked Manuela TO work."
  Verbs WITHOUT infinitive (no "to"): HELP / LET /
    MAKE / HAVE
    "I helped Manuela work."
    "I made Manuela work."
    "I let Manuela work."

PASSIVE CAUSATIVE — important = action done, not who
  Subject + HAVE / GET + object + PAST PARTICIPLE
    "I had the car washed."
    "I got my hair done."
    "I will have the customer called."
  HAVE = more formal · GET = more common spoken
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.119 verbatim: *"Most verbs use the infinitive. The rule is the same as for gerunds in Level A2. If the verb implies that the main action has not started, then we use the infinitive. If the verb implies that the main action has started, then we do not use the infinitive."* Book p.123 verbatim: *"The passive causative has the form: Subject + Causative verb ('have' or 'get') + Direct Object + Action (In Past Participle). Only 2 causative verbs exist; 'get' and 'have'. 'Get' is more common, while 'have' is more formal."*

### (8 min) Pair drilling + Podium — reversal perfect tenses + causative

**Pair drilling (4 min):** Hand each pair: 3 perfect-tense cards + 2 marker cards (FOR / SINCE / ALREADY / YET) + 1 causative card. Student A reads cards, Student B builds a full sentence. Swap every card. Walk and coach.

**Podium (4 min):** Draw a podium on the board. Pick 3 strong students. Each reverses a perfect-tense sentence into another perfect tense + a causative variant. Cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"*

```
1st: "I have finished the report."
     → PAST PERFECT:    "I had finished the report when she called."
     → CAUSATIVE:        "I had the report finished by the assistant."

2nd: "By Friday, I will complete the migration."
     → FUT PERFECT:      "By Friday, I WILL HAVE COMPLETED the migration."
     → PASSIVE CAUSE:    "By Friday, I will have the migration COMPLETED."

3rd: "She fixes the bug herself."
     → ACTIVE CAUSE:     "She MAKES the team FIX the bug."
     → PASSIVE CAUSE:    "She GETS the bug FIXED by the team."
```

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Place actions in time + delegate with shape. Prudence day 2 — precise placement, no slop."*

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads + live board errors

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Each triad runs 6 perfect + 4 causative statements. Reviewer speaks; responder reacts in 3 sec keeping tense family; observer marks. You circulate and WRITE EVERY RECURRING ERROR on the side of the board as you hear it. When 3 errors are on the board, freeze — cohort corrects in chorus 2×, erase, continue.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate roles. Push for PAST PERFECT + PASSIVE CAUSATIVE — at least 2 each per triad. Watch for missing past participle in passive causative.

## 6. HOT TOPIC — "WHAT DOES A PROFESSIONAL LOOK LIKE IN MY INDUSTRY?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural/lexical (describing people), not a narrative. Today is pure speaking. The topic: a GOOD professional in your industry — what do they look like? Last class you named clothes and colors. Today you name the person."*

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "WHAT DOES A PROFESSIONAL LOOK LIKE
              IN MY INDUSTRY?"

Discuss with your partner:

1. Describe a GOOD professional from your industry.
   Use "to be" + "to have" + 5+ personal adjectives
   (average height, slender, strong, calm, young,
   middle-aged, tall, short):
   "A good supervisor is somewhat tall and has a
    calm face. She has steady hands when the plan
    changes. She is average-height and average-
    weight — present, not loud."

2. Describe how they CARRY themselves at work —
   physically and behaviorally (NOT gossip;
   description only):
   "He has clear eyes in long meetings. He is
    quiet under pressure. His hands are steady on
    the keyboard when there is bad news."

3. "What does a PROFESSIONAL look like?" — give an
   HONEST answer: TEMPERANCE day 2 — describe the
   calm, steady version. Don't invent a hero, don't
   paint a villain.

Requirements (12 min):
- 5+ uses of "TO BE" or "TO HAVE" + personal
  adjective ("she is tall", "he has short hair",
  "she is calm")
- 3+ PERSONAL ADJECTIVES (tall, slender, calm,
  strong, young, average-height)
- 1+ MODIFIER ("very" / "somewhat")
- DESCRIPTION only — no judgment, no gossip
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

### Plenary — 3 min

2 students share a real professional description using 5+ "to be"/"to have" + personal adjectives, and why the clean description (no judgment) matters.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Listen to what you just said: 'she IS tall', 'he HAS steady hands', 'she IS somewhat young'. That structure has a name. Juan Diego gives it the name this afternoon."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE WITNESS DESCRIPTION" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. A new person came to your work — a client, a technician, a trainer, a candidate. Tomorrow a colleague has to recognize them at the door. Describe them PHYSICALLY so the colleague identifies them at first sight. Useful, neutral, professional — no gossip, no judgment."*

Board:

```
"THE WITNESS DESCRIPTION" — 3–4 min each

Describe a real person from your work this week so a
colleague identifies them tomorrow. Use "to be" + "to
have" + personal adjectives + body parts. Neutral,
professional.

POINT 1: "He is tall and slender."             (TO BE)
POINT 2: "He has short, curly hair."           (TO HAVE)
POINT 3: "He has a beard and a mustache."      (TO HAVE)
POINT 4: "His eyes are dark and his nose is
          straight."                           (BE+body)
POINT 5: "He is somewhat young — about 30."    (MODIFIER)
POINT 6: "He was wearing a light blue shirt
          and dark pants."                     (recycle M36)

USE: 5+ "to be" / "to have" + personal adjectives /
     body parts / 1+ modifier ("very" / "somewhat") /
     professional context ONLY / no judgment, no gossip
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me describe the new auditor who came on Monday. He is average height and somewhat slender. He has short, straight black hair, a small mustache, no beard. His face is round and his eyes are dark. He has long, thin fingers — quick on the keyboard. He is calm in the meeting room — quiet, but he asks one very precise question per hour. He is somewhat young, probably mid-thirties. He was wearing a dark gray suit and a light blue shirt — no tie. One thing I will not do here: I will not say if he is 'good' or 'bad' at his job — that is judgment, not description. Description only. Tomorrow when you see him at the front door, you will recognize him."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A presents the witness description (5–6 physical data points from work), B is the colleague who has to identify + counts how many "to be"/"to have" structures A used and whether it stayed clean (no hidden judgment, no gossip).

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their witness description in front of the group. Cohort counts "to be"/"to have" + adjectives. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

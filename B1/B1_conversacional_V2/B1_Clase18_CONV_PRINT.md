## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT NARRATIVE INTEGRATION (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande el MAPA DE LA HISTORIA
   (4 capas, cada una con su forma):

   A PAST STORY — WHICH FORM FOR WHICH LINE

   (1) THE SCENE / BACKGROUND
        used to + base  -> old habit/state
            "I used to work in a small office."
        was/were + -ing -> what was happening
            "One morning I was preparing a report."

   (2) THE TURNING POINT / EVENT
        when + past simple -> the thing that happened
            "...when the manager called me in."
        past simple        -> completed action
            "She gave me a huge new client."

   (3) THE STRUGGLE
        past simple + past continuous mixed
            "I didn't know the system. I was failing
             every test while the deadline came closer."

   (4) THE RESOLUTION / SEQUENCE
        first ... then ... after that ... finally
            "First I asked for help. Then I practiced
             every night. After that it got easier.
             Finally I closed the deal."

   *** Background = was -ing.  Event = simple. ***
   *** Old habit = used to.  Order = connectors. ***

2. SEÑALA capa (1). DI: "The scene is the slow part —
   what was already true or already happening. Old habit
   = used to. What was in progress = was -ing. This is
   Module 12: 'I was eating pasta when my brother
   arrived' — the was -ing is the background."

3. SEÑALA capa (2). DI: "The turning point is fast and
   finished. Past simple. The classic frame from the
   book: WAS -ING ... WHEN + PAST SIMPLE. The long
   action gets cut by the short one."

4. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is what the phrase does — I USED TO
   struggle (scene), I WAS failing WHEN the chance came
   (turning point), I PUSHED through, and THEN I won
   (sequence). Every form, one story."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I used to worked in sales."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I used to WORK in sales."
   DI: "After USED TO = base verb. No -ed." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces en coro.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I was prepare a report when she
   called." -> ESCRIBE "I was PREPARING a report when
   she called." DI: "Background = was + -ing. The event
   that cuts it = past simple." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande en board:

   SEQUENCING CONNECTORS (keep the order clean)

      first ...            -> the start
      then / after that ...-> the next step
      while ...            -> two things at once
      when ...             -> the moment one cut another
      finally / in the end -> the close

      *** Use them to walk the listener through time. ***
      *** A story without connectors sounds like a list. ***

8. SEÑALA connectors. DI: "These are the rails. 'First
   I asked for help. While I practiced, my confidence
   grew. Then the test came. Finally I passed.' Same
   facts, but now the listener follows the timeline."

9. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "Then I am winning the client."
   -> TACHA "am winning", ESCRIBE "Then I WON the
   client." DI: "Fortitude — stay in the past. The
   story does not jump to the present." COHORTE repite
   2 veces.

10. MODELA UNA historia completa en voz alta (60 seg),
    apuntando a cada capa del board mientras la dices:
    "I used to work alone. One afternoon I was finishing
    a report when my boss gave me a difficult client.
    First I panicked. I didn't know the product and I
    was losing time. Then I asked a colleague. After
    that I practiced every night. Finally I closed the
    deal." DI: "Hear it? Used to — was -ing — when +
    simple — then — finally. That is integration."

11. PIDELES en parejas: "Each person tells a 5-line
    past story about a real work or study challenge.
    Line 1: the scene (used to / was -ing). Line 2: the
    turning point (when + past simple). Line 3: the
    struggle. Lines 4-5: the resolution with first / then
    / finally. Partner listens and names which form you
    used in each line." 8 minutos.

12. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
    con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

13. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien salte al presente o
    use "used to went", DI suave: "Fortitude. Stay in
    the past. Hold the timeline — used to, was -ing,
    then it happened."

14. ESCRIBE una tercera seccion grande en board:

    STORY OPENERS & CLOSERS (so it sounds whole)

       OPEN:  "Let me tell you about the time I..."
              "A few years ago I used to..."
              "One day I was ... when ..."
       CLOSE: "...and in the end I learned that..."
              "...that's how I pushed through."
              "...and finally it worked out."

       *** A story needs a door in and a door out. ***

15. SEÑALA openers/closers. DI: "Don't start with the
    middle. Open with the scene, close with what you
    learned or how you pushed through. That frame turns
    five sentences into a story."

16. PIDELES en parejas (nueva): "Retell your story —
    but now add a real opener and a real closer, and
    make it 6-8 lines. Partner counts: did you use at
    least one used to OR was -ing, one when + simple,
    and two sequencing connectors?" 7 minutos.

17. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo encadene scene
    -> turning point -> resolution sin saltar al presente
    y con al menos 2 connectors. DI: "Stand up — we go
    to the interview."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y la cohorte aun mezcla presente y pasado a mitad de historia, NO agregues openers/closers. Reduce el objetivo a 3 capas (scene + turning point + 1 connector) y deja openers/closers como callback en Cl 19. Mejor UNA historia limpia de 4 lineas que 8 lineas con saltos de tiempo.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas narrando 6-8 lineas con used to/was -ing + when + past simple + connectors, manteniendose en pasado sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: JOB-INTERVIEW STORYTELLING — A CHALLENGE I OVERCAME (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "JOB-INTERVIEW STORYTELLING
   — A CHALLENGE I OVERCAME — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. One is the INTERVIEWER.
   One is the CANDIDATE. The interviewer asks the
   classic question: 'Tell me about a difficult
   situation you faced at work and how you handled it.'
   The candidate answers with a COMPLETE past story —
   6 to 8 sentences: set the scene (used to / was -ing),
   give the turning point (when + past simple), describe
   the struggle, and sequence the resolution (first,
   then, after that, finally). The interviewer asks 2
   follow-up questions in past simple. Then you switch
   roles — new story."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This interview IS the
   phrase in action — I used to struggle, I was failing
   when the chance came, I pushed through, and then I
   won. Weave every past form and the story of courage
   stands."

4. ESCRIBE en board el ESQUELETO que el candidate sigue
   (no un guion — un mapa):

     SCENE     -> "I used to..." / "I was ...-ing..."
     TURNING   -> "...when ... (past simple)"
     STRUGGLE  -> "I didn't... / I was ...-ing..."
     SEQUENCE  -> "First... Then... After that..."
     CLOSE     -> "...Finally... / and I learned..."

   Plus the 2 interviewer follow-ups (examples):
     "What did you do first?"
     "Why didn't it work the first time?"
     "How did you feel while it was happening?"
     "What did you learn in the end?"

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge roles.
   Candidate tells the full 6-8 sentence story.
   Interviewer asks 2 follow-ups in past simple. Then
   SWITCH roles — different story.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven
   a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch
   to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English.
   Don't freeze and don't drop into the present."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same format, pero
   ahora el candidate debe incluir AL MENOS un USED TO
   (old habit) Y un WAS -ING ... WHEN ... (interruption)
   in the SAME story. Interviewer adds 2 follow-ups.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la entrevista: si alguien
   narre saltando al presente o diga "used to went" /
   "when I was call her", DI suave: "Fortitude. Stay in
   the past. Hold the timeline — used to, was -ing,
   then it happened."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que cada historia tenga AL MENOS
    1 used to / was -ing (scene) + 1 when + past simple
    (turning point) + 2 sequencing connectors + 1 closer.
    Observer (third person of nearby pair) cuenta las
    capas presentes y los saltos al presente.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (1 mini-interview completa — pregunta +
    historia de 6 lineas + 2 follow-ups). Observer
    voluntario reporta en voz alta: "I counted: scene,
    turning point, struggle, and X sequencing
    connectors — all in the past." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What were you doing when it went wrong?" / "What did you do first?" / "How did it end?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de interviewer ni candidate.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con scene + turning point + struggle + sequence visibles, todo en pasado.

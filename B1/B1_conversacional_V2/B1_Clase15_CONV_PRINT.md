## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT PAST CONTINUOUS (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 2 columnas en board grandes (subject + form):

   WAS (+ -ing)          WERE (+ -ing)
   I was working         You were working
   He was working        We were working
   She was working       They were working
   It was working

   *** I / he / she / it  -> WAS + verb-ING ***
   *** you / we / they    -> WERE + verb-ING ***
   *** ALWAYS the -ing. ***

2. SEÑALA la regla. DI: "Two helpers. WAS for I, he,
   she, it. WERE for you, we, they. And the verb ALWAYS
   wears -ing. 'I WAS WORKING.' 'They WERE WAITING.'
   Never 'I was work.' Never 'they was working.'"

3. ESCRIBE una segunda seccion grande — el contraste:

   PAST SIMPLE vs PAST CONTINUOUS
      I worked at 8.        = done, finished at 8
      I was working at 8.   = in progress, still going at 8

      We waited.            = the wait is over
      We were waiting.      = the wait was happening

   *** Simple = the action is a closed dot. ***
   *** Continuous = the action is an open line. ***

4. SEÑALA el contraste. DI: "Past simple is a dot — done.
   Past continuous is a line — it was still running. 'I
   worked' closes. 'I was working' stays open in that
   moment. Today we live in the line."

5. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board. DI:
   "This is what the phrase says — was and were plus -ing
   hold what was STILL happening. While the action lived,
   we give it its fair, ongoing place."

6. ESCRIBE el bloque NEGATIVE en board:

   NEGATIVE — wasn't / weren't + -ing
      I wasn't driving the Model T last year.
      She wasn't studying at that time.
      They weren't driving to school.
      We weren't waiting for him.

   *** wasn't (I/he/she/it) · weren't (you/we/they) ***
   *** the -ing stays. ***

7. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I was play football"
   -> TACHA "play", ESCRIBE "I was PLAYING football."
   DI: "The verb wears -ing. Was PLAYING, not was play."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "They was working late"
   -> TACHA "was", ESCRIBE "They WERE working late."
   DI: "They, we, you take WERE. Not was." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

9. ESCRIBE el bloque QUESTION en board:

   YES/NO QUESTION — Was/Were + subject + -ing
      WAS he working last night?
      WERE you waiting for the supplier?
      WAS she studying for the exam?
      WERE they driving to the office?

   SHORT ANSWERS
      Yes, I was.   / No, I wasn't.
      Yes, we were. / No, we weren't.
      Yes, he was.  / No, he wasn't.

   *** Was/Were jumps to the FRONT. Verb keeps -ing. ***
   *** Short answer: never repeat the verb. ***

10. SEÑALA questions + short answers. DI: "To ask, was
    or were jumps to the front. 'WAS he working?' 'WERE
    you waiting?' The verb keeps its -ing. To answer
    short: 'Yes, I was.' 'No, they weren't.' You don't
    repeat the verb."

11. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee con was/were
    o se coma el -ing, DI suave: "Justice. The moment
    was in motion — was or were, plus the -ing. Give the
    action its ongoing place."

12. PIDELES en parejas (anchor con time from Cl 14):
    "5 sentences each in past continuous about last week
    at work — use a real time anchor: 'At 9 yesterday I
    was reviewing the report' / 'Last Tuesday morning we
    were waiting for the client.' Mix WAS and WERE." 6 min.

13. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca en
    2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores con
    NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

14. ESCRIBE una mini-seccion final — el book anchor:

    BOOK MODEL SENTENCES (A2 Module 12)
      "I was living in Bucaramanga in 2019."
      "Eliana was studying for her exams last week."
      "Paula was swimming in the pool in the morning."
      "Where was Joe working last year?"

15. SEÑALA las frases del libro. DI: "Straight from the
    book. Long, ongoing actions in the past — living,
    studying, swimming, working. Each one a line, not a
    dot. Each one was/were + -ing."

16. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "Where was Joe work last year?"
    -> TACHA "work", ESCRIBE "Where was Joe WORKING last
    year?" DI: "Even in a question — the -ing stays."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

17. PIDELES en parejas: "Each pair builds 4 Was/Were
    questions + 2 short answers + 2 negatives, all in
    past continuous, about a typical morning at the
    office last month." 6 minutos.

18. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo use was/were
    correctamente + mantenga el -ing sin titubear. DI:
    "Stand up — we go to the incident."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun se comen el -ing o confunden was/were, NO AVANCES a negatives + questions con fuerza. Reduce a 2 estructuras hoy (affirmative was/were + -ing + 1 question form), deja negatives complejos para callback en Cl 16 opener. Mejor LAND 2 bien que rush 4 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo affirmative + negative + Was/Were question + short answer manteniendo was/were + -ing sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: INCIDENT TIMELINE — WHAT WERE YOU DOING? (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "INCIDENT TIMELINE — WHAT
   WERE YOU DOING? — 3 ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "An incident happened at the office —
   pick one: the system went down, a big delivery
   arrived, a client walked in without an appointment.
   In pairs, you are two coworkers reconstructing the
   morning for a report. One asks 'What were you doing
   at 9? At 9:30? When it happened?' The other answers
   in PAST CONTINUOUS — 'At 9 I was reviewing the
   report. At 9:30 we were waiting for the supplier.'
   Every question: Was/Were + subject + -ing. Every
   answer: was/were + -ing. Then you switch roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This timeline IS the phrase
   in action — was and were plus -ing hold what was still
   happening. While the action lived, we give it its
   fair, ongoing place."

4. ESCRIBE en board los time anchors + 6 base questions
   (interviewer base):

     TIMELINE: 8:00 — 9:00 — 9:30 — 10:00 — incident

     1. "What WERE you DOING at 8 this morning?"
     2. "WAS the manager WORKING when you arrived?"
     3. "What WERE the others DOING at 9:30?"
     4. "WERE you WAITING for anyone?"
     5. "WAS anyone CALLING the supplier?"
     6. "What WERE you DOING when it happened?"

   Sample answers (was/were + -ing):
     "At 8 I was reviewing the report."
     "We were waiting for the delivery."
     "She wasn't answering the phone."

5. PIDELES round 1 (12 min): pareja escoge el incidente
   + roles. One asks the 6 questions in order along the
   timeline. The other answers in past continuous. Then
   SWITCH roles — same timeline, different answers.

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a
   parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch to
   Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English. Don't
   freeze."

8. ROTA parejas. Round 2 (12 min): same timeline pero
   ahora el que pregunta puede AGREGAR 2 follow-ups
   improvisados ("And what was the receptionist doing?"
   "Were you taking notes?"). El que responde mantiene
   was/were + -ing + agrega 1 negative ("I wasn't
   answering emails — I was on the call").

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante el timeline: si alguien
   diga "I was answer" o "they was waiting" o "we
   waiting", DI suave: "Justice. The moment was in
   motion — was or were, plus the -ing."

10. ROTA parejas otra vez. Round 3 (12 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada timeline aparezcan AL
    MENOS 5 Was/Were-questions + 4 was/were + -ing
    answers + 1 negative (wasn't/weren't) + 1 short
    answer ("Yes, I was" / "No, we weren't"). Observer
    (third person of nearby pair) counts -ing maintained
    + was/were agreement.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (1 mini-timeline — 4 questions + 4 answers + 1
    negative). Observer voluntario reporta en voz alta:
    "I counted X Was/Were-questions, Y was/were answers,
    Z negatives — all with -ing." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("What were you doing at 9?" / "Were you waiting for the delivery?" / "Was the manager calling?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de coworker.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con was/were + -ing + 1 negative + 1 short answer visibles.

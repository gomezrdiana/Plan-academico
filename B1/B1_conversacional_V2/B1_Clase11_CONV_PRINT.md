   indirect objects. Today — the verb itself

   marca: hoy cambia el verbo, no el pronombre.

   Three sounds: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/. We drill all four
   moves and all three sounds standing."

   you fix the ending."

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT PAST SIMPLE REGULAR (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 4 bloques en board grandes:

   SPELLING — 4 PATTERNS
      (A) base + ED         work -> worked
                            call -> called
                            play -> played
                            ask  -> asked
      (B) ends in E + D     live -> lived
                            love -> loved
                            arrive -> arrived
                            move -> moved
      (C) consonant + Y -> IED  study -> studied
                                try   -> tried
                                cry   -> cried
                                carry -> carried
      (D) CVC short -> DOUBLE + ED  stop -> stopped
                                    plan -> planned
                                    rob  -> robbed
                                    chat -> chatted

   PRONUNCIATION — 3 SOUNDS
      /t/   after voiceless (k, p, s, sh, ch, f)
            worked, asked, finished, looked, helped
      /d/   after voiced (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r, vowels)
            lived, played, called, opened, arrived
      /ɪd/  after t or d (extra syllable!)
            wanted, needed, started, ended, waited

   TIME MARKERS — when?
      yesterday          last week / last Monday
      2 days ago         in 2020
      when I was younger this morning

2. SEÑALA bloque SPELLING. DI: "Four small moves.
   ED is the default. If the verb ends in E, just
   add D. If it ends in CONSONANT + Y, change Y to
   I and add ED. If it is short — CONSONANT-VOWEL-
   CONSONANT — double the last consonant. Four
   moves, no exceptions today."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "Justice opens with the past — I worked, she
   studied, we lived, they called. Each verb has
   its right ending. The past speaks clean."

4. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I workt yesterday" -> TACHA
   "workt", ESCRIBE "I WORKED yesterday." DI:
   "Regular ED. Voiceless sound /t/, but the
   spelling is always ED — never T." COHORTE repite
   2 veces en coro.

5. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She studyed last week" ->
   TACHA "studyed", ESCRIBE "She STUDIED last week."
   DI: "Consonant + Y. Change the Y to I, then add
   ED. Study, studied. Try, tried. Cry, cried."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each in past
   simple regular. Use verbs from all 4 spelling
   patterns: one with -ED (work / call / play /
   ask), one with -D (live / love / move), one
   with -IED (study / try / carry), one with
   doubled consonant (stop / plan / chat). Add a
   time marker to each. About yesterday or last
   week." 5 minutos.

7. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error
   aparezca en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres).
   Anota errores con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

8. SEÑALA bloque PRONUNCIATION. DI: "Same ED on
   paper. THREE sounds in the mouth. /t/ after
   voiceless — worked, asked, finished. /d/ after
   voiced — lived, played, called. /ɪd/ after T or
   D — and ONLY there — adds a whole new syllable:
   wanted, needed, started."

9. MODELA en voz alta los 3 sonidos. COHORTE repite
   cada grupo 2 veces:
      Group 1 (/t/):  worked, asked, helped, looked, finished
      Group 2 (/d/):  lived, played, called, opened, arrived
      Group 3 (/ɪd/): wanted, needed, started, ended, waited

10. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee con la
    pronunciacion, DI suave: "Justice. Give the
    past its right ending. The form decides — ED,
    D, IED, or doubled. Each ending has its sound."

11. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "He stopt" -> TACHA "stopt",
    ESCRIBE "He STOPPED." DI: "Short verb, CVC —
    consonant + vowel + consonant. Double the last
    consonant, then add ED. Stop -> stopped. Plan
    -> planned. Rob -> robbed." COHORTE repite las
    3 veces.

12. [ERROR TIPICO #4] estudiante dice "waited" como
    /weɪt/ (sin syllable extra). DI: "Listen. WANT
    -ED, /ˈwɒntɪd/ — two syllables. WAIT-ED,
    /ˈweɪtɪd/ — two syllables. T or D before ED
    always adds a full /ɪd/ syllable. WAITED,
    NEEDED, STARTED, ENDED — never crush the
    syllable." COHORTE repite las 4 palabras 2
    veces, separando syllables con palmadas.

13. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "We livd" (escrito o dicho) ->
    TACHA "livd", ESCRIBE "We LIVED." DI: "Live
    ends in E. Just add D — not ED. Lived, loved,
    moved, arrived. The E is already there." COHORTE
    repite 2 veces.

14. SEÑALA bloque TIME MARKERS. DI: "The past needs
    a time anchor. YESTERDAY. LAST WEEK. TWO DAYS
    AGO. IN 2020. WHEN I WAS YOUNGER. Always one in
    every past sentence today."

15. PIDELES en parejas (segundo round): "4 sentences
    each — but this time say them OUT LOUD with
    correct pronunciation. Mix: 1 verb with /t/, 1
    with /d/, 2 with /ɪd/. Each with a time
    marker." 5 min.

16. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo construya
    spelling + pronunciation + time marker sin
    titubear. DI: "Stand up — we go to the
    simulation."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun confunden los 3 sonidos, NO AVANCES a la simulacion sin recortar. Reduce: solo /t/ y /d/ hoy, deja /ɪd/ para callback en Cl 12. Mejor LAND 2 sonidos bien que rush 3 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo past simple regular con spelling correcto + 3 sonidos diferenciados + time marker en cada oracion.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: YESTERDAY'S WORKDAY DEBRIEF (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "YESTERDAY'S WORKDAY
   DEBRIEF — 3 ROUNDS"

2. DI las reglas: "In pairs. Office scene at the
   end of the day — two colleagues catching up
   before going home. Each person tells the other
   5 things they did yesterday or earlier this
   week. EVERY sentence uses past simple regular
   + a time marker. Observer counts ED uses on
   paper AND corrects /t/-/d/-/ɪd/ pronunciation
   in real time. Then you rotate roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This debrief IS the
   phrase in action — I worked, she studied, we
   lived, they called. Each verb takes its right
   ending. The past speaks clean."

4. ESCRIBE en board 2 mini-scenarios:

   SCENARIO 1 — End of Monday, coffee station
     You and a colleague meet at the coffee
     machine at 5 PM. You both worked from home
     last week. Catch up: what tasks you finished,
     who you called, what reports you finished,
     where you traveled, who visited the office.
     5 sentences each. Time markers in every one.
     Observer counts ED + flags pronunciation.

   SCENARIO 2 — Friday wrap-up, hallway
     You and a colleague pass in the hallway at
     6 PM Friday. You both started a new project
     this week. Catch up: what you planned, who
     you contacted, what you delivered, what
     stopped working, who helped. 5 sentences
     each. Time markers in every one. Observer
     counts ED + flags pronunciation.

5. PIDELES round 1 (10 min): pareja escoge un
   scenario. Roles claros. Primero arranca: "I
   worked from home Monday and Tuesday. I called
   three clients yesterday morning. I finished the
   budget report last night..."

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3
   veces en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x ->
   vuelven a parejas.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck,
   switch to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to
   English. Don't freeze. The past is in your day —
   you lived it. Just give it the right ending."

8. ROTA roles dentro de la pareja. Round 2 (10
   min): cambien de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si
   alguien diga "I workt yesterday" o "She studyed"
   o "He stopt", DI suave: "Justice. Give the past
   its right ending. The form decides — ED, D,
   IED, or doubled."

10. ROTA roles otra vez. Round 3 (10 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada debrief aparezcan
    AL MENOS 1 verbo de cada patron spelling (ED, D,
    IED, doubled) Y AL MENOS 1 verbo de cada sonido
    (/t/, /d/, /ɪd/). Observer marca los 4 + los 3.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2
    veces en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en
    tu cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos frente
    al grupo (5 oraciones cada uno). Observer
    reporta en voz alta: "I counted X ED verbs, Y
    with /t/, Z with /d/, W with /ɪd/, and N
    different time markers." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Tell me what you finished yesterday." / "What did you start on Monday?" / "Who called you last week?") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de colega.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con past simple regular + 4 spelling patterns + 3 sonidos + time markers visibles.

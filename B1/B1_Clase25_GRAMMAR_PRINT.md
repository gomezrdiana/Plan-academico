## 6. BOOK M34 — STRUCTURE A "cost" + BOARD RACE — 12 min — STAND UP

Write on the board (before class):

```
M34 — DESCRIBING PRICES (A2 Book p.293)
       "How Much Does It Cost?"

From the book (Guide 1 — Describing Prices, p.294):
"1. There are two ways to ask and give the price of
 something:"

"a. With the verb 'cost' (irregular past: 'cost')."

QUESTION with "cost":
  How much | DOES | the car | COST?
  → "How much does the car cost?"
  → "¿Cuanto cuesta el carro?"
  How much | DO  | the cars | COST?

The question implies "how much money" but we do NOT
say "money" out loud.

ANSWER with "cost":
  It  | COSTS | $5    → "It costs $5."
  They| COST  | $5    → "They cost $5."

KEY: STRUCTURE A (cost):
       Q: "How much DOES/DO + subject + COST?"
       A: "It COSTS $X" / "They COST $X each"
       cost is a VERB; needs do/does in Q;
       -s in 3rd person singular present;
       past tense irregular: cost

(M35 does NOT exist in this book — M34 → M36 — same
way M30 → M32, M24 → M26, M18 → M20.)
```

Say it standing at the board: *"With 'cost': 'cost' is a verb, so the question needs 'do/does' before the subject. The answer uses the verb 'cost' itself: 'It costs 99 dollars'. We add -s for 'it costs' (3rd singular present), stays bare for 'they cost'."*

### BOARD RACE — describing prices — 7 min — 2 teams

Two columns on the board. Dictate a wrong sentence; first student of each row runs to the board and writes it correctly. Cohort verifies. Rotate.

```
Round (dictate WRONG; they write RIGHT):
1. "How much it costs?"
   → "How much DOES it cost?"
2. "How much costs the software?"
   → "How much DOES the software cost?"
3. "It cost 30 dollars."
   → "It costS 30 dollars." (-s in 3rd sing pres)
4. "How much do the new printer cost?"
   → "How much DOES the new printer cost?" (singular subj → does)
5. "How much does the chairs cost?"
   → "How much DO the chairs cost?" (plural subj → do)
6. "How much do the certification costs?"
   → "How much DO the certifications COST?"
```

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"'does' before subject; 'cost' as bare verb; 'It costs' with -s in the answer. Phrase first, then the rule."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Two zones in the room (tape labels on opposite walls):
- **COST zone (left):** STRUCTURE A — *"how much DOES + subject + COST?"* / *"It COSTS $X"*
- **BE zone (right):** STRUCTURE B — *"how much IS/ARE + subject?"* / *"It's $X"*

Hand each student **ONE slip card** with a price question in one structure.

### Round 1 — Walk it — 8 min

Each student:
1. Stands in COST zone, reads the COST-structure question + answer aloud.
2. Walks to BE zone, says the SAME question + answer in BE structure aloud.
3. Cohort confirms with thumbs.

Suggested slips:

```
"How much does the new software cost?" ↔ "How much is the new software?"
   → "It costs 30 dollars per user."   → "It's 30 dollars per user."
"How much do the spare parts cost?"     ↔ "How much are the spare parts?"
   → "They cost 12 dollars each."      → "They're 12 dollars each."
"How much does one hour of training cost?" ↔ "How much is one hour of training?"
   → "It costs 50 dollars."            → "It's 50 dollars."
"How much do the team lunches cost?"    ↔ "How much are the team lunches?"
   → "They cost 8 dollars each."       → "They're 8 dollars each."
"How much does the certification cost?" ↔ "How much is the certification?"
   → "It costs 200 dollars."           → "It's 200 dollars."
"Evaluations are handled by coordination, not by us."
   → (limit reminder — leave correct as is)
```

### Round 2 — Reverse — 4 min

Swap slips. Each student starts in BE, walks to COST saying out loud WHY 'cost' needs 'do/does': *"the verb 'cost' is an action — so the question needs 'do/does' before the subject, and the answer adds '-s' for 'it costs' or stays bare for 'they cost'."*

### Close — 2 min

2 volunteers walk their transformation in front of the group. Cohort counts correct Qs and As in both structures. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

   How much do the cookies cost? → They cost 50 cents each.

KEY: STRUCTURE B (be) — NO helper:
       Q: "How much IS + (singular)?" → "It's $X."

**Use Set A "DOES/DO/COST" cards + Set B "IS/ARE" cards + Set C (subjects).** Each student gets ONE card. You call a target sentence; relevant cards walk to the front. The "DOES/DO" card MUST be in the COST structure line — if missing, the sentence is not Structure A. Same for "IS/ARE" with Structure B.

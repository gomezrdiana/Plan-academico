### Pairs debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "How Much Do I Really Have?"
(UNCOUNTABLE only: time, money, energy, attention,
 information, coffee, work-as-effort)

Discuss with your partner:

1. Name THREE real UNCOUNTABLE things at your work
   and answer honestly HOW MUCH:
   "How much time do I really have this week?
    Not much — about three hours after 8 PM."
   "How much energy do I have after work?
    A little."
   "How much information do I have for the proposal?
    Some — not enough yet."

2. Name ONE uncountable you have TOO MUCH of and ONE
   you have NOT ENOUGH of:
   "I have too much email and not enough focus."
   "We have too much information and not enough action."

3. PRUDENCE day 4: name the AMOUNT honestly — not
   the amount that sounds enough. Why does an honest
   "how much" matter at work (planning, reporting,
   asking for help)?

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ "how much ___ ?" questions with UNCOUNTABLE nouns
- 6+ answers with NO number — only "a little / a lot /
  some / not much / none / enough / too much / just
  enough"
- 1 sentence about honesty in naming the amount
- NO countable plurals on the "how much" side —
  "how many emails" stays in last class's column.
```

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE RESOURCE AUDIT" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. You stand in front of your team/manager and answer HONESTLY how much UNCOUNTABLE resource your project really has — time, money, energy, attention, information. No numbers. You answer with 'a little / a lot / some / not much / none / enough / too much'."*

Board:

```
"THE RESOURCE AUDIT" — 3–4 min each

Your manager asks the team: "Before we plan the next
phase — how much of each resource do we REALLY have?"
You answer honestly. NO numbers. Use uncountable +
quantity word.

POINT 1 (TIME):
   "How much time do we have this week?"
   → "Not much — there is just enough time for one
       deliverable, not three."
POINT 2 (MONEY/BUDGET):
   "How much budget is left?"
   → "Some — but not enough for the new tool yet."
POINT 3 (ENERGY):
   "How much energy does the team have after the launch?"
   → "A little. We have too much fatigue and not
       enough recovery time."
POINT 4 (INFORMATION):
   "How much information do we have for the proposal?"
   → "A lot of raw data — but not much real insight yet."
POINT 5 (ATTENTION/EMAIL):
   "How much email noise is there?"
   → "Too much. We need just enough decisions, not
       more inbox."
POINT 6 (HONEST CLOSE):
   "Honestly — we have a lot of intention, but not
    enough time. That is the amount I need named."

USE: 5+ "how much ___ ?" with uncountable / 5+ answers
     WITHOUT number / professional ONLY / honest, not
     inflated
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me audit our resources. How much time do we have this week? Not much — just enough for one solid deliverable, not three. How much budget? Some — but not enough for the new tool yet. How much energy after the launch? A little — too much fatigue and not enough recovery time. How much information for the proposal? A lot of raw data — but not much real insight yet. How much email noise? Too much. One thing: if anyone asks about evaluations, midterm results or how the Final is graded, that goes only to coordination — not to me. Back to the audit: honestly, we have a lot of intention but not enough time, and that is the amount I want named."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A presents the resource audit (5–6 uncountable points); B is the manager/team and COUNTS how many "how much" questions A used, how many answers were given WITHOUT number, and if they were honest (inflated abundance? hidden scarcity?).

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their audit in front of the group. Cohort counts. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook (common: *"how much emails"*, *"a few of time"*, *"there are too much information"*).

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OBJECT PRONOUNS + INDIRECT OBJECTS (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE 3 columnas en board grandes:

   SUBJECT      OBJECT         POSSESSIVE (Cl 9 callback)
   I            ME             MY / mine
   you          YOU            your / yours
   he           HIM            his / his
   she          HER            her / hers
   it           IT             its / its
   we           US             our / ours
   they         THEM           their / theirs

   "He helped ME."  vs  "MY report is ready."
   "I called HIM."  vs  "HIS report is ready."
   "We met THEM."   vs  "THEIR report is ready."

2. SEÑALA columna OBJECT. DI: "Seven object pronouns.
   They go AFTER the verb. AFTER a preposition. NEVER
   as subject. 'He helped ME' — not 'He helped I'.
   'I called HIM' — not 'I called he'. 'They met US'
   — not 'they met we'."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase en el board.
   DI: "This is what the phrase says — call HIM, help
   US, give THEM the report. The pronoun goes AFTER
   the verb. Each word knows its place."

4. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "He helped I" -> TACHA "I",
   ESCRIBE "He helped ME." COHORTE repite 2 veces en
   coro.

5. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "Her called me" -> TACHA "Her",
   ESCRIBE "SHE called me." DI: "Subject position
   uses SHE, not HER. HER goes after the verb."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. PIDELES en parejas: "5 sentences each with object
   pronouns. Verbs: call, help, meet, see, invite,
   thank. About a real colleague or relative. Use 5
   different object pronouns." 4 minutos.

7. CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
   en 2 parejas distintas (sin nombres). Anota errores
   con NOMBRE en TU cuaderno.

8. ESCRIBE en board una segunda seccion grande:
   INDIRECT OBJECTS — DOS ORDENES

   ORDER A (no TO):  verb + IO (person) + DO (thing)
     "I gave HIM the report."
     "She sent US the invoice."
     "They told ME the news."

   ORDER B (with TO): verb + DO (thing) + TO + IO
     "I gave the report TO HIM."
     "She sent the invoice TO US."

   EXCEPTION: TELL and ASK never take TO
     "She TOLD ME the news."   (NOT "told to me")
     "He ASKED ME a question."  (NOT "asked to me")

9. SEÑALA Order A. DI: "When the indirect object is
   short (a pronoun), put it FIRST, right after the
   verb. No preposition needed. Verb + person + thing.
   'I gave HIM the report.' Clean."

10. [VIRTUD] activation: si alguien titubee con el
    orden, DI suave: "Measure. Pronoun first or thing
    first? Pick the right position. Close clean."

11. SEÑALA Order B. DI: "When you put the thing FIRST,
    you NEED 'TO' before the person. 'I gave the report
    TO HIM.' Both orders correct — but never mix:
    NEVER 'I gave to him the report.'"

12. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I gave to him the report"
    -> TACHA "to" y reordena: ESCRIBE
    "I gave HIM the report." y al lado
    "I gave the report TO HIM." DI: "Two clean orders.
    Pick one. Don't mix." COHORTE repite las 2 versiones
    2 veces.

13. SEÑALA EXCEPTION. DI: "TELL and ASK take NO 'TO'.
    Always Order A. 'She told ME the news' — never
    'told to me'. 'He asked ME a question' — never
    'asked to me'."

14. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "She told to me the news"
    -> TACHA "to", ESCRIBE "She told ME the news."
    DI: "TELL and ASK — no TO. Period." COHORTE
    repite 2 veces.

15. [ERROR TIPICO #5] CALLBACK CL 9 vs HOY:
    ESCRIBE en board lado a lado:
       HIS report  (possessive — before noun)
       I helped HIM (object pronoun — after verb)
    DI: "Same person — different position. HIS sits
    before the noun. HIM sits after the verb. Yesterday's
    rule and today's rule live together."
    COHORTE repite las 2 lineas 2 veces.

16. PIDELES en parejas: "4 sentences with verbs that
    take two objects — give, send, show, write, lend.
    For each one, say BOTH orders: 'I sent HIM the
    invoice' / 'I sent the invoice TO HIM'. Then add
    2 sentences with TELL or ASK (Order A only)." 6 min.

17. CIRCULA, CIERRA cuando 80% del grupo construya las
    dos ordenes sin titubear y respete la excepcion
    TELL/ASK. DI: "Stand up — we go to the simulation."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 30 min y aun confunden Order A con Order B, NO AVANCES a la excepcion. Reduce a 2 estructuras hoy (object pronoun simple + IO Order A), deja Order B + TELL/ASK exception para callback en Cl 11. Mejor LAND 2 bien que rush 4 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas construyendo los 2 ordenes + respetando la excepcion TELL/ASK sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: DELEGATION CALL (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "DELEGATION CALL — 3
   ROTACIONES"

2. DI las reglas: "In trios. One is the MANAGER on
   the phone. One is the EMPLOYEE taking instructions.
   One is the OBSERVER counting object-pronoun uses
   on paper. Manager delegates 4 tasks per call,
   always using object pronouns: 'Call HIM. Send HER
   the report. Tell THEM I'll be late. Ask US for
   the budget.' Employee confirms each instruction
   using object pronouns back: 'Got it — I'll call
   him at 2.' Then you rotate roles."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This call IS the phrase
   in action — call him, help us, give them the
   report. The pronoun goes after the verb. The
   action goes to its owner."

4. ESCRIBE en board 2 mini-scenarios:

   SCENARIO 1 — Monday morning delegation
     Manager calls from the airport.
     Delegates 4 tasks: send a report, call a
     client, tell the team about a delay, ask a
     colleague for a number.
     Employee confirms each with object pronouns.
     Observer counts object-pronoun uses.

   SCENARIO 2 — Crisis at 4 PM
     Manager calls from a meeting.
     Delegates 4 urgent tasks: send the proposal
     to the client, tell the team to wait, ask
     the boss for an extension, help the new
     intern with the slide deck.
     Employee confirms each with object pronouns.
     Observer counts object-pronoun uses + flags
     any "to" misplaced in Order A.

5. PIDELES round 1 (10 min): trio escoge un scenario.
   Roles claros. Manager arranca: "Hi — I need you to
   call HIM right now. Then send HER the budget."

6. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en el mismo trio -> board -> coro 2x -> vuelven a
   trios.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If stuck, switch
   to Spanish, think 5 seconds, come back to English.
   Don't freeze."

8. ROTA roles dentro del trio. Round 2 (10 min):
   cambien de scenario tambien.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante la simulacion: si
   alguien dice "I gave to him the report" o "her
   called", DI suave: "Measure. Where does the
   pronoun belong? Pick the right position. Close
   clean."

10. ROTA roles otra vez. Round 3 (10 min): mismo
    formato, push para que en cada call aparezcan
    AL MENOS 1 indirect object Order A + 1 Order B
    + 1 verb TELL or ASK (sin TO).

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces
    en trios distintos. Anota con NOMBRE en tu
    cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UN trio modelo: 90 segundos frente al
    grupo (1 call completa — manager + employee +
    observer). Observer reporta en voz alta: "I
    counted X object pronouns, Y Order A, Z Order B,
    1 TELL/ASK." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si un trio se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Call him about the meeting." / "Send her the invoice." / "Tell them I'll be 10 minutes late.") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio, NO tomes el rol de manager ni employee.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rotaciones + UNA demo al frente con object pronouns + ambos ordenes + 1 TELL/ASK visible.

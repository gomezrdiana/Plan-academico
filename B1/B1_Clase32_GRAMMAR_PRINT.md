## 6. BOOK M43 + BOARD RACE — 12 min — STAND UP

Write on the board (before class):

```
M43 — HOW DO YOU DO? / ADVERBS OF MANNER
       (A2 Book — module p.340+)

From the book (p.342) — verbatim:

"1. Usamos adverbios de manera para describir cómo
 se hace una acción. Para responder a una pregunta
 como: How do you drive?"

"2. La mayoría de los adverbios se forman con la
 terminación '-ly' (que corresponde a la terminación
 '-mente' en español) después del adjetivo.
 Ej. Sad → Sadly ('Triste' → 'Tristemente')
 Ej. Happy → Happily ('Feliz' → 'Felizmente')
 (Cambiamos la terminación '-ly' a '-ily'.)"

"3. Sin embargo, hay unos irregulares.
 Ej. Fast → Fast. No existe la palabra 'fastly'.
 Ej. Good → Well ('Bueno' → 'Bien')."

"4. En la gran mayoría de los casos, se coloca el
 adverbio de manera después del verbo principal o
 después del objeto directo, si hay.
 I play well. / He plays soccer well."

VOCABULARY (book p.341, verbatim):
   Well (bien) · Alone (solo) · Together (juntos) ·
   Early (temprano) · Late (tarde) · Fast (rápido —
   velocidad) · Slowly (lentamente) · Right
   (correctamente) · Wrong (incorrectamente) · Hard
   (duro) · Suddenly · Immediately · Completely ·
   Easily · Carefully

RULE: REGULAR adj + -ly = adverb. IRREGULAR = fast /
      good→well / hard / late / early / together /
      alone / right / wrong. POSITION = after verb
      OR after direct object (NOT between subject
      and verb).
```

Say at the board: *"Most adverbs are adjective + LY: careful → carefully, quick → quickly, clear → clearly. Adjectives ending in -y change to -ily: happy → happily, easy → easily. BUT rule 3: 'Hay unos irregulares — Fast → Fast (no existe fastly), Good → Well'. So 'she drives fast' (NOT 'fastly'), 'he plays well' (NOT 'goodly'), 'she works hard' (NOT 'hardly' — hardly means almost-not). Other irregulars: late, early, together, alone, right, wrong. Rule 4: 'se coloca el adverbio después del verbo principal o después del objeto directo, si hay. I play well / He plays soccer well.' So 'I play WELL' (after verb, no object) and 'He plays soccer WELL' (after object). Never 'she carefully organizes the data' (between subject and verb is WRONG). Next class Cl 33 is M44 'I Want You to Go' / The Active Causative — verbatim line 25055."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Four zones in the room (tape labels on walls):
- **WRONG FORM** (adjective where adverb goes: "He works good")
- **RIGHT FORM** ("He works well")
- **WRONG POSITION** (adverb between S and V: "She carefully organizes")
- **RIGHT POSITION** (after verb or after object: "She organizes the data carefully")

### Round 1 — Walk FORM — 4 min

Each student:
1. Reads FORM-error slip aloud (in WRONG FORM zone): "He works GOOD." / "She drives FASTLY." / "He plays GOODLY." / "She works HARDLY (= a lot)."
2. Walks to RIGHT FORM zone saying corrected: "He works WELL." / "She drives FAST." / "He plays WELL." / "She works HARD."
3. Cohort confirms thumbs + names the irregular: "good→well" / "fast=fast" / "hard=hard, hardly means almost-not".

Suggested FORM slips:

```
WRONG: "He works good."           → RIGHT: "He works WELL."           (good → well)
WRONG: "She drives fastly."       → RIGHT: "She drives FAST."         (fast = fast; no 'fastly')
WRONG: "He works hardly (effort)."→ RIGHT: "He works HARD."           (hardly = almost-not)
WRONG: "She studies intelligent." → RIGHT: "She studies INTELLIGENTLY." (regular adj + -ly)
WRONG: "He plays soccer good."    → RIGHT: "He plays soccer WELL."    (after object)
WRONG: "She writes beautiful."    → RIGHT: "She writes BEAUTIFULLY."  (regular adj + -ly)
```

### Round 2 — Walk POSITION — 6 min

Each student:
1. Reads POSITION-error slip aloud (in WRONG POSITION zone): "She carefully organizes the data." / "He quickly responds to clients." / "They clearly speak in standups." / "She slowly speaks the English."
2. Walks to RIGHT POSITION zone saying corrected: "She organizes the data CAREFULLY." / "He responds to clients QUICKLY." / "They speak CLEARLY in standups." / "She speaks English SLOWLY."
3. Cohort confirms + names the rule aloud ("after the OBJECT" or "after the VERB" — book p.342 rule 4).

Suggested POSITION slips:

```
WRONG: "She carefully organizes the data."     → RIGHT: "She organizes the data CAREFULLY." (after OBJECT)
WRONG: "He quickly responds."                  → RIGHT: "He responds QUICKLY." (after VERB; no object)
WRONG: "They clearly speak in standups."       → RIGHT: "They speak CLEARLY in standups." (after VERB)
WRONG: "She slowly speaks the English."        → RIGHT: "She speaks English SLOWLY." (after OBJECT; drop "the")
WRONG: "He hard works in the factory."         → RIGHT: "He works HARD in the factory." [Practice Guide p.345]
WRONG: "She early arrives every Monday."       → RIGHT: "She arrives EARLY every Monday."
WRONG: "She carefully spends."                 → RIGHT: "She spends CAREFULLY." [Practice Guide p.346]
```

### Close — 6 min

Swap slips and reverse the directions (RIGHT → WRONG explaining why). 2 volunteers walk their transformation in front of the group. Cohort counts correct + names which rule (2 / 3 / 4) applies. Note errors with NAMES.

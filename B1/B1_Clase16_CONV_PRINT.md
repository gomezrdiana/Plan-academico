 pick it up, get it done, and don't give up."

### Procedimiento

1. (30 seg) *"STAND UP. Today's phrase."*
2. (1 min) Lee 2x lento. Cohorte repite coro 3x.
3. (1 min) Explica: *"Look at the two-word verbs: 'put it OFF' = delay it, 'pick it UP' = take it on, 'get it DONE', 'give UP' = quit. A verb plus a small word becomes a new verb — that's a phrasal verb. That is also what FORTALEZA looks like on its first day with us: when work piles up, you don't put it off and you don't give up — you pick it up and get it done."*
4. (1 min) *"Sit. Notice when I use this phrase or parts of it during class."*

## 7. SIMULACIÓN — "THE HANDOVER BRIEFING" (00:42–01:02, 20 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Stand up. You are leaving for a week and someone covers your job. You brief them: what to turn off, what to pick up, what NOT to put off, who to look up, when to call you back. You use phrasal verbs the whole time."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Estructura sugerida (Tablero):**

```
"THE HANDOVER BRIEFING" — 3-4 min each

You hand your job to a colleague for one week. Give 6-7 real
instructions from your job. Use PHRASAL VERBS:

INSTRUCTION 1: "Turn off the machines before you leave."
INSTRUCTION 2: "Pick up the report from my desk at 9."
INSTRUCTION 3: "Don't put off the Friday calls — handle them."
INSTRUCTION 4: "Look up the client in the system first."
INSTRUCTION 5: "If a problem comes up, call me back."
INSTRUCTION 6: "Don't give up if the system goes down — restart it."

USE: 6+ phrasal verbs (turn off / pick up / put off / look up
     / call back / come up / give up / deal with / figure out)
     / professional context ONLY
```

### Modela (3 min)

> *"Let me hand my work to you. Before you leave each day, turn off the projector and pick up the printed plans from the desk. Don't put off the coordination check — do it the same day. If a teacher asks something about evaluations, you do not handle that — anything about midterms or finals goes only to coordination, never to us. If a real problem comes up, call me back; but don't give up on the small things just because they pile up. That part is fortitude — you carry it through, you don't drop it."*

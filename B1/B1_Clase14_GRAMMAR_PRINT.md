## 6. LIBRO M18 — PASSIVE VOICE + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Open Module 18, page 173. The passive voice. Then we race it on the board."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M18 (pág 173-174) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M18 — THE PASSIVE VOICE FOR REGULAR PARTICIPLES (A2 Book p.173-174)

From the book:
"Hasta ahora hemos usado la voz activa, donde el sujeto es el
 actor. A veces queremos enfocar más en el objeto de la frase;
 el que recibe la acción. Puede ser que el actor no es importante
 o incluso desconocido."

ACTIVE  (Subject=Agent + Verb + Object=Patient):
   "Juan prepares the lunch."

PASSIVE (Subject=Patient + TO BE + PAST PARTICIPLE + by + Agent):
   "The lunch IS PREPARED by Juan."
   "The lunch IS PREPARED."   (el agente se puede eliminar)

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.174):
   Sebastian plays the game.   → The game IS PLAYED by Sebastian.
   Mariana studies English.    → English IS STUDIED by Mariana.
   The boy loves the dog.      → The dog IS LOVED by the boy.
   We help the teacher.        → The teacher IS HELPED by us.
   The girls clean the house.  → The house IS CLEANED by the girls.
   Soccer IS PLAYED in Colombia.
   You ARE LOVED!
```

> Di: *"The verb becomes the past participle — the same V3 you learned Friday in M16."*

### BOARD RACE — active→passive (7 min) — DE PIE, 2 equipos

Dos columnas en el tablero. Dictas una frase ACTIVA; el primer estudiante de cada fila corre al tablero y escribe la PASIVA. Cohorte verifica. Rota.

```
Round (dicta en voz activa, ellos escriben pasiva):
1. "The manager signs the contract."  → The contract is signed by the manager.
2. "We send the reports."             → The reports are sent by us.
3. "The team finishes the project."   → The project is finished by the team.
4. "She reviews the budget."          → The budget is reviewed by her.
5. "They clean the office."           → The office is cleaned by them.
6. "Everyone shares the work."        → The work is shared by everyone.
```

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Look at #6 — 'the work IS SHARED by everyone.' That's our phrase: the work is done by everyone."*

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — M18 (00:34–00:50, 16 min) — DE PIE

> *"You become the sentence. Active on one side of the room, passive on the other. You walk the transformation."*

### Round 1 — Walk it (8 min)

Cada estudiante:
1. Se para en zona ACTIVE y lee su frase activa en voz alta.
2. Camina a zona PASSIVE diciendo la transformación pasiva en voz alta.
3. El cohorte (de pie) confirma con pulgar arriba/abajo.

**Tiras sugeridas (contexto profesional):**

```
"The accountant checks the invoices."  → The invoices are checked.

"The client signs the agreement."       → The agreement is signed.
"The team delivers the product."        → The product is delivered.
"The director makes the decision."      → The decision is made.
"We process the orders every morning."  → The orders are processed every morning.
"Coordination handles the evaluations." → The evaluations are handled by coordination.
```

### Round 2 — Reverse (4 min)

## 8. LIBRO M20 — GERUNDS AS SUBJECTS + SORTING FÍSICO (00:50–01:02, 12 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Module 20, page 181 — 'Playing is Fun!'. A verb can be the subject. Then we sort it with our hands."*

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie

**Cita textual del A2 Book M20 (pág 181-183) — esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M20 — GERUNDS AS SUBJECTS  (A2 Book p.181-183) — "Playing is Fun!"

From the book (Guía 1):
"El gerundio tiene la misma forma que el continuo: verbo + ing.
 Pero son muy diferentes. El orden Sujeto-Verbo-Objeto es estricto
 en inglés. Cuando usamos un verbo en la posición del sujeto,
 usamos el gerundio (verbo + ing)."

   I work.            → Yo trabajo.
   WORKING is important.   → Trabajar es importante.

BOOK EXAMPLES (p.183):
   Soccer is fun.            Playing is fun.        Playing soccer is fun.
   Working is boring.        Working in the morning is boring.
   Eating is important.      Eating breakfast is important.
   Living in London is expensive.
   Studying is difficult.    Studying Spanish is difficult.
   Speaking is easy.         Speaking English is easy.
   Watching a movie is fun.  Taking a taxi is easy.

KEY: gerund-as-subject is SINGULAR → "Speaking IS easy" (not "are").
```

> Di: *"Same -ing form as the continuous, but a completely different job — here it is the SUBJECT, the thing the sentence is about."*

### SORTING FÍSICO (7 min) — DE PIE, en grupo

Reparte tarjetas: unas con un verbo en infinitivo/base ("work", "speak", "study", "lead", "negotiate", "travel"), otras con "is + adjetivo del libro" ("is fun", "is boring", "is important", "is expensive", "is difficult", "is easy"). El cohorte, de pie, se empareja físicamente y forma frases válidas en voz alta:

```
"work" + "is important"  →  "WORKING is important."
"lead"  + "is difficult" →  "LEADING is difficult."
"speak" + "is easy"      →  "SPEAKING is easy."
"negotiate" + "is hard"  →  "NEGOTIATING is hard."
```

Cada pareja dice su frase al grupo. El cohorte confirma que el verbo se volvió gerundio (+ing).

> **Inserción #3 Frase del Día:** *"'SPEAKING is learned by speaking' — there's the gerund as subject AND the passive in one breath."*

## 6. LIBRO M14 — WHILE / DURING / AGAIN (00:24–00:39, 15 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"A2 Book Module 14. Three temporal connectors. Each works DIFFERENTLY. Today we lock the difference."*

### Concepto (8 min)

**Lee EN INGLÉS desde el libro:**

> *"Module 14 — Three connectors that locate actions in time relative to each other."*

**Tablero — escribe 3 reglas:**

```
WHILE = at the same time as ANOTHER ACTION (used with Past Continuous)
        Position: at start of clause + comma
        Example: "WHILE I was working, the phone rang."
                  ^ ongoing action     ^ interrupting action

DURING = at the same time as A NOUN (event/period)
         Position: before a NOUN (not a verb)
         Example: "DURING the meeting, my phone rang."
                   ^ event/period

AGAIN = repeating an action
        Position: at the END of the sentence
        Example: "I tried AGAIN."
                 "She called me AGAIN this morning."

KEY DIFFERENCE:
- WHILE + clause with verb (was V-ing)
- DURING + noun (the movie, the night, the conference)
- AGAIN = repetition (end of sentence)
```

**Ejemplos modelados:**
- *"While she was studying, the doorbell rang."* (WHILE + ongoing past)
- *"During the conference, three guests complained."* (DURING + event)
- *"He apologized again, but it didn't help."* (AGAIN + repetition)

### Speed Drill (7 min)

**Parejas:** cada pareja construye 6 oraciones — 2 con WHILE, 2 con DURING, 2 con AGAIN.

**Temas obligatorios:** hotel/work/conference (siguiendo contexto de ayer).

Ejemplos esperados:
- *"WHILE the chef was plating, the manager called AGAIN."*
- *"DURING the conference, several guests requested upgrades."*
- *"The technician tried AGAIN to fix the AC."*

## 7. LIBRO M15 — PRESENT PERFECT REGULAR (FORMALIZACIÓN) (00:39–00:54, 15 min) — SENTADOS

**OBJETIVO PARA EL ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Yesterday Danna introduced Present Perfect orally. Today I lock the grammar. Auxiliary HAVE/HAS + past participle. Three clear uses."*

### Concepto (10 min)

**Lee EN INGLÉS desde el libro:**

> *"Module 15 — Present Perfect with Regular Verbs."*

**Tablero — escribe la formación + 3 usos:**

```
FORM: SUBJECT + HAVE/HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE

REGULAR past participle = base verb + -ED (same as Past Simple form)
- work    → worked    → "I have worked here for 5 years."
- study   → studied   → "She has studied English since 2018."
- travel  → traveled  → "We have traveled to many countries."

NEGATIVE:
- "I HAVE NOT (haven't) worked since Monday."
- "She HAS NOT (hasn't) studied today."

QUESTION:
- "HAVE you worked here long?"
- "HAS she studied for the exam?"

3 USES (review from yesterday's Conv):
1. LIFE EXPERIENCE (unspecified time):
   "I have visited Bogotá." (no specific time = Present Perfect)
   vs "I visited Bogotá in 2019." (specific time = Past Simple)

2. CONTINUING ACTION (started past, still relevant):
   "She has lived here since 2018." (started 2018, still now)
   "We have worked together for 5 years." (still working together)

3. RECENT ACTION WITH PRESENT RELEVANCE:
   "He has just finished." (recent + relevant now)
   "I have already eaten." (relevant to now: I'm not hungry)
```

### Speed Drill (5 min)

**Parejas:** cada pareja escribe 4 oraciones con Present Perfect Regular:
- 1 con LIFE EXPERIENCE
- 1 con CONTINUING ACTION (since/for)
- 1 con RECENT ACTION (just/already)
- 1 NEGATIVE o QUESTION

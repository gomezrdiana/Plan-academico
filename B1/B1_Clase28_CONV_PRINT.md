## 6. HOT TOPIC — "TWO RIGHT WORDS BEAT TEN LOUD ONES" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural (order of multiple adjectives). Today is pure speaking. Topic: describe things, people, or places at your work with 2–3 adjectives in a natural order. Juan Diego names the technical sequence this afternoon. Today you produce it."*

Sample patterns to model briefly:
- size + color: *"a big black car"* (NOT *"a black big car"*)
- opinion + size + color: *"a beautiful small white office"*
- length + shape + color: *"long straight black hair"*
- quantity + opinion: *"2 reliable suppliers"* (NOT *"reliable 2 suppliers"*)

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "TWO RIGHT WORDS BEAT TEN LOUD ONES"

Discuss with your partner:

1. Pick THREE real things from your work and
   describe each with 2–3 adjectives in a NATURAL
   order (size before color, opinion before size,
   color before nationality):
   "We have a big black printer on the second floor."
   "The new supervisor is a tall serious British
    woman."
   "I work in a small dark quiet office at the back."

2. Take ONE bad example with too many random
   adjectives, and FIX it down to 2–3 ordered:
   BAD:  "She is a German tall amazing young
          short-hair serious very nice supervisor."
   GOOD: "She is a tall serious young German
          supervisor."

3. "Two right words beat ten loud ones." Why does
   the right ORDER matter at work (reports,
   descriptions, handover notes)?

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ descriptions with 2–3 adjectives in CORRECT
  order
- 1 FIX of a too-long badly-ordered description
- 1 sentence about why ordering matters
  professionally (TEMPERANCE: dosify, sequence,
  no noise)
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

### Plenary — 3 min

2 students share a real professional description (3 things, 2–3 adjectives each in order) + why the order matters.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Listen to your phrases: 'a big black printer', 'a tall serious British woman', 'a small dark quiet office'. Two-three adjectives, in a natural order. That sequence has a name. Juan Diego gives it the name this afternoon."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE LOST & FOUND DESK" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. You stand at the Lost & Found desk of a hotel/office reception and describe a lost item OR a missing person to your colleague so they can find them. Wrong order of adjectives = the wrong item is brought back."*

Board:

```
"THE LOST & FOUND DESK" — 3–4 min each

You describe lost items / missing people at the
reception desk so a colleague can find them. Use
2–3 adjectives in the natural order (quantity →
opinion → size → age → shape → color → origin →
material → noun):

ITEM 1: "A small old brown leather wallet was left
         in meeting room 3."
ITEM 2: "The lost suitcase is a large black hard-
         shell suitcase."
ITEM 3: "We are looking for a small grey company
         laptop from the fifth floor."
ITEM 4: "Lost: 2 silver office keys on a blue
         keychain."
PERSON: "Missing colleague: a tall young Colombian
         engineer with short black hair, wearing a
         dark blue suit."
CLOSE:  "Please bring me the item that matches that
         description, not any other one."

USE: 5+ descriptions in CORRECT order / professional
     context ONLY / temperate count (2–3 adjectives,
     not 10) / each item recognizable from your words
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me brief you for the Lost & Found desk. Today we have: a small old brown leather wallet left in meeting room 3 — please find that EXACT wallet, not a new black one. The missing suitcase is a large black hard-shell suitcase that belongs to the visiting client. We're also looking for a small grey company laptop, fifth floor — not the big black one, the small grey one. And a missing colleague — a tall young Colombian engineer with short black hair, wearing a dark blue suit; he looks somewhat tired but very calm. Back to Lost & Found: please bring me the item that matches the description, not any other one."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A is the one describing (5–6 items/persons in correct order); B is the colleague who has to decide which object to bring + counts how many adjectives in correct order A used and whether any were misordered (*"a black big car"? "a German red wallet"?*).

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their Lost & Found in front of the group. Cohort counts descriptions with correct order. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

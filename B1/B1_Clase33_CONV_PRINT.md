## 6. HOT TOPIC — "WHO DO YOU NEED SOMETHING FROM AT WORK?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No new Heiiu text today — Grammar topic is structural (volition with subject change), not narrative. So today is pure speaking. WHO you depend on at work, WHAT you ask them to do. Juan Diego names the table this morning — book p.348 rule 1, verbatim: 'Para expresar lo que queremos que otra persona haga, NO usamos la forma I want that you go to the store.' Right shape: subject + want/need/would like/expect/ask/tell + OBJECT (the OTHER person) + TO + verb. Examples from book p.349-350 verbatim: 'I want you to get a job' / 'You need me to buy a car' / 'Anita would like you to give her a flower' / 'Brayan wants us to play soccer' / 'Connie needs you to talk to her' / 'Eddy wants Norman to see the new house' / 'Your friends want you to play with them'. Book p.348 rule 4 verbatim: 'Estas frases sólo expresan deseos. No constituyen solicitudes o comandos.' This is volition — wish — not command."*

Sample patterns (professional):
- *"I want the design team TO send the mockups by Wednesday."* (S + want + OBJ + TO + verb)
- *"I need the dev team TO deploy before the demo."*
- *"My manager would like me TO lead the Friday review."*
- *"The client expects us TO send the proposal Monday."*
- *"My boss asked me TO take the new hire under my wing."*
- *"Our team lead told us TO finish the audit by Thursday."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE PROJECT DELEGATION MEETING" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. A Project Delegation Meeting (Guest Observer pattern — 1 delegator rotating, group observer, professor coach): each of you leads a 3-min delegation as the project lead. You delegate to 3 team members using the M44 shape (OBJ + TO + verb). Justice day 3: name fairly, no inflation, no abuse, no erasure."*

Board:

```
"THE PROJECT DELEGATION MEETING"
3-4 min per round

PATTERN VALIDATED (Guest Observer):
  - 1 DELEGATOR (rotates each round — plays
    project lead at a Monday kickoff)
  - GROUP OBSERVER (cohort listens + counts M44
    sentences + flags wrong forms "I want that
    you...")
  - PROFESSOR (coach, never plays delegator)

DELEGATOR SCRIPT (4 M44 sentences per round):
  Step 1 — Open: "Team, this is the Monday
           kickoff for [project X]. Three things
           by Friday."
  Step 2 — Delegate #1 (WANT + OBJ + TO):
           "I want [Maria] to own the design
            mockups — three versions by Wednesday."
  Step 3 — Delegate #2 (NEED + OBJ + TO):
           "I need [the dev team] to deploy to
            staging by Thursday — no production
            until I review."
  Step 4 — Delegate #3 (WOULD LIKE + OBJ + TO):
           "I would like [Diego] to coordinate
            with the client — daily Slack update,
            5 PM."
  Step 5 — Justice close (no abuse): "If any
           timeline is wrong, tell me now — I'd
           rather move the date than burn the team."

OBSERVER GROUP:
  - Count M44 sentences with correct shape (4+
    per round = strong)
  - Flag wrong form: "I want THAT you send" (no
    "that" — use OBJ + TO + verb)
  - Flag missing TO: "I want you send" → "I want
    you TO send"
  - Flag abuse: "I want you to do my Friday
    review" (offloading is not delegation)
  - Flag inflation: "I need EVERYTHING from
    Maria this week" (name the one thing)
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me model: 'Team, this is the Monday kickoff for the platform launch. Three things by Friday. I want Maria TO own the design mockups — three versions by Wednesday. ### Round 1 — Guest Observer — 10 min

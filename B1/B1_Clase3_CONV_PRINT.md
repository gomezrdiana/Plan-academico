## 2. ICEBREAKER — "WOULD YOU RATHER" CHALLENGES (00:07–00:20, 13 min)

**Setup (1 min):** Reorganiza el salón. Una línea imaginaria divide el salón en dos lados. Estudiantes de pie. Tú lees el dilema. Cada estudiante elige un lado físicamente. Justifica en 30 seg.

**Reglas:**
- ENGLISH ONLY.
- Justify in ONE sentence. Use "I should..." or "I shouldn't..." in your justification.

**6 dilemas (escoge 5-6):**
```
1. "Would you rather work 4 days a week with a 20% pay cut, OR 6 days a week
   with 20% more pay?"
2. "Would you rather know exactly when you'll die, OR exactly how you'll die?"
3. "Would you rather be famous for something amazing, OR rich and unknown?"
4. "Would you rather lose all your money but keep your friends, OR lose all
   your friends but keep your money?"
5. "Would you rather always be 30 minutes late, OR always be 30 minutes early?"
6. "Would you rather have to follow ALL the advice you've ever received,
   OR never receive advice again?"
```

**Para cada dilema (~2 min):**
- Read aloud.
- Students physically move to the side they choose.
- Pick 2 students (one from each side): "Why?" — they answer in 1 sentence.
- If their answer doesn't include "should/shouldn't," prompt: "Use SHOULD."

> Anota errores en Error Paper. NO interrumpas a cada error — solo los más graves.

## 3. HOT TOPIC — "BEST ADVICE EVER" (00:20–00:35, 15 min)

**Topic on the board:**
```
HOT TOPIC:
"Some people say YOUNG people should listen to OLD people.
Other people say WISDOM is built from your OWN mistakes —
not from someone else's advice."

Where do YOU stand? Defend your position with examples.
```

**Setup (2 min):** Read the topic aloud. Each student picks side A or side B with their seat (left/right of room). 

**Round 1 — pairs same side (5 min):** "Pairs with someone on YOUR side. Build the strongest argument together. 5 minutes."

**Round 2 — debate cross-side (8 min):** Pairs face the OTHER side. They argue. Rules:
- 1 minute attack from Side A.
- 1 minute counter from Side B.
- 30 seconds rebuttal each.
- Repeat with new pair.

3 mini-debates. The class judges who was MORE persuasive (clarity > volume).

> Camina con Error Paper. Anota errores graves de SHOULD/SHOULDN'T (estructura del módulo de hoy).

## 4. TBLT — "THE TRAVEL ADVISORY BOARD" (00:35–01:00, 25 min)

**The task:** Pairs are members of a Travel Advisory Board. They must produce 5 recommendations for a foreign tourist arriving in Bucaramanga next week. ALL recommendations must use "should" or "shouldn't" + a number (price, time, distance).

**Setup (3 min):** Pairs.
> "You are the Bucaramanga Travel Advisory Board. A tourist arrives Monday. Job: produce 5 recommendations IN ENGLISH. Each must include:
> - SHOULD or SHOULDN'T
> - A NUMBER (price, time, address, distance)
> Examples on the board:"

```
✓ "You should visit Chicamocha Canyon. The ticket costs 45,000 pesos."
✓ "You shouldn't take a taxi from the airport — it's about 80,000.
   Take an Uber for around 30,000 instead."
✓ "You should arrive at Mercagán before 7 PM. After that, the wait is 40 min."
```

**Pairs work (15 min):** Pairs write 5 recommendations. ENGLISH ONLY. Use phones for prices/info if needed.

**Presentation (7 min):** Each pair presents 1 recommendation as if to the tourist. Class scores 1-5 on:
- Clarity
- Use of SHOULD/SHOULDN'T
- Use of a real number

The board with the highest combined score wins **Travel Advisor of the Day**. Photo opportunity for redes.

> Camina durante el "Pairs work." Si una pareja se traba: "Pick a place you ACTUALLY know. Cabecera? La 33? Use English with what you know."

> **Si no recibiste la URL de ayer:** contacta a coordinación INMEDIATAMENTE. NO improvises con un video distinto — eso rompe el ciclo de 3 días.

**Recordatorio del modelo:**
- Ayer (Día 1) los estudiantes vieron este video por primera vez.
- Hoy (Día 2) repiten los 5 pasos. Conocen el video, profundizan.
- Mañana (Día 3) será PRESENTACIÓN sin video — grupos de 4 reproducen el diálogo de memoria.

**SI APARECE VOCABULARIO NUEVO O CONTRACCIONES (wanna, gonna, lemme, kinda):**
- Modela la pronunciación lentamente, 2 veces.
- Da el significado en 5 segundos.
- **NO expliques POR QUÉ se contrae.** Eso es interferir.
- Sigue al siguiente paso.

**STEP 1 — Listen with focus (2 min):** "Watch again. Just listen. Don't repeat."

**STEP 2 — Listen + read subtitles (2 min):** Subtitles ON. "Read while you listen."

**STEP 3 — Repeat in chunks (4 min):** Pause every 5-10 sec. "Repeat after the speaker." Whole class repeats together.

**STEP 4 — Shadow simultaneously (3 min):** "Speak AT THE SAME TIME as the video. Don't wait. Don't be perfect."

**STEP 5 — Pair role play (4 min):** "No video. Pairs face each other STANDING. One says a chunk from memory, the other continues. Match the tone."

> **MAÑANA es Día 3 — presentación de memoria.** Avísales: "Tomorrow: groups of 4 reproduce the dialogue from memory. Practice tonight if you want extra English Points."

## 6. PROJECT WORKSHOP — PAGE 2 DRAFT (01:15–01:35, 20 min)

**Project context:** Cada estudiante construye su proyecto personal "MY STORY, MY GOALS" durante B1. Class 1: brainstorm. Class 2: Page 1 draft. **Hoy: Page 2 — "MY MILESTONES."**

**Page 2 contains (write structure on board):**
```
MY MILESTONES:
- 3 things I have ACHIEVED in my life so far (use Present Perfect)
- 2 OBSTACLES I have overcome (use Past Simple)
- 1 LESSON each obstacle taught me (use SHOULD)

EXAMPLE:
"I have completed a technical degree at SENA.
I have learned to support myself financially.
I have started learning English at Heiiu.

I overcame depression in 2022. I overcame the loss of my father in 2018.

I should have asked for help sooner. Now I know:
when you struggle, talk to someone."
```

**Workshop (15 min):** Each student writes Page 2 in their notebook. SILENT. Camina anotando errores.

**Sharing (5 min):** 2 volunteers read 1 paragraph each. Class applauds. NO peer correction during sharing — that breaks the emotional space.

> Page 1 fue "MY STORY, MY GOALS." Page 2 conecta su Carta del Yo del Futuro (sellada en Clase 1) con realidades concretas. Si alguien se emociona — está bien. Eso es bueno.

## 7. ERROR PAPER LIVE (01:35–01:45, 10 min)

**Tu papel de errores debe tener mínimo 8-10 errores escritos de los bloques anteriores.**

**Escribe los 4-5 errores más graves en el tablero (sin nombres):**
```
ERRORS HEARD TODAY:
1. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
2. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
3. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
4. ❌ ___________  →  ✅ ___________
```

**Por cada error (~2 min):**
1. Read it aloud as if it's correct.
2. "What's wrong?" — class identifies.
3. If silence: model the correct version.
4. Class repeats 2 times in chorus.
5. 1 student uses the corrected version in a NEW sentence.

> **Sin nombres. Sin vergüenza.** Si surge un error de SHOULD/SHOULDN'T, refuerza extra — es el módulo del día.

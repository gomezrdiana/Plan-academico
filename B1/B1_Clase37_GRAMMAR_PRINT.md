Read once slowly. Cohort echoes 2×. Quick gloss: *"Third conditional is for things that DID NOT happen — and we know they cannot change. The IF clause is always the same — IF + HAD + past participle. What changes is the RESULT. STRAIGHT: the result is also in the past — WOULD HAVE + past participle. MIXED: the result is in the present — WOULD + base verb. Fortitude day 2: we name the past honestly even when we can't change it — and we keep moving."*

### (3 min) Warm-up — standing IF-clause board race
Two columns on the board. Call a past event in Spanish; the first student of each row runs and writes the IF clause in English (IF + HAD + past participle). Cohort verifies the participle. 8 rounds in 3 minutes.

```
"Si yo hubiera estudiado"
   → "If I had studied"
"Si tú hubieras llegado a tiempo"
   → "If you had arrived on time"
"Si ella hubiera enviado el correo"
   → "If she had sent the email"
"Si nosotros hubiéramos probado el demo"
   → "If we had tested the demo"
"Si él no hubiera olvidado el archivo"
   → "If he had not forgotten the file"
"Si yo hubiera sabido"
   → "If I had known"
"Si ellos hubieran venido"
   → "If they had come"
"Si yo hubiera hecho la tarea"
   → "If I had done the homework"
```

Quick note as you erase: *"Same shape every time. IF + subject + HAD + past participle. Don't change the HAD by person ('I had / she had / they had' — same word). Don't put 'would' in the IF clause — never. The 'would' belongs in the OTHER clause."*

> *"Today's content is M4 FORM: the one IF-clause shape, the two results — STRAIGHT (past result, would have + past participle) and MIXED (present result, would + base verb), the negative ('wouldn't have done' / 'wouldn't do today'), the question ('What would you have done?' / 'Where would you be now?'), and the 3 forbidden moves (no 'would' in IF clause, no past simple in IF clause, no missing HAVE in STRAIGHT result). Then we drill the form physically — 2 zones for STRAIGHT vs MIXED — and we land it in a 45-minute post-mortem review of a real project. Stay standing."*

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (8 min) Mini-talk — IF clause + STRAIGHT vs MIXED + the rules

Stand at the board. Book p.29, 33, 37 open. Write or have pre-written:

```
M4 — THIRD CONDITIONAL — FORM RULES
(B1 Book p.29 STRAIGHT · p.33 MIXED · p.37 compared)

ONE IF CLAUSE — same for both:
  IF + subject + HAD + past participle
  ("If I had studied.")
  ("If you had woken up on time.")
  ("If she had not sent the email.")

TWO RESULT CLAUSES — depending on time:

  STRAIGHT — past cause, PAST result:
    , WOULD HAVE + past participle.
    "If I had studied, I WOULD HAVE PASSED the exam."
    (I did not study → I did not pass — both past)

  MIXED — past cause, PRESENT result:
    , WOULD + base verb.
    "If I had studied, I WOULD WORK in Rome today."
    (I did not study → I do not work in Rome today)

NEGATIVE forms — same shape, add NOT:
  IF clause: "If I hadn't studied" /
              "If she hadn't sent the email"
  STRAIGHT:  "I wouldn't have passed."
  MIXED:     "I wouldn't be here today."

QUESTION forms — invert the WOULD:
  STRAIGHT: "What would you have done?"
            "Would he have called?"
  MIXED:    "Where would you be now?"
            "Would she be on the team today?"

THE 3 FORBIDDEN MOVES:
  ✗ "If I would have studied, I would have passed."
     (NEVER 'would' in the IF clause — only HAD)
  ✗ "If I studied, I would have passed."
     (past SIMPLE in IF clause = 2nd conditional, not
      3rd — use HAD + p.p. for the past-impossible)
  ✗ "If I had studied, I would passed."
     (STRAIGHT result needs HAVE — would HAVE + p.p.)

THE 0% RULE (book p.29):
  Probability of changing the past = 0%. Past
  events cannot be changed. We are talking about
  what WOULD HAVE BEEN or WOULD BE.
```

Talk through it standing, mark each rule with your hand. Book p.29 verbatim: *"The 3rd conditional (straight) refers to something that did (or did not) happen in the past, and therefore, a certain condition did (or did not) exist in the past."* Book p.33 verbatim: *"The 3rd conditional (mixed) is very similar … But, it refers to something that did (or did not) happen in the past, and therefore, a certain condition does (or does not) exist in the present/future."* Book p.30 examples drilled out loud: *"If I had gotten a bike for Christmas, I would have used it every day. / If you had woken up on time, we wouldn't have been late. / If you had done your homework, you wouldn't have been worried. / If your father had liked chicken, I would have made it for dinner."* Book p.34 examples for MIXED: *"If you had been on time, I would be amazed. / If we hadn't met, my life would be very sad. / If Phillip had bought the car, he wouldn't use a bike today. / If you had asked for directions, where would you be now?"*

Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1: *"If I had done, I would have done — STRAIGHT stays in the past. MIXED lands in the present. Fortitude day 2."*

### (15 min) Walking transformation — 2 zones STRAIGHT vs MIXED

Tape the 2 floor labels in 2 corners (or 2 opposite walls):
- **STRAIGHT — past cause, PAST result**
- **MIXED — past cause, PRESENT result**

Stand in the middle. Call ONE past event + ONE result out loud — students decide if the result is PAST (STRAIGHT) or PRESENT (MIXED), physically WALK to the zone, and say the full conditional sentence as they arrive. If wrong zone or wrong form, cohort calls "again" and they re-walk. 12 transformations in 15 min.

```
Past event + result called → Right zone + sentence
1.  "I didn't study + I failed the exam"      (past result)
       → STRAIGHT — "If I had studied, I would have passed."
2.  "I didn't study + I am unprepared today"  (present)
       → MIXED — "If I had studied, I would be prepared today."
3.  "She didn't send the doc + we missed the deadline"
       → STRAIGHT — "If she had sent the doc, we wouldn't have missed the deadline."
4.  "She didn't send the doc + we use vendor B today"
       → MIXED — "If she had sent the doc, we wouldn't use vendor B today."
5.  "We chose vendor A + the launch failed last week"
       → STRAIGHT — "If we hadn't chosen vendor A, the launch wouldn't have failed."
6.  "We chose vendor A + the site is broken now"
       → MIXED — "If we hadn't chosen vendor A, the site wouldn't be broken now."
7.  "He missed the call + he lost the lead last month"
       → STRAIGHT — "If he had taken the call, he wouldn't have lost the lead."
8.  "He missed the call + the deal is dead today"
       → MIXED — "If he had taken the call, the deal would be alive today."
9.  "I didn't sleep + I had a headache yesterday"
       → STRAIGHT — "If I had slept, I wouldn't have had a headache."
10. "I didn't sleep + I am tired right now"
       → MIXED — "If I had slept, I wouldn't be tired right now."
11. "We didn't test the demo + the demo crashed in the pitch"
       → STRAIGHT — "If we had tested the demo, it wouldn't have crashed."
12. "We didn't test the demo + the team has no confidence today"
       → MIXED — "If we had tested the demo, the team would have confidence today."
```

Coach: when someone goes to the wrong zone, don't say "wrong" — say "walk it again — is the RESULT in the past or in the present?" When form is right AND zone is right, cohort claps once. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook. Most common slips: "If I would have studied…" or "I would passed" (missing HAVE). Re-direct to the board, do NOT shame.

### (10 min) Pair drilling — 12 transformations

Hand each pair: 6 past-event cards (SET A) + 6 result cards (SET B). Pair work:

1. Student A picks a past-event card and a result card. Reads both aloud: *"I didn't study + I failed the exam."*
2. Student B labels the result as PAST or PRESENT, then produces the full third conditional: *"PAST — If I had studied, I would have passed the exam."*
3. Swap roles after every 2 transformations. Each pair must produce at least 3 MIXED transformations (present result is the new shape this class).

Walk and coach the FORM (HAD in IF clause never WOULD; STRAIGHT result has HAVE, MIXED does not; negative shapes contracted: hadn't / wouldn't). Call out one win publicly per pair. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"If I had done, I would have done. STRAIGHT past — MIXED present. Fortitude day 2 — the past does not change."*

### (7 min) Podium — reinforce questions and negatives

Draw a podium on the board (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Pick 3 strong students to come up one at a time. Each gets a SPECIAL prompt; produces the full sentence; cohort confirms in chorus *"Confirmed!"* If they hesitate or get it wrong, the cohort gives the chorus correction.

```
1st (STRAIGHT QUESTION):
   "Your client cancelled last week. Ask your
    teammate what they would have done."
       → "What would you have done if the client
          had cancelled?"

2nd (MIXED QUESTION):
   "Your coworker quit last year. Ask where the
    team would be NOW if they hadn't."
       → "Where would the team be now if she
          hadn't quit?"

3rd (DOUBLE NEGATIVE — both clauses negative):
   "We didn't test the demo. The demo failed.
    Say it as a third conditional both negative."
       → "If we hadn't skipped the test, the
          demo wouldn't have failed."
```

Then one final chorus drill — call a past event in Spanish + a time tag (past or present), cohort responds in unison:

> *"Si yo hubiera estudiado — habría pasado (past)"* → "If I had studied, I would have passed."
> *"Si yo hubiera estudiado — trabajaría en Roma (present)"* → "If I had studied, I would work in Rome."
> *"Si nosotros hubiéramos llegado a tiempo — no habríamos perdido el bus (past)"* → "If we had arrived on time, we wouldn't have missed the bus."

Note: *"Stand up — we move to the simulation."*

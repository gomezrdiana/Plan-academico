## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT FIVE-FORM MACHINE ON PAPER (~50 min) — STAND ~90%

```
1. (Stand at board) ESCRIBE la CADENA COMPLETA (las 5
   formas, una debajo de otra — toma 6 min, vale la pena):

   THE WHOLE PAST MACHINE — FIVE FORMS, ONE PARAGRAPH

   (1) OLD HABIT / STATE
        used to + base    "I used to work alone."
        would + base      "Every morning I would arrive early."

   (2) BACKGROUND (in progress)
        was/were + -ing   "I was finishing a report..."

   (3) THE EVENT (it cut the background)
        when + past simple "...when my manager called."
        past simple        "She gave me a hard client."

   (4) THE EARLIER EVENT (already done before)  [Cl 19]
        had + participle  "By then she had already chosen me."
                          "I had never managed an account alone."

   (5) THE ORDER
        first / then / after that / while / finally

   *** Habit = used to.  Background = was -ing. ***
   *** Event = simple.  Earlier = had + participle. ***
   *** Order = connectors.  The whole thing = PAST. ***

2. (Stand) SEÑALA cada capa con UN ejemplo rapido. DI:
   "You own each of these from Cl 15-19. Today we don't
   re-teach — we CHAIN them and write them clean. Watch
   one paragraph use all five."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2 (Stand): SEÑALA la frase. DI:
   "Five forms locked on one page — used to, was -ing,
   when it happened, what I had already done, and the
   order. One whole past written, no leak to the present."

4. (Stand) ESCRIBE el PARRAFO MODELO en board, etiquetando
   cada clausula con su numero de forma (60 seg):

     "(1) I used to work in a small office. (2) One
      morning I was finishing a report (3) when my manager
      called me in. (4) By then she had already chosen me
      for a difficult client, and I had never managed an
      account alone. (5) First I panicked, then I asked for
      help, and finally I closed the deal."

   DI: "Count them on the board: 1-used to, 2-was -ing,
   3-when + simple, 4-had + participle x2, 5-connectors.
   Every layer present, everything past. THIS is the shape."

5. [ERROR TIPICO #1] (dropped layer) parrafo sin past
   perfect cuando hay un evento anterior claro:
   "When I arrived, the meeting started" (ya habia
   empezado). -> ESCRIBE "...the meeting HAD already
   started." DI: "Don't drop layer 4 — if it was already
   done, had + participle." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

6. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I used to worked there."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I used to WORK there."
   DI: "After used to = base verb. Not worked — WORK."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "I was prepare a report."
   -> TACHA "prepare", ESCRIBE "I was PREPARING a report."
   DI: "Background = was + -ing. Not the base, not the
   simple." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "she had went."
   -> TACHA "went", ESCRIBE "she had GONE." DI: "go ->
   went -> GONE. Participle after HAD, never the past
   simple." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

9. [ERROR TIPICO #5] (present leak) "...then I am closing
   the deal." -> TACHA "am closing", ESCRIBE "...then I
   CLOSED the deal." DI: "Fortitude — the whole machine
   stays in the past. No jump to the present." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

10. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): cuando alguien deje caer
    una capa o escriba un presente, DI suave: "Fortitude.
    Finish the machine. Five forms, all past — which one
    is missing?"

11. (Stand) PIDELES LABELING DRILL en parejas DE PIE en
    el board: "I read a full model paragraph clause by
    clause; you write the FORM NUMBER (1-5) over each
    clause and, if it is wrong, fix it." 8 min. DICTA este
    parrafo (con 3 errores plantados):

       "I used to worked in sales. One evening I was drive
        home when my phone rang. By then the client already
        sent the contract. First I read it, then I signed,
        and finally I am celebrating."

    Correcciones esperadas: "used to WORK" (1), "was
    DRIVING" (2), "when my phone rang" (3, OK), "the client
    HAD already SENT" (4), connectors OK (5), "finally I
    CELEBRATED" (present leak). CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board
    cuando un error aparezca 2 veces.

12. PODIUM — "NAME THE FORM" SPRINT (7 min). DOS
    volunteers al frente DE PIE. Tu lees UNA clausula;
    ellos dicen que forma es (1-5) y si esta bien o mal la
    arreglan. Cohorte verifica en coro: "Form X — clean!"

    8 clausulas para llamar:
       "I used to smoke."                 -> Form 1 (clean)
       "I was cook dinner."               -> Form 2 (fix: was COOKING)
       "when the lights went out"         -> Form 3 (clean)
       "she had wrote three emails"       -> Form 4 (fix: had WRITTEN)
       "then I am leaving the office"     -> present leak (fix: then I LEFT)
       "by the time I called, he leave"   -> Form 4 (fix: he HAD LEFT)
       "every summer we would travel"     -> Form 1 (clean)
       "after that, I fixed it"           -> Form 5 + simple (clean)

13. Aplauso al final. (Stand) ESCRIBE la SELF-CHECK CARD
    (la misma que Conv uso esta mañana — asi la ven en los
    dos bloques):

    BEFORE YOU HAND IN A PAST STORY, CHECK:
       1. OLD habit? (used to / would)
       2. BACKGROUND? (was -ing)
       3. EVENT? (when + past simple)
       4. EARLIER event? (had + participle)
       5. ORDER? (first / then / finally)
       *** And is it PAST the whole way — zero present? ***

14. CIERRA cuando 80% etiquete las 5 formas y arregle los
    present leaks sin ayuda. DI: "Stand. Push chairs to the
    walls. We run the Mastery Relay."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 35 min y la cohorte aun deja caer 2+ capas o escribe presentes, NO corras al podium. Reduce a 4 formas (used to + was -ing + when + simple + had + participle) y deja la cadena completa de connectors como callback en el relay. Mejor un parrafo de 4 capas limpio que 5 capas con fugas.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas + podium etiquetando las 5 formas y sellando los present leaks, con el participio correcto, sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: MASTERY RELAY (~45 min) — STAND 100%

```
1. (Stand) ESCRIBE en board grande: "MASTERY RELAY — ONE
   STORY, EVERY CLAUSE LABELED WITH ITS FORM — 20+ BUILDS,
   ALL FIVE FORMS, 3 ROUNDS"

2. DI las reglas: "We build ONE long past story together
   on the board, standing. Each pair adds ONE clause AND
   writes the form number (1-5) above it. Say it out loud,
   then write it, then label it. The next pair must add a
   DIFFERENT form than the one before — so the story keeps
   cycling through all five. If a clause leaks to the
   present, uses the wrong participle, or drops the label,
   the cohort calls 'Fortitude!' and the pair fixes it
   before we move on. Goal: every form appears at least
   three times, and the story stays 100% past."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3 (Stand): DI: "This relay IS the
   phrase — five forms locked on one page, no layer
   dropped, no leak to the present. One whole past
   written."

4. (Stand) ESCRIBE la PRIMERA linea semilla y el banco de
   formas al lado:

     SEED: "(1) I used to work night shifts."

     FORM BANK (label every clause with its number):
        1 = used to / would        4 = had + participle
        2 = was -ing               5 = connector
        3 = when + past simple

   ROUND 1 (15 min) — HABIT + BACKGROUND + EVENT (forms
   1-3 dominant): pairs add clauses 2-8, cycling forms,
   each labeled. Target shape:
        "(1) I used to work night shifts. (2) One night I
         was closing the store (3) when a customer knocked
         hard on the glass. (5) At first I hesitated..."

5. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces en
   la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> sigue la cadena.

6. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): si una clausula salte al
   presente o deje caer el label, DI suave: "Fortitude.
   Finish the machine. Which form is this — and is it
   past?"

7. ROTA el turno. ROUND 2 (15 min) — DROP THE FLASHBACK
   LAYER IN (form 4 mandatory): pairs add clauses 9-14,
   and at least half must be form 4 (had + participle).
   Target shape:
        "(4) By then the manager had already left. (4) I
         had never handled an emergency alone, (5) so first
         I called security..."

8. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If a pair freezes,
   plan the clause in Spanish for 5 seconds: which form do
   we need next, and which event came first? Then write it
   in English and label it."

9. ROTA el turno otra vez. ROUND 3 (15 min) — CLOSE THE
   STORY, ALL FIVE VISIBLE: pairs add clauses 15-20+ to
   resolve the story, making sure by the end every form
   number (1-5) appears at least three times on the board.
   Target close:
        "(5) After that I stayed calm, (3) when help
         finally arrived, (4) I realized I had grown more
         in one night than I had expected in a year."
   Push: 20+ clausulas, las 5 formas etiquetadas, cero
   presentes. Observer cuenta present leaks y wrong labels.

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. CIERRA con LECTURA CORAL + FORM COUNT: la cohorte lee
    la historia completa del board en voz alta, de pie. UN
    voluntario cuenta en voz alta cuantas veces aparece
    cada forma: "Form 1 x3, Form 2 x4, Form 3 x4, Form 4
    x5, Form 5 x6 — all five locked, all past." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Add a form 4 — what HAD already happened before this?" / "Connect with a form 5 — 'after that'") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rounds completos (20+ clausulas etiquetadas, las 5 formas x3+, cero present leaks) + lectura coral + form count final.

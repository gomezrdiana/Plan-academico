## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT SPELLING + PRONUNCIATION TABLE (~50 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE la tabla completa en board (toma 5 min,
   vale la pena):

   SPELLING — 4 PATTERNS
      (A) base + ED         work    -> worked
                            call    -> called
                            play    -> played
                            ask     -> asked
                            finish  -> finished
                            help    -> helped
      (B) base ends in E + D  live  -> lived
                              love  -> loved
                              arrive-> arrived
                              move  -> moved
                              like  -> liked
      (C) consonant + Y -> IED  study -> studied
                                try   -> tried
                                cry   -> cried
                                carry -> carried
                                worry -> worried
                                NOTE: vowel + Y just adds ED
                                play -> played (NOT plaied)
      (D) CVC short + DOUBLE + ED  stop -> stopped
                                   plan -> planned
                                   rob  -> robbed
                                   chat -> chatted
                                   beg  -> begged
                                   NOTE: only 1-syllable CVC

   PRONUNCIATION — 3 SOUNDS
      /t/   after voiceless (k, p, s, sh, ch, f, x)
            worked, asked, helped, looked, finished,
            stopped, watched, washed, fixed
      /d/   after voiced (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r,
            vowel sounds)
            lived, played, called, opened, arrived,
            rained, listened, planned, robbed
      /ɪd/  after T or D ONLY (extra syllable!)
            wanted, needed, started, ended, waited,
            visited, decided, included

   TIME MARKERS — where to anchor the past
      yesterday          last Monday / last week
      2 days ago         in 2020
      when I was younger this morning
      a long time ago    a few hours ago

   CALLBACK CL 10 (pronoun stays, verb changes)
      Cl 10: "I called HIM."     (pronoun after verb)
      Cl 11: "I called HIM yesterday." (verb adds ED,
                                         pronoun stays)
      Same pronoun, new past shape.

2. SEÑALA bloque (A) base + ED. DI: "The default.
   If nothing special — just add ED. Work, worked.
   Call, called. Play, played. Ask, asked."

3. [FRASE] insertion 2: SEÑALA la frase. DI:
   "Worked /t/, lived /d/, wanted /ɪd/, studied
   /d/, stopped /t/. Four spellings, three sounds.
   Each verb has its right ending."

4. SEÑALA bloque (B) -E + D. DI: "Verb ends in E?
   Just add D. The E is already there. Live, lived.
   Love, loved. Arrive, arrived. Never write
   'liveed' or 'livd' — just LIVED."

5. SEÑALA bloque (C) consonant + Y -> IED. DI:
   "CONSONANT before Y — change Y to I, add ED.
   Study, studied. Try, tried. Cry, cried. BUT
   if a VOWEL comes before Y — keep the Y, just
   add ED. Play, played. Stay, stayed. Enjoy,
   enjoyed."

6. SEÑALA bloque (D) CVC double. DI: "Short verb,
   CONSONANT + VOWEL + CONSONANT pattern. Double
   the last consonant, then ED. Stop, stopped.
   Plan, planned. Rob, robbed. Chat, chatted. BUT
   if the consonant is W, X, or Y — never double.
   And if 2 vowels — never double. RAIN, rained.
   NEED, needed."

7. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I workt yesterday" -> TACHA
   "workt", ESCRIBE "I WORKED yesterday." DI:
   "Spelling is ALWAYS ED. The sound is /t/ —
   because K is voiceless — but on paper, ED.
   Never T." COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "She studyed" -> TACHA
   "studyed", ESCRIBE "She STUDIED." DI: "D before
   Y — that's a consonant. Change Y to I, then
   add ED. Study, studied. Try, tried. Cry, cried."
   COHORTE repite las 3 verbos 2 veces.

9. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "He stopt" -> TACHA "stopt",
   ESCRIBE "He STOPPED." DI: "Short CVC verb — S,
   T, O, P. Stop has CVC: T + O + P. Double the P,
   then ED. Stopped." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

10. [VIRTUD] activation: cuando alguien titubee
    entre patterns, DI suave: "Justice. Which
    pattern? Base, -E, consonant + Y, or CVC?
    Pick clean."

11. [ERROR TIPICO #4] estudiante dice "waited"
    como /weɪt/ (sin syllable extra). MODELA:
    "WAIT — /ˈweɪt/. WAITED — /ˈweɪtɪd/. T or D
    before ED ALWAYS adds a new syllable. Listen:
    WAIT-ED. NEED-ED. START-ED. END-ED." COHORTE
    repite las 4 palabras 2 veces, separando
    syllables con palmadas.

12. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "We livd" -> TACHA "livd",
    ESCRIBE "We LIVED." DI: "Verb ends in E —
    LIVE. Just add D. Lived, loved, arrived, moved.
    Never crush the vowel."

13. PIDELES en parejas: "Transform 8 verbs. I dict
    the base, you write the past simple AND mark
    the sound (/t/ /d/ /ɪd/)." 5 min.
    DICTA estos 8:
       a. work     -> worked   (/t/)
       b. live     -> lived    (/d/)
       c. study    -> studied  (/d/)
       d. stop     -> stopped  (/t/)
       e. want     -> wanted   (/ɪd/)
       f. play     -> played   (/d/)
       g. finish   -> finished (/t/)
       h. need     -> needed   (/ɪd/)
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error
    aparezca 2 veces.

14. PODIUM (7 min). DOS volunteers al frente. Tu
    das una base verb + un time marker, ellos
    producen la oracion completa con past simple
    regular y dicen el sonido en voz alta. Cohorte
    verifica en coro: "Confirmed — spelling clean,
    sound right!"

    8 trios para llamar:
       work    / yesterday          -> "I worked yesterday." /t/
       study   / last week           -> "She studied last week." /d/
       live    / in 2020             -> "We lived in 2020." /d/
       stop    / 2 days ago          -> "They stopped 2 days ago." /t/
       want    / when I was younger  -> "I wanted it when I was younger." /ɪd/
       play    / last Monday         -> "He played last Monday." /d/
       finish  / this morning        -> "She finished this morning." /t/
       need    / a few hours ago     -> "We needed it a few hours ago." /ɪd/

15. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% construya
    spelling + sound + time marker sin titubear.
    DI: "Stand. Push chairs to the walls. We go
    to the live application."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 35 min y aun confunden los 4 patterns spelling, NO AVANCES al podium. Reduce: solo (A) ED + (C) IED hoy, deja (B) -D + (D) CVC para Cl 12 opener. Mejor LAND 2 patterns bien que rush 4 shaky.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: ED PRONUNCIATION RELAY (~45 min)

```
1. ESCRIBE en board grande: "ED PRONUNCIATION
   RELAY — 3 COLUMNAS + 8 TRANSFORMACIONES"

2. PINTA 3 columnas grandes en board:
       /t/         /d/         /ɪd/

3. DI las reglas: "PHASE 1 — In pairs, I dictate
   20 regular verbs in past simple. You sort each
   verb into the right column on YOUR paper AND
   pronounce it out loud. Two minutes per round
   of 10 verbs. Then we check on the board: each
   pair sends one person up to write 1 verb in
   the column they chose. Cohorte verifies in
   chorus. PHASE 2 — 8 transformations base ->
   past, alternating pairs on the board."

4. [FRASE] insertion 3: DI: "This relay IS the
   phrase — worked /t/, lived /d/, wanted /ɪd/,
   studied /d/, stopped /t/. Three sounds, no
   confusion. Four spellings, no drift."

5. ESCRIBE en board los 20 verbos para dictar
   (PHASE 1):

   ROUND 1 (10 verbos):
     worked, lived, wanted, studied, asked,
     played, needed, stopped, called, finished

   ROUND 2 (10 verbos):
     helped, arrived, started, planned, looked,
     opened, ended, watched, listened, decided

6. PIDELES round 1 (10 min): pareja recibe los 10
   primeros. DICTA uno por uno, despacio. Pareja
   escribe en la columna correcta + pronuncia en
   voz baja. Cuando termines los 10, llamas a 10
   personas (una por verbo) al board: escribe el
   verbo en la columna correcta. Cohorte verifica:
   "Confirmed!" o "Move it!" Tu confirmas o
   corriges con la regla.

7. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If you doubt,
   say the base verb out loud — the LAST sound of
   the base decides. Voiceless (k, p, s, sh, ch,
   f) -> /t/. Voiced (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r,
   vowels) -> /d/. T or D -> /ɪd/. Say the base,
   feel the sound."

8. ROTA parejas + cambia roles. Round 2 (10 min):
   los 10 verbos de round 2.

9. [VIRTUD] activation durante los rounds: si
   alguien dice "waited" sin syllable o "worked"
   con T literal, DI suave: "Justice. Give the
   past its right ending. Voiceless /t/, voiced
   /d/, T or D /ɪd/."

10. PHASE 2 (15 min) — TRANSFORMACIONES base ->
    past. ESCRIBE en board 8 verbos en base form:
       1. live    (CVC? No — ends in E. Just D.)   -> lived /d/
       2. study   (consonant + Y -> IED)            -> studied /d/
       3. work    (default + ED)                    -> worked /t/
       4. stop    (CVC double)                      -> stopped /t/
       5. want    (default + ED, T before)          -> wanted /ɪd/
       6. play    (vowel + Y, just ED)              -> played /d/
       7. finish  (default + ED, sh before)         -> finished /t/
       8. need    (default + ED, D before)          -> needed /ɪd/

    Parejas alternan. Pareja 1 va al board, escribe
    el past de #1 + dice el sonido. Cohorte
    verifica. Pareja 2 hace #2. Y asi hasta 8.
    Cuando se equivoque alguien, TU senalas la
    regla en la tabla — pareja corrige sin
    explicacion verbal larga.

11. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2
    veces en parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE
    en tu cuaderno.

12. CIERRA con UNA pareja modelo: 90 segundos
    frente al grupo (escogen 4 verbos al azar de
    la lista, los transforman, los pronuncian, y
    los usan en 4 oraciones con time markers).
    COHORTE cuenta cuantos sonidos diferentes
    cazaron. Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos en PHASE 2, DROP UN PROMPT ("Stop ends in P — what pattern? CVC. Double it.") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 2 rounds de PHASE 1 completos + PHASE 2 con 8 transformaciones + UNA demo al frente con 4 verbos + sonidos correctos + time markers visibles.

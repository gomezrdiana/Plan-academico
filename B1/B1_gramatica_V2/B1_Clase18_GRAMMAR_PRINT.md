## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT INTEGRATED MAP + CONNECTORS (~50 min) — STAND ~90%

```
1. (Stand at board) ESCRIBE el MAPA INTEGRADO completo
   (toma 6 min, vale la pena):

   PAST NARRATIVE — ONE FORM PER CLAUSE

   SCENE / BACKGROUND
      used to + BASE      -> old habit/state
          "I used to work alone."   (not "used to worked")
      was/were + -ING     -> action in progress
          "I was preparing a report."  (Module 12)

   TURNING POINT / EVENT
      when + PAST SIMPLE  -> the action that cuts it
          "...when the manager called me."
      PAST SIMPLE         -> completed action
          "She gave me a new client."

   STRUGGLE
      simple + continuous mixed
          "I didn't know the system. The deadline was
           coming while I was failing every test."

   SEQUENCE / CONNECTORS
      first -> then -> after that -> finally
          "First I asked for help. Then I practiced.
           Finally I closed the deal."

   *** Background = was -ing.  Event = simple. ***
   *** Old habit = used to + BASE.  Order = connectors. ***
   *** The whole paragraph stays in the PAST. ***

2. (Stand) SEÑALA SCENE. DI: "Two ways to open: used to
   + BASE for an old habit — 'I used to work alone' —
   and was/were + -ING for what was in progress — 'I was
   preparing a report'. The book frame is Module 12:
   'I was eating pasta when my brother arrived.'"

3. [FRASE] insertion 2 (Stand): SEÑALA la frase. DI:
   "used to + base sets the scene, was -ing holds the
   background, when + simple cuts it, then and finally
   carry the order. One past, one timeline."

4. (Stand) SEÑALA TURNING POINT. DI: "The cut is fast
   and finished — past simple, usually after WHEN. The
   long background is was -ing; the short event that
   interrupts is simple. WAS -ING ... WHEN + SIMPLE."

5. (Stand) SEÑALA SEQUENCE. DI: "Connectors are the
   rails: first, then, after that, finally. They keep
   ONE timeline. Without them a story is a flat list."

6. [ERROR TIPICO #1] "I used to worked alone."
   -> TACHA "worked", ESCRIBE "I used to WORK alone."
   DI: "After USED TO = BASE verb. No -ed, no irregular."
   COHORTE repite 2 veces en coro.

7. [ERROR TIPICO #2] "I was call her when it happened."
   -> TACHA "was call", ESCRIBE "I was CALLING her when
   it happened." DI: "Background = was + -ing." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

8. [ERROR TIPICO #3] "When I was finished the report,
   he arrived." -> TACHA "was finished", ESCRIBE "When I
   FINISHED the report, he arrived." DI: "The event in
   the WHEN-clause is past simple, not was -ing." COHORTE
   repite 2 veces.

9. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): cuando alguien escriba
   un salto al presente, DI suave: "Fortitude. Stay in
   the past. Background = was -ing, event = simple,
   habit = used to."

10. [ERROR TIPICO #4] "So I work there and then he calls
    me." -> TACHA "work / calls", ESCRIBE "I WORKED
    there and then he CALLED me." DI: "The whole
    paragraph stays in the past. No jump to the present."
    COHORTE repite 2 veces.

11. [ERROR TIPICO #5] "...and then, after, finally,
    first I asked..." -> REORDENA en board: "First I
    asked. Then I practiced. Finally I closed it." DI:
    "Connectors carry order. First opens, finally
    closes." COHORTE repite 2 veces.

12. (Stand) PIDELES en parejas DE PIE en el board:
    "Transform 8 mixed clauses into the correct past
    form. I dictate the source; you write the clause on
    the board with the RIGHT form and say why." 8 min.
    DICTA estos 8:
       a. "I (used to) live in a small town"   -> "I USED TO LIVE in a small town" (base)
       b. "She (prepare) a report when I came"  -> "She WAS PREPARING a report when I came" (background)
       c. "...when the manager (call) me"       -> "...when the manager CALLED me" (event simple)
       d. "We (have) no system back then"       -> "We USED TO HAVE no system" / "We DIDN'T HAVE" (state)
       e. "The deadline (come) while I (fail)"  -> "The deadline WAS COMING while I WAS FAILING" (two backgrounds)
       f. "First I (ask) for help"              -> "First I ASKED for help" (simple)
       g. "Then I (practice) every night"       -> "Then I PRACTICED every night" (simple)
       h. "Finally I (close) the deal"          -> "Finally I CLOSED the deal" (simple)
    CIRCULA. CORRIGE en board cuando un error aparezca
    2 veces.

13. PODIUM — "FIX THE CLAUSE" SPRINT (7 min). DOS
    volunteers al frente DE PIE. Tu lees una clausula
    con la forma EQUIVOCADA y ellos la corrigen + nombran
    la capa (scene / event / sequence). Cohorte verifica
    en coro: "Confirmed — past, one timeline!"

    8 broken clauses para llamar:
       "I used to worked there"        -> "used to WORK" (scene)
       "I was call her"                -> "was CALLING" (background)
       "When I was finished"           -> "When I FINISHED" (event)
       "Then I am winning"             -> "Then I WON" (sequence/past)
       "She use to study a lot"        -> "She USED TO study" (scene)
       "While I worked, he was call"   -> "...he was CALLING" (background)
       "After that I have closed it"   -> "After that I CLOSED it" (sequence/simple)
       "Finally we are succeeding"     -> "Finally we SUCCEEDED" (sequence/past)

14. Aplauso al final. CIERRA cuando 80% elija la forma
    correcta por capa y mantenga el pasado sin titubear.
    DI: "Stand. Push chairs to the walls. We build a
    whole story on the board."
```

**[ALERTA]** si llegas a 35 min y aun mezclan presente/pasado o pegan -ed despues de used to, NO AVANCES al podium. Reduce a 3 capas (used to + base / was -ing background / when + simple event) y deja sequencing para Cl 19 opener. Mejor LAND 3 formas limpias que rush 5 shaky.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** parejas + podium eligiendo la forma correcta por clausula y manteniendo el pasado sin pedir ayuda.

## BLOCK 3 — LIVE: STORY-BUILD SPRINT (~45 min) — STAND 100%

```
1. (Stand) ESCRIBE en board grande: "STORY-BUILD SPRINT
   — ONE STORY, EVERY PAIR ADDS A CLAUSE — 18+ BUILDS,
   3 ROUNDS"

2. DI las reglas: "We build ONE long past story together
   on the board, standing. I write the first line. Then
   each pair, in turn, adds ONE clause that (a) is in the
   correct past form and (b) moves the story forward. You
   say it out loud, then write it on the board. The next
   pair must connect with a sequencing word. If a clause
   has the wrong form, the cohort calls 'Fortitude!' and
   the pair fixes it before we move on. Observer of the
   nearby pair watches for present-tense slips."

3. [FRASE] insertion 3 (Stand): DI: "This sprint IS the
   phrase — used to + base sets the scene, was -ing holds
   the background, when + simple cuts it, then and finally
   carry the order. One past, one timeline."

4. (Stand) ESCRIBE la PRIMERA linea semilla en board y
   el banco de connectors al lado:

     SEED: "A few years ago, I used to work in a small
            office downtown."

     CONNECTOR BANK (each new clause must use one):
        then / after that / while / when / one morning /
        suddenly / first / later / finally / in the end

   ROUND 1 (15 min) — BUILD THE SCENE + TURNING POINT:
   pairs add, in turn, clauses 2-7. Target shape:
        "...One morning I was preparing an important
         report WHEN my manager CALLED me in. She GAVE
         me a difficult new client..."
   Cada pareja: 1 clausula, forma correcta, conector.

5. CIRCULA. INTERVEN solo si un error aparece 3 veces
   en la misma pareja -> board -> coro 2x -> sigue la
   cadena.

6. [VIRTUD] activation (Stand): si una clausula salte al
   presente o use "used to went", DI suave: "Fortitude.
   Stay in the past. Background = was -ing, event =
   simple, habit = used to."

7. ROTA el turno. ROUND 2 (15 min) — BUILD THE STRUGGLE:
   pairs add clauses 8-13. Target shape:
        "First I PANICKED. I DIDN'T KNOW the product and
         I WAS LOSING time while the deadline WAS COMING
         closer. Then I ASKED a colleague for help..."
   Cada clausula mezcla simple + continuous correctamente.

8. [PROMPT] (translanguaging): DI: "If a pair freezes,
   plan the clause in Spanish for 5 seconds, then say it
   in English — past form, one connector."

9. ROTA el turno otra vez. ROUND 3 (15 min) — BUILD THE
   RESOLUTION + CLOSE: pairs add clauses 14-18+. Target:
        "After that I PRACTICED every night. Slowly it
         GOT easier. Finally I CLOSED the deal, and in
         the end I LEARNED that I was stronger than I
         thought."
   Push: al menos 18 clausulas en total, todas en pasado,
   con conectores en orden. Observer cuenta slips.

10. CIRCULA, CORRIGE cuando un error aparezca 2 veces en
    parejas distintas. Anota con NOMBRE en tu cuaderno.

11. CIERRA con LECTURA CORAL: la cohorte lee la historia
    completa del board en voz alta, de principio a fin,
    todos de pie. UN voluntario reporta: "X clauses, all
    past, Y connectors, one timeline." Aplauso.
```

**[ALERTA]** si una pareja se queda en silencio 15+ segundos, DROP UN PROMPT ("Add a clause: what were you doing when it changed?" / "Connect with 'then'") y RETIRATE. NO expliques, NO llenes el silencio.
**[OK] SEÑAL DE AVANCE:** 3 rounds completos (18+ clausulas encadenadas) + lectura coral final con la historia entera en pasado y conectores en orden.

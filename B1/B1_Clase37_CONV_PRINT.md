## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — third conditional in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model the form out loud.

> *"Third conditional in real work dialogue — one form, two halves, one preview. The form. IF clause: 'IF' + subject + 'HAD' + past participle. 'If I had sent the email Monday'. NOT 'If I would have sent', NOT 'If I sent' — that's second conditional. The IF half always uses HAD + participle, no exceptions. RESULT clause: 'WOULD HAVE' + past participle. 'I would have caught the bug.' Alternatives: 'could have' for past possibility, 'might have' for partial certainty — same form, different shade of meaning. Putting it together: 'If I had sent the email Monday, the client would have signed Tuesday.' Both halves are about the PAST. Both are hypothetical. The probability is zero — the past is the past. Why we use it: post-mortems. Lesson-extraction. Extracting the rule of what to do next time from the call that didn't happen this time. PREVIEW for next class — the MIXED third conditional. Same IF clause about the past — 'If I had taken the job in Bogotá' — but the result clause flips to the PRESENT — 'I would live there today'. Past cause, present consequence. We'll work it deeply next class. Today's focus: straight third conditional, both halves in the past. Fortitude day 2: name the past, name the lesson, no excuses."*

Demo on the board:

```
THIRD CONDITIONAL — STRAIGHT (today)
  IF + had + participle  ,  WOULD HAVE + participle

  "If you had sent it Monday, I would have approved it Tuesday."
   IF + HAD + sent          WOULD HAVE + approved

  Variants: could have / might have (same form,
  different certainty)

NEVER: "If I would have sent" · "If I sent"
       (that's 2nd cond — different module)

MIXED (preview for Cl 38)
  IF + had + participle  ,  WOULD + base verb
  "If I had taken the job, I would live there today."
   past cause                present consequence
```

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Roles assigned. FACILITATOR sets the project — "the Q3 launch slipped two weeks". ENGINEER and PM alternate, 8 sentences total. Observer marks the grid. You circulate triad by triad — coach the form when wrong, kill "If I would have" instantly (*"redo: 'If you HAD'"*), push for the "Next time I will…" carry-forward when it gets dropped.

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate. New FACILITATOR, new ENGINEER, new PM, new OBSERVER. New project — "the client didn't renew the contract". Same 8-sentence structure. By now the cohort knows the rhythm — push for SPEED. Full sentences in 8 seconds. Push for at least 2 COULD HAVE and 1 MIGHT HAVE per round (not just default WOULD HAVE).

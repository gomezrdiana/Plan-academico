## 2. ICEBREAKER — "HOW OFTEN DO YOU...?" (00:07–00:20, 13 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO:** Mover el cuerpo + practicar "How often" + frequency adverbs.

**Setup (2 min):** "Everybody STAND UP. I will say 'How often do you ___?' Each person answers with a frequency adverb + expression. Then move to the next person."

**Round 1 (4 min):** Profesor dispara preguntas, todos responden EN CADENA:
- "How often do you check your phone?" → I always check it. / I check it every 5 minutes. / I rarely check it.
- "How often do you cook at home?"
- "How often do you exercise?"
- "How often do you call your mom?"
- "How often do you read for fun?"

Cada estudiante responde una pregunta. Si solo asisten 8, dispara más rondas.

**Round 2 (4 min):** Estudiantes hacen preguntas a otros estudiantes. Mismo formato.
- "Catalina, how often do you...?"
- "Miguel, how often do you...?"

**Round 3 — confesiones (3 min):** "ONE thing you ALWAYS do that no one knows. ONE thing you NEVER do but everyone thinks you do."

**Cierre (2 min):** "SIT DOWN. Pick ONE confession you heard that surprised you. Tell the person next to you in 30 sec."

> Anota errores. Si oyes "I check often" → anótalo.

## 4. SIMULACIÓN — ENTREVISTA "TELL ME ABOUT YOUR WEEK" (00:38–01:00, 22 min) — ⚠️ NO SE PUEDE SALTAR

**OBJETIVO:** Practicar frequency adverbs + expressions en entrevista periodística simulada.

**Setup (3 min):** "STAND UP. Push chairs. ONE person interviewer (asks how often), other interviewee (answers with frequency). ENGLISH ONLY."

**Tarjetas — preguntas para el interviewer (escribir antes):**
```
INTERVIEWER ASKS:
1. "How often do you exercise?"
2. "How often do you cook?"
3. "How often do you see your family?"
4. "How often do you travel?"
5. "How often do you read books in English?"
6. "What's something you always do on Sundays?"
7. "What's something you never do?"
8. "What's something you should do more often?"
```

**Modela primero (2 min):**
- Interviewer: "How often do you cook at home?"
- Interviewee: "I usually cook three or four times a week. On weekends I always cook a big meal for my family. But I never cook breakfast — I drink coffee and that's it."

**Round 1 (8 min):** Pairs. Interviewer pregunta 4 questions. Interviewee responde con DETALLE (frequency adverb + expression + razón).

**Round 2 (7 min):** SWITCH partners + roles. 4 different questions.

**Mientras tanto:** Camina. Anota errores. Libreta: 3 con NOMBRE.

**Cierre (2 min):** 1 voluntario representa frente al grupo (60 seg). Aplauso.

> NO es roleplay. Estudiantes son ELLOS mismos hablando de su vida real.

## 6. PROJECT WORKSHOP ORAL — PAGE 6: MY HABITS NOW (01:15–01:33, 18 min)

**Project context:** Proyecto MY STORY, MY GOALS.
- Class 1: brainstorm
- Class 2: Page 1 — MY STORY, MY GOALS
- Class 3: Page 2 — MY MILESTONES
- Class 4: Page 3 — MY OBSTACLES IN ENGLISH
- Class 5: Page 4 — MY DAILY HABITS THAT BUILD MY ENGLISH
- Class 6: SPECIAL (sin escribir)
- Class 7: Page 5 — MY MILESTONES PAST
- **Hoy Class 8: Page 6 — MY HABITS NOW (frequency adverbs + expressions)**

**Por qué hoy:** los estudiantes acaban de aprender frequency adverbs. Lo aplican AL PROYECTO narrando con precisión sus rutinas.

**Page 6 estructura (escribe en tablero):**
```
MY HABITS NOW — IN FREQUENCY:

3 things I ALWAYS do (use ALWAYS + adverbs):
- I always _______ (every day / every morning).
- I usually _______ (twice a week).
- I never _______ (although I should).

ONE habit I do RARELY but want to do MORE OFTEN:
- I rarely _______, but I want to do it _______ (frequency).

ONE habit I do TOO OFTEN that I want to REDUCE:
- I _______ too often. I should only do it _______ (frequency).
```

**Setup (2 min):** "Pairs. ORAL FIRST. Tell your partner about your 3 always habits + 1 rare one + 1 too-often one. 5 min each. ENGLISH ONLY."

**Round 1 oral (10 min):** Pares. 5 min cada uno. NO escriben todavía.

**Round 2 escrito (5 min):** "Now WRITE Page 6 in your notebook. From memory. From what you JUST said."

**Cierre (1 min):** 1 voluntario lee 1 sección. Aplauso.

> Oral primero = construye habla real, NO ChatGPT.


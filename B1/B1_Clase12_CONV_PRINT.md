## 7. SIMULACIÓN PROFESIONAL — "JOB INTERVIEW WITH PRESENT PERFECT" (00:40–01:00, 20 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Real-life scene. Job interview. Present Perfect emerges naturally because the interviewer asks about your experience. Today: casual but professional register."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Una empresa multinacional con sede en Bucaramanga te entrevista para un cargo bilingüe. El entrevistador hace preguntas sobre tu experiencia + tus logros + lo que has aprendido.

**Roles (rotan):**
- Entrevistador (pregunta usando Present Perfect)
- Candidato (responde usando Present Perfect)

### Banco de preguntas estructuradas

**Tablero — escribe ANTES de clase:**

```
INTERVIEWER QUESTIONS (Present Perfect-based):

1. "Have you ever managed a team?"
2. "What experience have you had in customer service?"
3. "Have you worked with international clients before?"
4. "What's the biggest challenge you've faced professionally?"
5. "How long have you been in your current role?"
6. "What have you learned from a mistake at work?"
7. "Have you ever had to handle a difficult complaint?"

CANDIDATE FORMAT:
- "Yes, I HAVE [V3] for [duration]..."
- "I HAVE worked with [...] since [time]..."
- "The most difficult thing I HAVE encountered was..."
- "I HAVE just finished [recent action]..."
```

### Modela (3 min)

> *"Interviewer: 'Have you ever managed a team?'*
> *Candidate: 'Yes, I have managed teams of up to 10 people. In my last role, I have led the digital marketing team for two years. What I have learned is that listening matters more than speaking when you lead.'"*

### Round 1 — Parejas (7 min)

Cada pareja: 3 preguntas del banco. Entrevistador pregunta. Candidato responde con Present Perfect natural (no monólogo formal).

### Round 2 — Cambio de roles (5 min)

Roles cambian. 3 preguntas nuevas.

### Cierre (2 min)

1 pareja representa frente al grupo. Aplauso. Tú anotas errores con NOMBRE.

> **Esto es exactamente el bridge formal→casual.** Yesterday's texts and Hot Topic = formal register. Today's interview = casual but professional. Mismo Present Perfect.

## 9. PROJECT WORKSHOP — PAGE 11: ESCRITURA DEL BORRADOR (01:15–01:33, 18 min) — SENTADAS

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Today's writing emerges from yesterday's speaking. You spoke first (Cl 11 Hot Topic + Simulation). Today you WRITE the first draft of your professional narrative — using Present Perfect."*

**Page 11 estructura:**

```
MY PROFESSIONAL NARRATIVE — Draft 1

Use Present Perfect throughout. Include at least 1 from each:

PARAGRAPH 1 — Who you are and what you have done:
"My name is ___. I have worked in ___ for ___ years.
I have led ___. I have learned ___."

PARAGRAPH 2 — A first-time experience that has shaped you
(reference yesterday's Heiiu text thesis):
"The first time I ___, I ___ (Past Simple).
Since then, I have ___ (Present Perfect).
That experience has taught me ___."

PARAGRAPH 3 — What you are doing now + what you want next:
"Currently I am ___ (Present Continuous).
I have already ___ (Present Perfect).
What I want next is ___."

Length: ~150-200 words handwritten.
```

**Setup (2 min):** *"This is YOUR draft 1. Don't worry about perfection. Get the structure on paper. Present Perfect throughout."*

**Escritura silenciosa (12 min):** Cada estudiante escribe Page 11 a mano.

**Round oral compartir (3 min):** Parejas leen su párrafo 2 (first-time experience) a su pareja. Pareja escucha + sugiere 1 mejora.

**Cierre (1 min):** *"This is draft 1. Mañana refinamos. Mantén la hoja."*

> **Esto es B1 anti-fraude:** la escritura emerge en clase, no en casa con ChatGPT. Y emerge DESPUÉS de hablar el contenido — siguiendo el patrón "hablar → escribir" de B2.

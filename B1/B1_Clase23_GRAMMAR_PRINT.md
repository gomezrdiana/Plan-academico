## 6. LIBRO M32 — HOW MANY? + BOARD RACE (00:22–00:34, 12 min) — DE PIE

### Concepto DESDE EL LIBRO (5 min) — profesor de pie en el tablero

**Cita textual del A2 Book M32 (módulo pág 273; Guía 1 pág 275; Vocabulario pág 274) — escribe el esquema en tablero ANTES de clase:**

```
M32 — HOW MANY? (A2 Book — modulo pag 273)
       "How Many People Are There?" / Countable Quantities

From the book (Guia 1 — "How many?", pag 275):
"1. Las palabras interrogativas 'how many ...?' significan
 '¿Cuantos...?'"
"2. Se usan para elementos contables (ej. 1 perro, 2 perros,
 etc.)."
"3. Se coloca esta expresion antes del elemento de interes."

PREGUNTA CON PALABRA INTERROGATIVA (pag 275):
   Palabra Interr. | Elemento -> Ayudante | Sujeto | Verbo
   How many        | dogs      do          | you    | have?
   -> "How many dogs do you have?"
   -> "Cuantos perros tienes tu?"

"4. Tambien, podemos usarla con 'there are / are there'."
   "How many dogs are there?" -> "Cuantos perros hay?"

"5. Las expresiones 'many' o 'a lot of' o 'lots of'
 significan 'muchos' para objetos contables."
   "I have many friends." / "I have a lot of friends."
   -> "Tengo muchos amigos."

"6. La frase 'a few' significa 'unos / algunos', e implica
 una cantidad de aproximadamente 3 - 5."
   "I have a few friends." -> "Tengo unos amigos."

VOCABULARIO (pag 274):
   a person (sing.) / people (plural irregular)
   many = muchos / a lot of = lots of = muchos
   a few = unos (aprox. 3-5)

KEY: HOW MANY + COUNTABLE PLURAL + (do/does + subject +
     verb)? / HOW MANY + COUNTABLE PLURAL + are there?
     Answers: a NUMBER ("There are 7 nurses.") OR
              a few / many / a lot of / lots of.
     "People" is PLURAL irregular -> "there ARE", never
     "there is".
```

Today only COUNTABLE plural — Monday's M33 will do uncountable with 'how much'. Note: 'M31 does NOT exist in this book — it jumps M30 -> M32 — same way it jumped M24 -> M26 and M18 -> M20.

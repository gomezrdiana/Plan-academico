## 6. BOOK M33 + BOARD RACE — 12 min — STAND UP

Write on the board (before class):

```
M33 — HOW MUCH? / SPECIFYING UNCOUNTABLE QUANTITIES
       (A2 Book p.283 — Guide 1 p.285)

From the book (Guide 1, p.285):
"1. 'how much...?' = '¿Cuanto...?'"
"2. For UNCOUNTABLE substances (liquids, powders,
    gases, etc.)"
"3. Measured by UNITS:
    'I have 1 liter of milk.'
    'I have 1 kilo of butter.'
    'I have 300 pesos.' (Money is uncountable;
     pesos/dollars are the units.)"
"4. Also with 'there is / is there':
    'How much milk is there?' → '¿Cuanta leche hay?'"
"5. 'a lot of' / 'lots of' = 'mucho' for uncountable.
    'I have a lot of money.' → 'Tengo mucha plata.'"
"6. 'a bit of...' = 'un poco de...'.
    'I have a bit of money.' → 'Tengo un poco de plata.'"

QUESTION = "how much" + UNCOUNTABLE noun
ANSWER:
  (a) UNIT: "1 liter of milk", "3 kilos of cheese",
            "300 pesos"
  (b) QUALITATIVE: "a bit of" / "a little" /
                   "a lot of / lots of"
"there IS" + uncountable; "there ARE" only with a
PLURAL UNIT ("there are 4 liters of milk").
Previous class (M32) was "how many" + COUNTABLE
plural — different lane.
(M31 does NOT exist — book jumps M30 → M32.)
```

Say it standing at the board: *"One word, one rule: 'how much' goes with things you CANNOT count one by one — money, time, water, sugar, coffee, energy, attention. Two ways to answer. ONE: a UNIT — '1 liter of milk', '3 kilos of pasta', '300 pesos'. TWO: a quality — 'a bit of', 'a lot of', 'lots of'. And 'there IS' goes with uncountable — never 'there are' alone with uncountable. Previous class we did 'how many' for things we COUNT — today is 'how much' for things we MEASURE."*

### BOARD RACE — 2 teams — 7 min

```
1. "How many money do you have?"        → "How MUCH money do you have?"

3. "I have a few of water."             → "I have a little water." / "a bit of water."

```

## 7. WALKING TRANSFORMATION — 16 min — STAND UP

Two zones in the room (tape labels on opposite walls):
- **WRONG zone (left):** "how many" with uncountable / "a few" with uncountable / "there are" + uncountable / bare number for uncountable
- **RIGHT zone (right):** correct "how much" + uncountable + UNIT or qualitative

Hand each student **ONE slip card** with a wrongly-formed professional sentence.

### Round 1 — Walk it — 8 min

Each student:
1. Stands in WRONG zone, reads slip aloud with the wrong form.
2. Walks to RIGHT zone shouting the corrected form.
3. Cohort confirms with thumbs.

Suggested slips:

```
"How many money do you have for materials?"
   → "How MUCH money do you have for materials? — I have 300 dollars."
"How many time is there before the meeting?"
   → "How MUCH time is there before the meeting? — There is a bit of time, about 15 minutes."
"I have a few of coffee in the office."
   → "I have a little coffee in the office." / "a bit of coffee."
"There are too much email noise today."
   → "There IS too much email noise today."
"How much meetings do you have today?"
   → "How MANY meetings do you have today? — I have 3 meetings." (CONTRAST — countable plural)
"How much information do we have? — 5."
   → "How much information do we have? — A lot." / "Not much yet."
"Coordination handles results — not me."
   → (limit reminder — leave correct as is)
```

### Round 2 — Reverse — 4 min

Swap slips. Each student starts in RIGHT, walks to WRONG saying out loud WHY it's wrong: *"'how many' is wrong with 'money' because 'money' is UNCOUNTABLE — you can't say 'three moneys'; the units are 'dollars', 'pesos'. 'a few' is for COUNTABLE plurals — uncountable uses 'a little' or 'a bit of'. 'there are' with uncountable is wrong — 'there IS' with uncountable. Book p.285."*

### Close — 2 min

2 volunteers walk their transformation in front of the group. Cohort counts correct pairs. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

## 10. PODIUM + SPEED DRILL — 10 min — STAND UP

### Podium "Confirmed — how much indeed!" — 4 min — circle

Each student (chain) names an honest "how much" of their work + answers with UNIT or qualitative. Cohort responds in chorus: *"Confirmed — how much indeed!"*. Cover the 3 answer types (UNIT / a bit of / a lot of) — min 1 student per type.

```
A: "How much coffee do I have this morning?
    — I have 1 cup of coffee."              (UNIT)
   → "Confirmed — how much indeed!"
B: "How much real time do I have this week?
    — A bit of time — about 3 hours."        (a bit of + UNIT)
   → "Confirmed — how much indeed!"
C: "How much information do we have?
    — We have a lot of information."         (a lot of)
   → "Confirmed — how much indeed!"
D: "How much energy does the team have?
    — Not much — a little."                  (qualitative)
   → "Confirmed — how much indeed!"
```

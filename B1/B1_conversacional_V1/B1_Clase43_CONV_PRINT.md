#### (4 min) Mini-talk — M11 in real workplace description (B1 Book p.127-144)

Talk standing. Book p.129 + p.133 + p.137 + p.141 open. Board behind you with 4-line skeleton:

```
M11 — 4 SHAPES (B1 Book p.127-144)
  (1) NOUN-ADJ PAIRS (p.129): health/healthy
  (2) PREFIX / SUFFIX (p.133): un-/in-/im-/ir-/dis-
                               -less/-ful/-able
  (3) COMPOUND (p.137): well-known · 20-page
  (4) IMPERSONAL (p.141): "It is + adj + that/to..."
```

> *"M11 has four shapes. ONE — NOUN-ADJECTIVE PAIRS. Same root, two shapes. 'Health' the abstract, 'healthy' the description. 'Bravery / brave', 'happiness / happy', 'strength / strong', 'intelligence / intelligent', 'talent / talented', 'caution / cautious', 'wisdom / wise', 'enthusiasm / enthusiastic'. TWO — PREFIXES + SUFFIXES. Prefixes UN- / IN- / IM- / IR- / DIS- mean NOT (un-happy, in-correct, im-polite, ir-responsible, dis-honest); MIS- means wrongly (mis-understood); RE- means again (re-do, re-build). Suffixes -LESS means WITHOUT (fearLESS, hopeLESS, careLESS); -FUL means FULL OF (colorFUL, fearFUL, helpFUL); -ABLE means CAN BE DONE (workABLE, comfortABLE). Book p.133 verbatim: 'Similar to Spanish, adding a few letters to the beginning (prefix) or end (suffix) of a word changes its meaning.' THREE — COMPOUND ADJECTIVES. Two words joined with a hyphen, one descriptor before the noun: 'a 20-PAGE report', 'a WELL-KNOWN author', 'a SELF-CONFIDENT girl', 'ICE-COLD beer', 'a 3-YEAR-OLD child' — note SINGULAR 'year', not 'years'. Book p.137 verbatim: 'we use two separate words to create a single descriptor.' FOUR — IMPERSONAL 'IT IS + ADJ' expressions. Book p.141 verbatim: 'Subjects can be omitted in Spanish, but never in English.' Two patterns: 'It is + adj + TO + infinitive' ('It is important TO help') OR 'It is + adj + THAT/WHEN + clause' ('It is great THAT you came', 'It is tiring WHEN we teach 8 hours'). NEVER drop the 'it'."*

### PART B — M12 ADVANCED PRONOUNS — ~20 min

#### (4 min) Mini-talk — M12 in real workplace dialogue (B1 Book p.145-155)

Talk standing. Book p.147 + p.151 + p.155 open. Board behind you:

```
M12 — 3 SHAPES (B1 Book p.145-155)
  (1) INDEFINITE (p.147): every-/some-/any-/no- +
      -one/-body/-where/-thing → 3rd person SINGULAR verb
  (2) REFLEXIVE (p.151): subject = object → -self/-selves
      INTENSIVE: "by + reflexive" = alone
  (3) RECIPROCAL (p.155):
      EACH OTHER (2 actors) · ONE ANOTHER (3+ actors)
```

> *"M12 has three shapes. ONE — INDEFINITE PRONOUNS. Four prefixes (every / some / any / no) × four endings (-one / -body / -where / -thing). Book p.147 verbatim: 'Indefinite pronouns are always used as a 3rd person singular even if they refer to multiples. CORRECT: Everyone IS here. INCORRECT: Everyone ARE here.' SOME-family = affirmative. ANY-family = negative or question. NO-family = absence with a positive verb shape ('no one IS here', NOT 'no one isn't here'). 'Any' also means 'cualquier' — 'anything can happen'. TWO — REFLEXIVE PRONOUNS. Book p.151 verbatim: 'When the subject and object of a verb are the same, we use the reflexive pronoun.' MYSELF / YOURSELF / HIMSELF / HERSELF / ITSELF / OURSELVES / YOURSELVES / THEMSELVES. 'I cut MYSELF.' 'She introduced HERSELF.' INTENSIVE use — 'by + reflexive' = alone. Book p.151 verbatim: 'I am eating BY MYSELF = estoy comiendo solo.' THREE — RECIPROCAL PRONOUNS. Book p.155 verbatim: 'Each other = 2 actors. One another = 3 or more actors.' 'The CTO and the VP supported EACH OTHER.' 'The cohort taught ONE ANOTHER all year.' Three traps: (a) reflexive where object pronoun belongs — 'My manager invited my peer and ME', NOT 'myself'. (b) reflexive where reciprocal is meant — 'we supported EACH OTHER' (between separate actors), not 'we supported OURSELVES' (that means the group acted on itself). (c) plural verb with indefinite — 'EVERYONE IS', not 'everyone are'."*

#### (8 min) Walking pair drilling — M12 3 stations

- **INDEFINITE (every/some/any/no)**
- **REFLEXIVE (-self / -selves OR by + reflexive)**
- **RECIPROCAL (each other / one another)**

Pairs WALK to the right station + speak the full sentence. 12 prompts in 8 min.

```
Prompt called                                                 → Right station + sample full sentence
1.  "All people in the kickoff are ready."                    → INDEFINITE  — "EVERYONE IS ready." (singular verb)
2.  "I prepared for the meeting alone."                       → REFLEXIVE   — "I prepared MYSELF for the meeting."
3.  "The CTO and the VP supported each other through audit."  → RECIPROCAL  — "The CTO and the VP supported EACH OTHER through the audit."
4.  "No person in the office today."                          → INDEFINITE  — "NO ONE is in the office today."
5.  "She introduced — to the team."                           → REFLEXIVE   — "She introduced HERSELF to the team."
6.  "The team and the PM helped one another all quarter."     → RECIPROCAL  — "The team and the PM helped ONE ANOTHER all quarter."
7.  "I have — in my hand."                                    → INDEFINITE  — "I have SOMETHING in my hand."
8.  "They finished the deck alone, no help."                  → REFLEXIVE   — "They finished the deck BY THEMSELVES."
9.  "Sam, Biff, and Fred all hit each other."                 → RECIPROCAL  — "Sam, Biff, and Fred hit ONE ANOTHER." (3+ → one another)
10. "Is — in the office today?" (question)                    → INDEFINITE  — "Is ANYONE in the office today?"
11. "He hurts only — at work."                                → REFLEXIVE   — "He hurts only HIMSELF at work."
```

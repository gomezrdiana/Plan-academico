## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — reported speech in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model EACH pattern out loud.

> *"M8 has two halves. HALF ONE — SAY vs TELL vs ASK. SAY does NOT take an indirect object directly — 'She SAID the deck was ready' (no 'me'). If you want to add a listener, use 'SAID TO me' — 'She said TO me that the deck was ready.' TELL takes the indirect object directly — 'She TOLD ME the deck was ready' (no 'to'). ASK = preguntar, also takes indirect object directly — 'She ASKED ME if the deck was ready'. Book p.89 verbatim: SAY = direct words; TELL = information communicated to a person; ASK = preguntar, requires indirect object. NEVER 'She told that' (TELL needs a person). NEVER 'She said me' (SAY needs TO me or no listener). HALF TWO — TENSE BACK-SHIFT. When the control verb (said/told/asked) is PAST, the verb in the reported clause shifts ONE STEP BACK. Book p.93 verbatim table: simple present → simple past ('he plays' → 'he played'); simple past → past perfect ('he played' → 'he had played'); present perfect → past perfect ('he has played' → 'he had played'); present continuous → past continuous ('he is playing' → 'he was playing'); am/is/are going to → was/were going to; will → would ('he will play' → 'he would play'); can → could; must → had to. PRONOUNS shift too — 'I' → 'he/she'; 'we' → 'they'; 'you' → 'I/we' depending on direction. TIME and PLACE shift — 'today' → 'that day'; 'tomorrow' → 'the next day'; 'here' → 'there'. YES/NO QUESTIONS in reported speech use IF or WHETHER — 'Can we ship?' → 'She asked IF we could ship.' WH-QUESTIONS keep the WH and use AFFIRMATIVE order (same rule as M7 embedded questions) — 'When is the deck due?' → 'She asked WHEN the deck WAS due' (NOT 'when WAS the deck due'). Prudence day 1: open the block by reporting cleanly — no paraphrasing into your own opinion, no losing the original tense, no skipping the back-shift."*

Demo each rule on the same situation twice. Then:

> *"Most workplace recaps slip in three places. SAID with no shift ('she said the client IS satisfied' — wrong, must be WAS). TOLD with no listener ('She told that' — wrong). Yes/no reported with no IF ('She asked could we ship' — needs IF). Hear it, kill it."*

### (15 min) Guided practice in pairs — 12 prompt cards (peer teacher rotation embedded)

Hand each pair the deck of 12 cards (each card has a DIRECT quote in a speech bubble + a label saying who spoke). They take turns: one reads the card, the other reports it in past with right verb + right back-shift. Then swap. Walk between pairs.

Card deck (each card = direct quote + speaker label):
```
1.  Client: "I am sending the SOW today."             → reported (SAY)
2.  Manager (to me): "We have approved the budget."   → reported (TELL me)
3.  Dev lead: "Can the QA team start Monday?"         → reported (ASK + IF)
4.  PM: "I will draft the deck tonight."              → reported (SAY)
5.  Director: "The deck is meticulous."               → reported (SAY)
6.  Client (to me): "When does the demo happen?"      → reported (ASK me + WH affirmative order)
7.  Teammate: "I should have escalated yesterday."    → reported (SAY) — perfect modal does NOT shift
8.  Manager: "You must finish by Friday."             → reported (SAY) — MUST → HAD TO
9.  Client: "We are choosing your bid."               → reported (SAY) — pres cont → past cont
10. PM (to me): "Have you reviewed the contract?"     → reported (ASK me + IF + pres perf → past perf)
11. Director: "The team performed beautifully."       → reported (SAY) — past simple → past perfect
12. Manager (to me): "I am proud of you."             → reported (TELL me) — pronoun shift + pres cont → past cont
```

Coach softly: SAID + me (wrong), TOLD with no person, yes/no with no IF, WH-question with inversion ('when DID' inside report), failure to shift IS → WAS, pronoun left at 'I' when speaker is no longer present. Note errors with NAMES.

**Peer teacher inside this block (3 min):** halfway through, pause one pair and have ONE student read aloud the Grammar pase patterns from previous class M7 — "where IS the deck due" → "where the deck IS due" / "what DOES the client need" → "what the client NEEDS" / "missing IF on yes/no embed". Cohort echoes 2×. Bridge: *"M7 embedded questions use affirmative order; M8 reported WH-questions use the SAME affirmative order — plus back-shift. 'My manager asked WHEN the deck WAS due' (M7 affirmative + M8 back-shift)."* Resume pair work.

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Reporter delivers 6 reports back to back. Manager interrupts twice for clarification. Observer marks. You circulate — coach SAID/TOLD confusion on the spot, kill any inversion inside a WH-report, flag missing back-shift ("the client said it IS — should be WAS"), flag paraphrasing.

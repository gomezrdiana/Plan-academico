## 6. HOT TOPIC — "DRESS FOR THE ROLE, NOT THE SHOW" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural-lexical (describing clothing, adjective placement). Today is pure speaking. The topic: your REAL dress code at work — not aspirational, not a fashion show. What actually WORKS for a client meeting, a presentation, field work, a remote-office day?"*

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "Dress for the Role, Not the Show"
(TEMPERANCE day 1: the right amount, on purpose.)

Discuss with your partner — in ENGLISH ONLY:

1. Name what YOU are wearing right now — with colors
   and adjectives in their place. Use "I am wearing
   ___ ." or "I have ___ .":
   "I am wearing a dark blue shirt, black pants, and
    brown shoes. I have a brown belt and a black bag."

2. Pick 3 REAL work situations from your job (client
   meeting / internal presentation / field work /
   remote office day) and name what WORKS in each —
   the BALANCED choice, not too flashy, not too sloppy:
   "For a client meeting, a clean white shirt and
    dark pants is the right amount. A flashy red
    jacket is too much; a wrinkled T-shirt is too
    little."

3. Name ONE work outfit choice that LOOKED like the
   right amount but wasn't (too flashy / too sloppy /
   wrong code) — and what the BALANCED version
   would have been.

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ uses of "I am wearing ___" or "I have ___" with
  CORRECT ADJECTIVE ORDER (adj BEFORE noun, no plural
  on adjective: "red shirts" NOT "reds shirts")
- 3 COLORS from the M36 list (black, white, red,
  blue, green, yellow, brown, gray, pink, purple,
  silver, dark X, light X)
- 3 SPECIFIC professional situations
- 1 sentence about the BALANCED amount

NO brands as performance. Real, professional.
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

### Plenary — 3 min

2 students share what they would wear for a specific professional situation + why that's the right AMOUNT.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Listen to how you just said it: 'a CLEAN DARK shirt', 'a WRINKLED T-shirt is too little', 'a FLASHY red jacket is too much'. The adjective went BEFORE the noun. Always. That has a name — Juan Diego gives it this afternoon (M36)."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE PROFESSIONAL WARDROBE BRIEFING" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. You stand in front of your team and brief them on (a) what you're wearing NOW and (b) what you would wear for 3 specific professional contexts — with colors, adjectives in their place, and 'wearing / having'. Calm, professional, balanced."*

Board:

```
"THE PROFESSIONAL WARDROBE BRIEFING" — 3–4 min each

Stand up. Brief your team in 4 parts:

PART 1 — NOW (the right amount today):
   "Right now I am wearing a dark blue shirt, black
    pants, and brown shoes. I have a brown belt and
    a black bag. This is the right amount for today
    — a learning day, not a client day."

PART 2 — CLIENT MEETING (high-stakes balance):
   "For an important client meeting, I would wear
    a clean white shirt, dark grey pants, and black
    shoes. I would have a dark grey jacket. No
    flashy colors — not too much. No wrinkles —
    not too little."

PART 3 — INTERNAL PRESENTATION (calm authority):
   "For an internal presentation to my team, I
    would wear a light blue shirt and dark pants —
    a calm color, clear voice. I have a black
    notebook in hand."

PART 4 — FIELD WORK / REMOTE DAY (real and ready):
   "For a field-work day, I am wearing jeans, a
    green T-shirt, sneakers, and a jacket. The
    right amount for that role — not a suit, not
    pajamas."

USE: 6+ items from M36 vocab (shirt, pants, shoes,
     belt, jacket, jeans, T-shirt, sneakers, sweater,
     skirt, dress, bag, purse, hat, leggings, tie,
     glasses, socks, sandals, necklace, earrings,
     shorts) / 4+ colors with correct order / "dark
     X" or "light X" at least 2× / professional ONLY
     / temperate ("right amount") tone
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me brief you on my wardrobe choices for the week. Right now I am wearing a dark blue shirt, black pants, and brown shoes. I have a brown belt. This is the right amount for today, a learning day. For a client meeting, I would wear a clean white shirt, dark grey pants, and black shoes. I would have a dark grey jacket. No flashy colors, no wrinkles. For an internal review, I am wearing a light blue shirt with dark pants — a calm color and a clear voice. For field work, jeans, a green T-shirt, sneakers and a jacket. One thing: if anyone asks me about midterm results or how the Final will be graded, that goes only to coordination."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A presents the wardrobe briefing (4 parts); B is team/manager + counts M36 items + colors + adjective-before-noun + whether AMOUNT was temperate.

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their briefing. Cohort counts M36 vocab + colors + correct adjective placement. Note errors with NAMES.

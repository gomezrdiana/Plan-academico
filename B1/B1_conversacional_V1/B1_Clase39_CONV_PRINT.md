## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — connectors in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model EACH pattern out loud.

> *"Connectors in real workplace dialogue — three things to land. ONE: CLAUSE-RULE versus NOUN-RULE. CLAUSE-RULE connectors take a full sentence after them — 'BECAUSE the budget was delayed, I compressed the spec' / 'SINCE the budget was delayed' / 'AS the budget was delayed' / 'EVEN THOUGH the pressure was real' / 'ALTHOUGH the pressure was real' / 'HOWEVER, the team absorbed'. NOUN-RULE connectors take a noun phrase — 'BECAUSE OF the delayed budget' / 'DUE TO the delayed budget' / 'THANKS TO the dev team' / 'IN SPITE OF the pressure' / 'DESPITE the pressure'. NEVER mix. NOT 'because of the budget WAS delayed' (clause after a noun-rule). NOT 'despite OF' (despite already absorbs the 'of'). TWO: COMMAS. When HOWEVER opens a new clause after a period or a semicolon, it carries a comma — 'We compressed the spec. HOWEVER, the team absorbed it.' When SO opens a result, comma before — 'We compressed the spec, SO QA had a tighter window.' THREE — and this is the speaking layer the form alone misses — DON'T DROP THE CONSEQUENCE. Three causes + three contrasts and no consequence = a complaint, not a debrief. Fortitude day 4 in this room: 'cause, contrast, consequence — same breath, the line held.'"*

Demo each rule shape on the same situation twice. Then:

> *"Most workplace memos slip in the SAME three places. Cause+noun gets confused with cause+clause. Despite picks up a phantom 'of'. The consequence gets eaten because the speaker keeps editorialising the contrast. Fortitude day 4: hold the line — three connectors, three rules, no drop."*

### (15 min) Guided practice in pairs — 12 prompt cards (peer teacher rotation embedded)

Hand each pair the deck of 12 statement cards. They take turns: one reads the FIRST CLAUSE, the other transforms it into the SECOND CLAUSE using the connector listed. Then swap. Walk between pairs. Mark wrong shape physically with your hand (cross over for "wrong rule").

Card deck:
```
1.  "The QA capacity was full." → use BECAUSE + clause
2.  "The QA capacity was full." → use BECAUSE OF + noun (rephrase)
3.  "The QA capacity was full." → use DUE TO + noun (formal)
4.  "The budget cleared Wednesday." → use SINCE + clause
5.  "We rolled the release." + "the client accepted the delay." → use HOWEVER
6.  "The QA window was tight." + "no critical bug escaped." → use EVEN THOUGH
7.  "The rolled release." + "the client signed." → use DESPITE + noun
8.  "The timeline pressure." + "we shipped." → use IN SPITE OF + noun
9.  "The client accepted delivery." + "we moved infra next week." → use SO
10. "The release date held." + "the client signed without renegotiation." → use THAT'S WHY
11. "The dev team finished Wednesday." + "we shipped Friday." → use AS + clause
12. "We logged extra hours Thursday." + "the launch was clean." → use ON THE OTHER HAND
```

Coach softly while you walk: wrong rule (clause after noun-rule connector), missing comma after HOWEVER, "despite OF", stacked contrasts ("although however"), dropped consequence. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

**Peer teacher inside this block (3 min):** halfway through, pause one pair and have ONE student read aloud the Grammar pase patterns from previous class M5 — "I would OF asked" → "I would HAVE asked" / "I should HAD asked" → "I should HAVE asked" / "I wish I would have gone" → "I wish I HAD gone". Cohort echoes 2×. Then: *"M5 closes there. M6 today connects past, present, future events with cause and contrast — and you'll often combine the two: 'BECAUSE OF the missed timeline (M6), I SHOULD HAVE asked for help (M5)'."* Resume pair work.

### (15 min) Free pair derivation — "project debrief" exchanges

Same pairs, no cards. Build 5 short exchanges each. Pattern: project lead opens with cause + contrast, peer asks for consequence, project lead names it cleanly.

Board (write before class):


## BLOCK 3 — LIVE APPLICATION — 45 min — "PROJECT DEBRIEF MEMO"

ONE activity, no format change for 45 minutes. Rotating triads. Every student plays every role.

### (3 min) Setup
> *"Stand. You're project leads delivering a quarterly debrief memo OUT LOUD. You'll work in triads — three roles, rotating. PROJECT LEAD opens with 3 cause connectors (1 BECAUSE clause + 1 BECAUSE OF noun + 1 DUE TO noun) + 3 contrast connectors (1 HOWEVER with comma + 1 EVEN THOUGH + 1 DESPITE) + 1 consequence (SO or THAT'S WHY) + 1 forward step. SILENT TEAM MEMBER nods + adds one brief uptake — 'I take infrastructure' / 'I own the budget ask'. OBSERVER tracks on the printed grid: how many connector slots filled correctly, how many trap shapes appeared, whether the consequence was named or dropped. Three rounds. Each round 12 minutes. Every student plays every role exactly once. Fortitude day 4: hold the line — no drop under pressure."*

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Roles assigned. Lead delivers full memo. Silent team member nods + 1 uptake. Observer marks grid. **Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate. New lead, new silent member, new observer.

## 5. PREPARACIÓN FINAL DEL MIDTERM (00:18–00:35, 17 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Warm up your voice with the four shapes that hold your story — comparatives, superlatives, measurements, quantities. Breathe. Read your 5-word card. Set the room for hospitality."*

### Setup (2 min) — DE PIE en círculo

> *"Everyone stands. Five-word card in your hand — only five words, no full sentences. We warm up four shapes in chain: equal comparatives, unequal comparatives, superlatives, measurement questions, quantities. Then we breathe. Then we set the room. No one starts cold."*

### Warm-Up Integrador en cadena (8 min) — DE PIE

**Tablero (escribe ANTES):**

```
WARM-UP CHAIN — fast, in your mouth, no perfection

ROUND 1 — Equal comparative about your journey:
  "My English now is as strong as my Spanish 5 years ago."
ROUND 2 — Unequal comparative about your journey:
  "My speaking is much more confident than 3 months ago."
ROUND 3 — Superlative about your journey:
  "The hardest week was the one I learned the most in."
ROUND 4 — Measurement question about your progress:
  "How much progress have I really made? Enough to stand
   here today."
ROUND 5 — Quantity statement about your resources:
  "I don't have many perfect words, but I have enough
   honest ones to tell my story."
```

Profesora dice ROUND 1, cada estudiante en cadena suelta su frase ROUND 1. Luego ROUND 2 en cadena. Y así hasta ROUND 5. ENGLISH ONLY. Sin perfección — la meta es activar el músculo, no corregir.

### Respiración + tarjeta (4 min) — DE PIE

1. (1.5 min) **Respiración 4-4-6:** inhala 4 seg, retén 4 seg, exhala 6 seg × 4 ciclos. En silencio.
2. (1.5 min) **Tarjeta clave:** cada uno mira su tarjeta (max 5 palabras), la lee en silencio 3 veces, la guarda en el bolsillo.
3. (1 min) **Anclaje físico:** *"Pies firmes. Hombros abajo. Mirada al fondo del salón, no al piso. La tarjeta está en el bolsillo si la necesitas — pero la mirada está al frente."*

### Hospitalidad del cohorte (3 min) — DE PIE en U

> *"While someone presents, the rest of us are the audience. Three rules. One: silence — phones away, no whispering. Two: eye contact — you watch the person, not the floor. Three: at the end, you applaud. Then one of you asks ONE honest question — about the content, the goal, the story. Not about how they did it. Not about how 'nervous' they looked. Honest, content-question only. I will ask too. Three questions max per presentation."*

> **Inserción #2 Frase del Día:** *"Remember — 'Prudence is presenting what is true, not what is shiny.' That goes for the speaker AND the audience. We honor what is true."*

## 8. ERROR PAPER LIVE (01:35–01:45, 10 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA EL COHORTE:**

### Setup (1 min)

> *"I wrote down 4-5 phrases I heard during the presentations and the Q&A — Spanish-influenced. No names attached, no presentation attached. We fix them as a team. This is how we get better — together, in public, without shame."*

### Errores típicos esperados en midterm (ajusta a lo realmente escuchado)

**4-5 errores en tablero (sin nombres):**

```
1. ❌ "I have much friends in my work team."
   → ✅ "I have MANY friends in my work team."
      (countable -> "many", no "much")

2. ❌ "My progress is more big than last month."
   → ✅ "My progress is BIGGER than last month."
      (1 syllable -> "-er than", not "more")

3. ❌ "This was the most hard project of the year."
   → ✅ "This was THE HARDEST project of the year."
      (1 syllable -> "the -est", not "the most")

4. ❌ "I depend of my team to grow."
   → ✅ "I depend ON my team to grow."
      ("depend ON", not "depend of")

5. ❌ "I am here for share my story with you."
   → ✅ "I am here TO SHARE my story with you."
      (infinitive of purpose -> "to + verb", not "for + verb")
```

### Lectura + corrección (7 min)

Lee cada error. Cohorte (de pie) propone la corrección. Coro 2x. Tú confirmas la regla en 1 línea.

### Cierre (2 min)

> *"These are the slips your Spanish brings into your English. Today you presented anyway, and they did not stop you. Juan Diego will hear similar ones this afternoon — that is normal. The work is to NAME them, not to hide them. Prudence."*

> Los **nombres y citas literales** van en tu libreta personal, NUNCA en el tablero. Cosecha de hoy va a coordinacion en el reporte.

## 7. SIMULACIÓN — "THE RECOMMENDATION BRIEFING" (00:42–01:02, 20 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Stand up. You recommend ONE of two options to a colleague or a client — two providers, two plans, two shifts, two tools. You explain WHY it is better or worse using unequal comparatives the whole time, honestly."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Estructura sugerida (Tablero):**

```
"THE RECOMMENDATION BRIEFING" — 3-4 min each

You compare Option A and Option B and recommend ONE. Give
6-7 honest judgements. Use UNEQUAL COMPARATIVES:

POINT 1: "Option A is better than Option B."
POINT 2: "Option B is more expensive than Option A."
POINT 3: "The paid plan is faster than the free plan."
POINT 4: "This supplier is more reliable than the other one."
POINT 5: "The night shift is harder than the morning one."
POINT 6: "Honestly, the premium tool is stronger than the
          cheaper one — but it's also more expensive."

USE: 6+ unequal comparatives (-er than / more ... than /
     better/worse than) / professional context ONLY / honest
```

### Modela (3 min)

> *"Let me brief you on two options for our printing. Supplier A is better than Supplier B for deadlines, but Supplier B is cheaper than Supplier A. The premium paper is heavier and more professional than the standard paper, so for client work it's the better choice. One thing: if a teacher asks anything about evaluations, midterms or finals, you do not handle that — it goes only to coordination, never to us. Back to the options: I won't oversell it; honestly, Option A is better than Option B but more expensive than it — that's the fair recommendation, and being honest about the trade-off is the strong thing to do."*

### Round 1 — Parejas (10 min)

Cada pareja: 5 min cada una. Persona A presenta las dos opciones y recomienda una, B es el colega/cliente + cuenta cuántos comparativos desiguales usó A y si fueron honestos (¿infló al ganador? ¿escondió el costo?).

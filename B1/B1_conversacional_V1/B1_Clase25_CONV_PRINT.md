## 6. HOT TOPIC — "WHAT DOES IT REALLY COST?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — today's Grammar topic is structural (describing prices). The topic: name the REAL price of three things at your work — a tool, a service, an hour of something. Honest prices, not what you wish they cost. Previous class we asked 'how much money' (uncountable); today we ask 'how much does it cost'."*

### Pairs debate — 12 min

Board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "WHAT DOES IT REALLY COST?"

Discuss with your partner:

1. Pick THREE real things at your work. Give the
   HONEST PRICE using "how much does ___ cost?" +
   answer. Mix the two structures:
   "How much does the new software cost?
    — It costs about 30 dollars a month."
   "How much is one box of supplies?
    — It's 12 dollars."
   "How much do the spare parts cost?
    — They cost 5 dollars each."

2. Pick ONE thing that LOOKS cheap but really isn't
   — the hidden cost: "The free trial looks free,
   but it costs about 4 hours of setup time."

3. "Be honest — don't sell a discount that doesn't
   exist." Why does naming the REAL price matter at
   work (budgets, decisions, planning)?

Requirements (12 min):
- 6+ price questions ("How much does ___ cost?" /
  "How much is/are ___ ?")
- 6+ price answers ("It costs ___" / "It's ___" /
  "They cost ___ each" / "They're ___ each")
- 3 SPECIFIC items from your real work
- 1 sentence about a HIDDEN cost (PRUDENCE day 5:
  not the discount you wish for)
```

Pairs. English only. 12 min.

### Plenary — 3 min

2 students share a professional real price question + answer + why honesty matters.

**Insertion #2 of Phrase of the Day:** *"Listen to your phrases: 'How much DOES the software COST?', 'How much IS the box?', 'How much DO the parts COST?'. That structure has a name. Juan Diego gives it this afternoon."*

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE PRICE BRIEFING" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. You stand in front of your team and brief them on what next quarter's resources will COST — in dollars, in hours, in real terms. You use price questions and answers the whole time, honestly."*

Board:

```
"THE PRICE BRIEFING" — 3–4 min each

You brief your team on 5–6 honest prices of next
quarter's resources. Use questions and answers:

POINT 1: "How much does the new software cost?
          — It costs 30 dollars per user per month."
POINT 2: "How much do the training sessions cost?
          — They cost 200 dollars each."
POINT 3: "How much is the transport for the team
          retreat? — It's about 450 dollars."
POINT 4: "How much do the spare parts cost?
          — They cost 12 dollars each, and we need 8."
POINT 5: "How much does ONE hour of overtime cost
          us, really? — It costs about 25 dollars
          plus the energy."
POINT 6: "Honest hidden cost: the free trial looks
          free, but it costs about 4 hours of setup
          time — that's the real price, not the
          discount."

USE: 6+ price Qs / 6+ price answers / professional
     ONLY / honest (no wished discounts)
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me brief you on the quarter. How much does the new ticketing system cost? It costs 30 dollars per user per month — that's 360 dollars a year for one person. How much do the certification courses cost? They cost 200 dollars each, and we plan to do two — 400 dollars. How much is the team retreat transport? It's about 450 dollars, all in. How much does ONE overtime hour really cost? About 25 dollars, plus the energy of the next morning, which is the hidden price. One thing: if a teammate asks about evaluations, midterm or final, you do not handle that — it goes only to coordination. Back to prices: the free trial looks free, but it costs four setup hours — that's the real price, not the discount."*

### Round 1 — Pairs — 10 min

Each pair: 5 min each. Person A presents the price briefing (5–6 real prices); B is team/manager + counts price Qs and As, and whether they were honest.

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their price briefing. Cohort counts. Note errors with NAMES in your personal notebook.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### (5 min) Mini-talk — tags in real workplace dialogue

Talk standing, board behind you. Model EACH pattern out loud.

> *"Question tags in real work dialogue — three things to land. ONE: same auxiliary as the principal statement. If the statement says 'sent' (past simple) the tag pulls 'didn't' — 'You sent the email, didn't you?'. If the statement says 'isn't' (to be negative) the tag stays in to be positive — 'She isn't coming, is she?'. TWO: opposite sign. Affirmative statement → negative tag. Negative statement → affirmative tag. THREE — and this is the speaking layer the form alone misses — INTONATION. Rising intonation = genuine question, I don't actually know — 'You sent the email, didn't you?↗'. Falling intonation = I expect confirmation, I already know — 'You sent the email, didn't you?↘'. Watch my hand. Up = real ask. Down = expected confirm. The same words, two completely different professional moves."*

Demo both intonations on the same sentence twice. Mark with your hand visibly. Then:

> *"Most workplace tags are falling — you're confirming alignment, not interrogating. Rising tags are for when you genuinely need information. Justice day 4: don't load a falling tag with blame — 'you messed up, didn't you?↘' is not a confirmation, that's accusation disguised. Fair confirmation only."*

### (15 min) Guided practice in pairs — 12 prompt cards

Hand each pair the deck of 12 statement cards. They take turns: one reads the statement, the other adds the correct tag with the correct intonation (↗ or ↘). Then swap roles. Walk between pairs and physically mark intonation with your hand — up or down — so the cohort sees it from across the room.

```
1.  "You confirmed the budget."
2.  "The client signed yesterday."
3.  "Maria sent the deck Monday."
4.  "We can ship by Friday."
5.  "The QA team isn't done with the audit."
6.  "She is leading the standup."
7.  "They haven't reviewed the mockups."
8.  "You won't push to production tonight."
9.  "I am presenting third in the rehearsal."
10. "The dev team deploys on Fridays."
11. "Andres played soccer in college."
12. "Edith will take the new role."
```

Coach softly while you walk: wrong auxiliary, wrong polarity, flat intonation, "isn't it?" misused. Note errors with NAMES in your notebook.

### (15 min) Free pair derivation — "office confirmation" exchanges

Same pairs, no cards. Build 5 short exchanges each. Pattern: statement + falling tag (expecting confirmation) → partner confirms or disconfirms in one sentence.

Board (write before class):
```
FREE PAIR DERIVATION — 5 EXCHANGES (15 min)

Pattern:
  A: [statement + FALLING tag (↘ expecting confirm)]
  B: [confirm or disconfirm in one sentence]

Examples:
  A: "You sent the audit Thursday, didn't you?↘"
  B: "Yes, I sent it Thursday afternoon."

  A: "The client isn't on holiday next week, is he?↘"
  B: "No, he's back Monday — we're clear."

Requirements:
  - 5 exchanges total per pair
  - 3 with falling tag (confirmation) + 2 with
    rising tag (real question)
  - At least 1 with a modal (can / will / should)
  - At least 1 with past simple (pull-out did)
  - At least 1 negative statement + positive tag
  - JUSTICE day 4: no loaded blame tags
```

Rotate partners twice during this 15 min so each student speaks to at least 3 different people. Walk and coach. Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2: *"Same auxiliary, opposite sign — fast and fair. Justice day 4 — confirm before deciding."*

### (~40 min) 3 rounds × ~12 min — rotating triads

**Round 1 (12 min):** Triads form. Roles assigned. PM opens, runs 8 tag-checks, closes with a status statement. Subordinate answers each. Observer marks the grid. You circulate triad by triad — coach the PM on intonation with your hand (up / down), flag wrong auxiliary on the spot, flag any loaded blame tag immediately ("Justice day 4 — fair confirmation, not accusation").

**Round 2 (12 min):** Rotate. New PM, new subordinate, new observer. Same 8-tag structure. By now the cohort knows the rhythm — push for SPEED. Tags should land in 2 seconds, not 6.

**Round 3 (12 min):** Final rotation. Every student has now played every role. Push for SPECIAL CASES — at least one PM in each triad must use "I am → aren't I", or "let's → shall we", or an imperative tag.

## BLOCK 2 — CONTENT OF THE DAY — 40 min

### PART A — M9 PERFECT TENSES — ~20 min

#### (3 min) Mini-talk — perfect tenses in service history

Talk standing, board behind you.

> *"M9 has three shapes to land. ONE — PRESENT PERFECT vs PAST SIMPLE. Present perfect = an action with a duration touching now or with relevance now — 'We HAVE RUN two regressions THIS WEEK' (week is not over). Past simple = an action completely finished in a finished time — 'We RAN two regressions LAST WEEK' (last week is over). Time markers: present perfect with EVER / NEVER / SINCE / FOR / YET / ALREADY / JUST / SO FAR / THIS WEEK / RECENTLY / LATELY. Past simple with YESTERDAY / LAST WEEK / IN 2024 / WHEN I WAS A KID. FOR vs SINCE: FOR + period of time ('for three weeks'); SINCE + point in time ('since Monday'). HOW LONG questions get either — 'How long have you worked here?' → 'FOR five years' / 'SINCE 2021'. TWO — PAST PERFECT. The action that finished BEFORE another past action — 'When the client called, the team HAD ALREADY FIXED the bug.' Two past events; past perfect goes first chronologically, past simple second. THREE — PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS. The duration of an ongoing action that started in the past and continues now — 'We HAVE BEEN MIGRATING the database FOR three weeks.' (we're still migrating). Versus present perfect simple — 'We HAVE MIGRATED 60% of the database.' (focus on result, completed portion). Prudence day 2: pick the right shape for the right service history — don't smear it all into past simple."*

#### (12 min) Guided practice in pairs — 10 M9 cards

Hand each pair the deck of 10 M9 cards.

```
1. "Two regressions / this week (not over) / present perfect"
2. "Two regressions / last week / past simple"
3. "Three weeks of migration work / continues now / pres perf continuous"
4. "60% of migration / completed so far / pres perfect simple"
5. "Bug fixed / before the client called / past perfect + past simple"
6. "Five years here / present perfect with FOR"
7. "Here since 2021 / present perfect with SINCE"
8. "I have known her since 2015 / question with HOW LONG"
9. "Two demos this Q3 / two demos in Q2 / contrast pres perf vs past simple"
10. "Reviewed the doc / before submitting / past perfect"
```

Coach: present perfect where past simple should be (or vice versa), missing past perfect when two past events are sequenced, FOR + point in time / SINCE + period (swap), present perfect continuous misused for completed counts. Note errors with NAMES.

**Peer teacher inside this block (3 min):** halfway through, pause one pair and have ONE student read aloud the Grammar pase patterns from previous class M8 — "she said me" → "she said TO me / she TOLD me" / "the client said it IS" → "the client said it WAS" (back-shift) / "asked could we ship" → "asked IF we COULD ship". Cohort echoes 2×. Bridge: *"M8 back-shift uses past perfect when you report a present perfect or past simple — 'the team SAID they HAD migrated 60%' (originally: 'we have migrated' or 'we migrated'). That's the past perfect from M9 doing the job for M8."* Resume pair work.

#### (2 min) M9 close
Cohort fast-fires 3 distinctions: 'FOR or SINCE three weeks?' (FOR), 'FOR or SINCE Monday?' (SINCE), 'past perfect when?' (action before another past action).

### PART B — M10 THE CAUSATIVE — ~15 min

#### (3 min) Mini-talk — the causative in service history

Talk standing, board behind you.

> *"M10 has two halves. HALF ONE — ACTIVE CAUSATIVE. You make somebody else do something. Four control verbs. HAVE + object + BASE VERB — 'I HAD the QA team RUN the regression.' (no TO). MAKE + object + BASE VERB — 'My manager MADE us DOCUMENT the migration.' (no TO — force / obligation). LET + object + BASE VERB — 'My manager LET us TAKE a break.' (no TO — permission). GET + object + TO + BASE VERB — 'I GOT my peer TO review the deck.' (WITH TO — persuade / convince, less direct than HAVE). HALF TWO — PASSIVE CAUSATIVE. Somebody else does something FOR your object. Two control verbs. HAVE + object + PAST PARTICIPLE — 'I HAD the deck REVIEWED by my peer.' GET + object + PAST PARTICIPLE — 'We GOT the staging server UPGRADED.' Same shape. Use HAVE for arranged / formal services; use GET for more colloquial / arranged-with-effort services. The TRAP: mixing the two shapes — 'I had the deck PEER REVIEW it' (mixed active subject with passive form — wrong). Correct: 'I had MY PEER review the deck' (active, my peer is the object) OR 'I had THE DECK reviewed' (passive, the deck is the object). Prudence day 2: name what you did AND name what was done for you — both shapes, clean."*

#### (10 min) Guided practice in pairs — 8 M10 cards

Hand each pair the deck of 8 M10 cards.

```
1. "QA team / run regression / active causative HAVE"      → "I HAD the QA team RUN the regression." (no TO)
2. "Us / document the script / active causative MAKE"      → "My manager MADE us DOCUMENT the script." (no TO)
3. "Us / take a break / active causative LET"              → "My manager LET us TAKE a break." (no TO)
4. "My peer / review the deck / active causative GET"      → "I GOT my peer TO review the deck." (with TO)
5. "Deck / reviewed by my peer / passive causative HAVE"   → "I HAD the deck REVIEWED by my peer."
6. "Staging server / upgraded / passive causative GET"     → "We GOT the staging server UPGRADED."
7. "System / audited last quarter / passive causative HAVE" → "We HAD the system AUDITED last quarter."
8. "Slides / printed for the demo / passive causative GET"  → "We GOT the slides PRINTED for the demo."
```

Coach: GET without TO (in active), MAKE/LET with TO (wrong), passive causative with base verb instead of participle ('had the deck REVIEW'), mixed active/passive ('had the deck peer review it'). Note errors with NAMES.

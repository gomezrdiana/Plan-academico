## 6. HOT TOPIC — "HOW DO PEOPLE DO THEIR WORK?" — 17 min — PAIRS

### Setup — 2 min

> *"No text today — Grammar topic is structural (adverbs of manner), not narrative. So today is pure speaking. HOW your colleagues do the work. Juan Diego names the table this morning — book p.342: 'Usamos adverbios de manera para describir cómo se hace una acción.' Most adverbs are adjective + -ly (carefully, quickly, clearly, slowly, beautifully, intelligently, suddenly, immediately, completely, easily, seriously, happily, sadly). Irregulars: WELL (not 'goodly'), FAST (not 'fastly'), HARD (not 'hardly' — that means almost-not), LATE, EARLY. Position p.342: 'después del verbo principal o después del objeto directo, si hay.' So: 'I play WELL' (after verb) / 'He plays soccer WELL' (after object)."*

Sample patterns:
- *"She organizes the data CAREFULLY."* (regular -ly, after object)
- *"He responds to clients QUICKLY."* (regular -ly, after object)
- *"They speak CLEARLY in the daily standup."* (regular -ly, after verb + complement)
- *"The new hire works HARD."* (irregular, after verb)
- *"The team lead arrives EARLY every Monday."* (irregular, after verb)
- *"The senior dev codes SLOWLY but BEAUTIFULLY."* (two -ly adverbs)
- *"We delivered the demo WELL."* (irregular — not "goodly")
- *"She drives FAST."* (irregular — not "fastly")

### Hot Topic debate — 12 min

Write on the board:

```
HOT TOPIC — "HOW DO PEOPLE DO THEIR WORK?"

Discuss with your partner — describe HOW colleagues

   "My colleague organizes the data CAREFULLY and

   "Our team lead reviews proposals SERIOUSLY and

2. Pick ONE COLLEAGUE who uses an IRREGULAR adverb

3. Pick ONE thing YOU did this week and name HOW

Requirements (12 min):
```

## 7. SIMULATION — "THE TEAM PERFORMANCE RECOGNITION MEETING" — 20 min — STAND UP

### Setup — 3 min

> *"Stand up. A Team Performance Recognition Meeting (Guest Observer pattern — 1 recognizer rotating, group observer, professor coach): each of you publicly recognizes ONE colleague using 3-4 adverbs of manner. Right adverb for the right manner — Justice day 2, no inflation, no erasure."*

Board:

```
"THE TEAM PERFORMANCE RECOGNITION MEETING"
3-4 min per round

PATTERN VALIDATED (Guest Observer):
  - 1 RECOGNIZER (rotates each round)
  - GROUP OBSERVER (cohort listens + counts adverbs
    + flags wrong adverbs / wrong position)
  - PROFESSOR (coach, never plays recognizer)

RECOGNIZER SCRIPT (3-4 adverbs of manner per
recognition):
  Step 1 — Name colleague: "I want to recognize
           [Maria] from the [data team] this week."
  Step 2 — Name 2 things + HOW: "She organizes the
           client data CAREFULLY and presents the
           results CLEARLY in the Friday review."
  Step 3 — Name 1 thing where manner could be
           sharper (no inflation): "She responds to
           angry emails QUICKLY, but sometimes too
           aggressively — we are coaching her to
           respond CALMLY first, QUICKLY second."
  Step 4 — Close with the manner that matters most:
           "What we need MOST from her role is for
           the data to be presented CLEARLY — and
           she does that WELL every Friday."

OBSERVER GROUP:
  - Count adverbs used correctly (6+ per round =
    strong)
  - Flag wrong-adverb choice ("perfectly" if reality
    is "carefully" = inflation), wrong form ("she
    works fastly" — fast has no -ly), wrong position
    ("she carefully organizes" — should be "she
    organizes the data carefully")
```

### Model — 3 min

> *"Let me model: 'I want to recognize Maria from the data team this week. She organizes the client data CAREFULLY and presents the results CLEARLY in the Friday review. She also responds to angry client emails QUICKLY — which is good, but we are coaching her to respond CALMLY first, QUICKLY second. What we need MOST from her role is for the dashboard insights to be presented CLEARLY to non-technical stakeholders — and she does that WELL every Friday. Justice day 2: I name carefully because she IS careful (not perfect — careful); I name quickly because she IS quick; I name the coaching opportunity because erasing it would be unfair to her growth.' If a colleague asks about evaluations or finals — that goes only to coordination."*

### Round 1 — Guest Observer — 10 min

3 rounds (3–4 min each). Each round: 1 recognizer, 1 recognized colleague (silent + nod + brief thanks at end), rest = observer group. Professor coaches from the side, NEVER plays recognizer. Rotate.

### Close — 4 min

2 volunteers do their recognition. Cohort counts correct adverbs + flags errors. Note errors with NAMES. Common: "she works fastly" → "fast" (no -ly); "he works goodly" → "well"; "she carefully organizes the data" → "she organizes the data carefully" (after object); "the team works hardly" (hardly = almost-not) → "the team works HARD"; inflation "perfectly" when reality is "carefully"; erasure "doesn't contribute" when reality is "responds quickly when asked".

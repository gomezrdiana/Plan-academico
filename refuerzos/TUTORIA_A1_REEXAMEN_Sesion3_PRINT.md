**Session 3/3 · 2h · Foco: Listening + Preparación Reexamen A1 · IMMERSIVE NARRATIVE MODEL · ZERO PREP MATERIAL**
**Date:** ___/___/2026 · **Attending:** 1 student (1-on-1 tutoring)
**Reexamen context:** Final session of the 3-session tutoring arc. Today the student runs a full A1 Final mock — same conditions, same structure as the real reexamen. Listening declared primary weak area. Sessions 1 and 2 built diagnosis + capacity. Today we simulate and we close the arc.
**Virtue:** FORTALEZA day 3 of 3 — TODAY CLOSES the cycle. Day 1 named what is hard; day 2 built the muscle; today we run the mock and we walk her into the reexamen with a plan.
**Phrase of the Day:** *"Fortitude finishes — strength is walking into the room knowing you've already done the hard thing in practice."*
**Context:** Tutoring is private. Coordination handles results — not the tutor. The mock is administered honestly: real conditions, no shortcuts, no grade pronounced by the tutor. The grade goes to the tutor's notebook and to coordination — never to the student's ears from the tutor's mouth.

## BLOCK 1 — OPENER + SIMULACRO START (~10 min) — SIT

Open seated. Calm tone. The student knows what today is — set the tone clearly, no surprises.

Welcome her. Brief check — 20 seconds, no more — *"How did you sleep?"* If she says fine: move. If she says badly: *"Got it. The mock today is for practice, not for grades. The grades belong to coordination. Today is about the run-through — you do it tired, you do it nervous, and you find out what your version under pressure feels like. That's the gift."* Pivot quickly.

Land the Phrase of the Day. Point to the board where you wrote it before she came in, read once, she echoes twice, then solo from memory. Gloss it: *"Day three. The hard thing in practice is what we do today. The reexamen — when it comes — will not be the first time you've done this. It will be the second time. That's the whole point of today."* **Phrase of the Day INSERTION #1.**

```
PHRASE OF THE DAY — SESION 3
"Fortitude finishes — strength is walking into
 the room knowing you've already done the
 hard thing in practice."
```

Frame FORTALEZA day 3: *"Day one we named it. Day two we built it. Today we run it. Three days, one virtue. Same arc."* Brief, anchor, move.

Quick homework check — 60 seconds, no more. Did she bring the listening transcription? The 10 questions? Did the audio reach coordination WhatsApp? Yes/no for each. **Plan B if homework not done:** do NOT do another spoken diagnostic — today the mock IS the diagnostic. Note it in your notebook ("Sesion 3 — homework: not done") and move directly into the mock setup. The mock is the priority of the day.

Now set the frame for the mock — clear, no ambiguity:

```
A1 FINAL MOCK — TODAY (~70 min)

  4 SECTIONS, ADMINISTERED IN ORDER:
    1. LISTENING       (~20 min)  — tutor reads, student answers in notebook
    2. READING         (~15 min)  — printed A1 text, student answers in notebook
    3. GRAMMAR FILL-IN (~15 min)  — tutor dictates fill-in items, student writes
    4. WRITING         (~20 min)  — tutor dictates the prompt, student writes a paragraph

  RULES:
    - Tutor does NOT explain answers during the mock.
    - Tutor does NOT pronounce a grade at the end.
    - Tutor takes notes in her personal notebook the entire time.
    - Student writes everything in her own notebook.
    - At the end of block 2, the mock CLOSES; block 3 is the debrief.
    - Coordination handles results. Tutor handles the practice.
```

The moment the frame is clear, walk her through her notebook setup — *"Open to a fresh page. Title it 'MOCK A1 FINAL — SESION 3.' Underneath, write four headings stacked: LISTENING / READING / GRAMMAR / WRITING. You write each section's answers under its heading. Pen in hand. Eyes on me."*

⚠ **ANCHOR (safety):** if the student is visibly panicking before the mock starts (shaking hands, near tears, hyperventilating), DO NOT push into the mock immediately. Pause for 2 minutes — have her drink water, breathe in for 4 seconds and out for 6 seconds, three times. Then: *"The mock is practice. It is not the reexamen. Nothing today goes on a transcript. Coordination will not see your specific answers — they'll see the patterns I report. We do this for YOU to know where you stand. That's the only purpose."* Then start. If she still cannot start after 4 minutes of grounding, skip the listening section first (it's the highest-stress) and start with the reading section (printed paper, less time pressure) — restructure on the fly.

## BLOCK 2 — SIMULACRO FULL — A1 FINAL MOCK (~70 min) — SIT throughout

The mock runs in 4 sections. Tutor administers, takes notes, does NOT teach during the mock. The mock IS the data — interventions during the mock contaminate the data.

```
SECTION 1 — LISTENING (~20 min) — tutor reads, student answers

  Tutor reads aloud 4 short audio pieces (each 30-60 seconds,
  drawn from the A1 book — present simple paragraph, past simple
  paragraph, dialogue, future/modal paragraph). Each piece read
  TWICE at normal speed, NO third reading.

  After each piece, tutor reads aloud 3 comprehension questions.
  Student writes the answer in her notebook under LISTENING heading.

  Total: 4 pieces × 3 questions = 12 listening items.
```

Tutor's notebook tracking: write piece 1 / piece 2 / piece 3 / piece 4. Under each, note her answers verbatim (short — just the gist) and mark right/wrong/partial. No commentary to the student during the section.

After section 1 is done, brief pause — 30 seconds, water, then section 2. No discussion of section 1 yet. **Phrase of the Day INSERTION #2** as you transition: *"Strength is finishing. Section 2."*

```
SECTION 2 — READING (~15 min) — printed A1 text from the book

  Tutor hands the student ONE printed page from the A1 book — a
  text-heavy module section (vocabulary + examples + a paragraph
  of prose). Student reads silently. After ~5 min reading, tutor
  reads aloud 5 comprehension questions about the text. Student
  answers in her notebook under READING heading. No re-explanation.

  PLAN B: if no printed book, tutor writes a 100-word A1 text on
  the board (takes ~3 min); student reads from the board, then
  answers the 5 questions. Section runs ~5 min longer in Plan B —
  cut the writing section to 15 min to compensate.
```

Tutor's notebook: which questions did she answer correctly from the text? Which did she invent or guess? Which did she leave blank? That's the reading-comprehension map.

```
SECTION 3 — GRAMMAR FILL-IN (~15 min) — tutor dictates, student writes

  Tutor dictates 10 fill-in items at normal pace. Student writes
  the FULL sentence with the correct form filled in. Items cover
  the A1 spine: present simple, past simple, future, can/will,
  prepositions, articles, plurals.

  Example items (build live, dictate at normal pace):
    1. "He ___ (work) at a bank." -> "He works at a bank."
    2. "Yesterday she ___ (go) home." -> "Yesterday she went home."
    3. "Tomorrow I ___ (call) you." -> "Tomorrow I will call you."
    4. "She lives ___ Bogota." -> "She lives in Bogota."
    5. "There are three ___ (book) on the table." -> "...books..."
    6. "Can you ___ (swim)?" -> "Can you swim?"
    7. "I don't ___ (like) coffee." -> "I don't like coffee."
    8. "He didn't ___ (come) yesterday." -> "He didn't come yesterday."
    9. "We were ___ home last weekend." -> "...at home..."
   10. "Should I ___ (wait)?" -> "Should I wait?"
```

Tutor's notebook: which forms did she nail? Which forms collapsed under pressure (the same forms she may have known cold in session 2 — collapse-under-pressure is its own data point)?

```
SECTION 4 — WRITING (~20 min) — tutor dictates the prompt, student writes

  Tutor dictates ONE writing prompt at normal pace, twice.
  Student writes ONE paragraph in her notebook — minimum 8
  sentences, maximum 12 sentences. Tutor does NOT help with
  vocabulary, does NOT correct, does NOT comment.

  Suggested prompts (tutor picks ONE — pick the one most aligned
  with what the actual reexamen tends to ask):
    Option A — DESCRIBE: "Describe your typical day —
                          morning to night."
    Option B — PAST:     "Tell me about a recent weekend —
                          what did you do?"
    Option C — FUTURE:   "What are your plans for the next
                          month? Use 'will' and days of the week."
    Option D — MIX:      "Describe your family — who they are,
                          where they live, what they do."
```

Tutor's notebook: keep the student's paragraph for the personal-notebook record (or photograph it for coordination). Mark in your notebook: sentence count, verb tense errors, preposition errors, vocabulary range, paragraph coherence. This is the data block 3 will use.

When section 4's 20 minutes are up, even if she's not finished: *"Pens down. Mock is closed."* DO NOT say a grade. DO NOT say "good" or "rough." Neutral, matter-of-fact: *"That's the mock. We move to debrief. Stand up, walk around for 90 seconds, water, sit back down."*

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #3** at the close of the mock, neutrally: *"You finished. Strength is finishing — done is done. We talk about it in block 3, not before."*

⚠ **ANCHOR (safety):** if at any point in the mock the student stops working entirely (head down, pen down, eyes closed for more than 30 seconds), pause the section. Don't end it — pause. *"You're stuck. Skip what you can't do. Move to the next item. Coordination doesn't penalize blanks any more than wrong answers."* (TRUE for A1 — confirm with coordination if you're unsure but assume blanks vs wrong are neutral.) Then resume. If a whole section collapses (e.g. she does 0/12 listening items), abbreviate the remaining sections by 5 min each and protect block 3 — the debrief is what makes the mock useful, and a debrief is impossible if the mock eats block 3's time. Cap section 4 at 15 min if you're behind.

## BLOCK 3 — DEBRIEF + STRATEGY (~30 min) — SIT facing the board

Open the debrief calmly. *"We look at it together. I do not give you a grade — that's coordination's job. I tell you where points were lost, what works, what to do in the last days before the reexamen."*

Walk through each section on the board. Write the heading, list the count, list the dominant errors. Anonymous on the board (it's the protocol), even though it's just the two of you — names in your notebook.

```
DEBRIEF — MOCK A1 FINAL (anonymous on board; details in tutor's notebook)

LISTENING — ___/12 caught
  Top errors: _____________________________
              _____________________________

READING — ___/5 caught
  Top errors: _____________________________
              _____________________________

GRAMMAR — ___/10 correct
  Top errors: _____________________________
              _____________________________

WRITING — sentence count: ___ / coherence: ___ / dominant tense errors:
  _____________________________________________
```

For each section, name the pattern in ONE sentence — the same patterns from sessions 1 and 2 likely returned here. Make the continuity visible: *"In session 1 you missed contractions in the diagnostic. In session 2 they came back in dictation. Today, in the mock listening, you missed them again. That's your top target for the next few days."*

Now LISTENING STRATEGIES — the heart of the debrief. The student doesn't know what to DO when an audio plays. Give her 4 concrete strategies on the board:

```
LISTENING STRATEGIES FOR THE REEXAMEN — 4 RULES

RULE 1 — FIRST + LAST sentence carry the main idea.
         If you only catch two sentences, make them the
         first and the last. Main idea = those two.

RULE 2 — FIRST listen = keywords. SECOND listen = fill in.
         Don't try to write a full transcript on the first listen.
         First time: write down the keywords you hear (nouns,
         names, numbers, times). Second time: connect them.

RULE 3 — DON'T panic on missed words. The brain freezes when
         it misses ONE word and then misses the next ten.
         If you miss a word, mentally say "dash" and keep
         listening. Stay in the sound. (FORTALEZA.)

RULE 4 — LISTEN FOR THE QUESTION FIRST. If the comprehension
         question is read before the audio (sometimes), grab
         the question word: WHAT? WHERE? WHEN? WHO? WHY? HOW?
         That tells you what to listen FOR.
```

Read each rule out loud, she echoes. Then ONE micro-practice: you read ONE more A1 paragraph (30 seconds, normal speed, twice), she applies rules 1 and 2 specifically — first listen she writes keywords only; second listen she fills in. She reports back what she has. *"Did the rules help?"* If yes, name the win. If no, troubleshoot in 60 seconds and move.

Now EXAM-DAY STRATEGY — concrete and physical, no abstractions:

```
EXAM-DAY PLAN — write it on the board, she copies

NIGHT BEFORE:
  - Sleep 7-8 hours minimum. No screens after 9 PM.
  - Pack: ID, pen (TWO pens), water bottle, light snack.
  - Re-read your notes from sessions 1, 2, 3 — 20 min max.

MORNING OF:
  - Eat a real breakfast — protein + carb + fruit.
  - Arrive 30 min early. Walk in calm.
  - Bathroom BEFORE the exam starts.

DURING THE EXAM:
  - Breathe in 4 / out 6 before each section.
  - If you get stuck: SKIP, move on, come back.
  - Listening: apply the 4 rules. Stay in the sound.
  - Writing: count your sentences. Aim for 10.

AFTER:
  - Walk away clean. Don't post-mortem with classmates.
  - Coordination will tell you results when they're ready.
  - Whatever the result — you did the work.
```

Identify together the **TOP 3 weak points** to drill in the days remaining before the reexamen. These are the gaps that survived all 3 sessions:

```
TOP 3 TO REINFORCE — LAST DAYS BEFORE REEXAMEN
 1. _________________________________________________
 2. _________________________________________________
 3. _________________________________________________
```

**Phrase of the Day INSERTION #4** as you close the strategy: *"You've done the hard thing in practice. You walk into the reexamen knowing it. Strength is finishing — and you've already finished once today."*

⚠ **ANCHOR (safety):** if she is deflated after the debrief (the mock revealed gaps she hadn't realized were still there), DO NOT pile on. Name ONE concrete win from the mock — there is always one (e.g. "your writing had 9 sentences and they were all comprehensible" or "you caught 8 out of 12 listening items, which is 5 better than the diagnostic"). End the debrief on the win, then move to block 4. Confidence going into the reexamen matters more than absolute accuracy in the last 24 hours.

## BLOCK 4 — CLOSE + CARTA + HOMEWORK (~10 min) — SIT

Sit her down, calm. The session was long and intense. Pause for 5 seconds before speaking.

*"Three things to close. Different from the other sessions — today we end with a letter."*

**Thing 1: Carta a Mí Misma del Día del Examen.** Open her notebook to a fresh page. *"Write a short letter to yourself — to the version of you who walks into the reexamen room. Date it the day of the reexamen. Three things to include: ONE, one promise to yourself — what you will do when you sit down. TWO, the one breathing rhythm you will use before each section. THREE, what you trust about yourself — name ONE thing you trust. About 6-8 sentences. When you finish, you fold the page and keep it with you — on the day of the reexamen you read it before you walk in. Not before, not after. ONE time. The day of."*

```
CARTA A MÍ MISMA DEL DÍA DEL EXAMEN — 6-8 sentences

  Date: [day of the reexamen]

  ONE — Your promise: what you will do when you sit down.
        (e.g. "I will breathe in 4 / out 6 three times.")
  TWO — Your breathing rhythm. (in 4 / out 6.)
  THREE — One thing you trust about yourself.
        (e.g. "I trust that I have done six hours of practice.")
```

Silence while she writes — ~5 minutes. You sit. DO NOT read what she writes. When she finishes, she folds the page and puts it in her own notebook or wallet or bag — wherever SHE wants it. The carta belongs to her, not to coordination, not to the tutor.

*Plan B: if she can't write the carta for any reason (overwhelmed, frozen), dictate the 3 things ORALLY — she repeats each line aloud and commits to remembering them on exam day. The carta does not have to be written to count — naming the 3 things out loud anchors them.*

**Thing 2: Phrase close.** She says the Phrase of the Day from memory. Then she paraphrases it in her own words — any honest paraphrase that holds the *finishing + walking in having already done the hard thing* idea. Target shape: *"I already did the hard thing today; the reexamen is the second time."* Accept it.

**Thing 3: homework = final-week plan.** Write it on the board, she copies into her notebook:

```
HOMEWORK SESION 3 — FINAL WEEK PLAN — DUE: REEXAMEN DAY
TIME: 15-20 min per day, NOT fragmented per day.

DAILY UNTIL THE REEXAMEN:

(1) LISTENING (10 min) — ONE short A1 audio per day.
    Apply rules 1 + 2 from today's debrief. Keywords
    first listen, fill in second listen. Use YouTube
    "A1 English listening" or "easy English conversation".
    No transcript needed — just listen, write 3
    keywords, listen again.

(2) PATTERN DRILL (5-10 min) — pick ONE of your TOP 3
    weak points each day. Rotate them. Write 5 sentences
    using that pattern. Don't repeat the same pattern
    two days in a row.

(3) BREATHING (1 min) — practice 4 / 6 breathing once
    a day, eyes closed. Build the muscle so it's
    automatic on exam day.

PLAN B: if you skip a day, do not double up. Pick up the
next day. The exam plan is consistency, not volume.

ON THE EXAM DAY: read your Carta a Mí Misma ONCE before
you walk in. Then walk in.
```

Brief close — no long announce, no "see you next session." This is the last tutoring session.

Close: *"Today was day THREE of FORTALEZA. You ran the mock. You finished. That's the cycle complete. From here you have the plan, you have the strategies, you have the carta. The reexamen is the second time you do this. Coordination handles the result — you handle the work. Walk well."* Two-second pause. Then a clean handshake or nod — no extended goodbye, the door closes calmly.

⚠ **ANCHOR (safety):** if block 4 runs short (mock + debrief ate the clock), the priority order for the close is: (1) Carta MUST happen — even Plan B oral version if no time to write; (2) Phrase close MUST happen; (3) homework MUST be written on the board and copied; (4) final goodbye can be ONE sentence. The Carta is the closing ritual of the entire 6-hour arc — do NOT cut it. If absolutely necessary, end the debrief 5 min early to protect the Carta.

## NOTAS INTERNAS (no leer en clase)

- **REGLA PROFE-SIN-MATERIAL (Diana 2026-06-22):** la tutora entra a la sesión SOLO con reloj + 2-3 marcadores + cuaderno personal con notas Sesion 1 + Sesion 2 + libro A1 impreso (fuente del listening AND del reading section). CERO plantilla impresa del mock, CERO clave de respuestas, CERO rúbrica impresa. La única cosa impresa que toca la estudiante es la página del libro A1 en el reading section — porque el reexamen real le entregará papel impreso. Si no hay libro, Plan B = tutora escribe texto de 100 palabras en el tablero.
- **MODELO INMERSIVO NARRATIVO (Diana 2026-06-22):** 4 bloques largos con flujo narrativo continuo y señales de avance ("when section 4's 20 minutes are up, even if she's not finished…" / "if a whole section collapses…" / "if she is deflated after the debrief…"). NO sub-fases cronometradas internas tipo (8 min)/(15 min) — las 4 secciones del mock son sub-estructuras del bloque 2 grande, con tiempos totales por sección como referencia, no como cronómetro estricto. Una sola ⚠ ANCHOR por bloque.
- **Adaptación 1-on-1 día del mock:** la tutora administra, NO enseña durante el mock. Cualquier intervención pedagógica durante la administración contamina los datos. Las correcciones, las estrategias, los patrones — todo va a bloque 3 (debrief). El mock es el dato; el debrief es la enseñanza.
- **Plan B en cada bloque:** bloque 1 (Plan B = saltar listening si pánico extremo y empezar por reading printed), bloque 2 (Plan B = si una sección colapsa, abreviar las siguientes 5 min cada una y proteger bloque 3; Plan B reading sin libro = texto en tablero), bloque 3 (Plan B = si está deshecha, cerrar en UNA win concreta antes de pasar a bloque 4), bloque 4 (Plan B carta = dictar oralmente los 3 puntos si no puede escribir). Hoja suelta si no trae cuaderno.
- **Arco completo cerrado:** FORTALEZA día 1 nombró, día 2 construyó, día 3 cerró. Las 3 sesiones leen como un arco coherente. La Frase del Día varió por sesión pero la virtud sostuvo. Las 6 horas tienen ahora un mapa de progresión documentado en la libreta de la tutora — coordinación recibe la línea de patrones (Sesion 1 weak points → Sesion 2 gaps → mock results) y decide readiness para el reexamen. Sin nombres de metodología (no TBLT, no Dogme, no PPP). GoldList retirado desde 14/05 — solo Frase del Día. La Carta a Mí Misma es solo para el día del examen — la estudiante la lleva, NO va a archivo de coordinación.

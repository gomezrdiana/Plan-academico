## ⚠️ NOTA SOBRE EL CONTENIDO — M35 "GOING TO" AND "GONNA"

**M35 oficial (libro pág 403):** ""Going to" and "Gonna" — Another Form of the Future"

Habilidades objetivas del módulo: *sin dudar, aprender el vocabulario del módulo; sin dudar, formar frases y preguntas usando la construcción "going to".*

```
"GOING TO" Y "GONNA" — OTRA FORMA DEL FUTURO

Para los eventos fijos en el futuro, el español usa "ir a"
y el inglés usa "to be going to".
De forma: es simplemente "go" en presente continuo + un verbo.
"going to" a menudo sale rápido como "gonna" — común al hablar,
NUNCA en trabajo escrito.

Regla del libro (Guía 1, pág 405):
"I'm going to play." → "I'm gonna play." (solo hablado)

AFIRMATIVO / NEGATIVO            INTERROGATIVO
I am (not) going to play.        Am I going to play?
You are (not) going to play.     Are you going to play?
He is (not) going to play.       Is he going to play?
She is (not) going to play.      Is she going to play?
It is (not) going to play.       Is it going to play?
We are (not) going to play.      Are we going to play?
They are (not) going to play.    Are they going to play?

EJEMPLOS DEL LIBRO (pág 406):
- "Are you going to be a teacher?" / "Yes, I'm going to be a teacher."
- "What are you going to do tomorrow?" / "I'm going to study in the morning."
- "Are you gonna talk to the teacher?" / "Yes, I'm gonna talk to her."
- "What time is Diego gonna come to the class?" / "He's gonna come at 9:15."

CLAVE: be (am/is/are) + going to + verbo base.
       "gonna" = solo pronunciación informal, no se escribe.
```

Vocabulario base — **M35 (libro pág 404):** **buy** (comprar / pasado: bought), **feel** (sentirse / pasado: felt), **travel** (viajar), **come** (venir / pasado: came), **meet** (conocerse, reunirse / pasado: met), **finish** (terminar).

## 8. SIMULACIÓN — "THE FAIR PLAN MEETING" (00:58–01:23, 25 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO:** *"Real-life scene. Your team plans a future project — an event, a trip, a launch. Each person says what they're GOING TO do, and you divide the tasks FAIRLY, so nobody is overloaded and everyone contributes equally. That's justice."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Un equipo de trabajo se reúne para planear un proyecto futuro (un evento, un viaje de equipo, lanzar un servicio). Cada persona dice qué va a hacer (futuro: *"I'm going to buy the materials"*) y entre todos reparten las tareas de forma JUSTA — equitativa, nadie sobrecargado.

**Roles:** líder de reunión (pregunta + asigna) + miembros del equipo (cada uno propone qué va a hacer).

### Vocabulario forzado

- Mínimo 5 frases afirmativas con futuro *"I'm going to... / She's going to... / We're going to..."*
- Mínimo 3 preguntas con futuro *"Are you going to...? / What is he going to do? / When are we going to...?"*
- Mínimo 2 frases de reparto justo *"That's not fair — I'm going to take that too. / Let's share it equally."*
- Estructura: *"What are you going to do for the project?"* → *"I'm going to buy the materials and travel to the venue."* → *"That's a lot — I'm going to help you. We're going to finish together."*

### Modela (3 min)

> *Líder: "We're going to organize the event. What are you going to do?"*
> *Miembro: "I'm going to buy the food and come early to set up."*
> *Líder: "That's too much for one person. I'm going to help with the setup."*
> *Miembro 2: "I'm going to meet the guests and finish the report. That's fair — equal work."*

### Round 1 (9 min) — Grupos de 4

Cada grupo tiene un caso (papelitos: organizar un evento de la empresa / planear un viaje de equipo / lanzar un servicio nuevo / preparar una capacitación / repartir turnos del próximo mes / un plan justo vs. uno injusto). Cada miembro dice qué va a hacer con *going to* y reparten las tareas de forma JUSTA.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Rotación de roles

Nuevo líder de reunión. Nuevo caso.

### Cierre (2 min)

1 grupo representa frente al curso. Aplauso. Anotas errores con NOMBRE en tu libreta personal.

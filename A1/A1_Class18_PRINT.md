## 3. LIBRO: MÓDULO 18 — WANT / NEED / HAVE (00:13–00:31, 18 min)

**MÁXIMO 18 MINUTOS. NO TE PASES.**

**CONCEPTO CLAVE — escribe en el tablero (NO lo borres en toda la clase):**

```
AFIRMATIVO:
  I want a pen.       I need water.        I have a car.
  She wants a pen.    He needs water.      She HAS a car.   ← OJO HAS

NEGATIVO:
  I don't want any pizza.          She doesn't want any pizza.
  I don't need any help.           He doesn't need any help.
  I don't have any money.          She doesn't have any money.

PREGUNTA:
  Do you want any coffee?          Does she want any coffee?
  Do they need any help?           Does he need any help?
  Do you have any kids?            Does she have any kids?
```

> **REGLA DE ORO:** HAVE es IRREGULAR en 3ra persona → **HAS** (no "haves"). Si lo confunden, "No. Listen. She HAS." Repiten 2 veces.

**Setup (1 min):** Di: "Open your book to Module 18 — 'I Want, I Need, I Have.' Read with your partner. 5 minutes."

**5 min:** Leen en parejas. Camina **EN SILENCIO**. Si preguntan: "Read it again with your partner."

**12 min:** Siéntate con cada pareja (o trío si solo hay 3 estudiantes). **ELLOS te explican** (tú NO explicas):

1. ¿Cómo se conjuga **want** en 3ra persona? → wantS.
2. ¿Cómo se conjuga **need** en 3ra persona? → needS.
3. ¿Cómo se conjuga **have** en 3ra persona? → **HAS** (irregular, no "haves").
4. ¿Cómo se hace la negación con she? → doesn't want / doesn't need / doesn't have.
5. ¿Cómo se hace la pregunta con you? → Do you want / need / have...?

Si no pueden, vuelven a leer.

## 6. HISTORIA INTERACTIVA ABSURDA — "EL SASTRE Y LOS 100 BOTONES" (01:05–01:20, 15 min)

**Qué es:** TÚ narras una historia deliberadamente absurda con Want/Need/Have. PAUSAS en frases clave. Estudiantes REPITEN. Estudiantes AGREGAN al final.

**Setup (1 min):** "Sit. I'll tell you a story. When I pause, you repeat. ENGLISH ONLY."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO. Pausa para que repitan.**

> "In a small town in Boyacá, there is a tailor named Pedro."

Para. "Say it: 'There is a tailor named Pedro.'" Repiten.

> "Pedro WANTS to make 100 dresses for the town festival next week."

Para. "Say: 'Pedro WANTS to make 100 dresses.'" Repiten.

> "But Pedro NEEDS 100 buttons. He NEEDS them today!"

Para. "Say: 'He NEEDS 100 buttons today.'" Repiten.

> "Pedro HAS only 3 buttons in his shop."

Para. "Say: 'Pedro HAS only 3 buttons.'" Repiten. Pregunta: "He HAS or He HAVE?" Clase: "HAS!" "Why?" Respuesta: "Because PEDRO is HE."

> "Pedro's cat, Mateo, HAS 50 buttons hidden under the bed."

Para. "Say: 'Mateo HAS 50 buttons.'" Repiten. Pregunta: "Why does the cat have buttons?" Espera reacciones absurdas.

> "Pedro's mom DOESN'T HAVE any buttons. But she HAS some coffee."

Para. "Say: 'She DOESN'T HAVE any buttons.'" Repiten.

> "Pedro WANTS coffee. He NEEDS buttons. He HAS only the cat."

Para. Repiten cada frase. Pregunta: "What does Pedro need MOST: coffee, buttons, or the cat?" Espera respuestas.

**Ahora estudiantes AGREGAN (8 min):** Di: "Now YOU continue Pedro's story. Each person says ONE sentence. What does Pedro WANT? What does Mateo NEED? What does the mom HAVE? Use 3rd person -s and HAS."

Señala estudiante por estudiante. Si llegan 4: 4 frases x 2 rondas. Si llegan 3: 3 frases x 2-3 rondas. Si solo asisten 2: tú participas con 1 frase entre cada estudiante.

Si uno se queda mudo: "Take 5 seconds. ONE thing Pedro NEEDS." Si sigue mudo: "Pass to neighbor."

Si confunden HAS/HAVE: "No. Listen. He HAS." Repiten 2x.

**Cierre (1 min):** "Pedro is now famous. Aplauso para Pedro y Mateo el gato." Aplauden.

## 7. SPEED DATING — WANT vs NEED (01:20–01:32, 12 min)

**Setup (1 min):** "STAND UP. 2 lines facing each other (or 1 circle if 3 students)."

**5 rondas de 2 min cada una.** Pregunta de la ronda en el tablero:

```
R1: "What do you WANT for your birthday this year?"
R2: "What do you NEED in your house but DON'T HAVE?"
R3: "What does your mom or dad HAVE that you WANT?"
R4: "What do you NEED to learn in English?"
R5: "What do you HAVE that you DON'T NEED anymore?"
```

**Reglas (escríbelas):**
- Use SIMPLE PRESENT — I want / I need / I have (3rd person -s y HAS si hablas de otro).
- Si necesitan some/any en respuesta, úsenlo.
- Cambian de pareja entre rondas.

Camina **EN SILENCIO**. Anota errores. NO corrijas en caliente.

> **En tu libreta personal**: 3 errores con nombre.

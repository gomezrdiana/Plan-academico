## ⚠️ NOTA SOBRE EL CONTENIDO — M34 PRESENT SIMPLE vs. PRESENT CONTINUOUS

**M34 oficial (libro pág 395):** "Usually, but not Today — Present Simple vs. Present Continuous"

Habilidad objetiva del módulo: *sin dudar, diferenciar entre el presente simple y el presente continuo y usar ambos correctamente.*

```
USUALLY vs. TODAY — PRESENTE SIMPLE vs. PRESENTE CONTINUO

El presente SIMPLE = acción HABITUAL (usually, every day).
El presente CONTINUO = acción AHORA mismo (today, right now).

Regla del libro (Guía 1, pág 396):
"Yo juego futbol, pero estoy jugando golf hoy."
 → "I play soccer, but I am playing golf today."

CONTRASTE LADO A LADO:
                USUALLY (simple)        TODAY (continuous)
I               I play.                 I'm playing.
We              We live in the house.   We're living in the apartment.
You             You visit Pepita.       You're visiting Pepito.
He              He helps his father.    He's helping his mother.
She             She studies in London.  She's studying in Paris.
They            They shower at 7.       They're showering at 7.

NEGATIVO:
- I don't play at seven.        / I'm not playing at eight.
- We don't live in the house.   / We aren't living in the apartment.
- He doesn't help his father.   / He isn't helping his mother.
- She doesn't study in London.  / She isn't studying in Paris.

INTERROGATIVO:
- Do I play at seven?           / Am I playing at eight?
- Does he help his father?      / Is he helping his mother?
- Does she study in London?     / Is she studying in Paris?
- Do they shower at seven?      / Are they showering at eight?

CLAVE: simple = do / does + verbo base.
       continuo = be (am/is/are) + verbo "-ing".
```

Vocabulario base — **M34 NO trae lista nueva de vocabulario**; reusa verbos ya conocidos para contrastar simple vs. continuo: **play, live, visit, help, study, shower** (+ work, eat, drive, walk, watch, clean ya vistos en M33).

## 8. SIMULACIÓN — "THE FAIR SCHEDULE SWAP" (00:58–01:23, 25 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO:** *"Real-life scene. You ask a coworker to swap a shift. You explain what you USUALLY do and what you're doing TODAY, and you both agree on a FAIR exchange — nobody loses, everybody is treated equally. That's justice."*

### Setup (3 min)

**Escenario:** Dos compañeros de trabajo negocian un cambio de turno. Uno explica su horario habitual (presente simple: *"I usually work mornings"*) y lo que está pasando hoy (presente continuo: *"but today I'm covering for someone"*). Acuerdan un intercambio JUSTO — equitativo para ambos.

**Roles:** empleado que pide el cambio (explica usually vs. today) + compañero/supervisor (pregunta + propone un trato justo).

### Vocabulario forzado

- Mínimo 5 frases con presente simple *"I usually... / He works... / Do you usually...?"*
- Mínimo 5 frases con presente continuo *"today I'm... / She's covering... / Are you working...?"*
- Estructura: *"I usually work mornings, but today I'm working the night shift."* → *"That's not fair to you — let's swap. Are you working tomorrow?"* → *"No, I usually rest on Fridays."*

### Modela (3 min)

> *Empleado: "I usually work mornings, but today I'm covering the afternoon. Can we swap?"*
> *Compañero: "Do you usually work weekends?"*
> *Empleado: "No, I don't. I usually rest on Sunday."*
> *Compañero: "OK — that's fair. I'm taking your morning, you're taking my Friday. Equal."*

### Round 1 (9 min) — Grupos de 4

Cada grupo tiene un caso (papelitos: morning vs. night shift / weekend vs. weekday / usually drives but today walks / usually opens but today closes / covering for a sick coworker / a fair vs. an unfair offer). Negocian usando usually (simple) + today (continuo) y cierran un trato JUSTO.

### Round 2 (8 min) — Rotación de roles

Nuevo compañero/supervisor. Nuevo caso.

### Cierre (2 min)

1 grupo representa frente al curso. Aplauso. Anotas errores con NOMBRE en tu libreta personal.

## CONTEXTO DE HOY

**Módulo 20: EMOCIONES + should be** — los adjetivos básicos de cómo te sientes:
- happy, sad, angry, tired, worried, sick, bored, excited, nervous

**Estructuras clave:**
```
PRESENTE:  I AM happy.        She IS tired.       They ARE excited.
PASADO:    I WAS happy.       She WAS tired.      They WERE excited.
SHOULD:    "You SHOULD BE happy!"  "He SHOULDN'T BE so worried."
```

**Errores que NO se pueden fosilizar (lista para Error Paper Live):**
- "I am worry" → "I am **WORRIED**" (worry = verbo, worried = adjetivo)
- "She is boring" → "She is **BORED**" (boring = que aburre, bored = aburrida)
- "Yesterday I am tired" → "**WAS** tired" (pasado)
- "I feel happily" → "I feel **happy**" (después de feel va adjetivo, no adverbio)
- "She is exciting" → "She is **excited**"

**VIRTUD HOY:** FORTALEZA — DÍA 5 (último día — coraje para sentir lo que sientes y nombrarlo).

> Mañana Cl 21 = PRUDENCIA v2 día 1. Empieza la segunda vuelta de virtudes.

## 3. LIBRO: MÓDULO 20 — EMOCIONES + should be (00:13–00:28, 15 min)

**MÁXIMO 15 MINUTOS.**

**CONCEPTO CLAVE — escribe en el tablero (NO lo borres):**

```
EMOTIONS / FEELINGS (adjectives)
- happy           - sad           - angry
- tired           - worried       - sick
- bored           - excited       - nervous

PRESENT:  I AM tired.        She IS happy.       They ARE worried.
PAST:     I WAS tired.       She WAS happy.      They WERE worried.

SHOULD BE / SHOULDN'T BE:
- "You SHOULD BE proud of yourself."
- "He SHOULDN'T BE so nervous."

⚠️ TRICKY:
- I AM WORRY  ✗   →   I AM WORRIED   ✓
- I AM BORE   ✗   →   I AM BORED     ✓
- BORING (que aburre)  vs  BORED (aburrida)
- EXCITING (que emociona)  vs  EXCITED (emocionado)
```

**Setup (1 min):** "Open your book to Module 20 — How Are You? Read with partner. 5 minutes."

**5 min:** Leen. Camina **EN SILENCIO**.

**9 min:** Siéntate con cada pareja. **ELLOS te explican** (tú NO):

1. ¿Qué adjetivos hay en el módulo? → 8-9 adjetivos (happy, sad, etc).
2. ¿Cómo se dice "estoy preocupado"? → I am WORRIED (no "worry").
3. ¿Diferencia entre BORING y BORED? → Boring = la cosa que aburre. Bored = persona que se siente.
4. ¿Cómo se dice "ayer estaba cansado"? → I WAS tired (pasado de am).
5. ¿Cómo se usa "should be"? → "You should be happy" = consejo emocional.

Si no pueden, vuelven a leer.

## 5. SIMULACIÓN: "CÓMO TE SENTISTE HOY" (00:38–00:53, 15 min) — ⚠️ NO SE PUEDE SALTAR

**OBJETIVO:** Practicar emociones + presente/pasado en contexto cotidiano real.

**Setup (2 min):** "STAND UP. Push your chairs. Today: real conversation. Each pair: tell your partner about THREE moments today and ONE moment yesterday. ENGLISH ONLY."

**Modela primero (1 min):** Tú eres tú misma:
- "This morning I was tired because I didn't sleep well. At lunch I was happy because I saw my friend. Right now I am excited because we have class. Yesterday I was nervous before a meeting."

**Round 1 (5 min):** Parejas. Cada estudiante: 3 momentos hoy + 1 ayer. ENGLISH ONLY. 5 min total (~2.5 c/u).

**Round 2 (5 min):** "SWITCH partners. Now talk about a FAMILY MEMBER. 'My mom is usually happy in the morning. My dad was tired yesterday.'" Práctica de 3ra persona.

**Mientras tanto:** Camina con papel de errores anónimo. **Libreta personal**: 3 errores con NOMBRE.

**Cierre (2 min):** "Sit. ONE volunteer: most surprising emotion they heard from their partner. 30 seconds."

> **NO es roleplay.** Estudiantes son ELLOS mismos hablando de sus emociones reales.

## 7. JUEGO: EMOTION CHARADES (01:08–01:23, 15 min) — DE PIE

**Setup (2 min):** "STAND UP. Two teams. One person from each team acts an emotion WITHOUT speaking. The team guesses the emotion AND uses it in a sentence. Point per correct sentence."

**Reglas:**
- Actor NO habla
- Equipo dice: "He's angry!" → 1 punto solo si la oración es CORRECTA gramaticalmente
- Si actúa "tired" pero el equipo dice "He's tire" — punto al otro equipo
- 60 segundos por actor

**Round (12 min):** ~6 actores cada equipo (12 actores total con 6 estudiantes asistiendo significa cada uno actúa 2 veces). Pasen rapido.

Lista de emociones para sortear:
```
happy | sad | angry | tired | worried | sick | bored | excited | nervous | proud | scared | surprised
```

**Cierre (1 min):** Equipo ganador celebra con aplauso.

> **English Points**: +1 al equipo que use correctamente present/past en sus oraciones.

## 8. HISTORIA INTERACTIVA ABSURDA: "EL GATO QUE TENÍA SENTIMIENTOS" (01:23–01:33, 10 min)

**Setup (1 min):** Di: "Sit. I'll tell you a story. Pause + repeat. ENGLISH ONLY."

**Cuenta la historia. LENTO.**

> "In a small house in Bogotá, there is a cat named Pedrito."

Para. "Say: 'There is a cat named Pedrito.'" Repiten.

> "Pedrito is NOT a normal cat. Pedrito is always **WORRIED**. Yes, WORRIED."

Para. "Say: 'Pedrito is always WORRIED.'" Repiten. Pregunta: "Why is the cat worried?" Espera reacciones.

> "Yesterday Pedrito **WAS angry** because the food was cold. Today Pedrito **IS sad** because his favorite toy disappeared."

Para. "Say: 'Yesterday Pedrito was angry. Today Pedrito is sad.'" Repiten.

> "Pedrito's owner, Maria, **IS tired** of his emotions."

Para. "Say: 'Maria is tired of his emotions.'" Repiten.

> "Maria says: 'You should be HAPPY, Pedrito! You have food! You have a house!'"

Para. "Say: 'You should be happy!'" Repiten.

> "But Pedrito just looks at her. He **IS bored**. He **IS NOT excited** about anything."

Para. Repiten.

**Estudiantes AGREGAN (5 min):** Cada estudiante: 1 frase con emoción + presente/pasado sobre Pedrito o Maria.

**Cierre (1 min):** "Pedrito is now famous. Aplauso para Pedrito."

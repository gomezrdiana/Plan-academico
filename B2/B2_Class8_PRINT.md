### ☐ 00:00–00:15 | ICEBREAKER: RUMOR MILL (15 min)
**How it works:** You whisper a rumor about a fictional celebrity to ONE student. That student whispers it to the next. Chain of 6 (all students). The LAST student says the rumor out loud. Compare to the original. Then the class converts the rumor to passive reporting structures.

**Round 1 — Whisper this EXACTLY to the first student:** "Taylor Swift secretly bought a house in Bucaramanga."
After the last student says it out loud, write on the board what the last student said vs. the original. Then say: "Now convert this to passive reporting. Start with 'It is said that...' or 'She is believed to...'"
Class produces: "It is said that Taylor Swift bought a house in Bucaramanga." / "Taylor Swift is believed to have bought a house in Bucaramanga." Write BOTH forms on the board.

**Round 2 — Whisper:** "The president is planning to make English the official language."
Same process. Class produces: "It is reported that the president is planning to make English the official language." / "The president is reported to be planning to make English the official language."

**Round 3 — Whisper:** "A student in this class is actually a secret millionaire."
Same process. Class produces: "It is rumored that a student in this class is a secret millionaire." / "A student in this class is rumored to be a secret millionaire."

Say: "These structures — 'It is said that,' 'He is believed to' — this is today's module. Passive reporting."

> Keep it fast and fun. The whisper chain will naturally distort the rumor — that's the comedy. Use it.

### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 5B — PASSIVE REPORTING (20 min)
**What it's about:** Passive reporting = reporting what people say/believe/think WITHOUT identifying who said it. Two structures:

**Structure 1 — IT + passive reporting verb + THAT + clause:**
- "It is said that he is rich."
- "It is believed that she left the country."
- "It is thought that the company will close."
- "It is reported that five people were injured."
- "It is known that coffee helps concentration."

**Structure 2 — Subject + passive reporting verb + TO + infinitive:**
- "He is said to be rich."
- "She is believed to have left the country." (past = to have + past participle)
- "The company is thought to be closing."
- "Five people are reported to have been injured."
- "Coffee is known to help concentration."

**Write on the board EXACTLY:**
Active: "People say he is rich."
→ Passive 1: "It is said that he is rich."
→ Passive 2: "He is said to be rich."

Active: "People believe she left the country."
→ Passive 1: "It is believed that she left the country."
→ Passive 2: "She is believed to have left the country."

**Key verbs:** say, believe, think, report, know, expect, consider, understand, allege, rumor.

Say: "Open your B2 book to Module 5B. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 10 minutes."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain both structures to you. They give you examples using BOTH forms. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you. If they can't give you both structures, they read again.

### ☐ 01:10–01:45 | GRABO SELFIE: EYES-CLOSED VIDEO (35 min)
**THIS IS THE FIRST GRABO SELFIE VIDEO. Follow every step EXACTLY.**

**What it is:** Each student records themselves speaking for MINIMUM 2 MINUTES with their EYES CLOSED. No notes. No help. No stopping. No retakes. This forces them to access English from memory — no visual crutches.

**Write on the board EXACTLY:**
"GRABO SELFIE 1 — Topic: Talk about everything you have learned in this course so far. Use examples. Mention: question tags, adjective order, either/neither/both, conditionals, passive voice. MINIMUM 2 MINUTES. Eyes CLOSED the entire time."

**Step 1 — Prepare (5 min).** Say: "You will record yourself speaking for 2 minutes with your eyes closed. No notes during recording. Right now, write BULLET POINTS ONLY — key words, not sentences, not a script. Write: what modules you remember, one example of each." Walk around. If someone is writing full sentences, stop them: "Key words only. Not sentences." After 5 min, say: "Put your paper FACE DOWN. You cannot look at it again."

**Step 2 — Practice (5 min).** Say: "In pairs. Close your eyes. Practice talking about the modules. Your partner listens and counts how many modules you mention. After 1 minute, your partner gives feedback: which modules did you forget? What examples were wrong?" Each person practices once. Partner gives specific feedback: "You forgot adjective order" or "Your conditional example was wrong — you said 'If I would have' instead of 'If I had.'"

**Step 3 — Record (20 min).** Say: "Everyone records AT THE SAME TIME. This is a selfie video — you record YOURSELF. Set your phone timer for 2 minutes so you can see it. Prop your phone against something or hold it. Press record. Close your eyes. Talk for the full 2 minutes. If you forget something, keep talking — say 'um' if you need to, but do NOT stop. When the timer goes off, stop recording. NO RETAKES. The first recording is the final recording."

All 6 students record simultaneously. Spread out around the classroom so they don't hear each other too much. Say: "Find a spot. Sit or stand. Set your timer. When I say GO, everyone starts recording at the same time. GO."

**CRITICAL RULES (say these out loud BEFORE they start):**
- This is a SELFIE video — you record yourself.
- Eyes CLOSED the entire time.
- NO stopping. Keep talking for the full 2 minutes.
- NO retakes. The first recording is the final recording.
- Set your phone timer for 2 minutes so you know when to stop.

**Step 4 — Send (5 min).** Say: "Send your video to me via WhatsApp RIGHT NOW. Label it: GRABO SELFIE 1 — [your name]." Check that every student sends it before moving on. If someone's video is under 2 minutes, they record again during break.

**DELIVERABLE: Teacher MUST send ALL videos to coordination the SAME DAY. No exceptions.**

> This is the most important assessment tool in the course. It shows real fluency without crutches. Do NOT let students script it. Do NOT let them retake it. The raw version reveals their actual level.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG passive reporting — wrong structure, wrong verb form after "to," using active when passive reporting is needed, incorrect tense shifts. Read slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch.

"I love celebrity gossip. Let me tell you what I heard this week. People say that the famous actor Tom Cruise is move to Colombia. **(1 — is said to be moving / It is said that Tom Cruise is moving — wrong form: "is move")** It is believe that he wants to open a restaurant in Cartagena. **(2 — It is believed — need past participle "believed," not base form)**

His ex-wife is said that she is angry about it. **(3 — His ex-wife is said to be angry about it — wrong structure: can't use "is said that she")** It is reported that she has call her lawyers. **(4 — she has called — need past participle "called")**

People believe that the restaurant will being the biggest in South America. **(5 — will be — "will being" is wrong)** The restaurant is expected that it will open next year. **(6 — The restaurant is expected to open next year — wrong structure: can't combine "is expected that it")** Colombian chefs are said that they are excited about the news. **(7 — Colombian chefs are said to be excited — wrong structure)**

Meanwhile, it is know that a famous singer bought a private island near San Andres. **(8 — It is known — need past participle "known")** She is believe to have paid 5 million dollars. **(9 — She is believed to have paid — need "believed," not "believe")** It is thought that the island will been transformed into a hotel. **(10 — will be transformed — "will been" is wrong)**

Local fishermen are reported to losing their access to the beach. **(11 — are reported to be losing — missing "be")** It is alleged that the singer has not been pay taxes in Colombia. **(12 — has not been paying — need "-ing" form, not base "pay")** She is understood to has hired a Colombian lawyer. **(13 — She is understood to have hired — "to has" is wrong, must be "to have")**

The government is thought to being investigating the purchase. **(14 — is thought to be investigating — "to being" is wrong)** It is said that new laws will be pass to prevent foreigners from buying islands. **(15 — will be passed — need past participle "passed," not base form)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** 5 min each. One makes deliberate passive reporting errors, the other corrects. Switch roles. If they struggle to create their own, they take the 3 rumors from the icebreaker and report them with deliberate errors for their partner to catch.

### ☐ 03:10–03:25 | BLENDING: GOSSIP GAME WITH ALL MODULES (15 min)
**What is this?** Students sit in a circle. Each person adds to a gossip chain using passive reporting + at least 2 structures from previous modules (question tags, adjective order, either/neither/both, conditionals, passive voice).

**Teacher starts.** Say this EXACTLY: "It is said that Maria, who is both intelligent and beautiful, wouldn't have come to class if she hadn't been given a ride, isn't that right?"

Point to the next student. Say: "Add to the gossip. You MUST use at least 2 different module structures. Passive reporting is mandatory. Plus one more: a question tag, adjective order, either/neither/both, or a conditional."

**Examples of what students might say:**
- "She is also believed to have bought a breathtaking, intricate new dress, hasn't she?" (passive reporting + adjective order + question tag)
- "It is rumored that she will either move to Bogota or stay here, but if she had been offered the job earlier, she would have left, wouldn't she?" (passive reporting + either/or + mixed conditional + question tag)

**Go around twice.** If a student can't blend 2 modules, help them: "You used passive reporting — good. Now add a question tag at the end." They try again. Do NOT skip them — they must produce a correct sentence before moving on.

> This is the ultimate blending test. Listen carefully. Write errors on your error paper.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | HOT SEAT: PASSIVE REPORTING BLENDER (17 min)
**How it works:** One student sits facing the class (the Hot Seat). The class asks questions. The student in the Hot Seat MUST answer using passive reporting + at least 1 other module. If they don't blend, the class says "BLEND!" and they try again. Each student gets 2.5 minutes. Rotate through all 6 students.

**Teacher writes on board:** "Every answer must use 'It is said that...' or 'X is believed to...' PLUS one more module."

**Write these questions on the board (class picks which to ask):**
1. "What is believed about your city that isn't true?"
2. "It is said that money can't buy happiness. Do you agree?"
3. "What were you told as a child that turned out to be wrong?"
4. "It is reported that AI will replace teachers. What do you think?"
5. "What is the most exquisite place that is known to exist in Colombia?"
6. "If you were believed to be someone famous, who would it be?"

Say: "Class — YOU choose which question to ask. The person in the Hot Seat must answer using passive reporting plus one more module. If they forget, you say 'BLEND!' and they fix it."

> Teacher: listen for these blends per question — 1: question tag, 2: conditional, 3: advanced adjective, 4: either/neither/both, 5: adjective order, 6: conditional. If the student doesn't blend, prompt them: "Add a question tag" or "Use a conditional."

> WALK AROUND writing errors. The pressure of the Hot Seat forces quick thinking. Keep it fun — clap after each student.

## 1. MYSTERY HOMEWORK CHECK (18:45–18:50, 5 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE (decir en inglés):**
> *"This activity checks if you practiced at home. No nagging — just visible verification. Different every class."*

**Setup ANTES de clase:** prepara un sobre cerrado con esta instrucción adentro:

```
HOMEWORK MYSTERY CHECK — Cl 6

In 60 seconds, give me 2 sentences about YOUR topic of "Why Colombia":
- 1 in past tense (something you found out / heard about)
- 1 in future tense (something you'll explore next)
Use AT LEAST 1 academic B2 word.

GO!
```

**En clase (5 min):**
1. (30 seg) *"STAND UP. I have an envelope. Let's see what's inside."*
2. (30 seg) Abre + lee.
3. (1 min) Silencio para pensar.
4. (3 min) Llama 3 estudiantes AL AZAR. Quien responda fluido → reconocimiento verbal + anotación. Quien titubea → *"Sit. Thank you."* Sin regaño.

## 4. PEER TEACHER SLOT (18:55–19:02, 7 min) — DE PIE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"One of you teaches today. Teaching cements learning faster than 10 repetitions."*

**Pre-clase:** asigna a UNA estudiante (rotación visible en pared) 1-2 errores recurrentes observados en Cl 1-5.

**Errores sugeridos (escoge 1-2 — los más vistos en Fase 1):**
```
1. Tiempos verbales confundidos: "I went to Colombia and I am very impressed" → consistencia
2. Falta de "the/a" antes de sustantivos: "I want to talk about Colombia history" → "the history"
3. Pronunciación de "would" como "wood"
4. "He/she" intercambiados en respuestas rápidas
5. "Make/do" confundido: "make my homework" → "do my homework"
```

**En clase (7 min):**
1. (30 seg) *"Today's peer teacher is [name]. Please stand. The floor is yours."*
2. (5 min) La estudiante: explica error → corrige → pide al cohorte repetir 2x.
3. (1 min) *"Round of applause. Tomorrow it's [next name]."*
4. (30 seg) Anota cómo se sintió la enseñanza (claridad, volumen, ritmo).

> Si la peer teacher se traba, NO la rescates inmediato. Dale 10 seg. Que produzca.

## 5. ANCLAS DE CONEXIÓN (19:02–19:14, 12 min) — NUEVO BLOQUE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"When your brain goes blank in a presentation, you don't stay silent — you use an anchor. These phrases keep the conversation going while your mind catches up. They are the difference between freezing and finishing."*

**Escribe en sección visible del tablero (mantener TODA la clase):**

```
ANCHORS OF CONNECTION — When you go blank, USE these:

1. "Well, let me think for a second..."
2. "In other words, what I mean is..."
3. "Hold on, let me rephrase that..."
4. "Actually, the point I want to make is..."
5. "That's a good question — let me explore it..."
6. "Now, this is where it gets interesting..."
7. "What you might find surprising is..."
8. "If I had to summarize, I'd say..."
```

**Práctica (8 min):**

**Round 1 (3 min) — Repetir en voz alta:** cada estudiante lee una ancla x2. Pronunciación clara, volumen.

**Round 2 (5 min) — Práctica BAJO BLANCO:** profesora pregunta algo difícil (ej: *"Why is Colombia underrated as a travel destination?"*). Estudiante DEBE empezar con una ancla. Da 3 segundos máximo para arrancar — si no, "Sit. Next."

Rotar: cada estudiante 2 turnos.

**Cierre (1 min):** *"From now on, when you're stuck in your presentation, USE these. Memorize 3 favorites by Friday."*

> Las anclas son la habilidad B2 que más rápido las hace SONAR B2 reales — no es vocabulario, es continuidad.

## 6. CONTRASTE TIEMPOS VERBALES — DRILL (19:14–19:24, 10 min) — SENTADAS

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Past, present, future of the same sentence. Until it stops being a decision and becomes reflex. This is what's missing for B2."*

**Setup (1 min):** *"I give you a present sentence. You give me past + future. Fast. Don't think. React."*

**Drill (9 min):**

| Presente que doy | Pasado que tú das | Futuro que tú das |
|---|---|---|
| "I write about my topic every day" | "I wrote about my topic yesterday" | "I will write about my topic tomorrow" |
| "She visits the museum" | "She visited the museum" | "She will visit the museum" |
| "We study Colombian history" | "We studied Colombian history" | "We will study Colombian history" |
| "He explains the project" | "He explained the project" | "He will explain the project" |
| "They organize the trip" | "They organized the trip" | "They will organize the trip" |

**Lanzar 8-10 ejemplos. Cada estudiante rotando.** Si confunden los tiempos → repetir hasta que salga sin pensar.

**Cierre (1 min):** *"Past tense in your project: what already happened in Colombia's history. Future tense: what visitors will experience. Present: what's true now. CONFUSING them in your final = no B2."*

## 9. GOLDLIST LISTA 6 — 20 FRASES ARGUMENTATIVAS B2 (20:15–20:30, 15 min) — SENTADAS

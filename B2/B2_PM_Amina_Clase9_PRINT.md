## 9. 🆕 TOPIC-SPECIFIC VOCABULARY CHUNKS (19:24–19:38, 14 min) — SENTADAS + voz alta

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"This is what separates a student of English from an expert on a topic. Today: each of you builds 6-8 chunks specific to YOUR Why Colombia project."*

### Concepto (4 min)

**Tablero:**

```
TOPIC-SPECIFIC VOCABULARY CHUNKS — what they are

A CHUNK = a 3-6 word phrase specific to YOUR topic that sounds
like an EXPERT, not a learner.

Examples by topic:

If your topic is COFFEE PRODUCTION:
- "Arabica vs Robusta yield"
- "Single-origin specialty market"
- "Direct trade fair price model"

If your topic is TOURISM:
- "Sustainable tourism policy"
- "Carrying capacity of destinations"
- "Eco-tourism certification"

If your topic is EDUCATION:
- "Early childhood literacy gap"
- "STEM curriculum reform"
- "Teacher retention crisis"

Each student builds 6-8 chunks for HER topic.
```

### Procedimiento (10 min)

1. (3 min) Cada estudiante reflexiona 2 min sobre su tema. Anota a lápiz 6-8 chunks específicos.
2. (5 min) En orden: cada estudiante lee sus 6-8 chunks en voz alta. Las otras escuchan. Si una chunk suena rara o no es B2, Amina sugiere mejora.
3. (2 min) Cada una agrega 1-2 chunks adicionales basados en lo que escuchó de las otras.

**Resultado:** cada estudiante sale con 6-8 chunks PROPIOS listos para usar en Speak About + Final.

> **(Inserción #3 de Frase del Día):** si alguien sugiere una chunk que otra rechaza, modela: *"That's a fair concern — from my perspective, however, that chunk DOES work in academic context."*

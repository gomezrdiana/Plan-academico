**Virtue of the week: PRUDENCE (smart decisions, patience, discipline)**

```
PRUDENCE — "What's a recent decision you made that you regret?
            What would prudence have done differently?"
```

### ☐ 00:05–00:15 | ICEBREAKER: WOULD YOU RATHER? (10 min)
Write all 5 questions on the board BEFORE students arrive. Read one at a time. If they don't understand a word, clarify with a gesture/drawing/synonym — NO grammar explanation. Everyone STANDING, move to one side of the room based on their answer, justify in 20 sec.

1. Would you rather fight 100 duck-sized horses or 1 horse-sized duck?
2. Would you rather always have to sing instead of talk, or always have to dance instead of walk?
3. Would you rather have unlimited money but no friends, or be broke but have the best friends ever?
4. Would you rather live without music forever or live without TV/movies forever?
5. Would you rather be able to fly but only at walking speed, or run at 200 km/h but never fly?

### ☐ 00:15–00:30 | BOOK: MODULE 1 — QUESTION TAGS (15 min)
**What it's about:** Question Tags = short questions at the end of a sentence to confirm. Positive → negative tag ("You're coming, **aren't you?**"). Negative → positive tag ("She isn't here, **is she?**"). The book explains it in English with 20 examples.

Say: "Open your B1 book to Module 1. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 8 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you what they understood. They give you examples. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.
> You don't explain. They explain to you.

### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | EXERCISES + ANSWER KEYS (20 min)
Say: "Do the exercises. Check answers with the Answer Key. Ask your partner first. If neither knows, raise your hand." WALK AROUND — give the correct version only (never explain rules). If 50%+ get the same thing wrong: STOP, write on the board, they repeat.

### ☐ 00:50–01:12 | VIDEO: GRABO (22 min)
Board: "Make 5 statements about your partner + question tag. Ex: 'You like pizza, don't you?'"

**Before starting, say:** "Don't worry about being perfect. Just recording yourself is already powerful — you're stepping out of your comfort zone."

1. **Practice (10 min):** In pairs, they ask each other the questions.
2. **Record (10 min):** 5 min each. One records asking question tags, the other responds. Then switch. No script.
3. **Send (2 min):** Each student sends THEIR video to the teacher via WhatsApp. Activity complete = video sent.

> Teacher selects the best ones → send to coordination at end of class.

### ☐ 01:12–01:42 | DEBATE STRUCTURED (30 min)
3 topics on the board:
1. "Social media does more harm than good."
2. "It's better to live in a big city than a small town."
3. "AI will replace most jobs in 10 years."

**Choose (5 min):** Class votes on topic. 2 teams: FOR vs AGAINST.
**Prepare (7 min):** Each student writes their arguments using question tags.
**Debate (10 min):** Teams alternate. They CANNOT read their paper. Speak from memory. Teammates can help if someone gets stuck.
**Correct (5-7 min):** Read back errors you wrote on paper. "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat 2x.
> Walk around WITH PAPER during the debate writing down errors.

### ☐ 02:02–02:22 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Read slowly, pause after each tag. Students shout "WRONG!" + correct version. Points per catch.

"So, yesterday I went to the supermarket. It was really crowded, wasn't I? **(1)** I needed to buy some fruit. Bananas are delicious, isn't it? **(2)** I couldn't find the apples. The store had apples last week, didn't it? **(3)** I asked a worker for help. He was very nice, wasn't he? **(— CORRECT)** He didn't speak English, didn't he? **(4)** So we used sign language. That's pretty funny, wasn't it? **(5)**

Then I went to pay. The line was so long, isn't it? **(6)** I forgot my wallet. You've done that before, hasn't you? **(7)** I called my girlfriend. She can drive, can she? **(8)** She wasn't happy, was I? **(9)** She came anyway. She loves me, doesn't I? **(10)**

We drove home. The traffic was terrible, weren't it? **(11)** We don't have traffic like that in small towns, does we? **(12)** I cooked dinner. I'm a good cook, aren't you? **(13)** The food was ready at 8. That's not too late, was it? **(14)** We watched a movie after. You like movies, doesn't you? **(15)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** 5 min each. One speaks with deliberate errors, the other corrects. Switch roles.

### ☐ 02:22–02:42 | SHADOWING 5 STEPS (20 min)
YouTube on your phone. Video from break. Subtitles on student phones.
**STEP 1 — LISTEN (3 min):** Just listen.
**STEP 2 — WITH TEXT (4 min):** Repeat with subtitles.
**STEP 3 — WITHOUT TEXT (4 min):** Screens OFF.
**STEP 4 — EYES CLOSED (3 min):** Max concentration.
**STEP 5 — PAIR ROLE PLAY (6 min):** No audio, pairs imitate from memory.

### ☐ 02:42–03:07 | WORD GAME + WORD STORY (25 min)
**Part 1 — Guess the Word (10 min):** 6 words on separate papers. One student comes up, sees the word, describes it WITHOUT saying it. Others guess. Classmates can help. ENGLISH ONLY. Word guessed = written on board.

**Teacher example first.** Words: airplane, dog, birthday, hospital, secret, rain. Tell this story WITHOUT reading, show each tag with your fingers:

"Last week was my dog's birthday, wasn't it? I had a secret plan: we would take an airplane to the beach. But it started to rain, didn't it? My dog got sick and we ended up at the hospital. That's not how I planned it, was it? But he's fine now, isn't he?"

**Student words:** wedding, ghost, pizza, police, vacation, elevator.

**Part 2 — Word Story (15 min):** PAIRS: create a story using all 6 words + question tags. 7 min to prepare. Each pair tells their story (2 min). Class votes most creative.

### ☐ 03:22–03:37 | PORTFOLIO BUILD — DAY 1: MOCK INTERVIEW ROUND (15 min)

**⚠️ Use `ANEXO_PORTFOLIO_BUILD_B2_PROFESOR.pdf` (Day 1 section) and `ANEXO_BANCO_PREGUNTAS_STAR.pdf`.**

Today is **Day 1 of the Portfolio Build Cycle**. Students practice STAR-format interview answers.

**Setup (1 min):** Open the STAR Question Bank. Today's questions (Cycle 1, Day 1):
- **Pairs question:** #3 "Tell me about a time you faced a difficult challenge at work and how you handled it."
- **Volunteer recording question:** #5 "Describe a situation where you had to meet a tight deadline. How did you handle it?"

```
S — Situation: When? Where? What was happening?
T — Task:      What was your specific responsibility?
A — Action:    What did YOU specifically do?
R — Result:    Outcome (numbers / change if possible)
```

**Pairs round (8 min):**

**Volunteer recording (4 min):**

- After: "Good. Coordination will give detailed feedback." Send the recording to coordination via WhatsApp the same day.

**Group reflection (2 min):**
- "What was hardest about using STAR?" 2 students share.

**Your feedback during activity:**

- Filler words: signal "like" / "you know" with a hand gesture so they cut them.
- **Don't critique content.** Only English form.

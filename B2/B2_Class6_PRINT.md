### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 4B — MIXED CONDITIONALS (20 min)
**What it's about:** Mixed conditionals combine parts of 2nd and 3rd conditionals. Two types:
Type 1 — Past cause → Present result: "If I had studied medicine (past — but I didn't), I would be a doctor now (present)." Formula: If + past perfect, would + base verb.
Type 2 — Present cause → Past result: "If I were braver (present — but I'm not), I would have asked her out (past)." Formula: If + past simple, would have + past participle.
Key: the TIME in each clause is DIFFERENT. That's what makes them "mixed."

Say: "Open your B1 book to the Mixed Conditionals section. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 10 minutes."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain mixed conditionals to you. They give you examples of BOTH types. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you. If they can't explain the difference between the two types, they read again.

### ☐ 01:10–01:45 | TBLT: ALTERNATE LIVES (35 min)
**How it works:** Students create an "alternate version" of their own life by changing ONE decision from their past. Then they describe how their PRESENT would be different. This forces mixed conditionals naturally — past cause, present result.

**Step 1 — Choose (3 min).** Each student picks ONE real decision from their past that changed their life. Examples: "I chose to study engineering instead of music." "I moved to this city." "I didn't go to that party where I met my partner." Write it down.

**Step 2 — Build your alternate life (7 min).** Each student writes 6 sentences describing their alternate present using mixed conditionals. Example: "If I had studied music, I would be performing in bars right now." "If I hadn't moved here, I would be living with my parents." "If I had gone to that party, I wouldn't know my partner."

**Step 3 — Interview (15 min).** Pairs. Student A interviews Student B about their alternate life as if it were real. "So you're a musician now? Where do you play? Do you enjoy it?" Student B answers IN CHARACTER, staying in the alternate life. Use mixed conditionals when comparing: "Well, if I had chosen music, I would be happier, but I wouldn't have money." Switch after 7 min.

**Step 4 — Share (10 min).** Each student presents their partner's alternate life to the class. "Maria said that if she hadn't become an engineer, she would be a famous pianist right now." Class asks follow-up questions.

> Walk around WITH error paper. WRITE DOWN every conditional error you hear.

### ☐ 02:45–03:05 | TRANSLANGUAGING: LIFE TURNING POINTS (20 min)
**What is this?** This is the ONLY activity where students can briefly use Spanish — as a BRIDGE, not a crutch. Students get 30 seconds of Spanish to understand/brainstorm, then they MUST switch to English and present their answer using mixed conditionals.

**How to do it:** Read one prompt. Pairs get 30 sec in Spanish to process. Then 3-4 min in English to discuss and present. Each pair shares their answer with the class. They MUST use mixed conditionals. If they don't, stop them: "Use a mixed conditional — past cause, present result." 3 prompts total.

**Prompt 1:** "Think of a skill you never learned (instrument, sport, language). If you had learned it, how would your life be different RIGHT NOW?"
**Prompt 2:** "Think of a person you lost contact with (friend, family, ex). If you hadn't lost contact, what would your relationship be like TODAY?"
**Prompt 3:** "Think of a country your parents almost moved to but didn't. If they had moved there, what would YOUR life look like now?"

> The Spanish is only to unblock understanding. The moment they have the idea, it's English only.

### ☐ 03:05–03:25 | STEAM: TIMELINE REWRITE (20 min)
**What is this?** Students pick a real historical event and change it. Then they predict how the PRESENT would be different using mixed conditionals. This combines history, critical thinking, and grammar.

**How to do it:** Write 3 events on the board. Each pair picks one. They discuss and prepare 5 mixed conditional sentences about how the present would be different. Then present to the class.

**Event 1:** "The internet was never invented."
Example: "If the internet hadn't been invented, we would be reading newspapers right now." "If it hadn't existed, companies like Google wouldn't be the most powerful in the world."

**Event 2:** "Colombia never produced coffee."

**Event 3:** "Smartphones were invented in the 1980s instead of the 2000s."

**Prepare (5 min).** Each pair writes 5 mixed conditional sentences.
**Present (12 min).** Each pair presents (4 min each). Class can challenge: "But if the internet hadn't been invented, WOULD we really be reading newspapers?" Debate using conditionals.
**Vote (3 min).** Most creative alternate present?

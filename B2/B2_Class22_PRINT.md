### 00:00--00:15 | ICEBREAKER: FINISH MY SENTENCE (15 min)

Say: "Welcome. This is our last big review class before the final exam and the final presentation. After today, you should feel ready. But first --- Finish My Sentence."

**Write on the board:**
```
FINISH MY SENTENCE

I start a sentence. You finish it.
The catch: your ending MUST be grammatically
perfect. If it's not, I correct you and you
try again. Speed round. No thinking.
```

Say: "I start, you finish. Fast. Point at someone --- they answer."

**Sentences to start (point at a different student each time):**

1. "If I hadn't come to this class today..." (student finishes with a conditional result)
2. "Never in my life have I..." (student continues with an experience)
3. "The person who inspires me the most is..." (student uses a relative clause continuation)
4. "I wish I..." (student completes with wish structure)
5. "By the time I'm 40, I will have..." (student uses future perfect)
6. "Something nobody knows about me is..." (student uses indefinite pronoun)
7. "I've been studying English because..." (student uses present perfect continuous context)
8. "Not only do I want to pass this exam, but I also..." (student continues the inversion)
9. "The best advice I was ever given was..." (student uses passive)
10. "My parents always told me that..." (student uses reported speech)
11. "I recently had my..." (student uses causative)
12. "If only I could..." (student uses wish/if only)

Go around twice if time allows. Fast pace. If someone freezes for more than 5 seconds: "Come on. Say anything. Just make it grammatically correct."

After each answer, if there's an error: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Move on immediately.

**Last 3 min.** Say: "Now YOU start a sentence, and I finish it. Give me a challenge. Try to start a sentence I can't finish elegantly." 3 students try. This is fun and builds confidence --- they see that even you have to think sometimes.

> Error paper. Write errors even here. This is diagnostic --- which structures are still shaky?

### 00:25--00:55 | EXAM PREP: PRACTICE EXERCISES COVERING ALL 12 MODULES (30 min)

Say: "Final exam review. We are going to do rapid-fire exercises covering all 12 modules. This time, I want you to write your answers first, then we check. This simulates the written exam. You need to get used to writing correct English under pressure."

**Hand out blank paper or have them use their notebooks.**

Say: "I will read a sentence or give you an instruction. Write your answer. You have 30 seconds per question. Then we check. Ready?"

**Block 1 --- Modules 1-4 (8 min, 8 questions):**

Say each question. Give them 30 seconds to write. Then check.

1. "Complete: 'She _____ (work) at this company since 2019.'" (has been working)
   Check: "What did you write?" One student answers. If wrong: "No. Has been working. Present perfect continuous because she still works there. Write the correct answer."

2. "Complete: 'By tomorrow evening, they _____ (arrive) in Paris.'" (will have arrived)
   Check.

3. "Complete: 'This time next Monday, I _____ (take) my final exam.'" (will be taking)
   Check.

4. "Rewrite as a mixed conditional: 'I didn't learn English. I don't have a good job now.'" (If I had learned English, I would have a good job now.)
   Check.

5. "Rewrite as a mixed conditional: 'She is very shy. She didn't ask for a promotion last year.'" (If she weren't so shy, she would have asked for a promotion last year.)
   Check.

6. "Rewrite using causative: 'A tailor shortened my pants.'" (I had my pants shortened. / I got my pants shortened.)
   Check.

7. "Rewrite using causative: 'The dentist will clean my teeth tomorrow.'" (I will have my teeth cleaned tomorrow.)
   Check.

8. "Complete: 'She got her car _____ (wash) yesterday.'" (washed)
   Check.

**Block 2 --- Modules 5-8 (8 min, 8 questions):**

9. "Combine with a relative clause: 'That is the professor. His lectures are always full.'" (That is the professor whose lectures are always full.)
   Check.

10. "Combine: 'I bought a book. The book was recommended by my teacher.'" (I bought a book that/which was recommended by my teacher.)
    Check.

11. "Change to reported speech: 'We are going to the beach tomorrow.'" (They said (that) they were going to the beach the next day.)
    Check.

12. "Change to reported speech: 'Did you finish the homework?'" (She asked me if/whether I had finished the homework.)
    Check.

13. "Change to passive: 'They have cancelled the flight.'" (The flight has been cancelled.)
    Check.

14. "Change to passive: 'Someone should have warned us.'" (We should have been warned.)
    Check.

15. "Complete with wish: 'I don't have enough free time. I wish I _____.'" (had more free time)
    Check.

16. "Complete with wish: 'I ate too much at dinner. If only I _____.'" (hadn't eaten so much at dinner)
    Check.

**Block 3 --- Modules 9-12 (8 min, 8 questions):**

17. "Rewrite with an inversion: 'I had barely finished speaking when everyone started clapping.'" (Barely had I finished speaking when everyone started clapping.)
    Check.

18. "Rewrite with an inversion: 'She not only speaks English but she also speaks French.'" (Not only does she speak English, but she also speaks French.)
    Check.

19. "Replace the underlined word with a phrasal verb: 'I discovered an interesting article online.'" (I came across an interesting article online.)
    Check.

20. "Replace: 'She refused my invitation.'" (She turned down my invitation.)
    Check.

21. "Form the correct word: 'His _____ (decide) surprised everyone.'" (decision)
    Check.

22. "Form the correct word: 'The movie was very _____ (entertain).'" (entertaining)
    Check.

23. "Fix the error: 'Nobody know what happened.'" (Nobody KNOWS what happened.)
    Check.

24. "Fix the error: 'I don't have nothing to say about it.'" (I don't have ANYTHING to say about it. / I have NOTHING to say about it.)
    Check.

**Final review (6 min).** Say: "How many did you get right out of 24?" Let each student count and say their number.

If anyone got fewer than 18 correct: "You need to study tonight. Review the modules you got wrong."

Go through the 3-4 most commonly missed questions one more time. Say the correct answer. They write it. They repeat it aloud.

Say: "This is the level of the final exam. If you can do this confidently, you will pass. If you struggled, you know which modules to review."

> Track which questions were missed most. Report to coordination.

### 00:00--00:20 | ICEBREAKER: MADE ME DO IT (20 min)

Say: "Welcome back. Today's module is called The Devil Made Me Do It. We're going to learn how to talk about making people do things --- and having things done for you. But first, a warm-up."

Say: "Think about when you were a child. Your parents, your teachers, your older siblings --- they MADE you do things you didn't want to do. I'll start."

Give these 3 examples. Pause after each one:

1. "My mother made me eat vegetables every single night. I hated broccoli."
2. "My teacher made me stand in the corner when I talked too much."
3. "My older brother made me carry his backpack to school every morning."

Say: "Now it's your turn. Each of you --- tell us ONE thing someone made you do as a child. Who was it? What did they make you do? And how did you feel about it?"

Go around the room. Each student shares one. If someone says something like "My father made me TO study" --- say: "No. Listen. My father made me study." They repeat. Don't explain why there's no "to" --- just correct and move on.

After the first round, say: "Good. Second round. This time, tell us something someone DIDN'T let you do. Something you WANTED to do, but they wouldn't let you. For example: 'My parents didn't let me stay up past 9 o'clock.' Go."

Go around again. Listen for errors with "let" + bare infinitive. If someone says "didn't let me TO go" --- say: "No. Listen. Didn't let me go." They repeat.

> Don't correct grammar heavily yet --- this is a warm-up. Note on your error paper which students naturally use "to" after "make" and "let" (they shouldn't). This tells you who will struggle with Guide 1.

### 00:35--01:00 | BOOK: MODULE 10 GUIDE 1 --- ACTIVE CAUSATIVE (25 min)

Say: "Open your book to Module 10, Guide 1 --- Active Causative. This is one of the trickiest parts of English because some verbs need 'to' and some don't. Read the explanation and ALL the examples with your partner. You have 10 minutes. Read everything --- the examples, the notes, the rules. Don't skip anything."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading, not chatting.

**Last 15 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is the active causative?" (Expected: When someone causes another person to do something.)
- "Which verbs use 'to' before the action?" (ask, allow, get, tell, want, need)
- "Give me an example with ASK." (Expected: "I asked the teacher to help me.")
- "Give me an example with ALLOW." (Expected: "My parents don't allow me to go out late.")
- "Which verbs do NOT use 'to'?" (help, let, make, have)
- "Give me an example with MAKE." (Expected: "My boss made me work overtime.")
- "Give me an example with LET." (Expected: "She let me use her phone.")
- "Why do some verbs use 'to' and others don't? What's the logic?" (If the action hasn't started yet, we tend to use "to." If it's already happening or directly caused, no "to.")

**After checking all pairs, give them these 6 quick-fire situations. Say each one aloud:**

1. "Your boss says you must work Saturday." --- How do you say it? (Expected: "My boss made me work on Saturday." or "My boss told me to work on Saturday.")
2. "You want your friend to help you move apartments." (Expected: "I asked my friend to help me move." or "I got my friend to help me move.")
3. "A mother says her child can watch TV." (Expected: "She let her child watch TV." or "She allowed her child to watch TV.")
4. "A teacher says the students must read chapter 5." (Expected: "The teacher told the students to read chapter 5." or "The teacher made the students read chapter 5.")
5. "You help your sister finish a project." (Expected: "I helped my sister finish her project.")
6. "You want your brother to drive you to the airport." (Expected: "I want my brother to drive me to the airport." or "I need my brother to drive me to the airport.")

One situation per student. If they freeze, say: "Start with the person. Then pick a causative verb: make, let, have, get, ask, tell, allow, help. Then add the person and the action." They try again.

### 01:15--01:45 | PRODUCTION: THE BOSS (30 min)

Say: "We're going to play a game called The Boss. One of you is the boss of a small company. You have 5 employees." Point at the 5 other students. "The boss gives orders. But every order MUST use a causative verb --- make, let, have, get, ask, tell, allow, help, want, or need. And the employees must respond."

**Write on the board:**

```
BOSS VERBS:
WITH "to": ask ___ to ___, tell ___ to ___, get ___ to ___,
           want ___ to ___, need ___ to ___, allow ___ to ___
WITHOUT "to": make ___ ___, let ___ ___, have ___ ___, help ___ ___
```

**Demo first.** You are the boss. Point at students and give orders:

1. "María, I need you to finish the report by 3 PM."
2. "Carlos, I'm going to make you present to the client tomorrow."
3. "Valentina, I won't let you leave early today."
4. "Santiago, I want you to call every client on the list."
5. "Laura, I'll have Andrés help you with the presentation."

Say: "Did you hear the difference? Some had 'to,' some didn't. Now --- who wants to be the first boss?"

**Round 1 (6 min):** First boss. They must give at least 5 orders, each using a DIFFERENT causative verb. Employees respond naturally: "OK, I'll do it." / "That's not fair!" / "Can I do it tomorrow instead?" If the boss uses "to" where they shouldn't (or vice versa), the employee says: "I don't understand that order, boss." The boss must try again.

**Round 2 (6 min):** Second boss. Same rules.

**Round 3 (6 min):** Third boss. Now add a twist --- say: "This boss is TERRIBLE. You're the worst boss ever. You make people do ridiculous things." Examples: "I'm going to make you clean the bathroom with a toothbrush." "I won't allow you to sit down all day." "I need you to count every paperclip in the office."

**Round 4 (6 min):** Fourth boss. Twist: "This boss is AMAZING. The best boss ever. You let people do whatever they want and you help them with everything." Examples: "I'll let you leave early every Friday." "I'll have the company pay for your lunch." "I want you to take a vacation --- you deserve it."

**Remaining 6 min:** Fifth and sixth bosses. Quick rounds --- 3 min each. By now students should be more comfortable. Push for speed and creativity.

> Walk around with error paper the ENTIRE time. This is the core production for Guide 1. Write down every "to" error. If the same student makes the same error 3 times, stop them quietly and say: "Listen. MAKE me go. Not make me TO go. Say it." They repeat. Then continue.

### 02:05--02:25 | BOOK: MODULE 10 GUIDE 2 --- PASSIVE CAUSATIVE (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Guide 2 --- Passive Causative. This is DIFFERENT from what we just learned. Read the explanation and examples. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. Walk around silently.

**Last 13 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is the passive causative?" (Expected: We use it when someone else does something for us, but we don't say who.)
- "What is the form?" (Subject + have/get + object + past participle)
- "What's the difference between 'have' and 'get' here?" (Expected: "Get" is more common and informal. "Have" is more formal.)
- "Give me an example with GET." (Expected: "I got my hair done." / "I got my car washed.")
- "Give me an example with HAVE." (Expected: "I had my suit cleaned." / "I had the house painted.")
- "Is this the same as 'I cut my hair'?" (No. "I cut my hair" = I did it myself. "I got my hair cut" = someone else cut it for me.)

**After checking all pairs, give them these 6 situations. Say each one aloud:**

1. "You went to the mechanic. Your car is fixed now." (Expected: "I got my car fixed." or "I had my car fixed.")
2. "You went to the dentist. A tooth was removed." (Expected: "I got a tooth pulled." or "I had a tooth pulled.")
3. "You hired a painter. Your house looks great now." (Expected: "I got my house painted." or "I had my house painted.")
4. "You went to a tailor. Your pants are shorter now." (Expected: "I got my pants shortened." or "I had my pants altered.")
5. "You took your dog to the groomer." (Expected: "I got my dog groomed." or "I had my dog groomed.")
6. "You're at a restaurant and your steak isn't cooked enough. You want the waiter to cook it more." (Expected: "Can I get my steak cooked a bit more?" or "I'd like to have my steak cooked a bit more.")

One situation per student. If they freeze, say: "Start with 'I got...' or 'I had...' then the thing, then what happened to it --- as a past participle."

### 02:25--02:45 | SHADOWING 5 STEPS (20 min)

YouTube on your phone. Video from break. Subtitles on student phones.

**STEP 1 --- LISTEN (3 min):** Say: "Just listen. Don't repeat. Don't read. Just listen to the rhythm, the speed, the emotion." Play the clip 3 times.

**STEP 2 --- WITH TEXT (4 min):** Say: "Now pull up the subtitles on your phone. Listen and repeat WITH the text. Try to match the speaker's speed and intonation." Play 4 times. They repeat along with the audio.

**STEP 3 --- WITHOUT TEXT (4 min):** Say: "Screens OFF. Face down. Listen and repeat from memory. You won't get every word --- that's fine. Get the rhythm." Play 4 times.

**STEP 4 --- EYES CLOSED (3 min):** Say: "Close your eyes. Maximum concentration. Repeat everything you hear." Play 3 times.

**STEP 5 --- PAIR ROLE PLAY (6 min):** Say: "No audio. Pairs face each other. Take turns --- one person says a chunk from memory, the other continues. Try to reconstruct the whole clip from memory. Match the tone, speed, and emotion."

### 02:45--03:05 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: STORY WITH 12 ERRORS (20 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 12 errors --- all related to the causative, both active AND passive. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Last month, my friend Camila had a terrible week. On Monday, her boss made her TO work until midnight. **(1 --- made her WORK --- no 'to' after 'make')** She was exhausted but her boss didn't let her TO go home early on Tuesday either. **(2 --- didn't let her GO --- no 'to' after 'let')**

On Wednesday, Camila wanted that her boyfriend pick her up from work. **(3 --- wanted her boyfriend TO PICK her up --- 'want + person + to + verb', no 'that')** He said he was busy, so she asked her sister drive her home. **(4 --- asked her sister TO DRIVE her --- 'ask' needs 'to')**

Thursday was even worse. Her car broke down. She had to get her car to fix at the mechanic. **(5 --- get her car FIXED --- passive causative: get + object + past participle, no 'to')** The mechanic told her he would have the car repairing by Friday. **(6 --- have the car REPAIRED --- past participle, not gerund)**

On Friday morning, Camila went to the salon. She wanted to have her hair to dye before a party. **(7 --- have her hair DYED --- no 'to', past participle directly after object)** She also got her nails to paint. **(8 --- got her nails PAINTED --- no 'to', past participle directly after object)**

After the salon, she picked up her car. But the mechanic hadn't fixed it properly! She made him to fix it again. **(9 --- made him FIX it --- no 'to' after 'make')** She told to the mechanic that she was very angry. **(10 --- told THE MECHANIC --- no extra 'to' after 'tell' when there's a direct object)**

On Saturday, Camila finally relaxed. She had her apartment to clean by a cleaning service. **(11 --- had her apartment CLEANED --- no 'to', past participle)** Then she got a massage and she let the therapist to use hot stones. **(12 --- let the therapist USE --- no 'to' after 'let')**

What a week."

---

After reading: review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version first, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice.

**Pairs (5 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: say 4 sentences about your week using the causative --- but put deliberate errors in at least 2 of them. Partner B: catch and correct. After 2.5 minutes, SWITCH."

Walk around. Write down good corrections AND missed errors on your error paper.

> If the class catches fewer than 8 errors in the story, they need more practice with the to/no-to distinction. Note this for coordination.

### 03:05--03:20 | PRODUCTION: THE SERVICE CENTER (15 min)

Say: "Now you're at a service center. This is a place where you get EVERYTHING done. Hair, car, clothes, house, phone --- everything. You are customers. You need to explain what you want done. You MUST use the passive causative: 'I want to get ___ done' or 'I need to have ___ done.'"

**Write on the board:**

```
THE SERVICE CENTER --- SERVICES AVAILABLE:
1. Hair salon
2. Mechanic / Car wash
3. Tailor / Clothing alterations
4. Phone repair
5. House painting / Plumbing / Electrical
6. Pet grooming
7. Photography studio (passport photos, portraits)
8. Printing / Document services
```

**Step 1 --- Prepare (3 min).**
Say: "You are a customer. Think of 4 things you need to get done today. Write them down using the passive causative."

Write on the board:
```
MODELS:
"I need to get my hair cut and dyed."
"I want to have my car washed and waxed."
"I'd like to get my pants shortened."
"I need to have my phone screen replaced."
"Can I get my passport photos taken?"
"I want to have my dog groomed."
```

**Step 2 --- Role Play: Customer & Receptionist (12 min).**
Pair them up. One is the CUSTOMER, the other is the SERVICE CENTER RECEPTIONIST. The customer explains what they need done. The receptionist asks questions and confirms.

Say: "Customer --- you explain what you need. Receptionist --- you ask follow-up questions. Like: 'How do you want your hair done? Short? Long? What color?' or 'When do you need your car fixed by?'"

**Demo with one student:**
- You (customer): "Hi, I need to get my suit cleaned and pressed. I also want to have the buttons replaced --- they're falling off."
- Student (receptionist): "OK, when do you need the suit ready?"
- You: "I need to have it done by Friday. I have a wedding."
- Student: "We can have it cleaned by Thursday. The buttons will take an extra day."
- You: "Can I also get my shoes polished while I wait?"

Each pair does 4 minutes. Then SWITCH roles. 4 more minutes. Then switch PARTNERS and do 4 more minutes.

**Step 3 --- The Complaint (10 min).**
Say: "Now --- you went to this service center, and they RUINED everything. You're going back to complain. Tell the receptionist what went wrong."

Write on the board:
```
COMPLAINTS:
"I had my hair dyed but you made it GREEN!"
"I got my car washed but now there's a scratch!"
"I had my pants shortened but they're TOO short!"
"I got my phone fixed but the screen is cracked again!"
```

New pairs. One is the angry customer, the other is the receptionist trying to fix the problem. 5 minutes per pair. Encourage drama. The angrier, the better --- as long as they use the passive causative.

> Error paper. Walk around the ENTIRE time. This activity reveals whether they truly understand passive causative vs. active causative. Write down every error. Pay special attention to: "I got my hair to cut" (wrong), "I had my car fix" (wrong --- not past participle), "I made them to fix it" (wrong --- no 'to' with make).

### ☐ 00:00–00:05 | VATS: VIRTUE OF THE WEEK — PRUDENCE (5 min)

**Virtue of the week: PRUDENCE (smart decisions, patience, discipline)**

Write on the board:
```
PRUDENCE — "What does it mean to be prudent in your career? Give an example."
```

Say: "Before we start, this week we work on PRUDENCE — the first Heiiu virtue. This is a daily ritual. The question changes within the same virtue, but the virtue rotates weekly."

**V (1 min):** Read the question aloud. Point to the board.
**A (30 sec):** Say: "Think in silence."
**T (2 min):** "Pairs. Discuss. English only — give it a try."
**S (1.5 min):** 2 students share with the class. No correction now — celebrate the attempt.

> The VATS is the daily opening ritual. Tomorrow it starts the same way with PRUDENCE day 2. Virtues rotate weekly: Prudence → Temperance → Justice → Fortitude. See `CALENDARIO_VIRTUDES_2026`.

### ☐ 00:05–00:20 | ICEBREAKER: SPEED INTRODUCTIONS (15 min)
This is Day 1. Nobody knows each other (or they think they do). Your job: get them talking IMMEDIATELY.

**Round 1 — 90 sec each (9 min).** Pairs face each other. Each person has 90 sec to tell the other EVERYTHING about themselves — name, job, why they're learning English, what they do for fun. Then switch. After 90 sec, clap — they rotate to a new partner. 3 rotations = everyone meets at least 3 people.

**Round 2 — Present someone else (6 min).** Each student introduces ONE person they just met to the class. "This is Juan. He works in marketing and he's learning English because..." They CANNOT introduce themselves. They introduce someone else.

> If someone speaks Spanish: "English only. Try again." No exceptions from minute one.

### ☐ 00:20–00:40 | CLASSROOM RULES + EXPECTATIONS (20 min)
**Do NOT lecture.** Write 5 questions on the board. Pairs discuss for 8 min. Then class shares answers.

1. "Why do you think the teacher should talk LESS than 20% of the time?"
2. "Why should your phone be face-down during class?"
3. "What happens if you only listen but never speak?"
4. "Why do you think we correct errors by repeating the right version, not by explaining rules?"
5. "What's the difference between a teacher and a guide?"

**After sharing (5 min):** Read the rules out loud ONE time. They don't need to agree. They need to understand.

**Rules (write on board):**
- Phone FACE-DOWN. Always.
- You speak 80%. I speak 20%.
- I will NEVER explain grammar. I say the correct version, you repeat.
- You correct yourselves using Answer Keys.
- Every class has a self-check. Fill it completely. No blank fields.
- You will record yourselves on video. The videos are for YOU — to track your progress.

**GoldList setup (7 min):** Hand out notebooks (or tell them to bring one tomorrow). Explain: "This is your GoldList notebook. Every class you will write 7-10 new PHRASES — not single words. Phrases. With context. This notebook is sacred. You will use it for 50 classes."

> Don't over-explain GoldList. Show them by example. Write on the board: ❌ "achieve" → ✅ "I want to achieve my goals this year." That's it. They'll learn by doing.

### ☐ 00:40–01:05 | DIAGNOSTIC: CONVERSATION CAROUSEL (25 min)
**What is this?** You need to know where each student is. This is NOT a test — it's a conversation. But you ARE evaluating. Have your diagnostic sheet ready (one per student).

**5 stations, 5 min each.** Pairs rotate through topics. YOU walk around, listen, and take notes on each student's diagnostic sheet. Don't interrupt. Don't correct. Just LISTEN and WRITE.

**Station 1 — Personal:** "Tell your partner about your daily routine in detail."
**Station 2 — Opinion:** "Do you think university is worth it? Why or why not?"
**Station 3 — Narrative:** "Tell your partner about the best trip you ever took. What happened?"
**Station 4 — Hypothetical:** "If you could change one thing about your country, what would it be?"
**Station 5 — Description:** "Describe your best friend in as much detail as possible — personality, appearance, habits."

**What you're evaluating (mark on diagnostic sheet):**
- Can they use past tenses correctly?
- Can they give opinions with reasons?
- Can they describe in detail?
- Can they use conditionals (even basic)?
- Do they use connectors (but, however, although, because)?
- What is their fluency level? (hesitant / functional / smooth)
- What are their biggest grammar gaps?

> This tells you where the class REALLY is. Not where the book says they should be.

### ☐ 01:05–01:25 | BASELINE GRABO: VIDEO 0 (20 min)
**What is this?** This is their FIRST video. It's the baseline — the "before" picture. In 50 classes they'll watch this again and see how far they've come.

**Topic (write on board):** "Talk about yourself for 2 minutes. Who are you? What do you do? What are your goals with English? What are you afraid of?"

**Prepare (5 min).** They can write bullet points (NOT a script). Key words only.
**Record (12 min).** One at a time or in pairs (one records the other). 2 min per student. NO retakes. The first take IS the take. That's the point — it's raw.
**Send (3 min).** Everyone sends their video to the teacher via WhatsApp.

> Say: "This video is only for you and for me. Nobody else sees it. In 6 months you'll watch it and laugh at how much you've improved."

### 01:25–01:45 | BREAK (20 min)
Students use phones. YOU:
• Search YouTube for a Shadowing clip (60-90 sec, clear English, someone talking about themselves — introduction, vlog, interview).
• Review your diagnostic notes. Identify the 2-3 biggest gaps across the class.

### ☐ 01:45–02:10 | SHADOWING: INTRODUCTION (25 min)
**This is their first time doing Shadowing. Explain the concept ONCE:**
"Shadowing means you listen to someone speak and you repeat EXACTLY what they say, at the same time — like a shadow. You copy their words, their rhythm, their pronunciation. It feels weird at first. That's normal."

**Demo (3 min).** Play 10 seconds of the video. YOU shadow it live so they see what it looks like. Then say: "Your turn."

**STEP 1 — LISTEN (4 min):** Play the full clip. Just listen. No repeating.
**STEP 2 — WITH TEXT (5 min):** Play again. Subtitles on. They repeat along with the speaker.
**STEP 3 — WITHOUT TEXT (5 min):** Screens OFF. Play again. They repeat without reading.
**STEP 4 — EYES CLOSED (4 min):** Maximum concentration. They repeat with eyes closed.
**STEP 5 — PAIR ROLE PLAY (4 min):** No audio. Pairs try to recreate what they heard from memory.

> First time will be messy. That's fine. Say: "It gets easier every class. By class 10 you'll be amazed."

### ☐ 02:10–02:30 | INTAKE RITUAL — LETTER / CAPSULE (20 min)

**⚠️ Read and follow `ANEXO_CARTA_YO_FUTURO.pdf` and `ANEXO_CAPSULA_30_DIAS.pdf` — coordination delivers them with your permanent kit.**

**How to decide who does what — ask the group:**

> "Who is in Heiiu for the FIRST TIME?" (raise hand)
> "Who is continuing from B1?"

| Student | Does |
|---|---|
| New to the Heiiu system (first day at any level) | **Letter to Future Self** — sealed until graduation day |
| Already wrote a Letter (came from B1) | **30-Day Capsule** — returned exactly in 30 days |

**Setup (1 min):** Hand out the corresponding sheet + envelope per student. Set a 16-min timer visible.

**Introduction (3 min):** Speak slowly, calmly:

> "Before we go further into B2, we do a Heiiu ritual. Two versions depending on who you are.
>
> If today is your first day at Heiiu, you'll write a Letter to your Future Self — to the version of you on graduation day, when you finish B2. That letter gets sealed and stays in coordination's archive until that day. Nobody reads it but you, on that day.
>
> If you came from B1, you've already written your Letter. Today you write a 30-Day Capsule — a sheet with 4 questions, sealed, that comes back to your hand in exactly 30 days. It's a mini-letter to your near future.
>
> You can write in Spanish, English, or mixed. The only thing that matters is that it's honest. 14 minutes. Total silence."

**Individual writing (14 min):** Total silence. Visible timer. You sit in a corner. Do NOT walk by reading what they write. If a hand goes up, lean in and whisper: "whatever is true — there's no correct."

**Sealing & handover (2 min):** "Fold the sheet. Seal it in the envelope. Write your full name and today's date on the outside. If it's a Capsule, also write 'OPEN ON [today + 30 days]'."

Collect everything in a box. **Hand this box to coordination TODAY.** Letters and Capsules are filed separately.

> **If everyone is new:** all do Letter. If everyone is continuing: all do Capsule. If mixed: both rituals run in parallel — same silence, same timer. Full protocol in the annexes.

### ☐ 02:30–02:50 | DIAGNOSTIC: WRITING SAMPLE (20 min)
**What is this?** Written diagnostic. You need to see their writing level too — not just speaking.

**Topic (write on board):** "Write about a moment that changed your life. What happened? Why was it important? How are you different because of it? Write at least 10 sentences."

**They write (15 min).** Silently. No help from partners. No phones. No dictionaries. This is diagnostic — you need to see their REAL level.
**Collect (5 min).** Collect all papers. Tell them: "I'll read these and give you feedback next class. This is NOT graded — it helps me understand where you are."

> Read these at home. Note: tense usage, sentence structure, vocabulary range, connector usage, spelling patterns. This + the conversation diagnostic = full picture of each student.

### ☐ 02:50–03:10 | TBLT: PLAN YOUR DREAM WEEKEND (20 min)
**How it works:** Pairs plan a dream weekend together. Budget: unlimited. Only rule: they must agree on EVERYTHING and present the plan to the class.

**Plan (10 min).** Pairs discuss and write their weekend plan: Where would they go? What would they do Friday night, Saturday, Sunday? What would they eat? Where would they stay? They must agree — if they disagree, they negotiate.

**Present (10 min).** Each pair presents their dream weekend (3 min max). Class votes: which weekend would you steal?

> Light, fun activity after the writing diagnostic. Lets them end on a positive note.

### ☐ 03:10–03:25 | GOLDLIST: FIRST ENTRY (15 min)
**This is their first GoldList entry.** Walk them through it.

Say: "Open your notebook. Write today's date. Now write 10 phrases from today — things you heard, things you wanted to say but couldn't, new words from the conversations. Write the FULL phrase, not just one word."

**Examples on board:**
- ❌ "abroad" → ✅ "I want to work abroad next year."
- ❌ "improve" → ✅ "I need to improve my pronunciation."
- ❌ "achieve" → ✅ "She achieved her goals by working hard."

**They write (10 min).** Walk around. Check they're writing phrases, not single words. If someone writes a single word: "Make it a phrase. Use it in a sentence."

**Share (5 min).** 2-3 students share their favorite phrase. Class repeats it once.

> GoldList is SACRED. It happens every class. This is the ritual they need to internalize from day one.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | SPEED DATING: GET TO KNOW YOU (17 min)
2 rows of chairs facing each other (or standing). Teacher gives a question. Students have 2 min to discuss. Then one row shifts. 6 rounds.

1. "What's one thing you're really good at?"
2. "What's the most embarrassing thing that's happened to you?"
3. "What would your life look like in 5 years if everything goes perfectly?"
4. "What's something most people don't know about you?"
5. "If you could master any skill overnight, what would it be?"
6. "What's the best advice you've ever received?"

> End-of-day energy: keep it personal, fun, and flowing. Everyone standing = more energy.

### ☐ 03:42–03:52 | SELF-CHECK (10 min)
Hand out self-check sheet. Students fill ALL fields. COLLECT. CHECK EVERY ONE: blank field = hand it back, fill it NOW. No incomplete sheets accepted. Send to coordination TODAY.

> First self-check ever. Walk them through it: "Fill every box. Be honest. This is for YOUR progress, not a grade."

### ☐ 03:52–03:57 | HOMEWORK + CLOSE (5 min)
"Homework: Watch your video from today. Write 3 things you notice about your English — good or bad. Bring it tomorrow. Also: bring your GoldList notebook every class. See you tomorrow."

### ☐ 00:00–00:15 | ICEBREAKER: THIS OR THAT (15 min)
Put an imaginary line in the middle of the room. Read two options. Students move to LEFT for option A or RIGHT for option B. After each round, 1-2 students explain their choice using either/neither/both. Example: "I chose coffee because I like neither tea nor herbal drinks." 10 rounds, fast.

1. Coffee or Tea? 2. Beach or Mountains? 3. Morning person or Night owl? 4. Cook at home or Eat out? 5. Travel alone or With friends? 6. City or Country? 7. Books or Movies? 8. Summer or Winter? 9. Save money or Spend money? 10. Work from home or Office?

> Gets them moving and talking. They'll naturally start using either/neither/both without realizing it.

### ☐ 00:15–00:30 | ANALIZO: VIDEO REVIEW (15 min)
Homework from last class: watch your GRABO video and write 3 errors you heard.

**First 5 min:** In pairs, share your 3 errors. Help each other correct them.
**Last 10 min:** Each student shares 1 error with the class. ### ☐ 00:30–00:45 | BOOK: MODULE 3 — EITHER/NEITHER/BOTH (15 min)
**What it's about:** Either...or = choosing between two options ("You can have either tea or coffee"). Neither...nor = rejecting both options ("Neither the cat nor the dog wanted to go outside"). Both...and = including two options together ("Both my parents are doctors"). Either = one of two. Neither = not one, not the other. Both = the two together.

Say: "Open your B1 book to Module 3. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 7 minutes."

**First 7 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading.
**Last 8 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain either/neither/both to you. They give you examples. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you.

### ☐ 01:05–01:45 | DEBATE CLUB: EITHER...OR (40 min)
**How it works:** 3 pairs. Each pair picks 1 topic. One student argues FOR, the other AGAINST. They MUST use either/neither/both in their arguments. That's the rule — if they don't use them, stop the debate: "Use either, neither, or both in your argument. Try again."

**Topics (write all 3 on the board):**
1. "Either all schools should teach only in English, or students should learn in their native language first."
2. "Either social media should be banned for teenagers, or parents should have no control over it."
3. "Either universities should be free for everyone, or only the best students should get scholarships."

**Choose topic (3 min).** Each pair picks one.
**Prepare arguments (7 min).** Each student writes their argument. They MUST include at least 3 sentences using either/neither/both. They can help each other prepare, but they will argue opposite sides.
**Debate (20 min).** Each pair debates (5-6 min each). The other 4 students watch. After each debate, the audience votes: who was more convincing?
**Class correction (10 min).** You read errors you caught during the debates. Class corrects together. "No. Listen." + correct version. Repeat 2x.

> Walk around WITH error paper during debates. WRITE DOWN every error you hear.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG either/neither/both usage. Read slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch.

"Last weekend my friend Carlos and I went shopping. We saw two jackets. Carlos said, 'You can have both the blue or the black one.' **(1 — either...or, not both...or)** I couldn't decide, so I took either of them. **(2 — both, since he took two)**

We went to eat. The waiter said, 'We have neither pizza or pasta today.' **(3 — neither...nor, not neither...or)** Carlos wanted neither — he was starving! **(4 — both, since he was hungry he wanted them)** I told him, 'Either relax. The food will come.' **(5 — Just relax. 'Either' doesn't work alone here)**

After lunch, two movies were playing. Both were horror films. Carlos hates horror. He said, 'I like either of these movies.' **(6 — neither, since he hates them)** I said, 'Come on, let's enjoy neither of them together!' **(7 — both)**

We called two friends to join us. Either of them answered the phone. **(8 — Neither, since nobody answered)** At the theater they had two sizes of popcorn. Carlos said, 'I want neither the small or the large.' **(9 — neither...nor)** I said, 'I'll buy either for us.' **(10 — both, since he's buying for two people)**

The movie was about two thieves. Either of them escaped. **(11 — Neither, nobody escaped)** Carlos said, 'Both the story or the acting was terrible.' **(12 — Either...or OR Neither...nor)** I disagreed: 'I think either the music and the effects were great.' **(13 — both...and, not either...and)** We agreed we should have stayed home. He said, 'Let's neither go to movies or go shopping next weekend.' **(14 — neither...nor)** I said, 'Fine, we can have both pizza or sushi at home.' **(15 — either...or, not both...or)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** 5 min each. One makes deliberate either/neither/both errors, the other corrects. Switch roles.

### ☐ 02:45–03:05 | TRANSLANGUAGING: CHOICE DILEMMAS (20 min)
**What is this?** This is the ONLY activity where students can briefly use Spanish — as a BRIDGE, not a crutch. Students get 30 seconds of Spanish to understand/brainstorm, then they MUST switch to English and present their answer using either/neither/both.

**How to do it:** Read one dilemma. Pairs get 30 sec in Spanish to process. Then 3-4 min in English to discuss and present. Each pair shares their answer with the class. They MUST use either/neither/both. If they don't, stop them: "Use either, neither, or both." 3 dilemmas total.

**Dilemma 1:** "Your best friend and your partner both invite you to dinner on the same night. You can go to either one, but not both. What do you do?"
**Dilemma 2:** "You get two job offers: one pays well but is boring, the other is exciting but pays little. You can accept either one. What do you choose?"
**Dilemma 3:** "Neither your flight nor your hotel reservation is confirmed, and you leave tomorrow. What do you do?"

> The Spanish is only to unblock understanding. The moment they have the idea, it's English only.

### ☐ 03:05–03:25 | STEAM: SURVEY & REPORT (20 min)
**What is this?** Students create a mini survey, collect data, and report results using either/neither/both. This combines communication with basic data analysis.

**Create survey (3 min):** Each pair writes 4 either/or questions on paper. Example: "Do you prefer either dogs or cats?" "Would you rather either travel to Europe or Asia?"

**Survey (7 min):** Students walk around and ask ALL classmates their questions. Write down names and answers.

**Report (10 min):** Each pair presents results to the class. They MUST use either/neither/both. Example: "Neither Juan nor Maria chose cats. Both Sofia and Carlos preferred Europe. Either Pedro or Ana was undecided." Class asks follow-up questions.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | SPEED DATING: CHOICES (17 min)
2 rows of chairs facing each other (or standing). Teacher gives a scenario. Students have 2 min to discuss using either/neither/both. Then one row shifts. 6 rounds.

1. "You can either live in any country for free OR get $1 million but stay here forever."
2. "Neither your phone nor your computer works for a month. How do you survive?"
3. "Both your favorite band AND your favorite team play on the same night. Which do you go to?"
4. "You can either speak every language OR play every instrument."
5. "Neither coffee nor energy drinks exist anymore. What do you do?"
6. "Both time travel AND teleportation are real. Which do you use more?"

> WALK AROUND writing errors. Everyone standing = more energy.

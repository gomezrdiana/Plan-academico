### 02:05--02:30 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: MEGA STORY --- 15 ERRORS FROM ALL MODULES (25 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a long story. This story has 15 errors --- from ALL 12 modules. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. This is the biggest Correct the Teacher we've ever done. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"Let me tell you about my friend Marco. I have been know Marco since we were children. **(1 --- I have KNOWN Marco --- present perfect, not present perfect continuous with 'know')**

He is the kind of person which everyone likes immediately. **(2 --- the kind of person WHO/THAT everyone likes --- relative clause: 'which' for things, 'who/that' for people)**

When we were teenagers, Marco decided he wanted to become a musician. His parents told him that he has to go to university first. **(3 --- told him that he HAD to --- reported speech: backshift 'has' to 'had')**

But Marco didn't listen. He said: 'If I would follow my dream now, I will be successful.' **(4 --- 'If I follow my dream now, I will be successful' --- first conditional, no 'would' in the if-clause)**

So he moved to another city. He had the landlord to fix his apartment before he moved in. **(5 --- He had the landlord FIX his apartment / He had his apartment FIXED --- causative: 'have someone do' or 'have something done,' no 'to')**

In the new city, nobody know him. **(6 --- nobody KNEW him --- 3rd person singular past tense, also tense consistency)**

He wished he doesn't leave his hometown. **(7 --- He wished he HADN'T LEFT --- wish + past perfect for past regrets)**

But he kept going. He started playing in bars. One night, a music producer saw him. The producer said: 'I have never seen such a talented musician.' He was so impressed that he said: 'Not only I will sign you, but I will also produce your first album.' **(8 --- 'Not only WILL I sign you' --- inversion after 'not only')**

Marco couldn't believe it. His first album was record in a professional studio. **(9 --- was RECORDED --- passive voice: was + past participle)**

By the time the album came out, Marco has been working on his music for five years. **(10 --- Marco HAD been working --- past perfect continuous, not present perfect continuous, because we're talking about a past point in time)**

The album was a huge success. Everyone were talking about it. **(11 --- Everyone WAS talking --- 'everyone' takes 3rd person singular)**

Marco called his parents. He said: 'I have made it. I want to have a big house build for you.' **(12 --- have a big house BUILT --- causative: have something done, past participle)**

His mother started crying. She said that she always knew he would be succeed. **(13 --- he would SUCCEED --- 'would' + base form, not 'be succeed')**

Today, Marco has everything. He has been living in his dream house for three years. He got married to a woman who he met at one of his concerts --- the woman which sat in the front row every single night. **(14 --- the woman WHO sat --- relative clause: 'who' for people)**

Last week, I asked Marco: 'Do you have any regrets?' He said: 'If I hadn't took that risk, I wouldn't be here today.' **(15 --- If I hadn't TAKEN that risk --- past participle: take/took/taken)**

And he's right. Sometimes you have to risk everything to find out what you're capable of."

Make sure you have a clear introduction, body, and conclusion. Make sure you are using structures from multiple modules --- not just one or two. I will come to each of you individually."

**How this works --- you do NOT sit down and wait:**
- You have 6 students. That is about 4 minutes per student for individual feedback.
- Walk to each student. Read what they have so far. Ask them:
  - "What is your topic? Give me your thesis in one sentence."
  - "Walk me through your outline. What are your main points?"
  - "Show me where you're using structures from different modules. I want to see at least 6 different module structures in your presentation."
  - "Time yourself. Read your introduction and first section aloud to me right now. Are you on pace for 7 to 10 minutes?"
  - "What questions do you think the class will ask you at the end? Prepare for those."

- Give SPECIFIC feedback:
  - "Your introduction is too long. Cut this sentence."
  - "You need a conditional here. Try: 'If this hadn't happened, I wouldn't have...'"
  - "Use the passive here: 'The decision was made by...' instead of 'They made the decision.'"
  - "Add an inversion for emphasis: 'Never had I imagined that...'"
  - "This section has no phrasal verbs. Add one: 'I came across...' or 'I ended up...'"
  - "Where are your indefinite pronouns? Add: 'Everyone in my life has influenced...' or 'Nobody expected...'"

- If a student has very little written: "You are behind. Write your outline right now. Three main points. One sentence each. I will be back in 4 minutes to check."

**Remind them:**
Say: "Your final presentation is 7 to 10 minutes. Any topic. No reading from paper --- only note cards with key words. The class will ask questions at the end. You must answer those questions well. If you haven't started, you are seriously behind. Three weeks."

> This is NOT free time. This is structured work time where you actively coach each student.

### STAGE A — PRESENTATION MONOLOGUE (~6 min, target 5-7 min)

Setup (~30 sec): presenter to front. Visible countdown (counts up). Audience: other 4 in semicircle, eyes on speaker, no papers. Teacher silent with private notebook.

During: DO NOT interrupt. DO NOT correct. DO NOT audibly nod. Note internal structure (4 seams + 3 PILLARS + HOOK + CTA + physical brake + intonation + any returning fillers + B2 anchors used from Sets #1-#21).

If she trips >10 sec during monologue: ONE short prompt to NEXT SEAM (*"and from there..."*), never content. If still stuck >10 sec more, *"take a breath, where were you?"* and let her continue from where she remembers.

### STAGE B — LIVE Q&A (~5 min)

Open (10 sec): *"The floor is open for questions. Group, you go first."*

Group questions (2-3 questions, ~2-3 min): other 4 students ask REAL questions about her topic. Regulate: one per person max in this turn. If someone asks trap-question ("what's your favorite food?" when topic was food culture), redirect: *"That's a personal question, not about her topic — ask something about what she just told us."*

Teacher questions (1-2 questions, ~2 min): 1-2 B2-tier questions. NOT trick, NOT malice. Real questions a B2 evaluator would ask. Type:
- If topic was *Colombian food*: *"You mentioned arepas are central to your region. If a foreigner has tried arepas and felt they were 'just bread,' what would you say to help them see what they missed?"* (B2 asks reframe + perspective shift + cultural translation).
- If topic was *Colombian music & dance*: *"If you had to introduce someone who has never heard cumbia to the genre using only ONE song, which one and why?"* (B2 asks criterion + justification).
- If topic was *Colombian regions*: *"What is one assumption people make about your region that you'd push back on, and what's the evidence you'd give?"* (B2 asks critical thinking + evidence).
- If topic was *Why study English/Spanish in Colombia*: *"You argued X is the main reason. If someone challenged you with Y reason, how would you address that?"* (B2 asks counter-argument).
- If topic was *Colombian people: stereotypes vs reality*: *"Pick ONE stereotype you didn't address in your monologue, and acknowledge it honestly."* (B2 asks honesty + nuance).
- If topic was *Colombian innovation & business*: *"What's one limitation of the case you made — where would your argument get weaker?"* (B2 asks self-critique).
- If topic was *Why come to Colombia*: *"If a tourist with very limited time and budget asked you to qualify your top 3 reasons — which one would you say is non-negotiable?"* (B2 asks qualify + prioritize).

If stuck in Q&A >5 sec: ONE short prompt pointing to Set #22, *"you can clarify with..."* or *"you can address that from..."*. NOT the exact answer. If still stuck after prompt, *"that one's on hold — let's move to the next."* and next question.

If she recites a monologue chunk instead of responding: let her finish the chunk, then *"that was your PILLAR 2 — but the question was about X. Try again — 30 seconds, address X directly."* One re-try opportunity only.

## 13. ERROR PAPER LIVE — 4 min — STAND UP

```
Typical errors from live Q&A today:

1. ❌ Q: "What's the biggest challenge in your topic?"
   A: "I don't know, that's a hard question." (collapsed, no bridge)
   → ✅ "Let me acknowledge that the biggest challenge depends on the angle —
         from one side it's X, from another it's Y. I'd address X first because..."
        (acknowledge + address, Set #22 in action)

2. ❌ Q: "Why is arepa central to your region?"
   A: "Well, arepa is a traditional food that comes from..." (RECITES opening of PILLAR 1)
   → ✅ "Let me clarify — the question is WHY central, not WHAT it is. Central because
         three things: daily presence, family memory, regional identity. Strongest is..."
        (clarify + reorganize + address)

3. ❌ B2-demanding Q: "If someone said your argument has a weak side, where would they push?"
   A: "Yes but my argument is good because..." (defensive, no acknowledge)
   → ✅ "Honestly, the weakest side of my argument is X — I have to acknowledge that.
         What I'd qualify is..." (honesty + qualify + owning the weakness B2)

4. ❌ 12 seconds of silence without bridge after a hard question (visible panic)
   → ✅ 5 sec silence + "Let me clarify what I think you're asking" + respond.
         CALM silence is B2; PANIC silence is B1. Temperance day 4 = the difference.

5. ❌ Mid-Q&A presenter asked "can you repeat the question?" when she heard it fine
   (strategy to gain time)
   → ✅ Better: "Let me take a second on that" + 3 sec reflective silence + respond.
         Asking repetition when not needed sounds evasive to B2.

6. ❌ During monologue: SEAM #3 rushed because presenter knew Q&A came after
   (temperance day 3 broke under expectation of day 4)
   → ✅ Monologue executes as in Phase 4 — 4 calm seams. Q&A is ANOTHER exercise
         that follows. Don't anticipate, don't rush monologue out of Q&A fear.

7. ❌ Group feedback: someone said "she did better than the first one" (broke format)
```

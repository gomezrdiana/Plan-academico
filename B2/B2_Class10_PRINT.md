### ☐ 00:00–00:15 | ICEBREAKER: ALIBI (15 min)
Say: "A crime happened last night at 9 PM. You all need an alibi." Pick 2 students. Say: "You two — leave the room. You have 2 minutes to create your alibi story TOGETHER. Agree on: Where were you? What did you do? What time did you arrive and leave? What were you wearing?" Send them out. Set a 2-minute timer.

While they're out, tell the remaining 4 students: "You are detectives. Prepare 5 tough questions to catch them in a lie. Ask about tiny details — what color was the waiter's shirt? What song was playing?"

**Interrogation (5 min):** Bring Student A back ALONE. The 4 detectives ask their questions. Student A answers. Write key answers on the board. Send Student A out. Bring Student B back ALONE. Ask the SAME questions. Write answers next to Student A's. Compare.

**If stories don't match → "GUILTY!"** If they match → "NOT GUILTY."

**Report (3 min):** After the round, class reports using advanced reporting verbs. Write on board: "He claimed that..." "She denied..." "He insisted that..." Model: "He claimed that he had been at a restaurant. She denied being anywhere near the crime scene. He insisted that they had left before 10 PM." Each detective reports 1 sentence using a reporting verb.

Do 1 round if time is tight. If you finish fast, do a second round with 2 different students.

> The reporting verbs come naturally here. Don't teach them yet — just let students try. They'll meet them formally in the book activity.

### ☐ 00:30–00:50 | BOOK: MODULE 6B — REPORTING VERBS (20 min)
**What it's about:** Beyond "said" and "told." Reporting verbs change the structure. Write this list on the board BEFORE class starts:

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| suggest | + -ing | She suggested going to the park. |
| deny | + -ing | He denied stealing the money. |
| admit | + -ing | She admitted making a mistake. |
| promise | + to + infinitive | He promised to call me. |
| refuse | + to + infinitive | She refused to answer. |
| warn | + object + not to | He warned me not to go there. |
| encourage | + object + to | She encouraged me to try again. |
| recommend | + -ing | He recommended visiting the museum. |
| insist | + on + -ing | She insisted on paying. |
| apologize | + for + -ing | He apologized for being late. |

Say: "Open your B1 book to Module 6B — Reporting Verbs. Read the explanation and ALL examples. You have 10 minutes."

**First 10 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading. Point to the board if they look confused — the table is their reference.
**Last 10 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain the verb patterns to you. They must give you 5 examples using 5 different reporting verbs from the list. If wrong: "No. Listen." + correct version. They repeat. Next pair.

> You don't explain. They explain to you. If they mix up the patterns (suggest + to instead of suggest + -ing), just say the correct version. Don't say "suggest takes -ing."

### ☐ 01:10–01:45 | TBLT: PRESS CONFERENCE (35 min)
**How it works:** A mock press conference where 2 students defend a controversial policy and 4 students are journalists who must report what was said using reporting verbs.

**Step 1 — Setup (3 min).** Assign roles. Point at 2 students: "You are the Mayor and the Deputy Mayor. You just announced a controversial new policy: ALL PARKS IN THE CITY WILL BE CLOSED TO BUILD PARKING LOTS." Point at the other 4: "You are journalists. Your job is to ask tough questions and then write a news report."

**Step 2 — Prepare (7 min).** Mayor & Deputy Mayor sit together and prepare their defense. Tell them: "Write 5 arguments defending the policy. You MUST use passive voice and conditionals. Examples: 'This decision was made after careful study.' 'If more parking were built, traffic would be reduced.'" Journalists sit in pairs and prepare 8 questions. Tell them: "Ask about the environment, children, alternatives. Use question tags: 'This will destroy green spaces, won't it?'"

**Step 3 — Press Conference (15 min).** Mayor and Deputy Mayor sit at the front. Journalists raise hands and ask questions. Mayor/Deputy answer. Journalists take notes using reporting verbs — they write down what was said using the verbs from the board. Example notes: "The mayor insisted on closing the parks." "The deputy denied that the environment would be affected." "The mayor refused to consider alternatives."

**Step 4 — News Report (10 min).** Each journalist pair writes a 1-minute news report using reporting verbs + passive reporting. They present it to the class as if on TV. Model on the board: "It was reported that the mayor suggested building parking lots in all city parks. He denied that this would harm the environment. When asked about alternatives, the deputy refused to comment. Citizens were encouraged to submit complaints online." Each pair presents. Class votes: which news report was the most professional?

> Walk around WITH error paper during Steps 3 and 4. WRITE DOWN every reporting verb error.

### ☐ 02:05–02:25 | CORRECT THE TEACHER (20 min)
**Story with 15 errors (10 min).** Mix of WRONG reporting verb patterns — wrong verb pattern (suggest + to instead of -ing), wrong preposition, missing object, wrong form. Read the story slowly. Students shout "WRONG!" and say the correct version. Points per catch. Write the FULL story on the board or print it — students MUST see it.

"My boss had a meeting with us last week. She suggested us to work harder. **(1 — suggested working harder / suggested that we work harder)** She denied to know about the missing money. **(2 — denied knowing)** My colleague admitted to steal office supplies. **(3 — admitted stealing / admitted to stealing)**

After the meeting, my friend Carlos promised calling me later. **(4 — promised to call)** But he refused calling me back. **(5 — refused to call)** His girlfriend warned him to not ignore his friends. **(6 — warned him not to ignore)**

My mom encouraged to me try the new restaurant. **(7 — encouraged me to try)** She recommended to visit on a Thursday. **(8 — recommended visiting)** She insisted to pay for everything. **(9 — insisted on paying)**

My sister apologized for to be late. **(10 — apologized for being)** She claimed that to have traffic. **(11 — claimed that there had been traffic / claimed to have had traffic)** She denied to forget about the dinner. **(12 — denied forgetting / denied having forgotten)**

The doctor suggested me to exercise more. **(13 — suggested that I exercise / suggested exercising)** He warned not eat too much sugar. **(14 — warned me not to eat)** He encouraged that I to join a gym. **(15 — encouraged me to join)**"

**Pairs (10 min):** One student says the original (incorrect) sentences, the other converts using the correct reporting verb pattern. 5 min each. Switch roles. Go through at least 8 sentences each.

### ☐ 02:45–03:10 | PROJECT WORK: WRITING + TEACHER CORRECTIONS (25 min)
**What is this?** Dedicated time for students to advance their projects. You circulate and give individual written corrections.

**Step 1 — Status check (2 min).** Say: "Your projects are in the classroom. Take yours out and show me where you are." Walk around. For each student, quickly note: how far along are they? What section are they working on?

**Step 2 — Silent writing (15 min).** Say: "You have 15 minutes of writing time. Work on the weakest part of your project — the section you're least confident about. If you're not sure which section is weakest, ask your partner to read it and tell you." Classroom is SILENT. Students write. YOU circulate with a pen. When you see a grammar error in their writing: underline it. Do NOT write the correction — just underline. The student must figure out what's wrong. If they can't after 30 seconds, give them a hint: "Check the verb tense" or "This needs passive voice."

**Step 3 — Fix and improve (8 min).** Say: "Look at every underline I made. Fix each error. If you finished fixing all errors, keep writing new content." Walk around and verify corrections.

**DELIVERABLE: Take a photo of each student's draft showing your underlines AND their corrections. Send to coordination with progress notes.**

> This is individual attention time. Spend at least 2 minutes with each student's writing. Your underlines show coordination you actually read their work.

### ☐ 03:10–03:25 | BLENDING: COURTROOM DRAMA (15 min)
**What is this?** A role-play that forces students to use ALL modules from M1–M6B in a courtroom setting.

**Setup (2 min).** Assign roles — write them on the board and point at students:
- 1 Judge
- 1 Accused
- 1 Defense Lawyer
- 1 Prosecutor
- 2 Witnesses

Say: "The case: Someone is accused of stealing the exquisite golden trophy from the school."

**Prep (3 min).** Each person prepares their lines:
- **Witnesses** must report what they saw using reported speech + passive: "It was reported that the trophy was taken at midnight." "A neighbor told me that she had seen a menacing figure near the school."
- **Lawyers** must use conditionals: "If my client had stolen it, he would have been seen on camera." "If the witness were telling the truth, where is the evidence?"
- **Everyone** must use question tags, either/neither/both, and advanced adjectives somewhere in their lines.
- **Judge** delivers the verdict using reporting verbs: "The witness suggested that the accused had been present. The accused denied being anywhere near the school. Both the prosecutor and the defense presented intricate arguments."

**Performance (10 min).** Run the trial. You sit and watch. If someone forgets to blend modules, whisper: "Add a question tag" or "Use passive." Take notes on errors.

### ☐ 03:25–03:42 | SPEED DATING: VERB ROULETTE (17 min)
Write 6 reporting verbs on the board in BIG letters: **SUGGEST — DENY — ADMIT — WARN — PROMISE — REFUSE**

2 rows of chairs facing each other (or standing). Each round = 1 verb. Students MUST answer using THAT specific verb in their response. Then one row shifts. 6 rounds, ~2.5 min each.

1. **SUGGEST:** "Suggest an activity for this class that would be both fun and educational. Use 'suggest.'" (Example: "I would suggest doing a debate because...")
2. **DENY:** "Think of something you did as a child that you got in trouble for. Deny it to your partner." (Example: "I denied breaking the window. I told my mom that...")
3. **ADMIT:** "Admit something embarrassing that happened to you." (Example: "I have to admit falling asleep during a meeting once...")
4. **WARN:** "Warn your partner about something dangerous in your city." (Example: "I would warn you not to walk alone in that neighborhood at night.")
5. **PROMISE:** "Promise your partner something you'll do before next class." (Example: "I promise to study the reporting verbs for 15 minutes tonight.")
6. **REFUSE:** "Your partner asks you to do something crazy. Refuse and explain why." (Partner says the crazy request first, then the student refuses: "I refuse to eat a spider because...")

> WALK AROUND writing errors. Everyone standing = more energy. If a student uses "said" instead of the target verb, stop them: "Use the verb on the board."

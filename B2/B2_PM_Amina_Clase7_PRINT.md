## 6. CONECTORES DE ARGUMENTACIÓN (19:05–19:20, 15 min) — NUEVO BLOQUE

**OBJETIVO PARA LA ESTUDIANTE:**
> *"Anchors keep you talking when blank. Connectors STRUCTURE your argument. They show the listener HOW your ideas relate — and that's what makes you sound B2."*

**Escribe en sección visible del tablero (NUEVA sección — mantén toda la clase):**

```
CONNECTORS OF ARGUMENTATION — Group A: ADDITION & CONTRAST

ADDITION (more of the same):
1. "Furthermore..."
2. "What's more..."
3. "Not only that, but also..."

CONTRAST (opposite or different):
4. "However..."
5. "On the other hand..."
6. "Despite that..."

EMPHASIS (this is the main point):
7. "Most importantly..."
8. "What really matters is..."
9. "The key point is..."
```

**Práctica (12 min):**

**Round 1 (3 min) — Pronunciación + entendimiento:** cada estudiante lee 3 connectors (1 por categoría) en voz alta, claro.

**Round 2 (5 min) — Uso en frase aislada:** profesora da una frase corta. Estudiante agrega otra con un connector.
- *"Colombia has incredible biodiversity."* → *"Furthermore, it has a strong cultural identity."*
- *"The economy has improved."* → *"However, inequality remains."*
- *"There are many places to visit."* → *"Most importantly, the people make the difference."*

**Round 3 (4 min) — Argumento de 60 segundos:** cada estudiante hace 1 mini-argumento de 60 seg sobre su tema usando AL MENOS 2 connectors diferentes.

**Cierre (0 min):** *"In your Speak About in 20 minutes, you MUST use 2 connectors. Listening for them."*

> **Por qué Group A solo hoy:** mañana Cl 8 agregamos Group B (resultado/conclusión: "as a result", "consequently", "therefore"). NO sobrecargar.

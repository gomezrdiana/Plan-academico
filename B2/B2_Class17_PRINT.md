### 00:00--00:20 | ICEBREAKER: DESCRIBE WITHOUT NAMING (20 min)

Say: "Welcome back. Today we start Module 11 --- all about adjectives and how words change form. But first, a game. One of you will describe a person or a thing using ONLY adjectives. No names. No nouns that give it away. The rest of the class guesses what or who it is."

**Demo first.** Say: "I'll go first. Listen. This person is intelligent, enthusiastic, and incredibly brave. This person is also very colorful --- always wearing something bright. This person is strong but also a little foolish sometimes." Let them guess. If they struggle, add: "This person is very famous and fictional." (Answer can be anything --- the point is the demo.)

**Write on the board:**
```
RULES:
- Describe using ONLY adjectives
- No names, no obvious nouns
- Minimum 5 adjectives per description
- Class gets 3 guesses
```

**Round 1 --- People (10 min).** Each student thinks of a famous person or someone the class knows. They describe using only adjectives. Class guesses. Go around all 6 students (about 1.5 min each).

Say: "Think of someone famous or someone in this class. Describe them with at least 5 adjectives. Go."

If a student uses a noun instead of an adjective (e.g., "This person has beauty" instead of "This person is beautiful"), don't correct yet --- just note it on your error paper. This is diagnostic. You'll come back to noun vs. adjective forms in the book section.

**Round 2 --- Things (5 min).** 3 students describe an object or place using only adjectives. Class guesses.

Say: "Now describe a THING or a PLACE. Only adjectives. Make it hard."

> Pay attention to which students naturally use adjective forms vs. noun forms. Note who says "beauty" when they mean "beautiful" or "intelligence" when they mean "intelligent." This tells you who needs more work in the book section.

### 00:35--00:55 | BOOK: MODULE 11 GUIDE 1 --- NOUN-ADJECTIVE PAIRS (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Module 11, Guide 1 --- Noun-Adjective Pairs. Read the explanation and ALL 15 pairs with their examples. You have 8 minutes. Read everything --- the pairs, the notes, the examples. Don't skip anything."

**First 8 min:** They read in pairs. You don't speak. Walk around checking they're reading, not chatting.

**Last 12 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What is the difference between a noun form and an adjective form?" (Expected: The noun is the THING --- health, bravery, happiness. The adjective DESCRIBES --- healthy, brave, happy.)
- "Give me the adjective for BRAVERY." (brave)
- "Give me the noun for BEAUTIFUL." (beauty)
- "Give me the adjective for FOOLISHNESS." (foolish)
- "Give me the noun for ENTHUSIASTIC." (enthusiasm)
- "Give me the adjective for WISDOM." (wise)
- "Give me the noun for CARELESS." (carelessness)

If they get one wrong, say: "No. Listen." Give the correct answer. They repeat twice.

**Fill-in-the-blank test. Say each sentence aloud. Students give you the correct form (noun or adjective):**

1. "She has great ___. She is very ___." (beauty / beautiful)
2. "His ___ is admirable. He is extremely ___." (bravery / brave)
3. "I can see her ___. She is so ___." (sadness / sad)
4. "He has no ___. He is completely ___." (talent / talented) --- TRICK: "talented" is the adjective, but some may say "talentless." Accept both but confirm "talented" is the target.
5. "Your ___ could get you in trouble. Don't be so ___." (carelessness / careless)
6. "She has the ___ of a leader. She is truly ___." (strength / strong)
7. "His ___ about the project was contagious. He is incredibly ___." (enthusiasm / enthusiastic)
8. "The ___ of the painting was undeniable. It was truly ___." (beauty / beautiful)
9. "Her ___ saved the team. She made a very ___ decision." (wisdom / wise)
10. "His ___ was obvious to everyone. He is simply ___." (intelligence / intelligent)

One sentence per student. Go around. If they freeze, say: "Is it the THING or does it DESCRIBE? If it describes, it's the adjective. If it's the thing itself, it's the noun." They try again.

### 01:55--02:15 | BOOK: MODULE 11 GUIDE 2 --- COMMON PREFIXES AND SUFFIXES (20 min)

Say: "Open your book to Guide 2 --- Common Prefixes and Suffixes. Read the explanation and ALL the examples. You have 8 minutes."

**First 8 min:** They read in pairs. Walk around silently.

**Last 12 min:** Sit with each pair. THEY explain to you. Ask them:

- "What does the prefix UN- mean?" (Not. Countable becomes uncountable.)
- "What does IN- mean?" (Not. Exact becomes inexact.)
- "What is the difference between IN- and IM-?" (Same meaning --- 'not' --- but IM- is used before words starting with P, B, M. Possible becomes impossible. Patient becomes impatient.)
- "What does DIS- mean?" (Opposite or reversal. Agree becomes disagree.)
- "What does RE- mean?" (Again. Do becomes redo. Write becomes rewrite.)
- "What does OVER- mean?" (Too much. Work becomes overwork.)
- "What does UNDER- mean?" (Not enough. Estimate becomes underestimate.)
- "What does MIS- mean?" (Wrongly. Understand becomes misunderstand.)

Now suffixes:

- "What does -FUL mean?" (Full of. Beauty becomes beautiful. Care becomes careful.)
- "What does -LESS mean?" (Without. Care becomes careless. Hope becomes hopeless.)
- "What does -NESS mean?" (Turns adjective into noun. Happy becomes happiness. Sad becomes sadness.)
- "What does -MENT mean?" (Turns verb into noun. Enjoy becomes enjoyment.)
- "What does -TION or -SION mean?" (Turns verb into noun. Educate becomes education. Decide becomes decision.)
- "What does -ABLE or -IBLE mean?" (Can be done. Enjoy becomes enjoyable. Access becomes accessible.)
- "What does -OUS mean?" (Full of / having quality. Danger becomes dangerous.)
- "What does -IVE mean?" (Having tendency. Create becomes creative.)

**Root word drill. Say the root word. Students add the correct prefix or suffix:**

1. "Happy --- make it a noun." (happiness)
2. "Care --- make it mean 'without care.'" (careless)
3. "Care --- make it mean 'full of care.'" (careful)
4. "Possible --- make it negative." (impossible)
5. "Agree --- make it negative." (disagree)
6. "Understand --- make it mean 'understand wrongly.'" (misunderstand)
7. "Work --- make it mean 'work too much.'" (overwork)
8. "Estimate --- make it mean 'estimate too little.'" (underestimate)
9. "Enjoy --- make it a noun." (enjoyment)
10. "Enjoy --- make it an adjective meaning 'can be enjoyed.'" (enjoyable)
11. "Educate --- make it a noun." (education)
12. "Create --- make it an adjective." (creative)
13. "Danger --- make it an adjective." (dangerous)
14. "Do --- make it mean 'do again.'" (redo)
15. "Count --- make it mean 'cannot be counted.'" (uncountable)

One per student. Go around. If they freeze, say: "What does the prefix or suffix do? Think about the meaning." They try again. If wrong, say: "No. Listen." Give the correct answer. They repeat twice.

### 02:35--02:55 | CORRECT THE TEACHER: STORY WITH 12 ERRORS (20 min)

Say: "Now I'm going to tell you a story. This story has 12 errors --- all related to noun-adjective forms and wrong prefixes or suffixes. When you hear an error, shout 'WRONG!' and tell me the correct version. I'll keep score on the board. Ready?"

**Write on the board:** Student names in a row. Add a tally mark each time someone catches an error.

Read this story SLOWLY. Pause slightly before each error to give them time to catch it. Errors are marked for YOU --- do NOT read the corrections aloud:

---

"My friend Daniel is one of the most intelligence people I know. **(1 --- INTELLIGENT --- need the adjective form, not the noun 'intelligence')**

He has always been full of enthusiastic about science. **(2 --- full of ENTHUSIASM --- after 'full of' you need the noun, not the adjective 'enthusiastic')**

When we were kids, Daniel was very care with his experiments. **(3 --- very CAREFUL --- need -FUL suffix to mean 'full of care,' not the root word 'care')**

One time, he tried to build a rocket. He was impatient and a little careless. His careless almost burned down the garage. **(4 --- his CARELESSNESS --- need the noun form with -NESS, not the adjective 'careless')**

His parents were not happy. They said his behavior was unpossible to control. **(5 --- IMPOSSIBLE --- the prefix for 'possible' is IM-, not UN-)**

Daniel felt a lot of sad after that. **(6 --- a lot of SADNESS --- need the noun form, not the adjective 'sad')**

But his brave never disappeared. He kept experimenting. **(7 --- his BRAVERY --- need the noun form, not the adjective 'brave')**

In high school, Daniel was so talent that he won a science fair. **(8 --- so TALENTED --- need the adjective form, not the noun 'talent')**

The judges said his project was very educative and showed great create. **(9 --- great CREATIVITY --- need the noun form; and 'educative' should be EDUCATIONAL --- the -TION/-AL form, not -IVE)**

After high school, he went to university. He overunderestimated how hard it would be. **(10 --- UNDERESTIMATED --- you can't combine 'over' and 'under'; he thought it would be easy, so he underestimated it)**

He almost failed because he was too lazy. His lazy cost him an entire semester. **(11 --- his LAZINESS --- need the noun form with -NESS, not the adjective 'lazy')**

But Daniel redid his courses, worked harder, and today he is a very success scientist. His strong and wise have inspired many young people. **(12 --- his STRENGTH and WISDOM --- need the noun forms, not the adjective forms 'strong' and 'wise')**

Daniel always says: 'Fail beautifully --- ugly failures teach you nothing.'"

---

**After reading (5 min):** Review any errors the class missed. Say the wrong version first, then the correct version. They repeat the correct version twice.

**Pairs (8 min):** Say: "Now YOU are the teacher. Partner A: tell a short story --- 1 to 2 minutes --- with deliberate errors using noun-adjective swaps and wrong prefixes or suffixes. At least 4 errors. Partner B: catch and correct. After 4 minutes, SWITCH."

Walk around. Write down good corrections AND missed errors on your error paper.

> If the class catches fewer than 8 errors in the story, they need more practice. Note this for coordination.

### 02:55--03:10 | DEBATE: IS INTELLIGENCE MORE IMPORTANT THAN WISDOM? (15 min)

Say: "We're going to have a debate. The question is:" Write on the board:

```
DEBATE: Is intelligence more important than wisdom?

TEAM A: INTELLIGENCE (3 students)
TEAM B: WISDOM (3 students)

RULES:
- You MUST use both the NOUN and ADJECTIVE forms in your arguments
  (intelligence/intelligent, wisdom/wise)
- You MUST use at least 2 other noun-adjective pairs from today
- You MUST use at least 1 prefix or suffix word from Guide 2
```

**Step 1 --- Assign teams (1 min).** Count off: 1, 2, 1, 2, 1, 2. Ones = INTELLIGENCE. Twos = WISDOM. If someone disagrees with their team, say: "That's the point. Argue the position you're given."

**Step 2 --- Prepare (3 min).** Teams huddle. Each person prepares 2 arguments.

**Write example arguments on the board:**

```
INTELLIGENCE: "An intelligent person can solve problems
that a wise person cannot. Intelligence has led to every
major invention. Without intelligent scientists, we would
still be living in caves. Wisdom is overrated --- it is
just laziness disguised as patience."

WISDOM: "A wise person knows when NOT to act. Wisdom
is more valuable than intelligence because intelligent
people can be careless with their power. History is full
of intelligent people whose foolishness destroyed
everything. Wisdom brings happiness --- intelligence
brings stress."
```

**Step 3 --- Debate (8 min).** Format:
- Team A: Student 1 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 1 responds (1 min).
- Team A: Student 2 gives argument (1 min). Team B: Student 2 responds (1 min).
- Open floor: Anyone can jump in with a final point (2 min).
- Each response MUST use both noun AND adjective forms.

If someone gives an argument using only adjective forms, say: "Use the NOUN form too. Don't just say 'intelligent' --- say 'intelligence' in a sentence." They rephrase.

**Step 4 --- Vote (2 min).** Say: "Which team had the strongest arguments? Vote." Quick hand vote. Announce winner. Say: "Now --- what do YOU personally believe? One sentence. Use both forms." Go around. 6 students, ~15 seconds each.

> Error paper. This is free production --- errors will appear. Write them ALL down.

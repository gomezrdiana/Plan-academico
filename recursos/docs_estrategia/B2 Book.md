**Guide 1 вҖ“ Question Tags **



In Spanish, we often finish a statement with a question when we already know the answer or when we are confirming. For example: 

вҖңTГә eres Emile, Вҝverdad? 



In English, we call these вҖңquestion tagsвҖқ, and they have a very simple pattern. The question tag uses the same auxiliary as the principal statement. 

If the statement is affirmative, the tag is negative. 

E.g. вҖңWe will go tomorrow, wonвҖҷt\* we?вҖқ 



If the statement is negative, the tag is affirmative. 

E.g. вҖңWe wonвҖҷt go tomorrow, will we?вҖқ 

**\*Note: The question tag almost always uses the contraction \(E.g.: will not **вҶ’** wonвҖҷt\). **

For вҖңto beвҖқ: 

вҖңYou are sick, arenвҖҷt you?вҖқ 

вҖңYou arenвҖҷt sick, are you?вҖқ 

I am very tall, arenвҖҷt I? \(вҖңAmnвҖҷtвҖқ does not exist, so we use вҖңarenвҖҷtвҖқ for вҖңIвҖқ\) For the continuous: 

вҖңSam is playing soccer, isnвҖҷt he?вҖқ 

вҖңSam isnвҖҷt playing soccer, is he?вҖқ 

Also, for the perfect: 

вҖңSam has played soccer, hasnвҖҷt he?вҖқ 

вҖңSam hasnвҖҷt played soccer, has he?вҖқ 

Sometimes, we omit the auxiliary вҖңdo/does/didвҖқ in the affirmative. But we use it in the tag. 

вҖңSam plays soccer, doesnвҖҷt he?вҖқ 

вҖңYou play soccer, donвҖҷt you?вҖқ 

вҖңAndres played soccer, didnвҖҷt he?вҖқ

**Guide 1 вҖ“ вҖңEitherвҖҰorвҖқ **



We use вҖңeitherвҖқ to say вҖңoвҖқ or вҖңcualquierвҖқ. It indicates TWO options. We often use вҖңeitherвҖҰorвҖқ 

in a question or an affirmative sentence. 

1. Before a noun: 

вҖңPodemos hablar o espaГұol o ingles.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңWe can speak either Spanish or English.вҖқ 

вҖңYo prefiero o carne o pollo.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңI prefer either meat or chicken.вҖқ 



2. Before a verb: 

вҖңPodemos o nadar o volar.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңWe can either swim or fly.вҖқ 

вҖңDeberГӯamos o comer o tomar.вҖқ вҶ’ We should either eat or drink.вҖқ 



3. To indicate indifference between 2 options. Similar to Spanish, we can say вҖңofвҖқ after 

вҖңeitherвҖқ. 

вҖңCualquier de los chicos le puede ayudar.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңEither of the boys can help you.вҖқ 

вҖңCualquier de **ellos** le puede ayudar.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңEither of **them** can help you.вҖқ \(Note the object pronoun.\) 4. Finally, we can use вҖңeitherвҖқ in a negative sentence to say вҖңtampocoвҖқ. 

вҖңJohn doesnвҖҷt have a car. I donвҖҷt have a car, either.вҖқ 

вҖңJohn canвҖҷt play soccer, and I canвҖҷt, either.вҖқ

**Guide 2 вҖ“ вҖңNeitherвҖҰnorвҖқ **



вҖңNeitherвҖҰnorвҖқ \(вҖңniвҖҰniвҖқ\) is the negative version of вҖңeitherвҖҰorвҖқ in Spanish. It is also used to indicate 2 options. 

1. Before a noun: 

вҖңNo puedo hablar ni espaГұol ni ingles.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңI can speak neither Spanish nor English.вҖқ

вҖңI canвҖҷt speak neither Spanish nor English.вҖқ INCORRECT 

вҖңI can speak neither Spanish nor English.вҖқ CORRECT

вҖңNo podemos ni nadar ni volar.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңWe can neither swim nor fly.вҖқ

3. To indicate 0 of 2 options. Similar to Spanish, we can say вҖңofвҖқ after вҖңneitherвҖқ. 

вҖңNinguno de los chicos le puede ayudar.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңNeither of the boys can help you.вҖқ

**Guide 1 вҖ“ Verbs of Deduction **



Similar to Spanish, we use verbs of deduction to express a supposition \(something we think or believe\). The verb depends on the certainty the speaker feels: **Certainty **

**Verb of Deduction **

**English Example **

**Spanish Translation **

**Very certain **

Must 

He must be sick. 

Гүl debe estar enfermo. 

****





Might 

He might be sick. 

**Partially certain **

May 

He may be sick. 

Гүl podrГӯa estar enfermo. 

Could 

He could be sick. 

****





**Very certain of **

CanвҖҷt 

He canвҖҷt be sick. 

Гүl no puede estar enfermo. 

**the opposite **

Must not \(mustnвҖҷt\) 

He mustnвҖҷt be sick. 



It is very common to use deduction with the verb вҖңto beвҖқ. We often use it in the continuous and perfect tenses.

**Guide 1 вҖ“ The Past Modals **



In the 3rd Conditional \(straight\), we used **вҖңwouldвҖқ \+ the present perfect** to indicate our willingness \(voluntad\) to do something. 1. **вҖңWouldвҖқ ** вҖ“ Willingness in the past: 

**Would** \+ present 

Willingness \(voluntad\) in

вҖңHabrГӯa ido a la tienda.вҖқ 

perfect

perfect

**Guide 2 вҖ“ Expressing Regret **



вҖңShould/could/would haveвҖқ are sometimes used to express regrets. 

I should have gone to the game. Everyone had a great time\! 

Two other expressions are commonly used to express regret: 1. вҖңTo wishвҖқ. Similar to вҖңojalГЎвҖқ in Spanish. 

I wish I had gone to the game. вҶ’ OjalГЎ hubiera ido al partido. 

2. вҖңIf onlyвҖқ. More common in England. In North America, it is considered a bit dramatic. 

If only I had gone to the game. вҶ’ Si tan solo hubiera ido al juego. 



Note that both expressions use the вҖңhad \+ participleвҖқ form similar to the 3rd conditional. 



North American speakers often use both expressions with вҖңwould have \+ participleвҖқ. This is incorrect, but generally accepted English. 

I wish I would have gone to the game.

**Guide 1 вҖ“ Order of Objects **



1. Many sentences include both direct and indirect objects. 

2. The direct object is the person/thing that receives the action of the verb directly. 

I buy a flower. 

I give a flower. 

3. The *indirect object* is the person/thing affected by the action of the verb, but not directly. 

I buy a flower for *Anita*. 

I give a flower to *Anita*. 



4. I buy/give a flower \(direct\). I do not buy/give Anita \(indirect\). Anita is affected by the action. 

5. English permits either the direct or indirect object to be positioned first. 

a. Case 1: \[direct\] \+ вҖңto/forвҖқ \+ \[indirect\] вҖ“ the preposition вҖңtoвҖқ / вҖңforвҖқ must be included. 

I buy a flower for *Anita*. 

I give a flower to *Anita*. 

b. Case 2: \[indirect\] \+ \[direct\] вҖ“ no preposition between the objects. 

I buy *Anita* a flower. 

I give *Anita* a flower.

**Guide 5 вҖ“ Already and Yet **





1. These two words are commonly used with perfect tenses. 



2. Already: Indicates that the verb occurred earlier than the time referenced by the sentence. It is positioned as an adverb of frequency. Logically, it is usually used in affirmative sentences.

Past perfect: When you called, I had already played soccer. 

Future perfect: When you call, I will have already played soccer.

**Guide 4 вҖ“ Impersonal Expressions **



1. Impersonal expressions are common in Spanish and formal/technical English. In general, conversational English prefers active verbs to impersonal expressions. 

вҖңEs necesario que tГә ayudes.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңTГә necesitas ayudar.вҖқ 

2. Impersonal expressions can use a relative pronoun or an infinitive verb. 

a. \(Relative pronoun\): вҖңEs necesario que tГә ayudes.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңIt is necessary that you help.вҖқ

**Guide 3 вҖ“ Reciprocal Pronouns **



1. We use the reciprocal pronouns to indicate that a group of people \(or objects\) performed the same action, at the same time, that affected each of them in the same way. For example, if you are talk with someone, they are also talking with you. There are only two reciprocal pronouns: 

вҖңEach otherвҖқ вҖ“ 2 actors 

вҖңOne anotherвҖқ вҖ“ 3 or more actors 

2. Using reciprocal pronouns allows us to speak more efficiently. For example: 

вҖңBecky talked with Sandy, and Sandy talked with Becky.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңBecky and Sandy talked with each other.вҖқ 

вҖңSam hit Biff. Biff hit Fred. Fred hit Sam.вҖқ вҶ’ вҖңSam, Biff, and Fred hit one another.вҖқ 

3. вҖңOne anotherвҖқ is becoming less common in English. Many speakers use вҖңeach otherвҖқ for any number of subjects. 

Sam, Biff, and Fred hit each other.

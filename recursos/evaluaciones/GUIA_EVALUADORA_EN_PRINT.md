## SECTION 1 — THE ROLE'S PHILOSOPHY (read BEFORE the session — do not fill)

**Your job is not to test level. It's to open Heiiu's first door.**

Today you do 2 things at once:

1. **DIAGNOSE the level** (ladder A1 → B2 with concrete questions)
2. **ACTIVATE their competitive drive** (specific phrases planted at key moments)

**The prospect must leave this session with:**
- Clarity about their current level (without anxiety or shame)
- Awareness of the **GAP** between where they are and where they could be
- A self-image as someone **competitive with themselves**
- Genuine desire to commit to a real plan

**The profile Heiiu seeks:** students who see English as a tool to grow, who are competitive with themselves (not with others), who want to speak the world's language. Your session filters AND activates that profile.

**What you are NOT today:** an examiner, a teacher, a surface-level motivator. You are a sharp observer + a strategic seed-planter.

## SECTION 2 — OPENING (30 SECONDS — literal script)

[ ] Warm greeting, smile, use their name.

**If the prospect is clearly A1 or unknown (deliver in Spanish):**

> *"Hola [name]. Esto no es un examen — es una conversación para ver dónde estás HOY con el inglés. En 3 a 6 meses te vas a reír de este momento. Empezamos relajado y vamos subiendo cuando sienta que puedes más. ¿Listo?"*

**If the prospect can start in English (deliver in English):**

> *"Hey [name], nice to meet you. This isn't a test — it's a conversation to see where you are today. In 3 to 6 months you'll laugh at this moment. We start relaxed and go up when I see you can. Cool?"*

> **Why the opening works:** the phrase *"in 3 to 6 months you'll laugh"* plants growth from second 1. It's NOT anxiety ("let's see how bad you are") — it's trajectory ("this is your starting point, not your destination").

## SECTION 4 — ACTIVATOR CLOSING (3 min at end — literal script)

**What you say to the prospect when you finish the ladder. Adapt to THEIR detected level:**

### EN version (recommended for English-comfortable prospects):

> *"Here's what I see. Your level TODAY is [X]. The distance to [next level] is roughly [Y concrete structures — mention 2-3 they failed]. I'll be honest: the students who close that gap are the ones who come with real discipline. They're competitive with themselves, not with others. They speak the global language because they work it daily, not because they're gifted.*
>
> *Are you one of those?"*

**PAUSE. Listen.**

### ES version (recommended with Spanish-speaking prospects):

> *"Esto es lo que veo. Tu nivel HOY es [X]. La distancia hasta [siguiente nivel] son aproximadamente [Y estructuras concretas]. Te voy a ser honesta: los estudiantes que cierran ese gap son los que vienen con disciplina real, son competitivos consigo mismos, no con otros. Hablan el mismo idioma del mundo porque lo trabajan diariamente, no porque sean superdotados.*
>
> *¿Eres de esos?"*

### Reading their response:

- **Energy / yes / "absolutely"** → HOT → Mark below + write what they said literally.
- **Doubt / "I think so" / "depends"** → WARM → Ask *"What's your doubt?"* → Write the doubt.
- **Apathy / "I don't know" / long silence** → COLD → Close warmly without pressure. Mark COLD.

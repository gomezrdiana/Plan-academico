ANEXO HEIIU B2 — PORTFOLIO BUILD (TEACHER VERSION)
===

> **FOR THE TEACHER:** This annex is your script for the daily Portfolio Build block (15-20 min) in every B2 class. You do NOT need expertise in HR, recruiting, LinkedIn, or CV writing. **You only need to read the scripts and give feedback on pronunciation, clarity, and fluency.** The expert is the material, not you.

---

## THE 5-DAY CYCLE — RUNS CONTINUOUSLY

| Class # in cycle | Block name | What the student produces |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Mock Interview Round | 1 STAR answer (recorded if possible) |
| Day 2 | 60-Second Video Pitch | 1 pitch recorded (each cycle improves) |
| Day 3 | Project Presentation Sprint | 1 minute of project rehearsal |
| Day 4 | Mock Phone Call (BPO sim) | 1 simulated client call |
| Day 5 | Correct the Recruiter | Group correction of intentional errors |

**The cycle repeats: Class 6 = Day 1 of new cycle. Continuous, regardless of calendar.**

How to know which day you're on:
- Class 2 = Day 1 (Mock Interview)
- Class 3 = Day 2 (Pitch)
- Class 4 = Day 3 (Presentation)
- Class 5 = Day 4 (Phone Call)
- Class 6 = Day 5 (Correct the Recruiter)
- Class 7 = Day 1 of cycle 2... and so on.

> **Coordination tracks the cycle and writes "Today is Day X of Portfolio Cycle" in your daily class plan. If you don't see it, ask.**

---

## DAY 1 — MOCK INTERVIEW ROUND (15 min)

**What students do:** Each student answers 1 STAR question. Partner gives quick feedback. Then 1 student volunteers to be recorded by you while answering a 2nd question. Coordination reviews the recording.

**What you (teacher) do:**

1. **Setup (1 min):** Open `ANEXO_BANCO_PREGUNTAS_STAR.pdf`. Pick **2 random questions** for today (one for pairs, one for the volunteer recording).

2. **Pairs round (8 min):**
   - Write Question 1 on the board.
   - Say: "Pairs. Person A answers using STAR — Situation, Task, Action, Result. 90 seconds. Person B listens and times. Then switch."
   - **STAR reminder on board (always):**
     ```
     S — Situation: When? Where? What was happening?
     T — Task: What was your responsibility?
     A — Action: What did YOU specifically do?
     R — Result: What was the outcome? (numbers, change)
     ```
   - 90 sec each. Switch. Then 90 sec each AGAIN with their answer improved (45 sec/round × 4 = 6 min total speaking. 2 min for switching.)

3. **Volunteer recording (4 min):**
   - Ask: "Who wants to record their answer for coordination feedback?" Pick 1.
   - Show Question 2.
   - "60 seconds. Use STAR." Press record on YOUR phone. They speak.
   - After: **don't critique content.** Say: "Good. Coordination will give detailed feedback."
   - Send recording to coordination via WhatsApp the same day.

4. **Group reflection (2 min):**
   - "What was the hardest part of using STAR?" 2 students share.
   - **Don't teach STAR mechanics.** Just listen.

**Your feedback during the activity (oral):**
- Pronunciation correction: "No. Listen. **An-a-ly-sis** (4 syllables). Repeat." 2x.
- Clarity: "Slower. Pause between Situation and Task."
- Filler words: "Cut 'like' and 'you know.' Pause instead."

**What you do NOT do:**
- ❌ Give advice on what to put in their answer ("you should mention you led the team")
- ❌ Evaluate the quality of their work history
- ❌ Suggest career strategies
- ✅ Only: pronunciation, clarity, fluency, pacing.

## DAY 2 — 60-SECOND VIDEO PITCH (15 min)

**What students do:** Each student records a 60-second video pitch about themselves on their own phone. They get 1 minute to plan, 60 sec to record, 60 sec to listen back. Repeat with same pitch — they improve.

**What you (teacher) do:**

1. **Setup (1 min):** Write the pitch structure on the board:
   ```
   60-SECOND PITCH STRUCTURE:
   [10 sec] Hook: Who am I in one phrase?
   [15 sec] What I do / studied + 1 result
   [15 sec] What I'm passionate about + 1 example
   [15 sec] What I'm looking for / next step
   [5 sec]  Closing: name + email or LinkedIn
   ```

2. **Plan round (3 min):**
   - "Take 3 minutes. Write your 5 sections in your notebook. Single bullet point each. No paragraphs."

3. **Record round 1 (4 min):**
   - "Stand up. Find a spot. Phone in selfie mode. 60 seconds, hit record. GO." Set timer.
   - Walk silently. Don't interrupt. Don't make eye contact (it disturbs them).
   - At 60 sec: "Stop. Replay it. Listen to yourself for 60 seconds."

4. **Improve round (5 min):**
   - "What's one thing you want to change? Pick ONE — pace, a word, the hook, anything."
   - 1 minute to think. Then record AGAIN, 60 seconds.

5. **Submit (2 min):**
   - "Send the IMPROVED version to the WhatsApp group with the tag: PITCH-CYCLE[N]-[YourName]."
   - Coordination collects all pitches and gives written feedback per student before next Day 2.

**Your feedback during activity (oral):**
- Walk silently mostly.
- If someone freezes: "Just say one true sentence. Then keep going."
- After replay: "What did YOU notice? What's one fix?"

**What you do NOT do:**
- ❌ Tell them what to say or how to brand themselves
- ❌ Evaluate their qualifications
- ✅ Only: facilitate the timer, encourage replay, send to coordination.

## DAY 3 — PROJECT PRESENTATION SPRINT (15 min)

**What students do:** Each student is building 1 final project across the entire B2 (Shark Tank pitch, business plan, research presentation, etc — coordination defines the project for each cohort). On Day 3 of every cycle, each student gets ~1 min to rehearse 1 slide / 1 section out loud.

**What you (teacher) do:**

1. **Setup (1 min):** Write on board:
   ```
   TODAY'S SPRINT:
   - 1 minute per student
   - Speak only ABOUT the section you're working on this week
   - No reading from screen
   - Listen for: clarity + 1 strong sentence per section
   ```

2. **Rehearsal round (12 min):**
   - 12 students × ~1 min each. If less time, do all in 30 sec each. If you have 16 students, half present today, half next Day 3.
   - Each student stands. Says: "My project section this week is about X."
   - They speak ~1 min about that section. No notes.

3. **Peer feedback (2 min):**
   - After each student, ask the group: "What was clear? What was the strongest sentence?" One peer answers.
   - **Don't critique content. Only celebrate clarity.**

**Your feedback during activity:**
- Pronunciation, clarity, pacing.
- "Stronger ending — repeat that last sentence with more confidence." Make them repeat it.

**What you do NOT do:**
- ❌ Evaluate the project's business viability
- ❌ Give strategy advice
- ✅ Only: speaking quality.

> If a student doesn't have a project section ready, say: "Then practice your 60-sec pitch instead. 1 minute, GO." Don't penalize.

## DAY 4 — MOCK PHONE CALL (BPO SIMULATION) (15 min)

**What students do:** Pairs simulate a customer service phone call in English. One is the agent, one is the customer. They use a scenario you read aloud.

**What you (teacher) do:**

1. **Setup (1 min):** Pick 1 of these 5 scenarios:

   **SCENARIO 1 — Internet customer (irritated):**
   > Customer: "Internet is down for 3 hours. Already called twice. Wants compensation."
   > Agent's job: apologize, troubleshoot, offer credit.

   **SCENARIO 2 — Hotel booking issue:**
   > Customer: "Booked a non-smoking room online. Just arrived. Got smoking room. Furious."
   > Agent's job: apologize, offer alternatives, retain customer.

   **SCENARIO 3 — Product return:**
   > Customer: "Bought wireless headphones. Battery dies in 2 hours. Wants full refund. Past 30-day window."
   > Agent's job: empathize, find solution within policy.

   **SCENARIO 4 — Healthcare appointment:**
   > Patient: "Surgery scheduled tomorrow 7 AM. Just got call to reschedule for next month. Already took time off work."
   > Agent's job: apologize, find earliest reschedule, escalate if needed.

   **SCENARIO 5 — Tech support (B2B):**
   > Client: "Software crashes when uploading files >50MB. Lost 2 hours of work. Project deadline tomorrow."
   > Agent's job: empathize, give immediate workaround, follow-up commitment.

2. **Run the simulation (10 min):**
   - Read the scenario aloud.
   - Pairs choose roles. 4 minutes per call.
   - At minute 4: "Switch roles. New scenario? Use the same one — different perspective."

3. **Group debrief (4 min):**
   - "What phrases worked best? What was hard?" 2 students share each.
   - Write the BEST phrases on the board: "I understand your frustration." / "Let me see what I can do." / "I'll personally follow up by [time]."

**Your feedback during activity:**
- Walk pairs. Listen.
- Correct pronunciation immediately: "No. Listen. **Apologize** (a-PAW-lo-jize). Repeat."
- Listen for "filler" words (so, um, like) and signal them with a hand gesture.

**What you do NOT do:**
- ❌ Teach customer service strategy
- ❌ Evaluate business decisions
- ✅ Only: phrases, pronunciation, professional tone.

## DAY 5 — CORRECT THE RECRUITER (15 min)

**What students do:** YOU pretend to be a recruiter calling the student. You make intentional errors (grammar, pronunciation, professional vocabulary). The student listens and corrects you AT THE END (not during).

**What you (teacher) do:**

1. **Setup (1 min):**
   - Write on board: "I will pretend to be a recruiter with intentional errors. After I finish, you correct me."
   - Pick 1 of these 5 scripts (or rotate weekly).

2. **The script — read aloud as the "recruiter" (5 min):**

   **SCRIPT A — Phone screening:**
   > "Hello, this is Jennifer from RecruitGlobal. I am calling for inform you that we have receive your application. Your CV is *very impressing*. We want to schedule a interview for next Tuesday at three. Are you available in this time? The interview will *take* about thirty minutes and we will discuss about your experience. Please *confirm me* if you can attend."
   >
   > **Errors:** "calling for inform" (should be "to inform"), "have receive" (received), "very impressing" (impressive), "a interview" (an), "in this time" (at this time), "discuss about" (discuss, no 'about'), "confirm me" (confirm with me).

   **SCRIPT B — Job offer:**
   > "Good news! We *would like* offer you the position of Senior Analyst. Your salary will be six thousand *dollar* per month, plus *some* benefits like health insurance and twenty days of *vacations*. We need that you *start* on the first of next month. The contract will be sending to you by email. Please review *it carefully* and *return* signed copy."
   >
   > **Errors:** "would like offer" (would like to offer), "six thousand dollar" (dollars), "vacations" (vacation, uncountable), "we need that you start" (we need you to start), "return signed copy" (return a signed copy).

   **SCRIPT C — Rejection (delicate):**
   > "Thank you for *applying to our company*. After *carefully consideration*, we have decide to *go* with another candidate. Your interview was *very good*, but we found someone who is more *fitting* for the position. Please don't *take* it personally. We will *keep* your CV in our database for *future* opportunities. Good luck *in your search*."
   >
   > **Errors:** "carefully consideration" (careful), "have decide" (decided), "go with" (proceed with — too informal for rejection), "more fitting" (better suited), "in your search" (with your search).

   **SCRIPT D — Salary negotiation:**
   > "I see you are *asking for* sixty thousand *dollar*. We can offer fifty-five, but with *more benefits*. We have *very flexible* work schedule, you can work from home *two day per week*, and we *give* a yearly bonus *of* ten percent. Also, we offer professional development with *courses* and certifications. *What you think* about this?"
   >
   > **Errors:** "sixty thousand dollar" (dollars), "two day per week" (days), "we give" (we offer), "What you think" (What do you think).

   **SCRIPT E — Reference check:**
   > "Hi, I am calling for reference of [name]. Did *he/she worked* with you? How long? *Which were* their responsibilities? *How was* their performance? *Are* they reliable? *Did they leaved* on good terms? Would you *re-employ* them? Thank you for *your time*."
   >
   > **Errors:** "Did he/she worked" (worked), "Which were" (What were), "Did they leaved" (left), "re-employ" (re-hire is more natural).

3. **Correction round (8 min):**
   - "Now — what errors did I make? Don't say all at once. Raise hands. One at a time."
   - Each student catches 1 error → 1 English Point.
   - For each error: student says wrong version → student says correct version → class repeats correct version 2x.

4. **Reflection (1 min):**
   - "Which error would have been EMBARRASSING in a real interview?" 1-2 students share.

**Your feedback during activity:**
- Celebrate every catch. "Yes! Listen — discuss, no 'about.' Discuss something. Repeat."
- If they miss errors, give a hint: "I made a mistake with the verb 'discuss'..."

**What you do NOT do:**
- ❌ Lecture about each error
- ✅ Only: model correct version, have them repeat.

## WHAT YOU DO **NOT** TEACH

You are NOT responsible for:
- CV writing → Coordination provides templates. Students write at home. Coordination reviews.
- LinkedIn optimization → Same. Coordination handles.
- Career strategy → Coordination + students.
- Industry knowledge → Coordination + external speakers (Gringo Nights).

**You teach English. The portfolio is built BY the students using your speaking practice + coordination's templates. You are a facilitator, not a career coach.**

If a student asks you a non-English question ("Should I include this internship on my CV?"), say:
> "Great question for coordination. Today we work on speaking. Write the question down — bring it to coordination."

## REGLA DE ORO

**Si te sientes incómodo facilitando algo de Portfolio Build, sigue el script al pie de la letra. No improvises.** Los scripts están diseñados para que un profesor de inglés sin experiencia en HR pueda ejecutarlos. Si te apartas del script intentando ser experto en algo que no eres, perderás credibilidad. Si sigues el script, el material habla por sí mismo.

Tu trabajo: **leer, cronometrar, escuchar, corregir pronunciación.** Eso es todo. Y eso es exactamente lo que el Blueprint pide.
